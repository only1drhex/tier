Guest Question.

A tube, with 50 mm and 75 mm as inner and outer diameters respectively, is reinforced by shrinking a jacket with an outer diameter of 100 mm. The compound tube is to withstand an internal pressure of 35 MPa. The shrinkage allowance is such that the maximum tangential stress in each tube has same magnitude. Calculate
(i) the shrinkage pressure; and
(ii) the original dimensions of tubes.
Show the distribution of tangential stresses.
Assume E = 207 kN/mm².

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } \quad D_{1}=50 mm \quad D_{2}=75 mm \quad D_{3}=100 mm \).

\( E=207 kN / mm ^{2} \quad P_{i}=35 MPa \).

The resultant stresses in the tubes are obtained by superimposing the stresses due to internal pressure and those due to shrinkage pressure.
Step I Stresses due to internal pressure (Eq. 22.7)

\( \sigma_{t}=+\frac{P_{i} D_{1}^{2}}{\left(D_{3}^{2}-D_{1}^{2}\right)}\left[\frac{D_{3}^{2}}{4 r^{2}}+1\right] \) (22.7).

\( \sigma_{t}=+\frac{P_{i} D_{1}^{2}}{\left(D_{3}^{2}-D_{1}^{2}\right)}\left[\frac{D_{3}^{2}}{4 r^{2}}+1\right] \).

\( =+\frac{(35)(50)^{2}}{\left(100^{2}-50^{2}\right)}\left[\frac{100^{2}}{4 r^{2}}+1\right] \).

\( =+11.67\left[\left(\frac{50}{r}\right)^{2}+1\right] \).

Substituting the values of (r), the stresses are as follows:

50 37.5 25 r
23.34 32.42 58.35 \(\sigma_{t}\)

Step II Stresses due to shrinkage pressure
Jacket (Eq. 22.7)

\( \sigma_{t}=+\frac{P_{i} D_{1}^{2}}{\left(D_{3}^{2}-D_{1}^{2}\right)}\left[\frac{D_{3}^{2}}{4 r^{2}}+1\right] \) (22.7).

\( \sigma_{t}=+\frac{P_{i} D_{1}^{2}}{\left(D_{3}^{2}-D_{1}^{2}\right)}\left[\frac{D_{3}^{2}}{4 r^{2}}+1\right] \).

\( =+\frac{P(75)^{2}}{\left(100^{2}-75^{2}\right)}\left[\frac{100^{2}}{4 r^{2}}+1\right] \).

\( =+1.286 P\left[\left(\frac{50}{r}\right)^{2}+1\right] \).

The stresses are as follows:

50 37.5 r
(2.57P) (3.57P) \(\sigma_{t}\)

Inner tube (Eq. 22.17)

\( \sigma_{t}=-\frac{P D_{2}^{2}}{\left(D_{2}^{2}-D_{1}^{2}\right)}\left[1+\frac{D_{1}^{2}}{4 r^{2}}\right] \) (22.17).

\( \sigma_{t}=-\frac{P D_{2}^{2}}{\left(D_{2}^{2}-D_{1}^{2}\right)}\left[1+\frac{D_{1}^{2}}{4 r^{2}}\right] \) .

\( =-\frac{P(75)^{2}}{\left(75^{2}-50^{2}\right)}\left[1+\frac{50^{2}}{4 r^{2}}\right]=-1.8 P\left[1+\left(\frac{25}{r}\right)^{2}\right] \).

The stresses are as follows:

37.5 25 r
(–2.6 P) (–3.6 P) \(\sigma_{t}\)

Step III Shrinkage pressure
Equating stresses at the inner surfaces of tube and jacket

58.35 – 3.6 P = 32.42 + 3.57 P
or P = 3.62 MPa

Step V Original dimensions of tubes
From Eq. 22.22,

\( \delta=\frac{P D_{2}}{E}\left[\frac{2 D_{2}^{2}\left(D_{3}^{2}-D_{1}^{2}\right)}{\left(D_{3}^{2}-D_{2}^{2}\right)\left(D_{2}^{2}-D_{1}^{2}\right)}\right] \) (22.22).

\( \delta=\frac{P D_{2}}{E}\left[\frac{2 D_{2}^{2}\left(D_{3}^{2}-D_{1}^{2}\right)}{\left(D_{3}^{2}-D_{2}^{2}\right)\left(D_{2}^{2}-D_{1}^{2}\right)}\right] \) .

\( =\frac{(3.62)(75)}{\left(207 \times 10^{3}\right)}\left[\frac{2(75)^{2}\left(100^{2}-50^{2}\right)}{\left(100^{2}-75^{2}\right)\left(75^{2}-50^{2}\right)}\right] \)

= 0.0081 mm
The inner diameter of the jacket should be
(75 – 0.0081) or 74.9919 mm.
