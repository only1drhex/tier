Guest Question.

Gravimetric analysis of a mixture of three gases indicates 20% \( N _{2}, 40 \% CO _{2} \text {, and } 40 \% O _{2} \text {. } \). Find the heat transfer needed to increase the temperature of 20 lbm of the mixture from 80°F to 300°F in a rigid tank.

Admin
Solution

The heat transfer needed is given by the first law as (the work is zero for a rigid tank) Q = ΔU = mΔu =\( m C_{v} \Delta T \text {. We must find } C_{v} . \text { It is given by (12.18a) as } \)

\( C_{v}=\sum x_{i} C_{v, i} \quad \bar{C}_{v}=\sum y_{i} \bar{C}_{v, i} \) (12.18).

\( C_{v}=x_{1} C_{v, 1}+x_{2} C_{v, 2}+x_{3} C_{v, 3}=(0.2)(0.177)+(0.4)(0.158)+(0.4)(0.157) \).

= 0.161 Btu/ lbm-°R.

\( \text { The heat transfer is then } Q=m C_{v} \Delta T=(20)(0.161)(300-80)=708 \text { Btu. } \).
