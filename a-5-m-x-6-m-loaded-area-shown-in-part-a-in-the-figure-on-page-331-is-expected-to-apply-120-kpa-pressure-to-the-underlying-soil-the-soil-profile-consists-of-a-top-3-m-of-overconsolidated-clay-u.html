Guest Question.

A 5 m × 6 m loaded area shown in part (a) in the figure on page 331 is expected to apply 120 kPa pressure to the underlying soil. The soil profile consists of a top 3 m of overconsolidated clay underlain by 8 m of normally consolidated clay with the following soil properties:

O.C. Clay N.C. Clay
Average OCR 5 1
Average initial void ratio 0.8 1.2
Compression index 0.60 0.65
Recompression index 0.06 0.07
Saturated unit weight (kN/\(\left.m^{3}\right)\) 19.0 17.5
Undrained modulus (MPa) 20.0 16.0

Estimate the immediate settlement and the final consolidation settlement.

Admin
Solution

Immediate settlement: The charts to compute the immediate settlements (Figure 12.16) are valid only in homogeneous soils, not when there are two layers. Let’s make some adjustments, and using the principles of superposition, break the soil profile into three separate soil profiles as shown in part (b) in the figure on page 331 and compute the immediate settlements \(s_{i, 1}, s_{i, 2}, \text { and } s_{i, 3}\). The immediate settlement is given by \(s_{i, 1}+s_{i, 2}-s_{i, 3}\).

Profile 1:

 

\(D_{f} / B=0.75 / 5=0.15 \rightarrow \mu_{0}=0.97\)

 

\(B / L=5 / 6=0.833, H / B=2.25 / 5=0.45 \rightarrow \mu_{1}=0.15\)

 

\(s_{i, 1}=\frac{q B}{E_{u}} \mu_{0} \mu_{1}=\frac{120 \times 5000}{20,000} \times 0.97 \times 0.15 mm =4.4 mm\)

 

Profile 2:

 

\(D_{f} / B=0.75 / 5=0.15 \rightarrow \mu_{0}=0.97\)

 

\(B / L=5 / 6=0.833, H / B=10.25 / 5=2.05 \rightarrow \mu_{1}=0.62\)

 

\(s_{i, 2}=\frac{q B}{E_{u}} \mu_{0} \mu_{1}=\frac{120 \times 5000}{16,000} \times 0.97 \times 0.62 mm =22.6 mm\)

 

Profile 3:

 

\(D_{f} / B=0.75 / 5=0.15 \rightarrow \mu_{0}=0.97\)

 

\(B / L=5 / 6=0.833, H / B=2.25 / 5=0.45 \rightarrow \mu_{1}=0.15\)

 

\(s_{i, 3}=\frac{q B}{E_{u}} \mu_{0} \mu_{1}=\frac{120 \times 5000}{16,000} \times 0.97 \times 0.15 mm =5.5 mm\)

 

\(\therefore \text { Immediate settlement }=4.4+22.6-5.5=21.5 mm\)

 

Consolidation settlement: Let’s divide the N.C. layer into two, 4.0 m thick sublayers and compute \(\sigma_{\nu 0}^{\prime} \text { and } \Delta \sigma^{\prime}\) (using Figure 7.5) at the middle of the sublayers.

 

Layer 1 (OC) Layer 2 (NC) Layer 3 (NC)
\(\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}( kPa )\) 17.2 43.0 73.7
Depth below ftg (m) 1.125 4.25 8.25
\(\Delta \sigma^{\prime}( kPa )\) 112 55 22

 

\(\text { Layer 1: } \sigma_{ p }^{\prime}=5 \times 17.2=86 kPa <\sigma_{w 0}^{\prime}+\Delta \sigma^{\prime}=129.2 kPa\)

 

\(\Delta e=0.06 \log \frac{86}{17.2}+0.60 \log \frac{129.2}{86}=0.148 \rightarrow s_{c, 1}=\frac{0.148}{1+0.8} \times 2250=185 mm\)

 

Layer 2: Normally consolidated

 

\(\Delta e=0.60 \log \frac{43+55}{43}=0.215 \rightarrow s_{c, 2}=\frac{0.215}{1+1.2} \times 4000=391 mm\)

 

Layer 3: Normally consolidated

 

\(\Delta e=0.60 \log \frac{73.7+22}{73.7}=0.068 \rightarrow s_{c, 3}=\frac{0.068}{1+1.2} \times 4000=124 mm\)

 

Final consolidation settlement = 185 + 391 + 124 = 700 mm

Note: The settlement is high. Some ground improvement or alternate foundations may be proposed.
