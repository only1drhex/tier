Entropy Generation in a Heat Exchanger

Air in a large building is kept warm by heating it with steam in a heat exchanger (Fig. 7–67). Saturated water vapor enters this unit at 35°C at a rate of 10,000 kg/h and leaves as saturated liquid at 32°C. Air at 1-atm pressure enters the unit at 20°C and leaves at 30°C at about the same pressure. Determine the rate of entropy generation associated with this process.

Admin
Solution

Air is heated by steam in a heat exchanger. The rate of entropy generation associated with this process is to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 The heat exchanger is wellinsulated so that heat loss to the surroundings is negligible and thus heat transfer from the hot fluid is equal to the heat transfer to the cold fluid. 3 Changes in the kinetic and potential energies of fluid streams are negligible. 4 Air is an ideal gas with constant specific heats at room temperature. 5 The pressure of air remains constant.

Analysis The rate of entropy generation within the heat exchanger is determined by applying the rate form of the entropy balance on the entire heat exchanger:

\(\begin{aligned}\underbrace{\dot{S}_{\mathrm{in}}-\dot{S}_{\mathrm{out}}}_{\begin{array}{c}\text { Rate of net entropy } \\\text {transfer by heat} \\\text { and mass }\end{array}} + \underbrace{\dot{S}_{\mathrm{gen}}}_{\begin{array}{r}\text { Rate of entropy } \\\text { generation }\end{array}} &=\underbrace{d S_{\mathrm{system}}/dt^{\nearrow 0 (steady)}}_{\begin{array}{r}\text { Rate of change } \\\text { in entropy } \\\end{array}} \\\dot{m}_{\text {steam }} s_{1}+\dot{m}_{\text {air }} S_{3}-\dot{m}_{\text {steam }} s_{2}-\dot{m}_{\text {air }} s_{4}+\dot{S}_{\text {gen }}&=0\\\dot{S}_{\text {gen }}&=\dot{m}_{\text {steam }}\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)+\dot{m}_{\text {air }}\left(s_{4}-s_{3}\right)\end{aligned}\)

The specific heat of air at room temperature is \(c_{p}=1.005 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\) . The properties of the steam at the inlet and exit states are

\(\left.\begin{array}{l}T_{1}=35^{\circ} \mathrm{C} \\x_{1}=1\end{array}\right\} \begin{array}{l}h_{1}=2564.6 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \\s_{1}=8.3517 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}\end{array}\)

 

\(\left.\begin{array}{l}T_{2}=32^{\circ} \mathrm{C} \\x_{2}=0\end{array}\right\} \begin{array}{l}h_{2}=134.10 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \\s_{2}=0.4641 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}\end{array}\)

From an energy balance the heat transferred from steam is equal to the heat transferred to the air. Then, the mass flow rate of air is determined to be

\(\begin{array}{c}\dot{Q}=\dot{m}_{\text {steam }}\left(h_{2}-h_{1}\right)=(10,000 / 3600 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s})(2564.6-134.10) \mathrm{kJ} / \mathrm{kg}=6751 \mathrm{~kW} \\\\\dot{m}_{\mathrm{air}}=\frac{\dot{Q}}{c_{p}\left(T_{4}-T_{3}\right)}=\frac{6751 \mathrm{~kW}}{\left(1.005 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot{ }^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\right)(30-20)^{\circ} \mathrm{C}}=671.7 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s}\end{array}\)

Substituting into the entropy balance relation, the rate of entropy generation becomes

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{S}_{\text {gen }}=& \dot{m}_{\text {steam }}\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)+\dot{m}_{\text {air }}\left(s_{4}-s_{3}\right) \\=& \dot{m}_{\text {steam }}\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)+\dot{m}_{\text {air }} c_{p} \ln \frac{T_{4}}{T_{3}} \\=&(10,000 / 3600 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s})(0.4641-8.3517) \mathrm{kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K} \\&+(671.1 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s})(1.005 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}) \ln \frac{303 \mathrm{~K}}{293 \mathrm{~K}} \\=& 0.745 \mathrm{~kW} / \mathrm{K}\end{aligned}\)

Discussion Note that the pressure of air remains nearly constant as it flows through the heat exchanger, and thus the pressure term is not included in the entropy change expression for air.
