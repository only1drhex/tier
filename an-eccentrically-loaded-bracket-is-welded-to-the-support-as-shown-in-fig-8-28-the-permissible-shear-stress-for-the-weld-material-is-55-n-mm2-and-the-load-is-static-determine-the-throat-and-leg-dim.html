Guest Question.

An eccentrically loaded bracket is welded to the support as shown in Fig. 8.28. The permissible shear stress for the weld material is 55 N/mm² and the load is static. Determine the throat and leg dimensions for the welds.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { Given } P=25 kN \quad \tau=55 N / mm ^{2} \).

Step I Primary shear stress
There are two vertical welds \( W_{1} \text { and } W_{2} \) and one horizontal weld \( W_{3} \). By symmetry, the centre of gravity G of the welds is midway between the vertical welds.

\( \text { or } \quad \bar{x}=50 mm \).

Taking moments about the top weld and treating the weld as a line,

\( (150+150+100) \bar{y}=(150)(75)+(150)(75) + (100) (0) \).

\( \bar{y}=56.25 mm \) (from the top weld)
The areas of the three welds are as follows:

\( A_{1}=(150 t) mm ^{2} \).

\( A_{2}=(150 t) mm ^{2} \).

\( A_{3}=(100 t) mm ^{2} \).

The primary shear stress in the weld is given by,

\( \tau_{1}=\frac{P}{A}=\frac{25000}{400 t}=\frac{62.5}{t} N / mm ^{2} \) (i).

Step II Secondary shear stress
As seen from Fig. 8.28, A is the farthest point from the centre of gravity and its distance r is given by

\( r=\overline{G A}=\sqrt{(150-56.25)^{2}+(50)^{2}}=106.25 mm \).

\( \text { also, } \quad \tan \theta=\frac{(150-56.25)}{50} \quad \text { or } \quad \theta=61.93^{\circ} \).

\( \phi=90-\theta=90-61.93=28.07^{\circ} \).

Therefore, the secondary shear stress \( \tau_{2} \) is inclined at 28.07° with the horizontal

e = 50 + 100 = 150 mm
M = P × e = (25000) (150) = 3 750 000 N-mm
\( G_{1}, G_{2} \text { and } G_{3} \) are the centres of gravity of the three welds and the distances are as follows:

\( \overline{G_{1} G}=\overline{G_{2} G}=\sqrt{(75-56.25)^{2}+(50)^{2}}=53.4 mm \).

\( \text { or } \quad r_{1}=r_{2}=53.4 mm \).

\( r_{3}=\overline{G_{3} G}=\bar{y}=56.25 mm \).

From Eq. (8.25),

\( J_{G}=A\left[\frac{l^{2}}{12}+r_{1}^{2}\right] \) (8.25).

\( J_{1}=J_{2}=A_{1}\left[\frac{l^{2}}{12}+r_{1}^{2}\right] \).

\( =(150 t) \times\left[\frac{(150)^{2}}{12}+(53.4)^{2}\right] \).

\( =(708984 t) mm ^{4} \).

\( J_{3}=A_{3}\left[\frac{l^{2}}{12}+r_{3}^{2}\right] \).

\( =(100 t) \times\left[\frac{(100)^{2}}{12}+(56.25)^{2}\right] \).

\( =(399740 t) mm ^{4} \).

\( J=2 J_{1}+J_{3}=2(708984 t)+(399740 t) \).

\( =(1817708 t) mm ^{4} \).

The secondary shear stress at the point A is given by

\( \tau_{2}=\frac{M r}{J}=\frac{(3750000)(106.25)}{(1817708 t)} \).

\( =\left(\frac{219.2}{t}\right) N / mm ^{2} \) (ii).

Step III Resultant shear stress
The secondary shear stress is inclined at an angle of 28.07° with horizontal. It is resolved into vertical and horizontal components as shown in Fig. 8.29.
The components are as follows:

\( \text { Vertical component }=\left(\frac{219.2}{t}\right) \sin (28.07) \).

\( =\left(\frac{103.14}{t}\right) N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \text { Horizontal component }=\left(\frac{219.2}{t}\right) \cos (28.07) \).

\( =\left(\frac{193.42}{t}\right) N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \text { The primary shear stress }\left(\frac{62.5}{t}\right) \text { is vertically upward.} \)

Therefore, the total vertical component is

\( \left(\frac{103.14}{t}+\frac{62.5}{t}\right) \quad \text { or } \quad\left(\frac{165.64}{t}\right) \).

and the resultant shear stress is given by

\( \tau=\sqrt{\left(\frac{165.64}{t}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{193.42}{t}\right)^{2}}=\left(\frac{254.65}{t}\right) N / mm ^{2} \).

Step IV Size of weld
The permissible shear stress for the weld material is 55 N/mm². Therefore

\( 55=\left(\frac{254.65}{t}\right) \text { or } t=4.63 mm \).

\( h=\frac{t}{0.707}=\frac{4.63}{0.707}=6.55 \cong 7 mm \).
