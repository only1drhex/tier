Guest Question.

Computing Wattmeter Readings in Three-Phase Circuits

Calculate the reading of each wattmeter in the circuit in Fig. 11.20 if the phase voltage at the load is 120 V and (a) \(Z_Φ = 8 + j6 \ Ω\);(b) \(Z_Φ = 8 - j6 \ Ω\);(c) \( Z_Φ = 5 + j5\sqrt{3} \ Ω\); and (d) \(Z_Φ = 10\angle -75^\circ \ Ω\). (e) Verify for (a)–(d) that the sum of the wattmeter readings equals the total power delivered to the load.

Admin
Solution

a) \(Z_Φ = 10\angle 36.87^\circ \ Ω\),\(V_L=120\sqrt{3}\) V, and \(I_L=120/10 = 12\) A.

\(W_1 =(120\sqrt{3})(12) cos (36.87° + 30° )\)

= 979.75 W,

\(W_2 =(120\sqrt{3})(12) cos (36.87° – 30° )\)

= 2476.25 W,

b) \(Z_Φ = 10\angle -36.87^\circ \ Ω\) \(V_L=120\sqrt{3}\) V, and \(I_L=120/10 = 12\) A.

\(W_1 =(120\sqrt{3})(12) cos (-36.87° + 30° )\)

= 2476.25 W,

\(W_2 =(120\sqrt{3})(12) cos (-36.87° – 30° )\)

= 979.75 W.

c) \(Z_Φ =5(1 + j\sqrt{3}) =10\angle 60^\circ \ Ω\) \(V_L=120\sqrt{3}\) V, and \(I_L= 12\) A.

\(W_1 =(120\sqrt{3})(12) cos (60° + 30° )=0\)

\(W_2 =(120\sqrt{3})(12) cos (-60° – 30° )\)

= 2160 W.

d) \(Z_Φ = 10\angle -75^\circ \ Ω\) \(V_L=120\sqrt{3}\) V, and \(I_L= 12\) A.

\(W_1 =(120\sqrt{3})(12) cos (-75° + 30° )=1763.63 \) W

\(W_2 =(120\sqrt{3})(12) cos (-60° – 30° )=-645.53\) W

e) \(P_T(a) = 3(12)^2(8) = 3456\) W,

\(W_1+W_2= 979.75 + 2476.25\)

= 3456 W,

\(P_T(b) = P_T(a) = 3456\) W

\(W_1+W_2= 2476.25 + 979.75\)

= 3456 W,

\(P_T(c) = 3(12)^2(5) = 2160\) W,

\(W_1+W_2= 0 + 2160\)

= 2160 W,

\(P_T(d) =3(12)^2(2.5882) = 1118.10 \) W,

\(W_1+W_2=1763.63 – 645.53\)

= 1118.10 W.
