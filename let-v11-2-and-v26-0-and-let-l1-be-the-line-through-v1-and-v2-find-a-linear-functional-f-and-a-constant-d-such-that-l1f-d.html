Guest Question.

Let \( v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right ] \text{and} v_{2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 6 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \) , and let \( L_{1} \) be the line through \( v_{1} \) and \( v_{2} \). Find a linear functional f and a constant d such that \( L_{1} = [f : d] \).

Admin
Solution

The line \( L_{1} \) is parallel to the translated line \( L_{0} \) through \( v_{2} – v_{1} \) and the origin. The defining equation for \( L_{0} \) has the form

\( \left [ \begin{matrix} a & b \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} x \\ y \end{matrix} \right ] = 0 \text{or} n \cdot x = 0 , \text{where} n =\left [ \begin{matrix} a \\ b \end{matrix} \right ] \) (5)

Since n is orthogonal to the subspace \( L_{0} \), which contains \( v_{2} – v_{1} \), compute

\( v_{2} – v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 6 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] – \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 5 \\ -2 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

and solve

\( \left [ \begin{matrix} a & b \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} 5 \\ -2 \end{matrix} \right ] = 0 \)

By inspection, a solution is \( \left [ \begin{matrix} a & b \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & 5 \end{matrix} \right ] \). Let f(x,y) = 2x + 5y. From (5), \( L_{0} = [f:0] \), and \( L_{1} = [f:d] \) for some d. Since \( v_{1} \) is on line \( L_{1} \), \( d = f(v_{1}) = 2(1) + 5(2) = 12 \). Thus, the equation for \( L_{1} \) is 2x + 5y = 12. As a check, note that \(f(v_{2}) = f(6,0) = 2(6) + 5(0) = 12 \), so \( v_{2} \) is on \( L_{1} \), too.
