Guest Question.

In the article “Advances in Oxygen Equivalent Equations for Predicting the Properties of Titanium Welds” (D. Harwig, W. Ittiwattana, and H. Castner, The Welding Journal, 2001:126s-136s), the authors propose an oxygen equivalence equation to predict the strength, ductility, and hardness of welds made from nearly pure titanium. The equation is \(E=2 C+3.5 N+O\), where \(E\) is the oxygen equivalence, and \(C, N\), and \(O\) are the proportions by weight, in parts per million, of carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen, respectively (a constant term involving iron content has been omitted). Assume that for a particular grade of commercially pure titanium, the quantities \(C, N\), and \(O\) are approximately independent and normally distributed with means \(\mu_{C}=150\), \(\mu_{N}=200, \mu_{O}=1500\), and standard deviations \(\sigma_{C}=30, \sigma_{N}=60, \sigma_{O}=100\). Find the distribution of \(E\). Find \(P(E>3000)\).

Admin
Solution

Since \(E\) is a linear combination of independent normal random variables, its distribution is normal. We must now find the mean and variance of \(E\). Using Equation (4.26), we compute

\(c_{1} X_{1}+c_{2} X_{2}+\cdots+c_{n} X_{n} \sim N\left(c_{1} \mu_{1}+c_{2} \mu_{2}+\cdots+c_{n} \mu_{n}, c_{1}^{2} \sigma_{1}^{2}+c_{2}^{2} \sigma_{2}^{2}+\cdots+c_{n}^{2} \sigma_{n}^{2}\right)\) (4.26)

\(\begin{aligned}\mu_{E} &=2 \mu_{C}+3.5 \mu_{N}+1 \mu_{O} \\&=2(150)+3.5(200)+1(1500) \\&=2500 \\ \sigma_{E}^{2} &=2^{2} \sigma_{C}^{2}+3.5^{2} \sigma_{N}^{2}+1^{2} \sigma_{O}^{2} \\&=2^{2}\left(30^{2}\right)+3.5^{2}\left(60^{2}\right)+1^{2}\left(100^{2}\right) \\ &=57,700 \end{aligned}\)

We conclude that \(E \sim N(2500,57,700)\).

To find \(P(E>3000)\), we compute the \(z\)-score: \(z=(3000-2500) / \sqrt{57,700}=\) 2.08. The area to the right of \(z=2.08\) under the standard normal curve is \(0.0188\). So \(P(E>3000)=0.0188\).
