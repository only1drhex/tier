Guest Question.

A synchronous generator feeds power to a power system. The generator and power system data are:

Generator: 100 MVA, 11 kV
Unsaturated synchronous reactance \( X_{s} = 1.3 pu\)
Admin
Solution

The equivalent circuit model of the generator feeding the power system is drawn in Fig. 8.48(a).

Bases \( ( MVA )_{ B }=\) 100

\( ( kV )_{ B }\) =11

Power \( ( MW )_{ B }=\) 100

(a) \( E_{f}=1 pu , P_{e}=1 pu\), specified \( V_{ TH }=1 pu\)

Max. Power Supplied,

\( P_{e, \max }=\frac{E_{f} V_{ th }}{X_{s}+X_{ TH }}\)

Or \( P_{e, \max }=\frac{1}{1.3+0.24}=0.649 pu\) or \( V_{ TH }=1 pu\)

For power transferred from generator terminals to load

\( P_{e}=\frac{V_{t} V_{ TH }}{X_{ TH }} \sin \delta_{1}\)

1\( =\frac{1}{0.24} \sin \delta_{1}\)

 

\( \delta_{1}=\sin ^{-1}(0.24)=13.9^{\circ}\)

Reference phasor \( V_{ TH } \angle 0^{\circ}\)

 

\( \bar{V}_{t}=e^{j 13.9^{\circ}}\)

 

\( \bar{I}_{a}=\frac{V_{t}-V_{ TH }}{j X_{ TH }}=\frac{\left(e^{j 13.9^{\circ}}-1\right)}{j 0.24}=1+j 0.122=1.007 \angle 7^{\circ} pu\)

To find \( E_{f}\) and \( I_{f}\)

 

\( \bar{E}_{f}=\bar{V}_{ TH }+j\left(X_{s}+X_{ TH }\right) \bar{I}_{a}=(1+j 0)+j(1.54)(1+j 0.122)=1.74 e^{j 62.2^{\circ}}\)

 

\( \left|\bar{E}_{f}\right|=1.741 pu =19.15 kV\)

 

\( \delta=62.2^{\circ}\)

Slope of air-gap line, \( \frac{V_{o c}}{I_{f}}=\frac{11}{256} \text { V/A }\)

For \( F_{f}=\) 19.15 kV

\( I_{f}=\frac{256}{11} \times 19.15=\) 445.7 A

(b) \( E_{f} = 1 pu\) (held constant), \( P_{e}\) varied

Let \( \bar{E}_{f}=E_{f} e^{j \delta}\)

 

\( P_{e}=\frac{E_{f} V_{ TH }}{X_{s}+X_{ TH }}\)

 

\( P_{e}=\frac{1}{1.54} e^{j \delta}\) (i)

\( \bar{I}_{a}=\frac{E_{f} e^{j \delta}-V_{ TH } \angle 0^{\circ}}{j\left(X_{s}+X_{ TH }\right)}=\frac{e^{j \delta}-1}{j 1.54}\) (ii)

We then get

\( \bar{V}_{t}=\bar{V}_{ TH }+j X_{ TH } \bar{I}_{a}\)

 

\( \bar{V}_{t}=1+\frac{0.24}{1.54}\left(e^{j \delta}-1\right)\)

\(\bar{V}_{t}=1+\frac{0.24}{1.54}\left(e^{j \delta}-1\right) \) (iii)

Choose a value of \( P_{e}\) and find \( \delta\) from Eq. (i). Substitute in Eq. (ii) and find the magnitude \( V_{t}\) from Eq. (iii).

\( \bar{V}_{t}\) versus \( P_{e}\) is plotted as shown in the Fig. 8.48(b) by Using MATLAB program given below.

\( P_{e}=0: 0.01: 0.8\)

 

\( V_{t}=1+(0.24 / 1.54) *\left(1.54 * P_{e}-1\right)\)

Plot \( \left(P_{e}, V_{t}\right)\)

(c) \( V_{1}\) = 1 pu (controlled at this value)

\( P_{e}\) varied

\( P_{e}=\frac{V_{t} V_{ TH }}{X_{ TH }} \sin \delta_{1}=\frac{1}{0.24} \sin \delta_{1}\)

 

\( \bar{V}_{t}=e^{j \delta_{1}}\)

 

\( \bar{I}_{a}=\frac{e^{j \delta_{1}}-1}{j 0.24}\)

 

\( \bar{E}_{f}=\bar{V}_{ TH }+j 1.54 \bar{I}_{a}=1+(j 1.54) \frac{\left(e^{j \delta_{1}}-1\right)}{j 0.24}=1+\frac{1.54}{0.24}\left(e^{j \delta 1}-1\right)\)

 

\( \bar{I}_{f}=\frac{256}{11} \bar{E}_{f}=\frac{256}{11}\left[1+\frac{1.54}{0.24}\left(3^{j \delta_{1}}-1\right)\right]\)
