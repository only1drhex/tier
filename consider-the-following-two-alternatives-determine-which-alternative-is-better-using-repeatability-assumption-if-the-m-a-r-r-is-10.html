Guest Question.

Consider the following two alternatives:

Alternative A Alternative B
Investment \(\text { (₹) }\) 17,500 25,500
Annual revenue \(\text { (₹/year) }\) 9,500 12,500
Annual disbursements \(\text { (₹/year) }\) 3,225 6,915
Useful life (years) 4 8
Net salvage value \(\text { (₹) }\) 0 0
Admin
Solution

Solution of Example 6.5 by the Present Worth (P.W.) Method P.W. of alternative A is determined as:

 

\(\begin{aligned}( P . W .)_{ A }=&-₹ 17,500+₹ 9,500(P / A, 10 \%, 8)-₹ 3,225(P / A, 10 \%, 8) \\&-₹ 17,500(P / F, 10 \%, 4)\end{aligned}\)

 

\(( P . W .)_{ A }=-₹ 17,500+₹ 9,500(5.3349)-₹ 3,225(5.3349)-₹ 17,500(0.6830)\)

 

\(\text { (P.W.) }_{ A }=₹ 4,023.99\)

 

P.W. of alternative B is determined as:

 

\(\begin{array}{l}\text { (P.W.) }_{ B }=-₹ 25,000+₹ 12,500(P / A, 10 \%, 8)-₹ 6,915(P / A, 10 \%, 8) \\\text { (P.W.) }_{ B }=-₹ 25,000+₹ 12,500(5.3349)-₹ 6,915(5.3349) \\\text { (P.W.) }_{ B }=₹ 4,795.42\end{array}\)

 

On comparing \(\text { (P.W.) }_{A} \text { with (P.W.) }_{B}\), it is found that alternative B is better than alternative A and therefore, alternative B should be chosen.

Solution of Example 6.5 by the Future Worth (F.W.) Method F.W. of alternative A is determined as:

 

\(\begin{aligned}( F . W .)_{ A }=&-₹ 17,500(F / P, 10 \%, 8)+₹ 9,500( F / A , 10 \%, 8)-₹ 3,225(F / A, 10 \%, 8) \\&-₹ 17,500(F / P, 10 \%, 4)\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}( F . W .)_{ A }=&-₹ 17,500(2.1436)+₹ 9,500(11.4359)-₹ 3,225(11.4359) \\&-₹ 17,500(1.4641)\end{aligned}\)

 

\(( F . W .)_{ A }=₹ 8,625.52\)

 

F.W. of alternative B is determined as:

 

\(( F . W .)_{ B }=-₹ 25,000(F / P, 10 \%, 8)+₹ 12,500(F / A, 10 \%, 8)-₹ 6,915(F / A, 10 \%, 8)\)

 

\(( F . W .)_{ B }=-₹ 25,000(2.1436)+₹ 12,500(11.4359)-₹ 6,915(11.4359)\)

 

\(( F . W .)_{ B }=₹ 10,279.50\)

 

On comparing \(\text { (F.W.) }_{\text {A }} \text { with (F.W.) }_{\text {B }}\), it is found that alternative B is better than alternative A and therefore, alternative B should be chosen.

Solution of Example 6.5 by the Annual Worth (A.W.) Method A.W. of alternative A is determined as:

 

\(( A . W .)_{ A }=-₹ 17,500(A / P, 10 \%, 4)+₹ 9,500-₹ 3,225\)

 

\(\text { (A.W.) }_{A}=-₹ 17,500(0.3155)+₹ 9,500-₹ 3,225\)

 

\(\text { (A.W.) }_{A}=₹ 753.75\)

 

A.W. of alternative B is determined as:

 

\(\text { (A.W.) }_{\text {B }}=-₹ 25,000(A / P, 10 \%, 8)+₹ 12,500-₹ 6,915\)

 

\(\text { (A.W.) }_{\text {B }}=-₹ 25,000(0.1874)+₹ 12,500-₹ 6,915\)

 

\(( A . W .)_{ B }=₹ 900\)

 

On comparing \((\text { A.W. })_{A} \text { with (A.W.) }_{B}\) , it is found that alternative B is better than alternative A and therefore, alternative B should be chosen.

Solution of Example 6.5 by the Internal Rate of Return (I.R.R.) Method Let us compute I.R.R. by finding A.W. for the two alternatives at an unknown i’ and equating them.

 

\(-₹ 17,500\left(A / P, i^{\prime} \%, 4\right)+₹ 9,500-₹ 3,225=-₹ 25,000\left(A / P, i^{\prime} \%, 8\right)+₹ 12,500-₹ 6,915\)

 

Linear interpolation between 12% and 15% results in \(i^{\prime}=12.68 \%\), which is the I.R.R. Since 12.68% > 10%, the increment \(A \longrightarrow B\) is justified. Thus, alternative B is better than alternative A and it should be selected.

Solution of Example 6.5 by the External Rate of Return (E.R.R.) Method with e = 10%

 

Alternative A Alternative B
Investment \((₹)\) 17,500 25,000
FW of net annual cash flows \((₹)\): \(\begin{array}{l}₹(9,500-3,225)(F / A, 10 \%, 8) \\₹(12,500-6,915)(F / A, 15 \%, 8)\end{array}\) 71,760.27 63,869.50
FW of Salvage \((₹)\): – –
Increment considered:
Δ Investment \((₹)\) 7,500
Δ FW of savings \((₹)\) (71,760.27 + 63,869.50)= 1,35,629.77

 

Since E.R.R. (43.6%) >10%, the increment \(A \rightarrow B\) is justified. Thus, alternative B is better than alternative A and it should be selected.

Solution of Example 6.5 by the Explicit Reinvestment Rate of Return (E.R.R.R.) Method with e = 10%

 

Alternative A ternative B
Net annual cash flows \(\text { (₹) }\): (9,500 – 3,225) = 6,275 (12,500 – 6,915) = 5,585
Depreciation \(\text { (₹) }\): 1,529.50 2,185
Total annual cash flows \(\text { (₹) }\): 6,275 – 1,529.5 = 4,745.5 5,585 – 2,185 = 3,400
Δ Annual expenses (annual savings) \(\text { (₹) }\) 4,745.5 + 3,400 = 1,345.5
Δ Investment \(\text { (₹) }\): 25,000 – 17,500 = 7,500
E.R.R.R. on Δ Investment 1,345.5/7,500= 17.93%

 

Since E.R.R.R. (17.93%) >10%, the increment \(A \rightarrow B\) is justified. Thus, alternative B is better than alternative A and it should be selected.
