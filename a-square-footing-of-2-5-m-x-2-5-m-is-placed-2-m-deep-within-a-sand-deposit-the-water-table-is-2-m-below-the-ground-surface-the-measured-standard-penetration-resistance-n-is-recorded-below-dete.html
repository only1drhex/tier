Guest Question.

A square footing of \(2.5 \mathrm{~m} \times 2.5 \mathrm{~m}\) is placed \(2 \mathrm{~m}\) deep within a sand deposit. The water table is \(2 \mathrm{~m}\) below the ground surface. The measured standard penetration resistance \(N\) is recorded below. Determine the allowable bearing capacity for a limiting settlement of \(25 \mathrm{~mm}\) based on (a) Terzaghi and Peck (1943) and (b) Meyerhof (1965) approaches (assume \(\gamma=19 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\) and \(\gamma_{\text {sat }}=21 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\) ).

Depth (m) 1.5 2.5 3.5 4.5 5.5 6.5 7.5 8.5
N value 8 9 14 15 23 28 32 32
Admin
Solution

a. Terzaghi and Peck (1967) suggested considering \(N\) values from the base of the foundation to a depth equal to \(B\) below the square foundation (see table below).

Depth (m) Nvalue \(\sigma_{v}^{\prime}\) (kPa) \(C_{N} \) (from Fig. 9.4) N (corr.) = \((N ×C_{N} ) \)
1.5 8 – – –
2.5 9 43.6 1.28 12
3.5 14 54.8 1.2 17
4.5 15 66 1.14 17
5.5 23 77.17 1.09 25
6.5 28 88.36 1.04 29
7.5 32 – –
8.5 32 – –

The average value of \(N\) interpolated between depths \(2.5 \mathrm{~m}\) and \(6.5 \mathrm{~m}\) is 20 . The provisional value of allowable bearing capacity is \(210 \mathrm{kPa}\) (from Fig. 9.3), which should then be multiplied by \(C_{w}\) to correct for the effect of the water table. Thus:

\(\begin{aligned}C_{w} &=0.5+0.5 \frac{D_{w}}{D+B} \\&=0.5+0.5 \frac{2}{2+2.5}=0.72\end{aligned}\)

Therefore, the allowable bearing pressure is given by \(q_{a}=0.72 \times 210=151 \mathrm{kPa}\).

b. Meyerhof (1965) approach:

The average value of measured \(N\) between \(z=D\) and \(D+B\) is 15 . Hence:

\(q_{a}=\frac{25 \times 15}{2.84}\left(\frac{2.5+0.33}{2.5}\right)^{2}=169 \mathrm{kPa}\)

This example shows that the Terzaghi and Peck method yields more conservative values of \(q_{a}\) compared to the Meyerhof approach.
