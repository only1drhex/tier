Guest Question.

Given:

The soil profile and properties of Examples and . The stress increase in the clay layer is 100 kPa.

Required:

Compute the time required for the clay layer to settle 0.25 m.

Admin
Solution

To compute the average degree of consolidation,the final consolidation settlement \(s_{c}\), must be estimated as we did in Chapter 8. From Fig. Ex. 9.2, \(H_{o}=12 m \text { and } e_{o}=0.62\). For Chicago clay, the value of \(C_{c}\) may be found using the last relationship in Table 8.3, \(C_{c}=0.01 \times\left(w_{n}=23.2 \%\right)=0.23\). \(C_{c}\) may also be found from the secorid expression, knowing the initial void ratio, solving for \(C_{c \varepsilon}\) and dividing by \(\left(1+e_{o}\right), \text { giving } C_{c}=0.22\), or about the same. Determine œÅ for the soft clay and calculate \(\sigma_{v o}^{\prime}\) at the middepth of layer from Eqs. (6.14c) and (6.15). Assume the clay is normally consolidated. Use techniques from Chapter 2 to compute \(\rho_{\text {clay }}\). So,

 

\(\sigma=\sigma^{\prime}+u\) (6.14)

 

\(\sigma_{v}=\int_{0}^{h} \rho g d z\) (6.15a)

 

\(\sigma_{v}=\rho g h\) (6.15b)

 

\(\sigma_{v}=\sum_{i=1}^{n} \rho_{i} g z_{i}\) (6.15c)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{v o}^{\prime}=& \rho_{\text {sand }} g z_{1}+\left(\rho_{\text {sand }}-\rho_{w}\right) g z_{2}+\left(\rho_{\text {clay }}-\rho_{w}\right) g z_{3} \\\sigma_{v o}^{\prime}=& 1.8 Mg / m ^{3} \times 9.8 m / s ^{2} \times 1.5 m +(1.8-1) Mg / m ^{3} \times 9.8 m / s ^{2} \times 3 m \\& \quad+(2.02-1) Mg / m ^{3} \times 9.8 m / s ^{2} \times 6 m \\=& 110 kPa\end{aligned}\)

 

From Eq. (8.10),

 

\(s_{c}=C_{c} \frac{H_{o}}{1+e_{o}} \log \frac{\sigma_{2}^{\prime}}{\sigma_{1}^{\prime}}\) (8.10)

 

\(s_{c}=0.23 \frac{12 m }{1+0.62} \log \frac{110 kPa +100 kPa }{110 kPa }=0.48 m\)

 

The average degree of consolidation \(U_{\text {avg }}\) when the clay layer settles 0.25 m is [Eq. (9.12)]:

 

\(U_{ avg }=\frac{s(t)}{s_{c}}\) (9.12)

 

\(U_{ avg }=\frac{s(t)}{s_{c}}=\frac{0.25 m }{0.48 m }=0.52, \text { or } 52 \%\)

 

To obtain T we can use either Table 9.1 or Fig. 9.5. Or, since \(U_{ avg }<60 \%\), we can use Eq. (9.10):

 

\(T=\frac{\pi}{4} U^{2}=\frac{\pi}{4}\left(\frac{U \%}{100}\right)^{2}\) (9.10)

 

\(T=\frac{\pi}{4}(0.52)^{2}=0.212\)

 

From Eq. (9.5), \(t=T H_{ dr }^{2} / c_{v}, \text { where } H_{ dr }=6\) m for double drainage; or

 

\(T=c_{v} \frac{t}{H_{ dr }^{2}}\) (9.5)

 

\(\begin{aligned}t &=\frac{0.212 \times(6 m )^{2}}{8 \times 10^{-8} m ^{2} / s \times 3.1536 \times 10^{7} s / yr } \\&=3.0 yr\end{aligned}\)
