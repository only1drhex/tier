Guest Question.

Figure 3.32 shows the oneline diagram of a three-phase power system. By selecting a common base of 100 MVA and 22 kV on the generator side, draw an impedance diagram showing all impedances including the load impedance in per-unit. The data are given as follows:

\(\begin{array}{llll}G : & 90 MVA & 22 kV & x =0.18 \text { per unit } \\T_{1}: & 50 MVA & 22 / 220 kV & x =0.10 \text { per unit } \\T_{2}: & 40 MVA & 220 / 11 kV & x =0.06 \text { per unit } \\T_{3}: & 40 MVA & 22 / 110 kV & x =0.064 \text { per unit } \\T_{4}: & 40 MVA & 110 / 11 kV & x =0.08 \text { per unit } \\M : & 66.5 MVA & 10.45 kV & x =0.185 \text { per unit }\end{array}\)

Lines 1 and 2 have series reactances of 48.4 and 65.43 Ω, respectively. At bus 4, the three-phase load absorbs 57 MVA at 10.45 kV and 0.6 power factor lagging.

Admin
Solution

Select a common base of 100MVA and 22kV on the generator side; Base voltage at bus 1 is 22kV; this fixes the voltage bases for the remaining buses in accordance with the transformer turns ratios. Using Eq. 3.3.11, per-unit reactances on the selected base are given by

\(\begin{aligned}&G: X=0.18\left(\frac{100}{90}\right)=0.2 ; T_{1}: X=0.1\left(\frac{100}{50}\right)=0.2 \\&T_{2}: X=0.06\left(\frac{100}{40}\right)=0.15 ; T_{2}: X=0.06\left(\frac{100}{40}\right)=0.15 \\&T_{3}: X=0.064\left(\frac{100}{40}\right)=0.16 ; T_{4}: X=0.08\left(\frac{100}{40}\right)=0.2 \\&M: X=0.185\left(\frac{100}{66.5}\right)\left(\frac{10.45}{11}\right)^{2}=0.25\end{aligned}\)

For Line 1, \(Z_{B A S E}=\frac{(220)^{2}}{100}=484 \Omega \text { and } X=\frac{48.4}{484}=0.1\)

For Line 2, \(Z_{B A S E}=\frac{(110)^{2}}{100}=121 \Omega \text { and } X=\frac{65.43}{121}=0.54\)

The load complex power at 0.6 Lagging pf is \(\bar{S}_{L(3 \phi)}=57 \angle 53.13^{\circ} MVA\)

∴ The load impedance in OHMS is

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{Z}_{L}=& \frac{(10.45)^{2}}{57 \angle-53.13^{\circ}}=\frac{V_{L L}^{2}}{\bar{S}_{L(3 \phi)}^{*}} \\&=1.1495+j 1.53267 \Omega\end{aligned}\)

The base impedance for the load is \((11)^{2} / 100=1.21 \Omega\)

∴ Load Impedance in pu \(=\frac{1.1495+j 1.53267}{1.21}=0.95+j 1.2667\)
