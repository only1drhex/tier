Guest Question.

An estuary to a river empties to the ocean.The estuary can be approximated as a very wide channel with a depth of 7 m at its outlet, and with \(S_{0}=0.0001, n=0.015\). If the discharge into the ocean from the estuary is \(q=1.5 m ^{3} / s\) per meter width, determine the distances upstream from the ocean where the depth is equal to 6, 5, 4, and 2 m.

Admin
Solution

Use the varied flow function to compute the water surface profile.

 

\(y_{0}=\left(\frac{q^{2} n^{2}}{S_{0}}\right)^{3 / 10}=\left(\frac{1.5^{2} \times 0.015^{2}}{0.0001}\right)=1.627\) m,

 

\(y_{c}=\left(\frac{q^{2}}{g}\right)^{1 / 3}=\left(\frac{1.5^{2}}{9.81}\right)=0.612\)m

 

Therefore the estuary has a mild slope and an \(M _{1}\) curve exists upstream of the outlet. For a wide rectangular channel (), J = 2.5, N = 3.33, M = 3, and N/J = 1.33. Use Eq. 10.7.13 to compute x:

 

\(x=\frac{y_{0}}{S_{0}}\left[u-F(u, N)+\left(\frac{y_{c}}{y_{0}}\right)^{M} \frac{J}{N} F(v, J)\right]\) (10.7.13)

 

\(\begin{aligned}x &=\frac{1.627}{0.0001}\left[u-F(u, 3.33)+\left(\frac{0.612}{1.627}\right)^{3} \frac{2.5}{3.33} F(v, 2.5)\right] \\&=16270[u-F(u, 3.33)-0.04 F(v, 2.5)]\end{aligned}\)
