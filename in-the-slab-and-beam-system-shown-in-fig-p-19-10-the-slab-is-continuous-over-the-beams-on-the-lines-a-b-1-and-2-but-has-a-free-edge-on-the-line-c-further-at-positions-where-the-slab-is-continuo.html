Guest Question.

In the slab and beam system shown in Fig. P.19.10, the slab is continuous over the beams on the lines A, B, 1, and 2 but has a free edge on the line C. Further, at positions where the slab is continuous, it has a hogging moment of resistance per unit width of 1.5M. If the sagging moments of resistance per unit width are isotropic and of value M in panel I, calculate the maximum uniformly distributed load that panel I can carry. Also, calculate, in terms of M, the isotropic sagging moment the isotropic moments of resistance that panel II must have so that the collapse load intensity is the same as that for panel I.
Ans. \(59.12 M / L^{2}, 0.964 M\).

Admin
Solution

Consider Panel I shown in Fig. S.19.10(a).

Suppose that the collapse load intensity is n. The work absorbed in the yield lines is

Work (yield lines) \(=2(1.5 M+M) 0.5 L(1 / x L)+(1.5 M L+M 2 L)(1 / 0.5 L)\)

i.e.

Work (yield lines) \(=(3+4 x+2.5 / x) M\) (i)

The work done by the collapse load is
Work (collapse load) \(=n[0.5 L \times 2 x L / 3+0.5 L(L-2 x L) / 2]\)

i.e.

Work (collapse load) \(=n L^{2}(x / 3+1 / 4-x / 2)\) (ii)

Equating Eqs (i) and (ii) and simplifying

\(\frac{n L^{2}}{M}=\frac{3+4 x+2.5 / x}{1 / 4-x / 6}=\frac{12\left(3 x+4 x^{2}+2.5\right)}{3 x-2 x^{2}}\) (iii)

For the minimum collapse load dn/dx = 0. Therefore, differentiating Eq. (iii), equating to zero and simplifying

\(\frac{3 x+4 x^{2}+2.5}{3 x-2 x^{2}}=\frac{3+8 x}{3-4 x}\)

Simplifying gives a quadratic equation in x, i.e.

\(18 x^{2}+10 x-7.5=0\)

the solution of which is x = 0.425. Substituting this value of x in Eq. (iii) gives

\(n=59.12 M / L^{2}\)

For Panel II, shown in Fig. S.19.10 (b), suppose the sagging moment is m′.

Then

\(4\left(1.5 M+m^{\prime}\right) L \times 2 / L=n L^{2} / 3\)

Substituting the value of n gives \(m^{\prime}=0.964 M\).
