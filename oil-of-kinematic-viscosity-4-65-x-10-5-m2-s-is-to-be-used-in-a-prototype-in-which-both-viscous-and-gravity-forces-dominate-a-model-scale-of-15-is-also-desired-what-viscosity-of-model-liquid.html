Guest Question.

Oil of kinematic viscosity 4.65 x 10-5 m2/s is to be used in a prototype in which both viscous and gravity forces dominate. A model scale of 1:5 is also desired. What viscosity of model liquid is necessary to make both the Froude number and the Reynolds number the same in model and prototype?

Admin
Solution

Using the scale ratios for velocity for the Froude and Reynolds model laws (see and ), we equate

\((L_{r}g_{r})^{1/2}=\nu _{r}/L_{r} \)

Since \(g_{r}=1,L^{3/2}_{r}=\nu _{r} \) and \(\nu _{r}=(1/5)^{3/2}=0.0894 \)

This means that \(\frac{\nu _{m}}{\nu _{p}} =0.0894=\frac{\nu _{m}}{4.65\times 10^{-5} } \) , and thus \(\nu _{m}=4.16\times 10^{-6}m^2/s\) .

Using the time, acceleration, and discharge scale ratios will produce the same results. For example, equating the time ratios ( and ) yields

\(\frac{L^{1/2}_{r}}{g^{1/2}_{r}} =\frac{\rho _{r}L^2_{r}}{\mu _{r}} \) or, since \(g_{r}=1 \) , \(\frac{\mu _{r}}{\rho _{r}}=\nu _{r}=L^{3/2}_{r} \) , as before.
