Guest Question.

a. Find the electronic contribution to the heat capacity of copper at 15 K, using the same assumptions and data as in .

b. Find the ratio of the electronic contribution to the classical prediction of the electronic contribution.

c. Find the ratio of the electronic contribution to the lattice vibration contribution for copper at 15 K, using the Debye theory result with the Debye temperature 315 K.

Admin
Solution

a. From , \( ε_{F0}= 1.129 × 10^{−18} J. \)

 

\( ε_{F} = ε_{F0} \biggl(1 − \frac{(πk_{B}T )^{2}}{12ε_{F0}^{2}}\biggr) \)

 

\( = 1.129 × 10^{−18}J \biggr(1 − \frac{[π(1.38 × 10^{−23} JK^{−1})(15 K)]^{2}}{12(1.129 × 10^{−18} J)^{2}} \biggr)\)

 

\( = 1.129 × 10^{−18} J\)

This value is not significantly different from \(ε_{F0}. \) From Equation \((28.3-15): C_{el}≈\Bigl( \frac{∂U}{∂T}\Bigr)_{V}= \frac{\mathscr{N} π^{2}k^{2}_{B}T }{2ε_{F}} \) the electronic contribution to the heat capacity per unit volume is

\( C_{elec}= \frac{π^{2} \mathscr{N} k^{2}_{B} T }{2ε_{F}}= \frac{π^{2} (8.49 × 10^{28} m^{−3})(1.38 × 10^{−23} JK^{−1})^{2}(15K)}{2(1.129 × 10^{−18} J)} \)

 

\( = 1.06 × 10^{3} JK^{−1} m^{−3}\)

 

\( C_{m,elec}= \frac{(1.06 × 10^{3} JK^{−1} m^{−3})(0.063546 kg mol^{−1})}{8960 kgm^{−3}}= 0.00753 JK^{−1} mol^{−1}\)

 

b. Ratio\(=\frac{π^{2}k_{B}T}{3ε_{F}}= \frac{π^{2} (1.38 × 10^{−23} JK^{−1})(15 K)}{3(1.129 × 10^{−18} J)}= 0.0060\)

 

c. At 15 K,

\( C_{Debye}=\frac{9Nk_{B}}{n^{3}_{D}} \int^{ν_{D}}_{0} \Bigl(\frac{hν}{ k_{B}T} \Bigr)^{2} \frac{e^{hν/k_{B}T} ν^{2}}{\bigl(e^{hν/kBT} – 1 \bigr)^{2}} dν = 3Nk_{B}D \Bigl(\frac{θ_{D}}{T} \Bigl) \)

 

\( u_{D}= \frac{θ_{D}}{T} = \frac{315K}{15K} = 21.0\)

The integral must be evaluated numerically or by use of tabulated values. \( ^{a}\) The value of the Debye function for 15K is

\( D(21.0) = \frac{3}{u^{3}_{D}} \int^{21.0}_{0} u^{4} \frac{e^{u}}{(e^{u} − 1)^{2}} du = \frac{3}{(21.0)^{3}} (26.0) = 0.0084\)

 

\( C_{V,m,Debye}= 3RD = 3(8.3145 JK^{−1} mol^{−1})(0.0084) = 0.21JK^{−1} mol^{−1}\)

 

The ratio of the electronic contribution to the vibrational contribution is

\( Ratio =\frac{0.00753 JK^{−1} mol^{−1}}{0.21JK^{−1} mol^{−1}} =0.036\)

 

 

 

 

\(^{a} \) K. S. Pitzer and L. Brewer, Thermodynamics, McGraw-Hill, New York, 1961, p. 660.
