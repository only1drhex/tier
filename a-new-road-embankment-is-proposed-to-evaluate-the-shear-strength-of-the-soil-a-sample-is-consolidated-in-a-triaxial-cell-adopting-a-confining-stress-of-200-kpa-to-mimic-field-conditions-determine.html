Guest Question.

A new road embankment is proposed. To evaluate the shear strength of the soil, a sample is consolidated in a triaxial cell, adopting a confining stress of \(200 \mathrm{kPa}\) to mimic field conditions. Determine the mean effective stress, the effective major principal stress and the void ratio at failure when shearing is carried out under drained conditions simulating long-term analysis. The critical state soil parameters for this soil determined in an additional set of tests are \(\Gamma=2.2, \lambda=0.1, M=0.77\) and \(N=2.5\).

Admin
Solution

For drained conditions, the specific volume at the end of consolidation can be found by

\(v_{0}=N-\lambda \ln p^{\prime} \text { or }\)

 

\(v_{0}=2.5-0.1 \ln 200=1.97\)

and

\(\begin{aligned}&v_{0}=1+e_{0} \\ &e_{0}=0.97 \end{aligned}\)

Since the confining stress is constant, the stress path will have a slope of \(3: 1\) until it reaches the critical state line \(\left(q=0.77 p^{\prime}\right)\). Then,

\(\begin{aligned}&q_{f}=M p^{\prime} \\ &q_{f}=0.77 p_{f}^{\prime} \\ &p_{f}^{\prime}=269.06 \mathrm{kPa} \\ &\text { and } q_{f}=0.77 p_{f}^{\prime}=207.18 \mathrm{kPa}\end{aligned}\)

If \(q=\sigma_{1}^{\prime}-\sigma_{3}^{\prime}\) and \(p^{\prime}=\frac{\sigma_{1}^{\prime}+2 \sigma_{3}^{\prime}}{3}\), then

\(\begin{aligned}&p_{f}^{\prime}=\frac{\left(\sigma_{1 f}^{\prime}+2 \sigma_{3 f}^{\prime}\right)}{3}=269.06 \mathrm{kPa} \\ &q_{f}^{\prime}=\left(\sigma_{1 f}^{\prime}-\sigma_{3 f}^{\prime}\right)=207.18 \mathrm{kPa} \end{aligned}\)

Then, solving for \(\sigma_{1 f}^{\prime}\), we can obtain

\(\sigma_{1 f}^{\prime}=407.17 \mathrm{kPa}\)

To determine the void ratio at failure, the relationship that describes the CSL can be used as follows:

\(\begin{aligned}&v=\Gamma-\lambda \ln p^{\prime} \\ &v=2.2-0.1 \ln 270.3 \\ &v=1.64 \\ &e=0.64\end{aligned}\)
