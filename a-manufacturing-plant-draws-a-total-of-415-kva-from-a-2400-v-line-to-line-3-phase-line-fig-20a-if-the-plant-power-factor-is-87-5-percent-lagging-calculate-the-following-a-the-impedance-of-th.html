Guest Question.

A manufacturing plant draws a total of 415 kVA from a 2400 V (line-to-line), 3-phase line (Fig. 20a). If the plant power factor is 87.5 percent lagging,

Calculate the following:

a. The impedance of the plant, per phase
b. The phase angle between the line-to-neutral voltage and the line current
c. The complete phasor diagram for the plant

Admin
Solution

a. We assume a wye connection composed of three identical impedances Z(Fig. 20b). The voltage per branch is

 

\(\begin{aligned}E &=2400 / \sqrt{3} \\&=1386 V\end{aligned}\)

 

The current per branch is

 

\(\begin{aligned}I &=S /(E \sqrt{3}) \\&=415000 /(2400 \sqrt{3}) \\&=100 A\end{aligned}\)

 

The impedance per branch is

 

\(\begin{aligned}Z &=E / I=1386 / 100 \\&=13.9 \Omega\end{aligned}\)

 

b. The phase angle θ between the line-to-neutral voltage (1386 V) and the corresponding line current (100 A) is given by

 

\(\begin{aligned}\cos \theta &=\text { power factor }=0.875 \\\theta &=29^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

 

The current in each phase lags 29° behind the line-to-neutral voltage.

c. The complete phasor diagram is shown in Fig. 20c. In practice, we would show only one phase; for example, \(E_{ an }, I_{ a }\), and the phase angle between them.
