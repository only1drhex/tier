Energy—Pump Power

GIVEN A pump delivers water at a steady rate of 300 gal/min as shown in Fig. E5.20. Just upstream of the pump [section (1)] where the pipe diameter is 3.5 in., the pressure is 18 psi. Just downstream of the pump [section (2)] where the pipe diameter is 1 in., the pressure is 60 psi. The change in water elevation across the pump is zero. The rise in internal energy of water, \(\check{u}_{2}-\check{u}_{1}, \text { as- }\)sociated with a temperature rise across the pump is 93 ft lb/lbm. The pumping process is considered to be adiabatic.

FIND Determine the power (hp) required by the pump.

Admin
Solution

We include in our control volume the water contained in the pump between its entrance and exit sections. Application of Eq. 5.67 to the contents of this control volume on a time-average basis yields

 

\(\dot{m}\left[\check{u}_{\text {out }}-\check{u}_{\text {in }}+\left(\frac{p}{\rho}\right)_{\text {out }}-\left(\frac{p}{\rho}\right)_{\text {in }}+\frac{V_{\text {out }}^{2}-V_{\text {in }}^{2}}{2}+g\left(z_{\text {out }}-z_{\text {in }}\right)\right]= \dot{Q}_{\text {net in }}+\dot{W}_{\text{shaft net in}}\) (5.67)

 

\(\dot{m}\left[\check{u}_{2}-\check{u}_{1}+\left(\frac{p}{\rho}\right)_{2}-\left(\frac{p}{\rho}\right)_{1}+\frac{V_{2}^{2}-V_{1}^{2}}{2}+ \cancel{g\left(z_{2}-z_{1}\right)}^{0(\text{no elevation change})}\right]=\cancel{\dot{Q}_{\text {net in }}}^{0(\text{adiabatic flow})} +\dot{W}_{\text {shaft net in }}\) (1)

 

We can solve directly for the power required by the pump, \(W_{\text {shaft net in }}\) from Eq. 1, after we first determine the mass flowrate, , the speed of flow into the pump, \(V_1\), and the speed of the flow out of the pump, \(V_2\). All other quantities in Eq. 1 are given in the problem statement. From Eq. 5.6, we get

 

\(\dot{m}=\rho Q=\rho A V\) (5.6)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{m} &=\rho Q=\frac{\left(1.94 \text { slugs } / ft ^{3}\right)(300 gal / min )(32.174 lbm / slug )}{\left(7.48 gal / ft ^{3}\right)(60 s / min )} \\&=41.8 lbm / s\end{aligned}\) (2)

 

Also from Eq. 5.6,

 

\(V=\frac{Q}{A}=\frac{Q}{\pi D^{2} / 4}\)

 

so

 

\(\begin{aligned}V_{1} &=\frac{Q}{A_{1}}=\frac{(300 gal / min ) 4(12 in . / ft )^{2}}{\left(7.48 gal / ft ^{3}\right)(60 s / min ) \pi(3.5 in .)^{2}} \\&=10.0 ft / s\end{aligned}\) (3)

 

and

 

\(\begin{aligned}V_{2} &=\frac{Q}{A_{2}}=\frac{(300 gal / min ) 4(12 in . / ft )^{2}}{\left(7.48 gal / ft ^{3}\right)(60 s / min ) \pi(1 \text { in. })^{2}} \\&=123 ft / s\end{aligned}\) (4)

 

Substituting the values of Eqs. 2, 3, and 4 and values from the problem statement into Eq. 1 we obtain

 

.\(\underset{\substack{\text { shaft } \\ \text { net in }}}{ }=(41.8 \mathrm{lbm} / \mathrm{s})[(93 \mathrm{ft} \cdot 1 \mathrm{~b} / \mathrm{lbm})\)

\(+\frac{(60 \mathrm{psi})\left(144 \mathrm{in}^{2} / \mathrm{ft}^{2}\right)}{\left(1.94 \text { slugs/ } \mathrm{ft}^{3}\right)(32.174 \mathrm{lbm} / \mathrm{slug})}\)

 

\(-\frac{(18 \mathrm{psi})\left(144 \mathrm{in}^{2} / \mathrm{ft}^{2}\right)}{\left(1.94 \text { slugs/ } \mathrm{ft}^{3}\right)(32.174 \mathrm{lbm} / \mathrm{slug})}\)

 

\(\left.+\frac{(123 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s})^{2}-(10.0 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s})^{2}}{2\left[32.174(\mathrm{lbm} \cdot \mathrm{ft}) /\left(1 \mathrm{lb} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{2}\right)\right]}\right]\).

\(\times \frac{1}{[550(\mathrm{ft} \cdot \mathrm{lb} / \mathrm{s}) / \mathrm{hp}]}=32.2 \mathrm{hp}\)

 

COMMENT Of the total 32.2 hp, internal energy change accounts for 7.09 hp, the pressure rise accounts for 7.37 hp, and the kinetic energy increase accounts for 17.8 hp.
