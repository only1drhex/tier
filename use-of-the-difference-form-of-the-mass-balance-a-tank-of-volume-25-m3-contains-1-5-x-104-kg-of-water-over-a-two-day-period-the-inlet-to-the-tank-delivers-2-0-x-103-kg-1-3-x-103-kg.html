Use of the Difference Form of the Mass Balance

A tank of volume \(25 m ^{3} \text { contains } 1.5 \times 10^{4}\) kg of water. Over a two-day period the inlet to the tank delivers \(2.0 \times 10^{3} kg , 1.3 \times 10^{3}\) kg leaves the tank through the outlet port, and 50 kg of

Table 2.2-1 The Mass Conservation Equation

Mass Basis Molar Basis
Rate-of-change form of the mass balance
General equation \(\frac{d M}{d t}=\sum_{k=1}^{K} \dot{M}_{k}\) \(\frac{d N}{d t}=\sum_{k=1}^{K} \dot{N}_{k}\)
Special case: \(\frac{d M}{d t}=0\) \(\frac{d N}{d t}=0\)
Closed system M = constant N = constant
Difference form of the mass balance*
General equation \(M_{2}-M_{1}=\sum_{k=1}^{K} \Delta M_{k}\) \(N_{2}-N_{1}=\sum_{k=1}^{K} \Delta N_{k}\)
Special cases:
Closed system		 \(M_{2}=M_{1}\) \(N_{2}=N_{1}\)
Steady flow \(M_{2}-M_{1}=\sum_{k=1}^{K} \dot{M}_{k} \Delta t\) \(N_{2}-N_{1}=\sum_{k=1}^{K} \dot{N}_{k} \Delta t\)
Admin
Solution

Since we are interested only in the change in the mass of water in the tank over the two-day period, and not in the rate of change, we will use the difference form of the mass balance over the period from the initial time (which we take to be t = 0) until two days later (t = 2 days). We use Eq. 2.2-4, recognizing that we have three flow terms: \(M_{1} \text { (flow into the tank) }=+2.0 \times 10^{3}\) kg, \(M_{2} \text { (flow from the tank) }=-1.3 \times 10^{3}\) (evaporation) = –50 kg. (Remember, in our notation the + sign is for flow into the system, the tank, and the – sign is for flow out of the system.)

\(M\left(t_{2}\right)-M\left(t_{1}\right)=\sum_{k=1}^{K} \Delta M_{k}\) (2.2-4)

Therefore,

\(\begin{aligned}M(t=2 \text { days })-M(t=0) &=M_{1}+M_{2}+M_{3} \\M(t=2 \text { days })-1.5 \times 10^{4} kg &=2.0 \times 10^{3}-1.3 \times 10^{3}-50 \\M(t=2 \text { days }) &=1.5 \times 10^{4}+2.0 \times 10^{3}-1.3 \times 10^{3}-50 \\&=1.565 \times 10^{4} kg\end{aligned}\)
