find that area under the curve between z = 0 and z = +1.90 is.4713. Therefore, the area (probability) of values within -1.90 to + 1.90 is 2(.4713) = .9426, and the area beyond these values is I – .9426 =.0574. Hence, the alpha risk is 5.74 percent.

b. The alpha risk (Type I error probability) is always specified as an area in the tail (s) of a distribution. With control charts , you use two-sided control limits. Consequently , half of the risk lies in each tail i 1 percent, or.0100. This means that .4900 should be the area under the curve between z =0 and the value of z you are looking for. The closest value is . 4901 for z =2.33. Thus, control limits based on z = ±2.33 provide an alpha risk of about 2 percent.
