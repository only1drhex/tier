Guest Question.

Let

\( E_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -4 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] , E_{2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] , E_{3} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 \end{matrix} \right ] , \)

\( A = \left [ \begin{matrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \\ g & h & i \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Compute \( E_{1}A \), \( E_{2}A \), and \( E_{3}A \), and describe how these products can be obtained by elementary row operations on A.

Admin
Solution

Verify that

\( E_{1}A = \left [ \begin{matrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \\ g – 4a & h – 4b & i – 4c \end{matrix} \right ] , E_{2}A = \left [ \begin{matrix} d & e & f \\ a & b & c \\ g & h & i \end{matrix} \right ] , \)

\( E_{3}A = \left [ \begin{matrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \\ 5g & 5h & 5i \end{matrix} \right ] \) .

Addition of -4 times row 1 of A to row 3 produces \( E_{1}A \). (This is a row replacement operation.) An interchange of rows 1 and 2 of A produces \( E_{2}A \), and multiplication of row 3 of A by 5 produces \( E_{3}A \).
