Guest Question.

A mild steel pin-ended column is 2.5 mlong and has the cross section shown in Fig. P.21.14. If the yield stress in compression of mild steel is \(300 N / mm ^{2}\), determine the maximum load the column can withstand using the Robertson formula. Compare this value with that predicted by the Euler theory.
Ans. 576 kN, P (Robertson)/P (Euler) = 0.62.

Admin
Solution

The column will buckle about an axis parallel to its web. The second moment of area of the column is then given by

\(I=\frac{2 \times 8 \times 130^{3}}{12}+\frac{184 \times 6^{3}}{12}=2.93 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\)

The area of cross section is

\(A=2 \times 130 \times 8+184 \times 6=3184 mm ^{2}\)

Then

\(r^{2}=2.93 \times \frac{10^{6}}{3184}=920.2\)

so that

r = 30.3 mm

Then

\(\frac{L}{r}=\frac{2.5 \times 10^{3}}{30.3}=82.5\)

Also

\(\sigma_{ CR }=\frac{\pi^{2} \times 200000}{82.5^{2}}\) (see Eq. (21.25))

\(\sigma_{ CR }=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(L_{ e } / r\right)^{2}}\) (21.25)

i.e.

\(\sigma_{ CR }=290.0 N / mm ^{2}\)

Substituting these values etc. in Eq. (21.46)

\(\sigma=\frac{1}{2}\left[\sigma_{ Y }+\left(1+0.003 \frac{L}{r}\right) \sigma_{ CR }\right]-\sqrt{\frac{1}{4}\left[\sigma_{ Y }+\left(1+0.003 \frac{L}{r}\right) \sigma_{ CR }\right]^{2}-\sigma_{ Y } \sigma_{ CR }}\) (21.46)

gives

\(\sigma=180.9 N / mm ^{2}\)

Then the maximum load the column can withstand is given by

\(P=180.9 \times 3184 \times 10^{-3}=576 kN\)

The Euler buckling load is

\(P_{ CR }=290 \times 3184 \times 10^{-3}=923.4 kN\)

Then

\(\frac{P}{P_{ CR }}=\frac{576}{923.4}=0.62\).
