MASS FLOW IN TRUNCATED NOZZLE

Air exhausts through a truncated nozzle 3 cm in diameter from a reservoir at a pressure of 160 kPa and a temperature of 80°C. Calculate the mass flow rate if the back pressure is 100 kPa.

Problem Definition

Situation: Air flows through 3 cm diameter truncated nozzle.

Find: Mass flow rate (in kg/s) through nozzle.

Properties: From Table A.2, \(k_{\text {air }}=1.4\).

Sketch:

Plan

1. Determine exit condition by comparing exit pressure with back pressure. If \(p_{b} / p_{t}<p_{*} / p_{t}\), exit flow is sonic. If \(p_{b} / p_{t}>p_{*} / p_{t}\), exit flow is subsonic.

2. Calculate mass flow according to exit condition.

Admin
Solution

1. Ratio of exit pressure to total pressure

 

\(p_{b} / p_{t}=100 / 160=0.625\)

 

Because 0.625 is larger than the critical pressure ratio for
air (0.528), the flow at the nozzle exit must be subsonic.

 

2. Mach number at exit. From total pressure equation, Eq. (12.26),

 

\(p_{t}=p\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} M ^{2}\right)^{k /(k-1)}\) (12.26)

 

\(M _{e}^{2}=\frac{2}{k-1}\left[\left(\frac{p_{t}}{p_{b}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}-1\right]\)

 

\(M _{e}=0.85\)

 

Static temperature at exit. From total temperature equation, Eq. (12.22),

 

\(T_{t}=T\left(1+\frac{k-1}{2} M ^{2}\right)\) (12.22)

 

\(T_{e}=\frac{T_{t}}{\left\{1+[(k-1) / 2] M _{e}^{2}\right\}}=308\) K

 

Static density at exit. From ideal gas law,

 

\(\rho_{e}=\frac{p_{b}}{R T_{e}}=\frac{100 \times 10^{3} N / m ^{2}}{(287 J / kg K )(309 K )}=1.13 kg / m ^{3}\)

 

Speed of sound at the exit from speed-of-sound equation, Eq. (12.11),

 

\(c=\sqrt{k R T}\) (12.11)

 

\(c_{e}=\sqrt{k R T_{e}}=[(1.4)(287 J / kg K )(309 K )]^{1 / 2}\)

 

= 352 m ⁄ s

 

Mass flow rate.

 

\(\dot{m}=\rho_{e} A_{e} M_{e} c_{e}\)

 

\(\dot{m}=\left(1.13 kg / m ^{3}\right)(\pi / 4)\left(0.03^{2} m ^{2}\right)(0.85)(352 m / s )\)

 

= 0.239 kg ⁄ s

 

Review

Had \(p_{b} / p_{t}\) been less than 0.528, then Eq. (12.56) would have been used to calculate the mass flow rate.

 

\(\dot{m}=\frac{p_{t} A_{*}}{\sqrt{R T_{t}}} k^{1 / 2}\left(\frac{2}{k+1}\right)^{(k+1) / 2(k-1)}\) (12.56)
