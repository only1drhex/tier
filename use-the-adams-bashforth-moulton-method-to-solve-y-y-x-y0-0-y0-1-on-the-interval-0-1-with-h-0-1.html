Guest Question.

Use the Adams-Bashforth-Moulton method to solve y” – y = x; y(0) = 0, y'(0) = 1 on the interval [0, 1] with h = 0.1.

Admin
Solution

It follows from that \(f(x, y, z)=z \text { and } g(x, y, z)=y+x\) and from Table 20-3 that

\(\begin{array}{lll}x_{0}=0 & y_{0}=0 & z_{0}=1 \\x_{1}=0.1 & y_{1}=0.1003333 & z_{1}=1.0100083 \\x_{2}=0.2 & y_{2}=0.2026717 & z_{2}=1.0401335 \\x_{3}=0.3 & y_{3}=0.3090401 & z_{3}=1.0906769\end{array}\)

Using (20.6), we compute

\(\begin{aligned}&y_{0}^{\prime}=z_{0}=1 \quad y_{1}^{\prime}=z_{1}=1.0100083 \\&y_{2}^{\prime}=z_{2}=1.0401335 \quad y_{3}^{\prime}=z_{3}=1.0906769 \\&z_{0}^{\prime}=y_{0}+x_{0}=0+0=0 \\&z_{1}^{\prime}=y_{1}+x_{1}=0.1003333+0.1=0.2003333 \\&z_{2}^{\prime}=y_{2}+x_{2}=0.2026717+0.2=0.4026717 \\&z_{3}^{\prime}=y_{3}+x_{3}=0.3090401+0.3=0.6090401\end{aligned}\)

Then using (20.5), we compute

\(\begin{aligned}n=3: \quad x_{4} &=0.4 \\p y_{4} &=y_{3}+\frac{h}{24}\left(55 y_{3}^{\prime}-59 y_{2}^{\prime}+37 y_{1}^{\prime}-9 y_{0}^{\prime}\right) \\&=0.3090401+(0.1 / 24)[55(1.0906769)-59(1.0401335)+37(1.0100083)-9(1)] \\&=0.4214970 \\p z_{4} &=z_{3}+\frac{h}{24}\left(55 z_{3}^{\prime}-59 z_{2}^{\prime}+37 z_{1}^{\prime}-9 z_{0}^{\prime}\right) \\&=1.0906769+(0.1 / 24)[55(0.6090401)-59(0.4026717)+37(0.2003333)-9(0)] \\&=1.1621432 \\p y_{4}^{\prime} &=p z_{4}=1.1621432 \\p z_{4}^{\prime} &=p y_{4}+x_{4}=0.4214970+0.4=0.8214970 \\y_{4} &=y_{3}+\frac{h}{24}\left(9 p y_{4}^{\prime}+19 y_{3}^{\prime}-5 y_{2}^{\prime}+y_{1}^{\prime}\right) \\&=0.3090401+(0.1 / 24)[9(1.1621432)+19(1.0906769)-5(1.0401335)+1.0100083] \\&=0.4215046\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}z_{4} &=z_{3}+\frac{h}{24}\left(9 p z_{4}^{\prime}+19 z_{3}^{\prime}-5 z_{2}^{\prime}-z_{1}^{\prime}\right) \\&=1.0906769+(0.1 / 24)[9(0.8214970)+19(0.6090401)-5(0.4026717)+(0.2003333)] \\&=1.1621445 \\y_{4}^{\prime} &=z_{4}=1.1621445 \\z_{4}^{\prime} &=y_{4}+x_{4}=0.4215046+0.4=0.8215046\end{aligned}\)
