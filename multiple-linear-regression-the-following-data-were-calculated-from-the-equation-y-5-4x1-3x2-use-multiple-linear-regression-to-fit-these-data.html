Multiple Linear Regression

The following data were calculated from the equation \(y = 5 + 4x_1 - 3x_2\):

\(x_1\) \(x_2\) y
0 0 5
2 1 10
2.5 2 9
1 3 0
4 6 3
7 2 27
Admin
Solution

The summations required to develop Eq. (17.22)

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} n & \sum{x_{1i}} &\sum{ x_{2i}} \\ \sum{x_{1i}} & \sum{x^2_{1i}} & \sum{x_{1i}x_{2i}} \\ \sum{ x_{2i}} & \sum{x_{1i}x_{2i}} &\sum{ x^2_{2i}}\end{matrix} \right ] = \left \{ \begin{matrix} a_{0} \\ a_{1} \\ a_{2} \end{matrix} \right \}= \left \{ \begin{matrix} \sum{y_{i}} \\ \sum{x_{i}y_i} \\ \sum{x_{2i}y_i} \end{matrix} \right \}\) (17.22)

are computed in Table 17.5.

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 6 &16.5 &14 \\ 16.5 & 76.25 & 48 \\ 14 & 48 &54\end{matrix} \right ] = \left \{ \begin{matrix} a_{0} \\ a_{1} \\ a_{2} \end{matrix} \right \}= \left \{ \begin{matrix} 54 \\ 243.5 \\100 \end{matrix} \right \} \)

which can be solved using a method such as Gauss elimination for

\(a_0 = 5 a_1 = 4 a_2 = 23 \)

which is consistent with the original equation from which these data were derived.
