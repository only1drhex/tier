Guest Question.

A 240-V compound (cumulative) dc motor has the following open-circuit magnetization characteristic at normal full-load speed of 850 rev/min:

Excitation, AT/pole

 1200 2400 3600 4800 6000
Generated emf, V; 76 135 180 215

240
Admin
Solution

At full load, from Fig. 7.37

\( E_{a} (full-load)\equiv V-I_{a}(R_{a}+R_{se}) 240-25=215V\)

 

Corresponding \( AT_{net} \) from magnetizing of Fig. 7.38,

\( AT_{net} (full-load) = 4800\)

Then \( AT_{sh} = AT_{se} (full-load) = 2400\)

Now \(I_{a} (no-load) = \frac{3}{25} I_{a} (full-load); I_{a} (no-load) = I_{f} (shunt field)\)

 

\( AT_{se} (no-load) = \frac{3}{25} \times 2400 = 288\)

 

\( AT_{sh} = 2400 (no change)\)

 

\( AT_{net} (no-load) = 2400 + 288 = 2688\)

 

\( E_{a} (from the magnetizing curve) = 148 V\) at 850 rpm, at \( AT_{net} (no-load)\)

 

\( E_{a} (no-load) = 240 â€“ 3 = 237 V\)

 

\( E_{a}\propto n\) at given \( AT_{net}\)

 

n(no-load) = 850 237 148 = 1361 rpm

It will be seen that in a cumulatively compound dc motor, full-load speed is much less than no-load speed.
