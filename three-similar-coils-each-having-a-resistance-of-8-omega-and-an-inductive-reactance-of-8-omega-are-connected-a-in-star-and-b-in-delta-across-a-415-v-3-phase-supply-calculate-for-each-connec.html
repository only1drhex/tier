Guest Question.

Three similar coils, each having a resistance of 8 \(\Omega\) and an inductive reactance of 8 \(\Omega\) are connected (a) in star and (b) in delta, across a 415 V, 3-phase supply. Calculate for each connection the readings on each of two wattmeters connected to measure the power by the two-wattmeter method.

Admin
Solution

(a) \(\textbf{Star connection: } V_{L} = \sqrt{3} V_{p}\) and \(I_{L} = I_{p}\).

Phase voltage, \(V_{p} = \frac{V_{L}}{\sqrt{3}}=\frac{415}{\sqrt{3}}\)

and phase impedance,

\(Z_{p} =\sqrt{R_{P}^{2}+X_{L}^{2}}=\sqrt{8^{2}+8^{2}}=11.31\, \Omega\)

Hence phase current,

\(I_{p} = \frac{V_{p}}{Z_{P}}=\frac{\frac{415}{\sqrt{3}}}{11.31}=21.18\, A\)

Total power,

\(P = 3I_{P}^{2}R_{P}=3(21.18)^{2}(8) = 10\, 766\, W\)

If wattmeter readings are \(P_{1}\) and \(P_{2}\) then:

\(P_{1} + P_{2} = 10\, 766\) (1)

Since \(R_{p} = 8\, \Omega\) and \(X_{L} = 8\, \Omega\), then phase angle \(\phi = 45^{\circ}\) (from impedance triangle).

\(\tan\phi = \sqrt{3}\, (\frac{P_{1}\, -\, P_{2}}{P_{1}+ P_{2}})\)

hence \(\tan 45^{\circ} =\frac{\sqrt{3}(P_{1}\, -\, P_{2})}{10\, 766}\)

from which

\(P_{1} \, -\, P_{2} = \frac{(10\, 766)(1)}{\sqrt{3}}=6216\, W\) (2)

Adding Equations (1) and (2) gives:

\(2P_{1} = 10\, 766 + 6216 = 16\, 982 W\)

Hence \(P_{1} = 8491\, W\)

From Equation (1), \(P_{2} = 10\, 766 \, -\, 8491 =2275\, W\).

\(\textbf{When the coils are star-connected the wattmeter readings are thus 8.491 kW and 2.275 kW}\)

(b) \(\textbf{Delta connection: } V_{L} = V_{p}\) and \(I_{L} = \sqrt{3} I_{p}\).

Phase current, \(I_{p} = \frac{V_{p}}{Z_{P}}=\frac{415}{11.31}=36.69\, A\)

Total power,

\(P = 3I_{P}^{2}R_{P}=3(36.69)^{2}(8) = 32\, 310\, W\)

Hence \(P_{1} + P_{2} = 32\, 310\, W\) (3)

\(\tan\phi = \sqrt{3}\, (\frac{P_{1}\, -\, P_{2}}{P_{1}+ P_{2}})\) thus \(1=\frac{\sqrt{3}(P_{1}\, -\, P_{2})}{32\, 310}\)

from which,

\(P_{1} \, -\, P_{2} = \frac{32\, 310}{\sqrt{3}}=18\, 650\, W\) (4)

Adding Equations (3) and (4) gives:

\(2P_{1} = 50\, 960\) from which \(P_{1} = 25\, 480\, W\).

From Equation (3), \(P_{2} = 32\, 310 \, -\, 25\, 480 = 6830\, W\)

\(\textbf{When the coils are delta-connected the wattmeter readings are thus 25.48 kW and 6.83 kW}\)
