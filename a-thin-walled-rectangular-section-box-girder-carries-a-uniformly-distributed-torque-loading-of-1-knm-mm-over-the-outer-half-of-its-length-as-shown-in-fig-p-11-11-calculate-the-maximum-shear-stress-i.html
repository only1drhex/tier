Guest Question.

A thin-walled rectangular section box girder carries a uniformly distributed torque loading of 1 kNm/mm over the outer half of its length as shown in Fig. P.11.11. Calculate the maximum shear stress in the walls of the box girder and also the distribution of angle of twist along its length; illustrate your answer with a sketch. Take G = 70 000 N/mmÂ².

Admin
Solution

The maximum torque in the box girder is \( 1 \times 2 \times 10^{3} kNm \). Then, from Eq. (11.22)

\( \tau_{\max }=\frac{T_{\max }}{2 A t_{\min }} \) (11.22)

 

\( \tau_{\max }=\frac{1 \times 2 \times 10^{3} \times 10^{6}}{2 \times 1000 \times 750 \times 10}=133.3 N / mm ^{2} \)

The rate of twist of the girder is given by Eq. (11.25) since G is constant. Also

\( \frac{ d \theta}{ d x}=\frac{T}{4 A^{2} G} \oint \frac{ d s}{t} \) (11.25)

 

\( \oint \frac{ d s}{t}=\frac{2 \times 1000}{10}+\frac{2 \times 750}{15}=300 \)

Eq. (11.25) then becomes

\( \frac{ d \theta}{ d x}=\frac{T \times 300}{4 \times(1000 \times 750)^{2} \times 70000}=1.905 \times 10^{-15} T \)

In AB, \( T=2000 \times 10^{6} Nmm \). Therefore

\( \theta_{ AB }=2000 \times 10^{6} \times 1.905 \times 10^{-15} x+ B =3.81 \times 10^{-6} x+ B \)

When \( x=0, \theta_{ AB }=0 \text { so that } B =0 \text {. Also when } x=2000 mm \)

\( \theta_{ AB }=0.00762 rad =0.44^{\circ} \)

In BC, \( T=1 \times 10^{6}(4000-x) \) so that

\( \theta_{ BC }=1.905 \times 10^{-9}\left(4000 x-\frac{x^{2}}{2}\right)+ C \)

When \( x=2000 mm , \theta_{ BC }=0.00762 rad \text { so that } C =-0.00381 \). Then

\( \theta_{ BC }=1.905 \times 10^{-9}\left(4000 x-\frac{x^{2}}{2}\right)-0.00381 \)

hen \( x=4000 mm , \theta_{ BC }=0.01143 rad =0.65^{\circ} \)
The distribution of angle of twist in degrees is shown in Fig. S.11.11.
