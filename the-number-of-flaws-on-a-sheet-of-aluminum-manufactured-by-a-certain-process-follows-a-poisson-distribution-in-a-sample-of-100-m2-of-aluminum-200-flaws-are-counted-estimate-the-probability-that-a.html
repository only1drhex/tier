Guest Question.

The number of flaws on a sheet of aluminum manufactured by a certain process follows a Poisson distribution. In a sample of \(100 \mathrm{~m}^{2}\) of aluminum, 200 flaws are counted. Estimate the probability that a given square meter of aluminum has no flaws, and find the uncertainty in the estimate.

Admin
Solution

Let \(\lambda\) represent the mean number of flaws per square meter. We will begin by computing \(\widehat{\lambda}\) and its uncertainty. We have observed \(X=200\) flaws in \(t=100 \mathrm{~m}^{2}\) of aluminum. Therefore \(\widehat{\lambda}=200 / 100=2.00\). The uncertainty in \(\widehat{\lambda}\) is

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{\widehat{\lambda}} &=\sqrt{\frac{\lambda}{t}} \\&=\sqrt{\frac{2}{100}} \quad \text { approximating } \lambda \text { with } \widehat{\lambda}=2 \\ &=0.1414\end{aligned}\)

What we want to estimate is the probability that a square meter of aluminum contains no flaws. We first express this probability as a function of \(\lambda\). To do this, let \(Y\) represent the number of flaws in a \(1 \mathrm{~m}^{2}\) sheet of aluminum. Then \(Y \sim\) Poisson \((\lambda)\). We want to estimate \(P(Y=0)\). Using the Poisson probability mass function, this probability is given by

\(P(Y=0)=\frac{e^{-\lambda} \lambda^{0}}{0 !}=e^{-\lambda}\)

The probability that a square meter contains no flaws is therefore estimated with \(e^{-\widehat{\lambda}}=e^{-2.00}=0.1353\). To find the uncertainty in this estimate, we use the propagation of error method (Equation 3.10).

\(\sigma_{U} \approx\left|\frac{d U}{d X}\right| \sigma_{X}\) (3.10)

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{e^{-\hat{\lambda}}} &\approx\left|\frac{d}{d \widehat{\lambda}} e^{-\widehat{\lambda}}\right| \sigma_{\widehat{\lambda}} \\&=\left|-e^{-\widehat{\lambda}}\right| \sigma_{\widehat{\lambda}} \\&=e^{-2.00}(0.1414) \\&=0.0191\end{aligned}\)
