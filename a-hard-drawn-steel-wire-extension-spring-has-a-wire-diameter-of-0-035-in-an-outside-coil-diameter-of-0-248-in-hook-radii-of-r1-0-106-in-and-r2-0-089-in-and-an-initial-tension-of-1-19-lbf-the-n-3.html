Guest Question.

A hard-drawn steel wire extension spring has a wire diameter of 0.035 in, an outside coil diameter of 0.248 in, hook radii of \( r_{1}\) = 0.106 in and \( r_{2}\) = 0.089 in, and an initial tension of 1.19 lbf. The number of body turns is 12.17. From the given information:
(a) Determine the physical parameters of the spring.
(b) Check the initial preload stress conditions.
(c) Find the factors of safety under a static 5.25-lbf load.

Admin
Solution

(a)

\(D = OD − d = 0.248 − 0.035 = 0.213 in\)

\(C =\frac {D}{d} =\frac {0.213}{0.035 }= 6.086\)

\(K_{B} =\frac {4C + 2}{4C − 3} = 1.234\)

Eq. (10–40):

\(N_{a} = N_{b} +\frac {G}{E}\) (10–40)

\(N_{a} = N_{b} + G/E = 12.17 + 11.5/28.7 = 12.57\) turns

Eq. (10–9):

\(k\dot{=} \frac {d^{4}G}{8D^{3}N}\) (10–9)

\(k =\frac {d^{4}G}{8D^{3}N_{a}} =\frac {0.035^{4} (11.5)10^{6}}{8(0.213^{3})12.57} = 17.76 lbf/in\)

Eq. (10–39):

\(L_{0} = 2(D − d) + (N_{b} + 1)d = (2C − 1 + N_{b})d\) (10–39)

\(L_{0} = (2C − 1 + N_{b})d = [2(6.086) − 1 + 12.17] 0.035 = 0.817 in\)

The deflection under the service load is

\(y_{max} =\frac {F_{max} − F_{i}}{k} =\frac {5.25 − 1.19}{17.76} = 0.229 in\)

where the spring length becomes \(L = L_{0} + y = 0.817 + 0.229 = 1.046\) in.

(b) The uncorrected initial stress is given by Eq. (10–3) without the correction factor. That is,

\(τ =K_{s} \frac {8FD}{πd^{3}}\) (10–3)

\((τ_{i} )_{uncorr} =\frac {8F_{i} D}{πd^{3}} =\frac {8(1.19)0.213(10^{−3})}{π(0.035^{3})} = 15.1 kpsi\)

The preferred range is given by Eq. (10–41) and for this case is

\(τ_{i} =\frac {33 500}{exp(0.105C)} ± 1000 \left(4 − \frac {C − 3}{6.5}\right) psi\) (10–41)

\((τ_{i} )_{pref }=\frac {33 500}{exp(0.105C)} ± 1000 \left( 4 −\frac {C − 3}{6.5}\right)\)

\(=\frac {33 500}{exp[0.105(6.086)]} ± 1000 \left( 4 − \frac {6.086 − 3}{6.5}\right)\)

\(= 17 681 ± 3525 = 21.2, 14.2\) kpsi

Thus, the initial tension of 15.1 kpsi is in the preferred range.

(c) For hard-drawn wire, Table 10–4 gives m = 0.190 and A = 140 kpsi · \(in^{m}\) . From Eq. (10–14)

\(S_{ut} =\frac{A}{d^{m}}\) (10–14)

\(S_{ut} =\frac {A}{d^{m}} =\frac {140}{0.035^{0.190}} = 264.7 kpsi\)

For torsional shear in the main body of the spring, from Table 10–7,

\(S_{sy} = 0.45S_{ut} = 0.45(264.7) = 119.1 kpsi\)

The shear stress under the service load is

\(τ_{max} =\frac {8K_{B} F_{max}D}{πd^{3}} =\frac {8(1.234)5.25(0.213)}{π(0.035^{3})}(10^{−3}) = 82.0 kpsi\)

Thus, the factor of safety is

\(n =\frac {S_{sy}}{τ_{max}}=\frac {119.1}{82.0} = 1.45\)

For the end-hook bending at A,

\(C_{1} = 2r_{1}/d = 2(0.106)/0.0.035 = 6.057\)

From Eq. (10–35)

\((K)_{A} =\frac {4C^{2}_{1} − C_{1} − 1}{4C_{1}(C_{1} − 1)} C_{1} =\frac {2r_{1}}{d}\) (10–35)

\((K)_{A} =\frac {4C^{2}_{1} − C_{1} − 1}{4C_{1}(C_{1} − 1)} =\frac {4(6.057^{2}) − 6.057 − 1}{4(6.057)(6.057 − 1)} = 1.14\)

From Eq. (10–34)

\(σ_{A} = F \left[ (K)_{A} \frac {16D}{πd^{3}} +\frac {4}{πd^{2}}\right]\) (10-34)

\(σ_{A} = F_{max} \left[ (K)_{A} \frac {16D}{πd^{3}} +\frac {4}{πd^{2}}\right]\)
\(= 5.25 \left[ 1.14 \frac {16(0.213)}{π(0.035^{3})} +\frac {4}{π(0.035^{2})} \right] (10^{−3}) = 156.9 kpsi\)

The yield strength, from Table 10–7, is given by

\(S_{y} = 0.75S_{ut} = 0.75(264.7) = 198.5 kpsi\)

The factor of safety for end-hook bending at A is then

\(n_{A} =\frac {S_{y}}{σ_{A}} =\frac {198.5}{156.9} = 1.27\)

For the end-hook in torsion at B, from Eq. (10–37)

\((K)_{B} =\frac {4C_{2} − 1}{4C_{2} − 4} C_{2} =\frac {2r_{2}}{d}\) (10–37)

\(C_{2} = 2r_{2}/d = 2(0.089)/0.035 = 5.086\)

\((K)_{B} =\frac {4C_{2} − 1}{4C_{2} − 4} =\frac {4(5.086) − 1}{4(5.086) − 4} = 1.18\)

and the corresponding stress, given by Eq. (10–36), is

\(τ_{B} = (K)_{B} \frac {8FD}{πd^{3}}\) (10–36)

\(τ_{B} = (K)_{B} \frac {8F_{max}D}{πd^{3}} = 1.18 \frac {8(5.25)0.213}{π(0.035^{3})} (10^{−3}) = 78.4 kpsi\)

Using Table 10–7 for yield strength, the factor of safety for end-hook torsion at B is

\(n_{B} =\frac {(S_{sy})_{B}}{τ_{B}} =\frac {0.4(264.7)}{78.4} = 1.35\)

Yield due to bending of the end hook will occur first.
