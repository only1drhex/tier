Guest Question.

In a steam generator, compressed liquid water at 10 MPa, 30°C, enters a 30-mm diameter tube at the rate of 3 L/s. Steam at 9 MPa, 400°C exits the tube. Find the rate of heat transfer to the water.

Admin
Solution

C.V. Steam generator. Steady state single inlet and exit flow.

Constant diameter tube: \(A_{i}=A_{e}=\frac{\pi}{4}(0.03)^{2}=0.0007068 m ^{2}\)

Table B.1.4 \(\dot{ m }=\dot{ V }_{ i } / v _{ i }=0.003 / 0.0010003=3.0 kg / s\)

\(V _{ i }=\dot{ V }_{ i } / A _{ i }=0.003 / 0.0007068=4.24 m / s\)

Exit state properties from Table B.1.3

\(V _{ e }= V _{ i } \times v _{ e } / v _{ i }=4.24 \times 0.02993 / 0.0010003=126.86 m / s\)

 

The energy equation Eq. 4.12 is solved for the heat transfer as

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{Q} &=\dot{m}\left[\left(h_{e}-h_{i}\right)+\left( V _{ e }{ }^{2}- V _{ i }^{2}\right) / 2\right] \\&=3.0 kg / s \times\left[3117.8-134.86+\frac{126.86^{2}-4.24^{2}}{2 \times 1000}\right] kJ / kg = 8 9 7 3 kW\end{aligned}\)
