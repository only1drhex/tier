Guest Question.

A 302 stainless steel cylinder of height 12 cm and diameter 7 cm at room temperature is compressed to a height of 2 cm between large platens. Mineral oil is used as a lubricant between the cylinder and platens. Calculate the force necessary and stress on the platens.

Admin
Solution

\( F=Q_{a}\sigma _{t}A_{m} \) ; for 302 stainless steel: \( \sigma _{t}= 1300 \epsilon ^{0.3}MPA \) (Table 3.1); coefficient of friction with mineral oil, μ= 0.1 (Table 4.1); to find\( Q_{a}\) using Fig. 4.7, calculate d/h.

From volume constancy, \( A_{o} h_{o}=A_{m}h \) so that

\( A_{m}=\frac{A_{o} h_{o}}{h} \)

where the subscript m denotes the mean area or diameter. Therefore,

\( d^{2}_{m}= d^{2}_{o} \frac{h_{o}}{h} =7^{2}\frac{12}{2} ; d_{m}=17.2 cm \)

and

\( \frac{d_{m}}{h}=\frac{17.2}{2}= 8.57\)

From Fig. 4.7, \( Q_{a}\approx 1.3, so \sigma _{a}= Q_{a}\sigma _{t}\) , or

\( \sigma _{a}=Q_{a}K \left[\ln \frac{A_{o}}{A} \right] ^{n}\)

 

\( = 1.3(1300 \times 10^{6}) \left(\ln \left[\frac{7^{2}}{17.2^{2}} \right] \right) ^{0.3}= 2015 MPa \)

The force required is

\( F= \sigma _{a}A_{m}=2015 MPa \times \frac{\pi (0.172 m)^{2}}{4 }\)

 

\( \approx 46 500 kN (\approx 4740t )\)

Note: From this calculation, the relatively large forging press of Fig. 4.4 is not capable of cold upsetting the relatively small cylinder of this example.
