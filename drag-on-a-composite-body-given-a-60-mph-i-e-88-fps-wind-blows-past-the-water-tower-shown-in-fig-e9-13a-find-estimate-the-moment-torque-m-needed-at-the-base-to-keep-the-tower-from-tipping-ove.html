Guest Question.

Drag on a Composite Body

GIVEN A 60-mph (i.e., 88-fps) wind blows past the water tower shown in Fig. E9.13a.

FIND Estimate the moment (torque), M, needed at the base to keep the tower from tipping over.

Admin
Solution

We treat the water tower as a sphere resting on a circular cylinder and assume that the total drag is the sum of the drag from these parts. The free-body diagram of the tower is shown in Fig. E9.13b. By summing moments about the base of the tower, we obtain

 

\(M= D _{s}\left(b+\frac{D_{s}}{2}\right)+ D _{c}\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)\) (1)

 

where

 

\(D_{s}=\frac{1}{2} \rho U^{2} \frac{\pi}{4} D_{s}^{2} C_{D s}\) (2)

 

and

 

\(D_{c}=\frac{1}{2} \rho U^{2} b D_{c} C_{D c}\) (3)

 

are the drag on the sphere and cylinder, respectively. For standard atmospheric conditions, the Reynolds numbers are

 

\(Re _{s}=\frac{U D_{s}}{\nu}=\frac{(88 ft / s )(40 ft )}{1.57 \times 10^{-4} ft ^{2} / s }=2.24 \times 10^{7}\)

 

and

 

\(Re _{c}=\frac{U D_{c}}{\nu}=\frac{(88 ft / s )(15 ft )}{1.57 \times 10^{-4} ft ^{2} / s }=8.41 \times 10^{6}\)

 

The corresponding drag coefficients, \(C_{D s} \text { and } C_{D c}\), can be approximated from Fig. 9.21 as

 

\(C_{D s} \approx 0.3 \text { and } C_{D c} \approx 0.7\)

 

Note that the value of \(C_{D s}\) was obtained by an extrapolation of the given data to Reynolds numbers beyond those given (a potentially dangerous practice!). From Eqs. 2 and 3 we obtain

 

\(\begin{aligned}D _{s} &=0.5\left(0.00238 \text { slugs } / ft ^{3}\right)(88 ft / s )^{2} \frac{\pi}{4}(40 ft )^{2}(0.3) \\&=3470 lb\end{aligned}\)

 

and

 

\(\begin{aligned}D _{c} &=0.5\left(0.00238 slugs / ft ^{3}\right)(88 ft / s )^{2}(50 ft \times 15 ft )(0.7) \\&=4840 lb\end{aligned}\)

 

From Eq. 1 the corresponding moment needed to prevent the tower from tipping is

 

\(\begin{aligned}M &=3470 lb \left(50 ft +\frac{40}{2} ft \right)+4840 lb \left(\frac{50}{2} ft \right) \\&=3.64 \times 10^{5} ft \cdot lb\end{aligned}\)

 

COMMENT The above result is only an estimate because (a) the wind is probably not uniform from the top of the tower to the ground, (b) the tower is not exactly a combination of a smooth sphere and a circular cylinder, (c) the cylinder is not of infinite length, (d) there will be some interaction between the flow past the cylinder and that past the sphere so that the net drag is not exactly the sum of the two, and (e) a drag coefficient value was obtained by extrapolation of the given data. However, such approximate results are often quite accurate.
