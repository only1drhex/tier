Guest Question.

Design a muff coupling to connect two steel shafts transmitting 25 kW power at 360 rpm. The shafts and key are made of plain carbon steel 30C8 \( \left(S_{y t}=S_{y c}=400\right.\left. N / mm ^{2}\right) \). The sleeve is made of grey cast iron FG 200 \( \left(S_{u t}=200 N / mm ^{2}\right) \). The factor of safety for the shafts and key is 4. For the sleeve, the factor of safety is 6 based on ultimate strength.

Admin
Solution

Given kW = 25 n = 360 rpm.

\( \text { For shafts and key, } S_{y t}=S_{y c}=400 N / mm ^{2} \quad(f s)=4 \).

\( \text { For sleeve, } \quad S_{u t}=200 N / mm ^{2} \quad(f s)=6 \).

Step I Permissible stresses
For the material of shafts and key,

\( \sigma_{t}=\frac{S_{y t}}{(f s)}=\frac{400}{4}=100 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{S_{y c}}{(f s)}=\frac{400}{4}=100 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( \tau=\frac{S_{s y}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5 S_{y t}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5(400)}{4}=50 N / mm ^{2} \).

For sleeve material,

\( \tau=\frac{S_{s u}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5 S_{u t}}{(f s)}=\frac{0.5(200)}{6}=16.67 N / mm ^{2} \).

Step II Diameter of each shaft

\( M_{t}=\frac{60 \times 10^{6}( kW )}{2 \pi n}=\frac{60 \times 10^{6}(25)}{2 \pi(360)} \).

= 663 145.60 N-mm.

\( \tau=\frac{16 M_{t}}{\pi d^{3}} \quad \text { or } \quad 50=\frac{16(663145.60)}{\pi d^{3}} \).

\( \therefore \quad d=40.73 \text { or } 45 mm \).

Step III Dimensions of sleeve

D = (2d + 13 ) = 2× 45 + 13 = 103 or 105 mm
L = 3.5d = 3.5 (45) = 157.5 or 160 mm
The torsional shear stress in the sleeve is
calculated by treating it as a hollow cylinder.

\( J=\frac{\pi\left(D^{4}-d^{4}\right)}{32}=\frac{\pi\left(105^{4}-45^{4}\right)}{32} \).

\( =11530626.79 mm ^{4} \).

\( r=\frac{D}{2}=\frac{105}{2}=52.5 mm \).

\( \tau=\frac{M_{t} r}{J}=\frac{(663145.60)(52.5)}{(11530626.79)} \).

= 3.02 N/mm².

\( \therefore \quad \tau<16.67 N / mm ^{2} \).

Step IV Dimensions of key
From Table 9.3, the standard cross-section of flat sunk key for a 45 mm diameter shaft is 14 × 9 mm.
The length of key in each shaft is one-half of the length of sleeve. Therefore,

\( l=\frac{L}{2}=\frac{160}{2}=80 mm \).

The dimensions of the key are 14 × 9 × 80 mm.

Step VII Check for stresses in key
From Eq. (9.27),

\( \tau=\frac{2 M_{t}}{d b l} \) (9.27).

\( \tau=\frac{2 M_{t}}{d b l}=\frac{2(3978873.58)}{80(22)(140)} \).

\( =32.3 N / mm ^{2} \quad \therefore \tau<40 N / mm ^{2} \).

From Eq. (9.28),

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{4 M_{t}}{d h l} \) (9.28).

\( \sigma_{c}=\frac{4 M_{t}}{d h l}=\frac{4(3978873.58)}{80(14)(140)} \).

\( =101.5 N / mm ^{2} \quad \therefore \sigma_{c}<120 N / mm ^{2} \).

The design of key is safe from shear and compression considerations.
