Guest Question.

Air at Ma = 2.0 and p = 10 lbf/\(in^2\) absolute is forced to turn through 10° by a ramp at the body surface. A weak oblique shock forms as in Fig. E9.17. For k = 1.4 compute from exact oblique shock theory (a) the wave angle β, (b) \(Ma_2, and (c) p_2\). Also use the linearized theory to estimate (d) β and (e) \(p_2\).

Admin
Solution

EES

With \(Ma_1\) = 2.0 and θ = 10° known, we can estimate β ≈ 40° ± 2° from Fig. 9.23. For more (hand calculated) accuracy, we have to solve Eq. (9.86) by iteration. Or we can program Eq. (9.86) in EES with six statements (in SI units, with angles in degrees):

\(\tan \theta = \frac{2 \cot \beta (Ma_1^2 \sin^2 \beta – 1)}{Ma_1^2 (k + \cos 2\beta) + 2}\) (9.86)

Ma = 2.0
k = 1.4
Theta = 10
Num = 2*(Ma^2*SIN(Beta)^2 – 1)/TAN(Beta)
Denom = Ma^2*(k + COS(2*Beta)) + 2
Theta = ARCTAN(Num/Denom)

Specify that Beta > 0 and EES promptly reports an accurate result:

β = 39.32°

The normal Mach number upstream is thus

\(Ma_{n1} = Ma_1 \sin \beta = 2.0 \sin 39.32^{\circ} = 1.267\)

With \(Ma_{n1}\) we can use the normal shock relations (Table B.2) or Fig. 9.9 or Eqs. (9.56) to (9.58) to compute

\(Ma_{n2} = 0.8031 \frac{p_2}{p_1} = 1.707\)

\(\frac{p_2}{p_1} = \frac{1 + k Ma_1^2}{1 + k Ma^2_2}\) (9.56)

\(Ma^2_2 = \frac{(k – 1) Ma_1^2 + 2}{2k Ma_1^2 – (k – 1)}\) (9.57)

\(\frac{\rho_2}{\rho_1} = \frac{(k + 1) Ma_1^2}{(k – 1) Ma_1^2 + 2} = \frac{V_1}{V_2}\)

 

\(\frac{T_2}{T_1} = [2 + (k – 1) Ma_1^2] \frac{2k Ma^2_1 – (k – 1)}{(k + 1)^2 Ma_1^2}\)

 

\(T_{02} = T_{01}\)

 

\(\frac{p_{02}}{p_{01}} = \frac{\rho_{02}}{\rho_{01}} = \left[\frac{(k + 1) Ma_1^2}{2 + (k – 1) Ma_1^2}\right]^{k/(k-1)} \left[\frac{k + 1}{2k Ma^2_1 – (k – 1)}\right]^{1/(k-1)}\) (9.58)

Thus the downstream Mach number and pressure are

\(Ma_2 = \frac{Ma_{n2}}{\sin (\beta – \theta)} = \frac{0.8031}{\sin (39.32^{\circ} – 10^{\circ})} = 1.64\) \(p_2 = (10 lbf/in^2 absolute)(1.707) = 17.07 lbf/in^2 absolute\)

Notice that the computed pressure ratio agrees with Figs. 9.24 and B.2.

For the linearized theory the Mach angle is μ = \(\sin^{-1}\) (1/2.0) = 30°. Equation (9.88) then estimates that

\(\sin \beta = \sin \mu + \frac{k + 1}{4 \cos \mu} \tan \theta + … + \mathscr{O} (tan^2 \theta) + …\) (9.88)

\(\sin \beta \approx \sin 30^{\circ} + \frac{2.4 \tan 10^{\circ}}{4 \cos 30^{\circ}} = 0.622\)

or β ≈ 38.5°

Equation (9.89) estimates that

\(\frac{p_2 – p_1}{p_1} = \frac{k Ma_1^2}{(Ma_1^2 – 1)^{1/2}} \tan \theta + … + \mathscr{O} (\tan^2 \theta) + …\) (9.89)

\(\frac{p_2}{p_1} \approx 1 + \frac{1.4 (2)^2 \tan 10^{\circ}}{(2^2 – 1)^{1/2}} = 1.57\)

or \(p_2 \approx 1.57(10 lbf/in^2 absolute) \approx 15.7 lbf/in^2 absolute\)

These are reasonable estimates in spite of the fact that 10° is really not a “small” flow deflection.
