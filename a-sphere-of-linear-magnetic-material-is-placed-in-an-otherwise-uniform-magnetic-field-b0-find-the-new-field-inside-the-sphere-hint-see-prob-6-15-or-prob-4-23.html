Guest Question.

A sphere of linear magnetic material is placed in an otherwise uniform magnetic field \(B _{0}\). Find the new field inside the sphere. [Hint: See or .]

Admin
Solution

By the method of :

For large r, we want \(B (r, \theta) \rightarrow B _{0}=B_{0} \hat{ z }, \text { so } H =\frac{1}{\mu_{0}} B \rightarrow \frac{1}{\mu_{0}} B_{0} \hat{ z }\) , and hence \(W \rightarrow-\frac{1}{\mu_{0}} B_{0} z=-\frac{1}{\mu_{0}} B_{0} r \cos \theta\) .

“Potentials”:

\(\begin{cases}W_{ in }(r, \theta) & =\sum A_{l} r^{l} P_{l}(\cos \theta), & (r<R); \\ W_{\text {out }}(r, \theta) & =-\frac{1}{\mu_{0}} B_{0} r \cos \theta+\sum \frac{B_{l}}{r^{l+1}} P_{l}(\cos \theta), & (r>R).\end{cases}\)

Boundary Conditions:

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}\text { (i) } W_{\text {in }}(R, \theta)=W_{\text {out }}(R, \theta), \\\text { (ii) }-\left.\mu_{0} \frac{\partial W_{\text {out }}}{\partial r}\right|_{R}+\left.\mu \frac{\partial W_{\text {in }}}{\partial r}\right|_{R}=0.\end{array}\right.\)

(The latter follows from Eq. 6.26.)

\(B_{\text {above }}^{\perp}-B_{\text {below }}^{\perp}=0\) (6.26)

\(\text { (ii) } \Rightarrow \mu_{0}\left[\frac{1}{\mu_{0}} B_{0} \cos \theta+\sum(l+1) \frac{B_{l}}{R^{l+2}} P_{l}(\cos \theta)\right]+\mu \sum l A_{l} R^{l-1} P_{l}(\cos \theta)=0\) .

For \(l \neq 1,( i ) \Rightarrow B_{l}=R^{2 l+1} A_{l}, \text { so }\left[\mu_{0}(l+1)+\mu l\right] A_{l} R^{l-1}=0, \text { and hence } A_{l}=0\) .

For \(l=1,( i ) \Rightarrow A_{1} R=-\frac{1}{\mu_{0}} B_{0} R+B_{1} / R^{2}, \text { and (ii) } \Rightarrow B_{0}+2 \mu_{0} B_{1} / R^{3}+\mu A_{1}=0, \text { so } A_{1}=-3 B_{0} /\left(2 \mu_{0}+\mu\right)\) .

\(W_{ in }(r, \theta)=-\frac{3 B_{0}}{\left(2 \mu_{0}+\mu\right)} r \cos \theta=-\frac{3 B_{0} z}{\left(2 \mu_{0}+\mu\right)} . \quad H _{ in }=-\nabla W_{ in }=\frac{3 B_{0}}{\left(2 \mu_{0}+\mu\right)} \hat{ z }=\frac{3 B _{0}}{\left(2 \mu_{0}+\mu\right)}\) .

\(B =\mu H =\frac{3 \mu B _{0}}{\left(2 \mu_{0}+\mu\right)}=\left(\frac{1+\chi_{m}}{1+\chi_{m} / 3}\right) B _{0}\) .

By the method of :

Step 1: \(B _{0}\) magnetizes the sphere: \( M _{0}=\chi_{m} H _{0}=\frac{\chi_{m}}{\mu_{0}\left(1+\chi_{m}\right)} B _{0}\) . This magnetization sets up a field within the sphere given by Eq. 6.16:

\(B =\frac{2}{3} \mu_{0} M\) (6.16)

\(\left. B _{1}=\frac{2}{3} \mu_{0} M _{0}=\frac{2}{3} \frac{\chi_{m}}{1+\chi_{m}} B _{0}=\frac{2}{3} \kappa B _{0} \quad \text { (where } \kappa \equiv \frac{\chi_{m}}{1+\chi_{m}}\right)\) .

Step 2: \(B _{1}\) magnetizes the sphere an additional amount \(M _{1}=\frac{\kappa}{\mu_{0}} B _{1}\) . This sets up an additional field in the sphere:

\(B _{2}=\frac{2}{3} \mu_{0} M _{1}=\frac{2}{3} \kappa B _{1}=\left(\frac{2 \kappa}{3}\right)^{2} B _{0}\) , etc.

The total field is:

\(B = B _{0}+ B _{1}+ B _{2}+\cdots= B _{0}+(2 \kappa / 3) B _{0}+(2 \kappa / 3)^{2} B _{0}+\cdots=\left[1+(2 \kappa / 3)+(2 \kappa / 3)^{2}+\cdots\right] B _{0}=\frac{ B _{0}}{(1-2 \kappa / 3)} \).

\(\frac{1}{1-2 \kappa / 3}=\frac{3}{3-2 \chi_{m} /\left(1+\chi_{m}\right)}=\frac{3+3 \chi_{m}}{3+3 \chi_{m}-2 \chi_{m}}=\frac{3\left(1+\chi_{m}\right)}{3+\chi_{m}}, \text { so } B =\left(\frac{1+\chi_{m}}{1+\chi_{m} / 3}\right) B _{0}\) .
