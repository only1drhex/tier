Guest Question.

If positive-sequence voltages are assumed and the Y-Δ connection is considered, again with ideal transformers as in , find the complex voltage gain \(C_{3}\).
(a) What would the gain be for a negative-sequence set?
(b) Comment on the complex power gain.
(c) When terminated in a symmetric Y-connected load, find the referred impedance \(Z_{L}^{\prime}\), the secondary impedance \(Z_{L}\) referred to primary (i.e., the per-phase driving-point impedance on the primary side), in terms of \(Z_{L}\) and the complex voltage gain C.

Admin
Solution
\(\left.\begin{array}{l}\bar{V}_{a^{\prime} n^{\prime}}=\left(\frac{n}{\sqrt{3}} e^{j 30^{\circ}}\right) \bar{V}_{a n}=\bar{C}_{3} \bar{V}_{a n} \\\bar{V}_{b^{\prime} n^{\prime}}=\bar{C}_{3} \bar{V}_{b n} ; \bar{V}_{c^{\prime} n^{\prime}}=\bar{C}_{3} \bar{V}_{c n}\end{array}\right\} \text { For the positive seq. set }\)

 

(a) \(\bar{C}_{4}=\bar{C}_{3}^{*}\) for the negative sequence set

(b) Complex power gain \(=\bar{C}\left(1 / \bar{C}^{*}\right)^{*}=1\)

(c) \(\bar{Z}_{L}=\frac{\bar{V}_{a n}}{\bar{I}_{a}}=\frac{\bar{V}_{a^{\prime} n^{\prime}} / \bar{C}}{\bar{C}^{*} \bar{I}_{a}^{\prime}}=\frac{1}{C^{2}} \bar{Z}_{L}, \text { where } C=|\bar{C}| .\)
