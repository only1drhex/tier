Guest Question.

A flatplate solar collector with no cover plate has a selective absorber surface of emissivity 0.1 and solar absorptivity 0.95. At a given time of day the absorber surface temperature \(T_{s}\) is \(120^{\circ} C\) when the solar irradiation is \(750 W / m ^{2}\), the effective sky temperature is \(-10^{\circ} C\), and the ambient air temperature \(T_{\infty} \text { is } 30^{\circ} C\). Assume that the convection heat transfer coefficient for the calm day conditions can be estimated from

\(\bar{h}=0.22\left(T_{s}-T_{\infty}\right)^{1 / 3} W / m ^{2} \cdot K\)

Calculate the useful heat removal rate \(\left( W / m ^{2}\right)\) from the collector for these conditions. What is the corresponding efficiency of the collector?

Admin
Solution

Known: Operating conditions for a flatplate solar collector.

Find:
1. Useful heat removal rate per unit area, \(q_{u}^{\prime \prime}\left( W / m ^{2}\right)\).

2. Efficiency \(\eta\) of the collector.

Assumptions:
1. Steadystate conditions.
2. Bottom of collector well insulated.
3. Absorber surface diffuse.

Analysis:
1. Performing an energy balance on the absorber,

 

\(\dot{E}_{\text {in }}-\dot{E}_{\text {out }}=0\)

 

or, per unit surface area,

 

\(\alpha_{S} G_{S}+\alpha_{ sky } G_{ atm }-q_{ conv }^{\prime \prime}-E-q_{u}^{\prime \prime}=0\)

 

From Equation 12.73,

 

\(G_{ atm }=\sigma T_{ sky }^{4}\) (12.73)

 

Since the atmospheric irradiation is concentrated in approximately the same spectral region as that of surface emission, it is reasonable to assume that

 

\(\alpha_{ sky } \approx \varepsilon=0.1\)

With

\(q_{\text {conv }}^{\prime \prime}=\bar{h}\left(T_{s}-T_{\infty}\right)=0.22\left(T_{s}-T_{\infty}\right)^{4 / 3} \quad \text { and } \quad E=\varepsilon \sigma T_{s}^{4}\)

 

it follows that

 

\(q_{u}^{\prime \prime}=\alpha_{S} G_{S}+\varepsilon \sigma T_{\text {sky }}^{4}-0.22\left(T_{s}-T_{\infty}\right)^{4 / 3}-\varepsilon \sigma T_{s}^{4}\)

 

\(q_{u}^{\prime \prime}=\alpha_{S} G_{S}-0.22\left(T_{s}-T_{\infty}\right)^{4 / 3}-\varepsilon \sigma\left(T_{s}^{4}-T_{ sky }^{4}\right)\)

 

\(q_{u}^{\prime \prime}=0.95 \times 750 W / m ^{2}-0.22(120-30)^{4 / 3} W / m ^{2}\)

 

\(-0.1 \times 5.67 \times 10^{-8} W / m ^{2} \cdot K ^{4}\left(393^{4}-263^{4}\right) K ^{4}\)

 

\(q_{u}^{\prime \prime}=(712.5-88.7-108.1) W / m ^{2}=516 W / m ^{2}\)

 

2. The collector efficiency, defined as the fraction of the solar irradiation extracted as useful energy, is then

 

\(\eta=\frac{q_{u}^{\prime \prime}}{G_{S}}=\frac{516 W / m ^{2}}{750 W / m ^{2}}=0.69\)

 

Comments:
1. Since the spectral range of \(G_{ atm }\) is entirely different from that of \(G_{S}\), it would be incorrect to assume that \(\alpha_{\text {sky }}=\alpha_{S}\).

2. The convection heat transfer coefficient is extremely low \(\left(\bar{h} \approx 1 W / m ^{2} \cdot K \right)\). With a modest increase to \(\bar{h}=5 W / m ^{2} \cdot K\), the useful heat flux and the efficiency are reduced to \(q_{u}^{\prime \prime}=154 W / m ^{2} \text { and } \eta=0.21\). A cover plate can contribute significantly to reducing convection (and radiation) heat loss from the absorber plate.
