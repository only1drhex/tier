Guest Question.

Using Lagrange’s equation, derive the differential equation of motion of the system shown in Fig. 2.8.

Admin
Solution

The position vectors of the two masses are given by

\(r^{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ y_{1} \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix}l_{1}\sin \theta _{1} \\ -l_{1}\cos \theta _{1} \end{matrix} \right]\)

 

\(r^{2}=\left[\begin{matrix} x_{2} \\ y_{2} \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix}l_{1}\sin \theta _{1}+l_{2}\sin \theta _{2} \\ -l_{1}\cos \theta _{1}-l_{2}\cos \theta _{2} \end{matrix} \right] \)

The velocity vectors are given by

\(\dot{r} ^{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} \dot{x} _{1} \\ \dot{y} _{1} \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix}\dot{\theta }_{1}l_{1}\cos \theta _{1} \\ \dot{\theta }_{1}l_{1}\sin \theta _{1} \end{matrix} \right]=\begin{bmatrix} l_{1}\cos \theta _{1} & 0 \\ l_{1}\sin \theta _{1} & 0 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} \dot{\theta }_{1} \\ \dot{\theta }_{2} \end{matrix} \right] = B^{1}\dot{q}\)

 

\(\dot{r} ^{2}=\left[\begin{matrix} \dot{x} _{2} \\ \dot{y} _{2} \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix}\dot{\theta }_{1}l_{1}\cos \theta _{1} + \dot{\theta }_{2}l_{2}\cos \theta _{2} \\ \dot{\theta }_{1}l_{1}\sin \theta _{1} + \dot{\theta }_{2}l_{2}\sin \theta _{2} \end{matrix} \right]=\begin{bmatrix} l_{1}\cos \theta _{1} & l_{2}\cos \theta _{2} \\ l_{1}\sin \theta _{1} & l_{2}\sin \theta _{2} \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} \dot{\theta }_{1} \\ \dot{\theta }_{2} \end{matrix} \right] = B^{2}\dot{q}\)

Where

\(q=\left[\begin{matrix} q_{1} \\ q_{2} \end{matrix} \right] = \left[\begin{matrix} \theta _{1} \\ \theta _{2} \end{matrix} \right]\)

and the coefficient matrices \(B^{1} \) and \(B^{2}\) are given by

\(B^{1}=\begin{bmatrix} l_{1}\cos \theta _{1} & 0 \\ l_{1}\sin \theta _{1} & 0 \end{bmatrix} , B^{2}=\begin{bmatrix} l_{1}\cos \theta _{1} & l_{2}\cos \theta _{2} \\ l_{1}\sin \theta _{1} & l_{2}\sin \theta _{2} \end{bmatrix}\)

The kinetic energy of the system is

\(T=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{2}{T^{i} }=\frac{1}{2}\sum\limits_{i=1}^{2}{m^{i}\dot{r} ^{iT}\dot{r}^{i} }=\frac{1}{2} m_{1} \dot{r}^{1T} \dot{r}^{1} + \frac{1}{2} m_{2}\dot{r}^{2T}\dot{r}^{2}\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{2}\dot{q} ^{T}m_{1} B^{1T} B^{1}\dot{q} + \frac{1}{2}\dot{q}^{T} m_{2} B^{2T}B^{2}\dot{q}\)

 

\(= \frac{1}{2}\dot{q}^{T}\left[m_{1}B^{1T}B^{1}+m_{2}B^{2T}B^{2} \right]\dot{q} = \frac{1}{2}\dot{q} ^{T}M\dot{q}\)

where the mass matrix M is defined as

\(M=m_{1} B^{1T} B^{1} + m_{2} B^{2T} B^{2}\)

 

\(=m_{1} \begin{bmatrix}l_{1}\cos \theta _{1} & l_{1}\sin \theta _{1} \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}l_{1}\cos \theta _{1} &0 \\ l_{1}\sin \theta _{1} & 0 \end{bmatrix}\)

 

\(+ m_{2} \begin{bmatrix}l_{1}\cos \theta _{1} & l_{1}\sin \theta _{1} \\ l_{2}\cos \theta _{2} & l_{2}\sin \theta _{2} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}l_{1}\cos \theta _{1} & l_{2}\cos \theta _{2} \\ l_{1}\sin \theta _{1} & l_{2}\sin \theta _{2} \end{bmatrix}\)

 

\( =\begin{bmatrix} m_{1}l^{2}_{1} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} m_{2}l^{2}_{1} & m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}\cos \left(\theta _{1}-\theta _{2} \right) \\ m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}\cos \left(\theta _{1}-\theta _{2} \right) & m_{2}l^{2}_{2} \end{bmatrix}\)

 

\(=\begin{bmatrix} \left(m_{1}+m_{2} \right)l^{2}_{1} & m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}\cos \left(\theta _{1}-\theta _{2} \right) \\ m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}\cos \left(\theta _{1}-\theta _{2} \right) & m_{2}l^{2}_{2} \end{bmatrix}\)

The virtual work of the forces that act on the system is given by

\(\delta W= -m_{1}g\delta y_{1} -m_{2}g\delta y_{2} + M_{1}\delta \theta _{1} + M_{2}\delta \theta _{2}\)

where the virtual displacements \(\delta y_{1}\) and \(\delta y_{2}\) are

\(\delta y_{1} = l_{1}\sin \theta _{1}\delta \theta _{1}\)

 

\(\delta y_{2} = l_{1}\sin \theta _{1}\delta \theta _{1} + l_{2}\sin \theta _{2}\delta \theta _{2}\)

That is,

\(\delta W = -m_{1}gl_{1}\sin \theta _{1}\delta \theta _{1}-m_{2}g\left(l_{1}\sin \theta _{1}\delta \theta _{1} + l_{2}\sin \theta _{2}\delta \theta _{2} \right) + M_{1}\delta \theta _{1} + M_{2}\delta \theta _{2}\)

 

\(= \left(M_{1} – m_{1}gl_{1}\sin \theta _{1} – m_{2}gl_{1}\sin \theta _{1} \right)\delta \theta _{1} + \left(M_{2} – m_{2}gl_{2}\sin \theta _{2} \right)\delta \theta _{2}\)

 

which can be written as

\(\delta W= Q_{\theta 1}\delta \theta _{1} + Q_{\theta 2}\delta \theta _{2} = Q^{T}\delta q \)

Where \(Q_{\theta 1}\) and \(Q_{\theta 2}\) are, respectively, the generalized forces associated with the coordinates \(\theta _{1}\) and \(\theta _{2}\) and are given by

\(Q=\left[\begin{matrix} Q_{\theta 1} \\ Q_{\theta 1} \end{matrix} \right] = \left[\begin{matrix} M_{1} – m_{1}gl_{1}\sin \theta _{1} – m_{2}gl_{1}\sin \theta _{1} \\ M_{2} – m_{2}gl_{2}\sin \theta _{2} \end{matrix} \right]\)

Having determined the kinetic energy and the generalized forces, one can use Lagrange’s equation. To this end, we evaluate the following:

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\eth T}{\eth \dot{q} } \right) = \frac{d}{dt}\left(T_{\dot{q} } \right) = \frac{d}{dt}\left(\dot{q} ^{T}M \right) = \ddot{q} ^{T}M + \ddot{q} ^{T}\dot{M}\)

That is,

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left(T_{\dot{q} } \right)^{T} = M\ddot{q} + \dot{M} \dot{q}\)

where \(\dot{M}\) is the matrix

\(\dot{M} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -m_{2}l_{1}l_{2} \left(\dot{\theta }_{1} – \dot{\theta }_{2} \right) \sin \left(\theta _{1} – \theta _{2} \right) \\ -m_{2}l_{1}l_{2} \left(\dot{\theta }_{1} – \dot{\theta }_{2} \right) \sin \left(\theta _{1} – \theta _{2} \right) &0 \end{bmatrix}\)

Therefore,

\(\frac{d}{dt}\left(\frac{\eth T}{\eth \dot{q} } \right)^{T} = \begin{bmatrix} \left(m_{1} + m_{2} \right)l^{2}_{1} & m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}\cos \left(\theta _{1}-\theta _{2} \right) \\ m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}\cos \left(\theta _{1}-\theta _{2} \right) & m_{2}l^{2}_{2} \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix}\ddot{\theta _{1} } \\ \ddot{\theta _{2} } \end{matrix} \right] \)

 

\(-\left[\begin{matrix} m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}\dot{\theta } _{2}\left(\dot{\theta _{1} } -\dot{\theta _{2} } \right)\sin \left(\theta _{1}-\theta _{2} \right) \\ m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}\dot{\theta } _{2}\left(\dot{\theta _{1} } -\dot{\theta _{2} } \right)\sin \left(\theta _{1}-\theta _{2} \right) \end{matrix} \right]\)

One can also show that

\(\left(\frac{\eth T}{\eth q} \right)^{T}= \left(T_{q} \right)^{T} = \left[\begin{matrix} -m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}\dot{\theta _{1} } \dot{\theta _{2} }\sin \left(\theta _{1}-\theta _{2} \right) \\ -m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}\dot{\theta _{1} } \dot{\theta _{2} }\sin \left(\theta _{1}-\theta _{2} \right) \end{matrix} \right]\)

Substituting into Lagrange’s equation of motion given by Eq. 2.53 or 2.54, one obtains the differential equations of motion of this system.

Eq. 2.53 \(\frac{d}{dt}=\left(T_{\dot{q} } \right)^{T} – \left(T_{q} \right)^{T} = Q\)

Eq. 2.54 \(\frac{d}{dt}=\left(M_{\dot{q} } \right) – \left(T_{q} \right)^{T} = Q\)
