Guest Question.

Calculating the vapour pressure from a mass loss
Caesium (m.p. 29 °C, b.p. 686 °C) was introduced into a container and heated to 500 °C; the container is pierced by a hole of diameter 1.0 mm. It is found that in 1.00 h (3600 s) the mass of the container decreased by 84.4 mg. Calculate the vapour pressure of liquid caesium at 500 °C.

Admin
Solution

Collect your thoughts The pressure of vapour is constant inside the container despite the effusion of atoms because the hot liquid metal replenishes the vapour. The rate of effusion is therefore constant, and given by eqn 16A.12. \(\frac{pA_0N_A}{(2\pi MRT)^{1/2}} \)To express the rate in terms of mass, multiply the number of atoms that escape by the mass of each atom.
The solution The mass loss Δm in an interval Δt is equal to the number of molecules that strike the area of the hole in this interval multiplied by the mass of each molecule:

\(\Delta m= rate of effusion \times \Delta t \times m=\overbrace{\frac{pA_0N_A}{(2\pi MRT)^{1/2}} }^{eqn 16A.12} \times \Delta t \times m\)

where\( A_0\) is the area of the hole and M is the molar mass. Rearrangement of this equation gives an expression for p:

\( p=\left(\frac{2\pi RT}{M} \right) ^{1/2} \mid \frac{\Delta m}{A_0\Delta t}\)

Substitution of the data and M = 132.9 g \(mol^{−1}\) gives

\( p=\left(\frac{2\pi \times (8.3145JK^{-1}mol^{-1})\times (773K)}{0.1329kg mol^{-1}} \right) ^{1/2} \times \frac{84.4\times 10^{-3 }kg}{\underbrace{\pi \times (0.50\times 10^{-3}m)^2}_{A_0}\times (3600 s) } =1.6 \times 10^4 Pa or 16kPa\)
