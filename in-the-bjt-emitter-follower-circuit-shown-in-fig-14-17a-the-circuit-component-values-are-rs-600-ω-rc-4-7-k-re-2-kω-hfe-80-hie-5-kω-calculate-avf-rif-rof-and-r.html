Guest Question.

In the BJT emitter follower circuit shown in Fig. 14.17(a), the circuit component values are \(R_{s}\) = 600 Ω, \(R_{C}\) = 4.7 k, \(R_{E}\) = 2 kΩ, \(h_{fe}\) = 80, \(h_{ie}\) = 5 kΩ. Calculate \(A_{Vf}, R_{if}, R_{of}\) and \(R^{\prime}_{of}\).

Admin
Solution

\(A_{V} =\frac{h_{fe}R_{E}}{R_{s} + h_{ie}}=\frac{80 × 2 × 10^{3}}{600 + 5 × 10^{3}}\)= 28.57

The desensitivity is given by

D = 1 + β\(A_{V} =\frac{R_{s} + h_{ie} +h_{fe}R_{E}}{R_{s} + h_{ie}}=\frac{600 + 5 × 10^{3} + 80 × 2 × 10^{3}}{600 + 5 × 10^{3}} \) = 29.57

where β = \(\frac{V_{f}}{V_{o}}\) = 1.

Then, the voltage gain with feedback can be written as

\(A_{Vf} =\frac{A_{V}}{D}=\frac{28.57}{29.57}\)= 0.966 ≈ 1

This unity gain shows that it is an emitter follower circuit.
From Fig. 14.17 (c), the input resistance without feedback is given by

\(R_{i} = R_{s} + h_{ie} = 600 + 5 × 10^{3}\) = 5.6 kΩ

Hence, for voltage series feedback amplifier, the input resistance with feedback increases due to series mixing at the input and it is given by

\(R_{if} = R_{i}D = 5.6 × 10^{3}\) × 29.57 = 165.59 kΩ

From Fig. 14.17(c), the output resistance of the amplifier with feedback, without considering the external load resistance (\(R_{L}\) = R) is given by

\(R_{of} =\frac{R_{o}}{1 + βA_{v}} =\frac{∞}{∞}\)

where \(R_{o}\) = ∞ and \(A_{V} = \underset{R_{L} → ∞}{lim}A_{V}\) = ∞. This indeterminacy can be resolved by first evaluating \(R^{\prime}_{of}\) and then apply the limit \(R_{L}\) → ∞.
The output resistance of the amplifier with feedback by considering the external load, can be written as

\(R^{\prime}_{of} =\frac{R^{\prime}_{o}}{D}= \frac{2 × 10^{3}}{29.57}\) = 67.64 Ω

where \(R^{\prime}_{o}= R_{L} = R_{E}\).
This shows that the output resistance decreases due to voltage sampling at the output.

\(R_{of} = \underset{R_{L} → ∞}{lim}R^{\prime}_{of} = \frac{600 + 5 × 10^{3}}{80}\) = 70 Ω.
