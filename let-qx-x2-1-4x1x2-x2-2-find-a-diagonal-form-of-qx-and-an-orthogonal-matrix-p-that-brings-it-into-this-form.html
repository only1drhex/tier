Guest Question.

Let \(\mathrm{Q}(\vec{x}) = x^2_1 + 4x_{1}x_{2} + x^2_2\). Find a diagonal form of \(\mathrm{Q}(\vec{x})\) and an orthogonal matrix P that brings it into this form.

Admin
Solution

The corresponding symmetric matrix is \(A = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 2 \\ 2 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\). We have

\(C(\lambda ) = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 – \lambda & 2 \\ 2 & 1 – \lambda \end{matrix} \right ] = (\lambda − 3)(\lambda + 1)\)

The eigenvalues are \(\lambda _1 = 3\) and \(\lambda _2 = −1\).
For \(\lambda _1 = 3\), we get

\(A − \lambda _{1}I = \left [ \begin{matrix} -2 & 2 \\ 2 & -2 \end{matrix} \right ] \sim \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

An eigenvector for \(\lambda _1\) is \(\vec{v_1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\), and a basis for the eigenspace is \(\left\{\vec{v_1}\right\}\).

For \(\lambda _2 = −1\), we get

\(A − \lambda _{2}I = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & 2 \\ 2 & 2 \end{matrix} \right ] \sim \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

An eigenvector for \(\lambda _2\) is \(\vec{v_2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\), and a basis for the eigenspace is \(\left\{\vec{v_2}\right\}\).

Therefore, we see that A is orthogonally diagonalized by \(P = \frac{1}{√2} \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\) to \(D = \left [ \begin{matrix} 3 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{matrix} \right ]\). Thus, by Theorem 8.2.1(Let \(A \in M_{n \times n} (\mathbb{R})\) be a symmetric matrix and let \(\mathrm{Q}(\vec{x}) = \vec{x}^{T} A\vec{x}\). If P is an orthogonal matrix that diagonalizes A, then the change of variables \(\vec{x} = P\vec{y}\) brings \(\mathrm{Q}(\vec{x})\) into diagonal form. In particular, we get

\(\mathrm{Q}(\vec{x}) = \lambda_{1} y^2_1 + . . . \lambda _{n} y^2_n\)

where \(\lambda_{1}, . . . , \lambda_{n}\) are the eigenvalues of A corresponding to the columns of P.) we get that the change of variables \(\vec{x} = P\vec{y}\) brings \(\mathrm{Q}(\vec{x})\) into the form

\(\mathrm{Q}(\vec{x}) = 3y^2_1 – y^2_2\)
