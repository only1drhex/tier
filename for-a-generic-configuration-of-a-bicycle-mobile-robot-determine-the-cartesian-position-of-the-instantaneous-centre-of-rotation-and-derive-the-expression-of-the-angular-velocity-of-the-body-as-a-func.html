Guest Question.

For a generic configuration of a bicycle mobile robot, determine the Cartesian position of the instantaneous centre of rotation, and derive the expression of the angular velocity of the body as a function of the robot configuration q and of the modulus of the velocity of rear wheel centre. In theparticular case of the rear-wheel drive bicycle, show that such expression is consistent with the evolution of θ predicted by the kinematic model (11.19). Moreover, compute the velocity \(\upsilon_{p}\) of a generic point P on the robot chassis.

\( \left [ \begin{matrix}\dot{x} \\ \dot{y} \\ \dot{\theta } \\ \dot{\phi } \end{matrix} \right ]\) = \( \left [ \begin{matrix}\cos \theta \\\sin \theta \\ \tan {\phi }/{\ell } \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \upsilon + \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\omega\) (11.19)

Admin
Solution

With reference to Fig. 11.4, denote by (\( x_{C} \) , \( y_{C} \)) the Cartesian coordinates of the instantaneous centre of rotation C in the world reference frame, and by (\( x^{\prime }_{C}\) ,\( y^{\prime }_{C}\)) its coordinates in a moving frame having the origin at the centre of the rear wheel and the \( x^{\prime} \) axis aligned with the bicycle body. Simple geometry gives

\(\left[\begin{matrix} x^{\prime }_{c} \\ y^{\prime }_{c} \end{matrix} \right] \) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ {\ell}/{\tan {\phi }} \end{matrix} \right]\)

and thus

\(\left[\begin{matrix} x_{c} \\ y_{c} \end{matrix} \right] \) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} x \\ y \end{matrix} \right] + R\left(\theta \right) \left[\begin{matrix} x^{\prime }_{c} \\ y^{\prime }_{c} \end{matrix} \right]\) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} x – \ell {\sin \theta}/{\tan \theta} \\y + \ell {\cos \theta}/{\tan \theta} \end{matrix} \right]\)

where \( R\left(\theta \right)\) = \(\left[\begin{matrix} \cos \theta & -\sin \theta \\ \sin \theta & \cos \theta \end{matrix} \right] \)

is the (planar) rotation matrix of the moving frame with respect to the world frame.
Since the velocity vector of a generic point P on the robot body is tangent to the circle centred at C and passing through P, the modulus of the angular velocity of the bicycle is obtained as

\(\omega_{body} \)= \({\upsilon_{P} }/{R_{P} }\) (S11.3)

where \(\upsilon_{p}\) is the modulus of the velocity of P, and \(R_{P}\) is its instantaneous radius of rotation (i.e., its radius of curvature):

\(R_{P}\) = \(\sqrt{\left(x_{P} – x_{C}\right)^{2} + \left(y_{P} – y_{C}\right)^{2} }\)

The resulting \(\omega_{body} \) is obviously the same for any choice of P . In particular, by choosing P as the centre of the rear wheel, one has \(R_{P}\) = \({\ell } /{\tan\phi }\), and thus

\(\omega_{body} \) = \(\dot{\theta } \) = \(\upsilon {\tan\phi } /{\ell}\)

consistently with the evolution of θ predicted by the kinematic model (11.19). Finally, plugging this expression of ω in (S11.3) yields

\(\upsilon_{P}\) = \(R_{p} \upsilon {\tan\phi }/{\ell}\)
