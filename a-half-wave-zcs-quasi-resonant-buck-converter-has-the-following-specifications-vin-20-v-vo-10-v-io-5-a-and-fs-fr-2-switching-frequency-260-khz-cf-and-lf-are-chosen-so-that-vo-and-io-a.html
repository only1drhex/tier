Guest Question.

A half-wave ZCS quasi-resonant buck converter has the following specifications:

\(V_{in} = 20 V, \overline{V}_o = 10 V, \overline{I}_o = 5 A\), and \(f_s = \frac{f_R}{2}\) = switching frequency = 260 kHz. \(C_F\) and \(L_F\) are chosen so that \(v_o\) and \(i_o\) are pure dc.

Assuming ideal components perform the following:

a) Calculate the resonant capacitance and inductance.

b) Calculate the time durations of the modes.

c) Calculate the peak resonant current and voltage.

d) Verify the theoretical results with the respective simulation ones.

Admin
Solution

a) \(f_R = 2 \times f_s = 2 \times 260 kHz = 520 kHz\)

or \(\omega_R = 2\pi f_R = 2\pi \times 520 \times 10^3 = 3267.25 \times 10^3 \frac{rads}{s}\)

The values of the resonant components can be calculated using the equation

\(\omega_R = \frac{1}{\sqrt{L_RC_R}}\)

Therefore, choosing \(L_R = 1 \mu H\) the value of the resonant capacitor is:

\(C_R = \frac{1}{L_R(\omega_R)^2} =\frac{1}{1 \times 10^{-6}(3267.25 \times 10^3)^2} = 0.093 \mu F\)

 

\(|Z_R|\) = characteristic impedance of the resonant circuit

\(= \sqrt{\frac{L_R}{C_R}} = \sqrt{\frac{1 \times 10^{-6}}{0.093 \times 10^{-6}}} = 3.28 \Omega\)

 

\(\overline{I}_o \leq \frac{V_{in}}{|Z_R|}\) \(\frac{V_{in}}{|Z_R|} = \frac{20}{3.28} = 6.1 A\)

Therefore, from the above specifications \(\overline{I}_o = 5 A\), then the above relation is satisfied.

b) \(T_s\) = switching period =\(\frac{1}{f_s} =\frac{1}{260 \times 10^3} =3.846 \mu s\)

 

\(\tau_1 = \frac{\overline{I}_o L_R}{V_{in}} = \frac{5 \times 1 \times 10^{-6}}{20} = 0.25 \mu s\)

 

\(\tau_2 = \frac{1}{\omega_R}[\pi + \sin^{-1}(\frac{|Z_R| \overline{I}_o}{V_{in}})]\)

 

\(= \frac{1}{3267.25 \times 10^3}(\pi + \sin^{-1}(\frac{3.28 \times 5}{20})) = 1.26 \mu s\)

 

\(\tau_3 = \frac{C_R V_{in}[1 – \cos \omega_R \tau_2]}{\overline{I}_o}\)

 

\(= \frac{0.093 \times 10^{-6} \times 20[1 – \cos 3267.25 \times 10^3 \times 1.26 \times 10^{-6}]}{5} = 0.58 \mu s\)

 

\(\tau_3 = \frac{C_R V_{in}}{\overline{I}_o}[1 + \sqrt{1 – (\frac{|Z_R| \overline{I}_o}{V_{in}})^2}] = \frac{20 \times 0.093 \times 10^{-6}}{5}[1 + \sqrt{1 – (\frac{3.28 \times 5}{20})^2}]\)

 

\(=(0.372 \times 10^{-6})(1.57) = 0.58 \mu s\)

 

\(\tau_4 = T_s – (\tau_1 + \tau_2 + \tau_3) = 3.846 \mu s – (0.25 + 1.26 + 0.58) \mu s = 1.76 \mu s\)

c) \(\overline{V}_o = V_{in} f_s [\frac{\tau_1}{2} +\tau_2 + \tau_3]\)

 

\(= 20 \times 260 \times 10^3(0.25 + 1.26 + 0.58) \times 10^{-6} = 10.87 V\)

d) The peak resonant current is:

\(\widehat{I}_{LR}\) = peak resonant current =\(\overline{I}_o + \frac{V_{in}}{|Z_R|} = 5 + \frac{20}{3.28} = 11.098 A\)

From Eq. (\(\tau_1 = t_1 – t_0 =\frac{\overline{I}_o L_R}{V_{in}}\)) the maximum peak resonant voltage occurs when \(\omega_R(t – t_1) = 2.1415 rad\) and is given by:

\(\widehat{V}_{LR}\) = peak resonant current =\(2V_{in} = 40 V\)

e) The simulation results of this example are presented in Fig. 7.61 which are in close agreement with the respective theoretical ones.
