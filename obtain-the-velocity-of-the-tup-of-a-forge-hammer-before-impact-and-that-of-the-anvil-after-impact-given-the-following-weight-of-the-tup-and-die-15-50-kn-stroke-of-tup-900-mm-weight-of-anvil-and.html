Guest Question.

Obtain the velocity of the tup of a forge hammer before impact and that of the anvil after impact, given the following:

weight of the tup and die = 15.50 kN

stroke of tup = 900 mm

weight of anvil and frame = 342 kN

efficiency of drop = 0.9

coefficient of restitution = 0.5

steam pressure = 700 kN/\(m ^{2}\)

area of piston = 0.129 \(m ^{2}\)

Determine the amplitudes of vibration of the foundation and anvil if the limiting natural frequency of the anvil is 253 rad/s, and the two natural frequencies of the combined system are 272 rad/s and 54.3 rad/s.

Admin
Solution

Since stream pressure is given, this is a double-acting hammer.
The correction factor, α = 0.9 (the efficiency of drop).
Velocity of the tup before impact

\(\upsilon =\alpha \sqrt{\frac{2 g\left(W_{t}+p \cdot a\right) h}{W_{t}}}\)

Substituting \(\alpha=0.9, W_{t}=15.50 kN , p=700 kN / m ^{2}, a=0.129 m ^{2}\) , and h = 900 mm = 0.9 m,
We have

\(\upsilon=0.9 \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 9.81(15.50+700 \times 0.129) \times 0.9}{15.50}} m / s\)

= 9.88 m/s

Velocity of anvil after impact

\(\upsilon_{a}=\frac{(1+e)}{\left(1+\lambda_{a}\right)} \upsilon \text { where } \lambda_{a}=\frac{M_{a}}{M_{t}}=\frac{W_{a}}{W_{t}}=\frac{342}{15.5}=22.06\)

Substituting \(e=0.5, \lambda_{a}=22.06, \text { and } \upsilon=9.88 m / s\)

we have

\(\upsilon_{a}=\frac{(1+0.5)}{(1+22.06)} \times 9.88=0.643 m / s\)

Amplitude of the vibration of the foundation

\(A_{1}=-\frac{\left(\omega_{a}^{2}-\omega_{n 2}^{2}\right)\left(\omega_{a}^{2}-\omega_{n 1}^{2}\right)}{\omega_{a}^{2}\left(\omega_{n 1}^{2}-\omega_{n 2}^{2}\right) \omega_{n 2}} \upsilon_{a}\)

Substituting \(\omega_{a}=253 rad / s , \omega_{n 1}=272 rad / s , \omega_{n 2}=54.3 rad / s\),

and \(\upsilon_{a}=0.643 m / s\),

\(A_{1}=-\frac{\left(253^{2}-54.3^{2}\right)\left(253^{2}-272^{2}\right)}{253^{2}\left(272^{2}-54.3^{3}\right) 54.3} \times 0.643 m =1.58 mm\)

Amplitude of vibration of the anvil

\(\begin{aligned}A_{a} &=-\frac{\left(\omega_{a}^{2}-\omega_{n 1}^{2}\right)}{\left(\omega_{n 1}^{2}-\omega_{n 2}^{2}\right) \omega_{n 2}} v_{a} \\&=-\frac{\left(253^{2}-272^{2}\right)}{\left(272^{2}-54.3^{2}\right)(54.3)} \times 0.643 m =1.66 mm\end{aligned}\)
