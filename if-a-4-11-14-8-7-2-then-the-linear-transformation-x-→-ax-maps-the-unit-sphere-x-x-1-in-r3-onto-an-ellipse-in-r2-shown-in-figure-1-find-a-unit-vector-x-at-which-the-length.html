Guest Question.

If \( A = \left [ \begin{matrix} 4 & 11 & 14 \\ 8 & 7 & -2 \end{matrix} \right ] \) , then the linear transformation \( x \mapsto Ax \) maps the unit sphere \( \{x : \left\|x\right\| = 1\} \) in \( \mathbb{R} ^{3} \) onto an ellipse in \( \mathbb{R} ^{2} \) , shown in Figure 1. Find a unit vector x at which the length \( \left\|Ax\right\| \) is maximized, and compute this maximum length.

Admin
Solution

The quantity \( \left\|Ax\right\|^{2} \) is maximized at the same x that maximizes \( \left\|Ax\right\| \) , and \( \left\|Ax\right\|^{2} \) is easier to study. Observe that

\( \left\|Ax\right\|^{2} = (Ax)^{T}(Ax) =x^{T}A^{T}Ax = x^{T}(A^{T}A)x \)

Also, \( A^{T}A \) is a symmetric matrix, since \( (A^{T}A)^{T} = A^{T}A^{TT} = A^{T}A \). So the problem now is to maximize the quadratic form \( x^{T}(A^{T}A)x \) subject to the constraint \( \left\|x\right\| = 1 \). By Theorem 6 in Section 7.3, the maximum value is the greatest eigenvalue \( \lambda_{1} \) of \( A^{T}A \). Also, the maximum value is attained at a unit eigenvector of \( A^{T}A \) corresponding to \( \lambda_{1} \).

For the matrix A in this example,

\( A^{T}A = \left [ \begin{matrix} 4 & 8 \\ 11 & 7 \\ 14 & -2 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} 4 & 11 & 14 \\ 8 & 7 & -2 \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 80 & 100 & 40 \\ 100 & 170 & 140 \\ 40 & 140 & 200 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

The eigenvalues of \( A^{T}A \) are \( \lambda_{1} = 360 , \lambda_{2} = 90 , \text{and} \lambda_{3} = 0 \). Corresponding unit eigenvectors are, respectively,

\( v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1/3 \\ 2/3 \\ 2/3 \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} -2/3 \\ -1/3 \\ 2/3 \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{3} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2/3 \\ -2/3 \\ 1/3 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

The maximum value of \( \left\|Ax\right\|^{2} \) is 360, attained when x is the unit vector \( v_{1} \). The vector \( Av_{1} \) is a point on the ellipse in Figure 1 farthest from the origin, namely,

\( Av_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 4 & 11 & 14 \\ 8 & 7 & -2 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} 1/3 \\ 2/3 \\ 2/3 \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 18 \\ 6 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

For \( \left\|x\right\| = 1 \), the maximum value of \( \left\|Ax\right\| \) is \( \left\|Av_{1}\right\| = \sqrt{360} = 6\sqrt{10} \) .

THEOREM 6 :

Let A be a symmetric matrix, and define m and M as in (2). Then M is the greatest eigenvalue \( \lambda_{1} \) of A and m is the least eigenvalue of A. The value of \( x^{T}Ax \) is M when x is a unit eigenvector \( u_{1} \) corresponding to M. The value of \( x^{T}Ax \) is m when x is a unit eigenvector corresponding to m.

\( m = min\{x^{T}Ax : \left\|x\right\| = 1\} , M = max\{x^{T}Ax : \left\|x\right\| = 1\}\) (2)
