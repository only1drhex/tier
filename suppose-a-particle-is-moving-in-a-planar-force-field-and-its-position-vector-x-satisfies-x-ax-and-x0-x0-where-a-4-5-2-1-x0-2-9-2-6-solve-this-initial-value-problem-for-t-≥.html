Guest Question.

Suppose a particle is moving in a planar force field and its position vector x satisfies \( \acute{x} = Ax \) and \( x(0) = x_{0} \), where

\( A = \left [ \begin{matrix} 4 & -5 \\ -2 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] , x_{0} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2.9 \\ 2.6 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Solve this initial value problem for t ≥ 0, and sketch the trajectory of the particle.

Admin
Solution

The eigenvalues of A turn out to be \( \lambda_{1} = 6 \) and \( \lambda_{2} = -1 \), with corresponding eigenvectors \( v_{1} = (-5,2) \) and \( v_{2} = (1,1) \). For any constants \( c_{1} \) and \( c_{2} \), the function

\( x(t) = c_{1}v_{1}e^{\lambda_{1}t} + c_{2}v_{2}e^{\lambda_{2}t} = c_{1} \left [ \begin{matrix} -5 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right ]e^{6t} + c_{2}\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ]e^{-t} \)

is a solution of \( \acute{x} = Ax \). We want \( c_{1} \) and \( c_{2} \) to satisfy \( x(0) = x_{0} \), that is,

\( c_{1} \left [ \begin{matrix} -5 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right ] + c_{2} \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2.9 \\ 2.6 \end{matrix} \right ] \text{or} \left [ \begin{matrix} -5 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} c_{1} \\ c_{2} \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2.9 \\ 2.6 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Calculations show that \( c_{1} = -3/70 \) and \( c_{2} = 188/70 \), and so the desired function is

\( x(t) = \frac{-3}{70}\left [ \begin{matrix} -5 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right ]e^{6t} + \frac{188}{70}\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ] e^{-t} \)
