Guest Question.

The clay specimen (diameter \(38 \mathrm{~mm}\) and height of \(76 \mathrm{~mm}\) ) described in worked-out is then subjected to triaxial compression, and the results obtained are given in Table 7.9.

Table 7.9 Undrained triaxial compression test results.

0 300 148

Solution

a. Axial strain can be computed considering the initial height of the specimen (i.e. \(\left.h_{0}=76 \mathrm{~mm}\right)\) as follows:

\(\varepsilon_{a}=\frac{\Delta h}{h_{0}}\)

Deviator stress can be calculated as follows:

\(\left(\sigma_{1}^{\prime}-\sigma_{3}^{\prime}\right)=\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)\)

And excess pore water pressures can be computed considering the current pore water pressure \((u)\) and initial pore water pressure \(\left(u_{0}\right)\) :

\(\Delta u=u-u_{0}\)

Then the data in Table \(7.10\) can be expressed in terms of axial strain, deviator stress and excess pore water pressure as follows, and graphical representation is shown in Figure 7.12.

\(\Delta u=B\left[\Delta \sigma_{3}+A\left(\Delta \sigma_{1}-\Delta \sigma_{3}\right)\right]\)

Since the all-round confining pressure produces a pore pressure \(u=B \times \sigma_{3}\), and since we are considering the condition after shearing begins, the excess pore pressure is considered, then:

\(\begin{aligned} &A=\frac{\frac{\Delta u}{B}-\Delta \sigma_{3}}{\left(\Delta \sigma_{1}-\Delta \sigma_{3}\right)} \\ &A=\frac{\Delta u}{\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)}\end{aligned}\)
