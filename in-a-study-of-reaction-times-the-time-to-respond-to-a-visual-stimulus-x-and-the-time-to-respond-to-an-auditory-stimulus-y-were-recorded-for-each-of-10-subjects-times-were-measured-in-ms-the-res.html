Guest Question.

In a study of reaction times, the time to respond to a visual stimulus \((x)\) and the time to respond to an auditory stimulus \((y)\) were recorded for each of 10 subjects. Times were measured in ms. The results are presented in the following table.

x 161 203 235 176 201 188 228 211 191 178
y 159 206 241 163 197 193 209 189 169 201
Admin
Solution

Using Equation (7.1), we compute the sample correlation, obtaining \(r=0.8159\). Next we use Equation (7.4) to compute the quantity \(W\) :

\( r=\frac{1}{n-1} \sum_{i=1}^{n}\left(\frac{x_{i}-\bar{x}}{s_{x}}\right)\left(\frac{y_{i}-\bar{y}}{s_{y}}\right) \) (7.1)

\(\begin{aligned}W &=\frac{1}{2} \ln \frac{1+r}{1-r} (7.4) \\ &=\frac{1}{2} \ln \frac{1+0.8159}{1-0.8159} \\ &=1.1444\end{aligned}\)

Since \(W\) is normally distributed with known standard deviation \(\sigma=\sqrt{1 /(10-3)}=\) \(0.3780\) (Equation 7.6), a \(95 \%\) confidence interval for \(\mu_{W}\) is given by

\(\sigma_{W}^{2}=\frac{1}{n-3}\) (7.6)

\(\begin{aligned}1.1444-1.96(0.3780) &<\mu_{W}<1.1444+1.96(0.3780) \\0.4036 &<\mu_{W}<1.8852\end{aligned}\)

To obtain a \(95 \%\) confidence interval for \(\rho\) we transform the inequality using Equation (7.7), obtaining

\(\rho=\frac{e^{2 \mu_{W}}-1}{e^{2 \mu_{W}}+1}\) (7.7)

\(\begin{gathered}\frac{e^{2(0.4036)}-1}{e^{2(0.4036)}+1}<\frac{e^{2 \mu_{W}}-1}{e^{2 \mu_{W}}+1}<\frac{e^{2(1.8852)}-1}{e^{2(1.8852)}+1} \\ 0.383<\rho<0.955 \end{gathered}\)
