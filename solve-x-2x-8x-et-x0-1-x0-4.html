Guest Question.

Solve \(\ddot{x}+2 \dot{x}-8 x=e^{t} ; x(0)=1, \dot{x}(0)=-4\).

Admin
Solution

From , this initial-value problem is equivalent to Eq. (26.1) with

\(x (t)=\left[\begin{array}{l}x_{1}(t) \\x_{2}(t)\end{array}\right] \quad A =\left[\begin{array}{lr}0 & 1 \\8 & -2\end{array}\right] \quad f (t)=\left[\begin{array}{l}0 \\e^{i}\end{array}\right] \quad c =\left[\begin{array}{r}1 \\-4\end{array}\right]\)

and \(t_{0}=0\). The solution is given by either Eq. (26.2) or (26.3). Here, we use (26.2); the solution using (26.3) is found in . For this \(A , e^{ A t}\) has already been calculated in . Therefore,

\(e^{ A \left(t-t_{0}\right)} c =e^{ A t} c =\frac{1}{6}\left[\begin{array}{cc}4 e^{2 t}+2 e^{-4 t} & e^{2 t}-e^{-4 t} \\8 e^{2 t}-8 e^{-4 t} & 2 e^{2 t}+4 e^{-4 t}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}1 \\-4\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}e^{-4 t} \\-4 e^{-4 t}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(e^{- A s} f (s)=\frac{1}{6}\left[\begin{array}{cc}4 e^{-2 s}+2 e^{4 s} & e^{-2 s}-e^{4 s} \\8 e^{-2 s}-8 e^{4 s} & 2 e^{-2 s}+4 e^{4 s}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}0 \\e^{s}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}\frac{1}{6} e^{-s}-\frac{1}{6} e^{5 s} \\\frac{2}{6} e^{-s}+\frac{4}{6} e^{5 s}\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\int_{t_{0}}^{t} e^{- A s} f (s) d s=\left[\begin{array}{l}\int_{0}^{t}\left(\frac{1}{6} e^{-s}-\frac{1}{6} e^{5 s}\right) d s \\\int_{0}^{t}\left(\frac{1}{3} e^{-s}+\frac{2}{3} e^{5 s}\right) d s\end{array}\right]=\frac{1}{30}\left[\begin{array}{c}-5 e^{-t}-e^{5 t}+6 \\-10 e^{-t}+4 e^{5 t}+6\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}e^{ A t} \int_{t_{0}}^{t} e^{- A s} f (s) d s&=\left(\frac{1}{6}\right)\left(\frac{1}{30}\right)\left[\begin{array}{cc}4 e^{2 t}+2 e^{-4 t} & e^{2 t}-e^{-4 t} \\8 e^{2 t}-8 e^{-4 t} & 2 e^{2 t}+4 e^{-4 t}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}-5 e^{-t}-e^{5 t}+6 \\-10 e^{-t}+4 e^{5 t}+6\end{array}\right] \\&=\frac{1}{180}\left[\begin{array}{c}\left(4 e^{2 t}+2 e^{-4 t}\right)\left(-5 e^{-t}-e^{5 t}+6\right)+\left(e^{2 t}-e^{-4 t}\right)\left(-10 e^{-t}+4 e^{5 t}+6\right) \\\left(8 e^{2 t}-8 e^{-4 t}\right)\left(-5 e^{-t}-e^{5 t}+6\right)+\left(2 e^{2 t}+4 e^{-4 t}\right)\left(-10 e^{-t}+4 e^{5 t}+6\right)\end{array}\right] \\&=\frac{1}{30}\left[\begin{array}{c}-6 e^{t}+5 e^{2 t}+e^{-4 t} \\-6 e^{t}+10 e^{2 t}-4 e^{-4 t}\end{array}\right]\end{aligned}\)

Thus,

\(\begin{aligned}x (t) &=e^{ A \left(t-t_{0}\right)} c +e^{ A t} \int_{t_{0}}^{t} e^{- A s} f (s) d s \\&=\left[\begin{array}{c}e^{-4 t} \\-4 e^{-4 t}\end{array}\right]+\frac{1}{30}\left[\begin{array}{c}-6 e^{\prime}+5 e^{2 t}+e^{-4 t} \\-6 e^{\prime}+10 e^{2 t}-4 e^{-4 t}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}\frac{31}{30} e^{-4 t}+\frac{1}{6} e^{2 t}-\frac{1}{5} e^{t} \\-\frac{62}{15} e^{-4 t}+\frac{1}{3} e^{2 t}-\frac{1}{5} e^{t}\end{array}\right]\end{aligned}\)

and \(x(t)=x_{1}(t)=\frac{31}{30} e^{-4 t}+\frac{1}{6} e^{2 t}-\frac{1}{5} e^{t}\)

Eq. (26.1) : \(\dot{ x }(t)= A x (t)+ f (t) ; \quad x \left(t_{0}\right)= c\)

Eq. (26.2) : \(x (t)=e^{ A \left(t-t_{0}\right)} c +e^{ A t} \int_{t_{0}}^{t} e^{- A s} f (s) d s\)

Eq. (26.3) : \(x (t)=e^{ A \left(t-t_{0}\right)} c +\int_{t_{0}}^{t} e^{ A (t-s)} f (s) d s\)
