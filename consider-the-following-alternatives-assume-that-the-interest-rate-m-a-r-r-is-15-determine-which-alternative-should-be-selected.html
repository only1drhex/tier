Guest Question.

Consider the following alternatives:

Alternative 1 Alternative 2 Alternative 3
First cost (₹) 1,40,000 90,000 1,20,000
Net cash flow per year (₹/year) +27,500 +12,000 +24,000
Salvage value (₹) +7,500 0 +2,500
Useful life (years) 10 10 10

Solution

Solution of Example 6.4 by the Present Worth (P.W.) Method P.W. of alternative 1 is determined as:

 

\(\begin{array}{l}( P . W .)_{1}=-₹ 1,40,000+₹ 27,500(P / A, 15 \%, 10)+₹ 7,500(P / F, 15 \%, 10) \\( P . W .)_{1}=-₹ 1,40,000+₹ 27,500(5.0188)+₹ 7,500(0.2472) \\( P . W .)_{1}=-₹ 129\end{array}\)

 

P.W. of alternative 2 is determined as:

 

\(\begin{array}{l}( P . W .)_{2}=-₹ 90,000+₹ 12,000(P / A, 15 \%, 10) \\( P . W .)_{2}=-₹ 00,000+₹ 12,000(5.0188) \\( P . W .)_{2}=-₹ 29,774.4\end{array}\)

 

P.W. of alternative 3 is determined as:

 

\(\begin{array}{l}( P . W .)_{3}=-₹ 1,20,000+₹ 24,000(P / A, 15 \%, 10)+₹ 2,500(P / F, 15 \%, 10) \\( P . W .)_{3}=-₹ 1,20,000+₹ 24,000(5.0188)+₹ 2,500(0.2472) \\( P . W .)_{3}=+₹ 1,069.2\end{array}\)

 

On comparing P.W. of the three alternatives, it is found that P.W. of alternative 3 is maximum and therefore, alternative 3 should be chosen.

Solution of Example 6.4 by the Internal Rate of Return (I.R.R.) Method Let us consider the increment \(2 \rightarrow 3\) and compute I.R.R. by finding A.W. for the two alternatives at an unknown \(\dot{i}\) and equating them.

 

\(-₹ 90,000\left(A / P, i^{\prime} \%, 10\right)+₹ 12,000=-₹ 1,20,000\left(A / P, i^{\prime} \%, 10\right)+₹ 24,000+₹ 2,500(A / F \text {, }\)

 

Linear interpolation between 35% and 40% results in \(i^{\prime}=38.58 \%\), which is the I.R.R. Since 38.58% > 15%, the increment \(2 \rightarrow 3\) is justified. Thus, alternative 3 is better than alternative 2 and it should be selected.

Next increment considered is \(3 \rightarrow 1\). The I.R.R. is obtained by equating A.W. of the alternatives at an unknown i’.

 

\(\begin{array}{l}\quad ₹ ₹ 1,20,000(A / P, i ' \%, 10)+₹ 24,000+₹ 2,500\left(A / F, i^{\prime} \%, 10\right)=-₹ 1,40,000(A / P, i \% \%, 10) \\+₹ 27,500+₹ 7,500\left(A / F, i^{\prime} \%, 10\right)\end{array}\)

 

Linear interpolation between 13% and 14% results in i’ = 13.53%, which is the I.R.R.

Since 13.53% < 15%, the increment \(3 \rightarrow 1\) is not justified. Thus, alternative 3 is better than alternative 1 and it should be selected.
