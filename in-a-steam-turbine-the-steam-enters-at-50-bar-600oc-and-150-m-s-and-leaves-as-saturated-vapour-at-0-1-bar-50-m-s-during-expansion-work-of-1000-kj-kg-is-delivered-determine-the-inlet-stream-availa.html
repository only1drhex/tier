Guest Question.

In a steam turbine the steam enters at 50 bar, 600ºC and 150 m/s and leaves as saturated vapour at 0.1 bar, 50 m/s. During expansion, work of 1000 kJ/kg is delivered. Determine the inlet stream availability, exit stream availability and the irreversibility. Take dead state temperature as 25ºC.

Admin
Solution
\(h_{1} = h_{\text{at 50 bar, 600ºC}} = 3666.5 kJ/kg, s_{1} = s_{\text{at 50 bar, 600ºC}} = 7.2589 kJ/kg · K\\h_{2} = h_{\text{g at 0.1 bar}} = 2584.7 kJ/kg, s_{2} = s_{\text{g at 0.1 bar}} = 8.1502 kJ/kg · K\\\begin{aligned}\text{Inlet stream availability}&= \left\lgroup h_{1} + \frac{c_{1}^{2}}{2} \right\rgroup – T_{0}s_{1}\\&= \left\lgroup 3666.5 + \frac{(150)^{2} × 10^{-3}}{2} \right\rgroup – (298 × 7.2589)\\&= 1514.59 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Input stream availability is equal to the input absolute availability.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Exit stream availability}&= \left\lgroup h_{2} + \frac{c_{2}^{2}}{2} \right\rgroup – T_{0}s_{2}\\&= \left\lgroup 2584.7 + \frac{(50)^{2} × 10^{-3}}{2} \right\rgroup – (298 × 8.1502))\\&= 157.19 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{Exit stream availability is equal to the exit absolute availability.}\\\begin{aligned}W_{rev} &= 1514.59 – 157.19 = 1357.4 kJ/kg\\\text{Irreversibility}&= W_{rev} – W = 1357.4 – 1000 = 357.4 kJ/kg\end{aligned}\\\text{This irreversibility is in fact the availability loss.}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Inlet stream availability}&= \textbf{1514.59 kJ/kg}\\\text{Exit stream availability}&= \textbf{157.19 kJ/kg}\\\text{Irreversibility}&= \textbf{357.4 kJ/kg}\end{aligned}\)
