Guest Question.

A system comprising of a gas of 5 kg mass undergoes expansion process from 1 MPa and 0.5 \(\textbf{m}^{\textbf{3}}\) to 0.5 MPa. Expansion process is governed by, p.\(\textbf{v}^{\textbf{1.3}}\) = constant. The internal energy of gas is given by, u = 1.8 pv + 85, kJ/kg. Here ‘u’ is specific internal energy, ‘p’ is pressure in kPa, ‘v’ is specific volume in \(\textbf{m}^{\textbf{3}}\)/kg. Determine heat and work interaction and change in internal energy.

Admin
Solution
\(\text{Given mass of gas, m = 5 kg, }pv^{1.3} = \text{constant}\\\text{Assuming expansion to be quasi-static, the work may be given as,}\\\begin{aligned} W&= m\int{p . dv} \\&= \frac{p_{2}V_{2} – p_{1}V_{1}}{(1 – n)}\end{aligned}\\\text{From internal energy relation, change in specific internal energy,}\\\begin{aligned}Δu&= u_{2} – u_{1} = 1.8 (p_{2}v_{2} – p_{1}v_{1}), kJ/kg\\\text{Total change,} ΔU&= 1.8 × m × (p_{2}v_{2} – p_{1}v_{1}), kJ\\ΔU&= 1.8 × (p_{2}V_{2} – p_{1}V_{1})\\\text{Between states 1 and 2,} & \\p_{1}V_{1}^{1.3}&= p_{2}V_{2}^{1.3}\\\text{or} p_{1}V_{1}^{1.3}&= p_{2}V_{2}^{1.3}\\\Rightarrow V_{2}&= (0.5) . \left\lgroup\frac{1}{0.5} \right\rgroup ^{1/1.3}\\V_{2}&= 0.852 m^{3}\\\text{Total change in internal energy, ΔU}&= –133.2 kJ\\\text{Work,} W&= \frac{(0.5 × 0.852 – 1 × 0.5) × 10^{3}}{(1 – 1.3)}\\W&= 246.67 kJ\\\text{From first law,} &\\ΔQ&= ΔU + W\\&= – 133.2 + 246.7\\ΔQ&= 113.5 kJ\\\text{Heat interaction}&= \textbf{113.5 kJ}\\\text{Work interaction}&= \textbf{246.7 kJ}\\\text{Change in internal energy}&= \textbf{–133.2 kJ}\end{aligned}\)
