Guest Question.

Methane gas at 550 K and 5 bar undergoes a reversible adiabatic expansion to 1 bar. Assuming ideal-gas-state methane at these conditions, find its final temperature.

Admin
Solution

For this process \(\Delta S^{ig} = 0\), and Eq. (5.14) becomes:

\( \frac{\Delta S^{ig}}{R}=\frac{\left\langle C^{ig}_{P}\right\rangle_{S} }{R}\ln \frac{T}{T_{0}}-\ln \frac{P}{P_{0}}\) (5.14)

 

\( \frac{\left\langle C^{ig}_{P}\right\rangle_{S} }{R}\ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}=\ln \frac{1}{5}=-1.6094\)

Because \(\left\langle C^{ig}_{P}\right\rangle_{S}\) depends on \(T_{2}\), we rearrange this equation for iterative solution:

\( \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\frac{-1.6094}{\left\langle C^{ig}_{P}\right\rangle_{S}/R }\) or \(T_{2}=T_{1}\exp \left(\frac{-1.6094}{\left\langle C^{ig}_{P}\right\rangle_{S}/R } \right)\)

\( \frac{\left\langle C^{ig}_{P}\right\rangle_{S} }{R}=A+\left[B+\left(C+\frac{D}{T^{2}_{0}T^{2}} \right)\left(\frac{T+T_{0}}{2} \right) \right]\frac{T-T_{0}}{\ln (T/T_{0})}\) (5.13)

For an initial value of \(T_{2} \lt 550,\) compute a value of \(\left\langle C^{ig}_{P}\right\rangle_{S}/R\) for substitution into the equation for \(T_{2}\). This new value of \(T_{2}\) allows recalculation of \(\left\langle C^{ig}_{P}\right\rangle_{S}/R\) , and the process continues to convergence on a final value of \(T_{2}=411.34 K.\) As with a trial procedure is an alternative approach, with Microsoft Excel’s Goal Seekproviding a prototypical automated version.
