Guest Question.

Let us find the current \( I_{o} \) in the network in Fig. 5.13a using Thévenin’s theorem.

Admin
Solution

\( V_{ oc }\) is determined from the network shown in Fig. 5.13b. The presence of the three loops indicates three equations are required, together with the constraint equation for the dependent source. Since two of the currents are shown to go directly through the current sources, two of the necessary equations are

\(I_{1}=\frac{4}{ k }\)
\(I_{3}=2 I_{x}^{\prime}\)

The third equation is obtained by applying KVL around the two upper loops; that is,

\( 1 k \left(I_{1}+I_{2}\right)+12+1 k \left(I_{2}-I_{3}\right)+1 k \left(I_{1}+I_{2}-I_{3}\right)=0 \)

And the constraint equation for the dependent source is

\( I_{x}^{\prime}=I_{2}-2 I_{x}^{\prime} \)

Solving these equations yields

\(I_{2}=-\frac{12}{ k } A\)
\(I_{3}=-\frac{8}{ k } A \)

Then \( V_{ oc }\) can be determined from the KVL equation

\( -6+1 k \left(I_{3}-I_{2}-I_{1}\right)+V_{ oc }=0 \)

which yields \( V_{ oc }\) = 6 V.
Because of the presence of the dependent source, \( R_{ Th }\) must be determined from the equation

\( R_{ Th }=\frac{V_{ oc }}{I_{ sc }} \)

\( I_{ sc }\) is derived from the circuit in Fig. 5.13c. The node equation for the supernode is

\( \frac{\left(V_{1}-6\right)}{1 k }+\frac{4}{ k } +\frac{\left(V_{1}-12\right)}{1 k }+\frac{2\left(V_{1}-12\right)}{1 k }=0\)

which yields \( V_{1}=19 / 2 V , \text { and since } V_{2}=V_{1}-12, V_{2}=-5 / 2 V \). Then KCL at the center node is

\( \frac{6}{1 k }+\frac{4}{ k }=\frac{5}{2 k }+I_{ sc } \)

Yielding\( I_{ sc }=15 / 2 k A , \text { and since } R_{ Th }=V_{ oc } / I_{ sc }, R_{ Th }=12 k / 15 \) ohms. Finally, forming the Thévenin equivalent circuit and connecting up the 1k ohm resistor produces the network in Fig. 5.13d. The current \( I_{0} \) is then

\( I_{0}=\frac{6}{\left(\frac{12}{15}+1\right)} k =\frac{10}{3 k } A \)
