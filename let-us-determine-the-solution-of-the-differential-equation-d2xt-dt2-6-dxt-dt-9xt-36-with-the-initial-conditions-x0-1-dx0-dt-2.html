Guest Question.

Let us determine the solution of the differential equation

\(\frac{d^{2}x(t)}{dt^{2}}+ 6\frac{dx(t)}{dt}+ 9x(t)=36\)

With the initial conditions

x(0)=1 , \(\frac{dx(0)}{dt}=2\)

Admin
Solution

The particular integral solution is assumed to be a constant. Therefore,

\(\frac{d^{2}k_{o}}{dt^{2}}+6\frac{dk_{o}}{dt}+ 9 k_{o}=36\)

and hence,

\(k_{0}\)=4

The characteristic equation is
\(s^{2}\) + 6s + 9 = 0

and hence \(w_{o}\)= 3 and \(\zeta\)= 1. The roots of the equation are real and equal; that is,

\(s_{1}\) = -3

\(s_{2}\)= -3

Therefore, the solution is of the form

x( t) = 4 + \(k_{1}\) \(e^{-3t}\) +\( k_{2}\)\(e^{-3t}\)

Applying the initial conditions, we obtain

x(0) = 1 = 4 + \(k_{1}\)

\(\frac{dx(0)}{dt}=2= -3k_{1}+ k_{2}\)

We find that \(k_{1}\)= -3 and \(k_{2} \) = -7, and therefore the complete solution is

\(x(t) = 4 – 3e^{-3t} – 7te^{-3t}\)
