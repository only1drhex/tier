Guest Question.

A 3-phase, 400 V, 6-pole, 19 kW induction motor has the following parameters of its approximate circuit model.

\( R_{I}=1.4 \Omega\) \( R_{2}^{\prime}=0.6 \Omega\)

\( X_{1}=2 \Omega\) \( X_{2}^{\prime}=1 \Omega\)

\( X_{m}=50 \Omega\)

The rotational loss is 275 W. For a slip of 0.03, – 0.03, and 1.2, determine:

(a) the line current, pf and power input.

(b) the shaft torque, and mechanical output.

(c) the efficiency.

Admin
Solution

The approximate circuit is drawn in Fig. 9.32.

(i) Slip = 0.03

(a) \( R_{2}^{\prime} / s=0.6 / 0.03=20 \Omega\)

 

\( \bar{I}_{2}^{\prime}=\frac{231 \angle 0^{\circ}}{(1.4+20)+j 3}\)

 

\( =10.69 \angle-8^{\circ} A\)

= 10.58 – j 1.49 A

\( \bar{I}_{m}=\frac{231 \angle 0^{\circ}}{50 \angle 90^{\circ}}=-j 4.62 A\)

 

\( \bar{I}_{1}=\bar{I}_{m}+\bar{I}_{2}^{\prime}\)

= 10.58 – j 1.49 – j 4.62

\( =10.58-j 6.11=12.22 \angle-30^{\circ} A\)

 

\( I_{1}=12.22 A p f=\cos 30^{\circ}=0.866\) lagging

Power input \( =\sqrt{3} \times 400 \times 12.22 \times 0.866=7.33 kW\)

(b) \( P_{G}=\frac{3 \times(10.69)^{2} \times 0.6}{0.03}=6.86 kW\)

Mechanical output (gross) \( =(1-0.03) \times 6.86=6.65 kW\)

Rotational loss = 0.275 kW

Mechanical output (net) = 6.65 – 0.275 = 6.37 kW

\( n_{s}=1000 rpm , \quad \omega_{s}=104.72 rad / s\)

Torque (net) \( =\frac{6370}{104.72(1-0.03)}=62.22 Nm\)

(c) \( \eta=\frac{6.37}{7.27} \times 100=87.62 \% \)

(ii) Slip = –0.03

(a) \( R_{2}^{\prime} / s=-20 \Omega\) (negative resistance)

\( \bar{I}_{2}^{\prime}=\frac{231 \angle 0^{\circ}}{(1.4-20)+j 3}\)

 

\( =12.26 \angle-171.3^{\circ} A\)

= – 12.12 – j 1.85 A

\( \bar{I}_{m}=-j 4.62\)

 

\( \bar{I}_{1} \) =(-j 4.62)+(-12.12-j 1.85)

\( =-12.12-j 6.47=13.73 \angle-151.9^{\circ} A\)

 

\( \bar{I}_{1} \text { (out) }=-\bar{I}_{1}=13.73 \angle 28.1^{\circ} A\) (machine is generating)

\( I_{1} \text { (out) }=13.73 A , pf =\cos 28.1^{\circ}=0.882\) leading

Power output (elect) \( =\sqrt{3} \times 400 \times 13.73 \times 0.882\)

= 8.39 kW

Mechanical power output \( =(1-s)\left(\frac{3 I_{2}^{\prime 2} R_{2}^{\prime}}{s}\right)\)

 

\( =1.03 \times \frac{3 \times(12.26)^{2} \times 0.6}{-0.03}\)

= – 9.20 kW

Mechanical power input (net) = 9.20 kW

Mechanical power input (gross) = 9.20 + 0.275 (rotational loss)

= 9.4 kW

Shaft torque (gross) \( =\frac{9480}{104.72(1+0.03)}=87.9 Nm\)

 

\( \eta( gen )=\frac{8.49}{9.48} \times 100=88.5 \% \)

(iii) slip = 1.2

(a) \( R_{2}^{\prime} / s=0.6 / 1.2=0.5 \Omega\)

 

\( \bar{I}_{2}^{\prime}=\frac{231 \angle 0^{\circ}}{(1.4+0.5)+j 3}\)

 

\( =65.1 \angle-57.7^{\circ} A\)

= 34.79 – j 55.02 A

\( \bar{I}_{m}=-j 4.62\)

 

\( \bar{I}_{1}\) = (– j 4.62) + (34.79 + j 55.02)

= 34.79 – j 59.64 A

\( =69.05 \angle-59.7^{\circ} A\)

 

\( I_{1}=69.05 A , \quad pf =\cos 59.7^{\circ}=0.505\) lagging

Power input (elect) \( =\sqrt{3} \times 400 \times 69.05 \times 0.505=24.16 kW\)

(b) \( P_{G}=3 \times \frac{(65.1)^{2} \times 0.5}{1.2}=5.30 kW\)

Mechanical power output \( =(1-s) P_{ G }=(1-1.2) \times 5.30=-1.06 kW\)

or Mechanical power absorbed (net) = 1.06 kW

Rotational loss can be ignored as motor speed is

\( \eta=1500 \times(1-1.2)=-200 rpm\) or – 20.94 rad/s

Note: Motor runs in opposite direction of the air-gap field—absorbing mechanical power (braking action)

Torque developed \( =\frac{-1060}{-20.94}=50.62 Nm\)

This torque acts in direction opposite to that of the rotating field.

Total power dissipated (in the motor)

= 24.16 (elect) + 1.06 (mech)

= 25.22 kW

The power to be dissipated by the motor (mostly in windings) is larger than the motor rating. This can be permitted to happen only for a short period. Further the braking power is quite low. It can be seen from Fig. 9.14 that the braking torque would reduce as the speed increases further (in negative direction).
