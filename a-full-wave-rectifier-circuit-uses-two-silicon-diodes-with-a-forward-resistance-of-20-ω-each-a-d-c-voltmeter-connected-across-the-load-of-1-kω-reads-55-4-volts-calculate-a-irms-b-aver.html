Guest Question.

A full-wave rectifier circuit uses two silicon diodes with a forward resistance of 20 Ω each. A d.c. voltmeter connected across the load of 1 kΩ reads 55.4 Volts. Calculate

(a) \(I_{rms}\)
(b) average voltage across each diode
(c) ripple factor and
(d) transformer secondary voltage rating.

Admin
Solution

Given \(V_{d.c.}\) = 55.4 V and \(R_{L}\) = 1 kΩ

(a) \(I_{d.c.} =\frac{V_{d.c.}}{(r_{f} + R_{L})}=\frac{55.4}{20 + 1000}= 54.31 × 10^{– 3}\) A

we know that

\(I_{d.c.} =\frac{2I_{m}}{\pi}\) and \(I_{rms} =\frac{I_{m} }{\sqrt{2}}\)
\(I_{m} = I_{d.c.} × \frac{\pi}{ 2} = 54.31 × 10^{– 3} × \frac{\pi}{2}\) = 85.31 mA

\(I_{rms} = \frac{I_{m}}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{85.31 × 10^{– 3}}{\sqrt{2}}\) = 60.32 mA

(b) The average voltage across each silicon diode will be 0.72 V.
(c) To find ripple factor Γ

Γ = \( \sqrt{(\frac{I_{rms}}{I_{d.c.}})^{2}–1}\)

= \(\sqrt{(\frac{60.32 × 10^{– 3}}{54.31 × 10^{– 3}})^{2}–1}\) = 0.4833

To find transformer secondary voltage rating
We know that, \(V_{d.c.} =\frac{2V_{m}}{\pi}–I_{d.c.} (r_{s} + r_{f})\)
where \(r_{f}\) is the diode forward resistance and \(r_{s}\) is the transformer secondary winding resistance.

55.4 = \(\frac{2V_{m}}{\pi} – 54.31 × 10^{– 3} × 20 =\frac{2V_{m}}{\pi}\)– 1.086

56.49 = \(\frac{2V_{m}}{\pi}\)

Therefore, \(V_{m} = 56.49 ×\frac{\pi}{2}\) = 88.73 V

\(V_{rms} =\frac{V_{m}}{\sqrt{2}}=\frac{88.73}{\sqrt{2}}\) = 62.74 V

Hence, transformer secondary voltage rating is 65 V – 0 = 65 V
