Guest Question.

The top 4 m at a site consists of sand, which is underlain by clay. The water table is 1 m below the ground level. A sheet pile is to be driven into the ground to support an excavation to the top of the clay layer. How deep would you drive the sheet pile into the ground? The soil properties are as follows.

\(\text { Sand: } \gamma_{m}=16.0 kN / m ^{3}, \gamma_{ sat }=19.5 kN / m ^{3}, \phi^{\prime}=32^{\circ}\) \(\text { Clay: } \gamma_{\text {sat }}=19.5 kN / m ^{3}, \phi_{u}=0, c_{u}=45 kPa\)
Admin
Solution
\(\text { In sand, } \phi^{\prime}=32^{\circ} \rightarrow K_{A}=0.307\)

 

Let’s refer to Figure 14.9 and follow the procedure discussed in Section 14.3.2, with

 

\(h_{1}=1 m \text { and } h_{2}=3 m\)

 

\(\text { At } 1 m \text { depth, } \sigma_{ ha }^{\prime}=0.307 \times 16 \times 1=4.91 kPa\)

 

At the bottom of the sand,

 

\(\sigma_{ ha }^{\prime}=p_{1}=0.307[1 \times 16+3(19.5-9.81)=13.84 kPa\)

 

From the net pressure diagram (see Figure 14.9):

 

\(P=0.5 \times 4.91 \times 1+4.91 \times 3+0.5 \times 8.93 \times 3=2.46+14.73+13.40=30.59 kN \text { per m }\)

 

\(\bar{z}=\frac{2.46 \times 3.333+14.73 \times 1.5+13.40 \times 2.0}{30.59}=1.87 m\)

 

From Equation 14.33:

 

\(\left[4 c_{u}-\left(\gamma_{m, g} h_{1}+\gamma_{g}^{\prime} h_{2}\right)\right] d^{2}-2 P d-\frac{P\left(P+12 c_{u} \bar{z}\right)}{\left(\gamma_{m, g} h_{1}+\gamma_{g}^{\prime} h_{2}\right)+2 c_{u}}=0\) (14.33)

 

\(\begin{array}{c}{\left[4 c_{u}-\left(\gamma_{m, g} h_{1}+\gamma_{g}^{\prime} h_{2}\right)\right] d^{2}-2 P d-\frac{P\left(P+12 c_{u} \bar{z}\right)}{\left(\gamma_{m, g} h_{1}+\gamma_{g}^{\prime} h_{2}\right)+2 c_{u}}=0} \\{[4 \times 45-(16 \times 1+9.69 \times 3)] d^{2}-2 \times 30.59 d-\frac{30.59(30.59+12 \times 45 \times 1.87)}{(16 \times 1+9.69 \times 3)+2 \times 45}=0} \\134.93 d^{2}-61.18 d-235.62=0\end{array}\)

 

By trial and error, d = 1.57 m. Substituting d = 1.57 m in Equation 14.31:

 

\(h_{4}=\frac{[4 \times 45-(16 \times 1+9.69 \times 3)] 1.57-30.59}{4 \times 45}=1.01 m\)

 

From Equation 14.29:

 

\(\sigma_{ hn }=\sigma_{ hp }-\sigma_{ ha }=4 c_{u}-\left(\gamma_{m, g} h_{1}+\gamma_{g}^{\prime} h_{2}\right)=p_{2}=\overline{H B}\) (14.29)

 

\(p_{2}=4 c_{u}-\left(\gamma_{m, g} h_{1}+\gamma_{g}^{\prime} h_{2}\right)=4 \times 45-(16 \times 1+9.69 \times 3)=134.93 kPa\)

 

From Equation 14.30:

 

\(\sigma_{ hn }=\sigma_{ hp }-\sigma_{ ha }=4 c_{u}+\left(\gamma_{m, g} h_{1}+\gamma_{g}^{\prime} h_{2}\right)=p_{3}\) (14.30)

 

\(p_{3}=4 c_{u}+\left(\gamma_{m, g} h_{1}+\gamma_{g}^{\prime} h_{2}\right)=4 \times 45+(16 \times 1+9.69 \times 3)=225.07 kPa\)

 

The net pressure diagram can be drawn from these.

Let’s increase d by 50% to 2.35 m. The sheet pile has to be driven to 6.2 m below the ground level.
