Guest Question.

Apply nodal analysis to the network in Fig. 8.48.

Admin
Solution

Steps 1 and 2: The network has two nodes, as shown in Fig. 8.49.

The lower node is defined as the reference node at ground potential (zero volts), and the other node as \(V_1\), the voltage from node 1 to ground.

Step 3: \(I_1 and I_2\) are defined as leaving the node in Fig. 8.50, and Kirchhoff’s current law is applied as follows:

\(I = I_1 + I_2\)

The current \(I_2\) is related to the nodal voltage \(V_1\) by Ohm’s law:

\(I_2=\frac{V_{R_2}}{R_2}=\frac{V_1}{R_2} \)

The current \(I_1\) is also determined by Ohm’s law as follows:

\(I_1=\frac{V_{R_1}}{R_1} \)

with \(V_{R_1}=V_1-E\)

Substituting into the Kirchhoff’s current law equation

\(I=\frac{V_1-E}{R_1}+\frac{V_1}{R_2} \)

and rearranging, we have

\(I=\frac{V_1}{R_1}-\frac{E}{R_1}+\frac{V_1}{R_2}=V_1(\frac{1}{R_1}+\frac{1}{R_2} ) -\frac{E}{R_1} \)

 

or \(V_1(\frac{1}{R_1}+\frac{1}{R_2} )=\frac{E}{R_1}+1 \)

Substituting numerical values, we obtain

\(V_1(\frac{1}{6\Omega}+\frac{1}{12\Omega} )=\frac{24V}{6\Omega}+1A=4A+1A\\[0.5cm] V_1(\frac{1}{4\Omega} )=5A\\[0.5cm] V_1=20V \)

The currents \(I_1 and I_2\) can then be determined by using the preceding equations:

\(I_1=\frac{V_1-E}{R_1}=\frac{20V-24V}{6\Omega}=\frac{-4V}{6\Omega}=-0.67A \)

The minus sign indicates that the current \(I_1\) has a direction opposite to that appearing in Fig. 8.50. In addition,

\(I_2=\frac{V_1}{R_2}=\frac{20V}{12\Omega}=1.67A \)
