Cooling and Dehumidification of Air

Air enters a window air conditioner at 1 atm, 30°C, and 80 percent relative humidity at a rate of \(10 m^3/min\), and it leaves as saturated air at 14°C. Part of the moisture in the air that condenses during the process is also removed at 14°C. Determine the rates of heat and moisture removal from the air.

Admin
Solution

Air is cooled and dehumidified by a window air conditioner. The rates of heat and moisture removal are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 This is a steady-flow process and thus the mass flow rate of dry air remains constant during the entire process. 2 Dry air and the water vapor are ideal gases. 3 The kinetic and potential energy changes are negligible.

Properties The enthalpy of saturated liquid water at \(14^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\) is \(58.8 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}\) . Also, the inlet and the exit states of the air are completely specified, and the total pressure is \(1 \mathrm{~atm}\). Therefore, we can determine the properties of the air at both states from the psychrometric chart to be

\(\begin{aligned}h_{1} & =85.4 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \text { dry air }\\\omega_{1} & =0.0216 \mathrm{~kg} \mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O} / \mathrm{kg} \text { dry air }\\\mathrm{v}_{1} & =0.889 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg} \text { dry air }\end{aligned} \quad\quad\quad\begin{aligned}h_{2}&=39.3 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \text { dry air }\\\omega_{2}&=0.0100 \mathrm{~kg} \mathrm{H}_{2} \mathrm{O} / \mathrm{kg} \text { dry air }\end{aligned}\)

Analysis We take the cooling section to be the system. The schematic of the system and the psychrometric chart of the process are shown in Fig. 14-25. We note that the amount of water vapor in the air decreases during the process \(\left(\omega_{2}<\omega_{1}\right)\) due to dehumidification. Applying the mass and energy balances on the cooling and dehumidification section gives

\(\text{Dry air mass balance:}\quad\quad \dot{m}_{a_{1}}=\dot{m}_{a_{2}}=\dot{m}_{a}\)

 

\(\text{Water mass balance: }\quad\quad \dot{m}_{a_{1}} \omega_{1}=\dot{m}_{a_{2}} \omega_{2}+\dot{m}_{w} \rightarrow \dot{m}_{w}=\dot{m}_{a}\left(\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}\right)\)

 

\(\text{Energy balance:}\quad\quad\sum\limits_{\text {in }} \dot{m} h=\dot{Q}_{\text {out }}+\sum\limits_{\text {out }} \dot{m} h \rightarrow \dot{Q}_{\text {out }}=\dot{m}\left(h_{1}-h_{2}\right)-\dot{m}_{w} h_{w}\)

Then,

\(\begin{array}{c}\dot{m}_{a}=\frac{\dot{V}_{1}}{v_{1}}=\frac{10 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{min}}{0.889 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg} \text { dry air }}=11.25 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{min} \\\\\dot{m}_{w}=(11.25 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{min})(0.0216-0.0100)=0.131 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{min} \end{array}\)

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{Q}_{\text {out }}&=(11.25 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{min})[(85.4-39.3) \mathrm{kJ} / \mathrm{kg}]-(0.131 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{min})(58.8 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}) \\&=511 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{min}\end{aligned}\)

Therefore, this air-conditioning unit removes moisture and heat from the air at rates of 0.131 kg/min and 511 kJ/min, respectively.
