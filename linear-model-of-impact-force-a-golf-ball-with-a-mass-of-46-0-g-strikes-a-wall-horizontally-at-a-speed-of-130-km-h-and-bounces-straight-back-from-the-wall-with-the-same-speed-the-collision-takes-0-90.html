Linear Model of Impact Force

A golf ball with a mass of 46.0 g strikes a wall horizontally at a speed of 130. km/h and bounces straight back from the wall with the same speed. The collision takes 0.900 ms.

(a) Find the impulse delivered to the ball by the wall.

(b) Find the average force experienced by the ball during the collision.

(c) Assuming that the force changes linearly with time, plot a force versus time graph.

(d) Estimate the maximum value of the force of impact.

Admin
Solution

The situation is depicted in Figure 7-9(a). The initial momentum of the golf ball points to the right; after the collision, the golf ball moves to the left.

(a) For convenience, we take left as the positive x direction and convert the speed to metres per second. Then,

 

\(\vec{p}_{ i }=(0.0460 kg )\left(-130 . km \cdot h ^{-1}\right)\left(\frac{1000 m \cdot km ^{-1}}{3600 s \cdot h ^{-1}}\right) \hat{i}\)

 

\(=-1.66 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }\)

 

\(\vec{p}_{ f }=-\vec{p}_{ i }=1.66 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{i}\) (1)

 

\(\overrightarrow{ I }=\Delta \overrightarrow{ p }=\overrightarrow{ p }_{ f }-\overrightarrow{ p }_{ i }=3.32 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{ i }\) (2)

 

(b) From Equation 7-8, we have

 

\(\Delta \vec{p}=\vec{F}_{\text {net }}^{\text {avg }} \Delta t\)

 

\(\vec{F}_{ net }^{ avg }=\frac{\Delta \vec{p}}{\Delta t}=\frac{3.32 kg \cdot m \cdot s ^{-1} \hat{i}}{0.900 \times 10^{-3} s }=3.69 kN \hat{i}\) (3)

 

(c) Figure 7-9(b) shows the force as a function of time, taken to be linear.

(d) The average force is half the maximum value for the linear approximation in Figure 7-9(b); therefore, the maximum value of the force of impact is 7.38 kN.
