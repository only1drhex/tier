Guest Question.

The steel frame shown in Fig. P4.3 is subjected to a horizontal force F(t) applied at the girder level. The force decreases linearly from 5 kip at time t = 0 to zero at t = 0.6 s. Determine: (a) the horizontal deflection at t = 0.5 s and (b) the maximum horizontal deflection. Assume the columns massless and the girder rigid. Neglect damping.

Admin
Solution

Columns are massless and girder are rigid. Neglect damping

\(k_{1}+k_{2}=\frac{12EI}{{L_{1}}^{3}}+\frac{3EI}{{L_{2}}^{3}}\)

 

\(k=\frac{12\cdot 30\cdot 10^{6}\cdot 82.8}{(15\cdot 12)^{3}}+\frac{3\cdot 30\cdot 10^{6}\cdot 82.}{(20\cdot 12)^{3}}=5111.1+539.1=5650.2\) \(\frac{lb}{in}\).

 

\(\omega =\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}=\sqrt{\frac{5650.2\cdot 386}{20,000}}=10.44\) rad/sec

 

Then, for \(t< t_{d}\):

(a)

\(u(t)=\frac{F_{0}}{k}(1-\cos \omega t)+\frac{F_{0}}{kt_{d}}\left ( \frac{\sin \omega t}{\omega }-t \right )\)

 

at t = 0.5 sec.

\(u(t)=\frac{5,000}{5,650.2}(1-\cos (10.44\cdot 0.5))+\frac{5,000}{5,650.2\cdot 0.6}\left ( \frac{\sin (10.44\cdot 0.5)}{10.44}-0.5 \right )\)

= -0.4072 in.

 

(b) Maximum Horizontal Deflection using Chart Fig. 4.5

\(T=\frac{2\pi }{\omega }=\frac{2\pi }{10.44}=0.602\) sec.

 

\(\frac{t_{d}}{T}=\frac{0.6}{0.602}=0.9972\)

 

for \(\frac{t_{d}}{T}==0.9972\rightarrow (DLF)_{max}=1.55\)

 

\(\therefore \frac{u_{max}}{u_{st}}=1.55\)

 

\(u_{max}=1.55\cdot u_{st}=1.55\cdot \frac{5,000}{5,650.2}=1.37\) in.
