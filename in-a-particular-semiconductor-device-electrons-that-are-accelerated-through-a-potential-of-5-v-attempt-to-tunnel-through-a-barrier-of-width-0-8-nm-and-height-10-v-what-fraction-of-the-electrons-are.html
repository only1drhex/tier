Guest Question.

In a particular semiconductor device, electrons that are accelerated through a potential of 5 V attempt to tunnel through a barrier of width 0.8 nm and height 10 V. What fraction of the electrons are able to tunnel through the barrier if the potential is zero outside the barrier?

Strategy We use either Equation (6.69) or (6.70) to calculate the tunneling probability, depending on the value of \(\kappa L . \text { We need to know } V_{0}, \kappa, L, \text { and } E\). We are given L and the fact that the potential barrier has \(V_{0}=10\) eV and is zero outside the barrier. We determine from the accelerating voltage that the energy E of the electrons is K = 5 eV. We find the value of κ from a variation of Equation (6.62b) where we let \(i k_{ II } \rightarrow \kappa\).

\(k_{ II }=\frac{\sqrt{2 m\left(E-V_{0}\right)}}{\hbar} \quad \text { where } V=V_{0}\) (6.62b)

\(T=\left[1+\frac{V_{0}^{2} \sinh ^{2}(\kappa L)}{4 E\left(V_{0}-E\right)}\right]^{-1}\) (6.69)

\(T=16 \frac{E}{V_{0}}\left(1-\frac{E}{V_{0}}\right) e^{-2 \kappa L}\) (6.70)

Admin
Solution

We determine κ by using the mass of the electron and the appropriate energies.

 

\(\kappa=\frac{\sqrt{2 m\left(V_{0}-E\right)}}{\hbar}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&=\frac{\sqrt{2\left(0.511 \times 10^{6} eV / c^{2}\right)(10 eV -5 eV )}}{6.58 \times 10^{-16} eV \cdot s } \\&=\frac{3.43 \times 10^{18} s ^{-1}}{c}=\frac{3.43 \times 10^{18} s ^{-1}}{3 \times 10^{8} m / s }=1.15 \times 10^{10} m ^{-1}\end{aligned}\)

 

The value of \(\kappa L=\left(1.15 \times 10^{10} m ^{-1}\right)\left(0.8 \times 10^{-9} m \right)=9.2\), which might be considered to be much greater than 1, so we can try Equation (6.70). Let’s calculate the transmission probability using both equations. The approximate Equation (6.70) gives

 

\(T=16\left(\frac{5 eV }{10 eV }\right)\left(1-\frac{5 eV }{10 eV }\right) e^{-18.4}=4.1 \times 10^{-8}\)

 

The more accurate Equation (6.69) gives

 

\(T=\left[\frac{1+(10 eV )^{2} \sinh ^{2}(9.2)}{4(5 eV )(5 eV )}\right]^{-1}=4.1 \times 10^{-8}\) (6.71)

 

The approximate equation, valid when \(\kappa L \gg 1\), works well in this case.
