Guest Question.

Consider a simple ideal Rankine cycle using water at a supercritical pressure. Such a cycle has a potential advantage of minimizing local temperature differences between the fluids in the steam generator, such as the instance in which the high-temperature energy source is the hot exhaust gas from a gasturbine engine. Calculate the thermal efficiency of the cycle if the state entering the turbine is 8000 \(lbf / in .^{2}\), 1300 F, and the condenser pressure is 0.95 \(lbf / in .^{2}\). What is the steam quality at the turbine exit?

Admin
Solution

For the efficiency we need the net work and steam generator heat transfer.

State 1: \(s _{1}=0.1296 Btu / lbm R , \quad h _{1}=68.04 Btu / lbm\)

State 3: \(h _{3}=1547.5 Btu / lbm , \quad s _{3}=1.4718 Btu / lbm R\)

C.V. Pump. For this high exit pressure we use Table F.7.3 to get state 2.

Entropy Eq.:

\(\begin{aligned}& s _{2}= s _{1} \Rightarrow h _{2}=91.69 Btu / lbm \\& w _{ p }= h _{2}- h _{1}=91.69-68.04=23.65 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

C.V. Turbine. Assume reversible and adiabatic.
Entropy Eq.: \(s _{4}= s _{3}=1.4718=0.1296+ x _{4} \times 1.8526\)

\(x _{4}= 0 . 7 2 4 5\) Very low for a turbine exhaust

\(\begin{aligned}& h _{4}=68.04+ x _{4} \times 1036.98=751.29 Btu / lbm, \\& w _{ T }= h _{3}- h _{4}=796.2 Btu / lbm\end{aligned}\)

Steam generator: \(q _{ H }= h _{3}- h _{2}=1547.5-91.69=1455.8 Btu / lbm\)

\(\begin{aligned}& w _{ NET }= w _{ T }- w _{ p }=796.2-23.65=772.6 Btu / lbm \\&\eta= w _{ NET } / q _{ H }=772.6 / 1455.8= 0 . 5 3\end{aligned}\)
