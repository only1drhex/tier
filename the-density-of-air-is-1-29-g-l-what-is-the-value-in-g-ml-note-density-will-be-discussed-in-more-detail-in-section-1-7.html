Guest Question.

The density of air is \(1.29 \mathrm{~g} / \mathrm{L}\). What is the value in \(\mathrm{g} / \mathrm{mL}\) ? (Note: Density will be discussed in more detail in Section 1.7.)

Admin
Solution

This problem requires the use of one conversion factor. According to the metric system equalities relating \(\mathrm{mL}\) and \(\mathrm{L}\) provided in Table 1.2, the possible conversion factors are

Prefix Abbreviation Meaning Decimal Equivalent Equality with major metric units (g, m, or L are represented by x in each)
mega M 10\(^{6}\) 1,000,000.00 1Mx=10\(^{6}\)x
kilo k 10\(^{3}\) 1000 1kx=10\(^{3}\)x
deka da 10\(^{1}\) 10 1dax=10\(^{1}\)x
deci d 10\(^{-1}\) 0.1 1 dx=10\(^{-1}\)x
centi c 10\(^{-2}\) 0.01 1cx=10\(^{-2}\)x
milli m 10\(^{-3}\) 0.001 1 mx=10\(^{-3}\)x
micro μ 10\(^{-6}\) 0.000001 1μx=10\(^{-6}\)x
nano n 10\(^{-9}\) 0.000000001 1nx=10\(^{-9}\)x
\(\frac{10^{-3} \mathrm{~L}}{1 \mathrm{~mL}}=\frac{1 \mathrm{~mL}}{10^{-3} \mathrm{~L}}\)

Since the density given has \(L\) in the denominator, the conversion factor with \(L\) in the numerator should be used. If the incorrect conversion factor was used, the units would not cancel and the result would be \(\mathrm{g}\) and \(\mathrm{mL}\) in the numerator and \(\mathrm{L}^{2}\) in the denominator.

\(\frac{1.29 \mathrm{~g}}{\cancel{L}} \times \frac{10^{-3} \cancel{L}}{1 \mathrm{~mL}}=1.29 \times 10^{-3} \frac{\mathrm{g}}{\mathrm{mL}}\)

Data Given \(\times\) Conversion Factor \(=\) Desired Result
Helpful Hint: If the incorrect conversion factor was used, the units would not cancel and the result would be \(g\) and \(m L\) in the numerator and \(L^{2}\) in the denominator.
