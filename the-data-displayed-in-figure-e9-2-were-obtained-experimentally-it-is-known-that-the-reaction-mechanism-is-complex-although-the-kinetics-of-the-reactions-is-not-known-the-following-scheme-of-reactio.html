Guest Question.

The data displayed in Figure E9.2 were obtained experimentally. It is known that the reaction mechanism is complex, although the kinetics of the reactions is not known. The following scheme of reactions involved in the transformation of a substrate S into the product P in the presence of an enzyme E is proposed.
\(S+E\overset{k_{2}/k_{1}}{\leftrightarrow }ES^{*}\)

\(ES^{*}\left ( +H_{2}O \right )\overset{k_{3}}{\rightarrow}P+E\)
\(ES^{*}+E\overset{k_{4}/k_{5}}{\leftrightarrow }SES^{*}\)

Designating S for the substrate and E for the enzyme. The experimental results are
presented in the following table (adapted from Fogler, 2000):

0.6 0.6 0.5 1 1.5 2.5 \(V_{max}/(-r_{S})\)
0.4 0.3 0.1 0.06 0.04 0.02 [S](moles/L)

Derive the expression of the rate with respect to the substrate S and determine the constants. If possible, make simplifications. Calculate the constants using the data displayed in Figure E9.2.

Solution

\(\left ( -r_{S} \right )=k_{1}\left [ E \right ]\left [ S \right ]-k_{2}\left [ ES^{*} \right ]\) (9.26)

But:
\(r_{ES^{*}}=k_{1}\left [ E \right ]\left [ S \right ]-k_{2}\left [ ES^{*} \right ]-k_{3}\left [ ES^{*} \right ]-k_{4}\left [ ES^{*} \right ]\left [ S \right ]+k_{5}\left [ SES^{*} \right ]=0\) (9.27)
\(r_{SES^{*}}=k_{4}\left [ ES^{*} \right ]\left [ S \right ]-k_{5}\left [ SES^{*} \right ]=0\) (9.28)

But
\(\left [ E_{T} \right ]=[E]+[ES^{*}]+[SES^{*}]=0\) (9.29)

From Equation 9.28, we have:
\([SES^{*}]=\frac{k_{4}}{k_{5}}[ES^{*}][S]\) (9.30)

Substituting Equations 9.30 and 9.29 into Equations 9.27 and 9.26, we have:
\((-r_{S})=k_{1}[S][E_{T}]\frac{k_{3}\frac{k_{1}k_{4}}{k_{5}}[S]^{2}}{k_{1}[S]+k_{2}+k_{3}}\)

\((-r_{S})=\frac{k_{1}[S][E_{T}]}{k_{1}[S]+k_{M}}\)

\(k_{M}=\frac{\left ( k_{2}+k_{3} \right )}{k'_{3}}\) constant of Michaelis
in which
\(k_{1}[K_{T}]=V_{max}\)
WE then have:
\((-r_{S})=\frac{V_{max}[S]}{k_{1}[S]+K_{M}}\)

\(k_{1}+\frac{k_{M}}{[S]}=\frac{V_{max}}{-r_{S}}\) (9.31)
