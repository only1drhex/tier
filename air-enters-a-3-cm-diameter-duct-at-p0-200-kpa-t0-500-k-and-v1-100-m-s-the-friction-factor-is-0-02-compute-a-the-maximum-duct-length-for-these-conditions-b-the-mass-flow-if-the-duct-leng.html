Guest Question.

Air enters a 3-cm-diameter duct at \(p_0 = 200 kPa, T_0 = 500 K, and V_1 = 100 m/s\). The friction factor is 0.02. Compute (a) the maximum duct length for these conditions, (b) the mass flow if the duct length is 15 m, and (c) the reduced mass flow if L = 30 m.

Admin
Solution

Part (a)

First compute

\(T_1 = T_0 – \frac{\frac{1}{2}V^2_1}{c_p} = 500 – \frac{\frac{1}{2}(100 m(s))^2}{1005 m^2/s^2 \cdot K} = 500 – 5 = 495 K\)

 

\(a_1 = (kRT_1)^{1/2} \approx 20(495)^{1/2} = 445 m/s\)

Thus \(Ma_1 = \frac{V_1}{a_1} = \frac{100}{445} = 0.225\)

For this \(Ma_1\), from Eq. (9.66) or interpolation in Table B.3,

\(\frac{\bar{f}L^*}{D} = \frac{1 – Ma^2}{k Ma^2} + \frac{k + 1}{2k} \ln \frac{(k + 1) Ma^2}{2 + (k – 1) Ma^2}\) (9.66)

\(\frac{\bar{f}L^*}{D} = 11.0\)

The maximum duct length possible for these inlet conditions is

\(L^* = \frac{(\bar{f}L^*/D)D}{\bar{f}} = \frac{11.0(0.03 m)}{0.02} = 16.5 m\)

Part (b)

The given L = 15 m is less than \(L^*\), and so the duct is not choked and the mass flow follows from the inlet conditions:

\(\rho_{01} = \frac{p_{01}}{RT_0} = \frac{200,000 Pa}{287(500 K)} = 1.394 kg/m^3\)

 

\(\rho_1 = \frac{\rho_{01}}{[1 + 0.2(0.225)^2]^{2.5}} = \frac{1.394}{1.0255} = 1.359 kg/m^3\)

whence \(\dot{m} = \rho_1 AV_1 = (1.359 kg /m^3) \left[\frac{\pi}{4}(0.03 m)^2 \right] (100 m/s) = 0.0961 kg/s\)

Part (c)

Since L = 30 m is greater than \(L^*\), the duct must choke back until L = \(L^*\), corresponding to a lower inlet \(Ma_1\):

\(L^* = L = 30 m\)

 

\(\frac{\bar{f}L^*}{D} = \frac{0.02(30 m)}{0.03 m} = 20.0\)

EES

It is difficult to interpolate for fL/D = 20 in Table B.3 and impossible to invert Eq. (9.66) for the Mach number without laborious iteration. But it is a breeze for EES to solve Eq. (9.66) for the Mach number, using the following three statements:

k = 1.4
fLD = 20
fLD = (1 – Ma^2)/k/Ma^2 + (k + 1)/2/k*LN((k + 1)*Ma^2/(2 + (k – 1)*Ma^2))

Simply specify Ma < 1 in the Variable Information menu, and EES reports

\(Ma_{choked}\) = 0.174 (23 percent less)

\(T_{1,new} = \frac{T_0}{1 + 0.2(0.174)^2} = 497\) K

\(a_{1,new} \approx 20(497 K)^{1/2} = 446\) m/s

\(V_{1,new} = Ma_1 a_1 = 0.174(446) = 77.6\) m/s

\(\rho_{1,new} = \frac{\rho_{01}}{[1 + 0.2(0.174)^2]^{2.5}} = 1.373 kg/m^3\)

\(\dot{m}_{new} = \rho_1 AV_1 = 1.373 \left[\frac{\pi}{4}(0.03)^2 \right] (77.6) = 0.0753\) kg/s (22 percent less)
