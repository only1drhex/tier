Two-Phase Flow in a Vertical Pipe

Gas and oil are flowing upwards at volumetric flow rates \(G=10.0, L=0.1\) \(\mathrm{ft}^{3} / \mathrm{s}\) in a vertical pipeline of internal diameter \(D=0.25 \mathrm{ft}\). Estimate the fraction of gas, the pressure gradient, and the mean velocities of each phase. The physical properties are \(\rho_{g}=0.15, \rho_{l}=50.0 \mathrm{lb}_{\mathrm{m}} / \mathrm{ft}^{3}, \mu_{g}=0.025, \mu_{l}=20.0 \mathrm{lb}_{\mathrm{m}} / \mathrm{ft} \mathrm{hr}\); the Fanning friction factor is \(f_{\mathrm{F}}=0.0045\).

Admin
Solution

The following quantities are first calculated:

Cross-sectional area of pipe

\(A=\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}=\frac{\pi \times 0.25^{2}}{4}=0.0491 \mathrm{ft}^{2}.\)

Superficial velocities

\(j_{g}=\frac{G}{A}=\frac{10.0}{0.0491}=204, \quad j_{l}=\frac{L}{A}=\frac{0.1}{0.0491}=2.04 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s}.\)

Dimensionless superficial velocities from Eqn. (10.28)

\(j_{g}^{*}=j_{g} \sqrt{\frac{\rho_{g}}{g D\left(\rho_{l}-\rho_{g}\right)}}, \quad j_{l}^{*}=j_{l} \sqrt{\frac{\rho_{l}}{g D\left(\rho_{l}-\rho_{g}\right)}},\) (10.28)

\(\begin{array}{l}j_{g}^{*}=j_{g} \sqrt{\frac{\rho_{g}}{g D\left(\rho_{l}-\rho_{g}\right)}}=204 \sqrt{\frac{0.15}{32.2 \times 0.25 \times(50.0-0.15)}}=3.94, \\j_{l}^{*}=j_{l} \sqrt{\frac{\rho_{l}}{g D\left(\rho_{l}-\rho_{g}\right)}}=2.04 \sqrt{\frac{50.0}{32.2 \times 0.25 \times(50.0-0.15)}}=0.720 .\end{array}\)

Recall that from Eqn. (10.27) that the approximate value of \(j_{g}^{*}\) at which slug flow becomes annular flow is given by \(0.5+0.6 j_{l}^{*}\). This transition value is clearly exceeded, and the flow is taken to be annular. The appropriate pressure gradients for inclusion in Eqns. (10.43) and (10.44) are:

\(j_{g}^{*}=0.5+0.6 j_{l}^{*}\) (10.27)

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{\mathrm{tp}}=-\underbrace{\left(\frac{v_{g}^{2} D}{j_{g}^{2} D_{g}}\right)}_{\phi_{g}^{2}} \underbrace{\left(\frac{2 f_{\mathrm{F}} \rho_{g} j_{g}^{2}}{D}\right)}_{(d p / d z)_{g o}}-\rho_{g} g=\underbrace{\phi_{g}^{2}\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{g o}}_{\text {Friction }}-\underbrace{\rho_{g} g}_{\text {Gravity }}, \quad(10.43)\)

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{\mathrm{tp}}=\underbrace{\phi_{l}^{2}\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{l o}}_{\text {Friction }}-\underbrace{\left[\varepsilon \rho_{g}+(1-\varepsilon) \rho_{l}\right] g}_{\text {Gravity }} .\) (10.44)

Gas only

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{g o}=-\frac{2 f_{\mathrm{F}} \rho_{g} j_{g}^{2}}{D}=-\frac{2 \times 0.0045 \times 0.15 \times 204^{2}}{0.25 \times 32.2 \times 144}=-0.0485 \mathrm{psi} / \mathrm{ft}.\)

Liquid only

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{l o}=-\frac{2 f_{\mathrm{F}} \rho_{l} j_{l}^{2}}{D}=-\frac{2 \times 0.0045 \times 50.0 \times 2.04^{2}}{0.25 \times 32.2 \times 144}=-1.62 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{psi} / \mathrm{ft}.\)

Reynolds numbers

\(\begin{array}{l}\operatorname{Re}_{g}=\frac{0.15 \times 204 \times 0.25 \times 3,600}{0.025}=1.10 \times 10^{6}, \\ \operatorname{Re}_{l}=\frac{50.0 \times 2.04 \times 0.25 \times 3,600}{20.0}=4,590 .\end{array}\)

Thus, the flow is turbulent for the liquid and turbulent for the gas, with \(n=4.12\) (from Table 10.5) for use in Eqn. (10.26). Equations (10.43) and (10.44) now become:

\(\phi_{g}=\left(1+X^{2 / n}\right)^{n / 2}\) (10.26)

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{\mathrm{tp}}=-\underbrace{\left(\frac{v_{g}^{2} D}{j_{g}^{2} D_{g}}\right)}_{\phi_{g}^{2}} \underbrace{\left(\frac{2 f_{\mathrm{F}} \rho_{g} j_{g}^{2}}{D}\right)}_{(d p / d z)_{g o}}-\rho_{g} g=\underbrace{\phi_{g}^{2}\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{g o}}_{\text {Friction }}-\underbrace{\rho_{g} g}_{\text {Gravity }}, \quad(10.43)\)

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{\mathrm{tp}}=\underbrace{\phi_{l}^{2}\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{l o}}_{\text {Friction }}-\underbrace{\left[\varepsilon \rho_{g}+(1-\varepsilon) \rho_{l}\right] g}_{\text {Gravity }} .\) (10.44)

\(\frac{d p}{d z}=-0.0485 \phi_{g}^{2}-\frac{0.15 \times 32.2}{32.2 \times 144}=-0.0485 \phi_{g}^{2}-0.00104,\)

\(\frac{d p}{d z}=-1.62 \times 10^{-3} \phi_{l}^{2}-\frac{32.2[0.15 \varepsilon+50.0(1-\varepsilon)]}{32.2 \times 144}=-1.62 \times 10^{-3} \phi_{l}^{2}-0.347+0.346 \varepsilon.\)

Table 10.5 Exponents for Two-Phase Correlation
n Liquid Flow Gas Flow
4.12 Turbulent Turbulent
3.61 Viscous Turbulent
3.56 Turbulent Viscous
X < 1: 2.68 Viscous Viscous
X > 1 : 3.27 Viscous Viscous

Now equate the two pressure gradients, substitute \(\phi_{l}^{2}=\phi_{g}^{2} / X^{2}\) from Eqn. (10.23), and collect terms:

\(X^{2}=\frac{(d p / d z)_{l o}}{(d p / d z)_{g o}}=\frac{\phi_{g}^{2}}{\phi_{l}^{2}}.\) (10.23)

\(\phi_{g}^{2}\left(29.9-\frac{1}{X^{2}}\right)=214(1-\varepsilon),\)

which can be solved in conjunction with Eqns. (10.25) and (10.26) to give:

\(\varepsilon=\frac{1}{\left(1+0.0904 X^{0.548}\right)^{2.82}}.\) (10.25)

\(\phi_{g}=\left(1+X^{2 / n}\right)^{n / 2}\) (10.26)

\(X=0.198, \quad \varepsilon=0.902, \quad \phi_{g}=2.17, \quad \phi_{g}^{2}=4.70 .\)

Thus, the fraction of gas is \(0.902\), so the oil occupies a fairly thin film on the inside of the tube. The pressure gradient is next calculated.

Two-phase flow pressure gradient

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{t p}=\phi_{g}^{2}\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{g o}-\rho_{g} g=-4.70 \times 0.0485-\frac{0.15 \times 32.2}{32.2 \times 144}=-0.229 \mathrm{psi} / \mathrm{ft}.\)

Finally, the individual mean velocities are obtained:

Gas

\(v_{g}=\frac{G}{\varepsilon A}=\frac{10.0}{0.902 \times 0.0491}=226 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s}.\)

Liquid

\(v_{l}=\frac{L}{(1-\varepsilon) A}=\frac{0.1}{(1-0.902) \times 0.0491}=20.8 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s}.\)
