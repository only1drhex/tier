Guest Question.

Design a suitable block foundation for a two-cylinder vertical compressor for the following data:
crank angles: 0 and π/2
Weight of compressor = 200 kN
operating speed: 600 rpm
Total weight of rotating mass: 0.06 kN
Total weight of reciprocating mass: 0.27 kN
Radius of crank: 0.4 m
safe bearing capacity of soil under static conditions: \( 100 kN/m ^{2}\)
coefficient of elastic uniform compression: \(45,000 kN/m ^{3}\)

Admin
Solution

Calculation of Unbalanced Inertial Forces
crank angles 0 and \(\pi / 2\).
The crank mechanisms of both cylinders are taken to be identical. Resultant unbalanced inertial force in the vertical direction
\(\begin{aligned}P_{z} &=\left(M_{\mathrm{rec}}+M_{\mathrm{rot}}\right) R \omega^{2} \cos (\omega t+\pi / 4) \\M_{\mathrm{rec}} &=\frac{0.27}{9.81} \mathrm{kN} \sec ^{2} / \mathrm{m} M_{\mathrm{rot}}=\frac{0.06}{9.81} \mathrm{kN} \mathrm{sec}^{2} / \mathrm{m} \\P_{z_{\text {mac }}} &=\sqrt{2}\left(\frac{0.33}{9.81}\right) \times 0.4 \times\left(\frac{600}{60} \times 2 \pi\right)^{2} \mathrm{kN} \\&=75.124 \mathbf{k N}\end{aligned}\)
Similarly \(P_{x}\) also may be calculated.
\(\begin{aligned}P_{x} &=M_{\mathrm{rot}} R \omega^{2} \sin \left(\omega t+\frac{\pi}{4}\right) \\\therefore \quad P_{x_{\max }} &=\sqrt{2} \times \frac{0.06}{9.81} \times 0.4 \times\left(\frac{600}{60} \times 2 \pi\right)^{2} \mathrm{kN} \\&=13.7 \mathbf{k N} .\end{aligned}\)
Size of Foundation
Let us assume a block of size \(6 \mathrm{~m} \times 3 \mathrm{~m} \times 1 \mathrm{~m}\) high for a compressor foundation.
The compressor and motor should be so arranged that the eccentricity of the resultant forces due to the weight of the machine and foundation block do not exceed \(5 \%\).
Weight of foundation block \(=6 \times 3 \times 1 \times 24=432 \mathrm{kN}\)
(assuming the unit weight of concrete as \(24 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\) )
Pressure on Foundation Soil
Total weight coming on the soil \(=(200+432) \mathrm{kN}=632 \mathrm{kN}\)
Area of base \(=6 \times 3=18 \mathrm{~m}^{2}\).
\(\therefore\) Stress on soil \(=\frac{632}{18}=36.8 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\)
Safe bearing capacity of the soil under static loading conditions \(=100 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\)
Assuming a reduction factor of \(0.5\),
dynamic bearing capacity \(=50 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\)
Since this is more than the stress on the soil the size is satisfactory.
Amplitudes of motion
\(\begin{aligned} \omega_{n}{ }^{2} &=\frac{C_{u} A}{M}=\frac{45,000 \times 18}{(632 / 9.81)}=12,573 / \mathrm{sec}^{2} \\ \text { Amplitude of vibration } &=\frac{60}{M\left(\omega_{n}{ }^{2}-\omega^{2}\right)}, \text { neglecting damping of soil. } \\ &=\frac{(600 \times 2 \pi)^{2}}{M\left(\omega_{n}{ }^{2}-\omega^{2}\right)} \\ &=\frac{75.124}{(632 / 9.81)(12573-3948)} \mathrm{m} \\ &=\mathbf{0 . 1 3 5} \mathrm{mm} \end{aligned}\)
Since the permissible amplitude is \(0.15 \mathrm{~mm}\), this is safe.
Note:- Moments due to unbalanced forces may be determined from appropriate data regarding positioning of the compressor and the motor on the foundation block; also, analysis for other modes of vibration such as sliding and rocking may also be performed and the corresponding amplitudes may be obtained and verified.
