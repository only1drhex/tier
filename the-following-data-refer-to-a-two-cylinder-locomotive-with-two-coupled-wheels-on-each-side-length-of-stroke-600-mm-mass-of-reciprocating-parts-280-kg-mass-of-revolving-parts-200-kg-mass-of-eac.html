Guest Question.

The following data refer to a two cylinder locomotive with two coupled wheels on each side: Length of stroke = 600 mm; Mass of reciprocating parts = 280 kg
Mass of revolving parts =200 kg; Mass of each coupling rod = 240 kg
Radius of centre of coupling rod pin = 250 mm
Distance between cylinders = 0.6 m
Distance between wheels = 1.5 m
Distance between coupling rods = 1.8 m
The main cranks are at right angles and the coupling rod pins are at 180° to their respective main cranks. The balance masses are to be placed in the wheels at a mean radius of 670 mm in order to balance the complete revolving and 3/4th of the reciprocating masses. The balance mass for the reciprocating masses is divided equally between the driving wheels and the coupled wheels. Find (a) the magnitude and angular positions of the masses required for the driving and trailing wheels, and (b) the hammer blow at 120 km/h, if the wheels are 1.8 m diameter.

Admin
Solution

(a) The position of planes for the driving wheels B and E, cylinders C and D, and coupling rods A and F, are shown in Fig.12.19(a). The angular position of cranks C and D and coupling pins A and F are shown in Fig.12.19(b).

Mass of the reciprocating parts per cylinder to be balanced \( =3 \times \frac{280}{4}=210 kg \).

Mass to be balanced for driving wheels and trailing wheels \( =\frac{210}{2}=105 kg \).

Masses to be balanced for each driving wheel:

1. Half of the mass of coupling rod \( =\frac{240}{2}=120 kg \text { or } M_{A}=M_{F}=120 kg \).

2. Complete the revolving mass (200 kg) and 3/4th the mass of reciprocating parts (105 kg).

or \( M_{C}=M_{D}=200+105=305 kg \).

Driving wheels: Let \( M_{B} \text { and } M_{E} \) be the balance masses placed in the driving wheels B and E, respectively in plane of B as the reference plane.

Linear speed of the wheel = 120 km/h =33.33 m/s.
Diameter of wheel, D = 1.8m.

Angular speed of wheel, \( \omega=\frac{2 v}{D}=2 \times \frac{33.33}{1.8}=37 rad / s \).
