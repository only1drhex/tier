Guest Question.

Choose an Oiles 500 bushing to give a maximum wear of 0.001 in for 800 h of use with a 300 rev/min jour-nal and 50 lbf radial load. Use\( \hslash _{ CR} = 2.7 Btu/(h · ft^{2} · °F), T_{max} = 300°F, f_{s}\) = 0.03, and a design factor\( n_{d}\) = 2. Table 12–12 lists the available bushing sizes from the manufacturer.

Admin
Solution

Using Equation (12–49) with\( n_{d}F for F, f_{s}\) = 0.03 from Table 12–10, and \(\hslash _{CR}= 2.7 Btu∕(h · ft^{2}\) · °F), gives

\(L ≥\frac{720 f_{s} n_{d} FN}{J\hslash _{CR}(T_{f} − T_{∞})} =\frac{ 720(0.03)2(50)300}{778(2.7)(300 − 70)} = 1.34 in\)

From Table 12–12, the smallest available bushing has an ID =\( \frac{5}{8} in, OD =\frac{ 7}{8} in, and L = 1\frac{1}{2}\) in. However, for this case L∕D = 1.5∕0.625 = 2.4, and is outside of the recommendations of Equation (12–44). Thus, for the first trial, try the bushing with ID =\( \frac{3}{4} in, OD = 1\frac{1}{8} in, and L = 1\frac{1}{2} \)in (L∕D = 1.5∕0.75 = 2). Thus,

Equation (12–42):\( P_{max} = \frac{4}{π}\frac{n_{d}F}{DL} = \frac{4}{π}\frac{2(50)}{0.75(1.5)} = 113 psi < 3560 psi\) (OK)

\(P =\frac{ n_{d}F}{DL} =\frac{ 2(50)}{0.75(1.5) }= 88.9 psi\)

Equation (12–40): \(V = \frac{πDN}{12} =\frac{ π(0.75)300}{12} = 58.9 ft/min < 100 ft/min \) (OK)

PV = 88.9(58.9) = 5240 psi · ft/min < 46 700 psi · ft/min (OK)

Equation (12–43), with Table 12–9:

\(w =\frac{ Kn_{d}FN_{t}}{3L} =\frac{ 6(10^{−11})2(50)300(800)}{3(1.5)} = 0.000320 in < 0.001 \)in (OK)

\(Select ID = \frac{3}{4} in, OD = 1\frac{1}{8} in, and L = 1\frac{1}{2} in\)
