Guest Question.

Design a suitable foundation for a double-acting steam hammer for the following data:

Weight of tup = 50 kN

Height of fall = 1 m

Area of piston = 0.2 \(m ^{2}\)

Steam pressure on piston = 900 kN/\(m ^{2}\)

Weight of anvil and frame = 1000 kN

Safe bearing capacity under static loading conditions = 200 kN/\(m ^{2}\)

Coefficient of elastic uniform compression of soil = \(5 \times 10^{4} kN / m ^{3}\)

Base area of anvil (base area of elastic pad also) = 5.5 \(m ^{2}\)

Thickness of elastic pad = 0.60 m

Modulus of elasticity of the material of the pad = \(5 \times 10^{5} kN / m ^{2}\)

Coefficient of restitution = 0.5

Unit weight of soil = 16 kN/\(m ^{3}\)

Safe bearing capacity under static loading conditions = 200 kN/\(m ^{2}\)

Coefficient of elastic uniform compression of soil = \(5 \times 10^{4} kN / m ^{3}\)

Admin
Solution

Velocity of Tup before Impact

\(\upsilon =\alpha \sqrt{\frac{2 g\left(W_{t}+p . a\right) h}{W_{t}}}\)

Take α = 0.65
Substituting \(W_{t}=50 kN , p=900 kN / m ^{2}, a=0.2 m ^{2}, \text { and } h=1 m\)

 

\(\upsilon=0.65 \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 9.81(50+900 \times 0.2) \times 1}{50}} m / s\)

= 6.2 m/s
Preliminary Calculation of Weight of Foundation and Base Area

\(\begin{aligned}n_{a} &=\frac{W_{a}}{W_{t}}=\frac{1000}{50}=20 \\\frac{W_{f}}{W_{t}} &=n_{f}=8(1+e) v-n_{a} \\&=8(1+0.5) \times 6.2-20\end{aligned}\)

= 54.4

∴ \(W_{f}=54.4 \times 50=2720 kN\)

This is the minimum weight of foundation required (including that of backfill).

\(\frac{A}{W_{t}}=a_{f}=\frac{20(1+e) \upsilon}{q_{a}}=\frac{20(1+0.5) \times 6.2}{200}=0.93\)

∴ Minimum base area, A = 0.93 × 50 = 46.5 \(m ^{2}\)

Size of Foundation
The outline of the foundation block proposed, based on the preliminary values of weight and base area, is shown in Fig. 20.35.
Volume of concrete adopted

\(=7.5 \times 6.5 \times 1.5+4.5 \times 2 \times 0.5 \times 1.0+2 \times 4 \times 0.5 \times 10=91.50 m ^{3}\)

Volume of backfill used

\(2 \times 1.0 \times 6.5 \times 2.0+5.5 \times 1.25 \times 2.0 \times 2.0=53.5 m ^{3}\)

Total weight of foundation and backfill = 91.5 × 24 + 53.5 × 16 = 3,052 kN
Actual base area provided \(=7.5 \times 6.5=48.75 m ^{2}\)

Amplitudes of Motion
spring constant of soil

\(k_{1}=C_{u}{ }^{\prime} A_{1}=3 C_{u} A_{1}\) , taking the correction factor as 3.

\(\therefore \quad k_{1}=3 \times 15 \times 10^{4} \times 48.75=73.2 \times 10^{5} kN / m\)

spring constant of pad

\(k_{2}=\frac{E_{p} A_{p}}{t_{p}}=\frac{5 \times 10^{5} \times 5.5}{0.60}=46 \times 10^{5} kN / m\)

square of the limiting frequency of anvil

\(\omega_{a}^{2}=\frac{k_{2}}{M_{a}}=\frac{46 \times 10^{5} \times 9.81}{1000}=46 \times 981=45,126 / sec ^{2}\)

Mass of foundation and backfill \(\left(M_{f}\right)=\frac{3052}{9.81} kN \sec ^{2} / m\)

Frequencies
Square of the limiting frequency of the whole system

\(\omega_{l}^{2}=\frac{k_{1}}{\left(M_{a}+M_{f}\right)}=\frac{73.2 \times 10^{5}}{(4052 / 9.81)}=17722 sec ^{-2}\)

Ratio \(\lambda_{1}=\frac{M_{a}}{M_{f}}=\frac{W_{a}}{W_{f}}=\frac{1000}{3052}=0.327654\)

The frequency equation is

\(\omega_{n}{ }^{4}-\left(1+\lambda_{1}\right)\left(\omega_{a}{ }^{2}+\omega_{l}{ }^{2}\right) \omega_{n}{ }^{2}+\left(1+\lambda_{1}\right) \omega_{a}{ }^{2} \omega_{l}{ }^{2}=0\)

Substituting \(\omega_{a}^{2}=45,126, \omega_{l}^{2}=17,722, \lambda_{1}=0.327654\),

\(\omega_{n}{ }^{4}=(1+0.327654)(45126+17722) \omega_{n}{ }^{2}+(1+0.327654)(45126)(17722)=0\)

Solving, \(\omega_{n 1}^{2}=67.7744 \times 10^{3} sec ^{-2} \text { and } \omega_{n 2}^{2}=15.6660 \times 10^{3} sec ^{-2}\)

or \(\omega_{n 1}=260.335 rad / s \text { and } \omega_{n 2}=125.164 rad / s\)

Initial Velocity of Anvil after Impact

\(\begin{aligned}&\upsilon _{a}=\frac{(1+e)}{\left(1+\lambda_{1}\right)} \upsilon \\&\lambda_{1}=\frac{M_{a}}{M_{t}}=\frac{W_{a}}{W_{t}}=\frac{1000}{50}=20\end{aligned}\)

Substituting \(e=0.5, \upsilon =6.2 m / s , \text { and } \lambda_{1}=20\),

\(\upsilon_{a}=\frac{(1+0.5)}{(1+20)} \times 6.2=0.443 m / s\)

Amplitudes
The amplitude for the foundation-soil system

\(A_{1}=-\frac{\left(\omega_{a}{ }^{2}-\omega_{n 2}{ }^{2}\right)\left(\omega_{a}{ }^{2}-\omega_{n 1}{ }^{2}\right)}{\omega_{a}{ }^{2}\left(\omega_{n 1}{ }^{2}-\omega_{n 2}{ }^{2}\right) \omega_{n 2}} \upsilon_{a}\)

Substituting \(\omega_{a}^{2}=45.126 \times 10^{3}, \omega_{n 1}^{2}=67.7744 \times 10^{3}, \omega_{n 2}^{2}=15.666 \times 10^{3}\)

and \(upsilon_{a}=0.443 m / s\),

\(A_{1}=-\frac{10^{6}(45.126-15.666)(45.126-67.7744)(0.443)}{10^{6} \times 45.126(67.7744-15.6660)(125.164)} m\)

= 1.00 mm
The amplitude of the anvil

\(\begin{aligned}A_{a} &=-\frac{\left(\omega_{a}{ }^{2}-\omega_{n 1}{ }^{2}\right)}{\left(\omega_{n 1}{ }^{2}-\omega_{n 2}{ }^{2}\right) \omega_{n 2}} \cdot \upsilon _{a} \\&=-\frac{10^{3}(45.126-67.7744)(0.443)}{10^{3}(67.7744-15.6660)(125.164)} m\end{aligned}\)

= 1.54 mm
Stress in the Elastic Pad

\(\sigma_{p}=\frac{k_{2}\left(A_{a}-A_{1}\right)}{A_{p}}\)

Substituting \(k_{2}=46 \times 10^{5} kN / m , A_{a}=1.54 \times 10^{-3} m , A_{1}=1.00 \times 10^{-3} m\),

and \(A_{p}=5.5 m ^{2}\),

\(\sigma_{p}=\frac{46 \times 10^{5}(1.54-100) \times 10^{-3}}{5.5} kN / m ^{2}\),

= 451.64 kN/\(m ^{2}\)

Final Values of Salient Quantities
The Size of foundation: 7.5 m × 6.5 m × 1.5 m high.
Estimated amplitude of motion of the foundation: 1.00 mm
Estimated stress in the elastic pad: 452 kN/\(m ^{2}\).
