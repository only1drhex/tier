Calculating the Average Pressure and Force Exerted: What Force Must a Dam Withstand?

In , we calculated the mass of water in a large reservoir. We will now consider the pressure and force acting on the dam retaining water. (See Figure 11.11.) The dam is 500 m wide, and the water is 80.0 m deep at the dam. (a) What is the average pressure on the dam due to the water? (b) Calculate the force exerted against the dam and compare it with the weight of water in the dam (previously found to be \(\left.1.96 \times 10^{13} N \right)\).

Strategy for (a)

The average pressure \(\bar{P}\) due to the weight of the water is the pressure at the average depth \(\bar{h}\) of 40.0 m, since pressure increases linearly with depth.

 

Strategy for (b)
The force exerted on the dam by the water is the average pressure times the area of contact:

 

\(F=\bar{P} A\) (11.20)

Admin
Solution

Solution for (a)
The average pressure due to the weight of a fluid is

 

\(\bar{P}=\bar{h} \rho g. \) (11.18)

 

Entering the density of water from Table 11.1 and taking \(\bar{h}\) to be the average depth of 40.0 m, we obtain

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{P} &=(40.0 m )\left(10^{3} \frac{ kg }{ m ^{3}}\right)\left(9.80 \frac{ m }{ s ^{2}}\right) \\&=3.92 \times 10^{5} \frac{ N }{ m ^{2}}=392 kPa\end{aligned}\) (11.19)

 

Solution for (b)
We have already found the value for \(\bar{P}\). The area of the dam is \(A=80.0 m \times 500 m =4.00 \times 10^{4} m ^{2}\), so that

 

\(\begin{aligned}F &=\left(3.92 \times 10^{5} N / m ^{2}\right)\left(4.00 \times 10^{4} m ^{2}\right) \\&=1.57 \times 10^{10} N\end{aligned}\) (11.21)

 

Discussion

Although this force seems large, it is small compared with the 1.96×10¹³ N weight of the water in the reservoir—in fact, it is only 0.0800% of the weight. Note that the pressure found in part (a) is completely independent of the width and length of the lake—it depends only on its average depth at the dam. Thus the force depends only on the water’s average depth and the dimensions of the dam, not on the horizontal extent of the reservoir.
