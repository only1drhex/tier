Analyzing an Integrating Amplifier
Assume that the numerical values for the signal voltage shown in Fig. 7.41 are \(V_m = 50\) mV and \( t_1 \)= 1 s. This signal voltage is applied to the integrating-amplifier circuit shown in Fig. 7.40. The circuit parameters of the amplifier are \(R_s = 100\) kΩ , and \( C_f = 0.1\) mF, \(V_{CC} = 6\) V. The initial voltage on the capacitor is zero.

a) Calculate \(v_o(t).\)

b) Plot \(v_o(t)\) versus t.

Admin
Solution

a) For 0 ≤t ≤ 1 s,

\(v_o=\frac{-1}{(100 \times 10^3)(0.1 \times 10^{-6})}50 \times 10^{-3}t + 0 \)
= -5t V, \(0 \leq t\leq 1 s. \)

For 1 ≤t ≤ 2 s,

\(v_o = (5t – 10) \ V.\)
