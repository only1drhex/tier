Guest Question.

The steel frame shown in Fig. P.18.19 has members with different values of plastic moment as shown. The forces P and W may act independently or together, the load factor λ applying to both forces. Initially, calculate the value of \(M_{ P }\), the plastic moment parameter, that will just prevent plastic collapse of the frame when the vertical forces W, alone, are applied and the load factor is 1.5.
Also show that the maximum value of M that can be applied without plastic collapse occurring is 0.604W when both P andWare applied together with a load factor of 1.2 and where \(M_{ P }=0.5 WL\). Finally, calculate the support reactions.

Ans. \(M_{ P }=0.5 WL , R_{ H , A }=0.225 W , R_{ V , A }=1.05 W , R_{ H , G }=0.5 W , R_{ V , G }=1.35 W\).

Admin
Solution

Case (a). The collapse mechanism is shown in Fig. S.18.19(a).

Then

\(\lambda W(\theta L+\theta L / 2)=M_{ P } \theta+2 M_{ P } 3 \theta / 2+M_{ P } \theta / 2\)

so that

\(1.5 \lambda W \theta L=4.5 M_{ P } \theta\)

For λ = 1.5

\(M_{ P }=0.5 W L\)

Case (b). The sway mechanism for the frame is shown in Fig. S.18.19(b).
Then

\(2 \lambda P \theta L=4 M_{ P } \theta\),

which gives, for λ = 1.2 and \(M_{ P }=0.5 WL\)

P = 0.833 W

The combined beam plus sway mechanism is shown in Fig. S.18.19(c).

Then

\(2 \lambda P \theta L=4 M_{ P } \theta-M_{ P } \theta\)

and

\(1.5 \lambda W \theta L=4.5 M_{ P } \theta-M_{ P } \theta\)

Substituting λ = 1.2 and solving gives

P = 0.604 W,
which is clearly the critical value of P.
The support reactions are now calculated as follows. From Fig. S.18.19(d) and taking moments about F

\(R_{ G , H } 2 L=2 M_{ P }\)

so that

\(R_{ G , H }=0.5 W\)

Then

\(R_{ A , H }=1.2 \times 0.604 W -0.5 W =0.225 W\)

Now referring to Fig. S.18.19(e)

\(2 M_{ P }+1.2 W L+R_{ G , H } 2 L=M_{ P }+R_{ G , V } 2 L\),

which gives

\(R_{ G , V }=1.35 W\)

Then

\(R_{ A , V }=2.4 W -1.35 W =1.05 W\)
