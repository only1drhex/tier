Guest Question.

A beam has the singly symmetrical thin-walled cross section shown in Fig. P.10.7. Each wall of the section is flat and has the same length, a, and thickness, t. Determine the shear flow distribution round the section due to a vertical shear load, \( S_{y} \), applied through the shear centre and find the distance of the shear centre from the point C.

Admin
Solution

The beam section is singly symmetrical so that the shear centre lies on the axis of symmetry, the z axis. The shear flow distribution is given by Eq. (10.22) which, since \( I_{z y}=0 \text { and only } S_{y} \) is applied, reduces to

\( q_{s}=\frac{\left(S_{y} I_{z y}-S_{z} I_{z}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s+\frac{\left(S_{z} I_{z y}-S_{y} I_{y}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s \) (10.22)

\( q_{s}=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s \) (i)

where

\( I_{z}=2 \int_{0}^{a} t s^{2} \sin ^{2} \alpha d s+2 \int_{0}^{a} t(a \sin \alpha+s \sin \alpha)^{2} d s \)

In the first term on the right hand side of the expression for \(I_{z} \), s is measured in the direction CB from C and in the second term s is measured from B in the direction BA.
Then

\( I_{z}=\frac{16 a^{3} t \sin ^{2} \alpha}{3} \)

In AB,

\( y=a \sin \alpha+\left(a-s_{ A }\right) \sin \alpha=\left(2 a-s_{ A }\right) \sin \alpha \)

Substituting in Eq. (i)

\( q_{ AB }=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \int_{0}^{s} t\left(2 a-s_{ A }\right) \sin \alpha d s \)

which gives

\(q_{ AB }=\frac{-3 S_{y}\left(2 a s-\frac{s_{ A }^{2}}{2}\right)}{16 a^{3} \sin \alpha}\)

When \( s_{ A }=0, q_{ AB }=0 \text { and when } s_{ A }=a, q_{ AB }=-9 S_{y} / 32 a \sin \alpha \).
In BC, \( y=\left(a-s_{ B }\right) \sin \alpha \). Then, from Eq. (i)

\( q_{ BC }=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \int_{0}^{s} t\left(a-s_{ B }\right) \sin \alpha d s-\frac{9 S_{y}}{32 a \sin \alpha}\)

i.e.

\( q_{ BC }=\frac{-3 S_{y}\left(\frac{3}{2}+\frac{s_{ B }}{a}-\frac{s_{ B }^{2}}{2 a^{2}}\right)}{16 a \sin \alpha} \)

When \( s_{ B }=0, q_{ BC }=-9 S_{y} / 32 a \sin \alpha \text { and when } s_{ B }=a, q_{ BC }=-3 S_{y} / 8 a \sin \alpha \).
Taking moments about C

\( S_{y} z_{S}=-2 \int_{0}^{a} q_{ AB } a \sin 2 \alpha d s \)

Substituting for \( q_{ AB } \) from the above and integrating

\( S_{y} z_{S}=\frac{3 S_{y} \cos \alpha}{4 a^{2}}\left[a s^{2}-\frac{s_{A}^{3}}{6}\right]_{0}^{a} \)

which gives

\( z_{S}=\frac{5 a \cos \alpha}{8} \)
