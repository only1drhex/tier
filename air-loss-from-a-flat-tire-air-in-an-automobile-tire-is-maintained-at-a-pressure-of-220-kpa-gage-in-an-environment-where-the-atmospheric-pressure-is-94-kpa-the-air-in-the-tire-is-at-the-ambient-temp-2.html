Air Loss from a Flat Tire
Air in an automobile tire is maintained at a pressure of 220 kPa (gage) in an environment where the atmospheric pressure is 94 kPa. The air in the tire is at the ambient temperature of 25째C. A 4-mm-diameter leak develops in the tire as a result of an accident (Fig. 17-25). Approximating the flow as isentropic, determine the initial mass flow rate of air through the leak.

Admin
Solution

A leak develops in an automobile tire as a result of an accident. The initial mass flow rate of air through the leak is to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Air is an ideal gas with constant specific heats. 2 Flow of air through the hole is isentropic.

Properties The specific gas constant of air is R = 0.287 kPa-m\(^3\)/kg.K. The specific heat ratio of air at room temperature is k = 1.4.

Analysis The absolute pressure in the tire is

 

\(P=P_{\text {gage }}+P_{\text {atm }}=220+94=314 \mathrm{kPa}\)

 

The critical pressure is (from Table 17-2)

\(P^{*}=0.5283 P_{o}=(0.5283)(314 \mathrm{kPa})=166 \mathrm{kPa}>94 \mathrm{kPa}\)

 

 

Therefore, the flow is choked, and the velocity at the exit of the hole is the sonic speed. Then the flow properties at the exit become

\(\rho_{0}=\frac{P_{0}}{R T_{0}}=\frac{314 \mathrm{kPa}}{\left(0.287 \mathrm{kPa} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}\right)(298 \mathrm{~K})}=3.671 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \)

 

\(\rho^{*}=\rho\left(\frac{2}{k+1}\right)^{1 /(k-1)}=\left(3.67 \mathrm{lkg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\right)\left(\frac{2}{1.4+1}\right)^{1 /(14-1)}=2.327 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \)

 

\(T^{*}=\frac{2}{k+1} T_{0}=\frac{2}{1.4+1}(298 \mathrm{~K})=248.3 \mathrm{~K} \)

 

\(V=c=\sqrt{k R T^{*}}=\sqrt{(1.4)(0.287 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K})\left(\frac{1000 \mathrm{~m}^{2} / \mathrm{s}^{2}}{1 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}}\right)(248.3 \mathrm{~K})} \)

 

\(=315.9 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)

 

Then the initial mass flow rate through the hole is

\(\dot{m}=\rho A V =\left(2.327 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\right)\left[\pi(0.004 \mathrm{~m})^{2} / 4\right](315.9 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s})=0.00924 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s} \)

 

\(=0.554 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{min}\)

 

 

Discussion The mass flow rate decreases with time as the pressure inside the tire drops.
