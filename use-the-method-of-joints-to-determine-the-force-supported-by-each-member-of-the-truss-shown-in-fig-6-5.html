Guest Question.

Use the method of joints to determine the force supported by each member of the truss shown in Fig. 6.5.

Admin
Solution

We begin by obtaining the support reactions for the truss by drawing the FBD of the structure as a whole as shown in Fig. 6.6. Writing equilibrium equations provides

\(\sum{M_A} = 0\): \(-(10 kN)(6m) + E_y(12m)\)

 

\(+ (3 kN)(4m) = 0 \Rightarrow E_y = 4 kN\), (6.1)

\(\sum{F_y} = 0\): \(A_y + E_y – 10 kN = 0 \Rightarrow A_y = 6 kN\), (6.2)

\(\sum{F_x} = 0\): \(A_x – 3 kN = 0 \Rightarrow A_x = 3 kN\). (6.3)

Next, we draw FBDs of each joint (pin) as shown in Fig. 6.7. Since each joint has a concurrent force system, each joint is treated as a particle in equilibrium, and hence each joint permits two equilibrium equations to be written. Among the many joints that the truss has, a good strategy is to always (if possible) select a joint that has no more than two unknowns, since then the unknowns may be immediately solved for. Among the five possible joints shown in Fig. 6.7, joints A and E are the only two having only two unknowns. Noting that \(\alpha \tan^{-1}(4/3) = 53.1Â°\), we select joint A and write*

Joint A:

\(\sum{F_y} = 0\): \(6 kN + F_{AB} \sin\alpha = 0 \Rightarrow F_{AB} = -7.5 kN\), (6.4)

\(\sum{F_x} = 0\): \(3 kN + F_{AB} \cos\alpha + F_{AC} = 0 \Rightarrow F_{AC} = 1.5 kN\). (6.5)

We may now repeat this procedure for joint E, but we will continue with joint B instead, as now it has only two unknowns. Using the FBD for joint B gives

Joint B:

\(\sum{F_y} = 0\): \(-F_{AB} \sin\alpha – F_{BC} \sin\alpha = 0 \Rightarrow F_{BC} = 7.5 kN\), (6.6)

\(\sum{F_x} = 0\): \(-F_{AB} \cos\alpha + F_{BC} \cos\alpha + F_{BD} = 0\)

 

\(\Rightarrow F_{BD} = -9.0 kN\). (6.7)

Joints C, D, and E remain to be analyzed, and of these, joints D and E both have two unknowns. Furthermore, examination of the FBDs for joints D and E shows that E will entail slightly less algebra to solve for its unknowns, and thus we elect to write equilibrium equations for it.

Joint E:

\(\sum{F_y} = 0\): \(4 kN + F_{DE} \sin\alpha = 0 \Rightarrow F_{DE} = -5 kN\); (6.8)

\(\sum{F_x} = 0\): \(-F_{CE} – F_{DE} \cos\alpha = 0 \Rightarrow F_{CE} = 3 kN\). (6.9)

There is now only one unknown remaining, namely, \(F_{CD}\), and either joint C or D may be used to determine it. We select D because its FBD has one less force than the FBD for joint C. Thus

Joint D:

\(\sum{F_y} = 0\): \(-F_{CD} \sin\alpha – F_{DE} \sin\alpha = 0 \Rightarrow F_{CD} = 5 kN\). (6.10)

* Because of the nice geometry of this truss, we could avoid computing \(\alpha\) and use \(\cos \alpha = 3/5\) and \(\sin \alpha = 4/5\).
