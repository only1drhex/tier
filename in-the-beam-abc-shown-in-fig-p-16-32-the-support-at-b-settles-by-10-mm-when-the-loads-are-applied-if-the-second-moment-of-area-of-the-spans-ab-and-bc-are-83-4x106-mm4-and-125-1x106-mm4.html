Guest Question.

In the beam ABC shown in Fig. P.16.32 the support at B settles by 10 mm when the loads are applied. If the second moment of area of the spans AB and BC are \(83.4 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\) and \(125.1 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\), respectively, and Young’s modulus, E,of the material of the beam is \(207000 N / mm ^{2}\), calculate the support reactions using the moment distribution method.
Ans. \(R_{ C }=28.6 kN , R_{ B }=15.8 kN , R_{ A }=30.5 kN , M_{ A }=53.9 kNm\).

Admin
Solution

The distribution factors are

\(DF _{ BA }=\left(\frac{4 \times 83.4 \times 10^{6}}{4}\right) /\left[\left(\frac{4 \times 83.4 \times 10^{6}}{4}\right)+\left(\frac{3 \times 125.1 \times 10^{6}}{5}\right)\right]=0.53\)

so that \(DF _{ BC }=0.47\). Note that E divides out of the above expression.
The fixed end moments due to the applied loads and the sinking support at B are obtained from Table 16.6

FEMs
Local case \(M_{A B}^{F}\) \(M_{B A}^{F}\)
\(-\frac{W L}{8}\) \(+\frac{W L}{8}\)
\(-\frac{W a b^{2}}{L^{2}}\) \(+\frac{W a^{2} b}{L^{2}}\)
\(-\frac{w L^{2}}{12}\) \(+\frac{W L^{2}}{12}\)
\(-\frac{W}{L^{2}}\left[\frac{L^{2}}{2}\left(b^{2}-a^{2}\right)-\frac{2}{3} L\left(b^{3}-a^{3}\right)\right.\)

 

\(\left.+\frac{1}{4}\left(b^{4}-a^{4}\right)\right]\)

 \(+\frac{w b^{3}}{L^{2}}\left(\frac{L}{3}-\frac{b}{4}\right)\)
\(+\frac{M_{0} b}{L^{2}}(2 a-b)\) \(+\frac{M_{0} a}{L^{2}}(2 b-a)\)
\(-\frac{6 E / \delta}{L^{2}}\) \(-\frac{6 E / \delta}{L^{2}}\)
0 \(-\frac{3 E / \delta}{L^{2}}\)

and are

\(M_{ AB }^{ F }=\frac{6.25 \times 4^{2}}{12}+\frac{6 \times 207000 \times 10^{3} \times 83.4 \times 10^{6} \times 10^{-12} \times 0.01}{4^{2}}\)

= 8.3 + 64.8 = 73.1 kNm

\(M_{ BA }^{ F }=-8.3+64.8=56.5 kNm\)

 

\(M_{ BC }^{ F }=\frac{50 \times 5}{8}-\frac{3 \times 207000 \times 10^{3} \times 125.1 \times 10^{6} \times 10^{-12} \times 0.01}{5^{2}}\)

= 31.25 – 31.07 = 0.2 kNm

\(M_{ CB }^{ F }=-31.25 kNm\)

The moment distribution is carried out in Table S.16.32.

The support reactions then follow as before, i.e.

\(R_{ A }=\frac{6.25 \times 4}{2}+\frac{(53.9+18.2)}{4}=30.5 kN\) with a moment reaction of 53.9 kNm

\(R_{ B }=\frac{6.25 \times 4}{2}+\frac{50}{2}-\frac{18.2}{5}-\frac{(53.9+18.2)}{4}=15.8 kN\)

 

\(R_{ C }=\frac{50}{2}+\frac{18.2}{5}=28.6 kN\)
