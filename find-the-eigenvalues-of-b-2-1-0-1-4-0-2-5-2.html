Guest Question.

Find the eigenvalues of

\(B=\left[\begin{array}{lll}2 & 1 & 0 \\1 & 4 & 0 \\2 & 5 & 2\end{array}\right]\)
Admin
Solution

The characteristic polynomial is given by

 

\(\operatorname{det}|B-\lambda I|\)

 

where I is the 3 × 3 identity matrix and

 

\(\begin{aligned}I=& {\left[\begin{array}{lll}1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 1\end{array}\right] } \\\Rightarrow \lambda I=& {\left[\begin{array}{lll}\lambda & 0 & 0 \\0 & \lambda & 0 \\0 & 0 & \lambda\end{array}\right] }\end{aligned}\)

 

Therefore the characteristic polynomial is

 

\(\begin{aligned}\operatorname{det}|B-\lambda I| &=\operatorname{det}\left|\left[\begin{array}{ccc}2 & 1 & 0 \\1 & 4 & 0 \\2 & 5 & 2\end{array}\right]-\left[\begin{array}{ccc}\lambda & 0 & 0 \\0 & \lambda & 0 \\0 & 0 & \lambda\end{array}\right]\right| \\&=\operatorname{det}\left|\left[\begin{array}{ccc}2-\lambda & 1 & 0 \\1 & 4-\lambda & 0 \\2 & 5 & 2-\lambda\end{array}\right]\right|\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{l}=(2-\lambda) \operatorname{det}\left|\begin{array}{cc}4-\lambda & 1 \\5 & 2-\lambda\end{array}\right|-\operatorname{det}\left|\begin{array}{cc}1 & 0 \\2 & 2-\lambda\end{array}\right| \\=(2-\lambda)[(4-\lambda)(2-\lambda)-5]-(2-\lambda) \\=(2-\lambda)[(4-\lambda)(2-\lambda)-5-1] \\=(2-\lambda)\left[8-6 \lambda+\lambda^{2}-6\right] \\=(2-\lambda)\left[\lambda^{2}-6 \lambda+2\right]\end{array}\)

 

The characteristic equation is obtained by setting this equal to zero

 

\((2-\lambda)\left[\lambda^{2}-6 \lambda+2\right]=0\)

 

Each term in the product must be zero. We immediately obtain the first eigenvalue by setting the term on the left equal to zero

 

\(\begin{array}{l}2-\lambda=0 \\\Rightarrow \lambda_{1}=2\end{array}\)

 

To find the other eigenvalues, we solve

 

\(\lambda^{2}-6 \lambda+2=0\)

 

We find the solutions by recalling the quadratic formula. If the equation is

 

\(a \lambda^{2}+b \lambda+c=0\)

 

then

 

\(\lambda_{2,3}=\frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^{2}-4 a c}}{2 a}\)

 

In this case, the quadratic formula gives the solutions

 

\(\lambda_{2}=\frac{6+\sqrt{36-4(2)}}{2}=\frac{6+\sqrt{28}}{2}=\frac{6+\sqrt{(4)(7)}}{2}=\frac{6+2 \sqrt{7}}{2}=3+\sqrt{7}\)

 

\(\lambda_{3}=\frac{6-\sqrt{36-4(2)}}{2}=\frac{6-\sqrt{28}}{2}=3-\sqrt{7}\)

 

Notice that since B is a 3 × 3 matrix, it has three eigenvalues.
