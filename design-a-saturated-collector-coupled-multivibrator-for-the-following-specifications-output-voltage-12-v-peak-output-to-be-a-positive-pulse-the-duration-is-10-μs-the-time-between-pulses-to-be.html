Guest Question.

Design a saturated collector coupled multivibrator for the following specifications: Output voltage 12 V peak; Output to be a positive pulse; the duration is 10 μs; the time between pulses to be 20 μs. For the BJTs is used, \(h_{FE}\) (min) = 100; \(I_{CBO}\) = 0 and \(I_{C(ON)}\) = 1 mA.

Admin
Solution

To design a saturated collector coupled astable multivibrator
Given:

\(V_{cc}\) = 12 V (peak)
\(I_{C}\) (on) = 1 mA, \(h_{fe (min)}\) = 100
\(I_{CBO} = 0, T_{ON}\) = 10 μs, \(T_{OFF}\) = 20 μs

Let us assume that \(V_{CE}(sat)\) = 0.2 V
Refer to Fig. 16.62.
Here, ‘C’ can be kept constant and timing resistor ‘R’ can be varied to get appropriate \(T_{on}, T_{off}\) (or) ‘R’ can be kept constant ‘C’ can be varied.

Now R ≤ \(h_{fe} R_{c}\). Therefore, it is better to keep ‘R’ constant.

\(R_{C} =\frac{V_{CC} – V_{c2(sat)}}{I_{C(ON)}}\)

Assuminmg \(V_{C2}\) (sat) ≈ 0.2 V,

\(R_{C} =\frac{12 – 0.2}{1 × 10^{– 3}}\)= 11.8 kΩ
R ≤ \(h_{fe} R_{C}\)
≤ 100 × 11.8 × \(10^{3}\)
≤ 1.18 MΩ

Hence, let us assume that R = \(R_{1} = R_{2}\) = 1 MΩ

\(T_{OFF} = 0.693 RC_{1}\)
20 × \(10^{– 6} = 0.693 × 10^{6}× C_{1}\)

Therefore, \(C_{1}\) = 29 pF

\(T_{ON} = 0.693 RC_{2}\)
10 × \(10^{– 6} = 0.693 × 10^{6} × C_{2}\)

Therefore, \(C_{2}\) = 14.5 pF
