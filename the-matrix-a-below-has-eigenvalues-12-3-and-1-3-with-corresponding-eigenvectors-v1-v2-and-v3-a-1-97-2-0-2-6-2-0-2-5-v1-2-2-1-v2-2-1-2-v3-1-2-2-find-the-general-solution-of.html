Guest Question.

The matrix A below has eigenvalues \( 1 , \frac{2}{3} , \text{and} \frac{1}{3} \) , with corresponding eigenvectors \( v_{1} , v_{2} , \text{and} v_{3} \) :

\( A = \frac{1}{9}\left [ \begin{matrix} 7 & -2 & 0 \\ -2 & 6 & 2 \\ 0 & 2 & 5 \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} -2 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{3} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ -2 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Find the general solution of the equation \( x_{k+1} = Ax_{k} \text{if} x_{0} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 11 \\ -2 \end{matrix} \right ] \) .

Admin
Solution

The first step is to write \( x_{0} \) as a linear combination of \( v_{1} , v_{2} , \text{and} v_{3} \). Row reduction of \( \left [ \begin{matrix} v_{1} & v_{2} & v_{3} & x_{0} \end{matrix} \right ] \) produces the weights \( c_{1} = 2 , c_{2} = 1 , \text{and} c_{3} = 3 \) , so that

\( x_{0} = 2v_{1} + 1v_{2} + 3v_{3} \)

Since the eigenvalues are \( 1 , \frac{2}{3} , \text{and} \frac{1}{3} \) , the general solution is

\( x_{k} = 2 \cdot 1^{k}v_{1} + 1 \cdot \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{k}v_{2} + 3 \cdot \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{k}v_{3} \)

\( = 2 \left [ \begin{matrix} -2 \\ 2 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ] + \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{k}\left [ \begin{matrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right ] + 3 \cdot \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{k}\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ -2 \end{matrix} \right ] \) (12)
