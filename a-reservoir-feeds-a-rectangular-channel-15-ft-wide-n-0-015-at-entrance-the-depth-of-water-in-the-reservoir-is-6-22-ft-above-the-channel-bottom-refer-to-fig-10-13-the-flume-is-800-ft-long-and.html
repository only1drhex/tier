Guest Question.

A reservoir feeds a rectangular channel 15 ft wide, n = 0.015. At entrance, the depth of water in the reservoir is 6.22 ft above the channel bottom. (Refer to Fig. 10-13.) The flume is 800 ft long and drops 0.72 ft in this length. The depth behind a weir at the discharge end of the channel is 4.12 ft. Determine, using one reach, the capacity of the channel, assuming the loss at entrance to be \(0.25 V_{1}^{2} / 2 g\).

Admin
Solution

The Bernoulli equation, A to 1, datum 1, gives

\((0+\text { negl. }+6.22)-0.25 V_{1}^{2} / 2 g=\left(0+V_{1}^{2} / 2 g+y_{1}\right)\) (1).

and

\(L=\frac{\left(V_{2}^{2} / 2 g+y_{2}\right)-\left(V_{1}^{2} / 2 g+y_{1}\right)}{S_{0}-\left(\frac{n V_{m}}{1.486 R_{m}^{2 / 3}}\right)^{2}}\) (2).

Solve these equations by successive trials until L approximates or equals 800 ft.

\(\text { Try } y_{1}=5.0 ft \text {; then from }(1), V_{1}^{2} / 2 g=(6.22-5.00) / 1.25=0.976 ft , V_{1}=7.93 ft / sec \text {, and } q=y_{1} V_{1}\)\(=(5.0)(7.93)=39.6 cfs / ft , V_{2}=39.6 / 4.12=9.61 ft / sec\).

\(V_{\text {mean }}=\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)(7.93+9.61)=8.77 ft / sec\).

and

\(R_{\text {mean }}=\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)\left(R_{ 1 }+R_{2}\right)=\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)[(15 \times 5) / 25+(15 \times 4.12) / 23.24]=2.83 ft\).

Substituting in equation (2) above, we find L = 399 ft.
Increase the value of \(y_{1}\) to 5.50 ft and repeat the calculation. Results in tabular form are:

\( \begin{array}{cccccccc} y_{1} & V_{1} & q_{1} & V_{2} & V_{m} & R_{m} & L & \text { Notes } \\ 5.50 & 6.09 & 33.5 & 8.13 & 7.11 & 2.92 & 2782 ft & \therefore \text { decrease } y_{1} \\ 5.20 & 7.25 & 37.7 & 9.15 & 8.20 & 2.87 & 764 ft & \text { close enough } \end{array} \)

The capacity of the channel = 37.7 × 15 = 566 cfs.
Should greater accuracy be required, start at the lower end and, for unit flow q = 37.7 cfs, find the length of the reach to a point where the depth is about 10% more than 4.12 or about 4.50 ft, thence to a depth of 4.90 ft, and so on. If the sum of the lengths exceeds 800 ft, decrease the value of \(y_{1},\), obtaining an increase in q.
