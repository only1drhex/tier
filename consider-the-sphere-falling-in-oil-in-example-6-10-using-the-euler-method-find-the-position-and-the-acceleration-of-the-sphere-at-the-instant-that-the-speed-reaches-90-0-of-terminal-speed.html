Guest Question.

Consider the sphere falling in oil in . Using the Euler method, find the position and the acceleration of the sphere at the instant that the speed reaches 90.0% of terminal speed.

Admin
Solution

The net force on the sphere is

\( \Sigma F=-m g+b v \).

Thus, the acceleration values in the last column of Table 6.3 are

Table 6.3
The Euler Method for Solving Dynamics Problems
Acceleration Velocity Position Time Step
\(a_{0}=F\left(x_{0}, v_{0}, t_{0}\right) / m\) \(v_{0}\) \(x_{0}\) \(t_{0}\) 0
\(a_{1}=F\left(x_{1}, v_{1}, t_{1}\right) / m\) \(v_{1}=v_{0}+a_{0} \Delta t \) \(x_{1}=x_{0}+v_{0} \Delta t\) \(t_{1}=t_{0}+\Delta t\) 1
\(a_{2}=F\left(x_{2}, v_{2}, t_{2}\right) / m\) \(v_{2}=v_{1}+a_{1} \Delta t \) \(x_{2}=x_{1}+v_{1} \Delta t\) \(t_{2}=t_{1}+\Delta t\) 2
\(a_{3}=F\left(x_{3}, v_{3}, t_{2}\right) / m\) \(v_{3}=v_{2}+a_{2} \Delta t \) \(x_{3}=x_{2}+v_{2} \Delta t\) \(t_{3}=t_{2}+\Delta t\) 3
\( \vdots \) \( \vdots \) \( \vdots \) \( \vdots \) \( \vdots \)
\(a_{n}\) \(v_{n}\) \(x_{n}\) \(t_{n}\) n

\( a=\frac{\Sigma F(x, v, t)}{m}=\frac{-m g+b v}{m}=-g+\frac{b v}{m} \).

Choosing a time increment of 0.1 ms, the first few lines of the spreadsheet modeled after Table 6.3 look like Table 6.4. We see that the speed is increasing while the magnitude of the acceleration is decreasing due to the resistive force. We also see that the sphere does not fall very far in the first millisecond.
Further down the spreadsheet, as shown in Table 6.5, we find the instant at which the sphere reaches the speed \( 0.900 v_{T} \text {, which is } 0.900 \times 5.00 cm / s =4.50 cm / s \text {. This } \) calculation shows that this occurs at t = 11.6 ms, which agrees within its uncertainty with the value obtained in . The 0.1-ms difference in the two values is due to the approximate nature of the Euler method. If a smaller time increment were used, the instant at which the speed reaches \( 0.900 v_{T} \) approaches the value calculated in .
From Table 6.5, we see that the position and acceleration of the sphere when it reaches a speed of \( 0.900 v_{T} \text { are } \)

\( y=-0.035 cm \quad \text { and } \quad a=-99 cm / s ^{2} \).
