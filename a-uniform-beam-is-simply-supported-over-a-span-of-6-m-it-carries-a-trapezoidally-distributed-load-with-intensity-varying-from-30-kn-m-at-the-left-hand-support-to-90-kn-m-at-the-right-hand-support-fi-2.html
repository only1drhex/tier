Guest Question.

A uniform beam is simply supported over a span of 6 m. It carries a trapezoidally distributed load with intensity varying from 30 kN/m at the left-hand support to 90 kN/m at the right-hand support. Find the equation of the deflection curve and hence the deflection at the mid-span point. The second moment of area of the cross section of the beam is \(120 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\) and Young’s modulus \(E=206000 N / mm ^{2}\).
Ans. 41 mm (downwards).

Admin
Solution

The beam is as shown in Fig. S.13.3.

Taking moments about B

\(R_{ A } \times 6-\frac{30 \times 6^{2}}{2}-\frac{60}{2} \times 6 \times \frac{6}{3}=0\)

which gives

\(R_{ A }=150 kN\)

The bending moment at any section a distance x from A is then

\(M_{x}=150 x-\frac{30 x^{2}}{2}-(90-30)\left(\frac{x}{6}\right)\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)\left(\frac{x}{3}\right)\)

i.e.

\(M_{x}=150 x-15 x^{2}-\frac{5 x^{3}}{3}\)

Substituting in Eq. (13.3)

\(\frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=\frac{M}{E I}\) (13.3)

\(E I\left(\frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}\right)=150 x-15 x^{2}-\frac{5 x^{3}}{3}\)

 

\(E I\left(\frac{ d v}{ d x}\right)=75 x^{2}-5 x^{3}-\frac{5 x^{4}}{12}+ C _{1}\)

 

\(E I v=25 x^{3}-\frac{5 x^{4}}{4}-\frac{x^{5}}{12}+C_{1} x+C_{2}\)

When x = 0, υ = 0 so that \(C_{2}=0\) and when x = 6 m, υ = 0. Then

\(0=25 \times 6^{3}-\frac{5 \times 6^{4}}{4}-\frac{6^{5}}{12}+6 C_{1}\)

from which

\(C_{1}=-522\)

and the deflected shape of the beam is given by

\(E I v=25 x^{3}-\frac{5 x^{4}}{4}-\frac{x^{5}}{12}-522 x\)

The deflection at the mid-span point is then

\(E I v_{\text {mid }-\text { span }}=25 \times 3^{3}-\frac{5 \times 3^{4}}{4}-\frac{3^{5}}{12}-522 \times 3=-1012.5 kNm ^{3} \text { units }\)

Therefore

\(v_{\text {mid-span }}=\frac{-1012.5 \times 10^{12}}{120 \times 10^{6} \times 206000}=-41.0 mm\) (downwards).
