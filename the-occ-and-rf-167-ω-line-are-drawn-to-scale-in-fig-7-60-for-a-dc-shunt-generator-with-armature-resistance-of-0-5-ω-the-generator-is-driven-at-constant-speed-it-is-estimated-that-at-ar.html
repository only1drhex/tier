Guest Question.

The OCC and \( R_{f} = 167 \Omega\) line are drawn to scale in Fig. 7.60 for a dc shunt generator with armature resistance of \( 0.5 \Omega\). The generator is driven at constant speed. It is estimated that at armature current of 40 A, the armature reaction demagnetization has field current equivalent of 0.14 A.

Estimate thee acceptable terminal voltage when the armature is delivering 50 A. Also find the line current.

Admin
Solution

Using proportionality

\( I_{fd}\mid _{at I_{a}=50A} = 0.14 \times \frac{50}{40} = 0.175 A\)

 

\( I_{a}R_{a}=50 \times 0.5 = 25 V\)

Choose an arbitrary point \( \acute{D}\) on the \( R_{f}\) line. Draw a right angle triangle \( \acute{D} AD\) with \( \acute{D} A = 0.175 A\) (horizontal) \( \angle \acute{D} AD = 90°\), AD = 25 (vertical). Through D draw a line parallel to the \( R_{f}\) line interacting the OCC at R (higher voltage point) Now draw \( R\acute{R}\) parallel to \( D\acute{D} \) locating \( \acute{R} \) on the \( R_{f}\) line. Reading from \( \acute{R} \)

V (terminal) = 232.5 V

\( I_{f} = 1.5 A\)

Then \(I_{L} = I_{a} –I_{f} = 50 –1.5 A = 48.5A\)
