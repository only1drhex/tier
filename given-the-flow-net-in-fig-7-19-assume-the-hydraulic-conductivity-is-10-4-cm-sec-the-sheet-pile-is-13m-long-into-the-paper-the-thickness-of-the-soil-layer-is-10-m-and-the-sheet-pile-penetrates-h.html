Guest Question.

Given:

The flow net in Fig. 7.19.Assume the hydraulic conductivity is \(10^{-4}\) cm/sec. The sheet pile is 13m long (into the paper): The thickness of the soil layer is 10 m and the sheet pile penetrates halfway through. A head of 5m \(\left(h_{L}\right)\) of water separates both sides of the sheet pile.

Required:

a. Compute the amount of flow under the sheet pile for its full length in units of \(m ^{3} / s\).

b.· Evaluate the exit gradient and compute the factor of safety with respect to a quick condition

c.· Comment on what options a designer has to increase the factor of safety in part b.

Admin
Solution

a. The amount of flow is given by Eq. (7.20).

 

\(q=k h_{L} \frac{N_{f}}{N_{d}}\) (7.20)

 

\(q=k \Delta h \frac{N_{f}}{N_{d}} \text { per m of wall } \times \text { wall length }\)

 

\(q=10^{-4} cm / sec \times 0.01 m / cm \times 5 m \times \frac{3}{6} \times 13 m\)

 

\(q=3.25 \times 10^{-4} m ^{3} / sec =28 m ^{3} / \text { day }\)

 

b. The exit gradient is given by

 

\(\begin{aligned}&i_{E}=\frac{\Delta h}{L}=\frac{h_{L} / N_{d}}{L} \\&i_{E}=\frac{5 m / 6 drops }{2.5 m } \\&i_{E}=\frac{1}{3}\end{aligned}\)

 

where L = the scaled length taken from Fig. 7.19. The distance, L, is shown just below E-F and is approximately 2.5 m. The factor of safety with respect to boiling or quicksand is

 

\(\text { F.S. }=\frac{i_{c}}{i_{E}}\)

 

where \(i_{c}\) is the critical gradient as given in Eq. (7.15) or (7.16). The approximate value of the critical gradient is unity. So,

 

\(i_{c}=\frac{\rho_{s}-\rho_{w}}{(1+e) \rho_{w}}\) (7.15)

 

\(i_{c}=\frac{1}{1+e}\left(\frac{\rho_{s}}{\rho_{w}}-1\right)\) (7.16)

 

\(\text { F.S. }=\frac{1}{1 / 3}=3\)

 

c. Options available to increase the factor of safety in part b.

First, is the factor of safety adequate? If it is greater than one, is that OK? Because we do not know all the details about the subsurface soil properties and potential geologic “defects,” not to mention the consequences of failure, designers should be very conservative in this situation. A factor of safety of 5 to 10 would not be unreasonable.
