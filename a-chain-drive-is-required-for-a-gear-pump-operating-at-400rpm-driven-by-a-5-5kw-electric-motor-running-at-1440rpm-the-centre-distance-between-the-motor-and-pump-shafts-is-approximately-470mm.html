Guest Question.

A chain drive is required for a gear pump operating at 400rpm driven by a 5.5kW electric motor running at 1440rpm.The centre distance between the motor and pump shafts is approximately 470mm.

Admin
Solution

The desired reduction ratio is

\( \frac{1440}{440} = 3.6. \)

The nearest ratio available (see Table 8.9) using standard sized sprockets is 3.62.This requires a driving sprocket of 21 teeth and a driven sprocket of 76 teeth.
The application factor from Table 8.10 is \( f_{1} = 1.0. \)

The tooth factor is

\( f_{2} = \frac{19}{N_{1}} = \frac{19}{21} = 0.905. \)

The selection power is

\( 5.5 \times 1.0 \times 0.905 = 4.98 kW. \)

Using the BS/ISO selection chart, Figure 8.15, a 12.7 mm pitch simple BS chain drive is suitable.
The chain length is given by

\( L = \frac{N_{1} + N_{2}}{2} + \frac{2C}{p} + \frac{N_{2} – N_{1}}{2 \pi} ^{2} \frac{p}{C} \)
\( = \frac{21+76}{2} + \frac{2 \times 470}{12.7} + \frac{76 – 21}{2 \pi} ^{2} \frac{12.7}{470} = 124.6 \)pitches.

Rounding up to the nearest even integer gives L = 126 pitches.
The exact centre distance can now be calculated using

\( C = \frac{p}{8} \overrightarrow{2}L – N_{2} – N_{1} + \sqrt{(2L – N_{2} – N_{1})^{2} – \frac{\pi}{3.88} (N_{2} – N_{1})^{2} } \)
\( C = \frac{12.7}{8} \overrightarrow{2} \times 126 – 76 – 21 + \sqrt{(2 \times 126 – 76 – 21)^{2} – \frac{\pi}{3.88}( 76 – 21)^{2} } \)
\( = 479.2 mm. \)

From the rating chart, Figure 8.15, the required lubrication type is oil bath. Use of a SAE20/50 multigrade lubricant would likely suffice in the absence of more detailed knowledge concerning the operating conditions.
