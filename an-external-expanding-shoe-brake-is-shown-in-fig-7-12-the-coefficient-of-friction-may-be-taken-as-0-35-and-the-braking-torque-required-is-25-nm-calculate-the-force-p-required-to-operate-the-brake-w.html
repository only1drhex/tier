Guest Question.

An external expanding shoe brake is shown in Fig.7.12. The coefficient of friction may be taken as 0.35, and the braking torque required is 25 Nm. Calculate the force P required to operate the brake when the drum rotates (a) clockwise and (b) counter-clockwise.

Admin
Solution

(a) When drum rotates clockwise,

Total braking torque, \( T_{b}=\mu P_{\max } \cdot b \cdot r^{2}\left(\cos \theta_{1}-\cos \theta_{2}\right) \).

\( 25=0.35 \times P_{\max } \times b \times 0.12^{2}\left(\cos 30^{\circ}-\cos 150^{\circ}\right) \).

\( P_{\max } b=2863.84 \).

\( \text { Total moment of the normal force about the fulcrum } O_{1} \text {, } \)

\( M_{n}=0.5 P_{\max } b r a\left[\left(\theta_{2}-\theta_{1}\right)+0.5\left(\sin 2 \theta_{1}-\sin 2 \theta_{2}\right)\right] \).

\( =0.5 \times 2863.84 \times 0.12 \times 0.08\left[\frac{(150-30) \times \pi}{180}+0.5\left(\sin 60^{\circ}-\sin 300^{\circ}\right)\right] \).

= 40.695 Nm.

\( \text { Total moment of friction force about the fulcrum } O_{1} \text {, } \)

\( M_{f}=\mu P_{\max } b r\left[r\left(\cos \theta_{1}-\cos \theta_{2}\right)+0.25 a\left(\cos 2 \theta_{2}-\cos 2 \theta_{1}\right)\right] \).

\( =0.35 \times 2863.84 \times 0.12\left[0.12\left(\cos 30^{\circ}-\cos 150^{\circ}\right)+0.25\right. \)

\( \left.\times 0.08\left(\cos 300^{\circ}-\cos 60^{\circ}\right)\right] \).

= 25 Nm.

Total moment \( =M_{n}+M_{f}=65.695 Nm \).

\( \text { Taking moments about } O_{1} \text {, we have } \)

\( P \times 0.16=65.695 \).

P=410.6 N.

\( \text { (b) When drum rotates counter-clockwise, taking moments about } O_{1} \text {, we have } \)

\( P \times 0.16=M_{n}-M_{f}=15.695 \).

P=98.1 N.
