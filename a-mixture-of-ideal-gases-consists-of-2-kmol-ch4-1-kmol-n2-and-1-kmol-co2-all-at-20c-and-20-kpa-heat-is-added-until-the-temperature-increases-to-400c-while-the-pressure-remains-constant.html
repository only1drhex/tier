Guest Question.

A mixture of ideal gases consists of 2 kmol \( CH _{4}, 1 kmol N _{2} \text {, and } 1 kmol CO _{2} \), all at 20°C and 20 kPa. Heat is added until the temperature increases to 400°C while the pressure remains constant. Calculate (a) the heat transfer, (b) the work done, and (c) the change in entropy.

Admin

\( \left.\begin{array}{l} m_{1}=(2)(16)=32 kg \\ m_{2}=(1)(14)=14 kg \\ m_{3}=(1)(44)=44 kg \end{array}\right\} \quad \therefore m=90 kg \).

\( m f_{1}=\frac{32}{90}=0.356 \quad m f_{2}=\frac{14}{90}=0.1556 \quad m f_{3}=\frac{44}{90}=0.489 \).

\( \therefore C_{p}=\sum m f_{i} C_{p, i}=(0.356)(2.254)+(0.1556)(1.042)+(0.489)(0.842)=1.376 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

\( C_{v}=\sum m f_{i} C_{v, i}=(0.356)(1.735)+(0.1556)(0.745)+(0.489)(0.653)=1.053 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

For a constant-pressure process,

\( \text { (a) } Q=\Delta H=m C_{p} \Delta T=(90)(1.376)(400-20)=47060 kJ \text {. } \)

\( \text { (b) } W=Q-\Delta U=Q-m C_{v} \Delta T=47060-(90)(1.053)(400-20)=11050 kJ \).

\( \text { (c) } \Delta S=m\left(C_{p} \ln T_{2} / T_{1}-R \ln 1\right)=(90)(1.376 \ln 673 / 293)=103.0 kJ / K \).
