Guest Question.

An EM wave travels in free space with the electric field component

\(E_{s}=100e^{j(0.866y+0.5z)}a_{x} \)V/m

Determine
(a) ω and λ
(b) The magnetic field component
( c) The time average power in the wave

Admin
Solution

(a) Comparing the given E with

\(E_{s}=E_{0}e^{jk.r}=E_{0}e^{j(k_{x}x)+k_{y}y+k_{z}z}a_{x}\)
it is clear that
\(k_{x}=0, k_{y}=0.866, k_{z}=0.5\)
Thus
\(k=\sqrt{k^{2}_{x}+k^{2}_{y}+k^{2}_{z}}=\sqrt{(0.866)^{2}+(0.5)^{2}}=1\)
But in free space,
\(k=\beta =\omega \sqrt{\mu _{0}\varepsilon _{0}}=\frac{\omega }{c}=\frac{2\pi }{\lambda }\)
Hence,
\(\omega =kc=3\times 10^{8} \)rad/s
\(\lambda =\frac{2\pi }{k}=2\pi \)=6.283 m

(b) From eq. (10.106), the corresponding magnetic field is given by

\(H=\frac{1}{\omega \mu }K\times E =\frac{a_{k}\times E}{\eta }\) (10.106)
\(H_{s}=\frac{1}{\mu \omega } K\times E_{s}\)
\(=\frac{(0.866a_{y}+0.5a_{z})}{4\pi \times 10^{-7}\times 3\times 10^{8}}\times 100 a_{x}e^{jk.r}\)
or
\(H_{s}=(132.63a_{y}-229.7a_{z})e^{j(0.866y+0.5z)}\)mA/m

( c) The time-average power is
\(\mathscr{P}_{ave}=\frac{1}{2}Re(E_{s}\times H_{s}^{*})=\frac{E_{0}^{2}}{2\eta }a_{k}\)
\(=\frac{(100)^{2}}{2(120\pi )}(0.866a_{y}+0.5a_{z})\)
\(=11.49a_{y}+6.631a_{z}\) W/\(m^{2}\)
