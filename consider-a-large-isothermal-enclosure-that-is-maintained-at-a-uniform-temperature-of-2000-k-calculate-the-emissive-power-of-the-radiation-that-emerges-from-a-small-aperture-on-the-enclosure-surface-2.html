Guest Question.

Consider a large isothermal enclosure that is maintained at a uniform temperature of 2000 K. Calculate the emissive power of the radiation that emerges from a small aperture on the enclosure surface. What is the wavelength \(\lambda _{1}\) below which 10% of the emission is concen-trated? What is the wavelength \(\lambda _{2}\) above which 10% of the emission is concentrated? Deter-mine the maximum spectral emissive power and the wavelength at which this emission occurs. What is the irradiation incident on a small object placed inside the enclosure?

Admin
Solution

Known: Large isothermal enclosure at uniform temperature.

Find:
1. Emissive power of a small aperture on the enclosure.
2. Wavelengths below which and above which 10% of the radiation is concentrated.
3. Spectral emissive power and wavelength associated with maximum emission.
4. Irradiation on a small object inside the enclosure.

Assumptions: Areas of aperture and object are very small relative to enclosure surface.

Analysis:
1. Emission from the aperture of any isothermal enclosure will have the characteristics of blackbody radiation. Hence, from Equation 12.32 \(E_{b} =\sigma T^{4}\),

\(E = E_{b}(T) = \sigma T^{4} = 5.670 × 10^{-8} W/m^{2} . K^{4}(2000 K)^{4} \)

 

\(E = 9.07 × 10^{5} W/m^{2} \)

2. The wavelength \(\lambda _{1}\) corresponds to the upper limit of the spectral band \((0 → \lambda _{1}\)) containing 10% of the emitted radiation. With \(F_{(0 → \lambda _{1})}\) = 0.10 it follows from Table 12.2 that \(\lambda _{1}T = 2195 μm. K\). Hence

\(\lambda _{1} = 1.1 μm \)

The wavelength \(\lambda _{2}\) corresponds to the lower limit of the spectral band (\(\lambda _{2} → ∞ )\) containing 10% of the emitted radiation. With

\( F_{(\lambda _{2} → ∞)} = 1- F_{(0 → \lambda _{2})} = 0.1 \)

 

\( F_{(0 → \lambda _{2})} = 0.9 \)

it follows from Table 12.2 that \(\lambda _{2}T = 9382 μm. K\). Hence

\(\lambda _{2} = 4.69 μm \)

3. From Wien’s displacement law, Equation 12.31 \(\lambda _{max} T = C_{3}\), \(\lambda _{max}T = 2898 μm. K\). Hence

\(\lambda _{max} = 1.45 μm\)

The spectral emissive power associated with this wavelength may be computed from Equation 12.30 \( E_{\lambda ,b} (\lambda ,T ) = \pi I_{\lambda ,b}(\lambda ,T ) = \frac{C_{1}}{\lambda ^{5} \left[\exp (C_{2}/ \lambda T) -1\right] } \) or from the third column of Table 12.2. For \(\lambda _{max}T = 2898 μm. K\) it follows from Table 12.2 that

\(I_{λ,b} (1.45 μm, T) = 0.722 × 10^{-4} \sigma T^{5} \)

Hence

\(I_{λ,b} (1.45 μm, 2000 K) = 0.722 × 10^{-4} (μm . K . sr)^{-1} × 5.67 × 10^{-8} W/m^{2} . K^{4}(2000 K)^{5} \)

 

\(I_{λ,b} (1.45 μm, 2000 K) = 1.31 × 10^{5} W/m^{2} . sr . μm \)

Since the emission is diffuse, it follows from Equation 12.16 \(E_{\lambda }(\lambda ) = \pi I_{\lambda , e}(\lambda )\) that

\(E_{λ,b} = π I_{λ,b} = 4.12 × 10^{5} W/m^{2} .μm \)

4. Irradiation of any small object inside the enclosure may be approximated as being equal to emission from a blackbody at the enclosure surface temperature. Hence \(G = E_{b}(T)\), in which case

\(G = 9.07 × 10^{5} W/m^{2} \)
