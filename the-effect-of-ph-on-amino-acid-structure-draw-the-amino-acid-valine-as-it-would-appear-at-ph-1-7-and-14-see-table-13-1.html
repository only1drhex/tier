The effect of pH on amino acid structure

Draw the amino acid valine as it would appear at pH 1, 7, and 14 (see Table 13.1).

TABLE 13.1 α-AMINO ACIDS PRESENT IN PROTEINS
Common Name Formula Common Name Formula
Nonpolar Methionine (Met)
Glycine (Gly)
Alanine (Ala) Proline (Pro)
Valine (Val) Phenylalanine (Phe)
Leucine (Leu) Tryptophan (Trp)
Isoleucine (Ile)
Common Name Formula Common Name Formula
Polar-acidic Polar-neutral
Aspartic acid (Asp) Serine (Ser)
Glutamic acid (Glu) Threonine (Thr)
Polar-basic Cysteine (Cys)
Lysine (Lys) Tyrosine (Tyr)
Arginine (Arg)
Asparagine (Asn)
Histidine (His) Glutamine (Gln)

Strategy
As explained in Section 9.9, when the pH equals the \( pK_{a}\) for an acid, the concentration of acid and its conjugate base are equal. When the pH is less than the \( pK_{a}\) the acid predominates and when the pH is greater than \( pK_{a}\) the conjugate base predominates.
