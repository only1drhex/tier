Guest Question.

Proportion a trapezoidal combined footing for uniform pressure under dead load plus reduced live load, with the following data:

Allowable soil pressures:

150 kN\(/ m ^{2}\) for DL + reduced LL

225 kN\(/ m ^{2}\) for DL + LL

Admin
Solution

Column loads

\(\begin{array}{ccc} & \text { Column A } & \text { Column B } \\D L & 540 kN & 690 kN \\L L & 400 kN & 810 kN\end{array}\)

Distance c/c of columns = 5.4 m

Projection of footing beyond column not to exceed 0.5 m.

\(\begin{array}{cccc}\text { Resultant Column Loads } & \text { Column A } & \text { Column B } & \text { Total } \\D L+\text { reduced } L L & 740 kN & 1095 kN & 1835 kN \\D L+L L & 940 kN & 1500 kN & 2440 kN\end{array}\)

For uniform pressure under DL + reduced LL:
Let us use equal projections beyond columns A & B.

\(\begin{aligned}L^{\prime} &=5.4 m \\L &=L^{\prime}+2 e_{1}=5.4+2 \times 0.5= 6 . 4 ~ m .\end{aligned}\)

Total area required, A = 1835/150 = 12.23 \(m ^{2}\)

 

\(\bar{x}\) , distance of resultant column load from the left edge = 3.22 + 0.5 = 3.72

\(\begin{aligned}\therefore \quad B_{1} &=\frac{2 A}{L}\left(\frac{3 \bar{x}}{L}-1\right)=\frac{2 \times 12.23}{6.4}\left(\frac{3 \times 3.72}{6.4}-1\right)= 2 . 8 5 ~ m \\B_{2} &=\frac{2 A}{L}-B_{1}=\frac{2 \times 12.23}{6.4}-2.85= 1 . 0 5 ~ m\end{aligned}\)

Total area provided \(=\frac{2.85+105}{2} \times 6.4=12.48 m ^{2}( O . K .)\)

Total DL + LL = 2440 kN

\(\text { Location of c.g. from } B_{1}=(6.4 / 3) \frac{2 \times 105+2.05}{3.90}=2.70 m\)

Location of resultant DL + LL\(=\frac{940 \times 5.4}{2440}=2.08 m \text { from Col. B }\)

or 2.58 m from B

e = 0.12 m

Moment of inertia of the section about longer edge

\(=\frac{1}{3} \times 1.05 \times 6.4^{3}+\frac{1}{12} \times 1.80 \times 6.4^{3}=131.09 m ^{4}\)

M.I. about an axis through c.g. = 131.09 – 12.48 × 2.70 = 40.1\(m ^{4}\)

 

\(q_{\max }=\frac{2440}{12.48}+\frac{2440 \times 0.12 \times 2.7}{40.1}=215 kN / m ^{2}<225 kN / m ^{2}\) (O.K.)

\(q_{\min }=\frac{2440}{12.48}-\frac{2440 \times 0.12 \times 2.7}{40.1}=176 kN / m ^{2}\)

The structural design of the footing has now to follow.
