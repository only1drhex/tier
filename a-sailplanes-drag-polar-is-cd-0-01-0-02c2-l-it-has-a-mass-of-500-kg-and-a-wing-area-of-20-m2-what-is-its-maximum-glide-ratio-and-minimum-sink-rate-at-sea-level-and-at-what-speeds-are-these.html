Guest Question.

A sailplane’s drag polar is \(C_{D}=0.01+0.02 C_{L}^{2}\). It has a mass of 500 kg and a wing area of 20 \(m ^{2}\). What is its maximum glide ratio and minimum sink rate at sea level, and at what speeds are these achieved?

Admin
Solution

The aircraft’s maximum glide ratio is equal to its \((L / D)_{\max }:\)

\(\left(\frac{R}{h}\right)_{\max }=\left(\frac{L}{D}\right)_{\max }=\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{k C_{D_{0}}}}=\frac{1}{2 \sqrt{(0.01)(0.02)}}=35.4\)

The speed for \((L / D)_{\max }\) is determined by first finding the \(C_{L} \text { for }(L / D)_{\max }:\)

\(C_{L}=\sqrt{\frac{C_{D_{0}}}{k}}=\sqrt{\frac{0.01}{0.02}}=0.707\) \(L=W=C_{L} q S, \quad q=\frac{W}{C_{L} S}=\frac{m g}{C_{L} S}=\frac{(500 kg )\left(9.8 m / s ^{2}\right)}{0.707\left(20 m ^{2}\right)}=346.5 N / m ^{2}\)

At sea level, from the standard atmosphere chart \(\rho=1.225 kg / m ^{3}\), and so

\(V_{\infty}=\sqrt{\frac{2 q}{\rho}}=\sqrt{\frac{2\left(346.5 N / m ^{2}\right)}{1.225 kg / m ^{3}}}=23.8 m / s\)

for best glide range. The velocity for minimum sink rate is the velocity for minimum power required, where

\(3 C_{D_{0}}=k C_{L}^{2}, \quad C_{L}=\sqrt{\frac{3 C_{D_{0}}}{k}}=\sqrt{\frac{3(0.01)}{0.02}}=1.225\)

 

\(L=W=C_{L} q S, \quad q=\frac{W}{C_{L} S}=\frac{m g}{C_{L} S}=\frac{(500 kg )\left(9.8 m / s ^{2}\right)}{1.225\left(20 m ^{2}\right)}=200 N / m ^{2}\)

 

At sea level, from the standard atmosphere chart \(\rho=1.225 kg / m ^{3}\), and so

\(V_{\infty}=\sqrt{\frac{2 q}{\rho}}=\sqrt{\frac{2\left(200 N / m ^{2}\right)}{1.225 kg / m ^{3}}}=18.1 m / s\)

for minimum sink rate. Because \(3 C_{D_{0}}=k C_{L}^{2}\) for this condition, \(C_{D}=4 C_{D_{0}}\) , and the drag at this speed is

\(D=C_{D} q S=0.04\left(200 N / m ^{2}\right)\left(20 m ^{2}\right)=160 N\)

Then the minimum rate of sink is given by Eq. (5.29):

\(\text { minimum sink rate }=\frac{V_{\infty} D}{W}=\frac{V_{\infty} D}{m g}=\frac{18.1 m / s (160 N )}{500 kg \left(9.8 m / s ^{2}\right)}=0.59 m / s\)

Eq. (5.29): \(\text { sink rate }=V_{\infty} \sin \gamma=\frac{V_{\infty} D}{W}=\frac{P_{R}}{W}\)
