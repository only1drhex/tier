The Activity Coefficient of Water in an Aqueous Sodium Chloride Solution

Over the range of 0 to 6 M, we have shown that the mean ionic activity coefficient of sodium chloride in aqueous solutions at 25°C is well correlated with

\(\ln \gamma_{\pm}=-\frac{\alpha\left|z_{+} z_{-}\right| \sqrt{I}}{1+\sqrt{I}}+0.137 I\)

Determine the activity coefficient of water in aqueous sodium chloride solutions over this range of concentrations using the equation derived in this appendix.

Admin
Solution

For sodium chloride solutions \(I=\frac{1}{2}(1+1) M=M\). Therefore,

 

\(\begin{aligned}\ln x_{ W } \gamma_{ W } &=\ln \frac{55.51}{55.51+2 \cdot M_{ NaCl }}+\ln \gamma_{ W } \\&=-0.018 \times 2 \times M_{ NaCl }\left[1-\frac{1.178}{3} \sqrt{M_{ NaCl }} \cdot \sigma\left(M_{ NaCl }\right)+\frac{0.137}{2} M_{ NaCl }\right]\end{aligned}\)
