Guest Question.

Design of column bracket. A column bracket having the general features shown in Fig. 18.24 is to be designed to carry the end reaction from a long-span precast girder. Vertical reactions from service dead and live loads are 25 and 51 kips, respectively, applied at \(a_v\) = 5.5 in. from the column face. A steel bearing plate will be provided for the girder, which will rest directly on a \(5 \times 3 \times \frac{3}{8}\) in. steel angle anchored at the outer corner of the bracket. Bracket reinforcement will include main steel \(A_{sc}\) welded to the underside of the steel angle, closed hoop stirrups having total area \(A_h\) distributed appropriately through the bracket depth, and framing bars in a vertical plane near the outer face. Select appropriate concrete dimensions, and design and detail all reinforcement. Material strengths are \(f_c^\prime\) = 5000 psi and \(f_y\) = 60,000 psi.

Admin
Solution

The vertical factored load to be carried is
\(V_u = 1.2 \times 25 + 1.6 \times 51\) = 112 kips
In the absence of a roller or low-friction support pad, a horizontal tensile force of
\(N_{uc} = 0.20 \times 112\) = 22.4 kips

will be included. According to the shear friction provisions of the ACI Code, the nominal shear strength \(V_n\) must not exceed \(0.2 f_c^\prime bd\), (480 + 0.08\(f_c^\prime\))bd, or 1600bd. With \(f_c^\prime\) = 5000 psi, the second limit controls. Then, with \(V_u = ϕ V_n\) and with the column width b = 12 in.,

112 = \(0.75 \times 0.880 \times 12d\)

from which d = 14.14 in. Estimating 1 in. from the center of the main steel to the top surface of the bracket, a total depth h = 16 in. will be selected, with d approximately equal to 15 in., the exact value depending on the bar diameter chosen for \(A_{sc}\). If a 45° slope is used, as indicated in Fig. 18.24, the bracket depth at the outside of the bearing area will be 8 in. This is not less than 0.5d = 7.5 in., as required. For the bracket geometry selected, \(a_v / d\) = 5.5/15 = 0.36. This does not exceed the 1.0 limit imposed by the ACI Code.

The total shear friction steel is found from Eq. (18.9):

\(A_{vf}=\frac{V_u}{\phi \mu f_y}=\frac{112}{0.75\times1.4\times60}=1.78 in^2 \)

The bending moment to be resisted is
\(M_u = V_u a_v + N_{uc} ( h − d )\)
\( = 112 \times 5.5 + 22.4 \times 1\) = 638 in.-kips

The depth of the flexural compression stress block will be estimated to be 2 in., so, from Eq. (18.6),

\(A_f=\frac{M_u}{\phi f_y(d-a/2)}=\frac{638}{0.75\times60(15-2.0/2)}=1.01 in^2 \)

Checking the stress block depth gives

\(a=\frac{A_f f_y}{0.85f_c^\prime b}=\frac{1.01\times60}{0.85\times5\times12}=1.12\) in.

so the revised steel area is

\(A_f=\frac{638}{0.75\times60(15-1.12/2)}=0.98 in^2 \)

The tensile force of 22.4 kips requires an additional steel area, from Eq. (18.7), of

\(A_n=\frac{N_{uc}}{\phi f_y}=\frac{22.4}{0.75\times60}=0.50 in^2 \)

Thus, from Eqs. (18.8) and (18.10), respectively, the total steel area at the top of the bracket must not be less than

\(A_{sc} \geq A_f + A_n = 0.98 + 0.50 = 1.48 in^2\)

\(A_{sc} \geq A_f + A_n\) (18.8)

\(A_{sc} \geq \frac{2}{3} A_{vf} + A_n\) (18.10)

or less than
\(A_{sc} \geq \frac{2}{3} A_{vf} + A_n = \frac{2}{3} \times 1.78 + 0.50 = 1.69 in^2\)

The second requirement controls here. The minimum steel requirement of

\(A_{sc,min}=0.04\frac{f_c^\prime}{f_y}bd=0.04\times\frac{5}{60}\times12\times16=0.64 in^2\)

is seen not to control. A total of three No. 7 (No. 22) bars, providing \(A_{sc} = 1.80 in^2\), will be used.

Closed hoop steel having a total area \(A_h\) not less than \(0.5( A_{sc} – A_n )\) must be provided.
Thus,

\(A_h \geq 0.5 A_f = 0.5 \times 0.92 = 0.46 in^2\)

and
\(A_h \geq 0.5 \times \frac{2}{3} A_{vf} = \frac{1}{3} \times 1.78 = 0.60 in^2\)

The second requirement controls. Three No. 3 (No. 10) closed hoops will be provided, giving total area \(A_h = 0.66 in^2\). These must be placed within \(\frac{2}{3}\) of the effective depth of the main steel. A spacing of 2.5 in. will be satisfactory, as indicated in Fig. 18.24. A pair of No. 3 (No. 10) framing bars will be added at the inside corner of the hoops to improve anchorage, as shown.

Checking the required hook development length of the No. 7 (No. 22) top bars of the normal beam, including \(\psi _c\) = 0.7 for lateral cover in excess of 2.5 in. and cover over the bars extension of at least 2 in. using Eq. (6.6),

\(ℓ_{dh}=\left(\frac{f_y \psi _e \psi _c \psi _r}{50\lambda \sqrt{f_c^\prime}} \right)d_b=\left(\frac{60,000\times1.0\times0.7\times1.0}{50\times1.0\times \sqrt{5000}} \right)0.875=10.4 in. \)

In addition, the modification factor (\(A_s\) required)/(\(A_s\) provided) = 1.69/1.80 = 0.94 can be applied, giving
\(ℓ_{dh} = 0.94 \times 10.4\) = 9.8 in.

This requirement is easily met. The hook extension will be \(12d_b = 12 \times 0.875\) = 10.5 in. For the hoop bars, a standard 135° hook, as shown in Fig. 6.9b, will be used.
