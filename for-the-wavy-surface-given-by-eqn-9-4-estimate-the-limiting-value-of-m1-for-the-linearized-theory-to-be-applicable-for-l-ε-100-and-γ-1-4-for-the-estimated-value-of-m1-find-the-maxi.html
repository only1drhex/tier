Guest Question.

For the wavy surface given by Eqn. (9.4) \(y = f (x)=ε \sin κx \) , estimate the limiting value of \(M_{1}\) for the linearized theory to be applicable for \(L/ε = 100\) and \(γ = 1.4\). For the estimated value of \(M_{1}\), find the maximum local velocity and Mach number. Also find the value of \(M_{1}\) for the local velocity to be sonic.

Admin
Solution

For the applicability of the linearized theory,

\(\frac{2 \pi M^{2}_{1}\left(\gamma +1\right) \epsilon /L}{\left(1-M^{2}_{1}\right) ^{3/2}} \ll 1\)

Some typical functional values, \(f (M_{1}, γ, ε/L)\) of the left-hand side of this inequality as a function of M1 are given below (assuming \(γ = 1.4\)):

\(M_{1}\) \(f\left(M_{1}, \gamma ,\epsilon /\lambda \right) =\frac{2 \pi M^{2}_{1}\left(\gamma +1\right) \epsilon /L}{\left(1-M^{2}_{1}\right) ^{3/2}}\)

0.5 0.0580
0.6 0.1060

0.9 1.4748

Thus the estimated value of \(M_{1} = 0.6\) can be chosen as \(f\left(M_{1}, \gamma ,\epsilon /L \right)≈ 0.1≪1\).

For this Mach umber, the local maximum velocity is on the surface \(y = 0\) and at \(x = π/2α\), with \(α = 2 π/L\)

\(U^{2}=\left(U_{1} +\acute{u} \right) ^{2} +\acute{v} 2\approx U^{2}_{1} +2U_{1}\acute{u} =U^{2}_{1} \left[1+\frac{4 \pi \epsilon /L}{\sqrt{1-M^{2}_{1}} } \right]\)

At this surface location, the local speed of sound is given by Eqn. (8.37b) \(a^{2}=a^{2}_{1}-\left(\gamma -1\right) U_{1}\acute{u} \) :

\(a^{2} =a^{2}_{1} -\left(\gamma -1\right) U_{1} \acute{u} =U^{2}_{1}\left[\frac{1}{M^{2}_{1}} -\frac{2 \pi \left(\gamma -1\right) \epsilon /L}{\sqrt{1-M^{2}_{1}} } \right]\)

Therefore,

\(M^{2} =\frac{U^{2}}{a^{2}} ={\left[1+\frac{4 \pi \epsilon /L}{\sqrt{1-M^{2}_{1}} } \right]}/{\left[\frac{1}{M^{2}_{1}} -\frac{2 \pi \left(\gamma -1\right) \epsilon /L}{\sqrt{1-M^{2}_{1}} } \right] }=0.4213\)

The maximum local Mach number is

M = 0.649

which is approximately 10% larger than \(M_{1}\). The maximum local velocity is

\(U_{\max }=U_{1}+\acute{u} _{\max} =U_{1}\left[1+\frac{2 \pi \epsilon /L}{\sqrt{1-M^{2}_{1}} } \right] =1.0785U_{1}\)

From the tabulated values of \(M_{1}\), \(M_{cr} ≈ 0.873\).

Comments –

It appears that at the crest of the surface, the flow accelerates (i.e., \(\acute {u}> 0\)) and the pressure and temperature should decreased based on Bernoulli’s principle. Therefore, the speed of sound decreases and the 7.85% increase in the local velocity over the free-stream value results in an approximately 10% increase in Mach number. The reader is left to find that the Mach number at the trough is approximately 0.554 and \(U_{\min} = 0.9215U_{1}\).
