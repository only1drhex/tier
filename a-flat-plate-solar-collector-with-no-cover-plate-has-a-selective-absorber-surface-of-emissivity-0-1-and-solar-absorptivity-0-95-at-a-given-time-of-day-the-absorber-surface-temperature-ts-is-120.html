Guest Question.

A flat-plate solar collector with no cover plate has a selective absorber surface of emissivity 0.1 and solar absorptivity 0.95. At a given time of day the absorber surface temperature \(T_{s}\) is 120 °C when the solar irradiation is 750 W/m², the effective sky temperature is -10 °C, and the ambient air temperature \(T_{∞}\) is 30 °C. Assume that the heat transfer convection coef-ficient for the calm day conditions can be estimated from

\(\bar{h} = 0.22(T_{s} - T_{∞})^{1/3} W/m^{2} . K\)

Calculate the useful heat removal rate (W/m² ) from the collector for these conditions. What is the corresponding efficiency of the collector?

Admin
Solution

Known: Operating conditions for a flat-plate solar collector.

Find:
1. Useful heat removal rate per unit area,\(q^{\prime \prime }_{u} (W/m^{2})\) .
2. Efficiency \( \eta \)of the collector.

Assumptions:
1. Steady-state conditions.
2. Bottom of collector well insulated.
3. Absorber surface diffuse.

Analysis:
1. Performing an energy balance on the absorber,

\(\dot{E}_{in} – \dot{E}_{out} = 0\)

or, per unit surface area,

\( \alpha_{S} G_{S} + \alpha_{sky} G_{atm} – q^{\prime \prime }_{conv} – E -q^{\prime \prime }_{u} = 0\)

From Equation 12.73,

\(G_{atm} = \sigma T^{4}_{sky}\)

Since the atmospheric irradiation is concentrated in approximately the same spectral region as that of surface emission, it is reasonable to assume that

\( \alpha_{sky} ≈ \varepsilon = 0.1 \)

With

\( q^{\prime \prime }_{conv} = \bar{h} (T_{s} – T_{∞}) = 0.22 (T_{s} – T_{∞})^{4/3} \) and \( E = \varepsilon \sigma T^{4}_{s}\)

it follows that

\(q^{\prime \prime }_{u} = \alpha_{S} G_{S} + \varepsilon \sigma T^{4}_{sky} – 0.22 (T_{s} – T_{∞})^{4/3} – \varepsilon \sigma T^{4}_{s} \)

 

\(q^{\prime \prime }_{u} = \alpha_{S} G_{S} – 0.22 (T_{s} – T_{∞})^{4/3} – \varepsilon \sigma (T_{s}^{4} – T^{4}_{sky})\)

 

\(q^{\prime \prime }_{u} = 0.95 × 750 W/m^{2} – 0.22(120 – 30)^{4/3} W/m^{2} -0.1 × 5.67 × 10^{-8} W/m^{2} . K^{4}(393^{4} – 263^{4}) K^{4} \)

 

\(q^{\prime \prime }_{u} = (712.5 – 88.7 – 108.1) W/m^{2} = 516 W/m^{2} \)

2. The collector efficiency, defined as the fraction of the solar irradiation extracted as useful energy, is then

\(\eta = \frac{q^{\prime \prime }_{u}}{G_{s}} = \frac{516 W/m^{2}}{750 W/m^{2}} = 0.69 \)

Comments:
1. Since the spectral range of \(G_{atm}\) is entirely different from that of \(G_{S}\), it would be incorrect to assume that \(\alpha_{sky} = \alpha_{S}.\)
2. The convection heat transfer coefficient is extremely low \((\bar{h} ≈ 1 W/m^{2} . K)\). With a modest increase to \(\bar{h} = 5 W/m^{2} . K\), the useful heat flux and the efficiency are reduced to \(q^{\prime \prime }_{u} = 154 W/m^{2}\) and \(\eta\) 0.21. A cover plate can contribute significantly to reducing convection (and radiation) heat loss from the absorber plate.
