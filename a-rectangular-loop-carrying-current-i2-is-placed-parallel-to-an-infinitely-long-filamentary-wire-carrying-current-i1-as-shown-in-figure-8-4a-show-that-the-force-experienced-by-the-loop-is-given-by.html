Guest Question.

A rectangular loop carrying current \(I_{2}\) is placed parallel to an infinitely long filamentary wire carrying current \(I_{1}\) as shown in Figure 8.4(a). Show that the force experienced by the loop is given by

\(F_{\ell}=-\frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}I_{2}b}{2\pi}\left[\frac{1}{\rho_{o}}-\frac{1}{\rho _{o}+a}\right]a_{\rho}N\)

Admin
Solution

Let the force on the loop be

\(F_{\ell}=F_{1}+F_{2}+F_{3}+F_{4}=I_{2}\oint_{L}dl_{2}\times B_{1}\)

where \(F_{1},F_{2},F_{3}\), and \(F_{4}\) are, respectively, the forces exerted on sides of the loop labeled 1, 2, 3, and 4 in Figure 8.4(b). Owing to the infinitely long wire

\(B_{1}=\frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}}{2\pi\rho_{o}}a_{\phi}\)

Hence

\(F_{1}=I_{2}\int_{L}dl_{2}\times B_{1}=I_{2}\int_{z=0}^{b}dza_{z}\times\frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}}{2\pi\rho_{o}}a_{\phi}=-\frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}I_{2}b}{2\pi\rho_{o}}a_{\rho}\) (attractive)

\(F_{1}\) is attractive because it is directed toward the long wire; that is, \(F_{1}\) is along \(-a_{\rho}\) because loop side 1 and the long wire carry currents along the same direction. Similarly

\(F_{3}=I_{2}\int_{L}dl_{2}\times B_{1}=I_{2}\int_{z=b}^{0}dza_{z}\times\frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}}{2\pi(\rho_{o}+a)}a_{\phi}=\frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}I_{2}b}{2\pi(\rho_{o}+a)}a_{\rho}\) (repulsive)

\(F_{2}=I_{2}\int_{\rho=\rho_{o}}^{\rho_{o}+a}d\rho a_{\rho}\times\frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}a_{\phi}}{2\pi\rho}=\frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}I_{2}}{2\pi}\ln\frac{\rho_{o}+a}{\rho_{o}}a_{z}\) (parallel)

\(F_{4}=I_{2}\int_{\rho=\rho_{o}+a}^{\rho_{o}}d\rho a_{\rho}\times\frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}a_{\phi}}{2\pi\rho}=-\frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}I_{2}}{2\pi}\ln\frac{\rho_{o}+a}{\rho_{o}}a_{z}\) (parallel)

The total force \(F_{\ell}\) on the loop is the sum of \(F_{1},F_{2},F_{3}\), and \(F_{4}\); that is

\(F_{\ell}=\frac{\mu_{o}I_{1}I_{2}b}{2\pi}\left[\frac{1}{\rho_{o}}-\frac{1}{\rho_{o}+a}\right] (-a_{\rho})\)

which is an attractive force trying to draw the loop toward the wire. The force \(F_{w}\) on the wire, by Newton’s third law, is \(-F_{\ell}\); see Figure 8.4(b).
