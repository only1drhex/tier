Guest Question.

A motor has an output of 4.8 kW, an efficiency of 80 \(\%\) and a power factor of 0.625 lagging when operated from a 240 V, 50 Hz supply. It is required to improve the power factor to 0.95 lagging by connecting a capacitor in parallel with the motor. Determine (a) the current taken by the motor, (b) the supply current after power factor correction, (c) the current taken by the capacitor, (d) the capacitance of the capacitor, and (e) the kvar rating of the capacitor.

Admin
Solution

(a) Efficiency \(= \frac{power\: output}{power\: input}\)
hence \(\frac{80}{100}=\frac{4800}{power\: input}\)

and power input \(= \frac{4800}{0.8}=6000\, W\)

Hence, \(6000 = VI_{M} \cos \phi = (240)(I_{M})(0.625)\), since \(\cos \phi =\) p.f. \(= 0.625\). Thus current taken by the motor,

\(I_{M} = \frac{6000}{(240)(0.625)} = 40\, A\)

The circuit diagram is shown in Fig. 16.13(a). The phase angle between \(I_{M}\) and \(V\) is given by:
\(\phi = \cos^{-1} 0.625 = 51.32^{\circ}\), hence the phasor diagram is as shown in Fig. 16.16(b).

(b) When a capacitor \(C\) is connected in parallel with the motor a current \(I_{C}\) flows which leads \(V\) by \(90^{\circ}\). The phasor sum of \(I_{M}\) and \(I_{C}\) gives the supply current \(I\), and has to be such as to change the circuit power factor to \(0.95\) lagging, i.e. a phase angle of \(\cos^{-1} 0.95\) or \(18.19^{\circ}\) lagging, as shown in Fig. 16.13(c). The horizontal component of \(I_{M}\) (shown as oa)

= \( I_{M} \cos 51.32^{\circ}\)
\(= 40 \cos 51.32^{\circ} = 25\, A\)

The horizontal component of \(I\) (also given by oa)

= \( I \cos 18.19^{\circ}\)
\(= 0.95\, I\)

Equating the horizontal components gives:
\(25 = 0.95\, I\). Hence the supply current after p.f. correction,

\(I = \frac{25}{0.95}=26.32\, A\)

(c) The vertical component of \(I_{M}\) (shown as ab)

= \( I_{M} \sin 51.32^{\circ}\)
\(= 40 \sin 51.32^{\circ} = 31.22\, A\)

The vertical component of \(I\) (shown as ac)

= \( I \sin 18.19^{\circ}\)
\(= 26.32 \sin 18.19^{\circ} = 8.22\, A\)

The magnitude of the capacitor current \(I_{C}\) (shown as bc) is given by

\(ab \, -\, ac\) i.e. \(I_{C} = 31.22 \, -\, 8.22 = 23 A\)

(d) Current \(I_{C} = \frac{V}{X_{C}}=\frac{V}{(\frac{1}{2\pi fC})}=2\pi fCV\)

from which

\(C = \frac{I_{C}}{2\pi fV}=\frac{23}{2\pi (50)(240)}\, F=305\, \mu F\)

 

(e) kvar rating of the capacitor

= \( \frac{VI_{C}}{1000}=\frac{(240)(23)}{1000}=5.52\) kvar

In this problem the supply current has been reduced from \(40\, A\) to \(26.32\, A\) without altering the current or power taken by the motor. This means that the size of generating plant and the cross-sectional area of conductors supplying both the factory and the motor can be less â€“ with an obvious saving in cost.
