Guest Question.

Using LU factorization, solve the linear system Ax = b, where

\(A=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}3 & -1 & 2 \\-6 & 3 & 1 \\9 & -1 & 1\end{array}\right], \quad b=\left[\begin{array}{l}1 \\3 \\6\end{array}\right]\)
Admin
Solution

We use row addition operations to find U. Selecting \(a_{11}=3\) as the first pivot, we eliminate \(a_{21}=-6 \text { with } 2 R_{1}+R_{2} \rightarrow R_{2}\) giving

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc}3 & -1 & 2 \\0 & 1 & 5 \\9 & -1 & 1\end{array}\right]\)

 

Next we eliminate \(a_{31}=9 \text { with }-3 R_{1}+R_{3} \rightarrow R_{3}\) to obtain

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc}3 & -1 & 2 \\0 & 1 & 5 \\0 & 2 & -5\end{array}\right]\)

 

Moving down one row and over to the right one column, we select \(a_{22}=1\) as the next pivot. To eliminate the single term below this pivot, we use the row addition operation \(-2 R_{2}+R_{3} \rightarrow R_{3}\), resulting in the upper triangular matrix

 

\(U=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}3 & -1 & 2 \\0 & 1 & 5 \\0 & 0 & -15\end{array}\right]\)

 

The elementary matrices that correspond to each of these row operations are

 

\(2 R_{1}+R_{2} \rightarrow R_{2} \Rightarrow E_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{lll}1 & 0 & 0 \\2 & 1 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 1\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(-3 R_{1}+R_{3} \rightarrow R_{3} \Rightarrow E_{2}=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0 \\-3 & 0 & 1\end{array}\right]\)

 

and

 

\(-2 R_{2}+R_{3} \rightarrow R_{3} \Rightarrow E_{3}=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0 \\0 & -2 & 1\end{array}\right]\)

 

The inverses of these matrices are

 

\(E_{1}^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 0 & 0 \\-2 & 1 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 1\end{array}\right], \quad E_{2}^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0 \\3 & 0 & 1\end{array}\right], \quad E_{3}^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0 \\0 & 2 & 1\end{array}\right]\)

 

We have

 

\(E_{2}^{-1} E_{3}^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{lll}1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0 \\3 & 0 & 1\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{lll}1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0 \\0 & 2 & 1\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{lll}1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0 \\3 & 2 & 1\end{array}\right]\)

 

and so

 

\(L=E_{1}^{-1} E_{2}^{-1} E_{3}^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 0 & 0 \\-2 & 1 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 1\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{lll}1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0 \\3 & 2 & 1\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 0 & 0 \\-2 & 1 & 0 \\3 & 2 & 1\end{array}\right]\)

 

Now we solve the system Ly = b. We have

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & 0 & 0 \\-2 & 1 & 0 \\3 & 2 & 1\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}y_{1} \\y_{2} \\y_{3}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}1 \\3 \\6\end{array}\right]\)

 

The first row leads to

 

\(y_{1}=1\)

 

From the second row, we find

 

\(y_{2}=3+2 y_{1}=3+2=5\)

 

and from the third row we obtain

 

\(y_{3}=6-3 y_{1}-2 y_{2}=6-3-10=-7\)

 

Therefore we have

 

\(y=\left[\begin{array}{c}1 \\5 \\-7\end{array}\right]\)

 

To obtain a solution to the original system, we solve Ux = y. Earlier we found

 

\(U=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}3 & -1 & 2 \\0 & 1 & 5 \\0 & 0 & -15\end{array}\right]\)

 

Therefore the system to be solved is

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc}3 & -1 & 2 \\0 & 1 & 5 \\0 & 0 & -15\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}x_{1} \\x_{2} \\x_{3}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c}1 \\5 \\-7\end{array}\right]\)

 

Using back substitution, from the last line we find

 

\(x_{3}=\frac{7}{15}\)

 

Inserting this value into the equation represented by the second row, we obtain

 

\(x_{2}=-5 x_{3}+5=5\left(\frac{7}{15}\right)+5=-\frac{7}{3}+\frac{15}{3}=\frac{8}{3}\)

 

From the first row, we find \(x_{1}\) to be

 

\(x_{1}=\frac{1}{3}\left(x_{2}-2 x_{3}+1\right)=\frac{1}{3}\left(\frac{8}{3}-\frac{14}{15}+1\right)=\frac{1}{3}\left(\frac{41}{15}\right)=\frac{41}{45}\)
