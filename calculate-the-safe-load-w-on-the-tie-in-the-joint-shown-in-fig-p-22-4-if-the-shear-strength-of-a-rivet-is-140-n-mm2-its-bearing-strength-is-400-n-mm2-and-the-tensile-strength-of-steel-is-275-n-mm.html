Guest Question.

Calculate the safe load W on the tie in the joint shown in Fig. P.22.4 if the shear strength of a rivet is \(140 N / mm ^{2}\), its bearing strength is \(400 N / mm ^{2}\), and the tensile strength of steel is \(275 N / mm ^{2}\).
Ans. Shear is the critical condition and the safe load is 176.0 kN.

Admin
Solution

The angle rivets are in double shear and the gusset plate rivets are in single shear.
The double shear value of one 20 mm diameter rivet is

Double shear value \(=2 \times 140 \times \frac{\pi \times 20^{2}}{4} \times 10^{-3}=88.0 kN\)

The bearing value of one rivet in the 12-mm-thick gusset plate within the angle is

Bearing value \(=400 \times 20 \times 12 \times 10^{-3}=96.0 kN\)

Therefore, the shear load is critical and the total safe load on the angle rivets is

Safe load (angle rivets) \(=3 \times 88.0=264 kN\)

The single shear value of one gusset plate rivet is 44.0 kN.
The bearing value of one rivet in the 10 mm plate is

Bearing value \(=400 \times 20 \times 10 \times 10^{-3}=80.0 kN\)

Therefore, the shear load is critical and the total safe load is

Total safe load \(=4 \times 44=176.0 kN\).

The safe load on the joint is therefore 176.0 kN, but the plate strength should be checked.

Plate tensile strength \(=(120-20) \times 10 \times 275 \times 10^{-3}=275 kN\)

Therefore, the safe load is 176.0 kN.
