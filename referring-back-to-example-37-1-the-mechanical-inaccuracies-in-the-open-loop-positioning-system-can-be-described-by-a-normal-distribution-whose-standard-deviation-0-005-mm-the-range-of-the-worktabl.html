Guest Question.

Referring back to , the mechanical inaccuracies in the open-loop positioning system can be described by a normal distribution whose standard deviation = 0.005 mm. The range of the worktable axis is 550 mm, and there are 16 bits in the binary register used by the digital controller to store the programmed position. Determine (a) control resolution, (b) accuracy, and (c) repeatability for the positioning system.

Admin
Solution

(a) Control resolution is the greater of \(CR_{1}\) and \(CR_{2}\) as defined by Equations (37.14a) \(CR_{1} = \frac{p}{n_{s}r_{g}}\) and (37.15) \(CR_{2} = \frac{L}{2^{B} – 1}\):

\(CR_{1} = \frac{p}{n_{s}r_{g}} = \frac{5.0}{48(4)}\) = 0.0260 mm

\(CR_{2} = \frac{L}{2^{B} – 1} = \frac{550}{2^{16} – 1} = \frac{550}{65,535}\)= 0.0084 mm

CR = Max{0.0260, 0.0084} = 0.026 mm

(b) Accuracy is given by Equation (37.17) Ac = 0.5 CR + 3σ:

Ac = 0.5 (0.0260) + 3(0.005) = 0.0280 mm

(c) Repeatability Re = ±3(0.005) = ±0.015 mm
