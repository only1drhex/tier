Guest Question.

Consider a time-dependent current across a \(1 \mathrm{~F}\) capacitor given by

\(i_{C}(t)=\left\{\begin{aligned}1,\quad & 0<t<1 \mathrm{~s} \\-1,\quad & 1<t<3 \mathrm{~s} \\0, \quad & \text { otherwise. }\end{aligned} \quad\right. \text { (A). }\)

Assuming \(v_{C}(0)=0\), find and plot the voltage across the capacitor, as well as the power of the capacitor and the stored energy.

Admin
Solution

We again have \(v_{C}(t)=0\) for \(t<0\). In addition,

\(v_{C}(t)=(t) \mathrm{V}\)

for \(0<t<1 \mathrm{~s}\) and \(v_{C}(1)=1 \mathrm{~V}\), as in . Then, for \(1<t<3 \mathrm{~s}\), we have

\(v_{C}(t)=v_{C}(1)+\int_{1}^{t}(-1) d t^{\prime}=(-t+2) \mathrm{V},\)

leading to \(v_{C}(3)=-1 \mathrm{~V}\) and \(v_{C}(t)=-1 \mathrm{~V}\) for \(t>3 \mathrm{~s}\). Overall, we have

\(v_{C}(t)=\left\{\begin{array}{rl}0, & t<0 \\t, & 0<t<1 \mathrm{~s} \\-t+2, & 1<t<3 \mathrm{~s} \\-1, & t>3 \mathrm{~s}\end{array} \quad(\mathrm{V}) .\right.\)

Using the expressions for the current and voltage, the power of the capacitor can be found as

\(p_{C}(t)=v_{C}(t) i_{C}(t)=\left\{\begin{array}{rl}0, & t<0 \\t, & 0<t<1 \mathrm{~s} \\t-2, & 1<t<3 \mathrm{~s} \\0, & t>3 \mathrm{~s}\end{array} \quad(\mathrm{~W}) .\right.\)

Considering the expression above, it can be deduced that the capacitor stores energy (positive power) in the time periods \(0<t<1 \mathrm{~s}\) and \(2<t<3 \mathrm{~s}\), whereas it releases energy (negative power) for \(1<t<2 \mathrm{~s}\). Finally, the energy of the capacitor can be found to be

\(w_{C}(t)=\frac{1}{2} C\left[v_{C}(t)\right]^{2}=\left\{\begin{array}{rll}0, & t<0 \\t^{2} / 2, & 0<t<1 \mathrm{~s} \\ \left(t^{2}-4 t+4\right) / 2, & 1<t<3 \mathrm{~s} \\ 1 / 2, & t>3 \mathrm{~s}\end{array} \quad(\mathrm{~J}) .\right.\)
