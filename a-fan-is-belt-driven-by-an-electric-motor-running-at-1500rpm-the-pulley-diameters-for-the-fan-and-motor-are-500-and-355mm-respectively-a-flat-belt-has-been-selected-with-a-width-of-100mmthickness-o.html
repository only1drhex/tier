Guest Question.

A fan is belt driven by an electric motor running at 1500rpm.The pulley diameters for the fan and motor are 500 and 355mm, respectively. A flat belt has been selected with a width of 100mm,thickness of 3.5 mm, coefficient of friction of 0.8,density of \( 1100 kg/m^{3} \) and permissible stress of \( 11 MN/m^{2} \) .The centre distance is 1500mm. Determine the power capacity of the belt.

Admin
Solution

The arcs of contact for the driving and driven pulleys are:

\( \theta _{d} = \pi – 2 \sin^{-1} \frac{D – d}{2C} \)
\( = \pi – 2 \sin ^{-1} \frac{500 – 355 }{2 \times 1500} \)
\( = 3.045 rad, \)
\( \theta _{D} = \pi + 2 \sin^{-1} \frac{D – d}{2C} \)
\( = \pi + 2 \sin ^{-1} \frac{500 – 355}{2 \times 1500} \)
\( = 3.238 rad. \)

The maximum tension in the tight side is given as a function of the maximum permissible stress in the belt by:

\( F_{1} = \sigma _{max}A = 11 \times 10^{6}(3.5 \times 10^{-3} \times 100 \times 10^{-3}) = 3850 N.\)

The belt velocity is

\( V = 1500 \times \frac{2 \pi}{60} \times \frac{0.355}{2} = 27.88 m/s. \)

The mass per unit length is

\( m = \rho A = 1100 \times 0.1 \times 0.0035 = 0.385 kg/m. \)

The centrifugal load is given by

\( F_{c} = 0.385 (27.88)^{2} = 299.3 N \)

Using Eq.8.10 \( \frac{F_{1} – F_{c}}{F_{2} – F_{c}} = e^{\mu \theta } \)

\( \frac{F_{1} – F_{c}}{F_{2} – F_{c}} = e^{\mu \theta } = \frac{3850 – 299.3}{F_{2} – 299.3} = e^{0.8 \times 3.045} \)

which gives

\( F_{2} = 610.0 N. \)

The power capacity is given by \( (F_{1} – F_{2})V = (3850 – 610 )27.88 = 90.3 kW.\)
