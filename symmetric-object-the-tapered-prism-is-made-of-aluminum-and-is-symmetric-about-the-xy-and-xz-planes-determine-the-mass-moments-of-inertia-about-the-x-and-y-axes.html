Guest Question.

Symmetric Object

The tapered prism is made of aluminum and is symmetric about the \(xy\) and \(xz\) planes. Determine the mass moments of inertia about the \(x\) and \(y\) axes.

Admin
Solution

Road Map We will use a thin rectangular plate volume element, with the strategy described in Fig. 10.16(d) on p. 556, to evaluate \(I_x\) and \(I_y\).

\(I_x\)

Governing Equations & Computation We begin with Eq. (\(I_y = \int{dI_y}\)) on p. 556 (written in terms of x)

\(I_x = \int{dI_x}\) (1)

where \(dI_x\) is the moment of inertia of the thin rectangular plate mass element shown in Fig. 2 about the \(x\) axis. Note that the \(x\) axis and the \(x^â€²\) axis passing through the mass center of the thin plate element coincide, and therefore no parallel axis shift is required. From the Table of Properties of Solids on the inside back cover,

\(dI_x = \frac{1}{12}(m_{plate})(b^2 + h^2)\). (2)

The dimensions b and h of the thin plate element are functions of position as follows:

\(b = 20mm – (0.2)x\) and \(h = 40mm – (0.4)x\). (3)

The mass of the plate is the product of density \(\rho\) and the volume of the plate, hence

\(m_{plate} = \rho bhdx\). (4)

Combining Eqs. (1), (2), and (4) provides

\(I_x = \int_{0}^{50 mm}{\frac{1}{12}\rho bh (b^2 + h^2) dx }\). (5)

From Table 1.4 on p. 16, the density of aluminum is \(\rho = 2710 kg/m^3 = 2.710 \times 10^{-6} kg/mm^3\). Substituting b and h from Eq. (3) and the value for \(\rho\) into Eq. (5) and carrying out the integration yield

\(I_x = 7.00 kg \cdot mm^2\). (6)

\(I_y\)

Governing Equations & Computation We begin with Eq. (\(I_y = \int{dI_y}\)) on p. 556

\(I_y = \int{dI_y}\) (7)

where \(dI_y\) is the moment of inertia of the thin rectangular plate mass element shown in Fig. 2 about the y axis. Since the \(y\) and \(y^â€²\) axes do not coincide, the parallel axis theorem, Eq. (\(I_y = I_{y^â€²} + d^2_y m\)) on p. 556, is used to write the moment of inertia of the thin plate element about the y axis as

\(dI_y = dI_{y^â€²} + d^2_y dm\) (8)

where \(dI_{y^â€²}\) is the moment of inertia of the plate element about the \(y^â€²\) axis passing through its mass center, dm is the mass of the plate element, and \(d_y\) is the distance between the y and \(y^â€²\) axes, namely, \(d_y = x\). From the Table of Properties of Solids on the inside back cover, the moment of inertia of a thin plate is

\(dI_{y^â€²} = \frac{1}{12}(m_{plate})(h)^2\) (9)

where \(m_{plate}\) is given by Eq. (4). Combining Eqs. (4) and (7)â€“(9) provides

\(I_y = \int_{0}^{50 mm}{\frac{1}{12}\rho bh h^2dx } + \int_{0}^{50 mm}{x^2 \rho bh dx }\). (10)

Substituting b and h from Eq. (3) and the value for \(\rho\) into Eq. (10) and carrying out the integration yield

\(I_y = 41.7 kg\cdot mm^2\). (11)

Discussion & Verification A rough check of accuracy can be obtained by using the Table of Properties of Solids on the inside back cover to evaluate the moments of inertia for the uniform rectangular prism shown in Fig. 3, where dimensions have been chosen to approximate the shape and volume of the tapered prism. For the uniform prism shown in Fig. 3, you should verify that \(I_x = 5.72 kg\cdot mm^2\) and \(I_y = 55.4 kg\cdot mm^2\). The results for the tapered prism in Eqs. (6) and (11) are in reasonable agreement with these.
