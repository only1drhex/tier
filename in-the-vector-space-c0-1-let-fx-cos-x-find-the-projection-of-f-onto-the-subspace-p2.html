Guest Question.

In the vector space C[0, 1], let f(x) = cos x. Find the projection of f onto the subspace P2.

Admin
Solution

Let \(\left\{p_{0}, p_{1}, p_{2}\right\}\) be the orthogonal basis for \(P _{2}\) found in . (Note that the inner product used in coincides with the present inner product on C[0, 1]. By Theorem 13, the projection of f onto \(P _{2}\) is the polynomial \(p^{*}\) defined by

\(p^{*}(x)=\frac{\left\langle f, p_{0}\right\rangle}{\left\langle p_{0}, p_{0}\right\rangle} p_{0}(x)+\frac{\left\langle f, p_{1}\right\rangle}{\left\langle p_{1}, p_{1}\right\rangle} p_{1}(x)+\frac{\left\langle f, p_{2}\right\rangle}{\left\langle p_{2}, p_{2}\right\rangle} p_{2}(x)\),

where

\(\left\langle f, p_{0}\right\rangle=\int_{0}^{1} \cos (x) d x \simeq .841471\)
\(\left\langle f, p_{1}\right\rangle=\int_{0}^{1}(x-1 / 2) \cos (x) d x \simeq .038962\)
\(\left\langle f, p_{2}\right\rangle=\int_{0}^{1}\left(x^{2}-x+1 / 6\right) \cos (x) d x \simeq-.002394\).

From , we have \(\left\langle p_{0}, p_{0}\right\rangle=1,\left\langle p_{1}, p_{1}\right\rangle=1 / 12, \text { and }\left\langle p_{2}, p_{2}\right\rangle=1 / 180\). Therefore, \(p^{*}(x)\) is given by

\(p^{*}(x)=\left\langle f, p_{0}\right\rangle p_{0}(x)+12\left\langle f, p_{1}\right\rangle p_{1}(x)+180\left\langle f, p_{2}\right\rangle p_{2}(x)\)
\(\simeq .841471 p_{0}(x)-.467544 p_{1}(x)-.430920 p_{2}(x)\)
