Guest Question.

Purification of an Antibiotic

Benzylpenicillin is an older antibiotic effective against pneumococcal and meningoccal infections, anthrax, and Lyme disease. As part of a purification process, 200 mg of benzylpenicillin is mixed with 25 mL of n-octanol and 25 mL of water. After equilibrium is established, there is a water-rich phase that contains essentially no n-octanol and an octanol-rich phase that contains 74 mol % n-octanol and 26 mol % water. Determine the concentrations of benzylpenicillin in each of these phases.

Data: The molecular weight of benzylpenicillin is 334.5, that of n-octanol is 130.23, the liquid density of n-octanol is 0.826 g/cc, and \(K_{ OW , P }=65.5\).

Admin
Solution

First we need to determine the number of moles in each phase (on a benzylpenicillin-free basis). Since n-octanol is insoluble in water, it is all in the octanol-rich phase. The number of moles of n-octanol is

 

\(\frac{25 mL \times 0.826 g / mL }{130.23 g / mol }=0.1586 mol\)

 

and the amount of water in the octanol-rich phase is

 

\(0.1586 mol \text { octanol } \times \frac{0.26 mol \text { water }}{0.74 mol \text { octanol }}=0.0557 mol \text { water }\)

 

The volume of the n-octanol-rich phase, assuming no volume change of mixing, is

 

\(V_{ O }=\frac{0.1586 mol \times 130.23 g / mol }{0.826 g / mL }+\frac{0.0557 mol \times 18 g / mol }{1 g / mL }=26.0028 mL\)

 

where the first term is from the amount of n-octanol and the second from the amount of water in the octanol-rich phase. The volume of the water-rich phase is

 

\(V_{ W }=25-1.0028=23.997 mL\)

 

The total number of moles of benzylpenicillin is \((0.2 g ) /(334.4 g / mol )=5.981 \times 10^{-4}\) mol.

Now to find the concentration of benzylpenicillin in each phase we use a mass balance:

 

\(\begin{aligned}5.981 \times 10^{-4} mol &=C_{ B }^{ W } \cdot V^{ W }+C_{ B }^{ O } \cdot V^{ O }=C_{ B }^{ W } \cdot V^{ W }+K_{ OW , B } \cdot C_{ B }^{ W } \cdot V^{ O } \\&=C_{ B }^{ W } \cdot(23.997+65.5 \times 26.008)=C_{ B }^{ W } \cdot 1727.52 mL\end{aligned}\)

 

Therefore,

 

\(C_{ B }^{ W }=3.462 \times 10^{-7} \frac{ mol }{ mL }=1.158 \times 10^{-4} \frac{ g }{ mL }=0.1158 \frac{ mg }{ mL }\)

 

and

 

\(C_{ B }^{ O }=3.462 \times 10^{-7} \times 65.5=2.268 \times 10^{-5} \frac{ mol }{ mL }=7.585 \times 10^{-3} \frac{ g }{ mL }=7.585 \frac{ mg }{ mL }\)
