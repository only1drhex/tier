Guest Question.

A motor supplies 15 hp to gear A of the drive system shown in Figure P6.53/54. Shaft (1) is a solid 2.25-in.-diameter aluminum [G = 4,000 ksi] shaft with a length of \(L_{1}\) = 16 in. and an allowable shear stress of 6,000 psi. Shaft (2) is a solid 1.5-in.-diameter steel [G = 12,000 ksi] shaft with a length of \(L_{2}\) = 12 in. and an allowable shear stress of 8,000 psi. In addition to the allowable shear stresses, specifications require that the rotation angle of gear D with respect to gear B must not exceed 2Â°. Shafts (1) and (2) are connected at flange C, and the bearings shown permit free rotation of the shaft. What is the slowest rotation speed that is permissible for the motor?

Admin
Solution

Section properties: The polar moments of inertia for aluminum shaft (1) and steel shaft (2) are:

\(J_{1}=\frac{\pi}{32} d_{1}^{4}=\frac{\pi}{32}(2.25 in .)^{4}=2.516112 in .^{4} \quad J_{2}=\frac{\pi}{32} d_{2}^{4}=\frac{\pi}{32}(1.5 in .)^{4}=0.497010 in. ^{4}\)

Torque based on rotation angle of gear D: The rotation angle of gear D with respect to gear B is simply the sum of the angles of twist in shafts (1) and (2). The torque in each shaft has the same value.

\(T_{1}=T_{2}=T_{D}\)

 

\(\phi_{D / B}=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=T_{D}\left[\frac{L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}\right] \leq 2^{\circ}\)

 

\(T_{D} \leq \frac{\left(2^{\circ}\right)\left(\frac{\pi rad }{180^{\circ}}\right)}{\frac{16 in .}{\left(2.516112 in. ^{4}\right)\left(4 \times 10^{6} psi \right)}+\frac{12 in .}{\left(0.497010 in .^{4}\right)\left(12 \times 10^{6} psi \right)}}=9,691.462 lb \text {-in. }\)

 

Torque based on allowable shear stress: Based on the 6,000-psi allowable shear stress, the torque in aluminum shaft (1) must not exceed:

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{1} \leq \frac{\tau_{1} J_{1}}{c_{1}}=\frac{(6,000 psi )\left(2.516112 in.^{4}\right)}{(2.25 \text { in. } / 2)}=13,419.264 lb \text {-in } \\&T_{2} \leq \frac{\tau_{2} J_{2}}{c_{2}}=\frac{(8,000 psi )\left(0.497010 in .{ }^{4}\right)}{(1.50 in . / 2)}=5,301.437 lb – in.\end{aligned}\)

Controlling torque: The maximum torque that can be applied to the shaft at D is thus:

\(T_{D}=5,301.437 lb \text {-in. }=441.786 lb – ft\)

Power transmission: To satisfy the allowable torque constraints, the rotation speed of the shaft must exceed:

\(\omega_{D} \geq \frac{P}{T_{D}}=\frac{(15 hp )\left(\frac{550 lb – ft / s }{1 hp }\right)}{441.786 lb – ft }=18.6742 rad / s =\omega_{B}\)

The rotation speed of the motor is related to the rotation speed of gear B by the gear ratio; thus,

\(\omega_{\text {motor }} \geq \frac{60 \text { teeth }}{24 \text { teeth }}(18.6742 rad / s )=46.6855 rad / s\)

or

\(\omega_{\text {motor }} \geq\left(\frac{46.6855 rad }{ s }\right)\left(\frac{1 rev }{2 \pi rad }\right)\left(\frac{60 s }{1 min }\right)=445.8137 rpm =446 rpm\)
