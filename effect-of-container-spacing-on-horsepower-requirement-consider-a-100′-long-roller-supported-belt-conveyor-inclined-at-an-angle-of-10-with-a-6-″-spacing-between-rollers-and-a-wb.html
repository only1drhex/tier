Guest Question.

Effect of container spacing on horsepower requirement

Consider a \(100^{\prime}\)-long roller-supported belt conveyor inclined at an angle of \(10^{\circ}\), with a \(6^{\prime \prime}\) spacing between rollers and a WBR value of \(27^{\prime \prime} .\) (Use roller beds, not slider beds, for inclines; \(35^{\circ}\) is the maximum angle of incline for most situations.) Suppose the conveyor is used to transport tote boxes of material, with each tote box being \(18^{\prime \prime}\) long and weighing \(35 \mathrm{lb}\), loaded. (For stability of the load, it is recommended that a minimum of two rollers be under the load at all times; on an incline, three or more rollers should be under the load.) A spacing of \(12^{\prime \prime}\) is to be provided between tote boxes. The conveyor speed is planned to be \(90 \mathrm{fpm}\). What is the approximate horsepower requirement for the conveyor?

Admin
Solution

We first need to determine the maximum loading condition for the conveyor. In order to do so, let a load segment be defined as a tote box plus the clearance between tote boxes. In this case, the length of the load segment is equal to \(30^{\prime \prime}\). Hence, exactly 40 load segments will be on the \(100^{\prime}\)-long conveyor at all times. Thus, the weight of the load will be \(40 \times 35\), or \(1400 \mathrm{lb}\).

In this case, \(S=90 \mathrm{fpm}, R C=6^{\prime \prime}, W B R=27^{\prime \prime}, B V=(2 / 3)(27)=18, L F=0.61, T L=\) \(100^{\prime \prime}, F F=0.05, L=L L_{I}=1400 \mathrm{lb}\), and \(\alpha=10^{\circ} .\) Hence,

\(H P=[18+0.61(100)+0.05(1400)+1400(0.1737)](90) / 14,000=2.52 \mathrm{hp}\)

Thus, a \(2.75\) hp or \(3.0\) hp motor should be adequate to meet the demands placed on the above belt conveyor.

What if the spacing between consecutive tote boxes had been \(8^{\prime \prime}\), rather than \(12^{\prime \prime}\) ? Dividing the conveyor length by the length of the load segment yields a value of 46, plus a fraction. Hence, there would be 46 complete load segments, plus a partial load segment. The 46 load segments have a length of \(46\left(18^{\prime \prime}+8^{\prime \prime}\right)\), or \(1196^{\prime \prime}\). Thus, the maximum weight condition would be \(46 \mathrm{full}\) tote boxes and \(4^{\prime \prime}\) of a partial tote box. If the weight of a tote box is uniformly distributed over its length, then the partial tote box contributes \((4 / 18)(35)\), or \(7.78 \mathrm{lb}\). Therefore, the maximum load would be \(46(35)+7.78\), or \(1617.78 \mathrm{lb}\), and the horsepower requirement would be \(2.83 \mathrm{hp}\).
