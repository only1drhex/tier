Linear Programming Formulation of the Capital Allocation Problem

Five engineering projects are being considered for the upcoming capital budget period. The interrelationships among the projects and the estimated net cash flows of the projects are summarized in the following table:

Cash Flow ($000s)
for End of Year k
Project 0 1 2 3 4 PW ($000s) at
MARR = 10% per year
B1 -50 20 20 20 20 13.4
B2 -30 12 12 12 12 8.0
C1 -14 4 4 4 4 -1.3
C2 -15 5 5 5 5 0.9
D -10 6 6 6 6 9.0
Admin
Solution

The objective function and constraints for this problem are written as follows: Maximize

Net \(P W=13.4 X_{B 1}+8.0 X_{B 2}-1.3 X_{C 1}+0.9 X_{C 2}+9.0 X_{D}\),

subject to

\(50 X_{B 1}+30 X_{B 2}+14 X_{C 1}+15 X_{C 2}+10 X_{D} \leq 48 ;\)

(constraint on investment funds)

\(X_{B 1}+X_{B 2} \leq 1;\)

(B1 and \(B 2\) are mutually exclusive)

\(X_{C 1}+X_{C 2} \leq X_{B 2} ;\)

(C1 or \(C 2\) is contingent on \(B 2\) )

\(X_{D} \leq X_{C 1};\)

( \(D\) is contingent on \(C 1\) )

\(X_{j}=0 \text { or } 1 \text { for } j=B 1, B 2, C 1, C 2, D .\)

(No fractional projects are allowed.)

A problem such as this could be solved readily by using the simplex method of linear programming if the last constraint \(\left(X_{j}=0\right.\) or 1\()\) were not present. With that constraint included, the problem is classified as a linear integer programming problem. (Many computer programs are available for solving large linear integer programming problems.)
