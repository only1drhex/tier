Guest Question.

The switch in the circuit shown in Fig. 7.28 has been open for a long time. At t=0 the switch is closed.Find the expression for

a) \(v\left(t\right)\) when \( t \geq 0^{+} \) and

b)\(i\left(t\right)\) when \( t \geq 0 \) .

Admin
Solution

a) The switch has been open for a long time, so the initial current in the inductor is 5 A, oriented from top to bottom. Immediately after the switch closes, the current still is 5 A, and therefore the initial voltage across the inductor becomes \(20-5\left(1\right)\)or 15 V. The final value of the inductor voltage is 0 V. With the switch closed, the time constant is\(\frac{80}{1}\) , or 80 ms. We use Eq. 7.60 to write the expression for \(v\left(t\right)\) :

 

\(v\left(t\right)=0 +\left (15 – 0\right)e^{\frac{-t}{80\times 10^{-3}}}\)

 

\( =15e^{-12.5t} V, t \geq 0^{+}\).

 

b) We have already noted that the initial value of the inductor current is 5 A. After the switch has been closed for a long time, the inductor current reaches\(\frac{20}{1}\) , or 20 A. The circuit time constant is 80 ms, so the expression for \(i\left(t\right)\) is

 

\(i\left(t\right)=20 +\left (5 – 20\right)e^{-12.5t}\)

 

\( =\left(20-15e^{-12.5t}\right) A, t \geq 0\).

 

We determine that the solutions for\(v\left(t\right)\) and \(i\left(t\right)\) agree by noting that

 

\(v\left(t\right)=L\frac{di}{dt}\)

 

\(=80\times 10^{-3}\left[15\left(12.5\right)e^{-12.5t}\right]\)

 

\( =15e^{-12.5t} V, t \geq 0^{+}\).
