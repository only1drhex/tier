Guest Question.

Different Methods of Evaluating Gas Pressure

Predict the pressure of nitrogen gas at T = 175 K and v = 0.00375 m³/kg on the basis of (a) the ideal-gas equation of state, (b) the van der Waals equation of state, (c) the Beattie-Bridgeman equation of state, and (d) the Benedict-Webb-Rubin equation of state. Compare the values obtained to the experimentally determined value of 10,000 kPa.

Admin
Solution

The pressure of nitrogen gas is to be determined using four different equations of state.

Properties The gas constant of nitrogen gas is \( 0.2968 kPa \cdot m ^{3} / kg \cdot K \) (Table A–1).
Analysis (a) Using the ideal-gas equation of state, the pressure is found to be

\( P=\frac{R T}{v}=\frac{\left(0.2968 kPa \cdot m ^{3} / kg \cdot K \right)(175 K )}{0.00375 m ^{3} / kg }= 1 3 , 8 5 1 kPa \)

which is in error by 38.5 percent.
(b) The van der Waals constants for nitrogen are determined from Eq. 3–23 to be

\(\begin{aligned}&a=0.175 m ^{6} \cdot kPa / kg ^{2} \\&b=0.00138 m ^{3} / kg\end{aligned}\)

From Eq. 3–22,

\( \left(P+\frac{a}{v^{2}}\right)(v-b)=R T \)

 

\( P=\frac{R T}{v-b}-\frac{a}{v^{2}}=9471 kPa \)

which is in error by 5.3 percent.
(c) The constants in the Beattie-Bridgeman equation are determined from Table 3–4 to be

\(\begin{aligned}&A=102.29 \\&B=0.05378 \\&c=4.2 \times 10^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Also, \( \bar{v}=M v=(28.013 kg / mol )\left(0.00375 m ^{3} / kg \right)=0.10505 m ^{3} / kmol \). Substituting these values into Eq. 3–24, we obtain

\( P=\frac{R_{u} T}{\bar{v}^{2}}\left(1-\frac{c}{\bar{v} T^{3}}\right)(\bar{v}+B)-\frac{A}{\bar{v}^{2}} \)

 

\( P=\frac{R_{u} T}{\bar{v}^{2}}\left(1-\frac{c}{\bar{v} T^{3}}\right)(\bar{v}+B)-\frac{A}{\bar{v}^{2}}=10,110 kPa \)

which is in error by 1.1 percent.
(d) The constants in the Benedict-Webb-Rubin equation are determined from Table 3–4 to be

\(\begin{array}{ll}a=2.54 & A_{0}=106.73 \\b=0.002328 & B_{0}=0.04074 \\c=7.379 \times 10^{4} & C_{0}=8.164 \times 10^{5} \\\alpha=1.272 \times 10^{-4} & \gamma=0.0053\end{array}\)

Substituting these values into Eq. 3–26 gives

\( P=\frac{R_{u} T}{\bar{v}}+\left(B_{0} R_{u} T-A_{0}-\frac{C_{0}}{T^{2}}\right) \frac{1}{\bar{v}^{2}}+\frac{b R_{u} T-a}{\bar{v}^{3}}+\frac{a \alpha}{\bar{v}^{6}}+\frac{c}{\bar{v}^{3} T^{2}}\left(1+\frac{\gamma}{\bar{v}^{2}}\right) e^{-\gamma / \bar{v}^{2}} \)

 

\(\begin{aligned}P=& \frac{R_{u} T}{\bar{v}}+\left(B_{0} R_{u} T-A_{0}-\frac{C_{0}}{T^{2}}\right) \frac{1}{\bar{v}^{2}}+\frac{b R_{u} T-a}{\bar{v}^{3}} \\&+\frac{a \alpha}{\bar{v}^{6}}+\frac{c}{\bar{v}^{3} T^{2}}\left(1+\frac{\gamma}{\bar{v}^{2}}\right) e^{-\gamma / \bar{v}^{2}} \\=& 10,009 kPa\end{aligned}\)

which is in error by only 0.09 percent. Thus, the accuracy of the Benedict-Webb-Rubin equation of state is rather impressive in this case.
