Guest Question.

Find the response of the system of Example 3.1 to the step load given by F(t) = 10^4 [ 1 2 4 ] N.m Assume that the system is to start from rest and neglect the damping effect.

Admin
Solution

In order to be able to use Eq. 3.84, we first evaluate the vector of modal forces Q defined by Eq. 3.85 as \(Q=\Phi^{T} F\). Using the modal matrix \(\Phi\) obtained in Example 3.2, the vector Q is given by

Eq. 3.84 \(M_{p}\ddot{P} + M_{p}P = Q \)

Eq. 3.85 \(M_{p} = \Phi ^{T}M\Phi , K_{p}= \Phi ^{T}K\Phi , Q=\Phi ^{T}F \)

\(Q=\Phi^{T} F = \left[\begin{matrix} 1.0 & 0.649 & 0.302 \\ 1.0 & -0.607 & -0.679 \\ 1.0 & -2.54 & 2.438 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{matrix} \right] \times 10^{4} = 10^{4} \left[\begin{matrix} 3.506 \\ -2.930 \\ a5.670\end{matrix} \right] \)

The equations of motion can then be written in terms of the modal coordinates as

\(m_{i}\ddot{P} _{i} + k_{i} P_{i} = Q_{i}, i=1,2,3\)

where \(m_{i}\) and \( k_{i}\) , i=1,2,3, are, respectively, the modal mass and stiffness coefficients defined in Example 3.3. Since \(Q_{i}\) is constant, one can verify that the solutions of the above equations, in the case of zero initial conditions, are given by

\(P_{i} = \frac{Q_{i}}{k_{i}} \left(1-\cos \omega _{i}t\right) \)

Using the results of Examples 3.2 and 3.3, we have

\(P = \left[\begin{matrix} P_{1} \\ P_{2} \\ P_{3} \end{matrix} \right] = \left[\begin{matrix} 0.0463\left(1-\cos 14.52t\right) \\ -0.0062\left(1-\cos 31.05t\right) \\ 0.0006\left(1-\cos 46.1t\right) \end{matrix} \right] \)

The vector of torsional oscillations \(\theta\) is given by

\(\theta = \Phi P = \left[\begin{matrix} 0.1 & 0.1 & 0.1 \\ 0.649 & -0.607 & -2.54 \\ 0.302 & -0.679 & 2.438 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} P_{1} \\ P_{2} \\ P_{3} \end{matrix} \right] \)

\( =\left[\begin{matrix} P_{1}+P_{2}+P_{3} \\ 0.649P_{1}+0.607P_{2}-2.54P_{3} \\ 0.302P_{1}-0.679P_{2}-2.432P_{3} \end{matrix} \right] \)
