Guest Question.

For flow of a liquid metal through a circular tube, the velocity and temperature profiles at a particular axial location may be approximated as being uniform and parabolic, respectively. That is, \(u(r) = C_{1}\) and \(T(r) - T_{s} = C_{2}[1 - (r/r_{o})^{2}]\), where \(C_{1}\) and \(C_{2}\) are constants. What is the value of the Nusselt number \(Nu_{D}\) at this location?

Admin
Solution

Known: Form of the velocity and temperature profiles at a particular axial location for flow in a circular tube.

Find: Nusselt number at the prescribed location.

Assumptions: Incompressible, constant property flow.

Analysis: The Nusselt number may be obtained by first determining the convection coefficient, which, from Equation 8.27, is given as

\(q^{\prime \prime} _s = h (T_{s} – T_{m}) \) (8.27)

\(h = \frac{q^{\prime \prime }}{T_{s} – T_{m}} \)

From Equation 8.26, the mean temperature is

\(T_{m}= \frac{2}{u_{m} r^{2}_{o}} \int_{0}^{r_{o}}{uTr dr} = \frac{2 C_{1}}{u_{m} r^{2}_{o}} \int_{0}^{r_{o}}{\left\{ T_{s} + C_{2 } \left[1- \left(\frac{r}{r_{o}} \right)^{2} \right] \right\} } r dr\)

or, since \(u_{m} =C_{1}\) from Equation 8.8,

\( u_m = \frac{\int_{A_c}^{}{\rho u (r,x)dA_c} }{\rho A_c} = \frac{2\pi \rho }{\rho \pi r_o^2} \int_{0}^{r_o}{u(r,x)r \ dr} = \frac{2}{r_o^2} \int_{0}^{r_O}{u(r,x) r \ dr} \) (8.8)

\(T_{m}=\frac{2 }{r^{2}_{o}} \int_{0}^{r_{o}}{\left\{ T_{s} + C_{2 } \left[1- \left(\frac{r}{r_{o}} \right)^{2} \right] \right\} } r dr\)

 

\( T_{m} = \left. \frac{2}{r^{2}_{o}} \left[T_{s} \frac{r^{2}}{2} + C_{2}\frac{r^{2}}{2}- \frac{C_{2}}{4}\frac{r^{4}}{r^{2}_{o}} \right]\right|^{r_{o}}_{0}\)

 

\( T_{m} = \frac{2}{r^{2}_{o}}\left(T_{s} \frac{r^{2}_{o}}{2} +\frac{C_{2}}{2}r^{2}_{o} – \frac{C_{2}}{4}r^{2}_{0} \right) = T_{s} + \frac{C_{2}}{2}\)

The heat flux may be obtained from Fourier’s law, in which case

\(q^{\prime \prime }_{s} = k \frac{\partial T}{\partial r} |_{r = r_{o}} =-k C_{2} 2 \frac{r}{r^{2}_{o}} |_{r = r_{o}} = – 2 C_{2} \frac{k}{r_{o}}\)

Hence

\(h = \frac{q^{\prime \prime }_{s}}{T_{s} – T_{m}} = \frac{-2 C_{2} (k/r_{o})}{-C_{2}/2} =\frac{4k}{r_{o}}\)

and

\( Nu_{D} = \frac{h D}{k} = \frac{(4k/r_{o}) \times 2r_{o}}{k} =8\)
