Guest Question.

Hénon Map

(2-cycle) Consider the Hénon map with \(-1<b<1\). Show that the map has a 2 -cycle for \(a>a_{1}=\frac{3}{4}(1-b)^{2}\). For which values of \(a\) is the 2-cycle stable?

Admin
Solution

\(\begin{gathered}x_{n+2}=b x_{n}+1-a\left(y_{n}+1-a x_{n}^{2}\right)^{2} \quad y_{n+2}=b\left(y_{n}+1-a x_{n}^{2}\right) \\x=b x+1-a\left(y+1-a x^{2}\right)^{2} \quad y=b\left(y+1-a x^{2}\right)\end{gathered}\)

We can solve for \(y\) in the right-hand equation by rearranging a bit.

\(y=\frac{b\left(a x^{2}-1\right)}{b-1}\)

We can then substitute for \(y\) in the left-hand equation.

\(x=b x+1-a\left(y+1-a x^{2}\right)^{2}=b x+1-a\left(\frac{y}{b}\right)^{2}=b x+1-\frac{a}{b^{2}}\left(\frac{b\left(a x^{2}-1\right)}{b-1}\right)^{2}\)

Moving everything to one side and simplifying gives an equation without \(y\).

\(0=(b-1)^{3} x+(b-1)^{2}-a\left(a x^{2}-1\right)^{2}\)

The roots of this equation will be the \(x\) coordinates of the 2-cycle. However, two of the four roots to this equation will also be fixed points of the Hénon map \(x=y+1-a x^{2}=b x+1-a x^{2}\) and we can divide them out.

\(0=\frac{(b-1)^{3} x+(b-1)^{2}-a\left(a x^{2}-1\right)^{2}}{b x+1-a x^{2}-x}=a^{2} x^{2}+a(b-1) x-a+(b-1)^{2}\)

which has solutions

\(x=\frac{1-b \pm \sqrt{4 a-3(b-1)^{2}}}{2 a} \Rightarrow y=\frac{b\left(1-b \mp \sqrt{4 a-3(b-1)^{2}}\right)}{2 a} \quad a \neq 0\)

We have to throw out the \(a=0\) case since it’s not a 2-cycle.

The coordinates must be real for the 2-cycle to exist, meaning

\(4 a-3(b-1)^{2}>0 \Rightarrow a>a_{1}=\frac{3}{4}(1-b)^{2}\)

To determine stability, we’ll have to linearize and find when all \(|\lambda|<1\).

\(\begin{gathered}J=\left(\begin{array}{cc}\frac{ d x_{n+2}}{ d x_{n}} & \frac{ d x_{n+2}}{ d y_{n}} \\\frac{ d y_{n+2}}{ d x_{n}} & \frac{ d y_{n+2}}{ d y_{n}}\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{cc}b+4 a^{2} x\left(y+1-a x^{2}\right) & -2 a\left(y+1-a x^{2}\right) \\-2 a b x & b\end{array}\right) \\J_{\left(x^{*}, y^{*}\right)}=\left(\begin{array}{cc}b+\frac{4 a^{2} x^{*} y^{*}}{b} & -\frac{2 a y^{*}}{b} \\-2 a b x^{*} & b\end{array}\right)\end{gathered}\)

where we simplified using the \(y\) equation for the 2-cycle.

\(\begin{aligned}\lambda_{\pm} &=\frac{2 a^{2} x^{*} y^{*}+b^{2} \pm 2 \sqrt{a^{2} x^{*} y^{*}\left(a^{2} x^{*} y^{*}+b^{2}\right)}}{b} \\&=\frac{2\left(a^{2} x^{*} y^{*}\right)+b^{2} \pm 2 \sqrt{\left(a^{2} x^{*} y^{*}\right)^{2}+b^{2}\left(a^{2} x^{*} y^{*}\right)}}{b}\end{aligned}\)

We can use the equations from finding the 2 -cycle to simplify.

\(\begin{gathered}a^{2} x^{*} y^{*}=a^{2} x^{*} \frac{b\left(a\left(x^{*}\right)^{2}-1\right)}{b-1}=\frac{a b x^{*}\left(a^{2}\left(x^{*}\right)^{2}-a\right)}{b-1} \\0=a^{2}\left(x^{*}\right)^{2}+a(b-1) x^{*}-a+(b-1)^{2} \quad a^{2}\left(x^{*}\right)^{2}-a=-(b-1)\left(a x^{*}+(b-1)\right) \\a^{2} x^{*} y^{*}=-a b x^{*}\left(a x^{*}+(b-1)\right)=-b\left(a^{2}\left(x^{*}\right)^{2}+a(b-1) x^{*}\right) \\0=a^{2}\left(x^{*}\right)^{2}+a(b-1) x^{*}-a+(b-1)^{2} \quad a^{2}\left(x^{*}\right)^{2}+a(b-1) x^{*}=a-(b-1)^{2} \\a^{2} x^{*} y^{*}=-b\left(a-(b-1)^{2}\right)=b\left((b-1)^{2}-a\right)\end{gathered}\)

\(\begin{aligned}\lambda_{\pm} &=\frac{2\left(a^{2} x^{*} y^{*}\right)+b^{2} \pm 2 \sqrt{\left(a^{2} x^{*} y^{*}\right)^{2}+b^{2}\left(a^{2} x^{*} y^{*}\right)}}{b} \\&=\frac{2 b\left((b-1)^{2}-a\right)+b^{2} \pm 2 \sqrt{b^{2}\left((b-1)^{2}-a\right)^{2}+b^{3}\left((b-1)^{2}-a\right)}}{b} \\&=2\left((b-1)^{2}-a\right)+b \pm 2 \sqrt{\left((b-1)^{2}-a\right)^{2}+b\left((b-1)^{2}-a\right)}\end{aligned}\)

Finally, we look at \(\left|\lambda_{\pm}\right|<1\) to determine stability. This is a bit of a headache because we have to worry about complex eigenvalues. Eventually we obtain the following by assuming a positive argument to the square root, a negative argument to the square root, and the inequality from the existence of the 2 -cycle.

\(a<\frac{1}{4}\left(5 b^{2}-6 b+5\right) \quad-1<b<1 \quad a>\frac{3}{4}(b-1)^{2}\)
