Guest Question.

Mass Balance on a Bioreactor

Assuming that cells produced in this biochemical reaction can be represented by the Roels average biomass (that is, 1 C-mole cells = \(CH _{1.8} O _{0.5} N _{0.2}\)), how many moles of oxygen, nitrogen, and water are consumed for each mole of carbon monoxide consumed?

Admin
Solution

First, we test the consistency of the reported stoichiometry by checking that the number of moles of carbon atoms balance on each side of the equation.

 

\(\text { C-balance: } \quad 1=0.52+0.42+0.024+0.036=1\)

 

So the carbon-atom balance is correct. The other atom balances are as follows.

 

\(\text { O-balance: } \quad 1+2 x+z=0.52 \cdot 2+0.42+0.024 \cdot 0.5+0.036 \cdot 0.5=1.49\)

 

\(\text { N-balance: } \quad 2 \cdot y=0.036 \cdot 2=0.0072 \quad \text { or } \quad y=0.0036\)

 

\(\text { H-balance: } \quad 2 \cdot z=0.42 \cdot 2+0.024 \cdot 2+0.036 \cdot 1.8=0.9528 \quad \text { or } \quad z=0.4764\)

 

Using this last result in the oxygen-atom balance, we obtain

 

\(2 \cdot x=1.49-1-z=0.49-0.4764=0.0136 \quad \text { or } \quad x=0.0068 \)

 

so that the complete stoichiometry for this biochemical reaction (based on the assumed composition of the average biomass) is

 

\(\begin{aligned}CO +0.0068 \cdot O _{2}+0.0036 \cdot N _{2}+0.4764 \cdot H _{2} O \rightarrow 0.52 \cdot CO _{2}+0.42 \cdot CH _{2} O \\&+0.024 \cdot CH _{2} O _{0.5}+0.036 CH _{1.8} O _{0.5} N _{0.2}\end{aligned}\) (a)

 

or, returning to common chemical notation,

 

\(\begin{aligned}CO +0.0068 \cdot O _{2}+0.0036 \cdot N _{2}+0.4764 \cdot H _{2} O \rightarrow 0.52 \cdot CO _{2}+0.21 \cdot CH _{3} COOH \\+0.006 \cdot CH _{3} CH _{2} CH _{2} COOH +0.036 CH _{1.8} O _{0.5} N _{0.2}\end{aligned}\)
