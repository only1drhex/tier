Guest Question.

For the U-tube shown in Fig. P2.102, determine the pressure at points A, B, and C if:
(a) \(a_x = 0, a_z = 10 m/s^2, L = 60 cm\)
(b) \(a_x = 20 m/s^2, a_z = 0, L = 60 cm\)
(c) \(a_x = 20 m/s^2, a_z = 10 m/s^2, L = 60 cm\)
(d) \(a_x = 0, az=-60 ft/sec^2, L = 25 in\)
(e) \(a_x = 60 ft/sec^2, a_z = 0, L = 25 in\)
(f) \(a_x=-30 ft/sec^2, a_z = 30 ft/sec^2, L = 25 in\)

Admin
Solution

Use Eq. 2.5.2 :\( p_2 -p_1=-\rho a_x(x_2 – x_1) – \rho (g + a_z)(z_2 – z_1) \), with position 1 at the open end:

b) \( p_A = –1000 × 10 (0.9–0) = –\underline{9000 Pa} \) .

\(p_B = –1000 × 10 (0.9)–1000 × 9.81(−0.6) = \underline{–3114 Pa} \) .

\(pc = –1000 × 9.81 × (–0.6) = \underline{5886 Pa } \).

\( p_A = 1.94 × 60\left\lgroup -\frac{37.5}{12} \right\rgroup = \underline{−364 psf} \) .

\(p_B= 1.94 × 60\left\lgroup -\frac{37.5}{12} \right\rgroup – 1.94 × 32.2\left\lgroup-\frac{25}{12} \right\rgroup =\underline{–234 psf} \) .

\(p_C = –1.94 × 32.2 \left\lgroup-\frac{25}{12} \right\rgroup =\underline{130 psf} \).
