Guest Question.

Compute the inverse z-transform of the following functions.

\(F_{1}(z)=\frac{10z}{(z-1)(z-0.5)}, F_{2}(z)=\frac{2z^{3}+z}{(z-2)^{2}(z-1)}\)
\(F_{3}(z)=\frac{z^{2}+z+2}{(z-1)(z^{2}-z+1)}, F_{4}(z)=\frac{z(z+1)}{(z-1)(z-.25)^{2}} \)

Admin
Solution

a. \(\frac{F_{1}(z)}{z}=\frac{k_{1}}{z-1}+\frac{k_{2}}{z-0.5}=\frac{20}{z-1}+\frac{20}{z-0.5}\) (1)

Thus,

\(F_{1}(z)=20\frac{z}{z-1}- 20\frac{z}{z-0.5}\Rightarrow\)
\(f_{1}[k]=IZT[F_{1}(z)]=20(1-0.5^{k})u[k]\) (2)

b. First suggested solution: partial fraction expansion

\(\frac{F_{2}(z)}{z}=\frac{2z^{2}+1}{(z-2)^{2}(z-1)}=\frac{k_{1}}{z-1}+\frac{k_{21}}{z-2}+\frac{k_{22}}{(z-2)^{2}}\) (3)
\(k_{1}=\frac{2z^{2}+1}{(z-2)^{2}(z-1)}(z-1)|_{z=1}=\frac{3}{1}=3 \)(4)
\(k_{22}=\underset{z\rightarrow 2}{\lim }[\frac{2z^{2}+1}{z-1} ]=\frac{9}{1}=9\) (5)

\(k_{21}=\underset{z\rightarrow 2}{\lim }[\frac{2z^{2}+1}{z-1} ]'=\underset{z\rightarrow 2}{\lim }[\frac{4z(z-1)-(2z^{2}+1)}{(z-1)^{2}} ]=\frac{8-(9)}{1}=-1 \)(6)
Hence, \(\frac{F_{2}(z)}{z}=\frac{3}{z-1}-\frac{1}{z-2}+\frac{9}{(z-2)^{2}}\) (7)
or, \(F_{2}(z)=3\frac{z}{z-1}-\frac{z}{z-2}+9\frac{z}{(z-2)^{2}}\overset{IZT}{\Rightarrow }\)
\(f_{2}[k]=Z^{-1}[F_{2}(z)]=(3-2^{k}+9k2^{k-1})u[k] \)(8)

Second suggested solution: complex integration

\(F_{2}(z)=\frac{2z^{3}+z}{(z-2)^{2}(z-1)}=\frac{z(2z^{2}+1)}{(z-2)^{2}(z-1)}\Rightarrow\)
\(F_{2}(z)z^{k-1}=\frac{z^{k}(2z^{2}+1)}{(z-2)^{2}(z-1)}\) (9)

Using the complex integration method, we get:

\(\underset{z=1}{residue}(\Rightarrow \underset{z\rightarrow 1}{\lim }\frac{z^{k}(2z^{2}+1)}{(z-2)^{2}} =\frac{3}{1} =3)\) (10)

\(\underset{z=2}{residue}|\Rightarrow \underset{z\rightarrow 2}{\lim }[\frac{d}{dz}(\frac{z^{k}(2z^{2}+1)}{z-1} ) ]= \underset{z\rightarrow 2}{\lim }[(\frac{2z^{k+2}+z^{k}}{z-1} )']=\)
\(\underset{z\rightarrow 2}{\lim }[\frac{(2(k+2)z^{k+1}+kz^{k-1})(z-1)-(2z^{k+2}+z^{k})}{(z-1)^{2}} ]=\)
\(\frac{(2(k+2)2^{k+1}+k2^{k-1})-(2.2^{k+2}+2^{k})}{1}=\)
\(2k2^{k+1}+2^{2}2^{k+1}+k2^{k-1}-2.2^{k+2}-2^{k}\)

\(=4k2^{k}+8.2^{k}+\frac{k}{2}2^{k}-8.2^{k}-2^{k}=(4k+\frac{k}{2} )2^{k}-2^{k}=\frac{9k}{2}2^{k}-2^{k}\)
\(\underset{z=2}{residue}=9k.2^{k-1}-2^{k}\) (11)
So, \(f_{2}[k]=(3+9k2^{k-1}-2^{k})u[k]\) (12)

c.\(\frac{F_{3}(z) }{z}=\frac{z^{2}+z+2}{z(z-1)(z^{2}-z+1)}=\frac{k_{1}}{z}+\frac{k_{2}}{z-1}+\frac{k_{3}}{z-e^{j\frac{\pi }{3} }}+\frac{k_{4}=\bar{k_{3}} }{z-e^{-j\frac{\pi }{3} }}\) (13)
\(k_{1}=\underset{z\rightarrow 0}{\lim }(\frac{F_{3}(z) }{z}z)=\frac{2}{(-1)1}=-2\) (14)
\(k_{2}=\underset{z\rightarrow 1}{\lim }(\frac{F_{3}(z) }{z}(z-1))=\frac{1+1+2}{(1-1+1)1}=4\) (15)

\(k_{3}=\underset{z\rightarrow e^{j\frac{\pi }{3} }}{\lim }(\frac{F_{3}(z) }{z}(z-e^{j\frac{\pi }{3} }))=\frac{e^{j\frac{2\pi }{3} }+e^{j\frac{\pi }{3} }+2}{e^{j\frac{\pi }{3} }(e^{j\frac{\pi }{3} }-1)(e^{j\frac{\pi }{3} }-e^{-j\frac{\pi }{3} })}\)
