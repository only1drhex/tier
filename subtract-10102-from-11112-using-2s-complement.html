Guest Question.

Subtract \((1010)_{2}\) from \((1111)_{2}\) using 2’s complement.

Admin
Solution

M − N = \((1111)_{2} − (1010)_{2}\)

Step 1. 2’s complement of

\(\qquad \quad \overline{N}=(0101)\\ \quad \quad \quad \qquad \quad +1 \\ \overline{N^{\prime}} =\overline{N} =\overline{(0110)}\)

Step 2. Add this to M

\(\qquad \qquad M =1111 \\ \overline{N^{\prime}}=\overline{N}+1 =0110 \\ \qquad \qquad \qquad \overline{(1)0111}\)

Step 3. Ignore carry generated in step 2, confirming that result is +ve, and therefore the answer is 0111.

Case: (b) M − N where M < N
Step 1. Find the 2’s complement of N (larger).
Step 2. Add this to smaller number M. Here carry will not be there confirming that the result is –ve.
Step 3. Take 2’s complement and put –ve sign.
