Guest Question.

A 200 m long section of a 15 m wide canal is being deepened 1.5 m by means of a dredge. The effluent from the dredge has a unit weight of 12.4 \(kN / m ^{3}\). The soil at the bottom of the canal has an in situ unit weight of 18.7 \(kN / m ^{3}\). The specific gravity of the soil grains is 2.72. If the effluent is being pumped at a rate of 400 L per minute, how many operational hours will be required to complete the dredge work?

Admin
Solution

Let’s find the volume of solid grains \(\left(V_{s}=x\right.\)) to be removed by dredging.

 

\(\text { Volume of soil to be removed }=(1.5)(15)(200)=4500 m ^{3} \text {. }\)

 

\(\text { In situ unit weight (saturated) }=18.7 kN / m ^{3} \text {. }\)

 

\(\gamma_{\text {sat }}=\left(\frac{G_{s}+e}{1+e}\right) \gamma_{w} \rightarrow 18.7=\left(\frac{2.72+e}{1+e}\right) 9.81 \rightarrow e_{\text {in situ }}=0.898\)

 

\(e=0.898=\frac{4500-x}{x} \rightarrow x=2370.9 m ^{3} \text { of soil grains to be dredged }\)

 

Now, let’s see how much soil grains (\(V_{s}=y\)) are being pumped out every minute, where \(V_{t}=400 L =0.400 m ^{3}\)

 

\(\gamma_{\text {sat }}(\text { effluent })=12.4 kN / m ^{3} \rightarrow e_{\text {effluent }}=5.515\)

 

\(e=5.515=\frac{0.400-y}{y} \rightarrow y=0.0614 m ^{3} \text { of soil grains per minute }\)

 

\(\therefore \text { Operational hours required }=\frac{2370.9}{0.0614 \times 60}=644 \text { hours }\)
