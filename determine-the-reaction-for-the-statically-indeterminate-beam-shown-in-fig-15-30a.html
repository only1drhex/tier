Guest Question.

Determine the reaction for the statically indeterminate beam shown in Fig. 15.30(a)

Admin
Solution

The loads can be thought of as made up of a concentrated load at the middle of a simply supported beam and a couple on the left end of an identical simply supported beam, as shown in Fig. 15.30(b) and (c). The slope must be zero at A; therefore,

\(\theta _{A1}+\theta _{A2}=0\)

From diagrams (f) and (h) of of the Appendix,

\(\frac{PL^2}{16EI}+\frac{M_AL}{3EI}=0\)

 

\(M_A=-\frac{3}{16} PL\)

With one of the reactions known, the other two can be found from the equilibrium equations as follows:

\(\sum{M_A}=0\) \(-M_A-P \frac{L}{2} + B_yL=0\)

 

\(B_y=-\frac{3}{16} P+\frac{P}{2}=\frac{5P}{16} \)

 

\(\sum{M_B}=0\) \(-M_A – A_yL +P \frac{L}{2} =0\)

 

\(A_y=\frac{3}{16}P +\frac{P}{2}= \frac{11 P}{16} \)

Check:

\(\sum{F_y}=0\) \(A_y – P + B_y = 0\)

 

\(\frac{5}{16}P -P + \frac{11}{16}P= 0\) OK

An alternative solution, consisting of two cantilever beams that are fixed at A with the deflection at B made equal to zero, as in , is also possible.
