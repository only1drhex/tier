Guest Question.

At a point in a structural member the stresses on two mutually perpendicular planes are \(60 N / mm ^{2}\) tension and \(30 N / mm ^{2}\)compression together with a shear stress of \(15 N / mm ^{2}\). Calculate the principal stresses at the point, the maximum shear stress and the angle which the plane of maximum principal stress makes with the plane on which the \(60 N / mm ^{2}\) stress acts. Verify all your answers using a graphical method. If Young’s modulus \(E=200000 N / mm ^{2}\) and Poisson’s ratio \(\nu=0.3\) calculate the principal strains and the maximum shear strain.

Admin
Solution

We shall designate the \(60 N / mm ^{2}\) as \(\sigma_{x}\). Then, from Eq. (14.8)

\(\sigma_{ I }=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2}+\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right)^{2}+4 \tau_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.8)

 

\(\sigma_{ I }=\frac{60-30}{2}+\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left[(60+30)^{2}+4 \times 15^{2}\right]}\)

which gives

\(\sigma_{ I }=62.4 N / mm ^{2}\)

Similarly, from Eq. (14.9)

\(\sigma_{ II }=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2}-\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right)^{2}+4 \tau_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.9)

 

\(\sigma_{ II }=-32.4 N / mm ^{2}\)

Then, from Eq. (14.12)

\(\tau_{\max }=\frac{\sigma_{ I }-\sigma_{ II }}{2}\) (14.12)

 

\(\tau_{\max }=\frac{62.4+32.4}{2}=47.4 N / mm ^{2}\)

Alternatively, \(\tau_{\max }\) could have been obtained from Eq. (14.11)

\(\tau_{\max , \min }=\pm \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right)^{2}+4 \tau_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.11)

using the given values of stress although this would have involved slightly longer computation.

From Eq. (14.7)

\(\tan 2 \theta=-\frac{2 \tau_{x y}}{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}\) (14.7)

 

\(\tan 2 \theta=-\frac{2 \times 15}{60+30}=-0.33\)

so that

\(2 \theta=-18.4^{\circ} \text { or }-198.4^{\circ}\)

giving

\(\theta=-9.2^{\circ} \text { or }-99.2^{\circ}\)

From Mohr’s circle of stress (see Fig. 14.11) it is clear that the plane on which the maximum principal stress acts is at an angle of 9.2° to the plane on which the \(60 N / mm ^{2}\) stress acts. For this particular problem the solution using Mohr’s circle is shown in Fig. 14.15.

From Section 7.8

\(\varepsilon_{ I }=\frac{62.4}{200000}-\frac{0.3(-32.4)}{200000}=360.6 \times 10^{-6}\)

and

\(\varepsilon_{ II }=\frac{-32.4}{200000}-\frac{0.3 \times 62.4}{200000}=-255.6 \times 10^{-6}\)

and from Eq. (14.25)

\(\left(\frac{\gamma}{2}\right)_{\max }=\frac{\varepsilon_{ I }-\varepsilon_{ II }}{2}\) (14.25)

 

\(\frac{(\gamma)_{\max }}{2}=\frac{(360.6+255.6)}{2} \times 10^{-6}\)

which gives

\(\gamma_{\max }=616.2 \times 10^{-6}\)
