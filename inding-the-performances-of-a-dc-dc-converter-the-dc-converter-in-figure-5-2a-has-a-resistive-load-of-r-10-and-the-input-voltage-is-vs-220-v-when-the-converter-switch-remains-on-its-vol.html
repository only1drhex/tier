Guest Question.

inding the Performances of a Dc–Dc Converter

The dc converter in Figure 5.2a has a resistive load of R = 10 Ω  and the input voltage is \(V_s = 220 V\). When the converter switch remains on, its voltage drop is \(v-{ch} = 2 V\) and the chopping frequency is f = 1 kHz. If the duty cycle is 50%, determine (a) the average output voltage \(V_a\), (b) the rms output voltage \(V_o\), (c) the converter efficiency, (d) the effective input resistance \(R_i\) of the converter, (e) the ripple factor of the output voltage \(RF_o\), and (f) the rms value of the fundamental component of output harmonic voltage.

Admin
Solution

\(V_s = 220 V, k = 0.5, R = 10\) , and \(v_{ch }= 2 V\).

a. From Eq. (5.8),

\(V_a=\frac{1}{T} \int_{0}^{t_1}{\upsilon _0dt}=\frac{t_1}{T}V_s=ft_1V_s=kV_s\) (5.8)

\(V_a = 0.5 × (220 – 2) = 109 V\).

b. From Eq. (5.9),

\(V_o=\left(\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^{kt}{\upsilon _0^2dt} \right)^{1/2}=\sqrt{k}V_s\) (5.9)

\(V_o =\sqrt{0.5}× (220 – 2) = 154.15 V\).

c. The output power can be found from

\(P_0=\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^{kT}{\frac{\upsilon _0^2}{R}dt }=\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^{kt}\frac{(V_s -\upsilon _{ch}^2}{R}dt=k\frac{(V_s -\upsilon _{ch})^2}{R}\) (5.12)

 

\(=0.5\times \frac{(220 -2)^2}{10} =2376.2 W\)

The input power to the converter can be found from

\(P_i=\frac{1}{T}\int_{0}^{kt}{V_sidt}=\frac{1}{T} \int_{0}^{kT}{\frac{V_s(V_s – \upsilon _{ch})}{R} } dt=k\frac{V_s(V_s -\upsilon _{ch})}{R}\) (5.13)

 

\(=0.5 \times 220\times \frac{220 -2}{10} =2398 W\)

The converter efficiency is

\(\frac{P_0}{P_i}=\frac{2376.2}{2398}=99.09\%\)

d. From Eq. (5.11),

\(R_i = V_s/I_a = V_s(V_a/R) = 220 × (109/10) = 20.18 Ω\)

e. Substituting Va from Eq. (5.8) and Vo from Eq. (5.9) into Eq. (5.6) gives the ripple factor as

\(RF_0=\frac{V_r}{V_a} =\sqrt{\frac{1}{k}-1 } \) (5.14)

 

\(=\sqrt{1/0.5 -1}=100\% \)

f. The output voltage as shown in Figure 5.2b can be expressed in a Fourier series as

\(\upsilon _0(t)=kV_s +\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty }{\frac{V_s}{n\pi }sin 2nk cos 2nft }\)

 

\(+ \frac{V_s}{n\pi }\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty }{(1 – cos 2nk)sin 2nft} \) (5.15)

The fundamental component (for n = 1) of output voltage harmonic can be determined from Eq. (5.15) as

[\(\upsilon _1(t)=\frac{(V_s -\upsilon _{ch})}{\pi }[sin 2\pi k cos 2\pi ft + (1 – cos 2\pi k) sin 2\pi ft]\) (5.16)

 

\(=\frac{(220 -2)\times 2}{\pi }sin(2\pi \times 1000t)=138.78 sin(6283t)\)

and its root-mean-square (rms) value is \(V_1 = 138.78/\sqrt{2} = 98.13 V\).

Note: The efficiency calculation, which includes the conduction loss of the converter, does not take into account the switching loss due to turn-on and turn-off of practical converters. The efficiency of a practical converter varies between 92 and 99%.
