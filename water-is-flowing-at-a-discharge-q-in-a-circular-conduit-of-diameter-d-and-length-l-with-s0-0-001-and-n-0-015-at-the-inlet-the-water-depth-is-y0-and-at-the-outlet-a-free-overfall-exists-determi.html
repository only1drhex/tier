Guest Question.

Water is flowing at a discharge Q in a circular conduit of diameter d and length L, with \(S_{0}=0.001\), and n = 0.015. At the inlet the water depth is \(y_{0}\) and at the outlet a free overfall exists. Determine the nature of the water surface in the conduit.

(a) \(Q=2.5 m ^{3} / s , L=500 m , d=2.5 m\), \(y_{0}=0.4 m\)

(b) \(Q=88 ft ^{3} / sec , L=1600 ft , d=8 ft\), \(y_{0}=1.3 ft\)

Admin
Solution

(a) Compute \(y_{c} \text { using } F r^{2}=1\):

 

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{Q^{2} B}{g A^{3}} &=\frac{Q^{2} d \sin \alpha}{g\left[\left(d^{2} / 4\right)(\alpha-\sin \alpha \cos \alpha)\right]^{3}}=\frac{2.5^{2} \times 2.5 \sin \alpha}{9.81\left[\left(2.5^{2} / 4\right)(\alpha-\sin \alpha \cos \alpha)\right]^{3}} \\&=0.4175 \frac{\sin \alpha}{(\alpha-\sin \alpha \cos \alpha)^{3}}=1,\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\therefore\) Solving,

 

\(\alpha=1.115 rad , \text { and } \therefore y_{c}=\frac{d}{2}(1-\cos \alpha)=\frac{2.5}{2}(1-\cos 1.115)=\underline{0.70 m }\).

 

Compute \(y_{0}\) using

 

\(\frac{Q n}{c_{1} \sqrt{S_{0}}}=\frac{\left[\left(d^{2} / 4\right)(\alpha-\sin \alpha \cos \alpha)\right]^{5 / 3}}{(\alpha d)^{2 / 3}}, \frac{2.5 \times 0.015}{1 \times \sqrt{0.001}}=\frac{\left[\left(2.5^{2} / 4\right)(\alpha-\sin \alpha \cos \alpha)\right]^{5 / 3}}{(\alpha \times 2.5)^{2 / 3}}\)

 

which reduces to \(1.039=\frac{(\alpha-\sin \alpha \cos \alpha)^{5 / 3}}{\alpha^{2 / 3}}\).

 

\(\therefore \text { Solving, } \alpha=1.361\) rad, and

 

\(\therefore y_{0}=\frac{d}{2}(1-\cos \alpha)=\frac{2.5}{2}(1-\cos 1.361)=\underline{0.99 m } .\)

 

Since \(y_{0}>y_{c}\), a mild slope condition exists. The water surface consists of an \(M_{3}\) curve beginning at the inlet, followed by a hydraulic jump to an \(M_{2}\) curve, which terminates at critical depth at the outlet.
