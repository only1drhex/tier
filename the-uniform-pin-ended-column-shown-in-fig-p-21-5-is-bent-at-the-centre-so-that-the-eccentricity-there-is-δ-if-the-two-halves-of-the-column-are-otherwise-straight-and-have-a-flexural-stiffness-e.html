Guest Question.

The uniform pin-ended column shown in Fig. P.21.5 is bent at the centre so that the eccentricity there is δ. If the two halves of the column are otherwise straight and have a flexural stiffness EI find the maximum bending moment when the column carries a compressive load P.
Ans.

\(-P \frac{2 \delta}{L} \sqrt{(E I / P)} \tan \sqrt{(P / E I)} \frac{L}{2}\)

Admin
Solution

Under the action of the compressive load P the column will be displaced to the position shown in Fig. S.21.5.

The bending moment at any section of the column is proportional to the change in curvature at that section (see Eqs (13.2) and (13.3)).

\(\frac{1}{R}=\frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}\) (13.2)

\(\frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=\frac{M}{E I}\) (13.3)

Then

\(E I \frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}-E I \frac{ d ^{2} v_{0}}{ d x^{2}}=-P v\) (i)

In this case, since each half of the column is straight before the application of P, \(d ^{2} v_{0} / d x^{2}=0\) and Eq. (i) reduces to

\(E I \frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=-P v\) (ii)

The solution of Eq. (ii) is

\(v=C_{1} \cos \mu x+C_{2} \sin \mu x\) (iii)

in which

\(\mu^{2}=P / E I\).

When x = 0, υ = 0 so that \(C_{1}=0\) and Eq. (iii) becomes

\(v=C_{2} \sin \mu x\) (iv)

The slope of the column at sections immediately either side of its mid-point in its unloaded state is 2δ/L. This must be the slope of the column in its loaded state since a change in slope over zero distance would require an infinite bending moment. Therefore, from Eq. (iv)

\(\frac{ d v}{ d x}=\frac{2 \delta}{L}=\mu C_{2} \cos \frac{\mu L}{2}\)

which gives

\(C_{2}=\frac{2 \delta}{\mu L \cos (\mu L / 2)}\).

Then

\(v=\frac{2 \delta}{\mu L \cos (\mu L / 2)} \sin \mu x\) (v)

The maximum bending moment will occur when υ is a maximum, that is at the mid-point of the column. Then

\(M_{\max }=-P v_{\max }=\frac{-2 P \delta}{\mu L} \tan \frac{\mu L}{2}=\frac{-2 P \delta}{L} \sqrt{(E I / P)} \tan \sqrt{(P / E I)} \frac{L}{2}\)
