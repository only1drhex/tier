Guest Question.

A 400 MVA, 22 kV synchronous generator is tested for OCC and SCC. The following data are obtained from these characteristics extrapolated where needed.

\( I_{f}=1120 A , \quad V_{O C}=22 kV , \quad I_{S C}=13.2 kA\)

At \( I_{f}\) corresponding to \( I_{SC} = I_{rated}\) it is found that \( V_{OC}\) (air-gap line) = 24.2 kV (line).

(a) Determine \( X_{s}\) (saturated) in ohms and in pu.

(b) Determine SCR.

(c) Determine \( X_{s}\) (unsaturated) and \( I_{f}\) corresponding to \( V_{OC} = 22.4 kV\) on air gap line.

(d) The generator when operating on no-load rated terminal voltage, find the value of the generator current if it is short circuited.

Admin
Solution

Base \( ( MVA )_{B}=400,( kV )_{B}=22\) (line) or 12.7 (phase)

\( I_{B}=\frac{400}{22 \sqrt{3}}=10.49 kA\)

 

\( ( Ohm )_{B}=\frac{22 \sqrt{3}}{10.49}=1.21 \Omega\)

(a) \( I_{f} = 1120 A\)

 

\( V_{O C}=V_{\text {rated }}=\frac{22}{\sqrt{3}}=12.7 kV\) (phase)

 

\( I_{S C}=13.2 kA\)

Then \( X_{s}( sat )=\frac{12.7}{13.2}=0.962 \Omega\)

Or \( \frac{0.962}{1.21}=0.795 pu\)

(b) \( S C R=\frac{1}{X_{s}( sat )( pu )}=\frac{1}{0.795}=1.258\)

(c) \( I_{S C}=I_{ rated }=10.49 kA\)

 

\( V_{O C} \text { (air gap line) }=24.2 kV\) (line)

= 13.97 kV (phase)

\( X_{s} \text { (unsat) }=\frac{13.97}{10.49}=1.33 \Omega=\frac{1.33}{1.21}=1.1 pu\)

For finding \( I_{f}\) \( SCR =\frac{o f^{\prime}}{o f^{\prime \prime}}\) (Fig. 8.15)

\( 1.258=\frac{o f^{\prime}}{1120}\)

Or \( I_{f}=o f^{\prime}=1120 \times 1.258=1409 A\)
