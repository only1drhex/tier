Guest Question.

A column is fabricated from two 305 mm×305 mm×158 kg Universal Column sections placed side by side as shown in Fig. P.21.12. The column has fixed ends and an overall height of 12 m.

Given that the yield stress in compression of mild steel is \(250 N / mm ^{2}\) calculate the maximum allowable load for the column using the Perry-Robertson formula. Take \(E=200000 N / mm ^{2}\) and assume a factor of safety of 2. The properties of a single 305 mm×305 mm×158 kg UC are:
\(\text { Area }=201.2 cm ^{2}, I_{z}=38740 cm ^{4}, I_{y}=12524 cm ^{4}\)
Ans. 4328 kN.

Admin
Solution

The second moment of area about the axis of symmetry parallel to the flanges of the composite section is

\(I_{x}=2 \times 38740=77480 cm ^{4}\)

and about the axis of symmetry parallel to the webs of the section

\(I_{y}=2\left(12524+201.2 \times 19^{2}\right)=170314 cm ^{4}\)

The column will therefore buckle about the x axis.
The corresponding radius of gyration is then given by

\(r=\sqrt{77480 / 2 \times 201.2}=13.88 cm\)

The slenderness ratio \(=\frac{0.5 \times 12 \times 10^{3}}{13.88 \times 10}=43.2\)

and

\(\sigma_{ CR }=\frac{\pi^{2} \times 200000}{(43.2)^{2}}=1058 N / mm ^{2}\) (Eq. (21.25))

\(\sigma_{ CR }=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(L_{ e } / r\right)^{2}}\) (21.25)

The yield stress in compression of the material of the column is \(250 N / mm ^{2}\). Substituting these values in Eq. (21.46)

\(\sigma=\frac{1}{2}\left[\sigma_{ Y }+\left(1+0.003 \frac{L}{r}\right) \sigma_{ CR }\right]-\sqrt{\frac{1}{4}\left[\sigma_{ Y }+\left(1+0.003 \frac{L}{r}\right) \sigma_{ CR }\right]^{2}-\sigma_{ Y } \sigma_{ CR }}\) (21.46)

gives

\(\sigma=215.1 N / mm ^{2}\)

The maximum allowable load is then, taking the factor of safety as 2

\(P_{\max }=\frac{215.1 \times 2 \times 201.2 \times 10^{2} \times 10^{-3}}{2}=4328 kN\)
