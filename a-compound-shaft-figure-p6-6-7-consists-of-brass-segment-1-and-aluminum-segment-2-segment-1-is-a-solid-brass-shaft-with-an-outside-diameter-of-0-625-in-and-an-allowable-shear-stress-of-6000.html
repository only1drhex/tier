Guest Question.

A compound shaft (Figure P6.6/7) consists of brass segment (1) and aluminum segment (2). Segment (1) is a solid brass shaft with an outside diameter of 0.625 in. and an allowable shear stress of 6,000 psi. Segment (2) is a solid aluminum shaft with an outside diameter of 0.50 in. and an allowable shear stress of 9,000 psi. Determine the magnitude of the largest torque \(T_{C}\) that may be applied at C.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&J_{1}=\frac{\pi}{32}(0.625 in .)^{4}=0.014980 in .{ }^{4} \\&J_{2}=\frac{\pi}{32}(0.50 in .)^{4}=0.006136 in .{ }^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Allowable internal torques:

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{1} \leq \frac{\tau_{1} J_{1}}{c_{1}}=\frac{(6,000 psi )\left(0.014980 in. ^{4}\right)}{0.625 in . / 2}=287.621 lb \text {-in. } \\&T_{2} \leq \frac{\tau_{2} J_{2}}{c_{2}}=\frac{(9,000 psi )\left(0.006136 in .^{4}\right)}{0.50 in. / 2}=220.893 lb – in . \quad \leftarrow \text { controls }\end{aligned}\)

Equilibrium :

The internal torque magnitude in each segment equals the external torque; therefore, \(T_{1}=T_{2}=T_{C}\). The controlling internal torque is \(T_{2}\) = 220.893 lb-in.; therefore, the maximum external torque \(T_{C}\) that may be applied to the compound shaft is

\(T_{C} \leq 220.893 lb \text {-in. }=221 lb \text {-in. }\)
