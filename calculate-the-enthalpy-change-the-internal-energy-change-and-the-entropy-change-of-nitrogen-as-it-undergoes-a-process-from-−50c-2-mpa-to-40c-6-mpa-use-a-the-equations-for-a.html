Guest Question.

Calculate the enthalpy change, the internal-energy change, and the entropy change of nitrogen as it undergoes a process from −50°C, 2 MPa, to 40°C, 6 MPa. Use (a) the equations for an ideal gas with constant specific heats, (b) the Ideal-Gas Tables, and (c) the equations of this section.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { (a) } \Delta h=C_{p} \Delta T=(1.042)[40-(-50)]=93.8 kJ / kg \quad \Delta u=C_{v} \Delta T=(0.745)[40-(-50)]=67.0 kJ / kg \).

\( \Delta s=C_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}=1.042 \ln \frac{313}{223}-0.297 \ln \frac{6}{2}=0.0270 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

\( \Delta h=h_{2}-h_{1}=(9102-6479) / 28=93.7 kJ / kg \Delta u=u_{2}-u_{1}=(6499-4625) / 28=66.9 kJ / kg \).

\( \Delta s=s_{2}^{0}-s_{1}^{0}-R \ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}=(192.9-183.0) / 28-0.297 \ln (6 / 2)=0.0273 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

(c) Using (11.69) and the Enthalpy Departure Charts (Appendix H) we find

\( \frac{h^{*}-h}{T_{c r}}=-R T_{R}^{2} \int_{0}^{P_{R}}\left(\frac{\partial Z}{\partial T_{R}}\right)_{P_{R}} \frac{d P_{R}}{P_{R}} \) (11.69).

\( T_{R 1}=\frac{T_{1}}{T_{c r}}=\frac{223}{126.2}=1.77 \quad P_{R 1}=\frac{P_{1}}{P_{c r}}=\frac{2}{3.39}=0.590 \).

\( T_{R 2}=\frac{T_{2}}{T_{c r}}=\frac{313}{126.2}=2.48 \quad P_{R 2}=\frac{P_{2}}{P_{c r}}=\frac{6}{3.39}=1.770 \).

The Enthalpy Departure Charts (Appendix H) provide us with

\( \frac{\bar{h}_{1}^{*}-\bar{h}_{1}}{T_{c r}}=1.6 kJ / kmol \cdot K \quad \therefore h_{1}^{*}-h_{1}=\frac{(1.6)(126.2)}{28}=7.21 kJ / kg \).

\( \frac{\overline{h_{2}}^{*}-\bar{h}_{2}}{T_{c r}}=2.5 kJ / kmol \cdot K \quad \therefore h_{2}^{*}-h_{2}=\frac{(2.5)(126.2)}{28}=11.27 kJ / kg \).

Consequently,

\( \Delta h=\left(h_{2}-h_{2}^{*}\right)+\left(h_{1}^{*}-h_{1}\right)+\left(h_{2}^{*}-h_{1}^{*}\right)=-11.27+7.21+(1.042)[40-(-50)]=90 kJ / kg \).

To find the internal energy change, we use (11.70). The Z values are found, using the compressibility chart with the above \( T_{R} \text { and } P_{R} \text { values, to be } Z_{1}=0.99 \text { and } Z_{2}=0.985 \text {. Then } \)

\( u_{2}-u_{1}=h_{2}-h_{1}-R\left(Z_{2} T_{2}-Z_{1} T_{1}\right) \) (11.70).

\( \Delta u=\Delta h-R\left(Z_{2} T_{2}-Z_{1} T_{1}\right)=90-(0.297)[(0.985)(313)-(0.99)(223)]=64 kJ / kg \).

\( \text { To find the entropy change, we first find } s_{1}^{*}-s_{1} \text { and } s_{2}^{*}-s_{2} \text { using the Entropy Departure Charts (Appendix I). } \)

\( \bar{s}_{1}^{*}-\bar{s}_{1}=1.0 kJ / kmol \cdot K \quad \therefore \bar{s}_{1}^{*}-s_{1}=1.0 / 28=0.036 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

\( \bar{s}_{2}^{*}-\bar{s}_{2}=1.2 kJ / kmol \cdot K \quad \therefore \bar{s}_{2}^{*}-s_{2}=1.2 / 28=0.043 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

The entropy change is then

\( \Delta s=\left(s_{2}-s_{2}^{*}\right)+\left(s_{1}^{*}-s_{1}\right)+\left(s_{2}^{*}-s_{1}^{*}\right)=-0.043+0.036+1.042 \ln \frac{313}{223}-0.297 \ln \frac{6}{2}=0.02 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

Note that the real-gas effects in this example were not very pronounced. The temperatures were quite high compared to \( T_{c r} \) and the pressures were not excessively large. Also, accuracy using the small charts is quite difficult.
