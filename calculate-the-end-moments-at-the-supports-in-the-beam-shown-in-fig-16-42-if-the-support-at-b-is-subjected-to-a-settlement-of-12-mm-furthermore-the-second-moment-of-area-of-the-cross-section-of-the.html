Guest Question.

Calculate the end moments at the supports in the beam shown in Fig. 16.42 if the support at B is subjected to a settlement of 12 mm. Furthermore, the second moment of area of the cross section of the beam is \(9 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\) in the span AB and \(12 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\) in the span BC; Young’s modulus, E, is \(200000 N / mm ^{2}\).

Admin
Solution

In this example the FEMs produced by the applied loads are modified by additional moments produced by the sinking support. Thus, using Table 16.6

FEMs
Local case \(M_{A B}^{F}\) \(M_{B A}^{F}\)
\(-\frac{W L}{8}\) \(+\frac{W L}{8}\)
\(-\frac{W a b^{2}}{L^{2}}\) \(+\frac{W a^{2} b}{L^{2}}\)
\(-\frac{w L^{2}}{12}\) \(+\frac{W L^{2}}{12}\)
\(-\frac{W}{L^{2}}\left[\frac{L^{2}}{2}\left(b^{2}-a^{2}\right)-\frac{2}{3} L\left(b^{3}-a^{3}\right)\right.\)

 

\(\left.+\frac{1}{4}\left(b^{4}-a^{4}\right)\right]\)

 \(+\frac{w b^{3}}{L^{2}}\left(\frac{L}{3}-\frac{b}{4}\right)\)
\(+\frac{M_{0} b}{L^{2}}(2 a-b)\) \(+\frac{M_{0} a}{L^{2}}(2 b-a)\)
\(-\frac{6 E / \delta}{L^{2}}\) \(-\frac{6 E / \delta}{L^{2}}\)
0 \(-\frac{3 E / \delta}{L^{2}}\)
\(M_{ AB }^{ F }=-\frac{6 \times 5^{2}}{12}-\frac{6 \times 200000 \times 9 \times 10^{6} \times 12}{\left(5 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2} \times 10^{6}}=-17.7 kNm\)

 

\(M_{ BA }^{ F }=+\frac{6 \times 5^{2}}{12}-\frac{6 \times 200000 \times 9 \times 10^{6} \times 12}{\left(5 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2} \times 10^{6}}=+7.3 kNm\)

Since the support at C is an outside pinned support, the effect on the FEMs in BC of the settlement of B is reduced (see the last case in Table 16.6). Thus

\(M_{ BC }^{ F }=-\frac{40 \times 6}{8}+\frac{3 \times 200000 \times 12 \times 10^{6} \times 12}{\left(6 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2} \times 10^{6}}=-27.6 kNm\)

 

\(M_{ CB }^{ F }=+\frac{40 \times 6}{8}=+30.0 kNm\)

The DFs are

\(DF _{ BA }=\frac{K_{ BA }}{K_{ BA }+K_{ BC }}=\frac{\left(4 E \times 9 \times 10^{6}\right) / 5}{\left(4 E \times 9 \times 10^{6}\right) / 5+\left(3 E \times 12 \times 10^{6}\right) / 6}=0.55\)

Hence

\(DF _{ BC }=1-0.55=0.45\)

Note that in this example balancing the beam at B has a significant effect on the fixing moment at A; we therefore complete the distribution after a carry over to A.
