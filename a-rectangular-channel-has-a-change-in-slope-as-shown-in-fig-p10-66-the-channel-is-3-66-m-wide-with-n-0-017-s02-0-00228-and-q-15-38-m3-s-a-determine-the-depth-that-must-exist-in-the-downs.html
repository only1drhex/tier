Guest Question.

A rectangular channel has a change in slope as shown in Fig. P10.66. The channel is 3.66 m wide, with \(n=0.017, S_{02}=0.00228 \text {, and } Q=15.38 m ^{3} / s\).

(a) Determine the depth that must exist in the downstream channel for a hydraulic jump to terminate at uniform flow conditions.

(b) If \(y_{01}=0.6\) m, calculate the length to the jump, \(L_{j}\), using several increments of depth in a step calculation.

(c) Sketch the water surface and energy grade line, and identify all gradually varied flow profiles.

Admin
Solution

Compute normal depth using Chezy-Manning eqn:

 

\(\frac{Q n}{c_{1} \sqrt{S_{0}}}=\frac{15.38 \times 0.017}{1 \times \sqrt{0.00228}}=5.476=\frac{\left(3.66 y_{0_{2}}\right)^{5 / 3}}{\left(3.66+2 y_{0_{2}}\right)^{2 / 3}}\)

 

\(\therefore \text { Solving, } y_{0_{2}}=\underline{1.65 m}\).

 

Compute critical depth:

 

\(q=Q / b=15.38 / 3.66=4.20 m ^{2} / s , \quad \therefore y_{c}=\sqrt[3]{q^{2} / g}=\sqrt[3]{4.20^{2} / 9.81}=\underline{1.22 m }\).

 

âˆ´Upstream of slope change the channel is steep, and downstream, the channel is mild. The hydraulic jump will terminate at normal depth.

(a) Find depth conjugate to \(y_{0_{2}}\):

 

\(Fr _{02}^{2}=q^{2} / g y_{02}^{3}=4.20^{2} /\left(9.81 \times 1.65^{3}\right)=0.400\),

 

\(\therefore y_{c j}=\frac{y_{0_{2}}}{2}\left(\sqrt{1+8 Fr _{02}^{2}}-1\right)=\frac{1.65}{2}(\sqrt{1+8 \times 0.4}-1)=\underline{0.87 m }\).

 

(b) Step method:

 

\(E=y+\frac{q^{2}}{2 g y^{2}}=y+\frac{4.20^{2}}{2 \times 9.81 y^{2}}=y+\frac{0.900}{y^{2}}\),

 

\(\begin{aligned}S(y) &=\frac{Q^{2} n^{2}(b+2 y)^{4 / 3}}{(b y)^{10 / 3}}=\frac{15.38^{2} \times 0.017^{2}(3.66+2 y)^{4 / 3}}{(3.66)^{10 / 3} y^{10 / 3}} \\&=9.052 \times 10^{-4}(3.66+2 y)^{4 / 3} y^{-10 / 3}.\end{aligned}\)

 

 

(c) An \(M_{3}\) curve exists downstream of the change in channel slope, terminating in a hydraulic jump, approximately 50 m from the slope change.
