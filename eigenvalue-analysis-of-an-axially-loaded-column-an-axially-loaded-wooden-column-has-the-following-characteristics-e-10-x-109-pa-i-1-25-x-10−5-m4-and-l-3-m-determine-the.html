Eigenvalue Analysis of an Axially Loaded Column

An axially loaded wooden column has the following characteristics: \(E = 10 \times 10^9 Pa, I = 1.25 \times 10^{−5} m^4\) , and \(L = 3 m\). Determine the first eight eigenvalues and the corresponding buckling loads.

 

 

Admin
Solution

Equations (27.16) and (27.17) can be used to compute

n \(p, m^{-2}\) P, kN
1 1.0472 137.078
2 2.0944 548.311
3 3.1416 1233.701
4 4.1888 2193.245
5 5.236 3426.946
6 6.2832 4934.802
7 7.3304 6716.814
8 8.3776 8772.982

\(P =\frac{n\pi }{L} \) \(for n = 1, 2, 3, \cdot \cdot \cdot \) (27.16)

\(P=\frac{n^{2}\pi^{2}EI}{L^{2}} \) \(for n = 1, 2, 3,\cdot \cdot \cdot \) (27.17)

The critical buckling load is, therefore, \(137.078 kN\).
