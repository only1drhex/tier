Guest Question.

A uniform simply supported beam, span L, carries a distributed loading which varies according to a parabolic law across the span. The load intensity is zero at both ends of the beam and \(w_{ o }\) at its midpoint. The loading is normal to a principal axis of the beam cross section and the relevant flexural rigidity is EI. Assuming that the deflected shape of the beam can be represented by the series

\(v=\sum_{ i =1}^{\infty} a_{ i } \sin \frac{ i \pi x}{L}\)

find the coefficients \(a_{ i }\) and the deflection at the mid-span of the beam using only the first term in this series.
Ans. \(a_{ i }=32 w_{ o } L^{4} / E I \pi^{7} i ^{7} \text { (i odd), } w_{ o } L^{4} / 94.4 E I\).

Admin
Solution

The problem is solved in a similar manner to . Therefore Eq. (iii) of S.15.20 applies,

\(C=\int_{0}^{L} \frac{E I}{2}\left(\frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}\right)^{2} d x-\int_{0}^{L} w v d x\) Eq. (iii) of S.15.20

that is

\(C=\int_{L} \frac{E I}{2}\left(\frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}\right)^{2} d x-\int_{L} w v d x\) (i)

in which

\(v=\sum_{i=1}^{\infty} a_{i} \sin \frac{i \pi x}{L}\) (ii)

and w may be expressed as a function of x in the form \(w=4 w_{0} x(L-x) / L^{2}\) which satisfies the boundary conditions of w = 0 at x = 0 and x = L and \(w=w_{0}\) at x = L/2.
Substituting in Eq. (i)

\(C=\frac{E I}{2} \int_{0}^{L} \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} a_{i}^{2} \frac{i^{4} \pi^{4}}{L^{4}} \sin ^{2} \frac{i \pi x}{L} d x-\frac{4 w_{0}}{L^{2}} \int_{0}^{L} x(L-x) \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} a_{i} \sin \frac{i \pi x}{L} d x\) (iii)

Now

\(\int_{0}^{L} \sin ^{2} \frac{i \pi x}{L} d x=\int_{0}^{L} \frac{1}{2}\left(1-\cos \frac{i 2 \pi x}{L}\right) d x=\left[\frac{x}{2}-\frac{L}{i 2 \pi} \sin \frac{i 2 \pi x}{L}\right]_{0}^{L}=\frac{L}{2}\)

 

\(\int_{0}^{L} L x \sin \frac{i \pi x}{L} d x=L\left[-\frac{x L}{i \pi} \cos \frac{i \pi x}{L}+\int \frac{L}{i \pi} \cos \frac{i \pi x}{L} d x\right]_{0}^{L}=\frac{-L^{3}}{i \pi} \cos i \pi\)

 

\(\int_{0}^{L} x^{2} \sin \frac{i \pi x}{L} d x=\left[-\frac{x^{2} L}{i \pi} \cos \frac{i \pi x}{L}+\int \frac{L}{i \pi} \cos \frac{i \pi x}{L} 2 x d x\right]_{0}^{L}=-\frac{L^{3}}{i \pi} \cos i \pi+\frac{2 L^{3}}{i^{3} \pi^{3}}(\cos i \pi-1)\)

Substituting these expressions in Eq. (iii) gives

\(C=\sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \frac{E I a_{i}^{2} i^{4} \pi^{4}}{4 L^{3}}-\frac{4 w_{0}}{L^{3}} \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \frac{2 a_{i} L^{3}}{i^{3} \pi^{3}}(1-\cos i \pi)\) (iv)

The value of \((1-\cos i \pi)\) is zero when i is even and 2 when i is odd. Therefore Eq. (iv) may be written

\(C=\frac{E I a_{i}^{2} i^{4} \pi^{4}}{4 L^{3}}-\frac{16 w_{0} a_{i} L}{i^{3} \pi^{3}}\) i is odd

From the principle of the stationary value of the total complementary energy

\(\frac{\partial C}{\partial a_{i}}=\frac{E I i^{4} \pi^{4} a_{i}}{2 L^{3}}-\frac{16 w_{0} L}{i^{3} \pi^{3}}=0\)

which gives

\(a_{i}=\frac{32 w_{0} L^{4}}{E I i^{7} \pi^{7}}\)

Then

\(v=\sum_{i=1}^{\infty} \frac{32 w_{0} L^{4}}{E I i^{7} \pi^{7}} \sin \frac{\pi x}{L}\) i is odd

At mid-span where x = L/2 and using the first term only in this expression

\(v_{\text {mid }-\text { span }}=\frac{w_{0} L^{4}}{94.4 E I}\)
