Guest Question.

control charts for means and ranges . Processing new accounts at a bank is intended to average 10 minutes each. Five samples of four observations each have been taken. Use the sample data in conjunction with Table 10.2

TABLE 10.2
Type I and Type II errors

to construct upper and lower control limits for both a mean chart and a range chart. Do the results suggest that process is in control?

Sample 1 Sample 2 Sample 3 Sample 4 Sample 5
10.2 10.3 9.7 9.9 9.8
9.9 9.8 9.9 10.3 10.2
9.8 9.9 9.9 10.1 10.3
\(\underline{10.1}\) \(\underline{10.4}\) \(\underline{10.1}\) \(\underline{10.5}\) \(\underline{9.7}\)

Solution

a. Determine the mean and range of each sample.

\(\overline{x}= \frac{\sum{x} }{n}\), Range = Largest – Smallest

Sample Mean Range
1 40·0/4 =10·0 10.2 – 9.8 = .4
2 40.4/4 = 10.1 10.4 – 9.8 = .6
3 39.6/4 = 9.9 10.1 – 9.7 =.4
4 40.8/4 = 10.2 10.5 – 9.9 =.6
5 40·0/4 =10·0 10.3 – 9.7 =.6

b. Compute the average mean and average range:

\(\overset{=}{x} = \frac{10.0 + 10.1 + 9.9 + 10.2 +10.0}{5}=\frac{50.2}{5}=10.04\)

 

\(\overline{R}=\frac{.4 + .6 + .4 + .6 + .6}{5}= \frac{2.6}{5}= .52\)

c. Obtain factors \(A_2, D_4\),and \(D_3\) from Tabl 10.2 for n=4: \(A_2=.73, D_4=2.28, D_3=0\).

d. Compute upper and lower limits:

\(UCL_{\overline{x}}= \overset{=}{x}+A_2 \overline{R}=10.04 +.73(.52)=10.42\)

 

\(LCL_{\overline{x}}=\overset{=}{x}-A_2\overline{R}=10.04 -.73(.52)=9.66 \)

 

\(UCL_R= D_4\overline{R}=2.28(.52)=1.19 \)

 

\(LCL_R= D_3\overline{R}=0(.52)=0 \)

e. Verify that points are within limits. ( If they were not , the process would be investigated to correct assignable causes of variation)

The smallest sample mean is 9.9, and the largest is 10.2 Both are well within the control limits Similarly , the largest sample range.6, which is also within the control limits, Hence, the results suggest that the process is in control. Not, however, that for illustrative purposes, the number of samples is deliberately small; 20 or more samples would give a clear indication of control limits and whether the process is in control.
