Guest Question.

The symmetrical two-pinned arch shown in Fig. P.16.22 comprises two segments each having a radius of 8 m. Calculate the horizontal reactions at the supports and the bending moment at the crown; the section properties are uniform throughout.
Ans. 20.4 kN, 58.7 kNm.

Admin
Solution

Referring to Fig. S.16.22 the support reactions, from symmetry, are

\(R_{ A , V }=R_{ C , V }=W / 2\) and \(R_{ A , H }=R_{ C , H }\)

Release the arch at C, then

\(\Delta_{ C , H }-a_{11} R_{ C , H }=0\) (i)

where

\(\Delta_{ C , H }=\int \frac{M_{0} M_{1}}{E I} d s \quad a_{11}=\int \frac{M_{1}^{2}}{E I} d s\)

and

\(M_{0}=\frac{W R}{2}(1-\cos \theta), \quad M_{1}=-R \sin \theta\)

Then

\(\Delta_{ C , H }=\frac{2}{E I} \int_{0}^{\pi / 3} \frac{W R}{2}(1-\cos \theta)(-R \sin \theta) R d \theta=\frac{-W R^{3}}{E I} \int_{0}^{\pi / 3}(\sin \theta-\sin \theta \cos \theta) d \theta\)

which gives

\(\Delta_{ C , H }=\frac{-W R^{3}}{8 E I}\)

Also

\(a_{11}=2 \int_{0}^{\pi / 3} \frac{R^{3} \sin ^{2} \theta}{E I} d \theta=\frac{R^{3}}{E I} \int_{0}^{\pi / 3}(1-\cos 2 \theta) d \theta\)

from which

\(a_{11}=\frac{0.614 R^{3}}{E I}\)

Substituting in Eq. (i) for \(\Delta_{ C , H }\) and \(a_{11}\) gives

\(R_{ C , H }=0.204 W\)

so that

\(R_{ C , H }=20.4 kN\)

The bending moment at B is then

\(M_{ B }=\frac{100}{2} \times 4-20.4 \times 8 \sin 60^{\circ}=58.7 kNm\)
