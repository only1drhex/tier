Guest Question.

For the circuit shown, determine the impedance magnitude at resonance, at 1 KHz below resonance and, at 1 KHz above resonance.

Admin
Solution
\(f_{0}=\frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{100\times 10^{-3}\times 0.01\times 10^{-6}}}\)

 

= 5.03 KHz

 

Frequency \(f_{1}=5.03-1=4.03\) KHz

 

and \(f_{2}=5.03+1=6.03\) KHz

 

Note these \(f_{1}\) and \(f_{2}\) are not necessarily half power frequencies and hence \(Z_{1}\) need not necessarily equal \(Z_{2}\).

 

\(Z_{1}=\sqrt{10^{2}+(2.53-3.95)\times 10^{6}}\)

 

Since 10 is negligibly small as compared to \(X_{L}\) and \(X_{C}\) which are in \(K\Omega\) , therefore,

 

\(Z_{1}=-j1.42\, K\Omega\)

 

\(Z_{2}=j3.79-j2.64=j1.15\, K\Omega\)
