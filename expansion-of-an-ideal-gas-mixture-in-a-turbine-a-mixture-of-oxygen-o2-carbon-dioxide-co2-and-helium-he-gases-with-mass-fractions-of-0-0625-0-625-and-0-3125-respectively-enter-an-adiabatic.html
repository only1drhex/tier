Guest Question.

Expansion of an Ideal Gas Mixture in a Turbine

A mixture of oxygen (\(O_{2}\)), carbon dioxide (\(CO_{2}\)), and helium (He) gases with mass fractions of 0.0625, 0.625, and 0.3125, respectively, enter an adiabatic turbine at 1000 kPa and 600 K steadily and expand to 100 kPa pressure (Fig. 13–14). The isentropic efficiency of the turbine is 90 percent. For gas components assuming constant specific heats at room temperature, determine (a) the work output per unit mass of mixture and (b) the exergy destruction and the second-law efficiency of the turbine. Take the environment temperature to be \(T_{0}\) = 25°C.

Admin
Solution

The mass fractions of the components of a gas mixture that expands in an adiabatic turbine are given. The work output, the exergy destruction, and the second-law efficiency are to be determined.

Assumptions All gases will be modeled as ideal gases with constant specific heats.

Analysis (a) The mass fractions of mixture components are given to be \(mf_{CO_{2}} = 0.0625\), \(mf_{CO_{2}} = 0.625\), and \(mf_{He} = 0.3125\). The specific heats of these gases at room temperature are :

\(c_{v}, kJ/kg.K\) \(c_{p}, kJ/kg.K\)
\(O_{2} :\) 0.658 0.918
\(CO_{2} :\) 0.657 0.846
\(He :\) 3.1156 5.1926

Then, the constant-pressure and constant-volume specific heats of the mixture become

\(\begin{aligned}c_{p} &=\mathrm{mf}_{\mathrm{O}_{2}} c_{p, \mathrm{O}_{2}}+\mathrm{mf}_{\mathrm{CO}_{2}} c_{p, \mathrm{CO}_{2}}+\mathrm{mf}_{\mathrm{He}} c_{p, \mathrm{He}} \\&=0.0625 \times 0.918+0.625 \times 0.846+0.3125 \times 5.1926 \\&=2.209 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K} \\c_{v} &=\mathrm{mf}_{\mathrm{O}_{2}} c_{v, \mathrm{O}_{2}}+\mathrm{mf}_{\mathrm{CO}_{2}} c_{v, \mathrm{CO}_{2}}+\mathrm{mf}_{\mathrm{He}} c_{v, \mathrm{He}} \\&=0.0625 \times 0.658+0.625 \times 0.657+0.3125 \times 3.1156 \\&=1.425 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}\end{aligned}\)

The apparent gas constant of the mixture and the specific heat ratio are

\(\begin{array}{c}R=c_{p}-c_{v}=2.209-1.425=0.7836 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K} \\\\k=\frac{c_{p}}{c_{v}}=\frac{2.209 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}}{1.425 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}}=1.550\end{array}\)

The temperature at the end of the expansion for the isentropic process is

\(T_{2 s}=T_{1}\left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}=(600 \mathrm{~K})\left(\frac{100 \mathrm{kPa}}{1000 \mathrm{kPa}}\right)^{0.55 / 1.55}=265.0 \mathrm{~K}\)

Using the definition of turbine isentropic efficiency, the actual outlet temperature is

\(T_{2}=T_{1}-\eta_{T}\left(T_{1}-T_{2 s}\right)=(600 \mathrm{~K})-(0.90)(600-265) \mathrm{K}=298.5 \mathrm{~K}\)

Noting that the turbine is adiabatic and thus there is no heat transfer, the actual work output is determined to be

\(\begin{aligned}w_{\text {out }} &=h_{1}-h_{2}=c_{p}\left(T_{1}-T_{2}\right)=(2.209 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K})(600-298.5) \mathrm{K} \\&=666.0 \mathrm{k} \mathrm{J} / \mathrm{kg}\end{aligned}\)

(b) The entropy change of the gas mixture and the exergy destruction in the turbine are

\(\begin{array}{c}s_{2}-s_{1}=c_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}=(2.209 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}) \ln \frac{298.5 \mathrm{~K}}{600 \mathrm{~K}} \\\\-(0.7836 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}) \ln \frac{100 \mathrm{kPa}}{1000 \mathrm{kPa}}=0.2658 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K} \\\\x_{\mathrm{dest}}=T_{0} s_{\mathrm{gen}}=T_{0}\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)=(298 \mathrm{~K})(0.2658 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K})=79.2 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}\end{array}\)

The expended exergy is the sum of the work output of the turbine (exergy recovered) and the exergy destruction (exergy wasted),

\(x_{\text {expended }}=x_{\text {recovered }}+x_{\text {dest }}=w_{\text {out }}+x_{\text {dest }}=666.0+79.2=745.2 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}\)

The second-law efficiency is the ratio of the recovered to expended exergy,

\(\eta_{\mathrm{II}}=\frac{x_{\text {recovered }}}{x_{\text {expended }}}=\frac{w_{\text {out }}}{x_{\text {expended }}}=\frac{660.0 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}}{745.2 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}}=0.894 \text { or } 89.4 \%\)

Discussion The second-law efficiency is a measure of thermodynamic perfection. A process that generates no entropy and thus destroys no exergy always has a second-law efficiency of 100 percent.
