Newton-Raphson for a Nonlinear System

Use the multiple-equation Newton-Raphson method to determine roots of Eq. (6.19).

\(u(x, y) = x^2 + xy - 10 = 0\) (6.19a)

 

\(\upsilon (x, y) = y + 3xy^2 - 57 = 0\) (6.19b)

Note that a correct pair of roots is x = 2 and y = 3. Initiate the computation with guesses of x = 1.5 and y = 3.5.

Admin
Solution

First compute the partial derivatives and evaluate them at the initial guesses of x and y :

\(\frac{\partial u_0}{\partial x}= 2x + y = 2(1.5) + 3.5 =6.5 \frac{\partial u_0}{\partial y}= x = 1.5\)

 

\(\frac{\partial \upsilon _0}{\partial x}= 2y^2 = 3(3.5)^2 = 36.75 \frac{\partial \upsilon _0}{\partial y}= 1 + 6xy = 1+(1.5)(3.5)=32.5\)

Thus, the determinant of the Jacobian for the first iteration is

\(6.5(32.5) – 1.5(36.75) = 156.125\)

The values of the functions can be evaluated at the initial guesses as

\(u_0 = (1.5)- 1 1.5(3.5) – 10 = -2.5\)

 

\(\upsilon _0 = 3.5 + 3(1.5)(3.5)^2 – 57 = 1.625\)

These values can be substituted into Eq. (6.24)

\(x_{i+1}= x_i – \frac{u_i\frac{\partial \upsilon _i}{\partial y}-\upsilon _i\frac{\partial u_i}{\partial y} }{\frac{\partial u_i}{\partial x}\frac{\partial \upsilon _i}{\partial y} – \frac{\partial u_i}{\partial y}\frac{\partial \upsilon _i}{\partial x} }\) (6.24a)

 

\(y_{i+1}= y_i – \frac{\upsilon _i\frac{\partial u _i}{\partial x}- u _i\frac{\partial \upsilon _i}{\partial x} }{\frac{\partial u_i}{\partial x}\frac{\partial \upsilon _i}{\partial y} – \frac{\partial u_i}{\partial y}\frac{\partial \upsilon _i}{\partial x} } \) (6.24b)

to give

\(x = 1.5 -\frac{- 2.5(32.5) – 1.625(1.5)}{156.125}= 2.03603\)

 

\(y = 3.5 – \frac{1.625(6.5) – (22.5) (36.75)}{156.125}= 2.84388\)

Thus, the results are converging to the true values of x = 2 and y = 3. The computation can be repeated until an acceptable accuracy is obtained.
