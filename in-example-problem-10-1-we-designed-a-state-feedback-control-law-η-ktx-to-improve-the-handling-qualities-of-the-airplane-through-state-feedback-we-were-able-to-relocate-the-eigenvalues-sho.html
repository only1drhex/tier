Guest Question.

In we designed a state feedback control law, \(\eta =-k^Tx\), to improve the handling qualities of the airplane. Through state feedback we were able to relocate the eigenvalues (short-period roots) of the airplane. To implement the stability augmentation system all the states must be available. In this problem we assume that the states are not measured by onboard sensors and therefore are unavailable for feedback. To overcome this difficulty we design a state observer to provide predictions of the necessary states. The predicted states then can be used in conjunction with the state feedback control law determined in

Solution

The characteristic equation of the observer can be determined by expanding the following equation:

\(\left|\lambda I-(A-K_eC)\right| =0\)

where A and C are the plant and output matrices and \(K_e\)
is the observer gain vector. For this problem the output matrix is given by

\(C=\left[\begin{matrix}1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right] \)

Substituting the appropriate matrices into the preceding equation and expanding yields

\(\left|\begin{bmatrix} \lambda & 0 \\ 0 & \lambda \end{bmatrix} – \begin{bmatrix} – 0.334 & 1.0 \\ – 2.52 & – 0.387 \end{bmatrix}+ \left[\begin{matrix} K_{e_1} \\ K_{e_2} \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix}1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right] \right|=0 \)

 

\(\left|\begin{matrix} \lambda +0.334+K_{e_1} & -1.0 \\ 2.52+K_{e_2} & \lambda + 0.387\end{matrix} \right|=0 \)

 

\(\lambda ^2+(0.721+K_{e_1})\lambda +2.65+0.387k_{e_1}+k_{e_2}=0\)

As stated in Chapter 9 the observer roots should be more responsive than the roots of the state feedback system. We assume for this example problem that the observer roots are four times as responsive as the state feedback roots. From a mathematical standpoint the observer roots can be located anywhere in the left-hand portion of the complex plane, provided they are to the left of the desired closed-loop system roots. However, practical constraints similar to those discussed in Chapter 9 for the state feedback root location also apply to the placement of the observer roots. In the roots for the state feedback system were located at \(\lambda _{SF}=-2.1\pm 2.14i\).The desired observer characteristic equation is obtained by

\((\lambda -\lambda _{OB_1} ) (\lambda -\lambda _{OB_2} )=0\)

 

\((\lambda + 8.4 + 8.56i) (\lambda + 8.4 – 8.56i) = 0\)

 

\(\lambda ^2+ 16.8\lambda + 143.8 = 0\)

By equating the desired observer characteristic equation with the one as a function of the observer gains we obtain a set of equations for the unknown observer gains.

\(0.721 + k_{e_1} = 16.8\)

 

\(2.65 + 0.387 k_{e_1}+ k_{e_2} = 143.8\)

Solving for each \(K_e\) yields

\(k_{e_1}=16.08\)

 

\(k_{e_2}=134.9\)
