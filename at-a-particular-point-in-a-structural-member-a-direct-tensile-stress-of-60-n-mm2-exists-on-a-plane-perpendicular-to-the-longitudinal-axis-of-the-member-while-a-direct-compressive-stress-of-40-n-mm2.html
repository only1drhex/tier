Guest Question.

At a particular point in a structural member a direct tensile stress of \(60 N / mm ^{2}\) exists on a plane perpendicular to the longitudinal axis of the member while a direct compressive stress of \(40 N / mm ^{2}\) occurs on a plane parallel to this axis; in addition shear and complementary shear stresses of \(50 N / mm ^{2}\) act on these planes. If Young’s modulus E is \(200000 N / mm ^{2}\) and Poisson’s ratio ν is 0.3 calculate the direct and shear strains on these planes and hence, using a graphical method, determine the principal strains at the point, the maximum shear strain and the angle the plane of maximum principal strain makes with the longitudinal axis of the member. Finally, calculate the principal stresses at the point.

Ans. \(\varepsilon_{x}=3.6 \times 10^{-4}, \varepsilon_{y}=-2.9 \times 10^{-4}, \gamma_{x y}=6.5 \times 10^{-4}\)

\(\varepsilon_{ I }=4.95 \times 10^{-4}, \varepsilon_{ II }=-4.25 \times 10^{-4}, \gamma_{\max }=9.2 \times 10^{-4}\), 67.5°

\(\sigma_{ I }=80.8 N / mm ^{2}, \sigma_{ II }=-60.8 N / mm ^{2}\).

Admin
Solution

From the first of Eq. (7.13)

\(\varepsilon_{x}=\frac{\sigma_{x}}{E}-\frac{v \sigma_{y}}{E} \quad \varepsilon_{y}=\frac{\sigma_{y}}{E}-\frac{v \sigma_{x}}{E} \quad \varepsilon_{z}=-\frac{v \sigma_{x}}{E}-\frac{v \sigma_{y}}{E}\) (7.13)

\(\varepsilon_{x}=\frac{60}{200000}+\frac{0.3 \times 40}{200000}=3.6 \times 10^{-4}\)

and from the second

\(\varepsilon_{y}=\frac{-40}{200000}-\frac{0.3 \times 60}{200000}=-2.9 \times 10^{-4}\)

The shear modulus, G, is obtained using Eq. (7.21)

\(G=\frac{E}{2(1+v)} \text { or } E=2 G(1+v)\) (7.21)

and is

\(G=\frac{200000}{2(1+0.3)}=76923 N / mm ^{2}\)

so that, from Eq. (7.9)

\(G=\frac{\tau}{\gamma}\) (7.9)

\(\gamma_{x y}=\frac{50}{76923}=6.6 \times 10^{-4}\)

The principal strains are now found using Mohr’s circle of strain as shown in Fig. S.14.9 and are

\(\varepsilon_{ I }=4.95 \times 10^{-4}, \quad \varepsilon_{ II }=-4.25 \times 10^{-4}, \quad \gamma_{\max }=9.2 \times 10^{-4}\)

The angle the plane of maximumprincipal strain makes with the longitudinal axis of the member is 67.5°.

The principal stresses are obtained using Eqs (14.32) and (14.33)

\(\sigma_{ I }=\frac{E}{1-v^{2}}\left(\varepsilon_{ I }+v \varepsilon_{ II }\right)\) (14.32)

\(\sigma_{ II }=\frac{E}{1-v^{2}}\left(\varepsilon_{ II }+v \varepsilon_{ I }\right)\) (14.33)

and are

\(\sigma_{ I }=\frac{200000}{1-0.3^{2}}(4.95-0.3 \times 4.25) \times 10^{-4}=80.8 N / mm ^{2}\)

 

\(\sigma_{ II }=\frac{200000}{1-0.3^{2}}(-4.25+0.3 \times 4.95) \times 10^{-4}=-60.8 N / mm ^{2}\)
