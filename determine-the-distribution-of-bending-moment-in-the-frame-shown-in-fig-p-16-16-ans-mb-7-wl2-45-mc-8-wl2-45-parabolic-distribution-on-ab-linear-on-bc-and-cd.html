Guest Question.

Determine the distribution of bending moment in the frame shown in Fig. P.16.16.
Ans. \(M_{ B }=7 w L^{2} / 45, M_{ C }=8 w L^{2} / 45\). Parabolic distribution on AB, linear on BC and CD.

Admin
Solution

Referring to Fig. S.16.16(a) and taking moments about D

\(R_{ A , V }\left(\frac{2 L}{3}\right)+\left(\frac{w L}{2}\right)\left(\frac{2 L}{3}\right)=0\)

i.e.

\(R_{ A , V }=\frac{-w L}{2}\)

Therefore

\(R_{ D , V }=\frac{w L}{2}\)

For horizontal equilibrium

\(R_{ A , H }+\frac{w L}{2}=R_{ D , H }\) (i)

The total complementary energy of the frame is (see Eq. (i) of Ex. 15.14)

\(C=\int_{0}^{L} \int_{0}^{M} d \theta d M-P_{ f } v_{ B }-\int_{0}^{L} v w d x\) Eq. (i) of Ex. 15.14

given by

\(C=\int_{L} \int_{0}^{M} d \theta d M+\int_{0}^{L} w^{\prime} \Delta d x\) (ii)

Note that in Eq. (ii) the complementary energy due to the support reactions is zero since the support displacements are all zero. Also \(w^{\prime}\) is the load intensity at any section in AB a distance x from A where the horizontal displacement is Î”. From the principle of the stationary value of the total complementary energy and selecting the horizontal reaction at A as the variable

\(\frac{\partial C}{\partial R_{ A , H }}=\int_{L} d \theta\left(\frac{\partial M}{\partial R_{ A , H }}\right)=0\)

or, from Eq. (9.19)

\(M=\frac{E I_{z}}{\delta x} \delta \theta\) (9.19)

\(\int_{L}\left(\frac{M}{E I}\right)\left(\frac{\partial M}{\partial R_{ A , H }}\right) d x=0\) (iii)

In AB

\(M=-R_{ A , H } x-\frac{w x^{3}}{6 L}, \frac{\partial M}{\partial R_{ A , H }}=-x\)

In BC

\(M=R_{ A , V } x-R_{ A , H } L-\frac{w L^{2}}{6}, \frac{\partial M}{\partial R_{ A , H }}=-L\)

In DC

\(M=-R_{ D , H } x=-\left(R_{ A , H }+\frac{w L}{2}\right) x\)

from Eq. (i). Then

\(\frac{\partial M}{\partial R_{ A , H }}=-x\)

Substituting in Eq. (iii)

\(\int_{0}^{L}\left(\frac{1}{2 E I}\right)\left(-R_{ A , H } x-\frac{w x^{3}}{6 L}\right)(-x) d x+\int_{0}^{2 L / 3}\left(\frac{1}{E I}\right)\left(-\frac{w L x}{2}-R_{ A , H } L-\frac{w L^{2}}{6}\right)(-L) d x\)

 

\(+\int_{0}^{L}\left(\frac{1}{2 E I}\right)\left(-R_{ A , H }-\frac{w L}{2}\right) x(-x) d x=0\)

from which

\(2 R_{ A , H } L^{3}+\left(\frac{29}{45}\right) w L^{4}=0\)

so that

\(R_{ A , H }=\frac{-29 w L}{90}\)

Therefore, from Eq. (i)

\(R_{ D , H }=\frac{8 w L}{45}\)

Then

\(M_{ AB }=\frac{29 w L x}{90}-\frac{w x^{3}}{6 L}\)

When x = 0, \(M_{ AB }=0\) and when x = L, \(M_{ AB }=\frac{7 w L^{2}}{45}\). Also

\(\frac{ d M_{ AB }}{ d x}=0=\frac{29 w L}{90}-\frac{3 w x^{2}}{6 L}\)

Therefore \(M_{ AB }\) is a maximum when \(x=(\sqrt{29 / 45}) L\) and \(M_{ AB }(\max )=0.173 w L^{2}\). The bending moment distributions in BC and CD are linear and are shown in Fig. S.16.16(b).
