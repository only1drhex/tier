Guest Question.

The figure on the top of page 413 shows a cantilever sheet pile driven into a granular soil where the water table is 2 m below the top of the sand. The properties of the sand are: \(\phi^{\prime}\) \(=40^{\circ}, \gamma_{m}=17.5 kN / m ^{3}, \text { and } \gamma_{\text {ot }}=19 kN / m ^{3}\). It is proposed to excavate to a depth of 6 m below the ground level.Determine the depth to which the sheet pile must be driven, using the net pressure diagram.

Admin
Solution
\(\phi^{\prime}=40^{\circ} \rightarrow K_{A}=0.217, K_{P}=4.599, \text { and } K_{P}-K_{A}=4.382\)

 

\(\gamma^{\prime}=19-9.81=9.19 kN / m ^{3}\)

 

At the water table level to the right of the sheet pile:

 

\(\sigma_{h}^{\prime}=0.217(2 \times 17.5)=7.60 kPa\)

 

At excavation level to the right of the sheet pile:

 

\(\sigma_{h}^{\prime}=0.271(2 \times 17.5+4 \times 9.19)=19.45 kPa =\sigma_{1}^{\prime}\)

 

From Equation 14.25:

 

\(z_{0}=\frac{\sigma_{1}^{\prime}}{\left(K_{P}-K_{A}\right) \gamma^{\prime}}\) (14.25)

 

\(z_{0}=\frac{\sigma_{1}^{\prime}}{\left(K_{P}-K_{A}\right) \gamma^{\prime}}=\frac{19.45}{4.382 \times 9.19}=0.48 m\)

 

\(\text { Let's compute } P \text { and } \bar{z} \text { : }\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}P=(0.5 \times 7.60 \times 2)+(7.60 \times 4)+(0.5 \times 11.85 \times 4) &+(0.5 \times 19.45 \times 0.48) \\&=7.60+30.40+23.70+4.67=66.37 kN / m\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{r}P \bar{z}=7.60(0.67+4+0.48)+30.40(2+0.48)+23.70(1.33+0.48)+4.67 \times 0.32 \\=158.92 kNm / m\end{array}\)

 

\(\therefore \bar{z}=\frac{158.92}{66.37}=2.39 m\)

 

From Equation 14.28:

 

\(\sigma_{4}^{\prime}=K_{p}\left(\gamma_{m} h_{1}+\gamma^{\prime} h_{2}\right)+\left(K_{p}-K_{A}\right) \gamma^{\prime} z_{0}\) (14.28)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{4}^{\prime}=K_{p}\left(\gamma_{m} h_{1}+\gamma^{\prime} h_{2}\right)+\left(K_{P}-K_{A}\right) \gamma^{\prime} z_{0}=4.599(17.5&\times 2+9.19 \times 4) \\&+4.382 \times 9.19 \times 0.48=349.35 kPa\end{aligned}\)

 

\(A_{1}=\frac{\sigma_{4}^{\prime}}{\gamma^{\prime}\left(K_{P}-K_{A}\right)}=\frac{349.35}{9.19 \times 4.382}=8.68 m\)

 

\(A_{2}=\frac{8 P}{\gamma^{\prime}\left(K_{P}-K_{A}\right)}=\frac{8 \times 66.37}{9.19 \times 4.382}=13.18 m ^{2}\)

 

\(A_{3}=\frac{6 P\left[2 \bar{z} \gamma^{\prime}\left(K_{P}-K_{A}\right)+\sigma_{4}^{\prime}\right]}{\gamma^{\prime 2}\left(K_{p}-K_{A}\right)^{2}}=\frac{6 \times 66.37[2 \times 2.39 \times 9.19 \times 4.382+349.35]}{9.19^{2} \times 4.382^{2}}=133.05 m ^{3}\)

 

\(A_{4}=\frac{P\left(6 \bar{z} \sigma_{4}^{\prime}+4 P\right)}{\gamma^{\prime 2}\left(K_{P}-K_{A}\right)^{2}}=\frac{66.37(6 \times 2.39 \times 349.35+4 \times 66.37)}{9.19^{2} \times 4.382^{2}}=215.89 m ^{4}\)

 

Substituting in Equation 14.24:

 

\(z_{2}^{4}+A_{1} z_{2}^{3}-A_{2} z_{2}^{2}-A_{3} z_{2}-A_{4}=0\) (14.24)

 

\(z_{2}^{4}+8.68 z_{2}^{3}-13.18 z_{2}^{2}-133.05 z_{2}-215.89=0\)

 

Solving the above equation by trial and error:

 

\(z_{2}=4.30 m\)

 

\(d=z_{2}+z_{0}=4.30+0.48=4.78 m\)

 

Increasing d by 30%, let’s provide a total depth of 12.25 m below the original ground level.
