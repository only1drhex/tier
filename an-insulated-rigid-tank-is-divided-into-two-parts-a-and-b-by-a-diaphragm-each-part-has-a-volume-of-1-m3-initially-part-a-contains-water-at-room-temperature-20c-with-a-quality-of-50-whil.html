Guest Question.

An insulated rigid tank is divided into two parts, A and B, by a diaphragm. Each part has a volume of \(1 m ^{3}\). Initially, part A contains water at room temperature, 20°C, with a quality of 50%, while part B is evacuated. The diaphragm then ruptures and the water fills the total volume. Determine the reversible work for this change of state and the irreversibility of the process.

Control mass: Water
Initial state: \(T_{1}, x_{1}\) known; state fixed.
Final state: \(V_{2}\) known.
Process: Adiabatic, no change in kinetic or potential energy.
Model: Steam tables.

There is a boundary movement for the water, but since it occurs against no resistance, no work is done. Therefore, the first law reduces to

\(m\left(u_{2}-u_{1}\right)=0\)

From Eq. 8.17 with no change in internal energy and no heat transfer,

\({ }_{1} W_{2}^{\text {rev }}=\sum\left(1-\frac{T_{0}}{T_{j}}\right){ }_{1} Q_{2 j}-\left[E_{2}-E_{1}-T_{0}\left(S_{2}-S_{1}\right)\right] (8.17)\)

\({ }_{1} W_{2}^{ rev }=T_{0}\left(S_{2}-S_{1}\right)=T_{0} m\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)\)

From Eq. 8.18

\(=T_{0}\left(S_{2}-S_{1}\right)-\sum \frac{T_{0}}{T_{j}}{ }_{1} Q_{2 j} (8.18)\)

\({ }_{1} I_{2}={ }_{1} W_{2}^{ rev }-{ }_{1} W_{2}={ }_{1} W_{2}^{ rev }\)

Admin
Solution

From the steam tables at state 1,

\(u_{1}=1243.5 kJ / kg \quad v_{1}=28.895 m ^{3} / kg \quad s_{1}=4.4819 kJ / kg K\)

Therefore,

\(v_{2}=V_{2} / m=2 \times v_{1}=57.79 m ^{3} / kg \quad u_{2}=u_{1}=1243.5 kJ / kg\)

These two independent properties, \(v_{1}\) and \(u_{2}\), fix state 2. The final temperature \(T_{1}\) must be found by trial and error in the steam tables.

\(\text { For } \quad T_{2}=5^{\circ} C \quad \text { and } \quad v_{2} \Rightarrow x=0.3928, \quad u=948.5 kJ / kg\)

\(\text { For } \quad T_{2}=10^{\circ} C \quad \text { and } \quad v_{2} \Rightarrow x=0.5433, \quad u=1317 kJ / kg\)

so the final interpolation in u gives a temperature of 9°C. If the software is used, the final state is interpolated to be

\(T_{2}=9.1^{\circ} C \quad x_{2}=0.513 \quad s_{2}=4.644 kJ / kg K\)

with the given u and v. Since the actual work is zero, we have

\({ }_{1} I_{2}={ }_{1} W_{2}^{ rev }=T_{0}\left(V_{1} / v_{1}\right)\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)\)

\(=293.2(1 / 28.895)(4.644-4.4819)=1.645 kJ\)
