Guest Question.

Units Conversion

Part 1. Express \(65 \mathrm{mph}\) in (a) \(\mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s}\), and (b) \(\mathrm{m} / \mathrm{s}\).

Part 2. The density of \(35^{\circ}\) API crude oil is \(53.1 \mathrm{lb}_{\mathrm{m}} / \mathrm{ft}^{3}\) at \(68^{\circ} \mathrm{F}\), and its viscosity is \(32.8 \mathrm{lb}_{\mathrm{m}} / \mathrm{ft} \mathrm{hr}\). What are its density, viscosity, and kinematic viscosity in SI units?

Admin
Solution

Part 1. The solution is obtained by employing conversion factors taken from Table 1.11:

Table 1.11 Commonly Used Conversion Factors
Area 1 \(mile^{2} \) = 640 acres
1 acre = 0.4047 ha
Energy 1 BTU = 1,055 J
1 cal = 4.184 J
1 J = 0.7376 ft \(lb_{f}\)
1 erg = 1 dyne cm
Force 1 \(lb_{f}\) = 4.448 N
1 N = 0.2248 \(lb_{f}\)
Length 1 ft = 0.3048 m
1 m = 3.281 ft
1 mile = 5,280 ft
Mass 1 \(lb_{m}\) = 0.4536 kg
1 kg = 2.205 \(lb_{m}\)
Power 1 HP = 550 ft\(lb_{f}\)/s
1 kW = 737.6 ft\(lb_{f}\)/s
Pressure 1 atm = 14.696\(lb_{f} /in^{2} \)
1 atm = 1.0133 bar
1 atm = 1.0133 × \(10^{5}\) Pa
Time 1 day = 24 hr
1 hr = 60 min
1 min = 60 s
Viscosity 1 cP = 2.419 \(lb_{m}\)/ft hr
1 cP = 0.001 kg/m s
1 cP = 0.000672 \(lb_{m}\)/ft s
1 \(lb_{f} s /ft^{2} \) = 4.788 × \(10^{4}\) cP
Volume 1 \(ft^{3} \) = 7.481 U.S. gal
1 U.S. gal = 3.785 l
1 \(m^{3} \) = 264.2 U.S. gal

(a) \(65 \frac{\mathrm{mile}}{\mathrm{hr}} \times \frac{1}{3,600} \frac{\mathrm{hr}}{\mathrm{s}} \times 5,280 \frac{\mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{mile}}=95.33 \frac{\mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{s}}\).

(b) \(95.33 \frac{\mathrm{ft}}{\mathrm{s}} \times 0.3048 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{ft}}=29.06 \frac{\mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{s}}\).

Part 2.

\(\begin{aligned}\rho &=53.1 \frac{\mathrm{lb}_{\mathrm{m}}}{\mathrm{ft}^{3}} \times 0.4536 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{lb}_{\mathrm{m}}} \times \frac{1}{0.3048^{3}} \frac{\mathrm{ft}^{3}}{\mathrm{~m}^{3}}=851 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}}. \\ \mu &=32.8 \frac{\mathrm{lb}_{\mathrm{m}}}{\mathrm{ft} \mathrm{hr}} \times \frac{1}{2.419} \frac{\text { centipoise }}{\mathrm{lb}_{\mathrm{m}} / \mathrm{ft} \mathrm{hr}} \times 0.01 \frac{\text { poise }}{\text { centipoise }}=0.136 \text { poise. }\end{aligned}\)

Or, converting to SI units, noting that \(\mathrm{P}\) is the symbol for poise, and evaluating \(\nu\) :

\(\begin{array}{l}\mu=0.136 \mathrm{P} \times 0.1 \frac{\mathrm{kg} / \mathrm{m} \mathrm{s}}{\mathrm{P}}=0.0136 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m} \mathrm{s}}. \\ \nu=\frac{\mu}{\rho}=\frac{0.0136 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m} \mathrm{s}}{851 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}}=1.60 \times 10^{-5} \frac{\mathrm{m}^{2}}{\mathrm{~s}} \quad(=0.160 \mathrm{St}).\end{array}\)
