Guest Question.

Design a square pile group to carry 400 kN in clay with an unconfined compression strength of 60 kN/\(m ^{2}\). The piles are 30 cm diameter and 6 m long. Adhesion factor may be taken as 0.6.

Admin
Solution

Cohesion, \(c=\frac{60}{2}=30 kN / m ^{2}\)

Frictional resistance of one pile
= α . c . πd . L
= 0.6 × 30 × π × 0.3 × 6 = 101.8 kN

Safe load per pile \(=\frac{101.8}{3} \approx 34 kN\) (with a factor of safety of 3)

Approximate number of piles required \(=\frac{400}{34} \approx 12\)

Let us try a square 16-pile group with centre-to-centre spacing of 60 cm.

Efficiency \(\eta_{g}=1-\frac{\phi}{90^{\circ}}\left[\frac{m(n-1)+n(m-1)}{m n}\right]\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\phi &=\tan ^{-1}(d / s)=\tan ^{-1}(d / 2 d)=26.565 \\&=1-\frac{26.565}{90}\left[\frac{4 \times 3+4 \times 3}{4 \times 4}\right]=0.56\end{aligned}\)

Safe load on the pile group = 0.56 × 16 × 34 ≈ 305 kN with a factor of safety of 3.
It will be \(400 kN \text { with } \eta=\frac{305 \times 3}{400}=2.3\)

Check for block failure:
Frictional resistance of the block
= 2.1 × 4 × 6 × 30
= 1512 kN
Safe load with \(\eta=2.5 \text { is } \frac{1512}{2.5} \approx 605 kN\)

Hence, the safe load may be taken as 400 kN for the group, although the factor of safety falls short of 2.5 slightly.
