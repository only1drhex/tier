Guest Question.

Solve \(\ddot{y}-6 \dot{y}+25 y=64 e^{-t}\) .

Admin
Solution

From ,

\(y_{h}=c_{1} e^{3 t} \cos 4 t+c_{2} e^{3 t} \sin 4 t\)

Here \(\phi(t)\) has the form displayed in Case 2 with the independent variable t replacing x, \(k=64 \text { and } \alpha=-1\). Using (11.2), with t replacing x, we assume that

\(y_{p}=A e^{-t}\) (1)

Consequently, \(\dot{y}_{p}=-A e^{-i} \text { and } \ddot{y}_{p}=A e^{-t}\) . Substituting these results into the differential equation, we obtain

\(A e^{-t}-6\left(-A e^{-t}\right)+25\left(A e^{-t}\right)=64 e^{-t}\)

or, equivalently, \(32 A e^{-t}=64 e^{-t}\). It follows that 32A = 64 or A = 2, so that (1) becomes \(y_{p}=2 e^{-t}\). The general solution is

\(y=y_{h}+y_{p}=c_{1} e^{3 t} \cos 4 t+c_{2} e^{3 t} \sin 4 t+2 e^{-t}\)
