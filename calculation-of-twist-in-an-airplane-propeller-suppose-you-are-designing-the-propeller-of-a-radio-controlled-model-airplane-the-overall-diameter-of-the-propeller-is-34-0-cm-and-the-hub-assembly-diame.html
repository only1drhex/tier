Calculation of Twist in an Airplane Propeller

Suppose you are designing the propeller of a radio-controlled model airplane. The overall diameter of the propeller is 34.0 cm, and the hub assembly diameter is 5.5 cm (Fig. 14‚Äì53). The propeller rotates at 1700 rpm, and the airfoil chosen for the propeller cross section achieves its maximum efficiency at an angle of attack of 14¬∞. When the airplane flies at 30 mi/h (13.4 m/s), calculate the blade pitch angle from the root to the tip of the blade such that ùõº = 14¬∞ everywhere along the propeller blade.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION We are to calculate blade pitch angle ùúÉ from the root to the tip of the propeller such that the angle of attack is ùõº = 14¬∞ at every radius along the propeller blade.

Assumptions 1 The air at these low speeds is incompressible. 2 We neglect the secondary effects of swirl and acceleration of the air as it approaches the propeller; i.e., the magnitude of \(\vec{V}_{\text {in }}\) is approximated to be equal to the speed of the aircraft. 3 The airplane flies level, such that the propeller axis is parallel to the incoming air velocity.

Analysis The velocity of the air relative to the blade is approximated to first order at any radius by using Eq. 14‚Äì27. A sketch of the velocity vectors at some arbitrary radius r is shown in Fig. 14‚Äì54. From the geometry we see that

 

\(\vec{V}_{\text {relative }} \cong \vec{V}_{\text {in }}-\vec{V}_{\text {blade }}\) (Eq. 14-27)

 

Pitch angle at arbitrary radius r: ùúÉ = ùõº + ùúô (1)

 

and

 

\(\phi=\arctan \frac{\left|\vec{V}_{\text {in }}\right|}{\left|\vec{V}_{\text {blade }}\right|}=\arctan \frac{\left|\vec{V}_{\text {in }}\right|}{\omega r}\) (2)

 

where we have also used Eq. 14‚Äì26 for the blade speed at radius r. At the root \(\left(r=D_{\text {hub }} / 2=2.75 cm \right) \text {, Eq. } 2 \text { becomes }\)

 

\(u_{\theta}=\omega r\) (Eq. 14-26)

 

\(\theta=\alpha+\phi=14^{\circ}+\arctan \left[\frac{13.4 m / s }{(1700 rot / min )(0.0275 m )}\left(\frac{1 rot }{2 \pi rad }\right)\left(\frac{60 s }{ min }\right)\right]= 8 3 . 9 ^ { \circ }\)

 

Similarly, the pitch angle at the tip \(\left(r=D_{\text {propeller }} / 2=17.0 cm \right) \text { is }\)

 

\(\theta=\alpha+\phi=14^{\circ}+\arctan \left[\frac{13.4 m / s }{(1700 \operatorname{rot} / min )(0.17 m )}\left(\frac{1 \text { rot }}{2 \pi rad }\right)\left(\frac{60 s }{\min }\right)\right]= 3 7 . 9 ^ { \circ }\)

 

At radii between the root and the tip, Eqs. 1 and 2 are used to calculate ùúÉ as a function of r. Results are plotted in Fig. 14‚Äì55.

Discussion The pitch angle is not linear because of the arctangent function in Eq. 2.
