Guest Question.

A thin rectangular plate \(a \times b\) is simply supported along its edges and carries a uniformly distributed load of intensity \(q_{0}\). Determine the deflected form of the plate and the distribution of bending moment.

Admin
Solution

Since \(q(x, y)=q_{0}\), we find from Eq. (7.29) that,

 

\(a_{m^{\prime} n^{\prime}}=\frac{4}{a b} \int_{0}^{a} \int_{0}^{b} q(x, y) \sin \frac{m^{\prime} \pi x}{a} \sin \frac{n^{\prime} \pi y}{b} d x d y\) (7.29)

 

\(a_{m n}=\frac{4 q_{0}}{a b} \int_{0}^{a} \int_{0}^{b} \sin \frac{m \pi x}{a} \sin \frac{n \pi y}{b} d x d y=\frac{16 q_{0}}{\pi^{2} m n}\)

 

where m and n are odd integers. For m or n even, \(a_{m n}=0\). Hence, from Eq. (7.30),

 

\(w=\frac{1}{\pi^{4} D} \sum\limits_{m=1}^{\infty} \sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{a_{m n}}{\left[\left(m^{2} / a^{2}\right)+\left(n^{2} / b^{2}\right)\right]^{2}} \sin \frac{m \pi x}{a} \sin \frac{n \pi y}{b}\) (7.30)

 

\(w=\frac{16 q_{0}}{\pi^{6} D} \sum\limits_{m=1,3,5}^{\infty} \sum\limits_{n=1,3,5}^{\infty} \frac{\sin (m \pi x / a) \sin (n \pi y / b)}{m n\left[\left(m^{2} / a^{2}\right)+\left(n^{2} / b^{2}\right)\right]^{2}}\) (i)

 

The maximum deflection occurs at the center of the plate, where \(x=a / 2, y=b / 2\). Thus,

 

\(w_{\max }=\frac{16 q_{0}}{\pi^{6} D} \sum\limits_{m=1,3,5}^{\infty} \sum\limits_{n=1,3,5}^{\infty} \frac{\sin (m \pi / 2) \sin (n \pi / 2)}{m n\left[\left(m^{2} / a^{2}\right)+\left(n^{2} / b^{2}\right)\right]^{2}}\) (ii)

 

This series is found to converge rapidly, the first few terms giving a satisfactory answer. For a square plate, taking \(v=0.3\), summation of the first four terms of the series gives

 

\(w_{\max }=0.0443 q_{0} \frac{a^{4}}{E t^{3}}\)

 

Substitution for w from Eq. (i) into the expressions for bending moment, Eqs. (7.7) and (7.8), yields

 

\(M_{x}=-D\left(\frac{\partial^{2} w}{\partial x^{2}}+v \frac{\partial^{2} w}{\partial y^{2}}\right)\) (7.7)

 

\(M_{y}=-D\left(\frac{\partial^{2} w}{\partial y^{2}}+v \frac{\partial^{2} w}{\partial x^{2}}\right)\) (7.8)

 

\(M_{x}=\frac{16 q_{0}}{\pi^{4}} \sum\limits_{m=1,3,5}^{\infty} \sum\limits_{n=1,3,5}^{\infty} \frac{\left[\left(m^{2} / a^{2}\right)+v\left(n^{2} / b^{2}\right)\right]}{m n\left[\left(m^{2} / a^{2}\right)+\left(n^{2} / b^{2}\right)\right]^{2}} \sin \frac{m \pi x}{a} \sin \frac{n \pi y}{b}\) (iii)

 

\(M_{y}=\frac{16 q_{0}}{\pi^{4}} \sum\limits_{m=1,3,5}^{\infty} \sum\limits_{n=1,3,5}^{\infty} \frac{\left[v\left(m^{2} / a^{2}\right)+\left(n^{2} / b^{2}\right)\right]}{m n\left[\left(m^{2} / a^{2}\right)+\left(n^{2} / b^{2}\right)\right]^{2}} \sin \frac{m \pi x}{a} \sin \frac{n \pi y}{b}\) (iv)

 

Maximum values occur at the center of the plate. For a square plate \(a=b\) and the first five terms give

 

\(M_{x, \max }=M_{y, \max }=0.0479 q_{0} a^{2}\)

 

Comparing Eqs. (7.3) with Eqs. (7.5) and (7.6), we observe that

 

\(\left.\begin{array}{l}\sigma_{x}=\frac{E z}{1-v^{2}}\left(\frac{1}{\rho_{x}}+\frac{v}{\rho_{y}}\right) \\\sigma_{y}=\frac{E z}{1-v^{2}}\left(\frac{1}{\rho_{y}}+\frac{v}{\rho_{x}}\right)\end{array}\right\}\) (7.3)

 

\(M_{x}=D\left(\frac{1}{\rho_{x}}+\frac{v}{\rho_{y}}\right)\) (7.5)

 

\(M_{y}=D\left(\frac{1}{\rho_{y}}+\frac{v}{\rho_{x}}\right)\) (7.6)

 

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{12 M_{x} z}{t^{3}}, \quad \sigma_{y}=\frac{12 M_{y} z}{t^{3}}\)

 

Again the maximum values of these stresses occur at the center of the plate at \(z=\pm t / 2\), so that

 

\(\sigma_{x, \max }=\frac{6 M_{x}}{t^{2}}, \quad \sigma_{y, \max }=\frac{6 M_{y}}{t^{2}}\)

 

For the square plate,

 

\(\sigma_{x, \max }=\sigma_{y, \max }=0.287 q_{0} \frac{a^{2}}{t^{2}}\)

 

The twisting moment and shear stress distributions follow in a similar manner.
