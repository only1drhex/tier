Guest Question.

Consider again the nozzle of , assuming now that steam exists in its ideal-gas state and constant heat capacity. Calculate:
(a) The critical pressure ratio and the velocity at the throat.
(b) The discharge pressure for a Mach number of 2.0 at the nozzle exhaust.

Admin
Solution

(a) The ratio of specific heats for steam is about 1.3. Substituting in Eq. (7.12),

\( \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}=\left(\frac{2}{\gamma +1} \right)^{\gamma /(\gamma -1)}\) (7.12)

 

\( \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}=\left(\frac{2}{1.3+1} \right)^{1.3 /(1.3-1)}=0.55\)

The velocity at the throat, equal to the speed of sound, is found from Eq. (7.11), which contains the product \(P_{1}V_{1}.\)For steam in its ideal-gas state:

\( u^{2}_{2}-u_{1}^{2}=-2\int_{P_{1}}^{P_{2}}{V dP}=\frac{2\gamma P_{1}V_{1}}{\gamma -1}\left[1-\left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \right)^{(\gamma -1)/\gamma } \right]\) (7.11)

 

\( P_{1}V_{1}=\frac{RT_{1}}{M}=\frac{(8.314)(573.15)}{0.01802}=264,511 m^{2}\cdot s^{-2}\)

In this equation R/M has the units:

\( \frac{J}{kg\cdot K}=\frac{N\cdot m}{kg\cdot K}=\frac{kg\cdot m\cdot s^{-2}m}{kg\cdot K}=\frac{m^{2}\cdot s^{-2}}{K}\)

Thus RT/M, and hence \(P_{1}V_{1}\), is in \(m^{2}\cdot s^{−2},\) the units of velocity squared. Substitu-tion in Eq. (7.11) gives:

\( u^{2}_{throat}=(30)^{2}+\frac{(2)(1.3)(264,511)}{1.3-1}[1-(0.55)^{(1.3-1)/1.3}]=296,322\)
\( u_{throat}=544.35m\cdot s^{-1}\)

This result is in good agreement with the value obtained in , because the behavior of steam at these conditions closely approximates the ideal-gas state.
(b) For a Mach number of 2.0 (based on the velocity of sound at the nozzle throat) the discharge velocity is:

\( 2u_{throat} = (2) (544.35) = 1088.7 m\cdot s^{−1}\)

Substitution of this value in Eq. (7.11) allows calculation of the pressure ratio:

\((1088.7)^{2}-(30)^{2}=\frac{(2)(1.3)(264,511)}{1.3-1}\left[1-\left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \right)^{(1.3-1)/1.3} \right]\)

 

\( \left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \right)^{(1.3-1)/1.3}=0.4834 and P_{2}=(0.0428)(700)=30.0kPa\)
