Guest Question.

A pumped well and two observation wells have been installed through a fine-to-medium sand as shown in Figure 8.23. A pump has been discharging water from the pumped well for a sufficient time to achieve steady-state conditions. The water levels in observation wells A and B were then observed to be 20 and 35 ft below the ground surface, respectively. Compute the hydraulic conductivity of the aquifer.

Admin
Solution

This is an unconfined aquifer, so use Equation 8.33 \(k=\frac{Q\ln \left(r_{1}/r_{2}\right) }{\pi \left(h^{2}_{1} – h^{2}_{2}\right) }\).

\(Q=\left(150 gal/min\right) \left\lgroup\frac{1 ft^{3}}{7.48 gal} \right\rgroup \left\lgroup\frac{1 min}{60 s} \right\rgroup =0.33 ft^{3}/s\)

 

\(h_{1} = 90 – 20 = 70 ft \)

 

\(h_{2} = 90 – 35 = 55 ft \)

 

\(k=\frac{Q\ln \left(r_{1}/r_{2}\right) }{\pi \left(h^{2}_{1} – h^{2}_{2}\right) } =\frac{0.33 ft^{3}/s\ln \left(170 ft/20 ft\right) }{\pi \left[\left(70 ft\right) ^{2} – \left(55 ft\right) ^{2}\right] } =1\times 10^{-4} ft/s\)

This answer seems reasonable, per Table 7.1.
