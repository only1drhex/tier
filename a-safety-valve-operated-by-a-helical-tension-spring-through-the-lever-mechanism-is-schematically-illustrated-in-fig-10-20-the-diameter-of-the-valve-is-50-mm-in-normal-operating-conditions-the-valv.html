Guest Question.

A safety valve operated by a helical tension spring through the lever mechanism is schematically illustrated in Fig. 10.20. The diameter of the valve is 50 mm. In normal operating conditions, the valve is closed and the pressure inside the chamber is 0.5 N/mm². The valve is opened when the pressure inside the chamber increases to 0.6 N/mm². The maximum lift of the valve is 5 mm. The spring index can be taken as 8. The spring is made of patented and cold-drawn steel wire with ultimate tensile strength of 1200 N/mm² and modulus of rigidity of 81 370 N/mm². The permissible shear stress for the spring wire can be taken as 30% of the ultimate tensile strength. Design the spring and calculate:
(i) wire diameter;
(ii) mean coil diameter; and
(iii) number of active coils.

Admin
Solution

Step I Wire diameter
Suppose, \( P_{v} \text { and } P_{s} \) are denoted as the forces acting on the valve and the spring respectively. Taking moment of these forces about the pivot,

\( P_{v} \times 75=P_{s} \times 150 \quad \text { or } \quad P_{s}=\frac{1}{2} P_{v} \) (a)
When the valve is closed,

\( P_{v}=\frac{\pi}{4}(50)^{2}(0.5)=981.75 N \).

From Eq. (a), the force acting on the spring is
(981.75/2) or 490.87 N
When the valve is open,

\( P_{v}=\frac{\pi}{4}(50)^{2}(0.6)=1178.10 N \).

From Eq. (a), the force acting on the spring is
(1178.10/2) or 589.05 N.
The valve is lifted through 5 mm and corresponding extension of the spring is given by,

\( \delta=5 \times \frac{150}{75}=10 mm \).

Therefore, the stiffness of the spring is given by,

\( k=\frac{589.05-490.87}{10}=9.82 N / mm \).

The maximum force acting on the spring is 589.05 N.
P = 589.05 N
The permissible shear stress for the spring wire is given by,

\( \tau=0.3 S_{u t}=0.3(1200)=360 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( K=\frac{4 C-1}{4 C-4}+\frac{0.615}{C}=\frac{4(8)-1}{4(8)-4}+\frac{0.615}{8}=1.184 \).

From Eq. (10.13),

\( \tau=K\left(\frac{8 P C}{\pi d^{2}}\right) \) (10.13).

\( \tau=K\left(\frac{8 P C}{\pi d^{2}}\right) \text { or } 360=1.184\left\{\frac{8(589.05)(8)}{\pi d^{2}}\right\} \).

∴ d = 6.28 or 7 mm (i)
Step II Mean coil diameter
D = Cd = 8(7) = 56 mm (ii)
Step III Number of active coils
From Eq. (10.9),

\( k=\frac{G d^{4}}{8 D^{3} N} \) (10.9).

\( k=\frac{G d^{4}}{8 D^{3} N} \quad \text { or } \quad 9.82=\frac{(81370)(7)^{4}}{8(56)^{3} N} \).

∴ N = 14.16 or 15 coils (iii).
