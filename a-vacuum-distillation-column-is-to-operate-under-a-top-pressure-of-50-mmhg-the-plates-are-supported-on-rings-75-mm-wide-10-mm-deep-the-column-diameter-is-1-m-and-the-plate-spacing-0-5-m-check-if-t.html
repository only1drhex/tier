Guest Question.

A vacuum distillation column is to operate under a top pressure of 50 mmHg. The plates are supported on rings 75 mm wide, 10 mm deep. The column diameter is 1 m and the plate spacing 0.5 m. Check if the support rings will act as effective stiffening rings. The material of construction is carbon steel and the maximum operating temperature \(50^{\circ} C\). If the vessel thickness is 10 mm, check if this is sufficient.

Admin
Solution

Take the design pressure as 1 bar external

 

\(\text { From equation } 13.55 \text { the load on each ring }=0.5 \times 10^{5} N / m\)

 

\(F_{r}=P_{e} L_{s}\) (13.55)

 

Taking E for steel at \(50^{\circ} C \text { as } 200,000 N / mm ^{2}=2 \times 10^{11} N / m ^{2}\), and using a factor of safety of 6, the second moment of area of the ring to avoid buckling is given by: equation 13.57

 

\(P_{e} L_{s} \ngtr \frac{24 E I_{r}}{D_{r}^{3}} \div(\text { factor of safety })\) (13.57)

 

\(0.5 \times 10^{5}=\frac{24 \times 2 \times 10^{11} \times I_{r}}{1^{3} \times 6}\)

 

\(I_{r}=6.25 \times 10^{-8} m ^{4}\)

 

For a rectangular section, the second moment of area is given by

 

\(I=\frac{\text { breadth } \times \text { depth }^{3}}{12}\)

 

\(\text { so } I_{r} \text { for the support rings }=\frac{10 \times(75)^{3} \times 10^{-12}}{12}\)

 

\(=3.5 \times 10^{-7} m ^{4}\)

 

and the support ring is of an adequate size to be considered as a stiffening ring.

 

\(\frac{L^{\prime}}{D_{0}}=\frac{0.5}{1}=0.5\)

 

\(\frac{D_{0}}{t}=\frac{1000}{10}=100\)

 

\(\text { From Figure } 13.16 K_{c}=75\)

 

\(\text { From equation } 13.52\)

 

\(P_{c}=K_{c} E\left(\frac{t}{D_{0}}\right)^{3}\) (13.52)

 

\(P_{c}=75 \times 2 \times 10^{11}\left(\frac{1}{100}\right)^{3}=15 \times 10^{6} N / m ^{2}\)

 

which is well above the maximum design pressure of \(10^{5} N / m ^{2}\).
