Guest Question.

Predict the formula of the compound formed from the combination of ions of aluminum and sulfur.

Admin
Solution

Determine the charges of the ions from the periodic table. Use subscripts to create a neutral compound.
Step 1. Aluminum is in Group IIIA (or 13); it has three valence electrons. Loss of these electrons produces \(\mathrm{Al}^{3+}\).

\(\cdot\dot{\underset{\cdot}{\mathrm{A}}} \mathrm{l} \longrightarrow \mathrm{Al}^{3+}+3 \mathrm{e}^{-}\)

Step 2. Sulfur is in Group VIA (or 16); it has six valence electrons. A gain of two electrons (to create a stable octet) produces \(S^{2-}\).

\(\cdot \ddot{\underset{\ddot{}}{\mathrm{S}}} \text { . }+2 \mathrm{e}^{-} \longrightarrow[:\ddot{\underset{\ddot{}}{\mathrm{S}}}:]^{2-}\)

Step 3. In order to combine \(\mathrm{Al}^{3+}\) and \(\mathrm{S}^{2-}\) to yield a unit charge of zero, the least common multiple of 3 and 2, which is 6 , is used. Each charge is multiplied by a different factor to achieve a charge of \(6^{+}\)and \(6^{-}\).
For \(\mathrm{Al}: 2 \mathrm{Al}^{3+}=6^{+}\)charge
For \(\mathrm{S}: 3 \mathrm{~S}^{2-}=6^{-}\)charge

Step 4. The subscript 2 is used to indicate that the formula unit contains two aluminum ions. The subscript 3 is used to indicate that the formula unit contains three sulfide ions. Thus, the formula of the compound is \(\mathrm{Al}_{2} \mathrm{~S}_{3}\).

Helpful Hint: Electrons are transferred when this neutral ionic compound is formed. When \(2 \mathrm{Al}\) atoms form \(2 \mathrm{Al}^{3+}\) cations, there are a total of \(\left(2 \times 3 \mathrm{e}^{-}\right)\)six electrons produced. Since each \(\mathrm{S}\) requires two electrons to form each \(\mathrm{S}^{2-}, 3 \mathrm{~S}\) atoms require a total of \(\left(3 \times 2 \mathrm{e}^{-}\right)\)six electrons.
