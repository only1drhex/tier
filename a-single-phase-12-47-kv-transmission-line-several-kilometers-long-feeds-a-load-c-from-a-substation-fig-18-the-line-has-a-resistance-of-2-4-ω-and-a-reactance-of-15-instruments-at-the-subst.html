Guest Question.

A single-phase 12.47 kV transmission line several kilometers long feeds a load C from a substation (Fig. 18). The line has a resistance of 2.4 Ω and a reactance of 15 . Instruments at the substation indicate that the active and reactive power inputs to the line are 3 MW and 2 Mvar, respectively.

Calculate

a. The line current and its phase angle with respect to the line voltage at the substation
b. The active power absorbed by the load
c. The reactive power absorbed by the load
d. The line voltage at the load
e. The phase angle between the voltage at the load and that at the substation

Admin
Solution

a. Apparent power delivered to the line:

 

\(\begin{aligned}S &=\sqrt{P^{2}+Q^{2}}=\sqrt{3^{2}+2^{2}} \\&=3.60 MVA\end{aligned}\)

 

Line current:

 

\(I=\frac{S}{E}=\frac{3600000 VA }{12470 V }=289 A\)

 

Power factor at the substation:

 

\(F P=\frac{P}{S}=\frac{3 MW }{3.6 MVA }=0.833\)

 

Phase angle between the voltage and current at the substation:

 

\(\theta=\arccos 0.833=33.6^{\circ}\)

 

b. Active power dissipated in the line:

 

\(\begin{aligned}P_{ L } &=R I^{2}=2.4 \times 289^{2} \\&=0.2 \times 10^{6}=0.2 MW\end{aligned}\)

 

Active power absorbed by the load:

 

\(\begin{aligned}P_{ C } &=P_{ sub }-P_{ L }=3 MW -0.2 MW \\&=2.8 MW\end{aligned}\)

 

c. Reactive power absorbed by the line:

 

\(Q_{ L }=X_{ L } I^{2}=15 \times 289^{2}=1.25 \times 10^{6}=1.25 Mvar\)

 

Reactive power absorbed by the load:

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{ C } &=Q_{\text {sub }}-Q_{ L }=2 Mvar -1.25 Mvar \\&=0.75 Mvar\end{aligned}\)

 

d. Apparent power at the load:

 

\(\begin{aligned}S_{ C } &=\sqrt{P_{ C }^{2}+Q_{ C }^{2}}=\sqrt{2.8^{2}+0.75^{2}} \\&=2.90 MVA\end{aligned}\)

 

Voltage at the load end of the line:

 

\(E_{ C }=\frac{S_{ C }}{I}=\frac{2.90 MVA }{289 A }=10.03 kV\)

 

Power factor at the load end of the line:

 

\(F P=\frac{P_{ C }}{S_{ C }}=\frac{2.8 MW }{2.90 MVA }=0.965 \text { or } 96.5 \%\)

 

Phase angle between the voltage and current at the load:

 

\(\theta_{ C }=\arccos 0.965=15.2^{\circ}\)

 

It follows that the phase angle between the voltage at the substation and that at the load is (33.6° – 15.2°) = 18.4°.
Fig. 18 summarizes the results of this analysis.
We could have found the same values using vector algebra. However, on account of its simplicity, the power method of solving this problem is very appealing.
