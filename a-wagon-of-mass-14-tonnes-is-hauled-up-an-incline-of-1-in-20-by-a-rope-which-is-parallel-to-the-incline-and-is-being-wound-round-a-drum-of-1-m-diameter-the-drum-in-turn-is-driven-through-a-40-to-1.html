Guest Question.

A wagon of mass 14 tonnes is hauled up an incline of 1 in 20 by a rope which is parallel to the incline and is being wound round a drum of 1 m diameter. The drum, in turn, is driven through a 40 to 1 reduction gear by an electric motor. The frictional resistance to the movement of the wagon is 1.2 kN, and the efficiency of the gear drive is 85 per cent. The bearing friction at the drum and motor shafts may be neglected. The rotating parts of the drum have a mass of 1.25 tonnes with a radius of gyration of 450 mm and the rotating parts on the armature shaft have a mass of 110 kg with a radius of gyration of 125 mm.
At a certain instant the wagon is moving up the slope with a velocity of 1.8 m/s and an acceleration of 0.1 m/\(s^{2}\) . Find the torque on the motor shaft and the power being developed.

Admin
Solution

Given : m = 14 t = 14 000 kg ; Slope = 1 in 20 ; d = 1m or r = 0.5 m ; F = 1.2 kN = 1200 N ; \(\eta=85 \%=0.85\) ; \(m_{1}=1.25 t =1250 kg ; k_{1}=450 mm =0.45 m ; m_{2}=110 kg\) ; \(k_{2}\) = 125 mm = 0.125 m; v = 1.8 m/s ; a = 0.1 m/\(s^{2}\)

Torque on the motor shaft

We know that tension in the rope,

\(P_{1}\) = Forces opposing the motion as shown in Fig. 3.6.

= Component of the weight down the slope

+ Inertia force + Frictional resistance

\(=m \cdot g \cdot \times \frac{1}{20}+m \cdot a+F\) \(=\frac{14000 \times 9.81}{20}+14000 \times 0.1+1200=9467 N\)

\(\therefore\) Torque on the drum shaft to accelerate load,

\(T_{1}=P_{1} \cdot r=9467 \times 0.5=4733.5 N – m\)

We know that mass moment of inertia of the drum,

\(I_{1}=m_{1}\left(k_{1}\right)^{2}=1250(0.45)^{2}=253 kg – m ^{2}\)

and angular acceleration of the drum,

\(\alpha=a / r=0.1 / 0.5=0.2 rad / s\)

\(\therefore\) Torque on the drum to accelerate drum shaft,

\(T_{2}=I_{1} \cdot \alpha_{1}=253 \times 0.2=50.6 N – m\)

Since the drum is driven through a 40 to 1 reduction gear and the efficiency of the gear drive is 85%, therefore

Torque on the armature to accelerate drum and load,

\(T_{3}=\left(T_{1}+T_{2}\right) \frac{1}{40} \times \frac{1}{0.85}=(4733.5+50.6) \frac{1}{40} \times \frac{1}{0.85}=140.7 N – m\)

We know that mass moment of inertia of the armature,

\(I_{2}=m_{2}\left(k_{2}\right)^{2}=110(0.125)^{2}=1.72 kg – m ^{2}\)

and angular acceleration of the armature,

\(\alpha_{2}=\frac{a}{r} \times 40=\frac{0.1}{0.5} \times 40=8 rad / s ^{2}\) … (\(\therefore\) Armature rotates 40 times that of drum)

\(\therefore\) Torque on the armature to accelerate armature shaft,

\(T_{4}=I_{2} \cdot \alpha_{2}=1.72 \times 8=13.76 N – m\)

and torque on the motor shaft

\(T=T_{3}+T_{4}=140.7+13.76=154.46 N – m\)

Power developed by the motor

We know that angular speed of the motor,

\(\omega=\frac{v}{r} \times 40=\frac{1.8}{0.5} \times 40=144 rad / s\)

\(\therefore\) Power developed by the motor

\(=T \cdot \omega=\) 154.46 Ã— 144 = 22 240 W = 22.24 kW
