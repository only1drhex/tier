Guest Question.

In , if the locked-rotor power factor of the motor alone is 0.35, calculate the value of the series resistors and the power they dissipate.

Admin
Solution

We will solve this problem by considering active and reactive powers and using the power triangle method. The apparent power drawn by the motor at reduced voltage is

 

\(S_{ m }=471 kVA \text { (from Example 2) }\)

 

The corresponding apparent power drawn by the line is

 

\(S_{ L }=724 kVA \text { (from Example 2) }\)

 

The active power drawn by the motor is

 

\(\begin{aligned}P_{ m } &=S_{ m } \cos \theta=471 \times 0.35 \\&=165 kW\end{aligned}\)

 

The reactive power absorbed by the motor is

 

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{ m } &=\sqrt{S_{ m }^{2}-P_{ m }^{2}}=\sqrt{471^{2}-165^{2}} \\&=441 kvar\end{aligned}\)

 

The resistors can only absorb active power in the circuit. Consequently, the reactive power supplied by the line must be equal to that absorbed by the motor:

 

\(Q_{ L }=441 kvar\)

 

The active power supplied by the line is

 

\(\begin{aligned}P_{ L } &=\sqrt{S_{ L }^{2}-Q_{ L }^{2}}=\sqrt{724^{2}-441^{2}} \\&=574 kW\end{aligned}\)

 

The active power absorbed by the three resistors is

 

\(\begin{aligned}P_{ R } &=P_{ L }-P_{ m } \\&=574-165 \\&=409 kW\end{aligned}\)

 

The active power per resistor is

 

\(P=P_{ R } / 3=409 / 3=136 kW\)

 

The current in each resistor is

 

\(I=910 A (\text { from Example } 2)\)

 

The value of each resistor is

 

\(\begin{aligned}P &=I^{2} R \\136000 &=910^{2} R \\R &=0.164 \Omega\end{aligned}\)

 

The three resistors must therefore each have a resistance of 0.164 Î© and a short-term rating of 136 kW.
The physical size of these resistors is much smaller than if they were designed for continuous duty.
