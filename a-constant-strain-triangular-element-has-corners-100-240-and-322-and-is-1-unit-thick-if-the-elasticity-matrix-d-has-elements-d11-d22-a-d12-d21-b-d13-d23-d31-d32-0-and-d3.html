Guest Question.

A constant strain triangular element has corners 1(0,0), 2(4,0) and 3(2,2) and is 1 unit thick. If the elasticity matrix [D] has elements \(D_{11}=D_{22}=a, D_{12}=D_{21}=b, D_{13}=D_{23}=D_{31}=D_{32}=0\) and \(D_{33}=c\), derive the stiffness matrix for the element.
Ans. \(\left[K^{ e }\right]=\frac{1}{4}\left[\begin{array}{cccccc}a+c & & & & & \\b+c & a+c & & & & \\-a+c & -b+c & a+c & & & \\b-c & a-c & -b-c & a+c & & \\-2 c & -2 c & -2 c & 2 c & 4 c & \\-2 b & -2 a & 2 b & -2 a & 0 & 4 a\end{array}\right]\)

Admin
Solution

Assume

\(u(x, y)=\alpha_{1}+\alpha_{2} x+\alpha_{3} y\)

 

\(v(x, y)=\alpha_{4}+\alpha_{5} x+\alpha_{6} y\)

Then

\(u_{1}=\alpha_{1}, \quad u_{2}=\alpha_{1}+4 \alpha_{2}, \quad u_{3}=\alpha_{1}+2 \alpha_{2}+2 \alpha_{3}\)

from which

\(u_{1}-u_{2}=-4 \alpha_{2}\)

so that

\(\alpha_{2}=\frac{u_{2}-u_{1}}{4}\)

and

\(\alpha_{3}=\frac{2 u_{3}-u_{1}-u_{2}}{4}\)

Therefore

\(u=u_{1}+\frac{\left(u_{2}-u_{1}\right)}{4} x+\frac{\left(2 u_{3}-u_{1}-u_{2}\right)}{4} y\)

or

\(u=\left(1-\frac{x}{4}-\frac{y}{4}\right) u_{1}+\left(\frac{x}{4}-\frac{y}{4}\right) u_{2}+\frac{y}{2} u_{3}\)

Similarly

\(\nu=\left(1-\frac{x}{4}-\frac{y}{4}\right) \nu_{1}+\left(\frac{x}{4}-\frac{y}{4}\right) \nu_{2}+\frac{y}{2} \nu_{3}\)

Now

\(\varepsilon_{x}=\frac{\partial u}{\partial x}=-\frac{u_{1}}{4}+\frac{u_{2}}{4}\)

 

\(\varepsilon_{y}=\frac{\partial \nu}{\partial y}=-\frac{\nu_{1}}{4}-\frac{\nu_{2}}{4}+\frac{\nu_{3}}{2}\)

 

\(\gamma_{x y}=\frac{\partial u}{\partial y}+\frac{\partial \nu}{\partial x}=-\frac{u_{1}}{4}-\frac{u_{2}}{4}-\frac{\nu_{1}}{4}+\frac{\nu_{2}}{4}\)

Therefore

\([B]\left\{\delta^{ e }\right\}=\left[\begin{array}{c}\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} \\\frac{\partial \nu}{\partial y} \\\frac{\partial u}{\partial y}+\frac{\partial \nu}{\partial x}\end{array}\right]=\frac{1}{4}\left[\begin{array}{cccccc}-1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\0 & -1 & 0 & -1 & 0 & 2 \\-1 & -1 & -1 & 1 & 2 & 0\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}u_{1} \\\nu_{1} \\u_{2} \\v_{2} \\u_{3} \\\nu_{3}\end{array}\right\}\)

Also

\([D]=\left[\begin{array}{lll}a & b & 0 \\b & a & 0 \\0 & 0 & c\end{array}\right]\)

Then

\([D][B]=\frac{1}{4}\left[\begin{array}{cccccc}-a & -b & a & -b & 0 & 2 b \\-b & -a & b & -a & 0 & 2 a \\-c & -c & -c & c & 2 c & 0\end{array}\right]\)

And

\([B]^{ T }[D][B]=\frac{1}{16}\left[\begin{array}{cccccc}a+c & b+c & -a+c & b-c & -2 c & -2 b \\b+c & a+c & -b+c & a-c & -2 c & -2 a \\-a+c & -b+c & a+c & -b-c & -2 c & 2 b \\b-c & a-c & -b-c & a+c & 2 c & -2 a \\-2 c & -2 c & -2 c & 2 c & 4 c & 0 \\-2 b & -2 a & 2 b & -2 a & 0 & 4 a\end{array}\right]\)

Since \(\left[K^{ e }\right]=[B]^{ T }[D][B]\) then

\(\left[K^{e}\right]=\frac{1}{4}\left[\begin{array}{cccccc}a+c & & & & & \text { SYM } \\b+c & a+c & & & & \\-a+c & -b+c & a+c & & & \\b-c & a-c & -b-c & a+c & & \\-2 c & -2 c & -2 c & 2 c & 4 c & \\-2 b & -2 a & 2 b & -2 a & 0 & 4 a\end{array}\right]\)
