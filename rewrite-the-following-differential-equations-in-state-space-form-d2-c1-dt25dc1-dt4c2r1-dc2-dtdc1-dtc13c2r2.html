Guest Question.

Rewrite the following differential equations in state-space form:

\(\frac{d^2c_1}{dt^2}+5\frac{dc_1}{dt} +4c_2=r_1 \)

 

\(\frac{dc_2}{dt} +\frac{dc_1}{dt}+c_1+3c_2=r_2\)
Admin
Solution

Let the states be:

\(x_1=c_1\)

 

\(x_2=\frac{dc_1}{dt}\)

 

\(x_3=c_2\)

Taking the derivative of the states yields:

\(\dot{x_1}= \frac{dc_1}{dt}=x_2\)

 

\(\dot{x_2}=\frac{d^2c_1}{dt^2}=-5\frac{dc_1}{dt}-4c_2+r_1\)

 

\(\dot{x_2}=-5x_2-4x_3+r_1\)

 

\(\dot{x_3}=\frac{dc_2}{dt}=- \frac{dc_1}{dt}-c_1-3c_2+r_2\)

 

\(\dot{x_3}=-x_2 -x_1-3x_3+r_2\)

These equations can now be put into the state-space form, \(\dot{x} =Ax+B\eta \):

\(\left[\begin{matrix} \dot{x_1} \\ \dot{x_2} \\ \dot{x_3} \end{matrix} \right] = \left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 &-5 & -4 \\ -1 & -1 & -3 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} {x_1} \\ {x_2} \\ {x_3} \end{matrix} \right]+\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right]\left[\begin{matrix} r_1 \\ r_2 \end{matrix} \right] \)

where the plant and control matrices are:

\(A=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 &-5 & -4 \\ -1 & -1 & -3 \end{matrix} \right] and B=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right]\)

and the state and control vectors are:

\(x=\left[\begin{matrix} {x_1} \\ {x_2} \\ {x_3} \end{matrix} \right], \eta =\left[\begin{matrix} r_1 \\ r_2 \end{matrix} \right]\)

The output equation

\(y= Cx+D\eta \)

can be expressed as follows:

\(\left[\begin{matrix} y_1 \\ y_2 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} {x_1} \\ {x_2} \\ {x_3} \end{matrix} \right] \)

where D is a null matrix. It also should be noted that a transfer function can be
rewritten in the state variable form.

The solution of the state equations will be discussed in the next several sections.

Both analytical and numerical solutions of the state equations will be presented.
