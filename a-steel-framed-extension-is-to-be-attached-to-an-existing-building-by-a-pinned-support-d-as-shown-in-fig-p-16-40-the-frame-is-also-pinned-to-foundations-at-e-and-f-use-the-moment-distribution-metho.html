Guest Question.

A steel-framed extension is to be attached to an existing building by a pinned support D as shown in Fig. P.16.40; the frame is also pinned to foundations at E and F. Use the moment distribution method to determine the bending moments at the joints of the frame produced by the idealised wind loading shown. The relative second moments of area of the members of the frame are as shown. Sketch the bending moment diagram for the frame and its displaced shape.

Ans. \(M_{ A }=15.5 kNm , M_{ B }(\text { in } BA )=2.5 kNm , M_{ B }(\text { in } BF )=41.8 kNm\),

\(M_{ B }(\text { in } BC )=39.4 kNm , M_{ C }=23.3 kNm\).

Admin
Solution

The distribution factors are, from Eq. (16.36)

\(M_{ BA }^{\prime}=\frac{K_{ BA }}{K_{ BA }+K_{ BC }}\left(-M_{ B }\right) \quad M_{ BC }^{\prime}=\frac{K_{ BC }}{K_{ BA }+K_{ BC }}\left(-M_{ B }\right)\) (16.36)

\(DF _{ AE }=\frac{3 \times 2 E I / 3}{3 \times 2 E I / 3+4 \times 3 E I / 6}=0.5, \quad DF _{ AB }=0.5\)

 

\(DF _{ BA }=\frac{4 \times 3 E I / 6}{4 \times 3 E I / 6+3 \times 2 E I / 3+4 \times 2 E I / 3}=0.3\)

 

\(DF _{ BF }=\frac{3 \times 2 E I / 3}{4 \times 3 E I / 6+3 \times 2 E I / 3+4 \times 2 E I / 3}=0.3\)

 

\(DF _{ BC }=0.4\)

 

\(DF _{ CB }=\frac{4 \times 2 E I / 3}{4 \times 2 E I / 3+3 \times 3 E I / 6}=0.64\)

 

\(DF _{ CD }=0.36\)

The FEMs corresponding to the no-sway case are all zero.
Apply an arbitrary sway as shown in Fig. S.16.40(a).

Then the arbitrary sway moments are

\(M_{ AE }=-3 \times 2 E I \theta / 3^{2}=-2 E I \theta / 3\)

 

\(M_{ BF }=-3 \times 2 E I \theta / 3^{2}=-2 E I \theta / 3\)

 

\(M_{ CB }=+6 \times 2 E I \theta / 3^{2}=+4 E I \theta / 3\)

Therefore apply arbitrary sway moments of -100, -100 and + 200 kNm. The arbitrary moments are then calculated in Table S.16.40.

From the principle of virtual work

\(M_{ AB } \theta+M_{ BF } \theta-M_{ BC } \theta-M_{ CB } \theta+40 \times 3 \theta=0\)

Then

\(M_{ AB }+M_{ BF }-M_{ BC }-M_{ CB }=-120\)

Therefore

k (-44.6 -120.2 -113.1 -66.9) = – 120

from which

k = 0.348

The final moments are then

\(M_{ AE }=-M_{ AB }=-15.5 kNm\)

 

\(M_{ BA }=+2.5 kNm\)

 

\(M_{ BF }=-41.8 kNm\)

 

\(M_{ BC }=+39.4 kNm\)

 

\(M_{ CB }=-M_{ CD }=+23.3 kNm\)

The bending moment diagram and displaced shape are shown in Fig. S.16.40(b).
