Guest Question.

Consider a system with state matrices,

\(F =\left[\begin{array}{rr} -2 & 1 \\ 0 & -3\end{array}\right], G =\left[\begin{array}{l} 1 \\ 1\end{array}\right], H =\left[\begin{array}{ll} 1 & 3\end{array}\right]\).

a) Use feedback of the form \(u(t)=- K x (t)+\bar{N} r(t)\), where \(\bar{N}\) is a nonzero scalar, to move the poles to – 3 ± 3j.

b) Choose \(\bar{N}\) so that if r is a constant, the system has zero steady-state error; that is \(y(\infty)=r\).

c) Show that if F changes to \(F +\delta F\), where \(\delta F\) is an arbitrary 2×2 matrix, then your choice of \(\bar{N}\) in part (b) will no longer make \(y(\infty)=r\). Therefore, the system is not robust under changes to the system parameters in F.

d) The system steady-state error performance can be made robust by augmenting the system with an integrator and using unity feedback; that is, by setting \(\dot{x}_{I}=r-y\), where \(x_{I}\) is the state of the integrator. To see this, first use state feedback of the form \(u=- K x -K_{1} x_{I}\) so that the poles of the augmented system are at \( -3,-2 \pm j \sqrt{3}\).

e) Show that the resulting system will yield \(y(\infty)=r\) no matter how the matrices F and G are changed, as long as the closed-loop system remains stable.

f) For part (d), use MATLAB (Simulink) software to plot the time response of the system to a constant input. Draw Bode plots of the controller as well as the sensitivity function \((\mathcal S)\) and the complementary sensitivity function \((\mathcal T )\).

Admin
Solution

(a) Using feedback of the form, u = – Kx + Nr, we have,

\(\operatorname{det}(s I – F + G K)=\left(s+2+k_{1}\right)\left(s+3+k_{2}\right)+k_{1}\left(1-k_{1}\right)=s^{2}+6 s+18\),

when \(K =\left[\begin{array}{ll} 5 & -4\end{array}\right]\). This result can be verified using the MATLAB place command.

(b) We can find the desired value for N by setting the DC gain from r to y equal to unity. The closed-loop system equations are,

\(\dot{ x }= F x + G (- K x +N r)=( F – G K ) x + G N r\),

\(y= H x\).

Therefore, the transfer function is,

\(D(s)= H (s I – F + G K )^{-1} G N\),

and the DC gain is simply,

\(D(0)= H (- F + G K )^{-1} G N=\frac{5}{9} N=1\).

Hence, we choose \(N=\frac{9}{5}\).

(c) Change \(F \text { to }( F +\delta F )\), and let the value of N that keeps the tracking error at zero be \(N^{\prime}\). Then letting \(T^{\prime}(s)\) be the transfer function associated with the perturbed system,

\(N^{\prime-1}=T^{\prime}(0)=- H ( F +\delta F – G K )^{-1} G \),

\(=- H \left[( F – G K )\left( I -( F – G K )^{-1} \delta F \right)\right]^{-1}G\),

\(=- H \left( I -( F – G K )^{-1} \delta F \right)^{-1}( F – G K )^{-1} G\).

For δF small,

\(\left( I -( F – G K )^{-1} \delta F \right)^{-1}= I +( F – G K )^{-1} \delta F\).

Hence,

\(N^{\prime-1}=\underbrace{- H ( F – G K )^{-1} G }_{N^{-1}}- H ( F – G K )^{-1} \delta F ( F – G K )^{-1} G\).

And for arbitrary δF we arrive at,

\(N^{\prime-1} \neq N^{-1}\).

Therefore, small changes in the plant matrix F prevent the steady-state error from reaching zero. The control system is not robust with respect to changes in F.

(d) Augmenting the system equations with an integrator state, \(x_{I}\) , the state equation become,

\(\left[\begin{array}{c}\dot{ x } \\ \dot{x}_{I}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc} F & 0 \\ – H & 0\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} x \\ x_{I}\end{array}\right]+\left[\begin{array}{c} G \\ 0\end{array}\right] u+\left[\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 1\end{array}\right] r\),

\(y=\left[\begin{array}{ll} H & 0\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} x \\ x_{I}\end{array}\right]\).

or with \(z =\left[\begin{array}{ll} x & x_{I}\end{array}\right]^{T}\),

\(\dot{ z }= F _{a} z + G _{a} u+ G _{r} r\),

\(y= H _{a} z\).

Using feedback of the form \(u=- K x -k_{I} x_{I}=- K _{a} z\), we have,

\(\operatorname{det}\left(s I – F _{a}+ G _{a} K _{a}\right)=0 \text { for } s=-3,-2 \pm j \sqrt{3}\),

when \(K _{a}=\left[\begin{array}{lll} 0.3 & 1.7 & -2.1\end{array}\right]\). This result can be verified using the MATLAB place command.

(e) We can show that the closed-loop DC gain from r to y is independent of F,

\(y_{\infty}=T(0) r_{\infty}=\left[\begin{array}{ll}H & 0\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{cc} – F + G K & G k_{I} \\ H & 0 \end{array}\right]^{-1}\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1\end{array}\right] r_{\infty}\)

 

\(=\left[\begin{array}{ll} H & 0\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{cc}* & ( F – G K )^{-1} G k_{I}\left[ H ( F – G K )^{-1} G k_{I}\right]^{-1} \\ * & *\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1\end{array}\right] r_{\infty}\)

 

\(=\left[ H ( F – G K )^{-1} G k_{I}\right]\left[ H ( F – G K )^{-1} G k_{1}\right]^{-1} r_{\infty}=r_{\infty}\) independent of F,G,H.
