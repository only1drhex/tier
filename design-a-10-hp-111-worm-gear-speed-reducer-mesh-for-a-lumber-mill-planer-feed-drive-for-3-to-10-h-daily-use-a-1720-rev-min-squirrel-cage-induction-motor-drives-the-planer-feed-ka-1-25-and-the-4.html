Guest Question.

Design a 10-hp 11:1 worm-gear speed-reducer mesh for a lumber mill planer feed drive for 3- to 10-h daily use. A 1720-rev/min squirrel-cage induction motor drives the planer feed (\(K_{a}\) = 1.25), and the ambient tem-perature is 70°F.

Admin
Solution

Function:\( H_{0}\) = 10 hp,\( m_{G} = 11, n_{W}\) = 1720 rev/min.

Design factor:\( n_{d} \)= 1.2.

Materials and processes: case-hardened alloy steel worm, sand-cast bronze gear.

Worm threads: double,\( N_{W} = 2, N_{G} = m_{G}N_{W} = 11(2)\) = 22 gear teeth acceptable for \(ϕ_{n} \)= 20°, according to

Table 15–10.

Decision 1: Choose an axial pitch of worm\( p_{x}\) = 1.5 in. Then,

\(P_{t} = π∕p_{x} = π∕1.5\) = 2.0944

D = \(N_{G}∕P_{t} = 22∕2.0944 = \)10.504 in

Eq. (15–39): a = 0.3183\(p_{x} = 0.3183(1.5) = 0.4775 \)in (addendum)

\(a=\frac{P_{x}}{\pi } =0.3183P_{x}\) Eq. (15–39)

Eq. (15–40): b = 0.3683(1.5) = 0.5525 in (dedendum)

\(b=\frac{1.157P_{x}}{\pi } =0.3683P_{x}\) Eq. (15–40)

Eq. (15–41): \( h_{t}\) = 0.6866(1.5) = 1.030 in

\(h_{t}=\begin{cases}\frac{2.157p_{x}}{\pi }=0.6866p_{x} & P_{x}\geq 0.16 in \\\frac{2.200p_{x}}{\pi } +0.002=0.7003p_{x}+0.002 & p_{x}<0.16 in\end{cases} \) Eq. (15–41)

Decision 2: Choose a mean worm diameter d = 2.000 in. Then

C = (d + D)∕2 = (2.000 + 10.504)∕2 = 6.252 in

\((d)_{lo} = 6.252^{0.875} ∕3 = 1.657\) in

\((d)_{hi} = 6.252^{0.875} ∕1.6 = 3.107 \)in

The range, given by Eq. (15–27), is 1.657 ≤ d ≤ 3.107 in, which is satisfactory. Try d = 2.500 in.

\(\frac{C^{0.875}}{3}\leq d\leq \frac{C^{0.875}}{1.6} \) Eq. (15–27)

Recompute C:

C = (2.5 + 10.504)∕2 = 6.502 in

The range is now 1.715 ≤ d ≤ 3.216 in, which is still satisfactory. Decision: d = 2.500 in. Then

Eq. (13–27): \(L = p_{x} N_{W} = 1.5(2)\) = 3.000 in

Eq. (13–28): \(λ = tan^{−1} [L∕(πd)] = tan^{−1} [3∕(π2.5)] = 20.905°\) (from Table 15–9 lead angle OK)

Eq. (15–62): \( V_{s} =\frac{ πdn_{W}}{12 cos λ} =\frac{ π(2.5)1720}{12 cos 20.905°} = 1205.1 \)ft/min

\(V_{W} = \frac{πdn_{W}}{12} = \frac{π(2.5)1720}{12} = 1125.7 \)ft/min

\(V_{G} =\frac{ πDn_{G}}{12} =\frac{ π(10.504)1720∕11}{12} = 430.0 ft/min \)

Eq. (15–33): \(C_{s} = 1190 − 477 log 10.504 = \)702.8

\(C_{s}=\begin{cases}1000 & C>3 D_{m}\leq 2.5in \\1190-477 logD_{m} & C>3 D_{m}>2.5in\end{cases} \) Eq. (15–33)

Eq. (15–36): \(C_{m} = 0.02\sqrt{−11^{2} + 40(11) − 76} + 0.46 = 0.772\)

\(C_{m}=\begin{cases} 0.02\sqrt{-m_{G}^{2}+40m_{G}-76}+0.46&3\lt m_{G}\leq 20 \\0.0107\sqrt{-m_{G}^{2}+ 56m_{G}+5145} & 20\lt m_{G}\leq 76 \\1.1483-0.006 58m_{G} &m_{G}\gt 76\end{cases} \) Eq. (15–36)

Eq. (15–37): \(C_{v} =13.31(1205.1)^{−0.571}\) = 0.232

\(C_{v}=\begin{cases} 0.659 exp(-0.0011V_{x})&V_{s}=0 \\13.31V_{s}^{-0.571} & 700\leq V_{s}\lt 3000 ft/min \\65.52V_{s}^{-0.774} &V_{s}\gt 3000 ft/min\end{cases} \) Eq. (15–37)

Eq. (15–38): f = 0.103 exp[−0.11\((1205.1)^{ 0.45}] + 0.012 = \)0.0191

\(f=\begin{cases} 0.15&V_{s}=0 \\0.124 exp(-0.074V_{s}^{0.645}) & 0 \lt V_{s} \leq10 ft/min \\0.103exp(-0.110V_{s}^{0.450}) &V_{s}\gt 10 ft/min\end{cases} \) Eq. (15–38)

Eq. (15–54) \( e_{W} =\frac{ cos 20° − 0.0191 tan 20.905°}{cos 20° + 0.0191 cot 20.905°} = 0.942 \)

\(e_{W}=\frac{\cos \phi _{n}-f\tan \lambda }{\cos \phi _{n}+f\tan \lambda} \) Eq. (15–54)

(If the worm gear drives,\( e_{G}\) = 0.939.) To ensure nominal 10-hp output, with adjustments for \(K_{a}, n_{d}\), and e,

Eq. (15–57): \( W^{t}_{W} = 1222\frac{cos 20° sin 20.905° + 0.0191 cos 20.905°}{cos 20° cos 20.905° − 0.0191 sin 20.905° }= 495.4 lbf \)

\(W_{W}^{t}=W_{G}^{t}\frac{\cos \phi _{n}\sin \lambda +f\cos \lambda }{\cos \phi _{n}\cos \lambda-f\sin \lambda} \) Eq. (15–57)

Eq. (15–58): \( W^{t}_{G} =\frac{ 33 000(1.2)10(1.25)}{430(0.942)} = 1222 lbf \)

\(W_{G}^{t}=\frac{33 000n_{d}H_{0}K_{a}}{V_{G}e} \) Eq. (15–58)

Eq. (15–59): \(H_{W} =\frac{ π(2.5)1720(495.4)}{ 12(33 000)} = 16.9 hp \)

\(H_{W}=\frac{W_{W}^{t}V_{W}}{33000} =\frac{\pi d_{W}n_{W}W_{W}^{t}}{12(33000)} \) Eq. (15–59)

Eq. (15–60): \( H_{G} =\frac{ π(10.504)1720∕11(1222)}{12(33 000)} = 15.92 hp \)

\(H_{G}=\frac{W_{W}^{G}V_{G}}{33000} =\frac{\pi d_{G}n_{G}W_{G}^{t}}{12(33000)} \) Eq. (15–60)

Eq. (15–61): \( W_{f} = \frac{0.0191(1222)}{ 0.0191 sin 20.905° − cos 20° cos 20.905° }= −26.8 lbf \)

\(W_{f}=\frac{fW_{G}^{t}}{f\sin \lambda -\cos \phi _{n}\cos \lambda } \) Eq. (15–61)

Eq. (15–63): \( H_{f} =\frac{ ∣−26.8∣1205.1}{33 000 }= 0.979 hp \)

\(H_{f}=\frac{\left|W_{f}\right| V_{s}}{33000} hp \) Eq. (15–63)

From Eq. (15–28), with \(C_{s} = 702.8, C_{m} = 0.772, and C_{v} = 0.232,\)

\((W^{t})_{all}=C_{s}D_{m}^{0.8}F_{e}C_{m}C_{v}\) Eq. (15–28)

\( (F_{e})_{req} = \frac{W^{t}_{G}}{C_{s} D^{0.8}C_{m} C_{v}}=\frac{ 1222}{702.8(10.504)0.80.772(0.232)}= 1.479 in \)

Decision 3: The available range of \((F_{e})_{G}\) is 1.479 ≤ \((F_{e})_{G}\) ≤ 2d∕3 or 1.479 ≤\( (F_{e})_{G} \)≤ 1.667 in. Set \((F_{e})_{G} \)= 1.5 in.

Eq. (15–28): \( W ^{t}_{all} =702.8 (10.504)^{0.8}1.5(0.772)0.232 = 1239 lbf \)

This is greater than 1222 lbf. There is a little excess capacity. The force analysis stands.

Decision 4:

Eq. (15–50): \(h_{CR} = \frac{n_{W}}{6494} + 0.13 = \frac{1720}{6494} + 0.13 = 0.395 ft · lbf/(min · in^{2} · °F) \)

\(\hslash _{CR}=\begin{cases}\frac{n_{W}}{6494}+0.13& no fan on worm shaft\\ \frac{n_{W}}{3939}+0.13 & fan on worm shaft\end{cases}\) Eq. (15–50)

Eq. (15–49): \( H_{loss} = 33 000(1 − e)H_{W} = 33 000(1 − 0.942)16.9 = 32 347 \)ft · lbf/min

The AGMA area, from Eq. (15–52), is \(A_{min} = 43.2 C^{1.7} = 43.2(6.502)^{1.7} = 1041.5 in^{2} . \)A rough estimate of the lateral area for 6-in clearances:

Vertical: d + D + 6 = 2.5 + 10.5 + 6 = 19 in

Width: D + 6 = 10.5 + 6 = 16.5 in

Thickness: d + 6 = 2.5 + 6 = 8.5 in

Area: 2(19)16.5 + 2(8.5)19 + 16.5(8.5) ≈ 1090 in\(^{2}\)

Expect an area of 1100 in\(^{2}\) . Choose: Air-cooled, no fan on worm, with an ambient temperature of 70°F

\(t_{s} = t_{a} +\frac{H_{loss}}{h_{CR} A} = 70 +\frac{32 350}{0.395(1100)} = 70 + 74.5 = 144.5°F \)

Lubricant is safe with some margin for smaller area.

Eq. (13–18): \(P_{n} =\frac{ P_{t}}{cos λ} =\frac{ 2.094}{cos 20.905° }= 2.242\)

\(p_{n} =\frac{ π}{P_{n}}= \frac {π}{2.242 }= 1.401 in \)

Gear bending stress, for reference, is

Eq. (15–53): \( σ =\frac{ W^{t}_{G}}{p_{n} F_{e}y} = \frac{1222}{1.401(1.5)0.125 }= 4652 psi \)

The risk is from wear, which is addressed by the AGMA method that provides\( (W^{t} _{G})_{all}.\)
