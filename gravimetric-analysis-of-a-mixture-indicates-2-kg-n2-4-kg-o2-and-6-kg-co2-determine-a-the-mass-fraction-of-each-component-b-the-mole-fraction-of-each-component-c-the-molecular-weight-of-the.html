Guest Question.

Gravimetric analysis of a mixture indicates 2 kg \( N _{2}, 4 kg O _{2} \text {, and } 6 kg CO _{2} \text {. } \). Determine (a) the mass fraction of each component, (b) the mole fraction of each component, (c) the molecular weight of the mixture, and (d) its gas constant.

Admin
Solution

(a) The total mass of the mixture is m = 2 + 4 + 6 = 12 kg. The respective mass fractions are

\( x_{1}=\frac{2}{12}=0.1667 \quad x_{2}=\frac{4}{12}=0.3333 \quad x_{3}=\frac{6}{12}=0.5 \).

(b) To find the mole fractions we first determine the number of moles of each component:

\( N_{1}=\frac{2}{28}=0.0714 kmol \quad N_{2}=\frac{4}{32}=0.125 kmol \quad N_{3}=\frac{6}{44}=0.1364 kmol \).

The total number of moles is N = 0.0714 + 0.125 + 0.1364 = 0.3328 mol. The respective mole fractions are

\( y_{1}=\frac{0.0714}{0.3328}=0.215 \quad y_{2}=\frac{0.125}{0.3328}=0.376 \quad y_{3}=\frac{0.1364}{0.3328}=0.410 \).

(c) The molecular weight of the mixture is M = m /N = 12/ 0.3328 = 36.1 kg/ kmol. Alternatively, we could write

\( M=\sum y_{i} M_{i}=(0.215)(28)+(0.376)(32)+(0.410)(44)=36.1 kg / kmol \).

\( \text { (d) The gas constant is } R=\frac{R_{u}}{M}=\frac{8.314}{36.1}=0.230 kJ / kg \cdot K \text {. } \)
