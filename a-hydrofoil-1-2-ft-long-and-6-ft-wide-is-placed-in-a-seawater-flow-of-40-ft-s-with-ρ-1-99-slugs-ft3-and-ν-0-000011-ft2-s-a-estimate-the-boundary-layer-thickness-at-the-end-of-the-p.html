Guest Question.

A hydrofoil 1.2 ft long and 6 ft wide is placed in a seawater flow of 40 ft/s, with ρ = 1.99 slugs/\(ft^3\) and \(\nu = 0.000011 ft^2/s\). (a) Estimate the boundary layer thickness at the end of the plate. Estimate the friction drag for (b) turbulent smooth-wall flow from the leading edge, (c) laminar turbulent flow with \(Re_{trans} = 5 × 10^5\), and (d) turbulent rough-wall flow with ε = 0.0004 ft.

Admin
Solution

Part (a)

The Reynolds number is

\(Re_L = \frac{UL}{\nu} = \frac{(40 ft/s)(1.2 ft)}{0.000011 ft^2/s} = 4.36 \times 10^6\)

Thus the trailing-edge flow is certainly turbulent. The maximum boundary layer thickness would occur for turbulent flow starting at the leading edge. From Eq. (7.42),

\(Re_{\delta} \approx 0.16 Re_x^{6/7} or \frac{\delta}{x} \approx \frac{0.16}{Re_x^{1/7}}\) (7.42)

\(\frac{\delta(L)}{L} = \frac{0.16}{(4.36 \times 10^6)^{1/7}} = 0.018\)

or \(\delta\) = 0.018(1.2 ft) = 0.0216 ft

This is 7.5 times thicker than a fully laminar boundary layer at the same Reynolds number.

Part (b)

For fully turbulent smooth-wall flow, the drag coefficient on one side of the plate is, from Eq. (7.45),

\(C_D = \frac{0.031}{Re_L^{1/7}} = \frac{7}{6}c_f(L)\) (7.45)

\(C_D = \frac{0.031}{(4.36 \times 10^6)^{1/7}} = 0.00349\)

Then the drag on both sides of the foil is approximately

\(D = 2C_D(\frac{1}{2} \rho U^2)bL = 2(0.00349)(\frac{1}{2})(1.99)(40)^2(6.0)(1.2) = 80 lbf\)

Part (c)

With a laminar leading edge and \(Re_{trans} = 5 \times 10^5\), Eq. (7.49a) applies:

\(C_D \approx \begin{cases} \frac{0.031}{Re_L^{1/7}} – \frac{1440}{Re_L} & Re_{trans}=5\times 10^5 (7.49a)\\ \frac{0.031}{Re_L^{1/7}}-\frac{8700}{Re_L} & Re_{trans}=3\times 10^6 (7.49b)\end{cases}\)

 

\(C_D = 0.00349 – \frac{1440}{4.36 \times 10^6} = 0.00316\)

The drag can be recomputed for this lower drag coefficient:

\(D = 2C_D(\frac{1}{2} \rho U^2)bL = 72 lbf\)

Part (d)

Finally, for the rough wall, we calculate

\(\frac{L}{\epsilon} = \frac{1.2 ft}{0.0004 ft} = 3000\)

From Fig. 7.6 at \(Re_L = 4.36 \times 10^6\), this condition is just inside the fully rough regime. Equation (7.48b) applies:

\(C_D \approx \left(1.89 + 1.62 \log \frac{L}{\epsilon}\right)^{-2.5}\) (7.48b)

\(C_D = (1.89 + 1.62 \log 3000)^{-2.5} = 0.00644\)

and the drag estimate is

\(D = 2C_D(\frac{1}{2} \rho U^2)bL = 148 lbf\)

This small roughness nearly doubles the drag. It is probable that the total hydrofoil drag is still another factor of 2 larger because of trailing-edge flow separation effects.
