Guest Question.

Determine the enthalpy and entropy changes of liquid water for a change of state from 1 bar and \(25^{\circ}C\) to 1000 bar and \(50^{\circ}C.\) Data for water are given in the following table.

\(\beta /K^{-1}\) \(V/cm^{3}\cdot mol^{-1}\) \(C_{P}/J\cdot mol^{-1}\cdot K^{-1}\) P/bar \(t^{\circ }C\)
\(256 \times 10^{−6}\) 18.071 75.305 1 25
\(366 \times 10^{−6}\) 18.012 …… 1000 25
\(458 \times 10^{−6}\) 18.234 75.314 1 50
\(568 \times 10^{−6}\) 18.174 …… 1000 50
Admin
Solution

For application to the change of state described, Eqs. (6.27) and (6.28) require integration. Enthalpy and entropy are state functions, and the path of integration is arbitrary; the path most suited to the given data is shown in Fig. 6.1. Because the data indicate that \(C_{P}\) is a weak function of T and that both V and \(\beta\) change relatively slowly with P, integration with arithmetic means is satisfactory. The integrated forms of Eqs. (6.27) and (6.28) that result are:

\( dH=C_{P}dT+(1-\beta T)V dP\) (6.27)

 

\( dS=C_{P}\frac{dT}{T}-\beta V dP\) (6.28)

 

\( \Delta H=\left\langle C_{P}\right\rangle(T_{2}-T_{1})+(1-\left\langle \beta \right\rangle T_{2} )\left\langle V\right\rangle(P_{2}-P_{1})\)

 

\( \Delta S=\left\langle C_{P}\right\rangle\ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-\left\langle \beta \right\rangle \left\langle V\right\rangle(P_{2}-P_{1})\)

For P = 1 bar,

\( \left\langle C_{P}\right\rangle=\frac{75.305+75.314}{2}=75.310 J\cdot mol^{-1}\cdot K^{-1}\)

For \(t = 50^{\circ }C,\)

\( \left\langle V\right\rangle=\frac{18.234+18.174}{2}=18.204 cm^{3}\cdot mol^{-1}\)

and

\( \left\langle \beta \right\rangle=\frac{458+568}{2}\times 10^{-6}=513\times 10^{-6}K^{-1}\)

Substitution of these numerical values into the equation for \(\Delta H\) gives:

\(\Delta H=75.310(323.15-298.15) J\cdot mol^{-1}\)

 

\( +\frac{\left[1-(513\times 10^{-6})(323.15)\right](18.204)(1000-1)bar\cdot cm^{3}\cdot mol^{-1} }{10 bar\cdot cm^{3}\cdot J^{-1}}\)

 

\( =1883+1517=3400 J\cdot mol^{-1}\)

Similarly for \(\Delta S,\)

\( \Delta S=75.310\ln \frac{323.15}{298.15} J\cdot mol^{-1}\cdot K^{-1}\)

 

\( -\frac{(513\times 10^{-6})(18.204)(1000-1)bar\cdot cm^{3}\cdot mol^{-1}\cdot K^{-1}}{10 bar\cdot cm^{3}\cdot J^{-1}}\)

 

\( =6.06-0.93=5.13 J\cdot mol^{-1}\cdot K^{-1}\)

Note that the effect of a pressure change of almost 1000 bar on the enthalpy and entropy of liquid water is less than that of a temperature change of only \(25^{\circ}C.\)
