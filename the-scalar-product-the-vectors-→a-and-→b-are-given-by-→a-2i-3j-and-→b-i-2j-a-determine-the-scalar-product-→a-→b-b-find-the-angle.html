The Scalar Product

The vectors \(\overrightarrow{A}\) and \(\overrightarrow{B}\) are given by \(\overrightarrow{A}=2 \hat{i}+3 \hat{j}\) and \(\overrightarrow{B}=-\hat{i}+2 \hat{j}\) .

(A) Determine the scalar product \(\overrightarrow{A} \cdot \overrightarrow{B}\) .

(B) Find the angle θ between \(\overrightarrow{A} \text { and } \overrightarrow{B}\) .

 

Admin
Solution

Conceptualize There is no physical system to imagine here. Rather, it is purely a mathematical exercise involving two vectors.

Categorize Because we have a definition for the scalar product, we categorize this example as a substitution problem.

Substitute the specific vector expressions for \(\overrightarrow{A} \text { and } \overrightarrow{B}\) :

\(\begin{aligned}\overrightarrow{A} \cdot \overrightarrow{B} &=(2 \hat{i}+3 \hat{j}) \cdot(-\hat{i}+2 \hat{j}) \\&=-2 \hat{i} \cdot \hat{i}+2 \hat{i} \cdot 2 \hat{j}-3 \hat{j} \cdot \hat{i}+3 \hat{j} \cdot 2 \hat{j} \\&=-2(1)+4(0)-3(0)+6(1)=-2+6=4\end{aligned}\)

The same result is obtained when we use Equation 7.6 directly, where \(A_{x}=2, A_{y}=3, B_{x}=-1, \text { and } B_{y}=2\).

\(\overrightarrow{A} \cdot \overrightarrow{B}=A_{x} B_{x}+A_{y} B_{y}+A_{z} B_{z}\) (7.6)

Evaluate the magnitudes of \(\overrightarrow{A} \text { and } \overrightarrow{B}\) using the Pythagorean theorem:

\(\begin{aligned}&A=\sqrt{A_{x}^{2}+A_{y}^{2}}=\sqrt{(2)^{2}+(3)^{2}}=\sqrt{13} \\&B=\sqrt{B_{x}^{2}+B_{y}^{2}}=\sqrt{(-1)^{2}+(2)^{2}}=\sqrt{5}\end{aligned}\)

Use Equation 7.2 and the result from part (A) to find the angle:

\(\overrightarrow{A} \cdot \overrightarrow{B} \equiv A B \cos \theta\) (7.2)

\(\cos \theta=\frac{\overrightarrow{A} \cdot \overrightarrow{B}}{A B}=\frac{4}{\sqrt{13} \sqrt{5}}=\frac{4}{\sqrt{65}}\)

\(\theta=\cos ^{-1} \frac{4}{\sqrt{65}}=60.3^{\circ}\)
