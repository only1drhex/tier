Guest Question.

Given that \(D=z\rho \cos ^{2}\phi a_{z} C/m^{2} \), calculate the charge density at ( 1, π/4, 3) and the total charge enclosed by the cylinder of radius 1 m with – 2≤z≤ 2 m.

Admin
Solution
\(\rho _{v}=\triangledown .D=\frac{\partial D_{z}}{\partial z}= \rho \cos ^{2}\phi \)

At (1,π/4, 3), \(\rho _{v}=1. \cos ^{2}(\pi /4)=0.5 C/m^{3}. \) The total charge enclosed by the cylinder can be found in two different ways.

Method 1: This method is based directly on the definition of the total volume charge.

\(Q=\int\limits_{v}^{}{\rho _{v} dv}=\int\limits_{v}^{}{\rho \cos ^{2}\phi\rho d\phi d\rho dz} \)
\(=\int\limits_{z=-2}^{2}{dz}\int\limits_{\phi =0}^{2\pi }{\cos ^{2}\phi d\phi } \int\limits_{\rho =0}^{1}{\rho ^{2} d\rho }=4(\pi )(1/3) \)
\(=\frac{4\pi }{3}\) C

Method 2: Alternatively, we can use Gauss’s law
\(Q=\psi =\oint\limits_{S}^{}{D.dS}=[\int\limits_{S}^{}{}+\int\limits_{t}^{}{}+\int\limits_{b}^{}{} ]D.dS \)
\(=\psi _{s}+\psi _{t}+\psi _{b} \)

where \(\psi _{s},\psi _{t} \), and \(\psi _{b} \) are the flux through the sides (curved surface), the top surface, and the bottom surface of the cylinder, respectively (see Figure 3.18). Since D does not have component along \(a_{\rho}, \psi _{s}= 0 \), for \(\psi _{t}, dS =\rho d\phi d\rho a_{z} \) so
\(\psi _{t}=\int\limits_{\rho =0}^{1}{}\int\limits_{\phi =0}^{2\pi }{z\rho \cos ^{2}\phi \rho d\phi d\rho }|_{z=2}=2\int\limits_{0}^{1}{\rho ^{2} d\rho }\int\limits_{0}^{2\pi }{\cos ^{2}\phi d\phi } \)
\(=2(\frac{1}{3} )\pi =\frac{2\pi }{3} \)

and for \(\psi _{b},dS=-\rho d\phi d\rho a_{z}, \) so
\(\psi _{b}=-\int\limits_{\rho =0}^{1}{}\int\limits_{\phi =0}^{2\pi }{z\rho \cos ^{2}\phi\rho d\phi d\rho }|_{z=-2}=2\int\limits_{0}^{1}{\rho ^{2}d\rho }\int\limits_{0}^{2\pi }{\cos ^{2}\phi d\phi } \)
\(=\frac{2\pi }{3} \)

Thus
\(Q=\psi =0+\frac{2\pi }{3}+\frac{2\pi }{3}=\frac{4\pi }{3} \) C

as obtained earlier.
