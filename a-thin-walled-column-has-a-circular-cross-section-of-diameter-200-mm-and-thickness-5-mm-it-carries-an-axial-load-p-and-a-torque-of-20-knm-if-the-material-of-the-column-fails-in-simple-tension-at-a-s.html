Guest Question.

A thin-walled column has a circular cross section of diameter 200 mm and thickness 5 mm. It carries an axial load P and a torque of 20 kNm. If the material of the column fails in simple tension at a stress of \(240 N / mm ^{2}\) find the maximum allowable value of P using the Tresca and von Mises theories of elastic failure.
Ans. 640 kN (Tresca), 670.6 kN (von Mises).

Admin
Solution

The direct stress due to the axial load is given by Eq. (7.1)

\(\sigma=\frac{P}{A}\) (7.1)

and is

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{P}{A}=\frac{P}{\pi \times 200 \times 5}=3.18 \times 10^{-4} P N / mm ^{2}\) (P in N)

The shear stress at any point in the wall of the cylinder is given by Eq. (11.21).

\(\tau=\frac{q}{t}=\frac{T}{2 A t}\) (11.21)

That is

\(\tau_{ xy }=\frac{T}{2 A t}=\frac{20 \times 10^{6}}{2 \times\left(\pi \times 200^{2} / 4\right) \times 5}=63.6 N / mm ^{2}\)

Since P is a compressive load (the structural member is a column) \(\sigma_{x}\) is negative and also, since \(\sigma_{y}=0\), it is clear from Eqs (14.8) and (14.9)

\(\sigma_{ I }=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2}+\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right)^{2}+4 \tau_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.8)

\(\sigma_{ II }=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2}-\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right)^{2}+4 \tau_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.9)

that \(\sigma_{ I }\) will be positive and algebraically greater than \(\sigma_{ II }\), which is negative. The form of the Tresca theory given by Eq. (14.42)

\(\sigma_{ I }-\sigma_{ II }=\sigma_{ Y }\) (14.42)

therefore applies. Then

\(\sigma_{ I }-\sigma_{ II }=\sigma_{ Y }=\sqrt{\left[\left(3.18 \times 10^{-4} P\right)^{2}+4 \times 63.6^{2}\right]}=240\)

which gives
P = 640 000 N = 640 kN
The value of P given by the von Mises theory, Eq. (14.55)

\(\sigma_{ I }^{2}+\sigma_{ II }^{2}-\sigma_{ I } \sigma_{ II }=\sigma_{ Y }^{2}\) (14.55)

is most easily obtained by reducing Eqs (14.8) and (14.9) to the form

\(\sigma_{ I }= A + B , \quad \sigma_{ II }= A – B\)

Substituting in Eq. (14.55) and simplifying gives

\(A ^{2}+3 B ^{2}=\sigma_{ Y }^{2}\) (i)

Now, substituting for \(A=3.18 \times 10^{-4} P / 2\) and \(B =(1 / 2) \sqrt{\left[\left(3.18 \times 10^{-4} P\right)^{2}+4 \times 63.6^{2}\right]}\) in Eq. (i) gives

P = 670 600 N = 670.6 kN
