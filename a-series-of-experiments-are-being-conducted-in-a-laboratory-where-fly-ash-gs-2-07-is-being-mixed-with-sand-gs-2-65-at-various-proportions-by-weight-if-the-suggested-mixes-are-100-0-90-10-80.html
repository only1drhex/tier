Guest Question.

A series of experiments are being conducted in a laboratory where fly ash (\(G_{s}\) = 2.07) is being mixed with sand (\(G_{s}\) = 2.65) at various proportions by weight. If the suggested mixes are 100/0, 90/10, 80/20…10/90, and 0/100, compute the average values of the specific gravities for all the mixes. Show the results graphically and in tabular form.

Admin
Solution

Let’s show here a specimen calculation for a 70/30 mix, which contains 70% fly ash and 30% sand by weight. Let’s consider 700 g of fly ash and 300 g of sand.

 

\(\text { Volume of fly ash }=700 / 2.07=338.2 cm ^{3}\)

 

\(\text { Volume of sand }=300 / 2.65=113.2 cm ^{3}\)

 

\(\text { Total mass }=1000 g\)

 

\(\text { Total volume }=338.2+113.2=451.4 cm ^{3}\)

 

\(\therefore \text { Density }=1000 / 451.4=2.22 g / cm ^{3} \rightarrow G_{s}=2.22\)
