Guest Question.

The data in have come to the designer in the histographic form of the first two columns of Table 20–4. Using the data in this form, find the mean \(\overline{x}\), standard deviation \(s_{x}\) , and the coefficient of variation \(C_{x}\) .

Table 20–4
Grouped Data of Ultimate Tensile Strength from Nine Tensile Test Specimens from a Shipment of 1030 Hot-Rolled Steel Barstock

Extension Class Frequency f Class Midpoint x,kpsi
\(fx^{2}\) \(fx\)
8 064.50 127 2 63.5
8 844.50 133 2 66.5
14 480.75 208.5 3 69.5
10 513.50 145 2 72.5
41 912.25 613.5 ∑ 9

 

The data in Table 20– 4 have been extended to provide \(\sum{f_{i} x_{i}}\) and \( \sum{f_{i} x^{2}_{i}}\) .

Admin
Solution

From Eq. (20–9),

\(\overline{x} =\frac{1}{N} \sum\limits_{i=1}^{k}{f_{i} x_{i}}\) (20-9)

\(\overline{x} =\frac{1}{N} \sum\limits_{i=1}^{4}{f_{i} x_{i}} =\frac{1}{9}(613.5) = 68.17\) kpsi

From Eq. (20–10),

\(s_{x} =\sqrt{ \frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{k}{f_{i} x^{2}_{i}} −[ (\sum\limits_{i=1}^{k}{f_{i} x^{2}_{i}})^{2}/N]}{N − 1}} =\sqrt{\frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{k}{f_{i} x^{2}_{i}}− N \overline{x}^{2}}{N − 1}}\) (20–10)

\(s_{x} =\sqrt{\frac{41 912.25 − 613.5^{2}/9}{9 − 1}} = 3.391\) kpsi

From Eq. (20–12),

\(C_{x} =\frac{s_{x}}{\overline{x}}\) (20-12)

\(C_{x} =\frac{s_{x}}{\overline{x}} =\frac{3.391}{68.17} = 0.0497\)

Note the small changes in \(\overline{x}, s_{x} , and C_{x}\) due to small changes in the summation terms.
