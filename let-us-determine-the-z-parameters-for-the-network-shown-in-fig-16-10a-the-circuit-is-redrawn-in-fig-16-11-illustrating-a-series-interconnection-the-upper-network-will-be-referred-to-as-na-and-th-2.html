Guest Question.

Let us determine the Z parameters for the network shown in Fig. 16.10a. The circuit is redrawn in Fig. 16.11, illustrating a series interconnection. The upper network will be referred to as \(N_a\), and the lower network as \(N_b\).

Admin
Solution

The Z parameters for \(N_{a}\) are

 

\(\begin{array}{ll}\mathrm{z}_{11 a}=\frac{2-2 j}{3-2 j} \Omega & \mathrm{z}_{12 a}=\frac{2}{3-2 j} \Omega \\\\\mathrm{z}_{21 a}=\frac{2}{3-2 j} \Omega & \mathrm{z}_{22 a}=\frac{2-4 j}{3-2 j} \Omega\end{array}\)

 

and the Z parameters for \(N_{b}\) are

 

\(\mathrm{z}_{11 b}=\mathrm{z}_{12 b}=\mathrm{z}_{21 b}=\mathrm{z}_{22 b}=1 \Omega\)

 

Hence the Z parameters for the total network are

 

\(\begin{array}{ll}\mathbf{z}_{11}=\frac{5-4 j}{3-2 j} \Omega & \mathbf{z}_{12}=\frac{5-2 j}{3-2 j} \Omega \\\\\mathbf{z}_{21}=\frac{5-2 j}{3-2 j} \Omega & \mathbf{z}_{22}=\frac{5-6 j}{3-2 j} \Omega\end{array}\)

 

We could easily check these results against those obtained in by applying the conversion formulas in Table 16.1.

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}\frac{z_{22}}{\Delta_{z}} & \frac{-\mathbf{z}_{12}}{\Delta_{z}} \\\frac{-\mathbf{z}_{21}}{\Delta_{z}} & \frac{\mathbf{z}_{11}}{\Delta_{z}}\end{array}\right]\) \(\left[\begin{array}{ll}y_{11} & y_{12} \\y_{21} & y_{22}\end{array}\right]\) \(\left[\begin{array}{cc}\frac{\text { D }}{\text { B }} & \frac{-\Delta_{T}}{\text { B }} \\-\frac{1}{B} & \frac{\text { A }}{\text { B }}\end{array}\right]\) \(\left[\begin{array}{ll}\frac{1}{\mathbf{h}_{11}} & \frac{-\mathbf{h}_{12}}{\mathbf{h}_{11}} \\\frac{\mathbf{h}_{21}}{\mathbf{h}_{11}} & \frac{\Delta_{H}}{\mathbf{h}_{11}}\end{array}\right]\)
