\(D_{10}( mm )\) 0.2 0.73 0.08
\(D_{30}( mm )\) 1.8 1.1 0.3

\(C_{c}\) 2.2 1.1 0.25

% fines 6.1 0 9

 

With 6.1% fines, Soil A would be classified as a coarse-grained soil with dual symbols. Since the fines have low dry strength, they are silty. It can be classified as well-graded, silty, sandy gravel with a symbol of GW-GM.

Soil B is uniformly graded sand, with all grains in the range of 0.5â€“3.0 mm. It can be classified as uniformly graded sand with a symbol of SP.

Soil C is a gap-graded soil that has no grains present in the size range of 0.5â€“2.0 mm. With 9% fines, it requires a dual symbol. PI and LL values plot above the A-line in Casagrandeâ€™s PI-LL chart, implying that the fines are clayey. Therefore, the soil can be classified as gap-graded, clayey gravelly sand with a symbol of SP-SC.
