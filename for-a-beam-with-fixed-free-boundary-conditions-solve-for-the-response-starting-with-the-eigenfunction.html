Guest Question.

For a beam with fixed-free boundary conditions, solve for the response starting with the eigenfunction.

Admin
Solution

We begin with Equation 7.86,

 

\(\begin{aligned}Y(x) &=C_{1} \sin \beta x+C_{2} \cos \beta x \\&+C_{3} \sinh \beta x+C_{4} \cosh \beta x .\end{aligned}\)

 

For a beam fixed at \(x\) = 0 and free at\(x=L\), the boundary conditions for the eigenfunctions are

 

\(\begin{aligned}Y(0)=0, & Y^{\prime}(0)=0 \\Y^{\prime \prime}(L)=0, & Y^{\prime \prime \prime}(L)=0\end{aligned}\)

 

At the fixed end, the displacement and slope equal zero, and at the free end, the moment and shear force equal zero. Applying the boundary conditions for \(x\) = 0, we find

 

\(\begin{aligned}&C_{2}+C_{4}=0 \\&C_{1}+C_{3}=0\end{aligned}\)

 

so that the eigenfunction is reduced to

 

\(\begin{aligned}Y(x) &=C_{1}(\sin \beta x-\sinh \beta x) \\&+C_{2}(\cos \beta x-\cosh \beta x) .\end{aligned}\) (7.93)

 

Applying the two conditions at \(x=L\) results in

 

\(C_{1}(\sin \beta L+\sinh \beta L)+C_{2}(\cos \beta L+\cosh \beta L)=0\) (7.94)

 

\(C_{1}(\cos \beta L+\cosh \beta L)-C_{2}(\sin \beta L-\sinh \beta L)=0\) (7.95)

 

Solving Equation 7.95 for \(C_{2}\) in terms of \(C_{1}\) and substituting the result into Equation 7.93 yields

 

\(\begin{aligned}Y(x) &=\frac{C_{1}}{\sin \beta L-\sinh \beta L} \\& \times[(\sin \beta L-\sinh \beta L)(\sin \beta x-\sinh \beta x)\\&+(\cos \beta L+\cosh \beta L)(\cos \beta x-\cosh \beta x)],\end{aligned}\) (7.96)

 

where \(C_{1}\) is eventually incorporated into the complete solution.36
To establish the frequencies that the beam can sustain in free vibration, we rewrite Equations 7.94 and 7.95 in matrix-vector form,

 

\(\begin{gathered}{\left[\begin{array}{cc}(\sin \beta L+\sinh \beta L) & (\cos \beta L+\cosh \beta L) \\(\cos \beta L+\cosh \beta L) & -(\sin \beta L-\sinh \beta L)\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}C_{1} \\C_{2}\end{array}\right\}} \\=\left\{\begin{array}{l}0 \\0\end{array}\right\} .\end{gathered}\)

 

The determinant of the matrix must be equal to zero for a nontrivial solution to exist, that is,

 

\(\begin{gathered}(\sin \beta L+\sinh \beta L)(\sin \beta L-\sinh \beta L) \\+(\cos \beta L+\cosh \beta L)^{2}=0 .\end{gathered}\)

 

This is the characteristic equation and it simplifies to the transcendental equation

 

\(\cos \beta L \cosh \beta L=-1,\)

 

which has no analytic solution and must be solved numerically for \(\beta L\). We find the first three roots to be \(\beta L\) = 1.875, 4.694, and 7.855 and, using Equation 7.89, the natural frequencies are

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\omega_{1}=(1.875)^{2} \sqrt{\frac{E I}{m L^{4}}} \\&\omega_{2}=(4.694)^{2} \sqrt{\frac{E I}{m L^{4}}} \\&\omega_{3}=(7.855)^{2} \sqrt{\frac{E I}{m L^{4}}} .\end{aligned}\)

 

The first three modes of Equation 7.96 are plotted in Figure 7.24 for \(L\) = 120 in, assuming \(C_{1}\) = 1. For example, using Equation 7.96, for \(\beta L\) = 1.875, the first mode

 

is given by

 

\(\begin{aligned}Y_{1}(x) &=\frac{1}{-2.230}[-2.230(\sin \beta x-\sinh \beta x)\\&+3.037(\cos \beta x-\cosh \beta x)]\end{aligned}\)

 

where \(\beta=1.875 / 120=0.0156 \text { in }^{-1}\). Based on Equation
7.78, each modal response is given by

 

\(\begin{aligned}y_{r}(x, t) &=B_{r} Y_{r}(x) F_{r}(t) \\&=\bar{C}_{r} Y_{r}(x) \cos \left(\omega_{r} t-\phi_{r}\right)\end{aligned}\) (7.97)

 

where \(\bar{C}_{r}=A B_{r}\). The complete solution is then the
sum

 

\(y(x, t)=\sum_{r=1}^{\infty} y_{r}(x, t)\)

 

where \(\bar{C}_{r}\) and \(\phi_{r}\) are determined by satisfying the initial conditions. Note that having assumed \(C_{1}\) = 1 there was no need to normalize the mode.
