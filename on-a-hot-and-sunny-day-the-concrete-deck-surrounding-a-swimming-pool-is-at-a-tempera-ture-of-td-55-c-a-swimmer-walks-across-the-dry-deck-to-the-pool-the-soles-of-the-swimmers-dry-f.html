Guest Question.

On a hot and sunny day, the concrete deck surrounding a swimming pool is at a tempera-ture of \(T_{d}\) = 55 °C. A swimmer walks across the dry deck to the pool. The soles of the swimmer’s dry feet are characterized by an \(L_{sf}\) = 3-mm-thick skin/fat layer of thermal con-ductivity \(k_{sf}\) = 0.3 W/m. K. Consider two types of concrete decking; (i) a dense stone mix and (ii) a lightweight aggregate characterized by density, specific heat, and thermal conduc-tivity of \(ρ_{lw} = 1495 kg/m^{3}, c_{p,lw} = 880 J/kg . K,\) and \(k_{lw} = 0.28 W/m. K\), respectively. The density and specific heat of the skin/fat layer may be approximated to be those of liquid water, and the skin/fat layer is at an initial temperature of \(T_{sf,i}\) = 37 °C. What is the tempera-ture of the bottom of the swimmer’s feet after an elapsed time of t = 1 s?

Admin
Solution

Known: Concrete temperature, initial foot temperature, and thickness of skin/fat layer on the sole of the foot. Skin/fat and lightweight aggregate concrete properties.

Find: The temperature of the bottom of the swimmer’s feet after 1 s.

Assumptions:
1. One-dimensional conduction in the x-direction.
2. Constant and uniform properties.
3. Negligible contact resistance.

Properties: Table A.3 stone mix concrete \((T = 300 K): ρ_{sm} = 2300 kg/m^{3}, k_{sm} = 1.4 W/m.K, c_{sm} = 880 J/kg . K\).

Analysis: If the skin/fat layer and the deck are both semi-infinite media, from Equation 5.66 \( T_{s} = \frac{( k\rho c)^{1/2}_{A} T_{A,i} + ( k\rho c)^{1/2}_{B} T_{B,i}}{( k\rho c)^{1/2}_{A} + ( k\rho c)^{1/2}_{B}} \) the surface temperature \(T_{s}\) is constant when the swimmer’s foot is in contact with the deck. For the lightweight aggregate concrete decking, the thermal penetration depth at t = 1 s is

\(\delta _{p,lw} =2.3 \sqrt{\alpha _{lw} t} = 2.3 \sqrt{\frac{k_{lw} t}{\rho _{lw} c_{lw} } } =2.3 \sqrt{\frac{0.28 W/m. K \times 1s}{1495 kg/m^{3} \times 880 J/kg . K } } =1.06 \times 10^{-3} m =1.06 mm \)

Since the thermal penetration depth is relatively small, it is reasonable to assume that the light-weight aggregate deck behaves as a semi-infinite medium. Similarly, the thermal penetration depth in the stone mix concrete is \(\delta _{p,sm}\) = 1.91 mm, and the thermal penetration depth associ-ated with the skin/fat layer of the foot is \(\delta _{p,sf} \)= 0.62 mm. Hence, it is reasonable to assume that the stone mix concrete deck responds as a semi-infinite medium, and, since \(\delta _{p,sf} < L_{sf}\), it is also correct to assume that the skin/fat layer behaves as a semi-infinite medium. Therefore, Equa-tion 5.66 \( T_{s} = \frac{( k\rho c)^{1/2}_{A} T_{A,i} + ( k\rho c)^{1/2}_{B} T_{B,i}}{( k\rho c)^{1/2}_{A} + ( k\rho c)^{1/2}_{B}} \) may be used to determine the surface temperature of the swimmer’s foot for exposure to the two types of concrete decking. For the lightweight aggregate,

\( T_{s ,lw} = \frac{( k\rho c)^{1/2}_{lw} T_{d,i} + ( k\rho c)^{1/2}_{sf} T_{sf,i}}{( k\rho c)^{1/2}_{lw} + ( k\rho c)^{1/2}_{sf}} \)

 

\( T_{s,lw} = \frac{\left[(0.28 W/m . K \times 1495 kg/m^{3} \times \times 880 J/kg . K)^{1/2}\times 55°C + (0.3 W/m . K \times 993 kg/m^{3} \times 4178 J/kg . K)^{1/2} \times 37°C \right] }{\left[(0.28 W/m . K \times 1495 kg/m^{3} \times \times 880 J/kg . K)^{1/2} + (0.3 W/m . K \times 993 kg/m^{3} \times 4178 J/kg . K)^{1/2} \right] } = 43.3 °C \)

Repeating the calculation for the stone mix concrete gives \(T_{s,sm} = 47.8 °C.\)

Comments:
1. The lightweight aggregate concrete feels cooler to the swimmer, relative to the stone mix concrete. Specifically, the temperature rise from the initial skin/fat temperature that is associated with the stone mix concrete is \(ΔT_{sm} = T_{sm} – T_{sf,i} = 47.8 °C – 37 °C = 10.8 °C,\) whereas the temperature rise associated with the lightweight aggregate is \(ΔT_{lw} = T_{lw} –T_{sf,i} = 43.3 °C – 37 °C = 6.3 °C.\)
2. The thermal penetration depths associated with an exposure time of t = 1 s are small. Stones and air pockets within the concrete may be of the same size as the thermal penetra-tion depth, making the uniform property assumption somewhat questionable. The pre-dicted foot temperatures should be viewed as representative values.
