Guest Question.

Use the matrix method to derive expressions for the node voltages \(V_1\) and \(V_2\) in the circuit in Fig. A.1.

Admin
Solution

Summing the currents away from nodes 1 and 2 generates the following set of equations:

\(\frac{V_1 – V_g}{R} +V_1sC + (V_1 – V_2)sC = 0,\)

(A.92)

\(\frac{V_2}{R} +(V_2 – V_1)sC+(V_2 – Vg)sC = 0.\)

Letting G = 1/R and collecting the coefficients of \(V_1\) and \(V_2\) gives us

\((G + 2sC)V_1 – sCV_2 = GV_g,\)

\(-sCV_1 + (G + 2sC)V_2 = sCV_g.\) (A.93)

Writing Eq. A.93 in matrix notation yields

A V = I , (A.94)

where

\( A=\left [ \begin{matrix} G + 2sC & -sC \\ -sC & G + 2sC \end{matrix} \right ] ,\)

\(V=\left [ \begin{matrix} V_{1} \\ V_{2} \end{matrix} \right ]\) , and \(I=\left [ \begin{matrix} GV_{g} \\ sCV_{g} \end{matrix} \right ].\)

It follows from Eq. A.94 that

\(V = A^{-1}I.\) (A.95)

As before, we find the inverse of the coefficient matrix by first finding the adjoint of A and the determinant of A.The cofactors of A are

\(Δ_{11}= (-1)^2[G + 2sC] = G + 2sC,\)

\(Δ_{12}=(-1)^3(-sC) = sC,\)

\(Δ_{21}=(-1)^3(-sC) = sC,\)

\(Δ_{22}=(-1)^4[G + 2sC] = G + 2sC.\)

The cofactor matrix is

\( B=\left [ \begin{matrix}G + 2sC & sC \\ sC & G + 2sC \end{matrix} \right ] ,\) (A.96)

and therefore the adjoint of the coefficient matrix is

adj \( A=B^T=\left [ \begin{matrix}G + 2sC & sC \\ sC & G + 2sC \end{matrix} \right ] \) . (A.97)

The determinant of A is

det \( A=\left | \begin{matrix} G + 2sC & sC \\ sC & G + 2sC \end{matrix} \right |=G^2 + 4sCG + 3s^2C^2. \)

(A.98)

The inverse of the coefficient matrix is

\(A^{-1}=\frac{\left [ \begin{matrix}G + 2sC & sC \\ sC & G + 2sC \end{matrix} \right ]}{(G^2 + 4sCG + 3s^2C^2)} .\) (A.99)

It follows from Eq. A.95 that

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} V_{1} \\ V_{2} \end{matrix} \right ] =\frac{\left [ \begin{matrix}G + 2sC & sC \\ sC & G + 2sC \end{matrix} \right ]\left [ \begin{matrix} GV_{g} \\ sCV_{g} \end{matrix} \right ] }{(G^2 + 4sCG + 3s^2C^2)} .\) (A.100)

Carrying out the matrix multiplication called for in Eq. A.100 gives

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} V_{1} \\ V_{2} \end{matrix} \right ] =\frac{1}{(G^2 + 4sCG + 3s^2C^2)} \left [ \begin{matrix} (G^2 + 2sCG + s^2C^2)V_g \\ (2sCG + 2s^2C^2)V_g \end{matrix} \right ] .\)

(A.101)

Now the expressions for \(V_1\) and \(V_2\) can be written directly from Eq. A.101; thus

\(V_1=\frac{(G^2 + 2sCG + s^2C^2)V_g}{(G^2 + 4sCG + 3s^2C^2)}, \) (A.102)

and

\(V_2=\frac{2(sCG + s^2C^2)V_g}{(G^2 + 4sCG + 3s^2C^2)}. \) (A.103)
