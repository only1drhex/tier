Guest Question.

We wish to determine the exponential Fourier series for the periodic voltage waveform shown in Fig. 15.3.

Admin
Solution

The Fourier coefficients are determined using Eq. (15.9) by integrating over one complete period of the waveform,

Eq. (15.9): \(c_{n} = \frac{1}{T_{0}} \int_{t_{1}}^{t_{1}+T_{0}}{f(t)e^{-jnω_{0}t} dt}\)

\(c_{n} = \frac{1}{T} \int_{-T/2}^{T/2}{f(t)e^{-jnω_{0}t} dt}\)
= \( \frac{1}{T} \int_{-T/2}^{-T/4} -Ve^{-jnω_{0} t} dt \)
\(+\int_{-T/4}^{T/4}Ve^{-jnω_{0}t} dt + \int_{T/4}^{T/2} -Ve^{-jnω_{0} t} dt \)

= \(\frac{V}{jnω_{0}T} \Big[ +e^{-jnω_{0}t} \mid^{-T/4}_{-T/2}- e^{-jnω_{0}t} \mid^{T/4}_{-T/4}+ e^{-jnω_{0}t} \mid^{T/2}_{T/4} \Big] \)

= \(\frac{V}{jnω_{0}T}(2e^{jn \pi /2} – 2e^{-jn \pi /2} + e^{-jn \pi} – e^{+jn \pi})\)
= \(\frac{V}{nω_{0}T} \Big[ 4 sin\frac{n \pi}{2} – 2 sin (n \pi) \Big] \)
= 0 for n even
= \(\frac{2V}{n \pi}sin\frac{n \pi}{2}\) for n odd

\(c_{0}\) corresponds to the average value of the waveform. This term can be evaluated using the original equation for \(c_{n}\). Therefore,

\(c_{n}= \frac{1}{T} \int_{-\frac{T}{2}}^{\frac{T}{2}}{v(t) dt}\)

= \(\frac{1}{T} \Big[ \int_{-\frac{T}{2}}^{-\frac{T}{4}}- V dt + \int_{-\frac{T}{4}}^{\frac{T}{4}}V dt +\int_{\frac{T}{4}}^{\frac{T}{2}}- V dt \Big] \)

= \( \frac{1}{T} \Big[ -\frac{VT}{4} +\frac{VT}{2} – \frac{VT}{4} \Big] \) = 0

Therefore,

v(t) = \(\sum\limits_{\begin{matrix}n= -\infty \\ n\neq 0\\ n\,odd\end{matrix}}^{\infty}{\frac{2V}{n \pi} sin \frac{n \pi}{2}e^{-jnω_{0}t}}\)

This equation can be written as

v(t) = \(\sum\limits_{\begin{matrix}n= 1\\ n\,odd\end{matrix}}^{\infty}{\frac{2V}{n \pi} sin \frac{n \pi}{2}e^{jnω_{0}t}} + \sum\limits_{\begin{matrix}n= -1\\ n\,odd\end{matrix}}^{-\infty}{\frac{2V}{n \pi} sin \frac{n \pi}{2}e^{jnω_{0}t}}\)

= \(\sum\limits_{\begin{matrix}n= 1\\ n\,odd\end{matrix}}^{\infty}{(\frac{2V}{n \pi} sin \frac{n \pi}{2})e^{jnω_{0}t}} + (\frac{2V}{n \pi} sin \frac{n \pi}{2})^{*}e^{-jnω_{0}t}\)

Since a number plus its complex conjugate is equal to two times the real part of the number, v(t) can be written as

v(t) = \(\sum\limits_{\begin{matrix}n=1\\ n\,odd\end{matrix}}^{\infty} 2 Re({\frac{2V}{n \pi} sin \frac{n \pi}{2}e^{-jnω_{0}t}})\)

or

v(t) = \(\sum\limits_{\begin{matrix}n= 1\\ n\,odd\end{matrix}}^{\infty}{\frac{4V}{n \pi} sin \frac{n \pi}{2} cos \, nω_{0}t}\)

Note that this same result could have been obtained by integrating over the interval -T/4 to 3T/4.
