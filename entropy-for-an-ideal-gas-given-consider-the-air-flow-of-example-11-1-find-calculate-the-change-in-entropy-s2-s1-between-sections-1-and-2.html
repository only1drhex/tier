Entropy for an Ideal Gas

GIVEN Consider the air flow of .

FIND Calculate the change in entropy, \(s_{2}-s_{1}\), between sections (1) and (2).

Admin
Solution

Assuming that the flowing air in Fig. E11.1 behaves as an ideal gas, we can calculate the entropy change between sections by using either Eq. 11.21 or Eq. 11.22. We use both to demonstrate that the same result is obtained either way.

From Eq. 11.21,

 

\(s_{2}-s_{1}=c_{v} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}+R \ln \frac{\rho_{1}}{\rho_{2}}\) (11.21)

 

\(s_{2}-s_{1}=c_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}\) (11.22)

 

\(s_{2}-s_{1}=c_{v} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}+R \ln \frac{\rho_{1}}{\rho_{2}}\) (1)

 

To evaluate \(s_{2}-s_{1}\) from Eq. 1 we need the density ratio, \(\rho_{1} / \rho_{2}\), which can be obtained from the ideal gas equation of state (Eq. 11.1) as

 

\(\rho=\frac{p}{R T}\) (11.1)

 

\(\frac{\rho_{1}}{\rho_{2}}=\left(\frac{p_{1}}{T_{1}}\right)\left(\frac{T_{2}}{p_{2}}\right)\) (2)

 

and thus from Eqs. 1 and 2,

 

\(s_{2}-s_{1}=c_{v} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}+R \ln \left[\left(\frac{p_{1}}{T_{1}}\right)\left(\frac{T_{2}}{p_{2}}\right)\right]\) (3)

 

By substituting values already identified in the problem statement and solution into Eq. 3 with

 

\(\left(\frac{p_{1}}{T_{1}}\right)\left(\frac{T_{2}}{p_{2}}\right)=\left(\frac{100 psia }{540^{\circ} R }\right)\left(\frac{453^{\circ} R }{18.4 psia }\right)=4.56\)

 

we get

 

\(\begin{aligned}s_{2}-s_{1}=& {\left[133( ft \cdot lb ) /\left(1 bm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\right] \ln \left(\frac{453^{\circ} R }{540^{\circ} R }\right) } \\&+\left[53.3( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\right] \ln 4.56\end{aligned}\)

 

or

 

\(s_{2}-s_{1}=57.5( ft \cdot lb ) /\left(1 bm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\)

 

From Eq. 11.22,

 

\(s_{2}-s_{1}=c_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{p_{2}}{p_{1}}\) (4)

 

By substituting known values into Eq. 4 we obtain

 

\(\begin{aligned}s_{2}-s_{1}=& {\left[186( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\right] \ln \left(\frac{453{ }^{\circ} R }{540{ }^{\circ} R }\right) } \\&-\left[53.3( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\right] \ln \left(\frac{18.4 psia }{100 psia }\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

or

 

\(s_{2}-s_{1}=57.5( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\)

 

COMMENT As anticipated, both Eqs. 11.21 and 11.22 yield the same result for the entropy change, \(s_{2}-s_{1}\).

Note that since the ideal gas equation of state was used in the derivation of the entropy difference equations, both the pressures and temperatures used must be absolute.
