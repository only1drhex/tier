Guest Question.

In a medium characterized by σ= 0, \(μ= μ_{0} ,ε=4ε_{0}\),and

\(E=20\sin (10^{8}t-\beta z)a_{y}\) V/m

calculate β and H.

Admin
Solution

This problem can be solved directly in time domain or by using phasors. As in we find {3 and H by making E and H satisfy Maxwell’s four equations.
Method 1 (time domain):
Let us solve this problem the harder way- in time domain. It is evident that Gauss’s law for electric fields is satisfied; that is,

\(\triangledown .E=\frac{\partial E_{y}}{\partial y}=0\)

From Faraday’s law,
\(\triangledown \times E=-\mu \frac{\partial H}{\partial t}\rightarrow H=-\frac{1}{\mu }\int{(\triangledown \times E)dt}\)
But
\(\triangledown \times E=\begin{vmatrix} a_{x} & a_{y} & a_{z} \\ \frac{\partial }{\partial x} & \frac{\partial}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \\ 0& E_{y} & 0 \end{vmatrix} =-\frac{\partial E_{y}}{\partial z}a_{x}+\frac{\partial E_{y}}{\partial x} a_{z}\)
\(=20\beta \cos (10^{8}t-\beta z)a_{x}+0\)
Hence,
\(H=-\frac{20\beta }{\mu }\int{\cos (10^{8}t-\beta z)dt a_{x}}\)
\(=-\frac{20\beta }{\mu 10^{8}}\sin (10^{8}t-\beta z) a_{x} \)(9.8.1)

It is readily verified that
\(\triangledown .H=\frac{\partial H_{x}}{\partial x}=0\)

showing that Gauss’s law for magnetic fields is satisfied. Lastly, from Ampere’s law
\(\triangledown \times H=\sigma E+\varepsilon \frac{\partial E}{\partial t}\rightarrow E=\frac{1}{\varepsilon }\int{(\triangledown \times H)dt}\) (9.8.2)
because \(\sigma =0.\)
But
\(\triangledown \times H=\begin{vmatrix} a_{x} & a_{y} & a_{z} \\ \frac{\partial }{\partial x} & \frac{\partial}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \\ H_{x}& 0 & 0 \end{vmatrix} =\frac{\partial H_{x}}{\partial z}a_{y}-\frac{\partial H_{x}}{\partial y} a_{z}\)

\(=\frac{20\beta ^{2}}{\mu 10^{8}}\cos (10^{8}t-\beta z)dt a_{y}+0\)

where H in eq. (9.8.1) has been substituted. Thus eq. (9.8.2) becomes
\(E=\frac{20\beta ^{2}}{\mu \varepsilon 10^{8}}\int{\cos (10^{8}t-\beta z)dt a_{y}}\)
\(=\frac{20\beta ^{2}}{\mu \varepsilon 10^{16}}\sin (10^{8}t-\beta z) a_{y}\)

Comparing this with the given E, we have
\(\frac{20\beta ^{2}}{\mu \varepsilon 10^{16}}=20\)
or
\(\beta =\pm 10^{8}\sqrt{\mu \varepsilon }=\pm 10^{8}\sqrt{\mu _{0}.4\varepsilon _{0}}=\pm \frac{10^{8}(2)}{3\times 10^{8}}\)
\(=\pm \frac{2}{3}\)

The β would be negative only in metamaterials, For an isotropic medium,\(β=\frac{2}{3}.\)

From eq. (9.8.1 ),
\(H=+\frac{20(2/3)}{4\pi .10^{-7}(10^{8})}\sin (10^{8}t+\frac{2z}{3} )a_{x}\)
or

\(H=+\frac{1}{3\pi }\sin (10^{8}t+\frac{2z}{3} )a_{x}\) A/m

Method 2 (using phasors):
\(E=Im(E_{s}e^{j\omega t})\rightarrow E_{s}=20 e^{-j\beta z}a_{y}\) (9.8.3)
where \( \omega =10^{8}.\)
Again
\(\triangledown .E_{s}=\frac{\partial E_{ys}}{\partial y}=0\)
\(\triangledown \times E_{s}=-j\omega \mu H_{s}\rightarrow H_{s}=\frac{\triangledown \times E_{s}}{-j\omega \mu }\)
or
\(H_{s}=\frac{1}{-j\omega \mu }[-\frac{\partial E_{ys}}{\partial z}a_{x}]=-\frac{20\beta }{\omega \mu }e^{-j\beta z}a_{x}\) (9.8.4)

Notice that \(∇.H_{s}= 0\) is satisfied.

\(\triangledown \times H_{s}=-j\omega \varepsilon E_{s}\rightarrow E_{s}=\frac{\triangledown \times H_{s}}{j\omega \varepsilon }\) (9.8.5)

Substituting \(H_{s}\) in eq. (9.8.4) into eq. (9.8.5) gives
\(E_{s}=\frac{1}{j\omega \varepsilon }\frac{\partial H_{xs}}{\partial z}a_{y}=\frac{20\beta ^{2}e^{-j\beta z}}{\omega ^{2}\mu \varepsilon }a_{y}\)

Comparing this with the given \(E_{s}\) in eq. (9.8.3), we have
\(20=\frac{20\beta ^{2}}{\omega ^{2}\mu \varepsilon}\)
or
\(\beta =+\omega \sqrt{\mu \varepsilon }=+\frac{2}{3}\)

as obtained before. From eq. (9.8.4),
\(H_{s}=+\frac{20(2/3)e^{+j\beta z}}{10^{8}(4\pi \times 10^{-7})}a_{x}=+\frac{1}{3\pi }e^{+j\beta z}a_{x}\)
\(H=Im(H_{s}e^{j\omega t})\)
\(=\pm \frac{1}{3\pi }\sin (10^{8}t+\beta z)a_{x}\) A/m

as obtained before. It should be noticed that working with phasors is considerably simpler than working directly in time domain. Also, notice that we have used

\(A=Im(A_{s}e^{j\omega t})\)

because the given E is in sine form and not cosine. If we had used

\(A=Re(A_{s}e^{j\omega t})\)

sine would been expressed in terms of cosine, and eq. (9.8.3) would have been

\(E=20\cos (10^{8}t-\beta z-90^{\circ })a_{y}=Re(E_{s}e^{j\omega t})\)
or
\(E_{s}=20e^{-j\beta z-j90^{\circ }}a_{y}=-j20e^{-j\beta z}a_{y}\)

and we follow the same procedure.
