Guest Question.

Two 50 mm diameter undisturbed samples A and B are taken from the clay at the depths shown. It is expected that sample A is slightly overconsolidated and B is normally consolidated. For the clay LL = 65 and PL = 27:

a. Estimate the undrained shear strength of sample B

b. Assuming OCR = 2 for sample A, estimate its undrained shear strength

c. If an unconfined compression test is carried out on sample B, what would be the failure load?

Admin
Solution

a. For sample B, using Equation 9.20:

 

\(\frac{c_{u}}{\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}}=0.0037 PI +0.11\) (9.20)

 

\(\frac{c_{u}}{\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}}=0.0037 \times 38+0.11=0.251\)

 

At this depth:

 

\(\begin{array}{c}\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}=3 \times 17+9 \times(19-9.81)=133.7 kPa \\\therefore c_{u}=0.251 \times 133.7=34 kPa\end{array}\)

 

b.

 

\(\left(c_{u} / \sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}\right)_{ NC }=0.251\)

 

From Equation 9.21:

 

\(\left(\frac{c_{u}}{\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}}\right)_{O C}=\left(\frac{c_{u}}{\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}}\right)_{N C}( OCR )^{0.8}\) (9.21)

 

\(\left(c_{u} / \sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}\right)_{ OC }=0.251 \times 2^{0.8}=0.437\)

 

At the depth of sample A:

 

\(\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}=3 \times 17+2 \times(19-9.81)=69.4 kPa\)

 

∴ Undrained shear strength of sample A, \(c_{u}=0.437 \times 69.4=30 kPa\)

 

c. Unconfined compressive strength of sample A, \(q_{u}=2 c_{u}=60 kPa\)

 

∴ Failure load \(=60 \times\left(\pi \times 0.025^{2}\right) kN =118 N\)
