Guest Question.

Obtain the bending moment diagram for the portal frame shown in Fig. 16.47(a). The flexural rigidity of the horizontal member BC is 2EI while that of the vertical members AB and CD is EI.

Admin
Solution

First we shall determine the end moments in the members assuming that the frame does not sway. The corresponding FEMs are found using the results in Table 16.6

and are as follows:

\(M_{ AB }^{ F }=M_{ BA }^{ F }=0 \quad M_{ CD }^{ F }=M_{ DC }^{ F }=0\)

 

\(M_{ BC }^{ F }=-\frac{4 \times 5 \times 10^{2}}{15^{2}}=-8.89 kNm\)

 

\(M_{ CB }^{ F }=+\frac{4 \times 10 \times 5^{2}}{15^{2}}=+4.44 kNm\)

The DFs are

\(DF _{ BA }=\frac{K_{ BA }}{K_{ BA }+K_{ BC }}=\frac{4 E I / 10}{4 E I / 10+4 \times 2 E I / 15}=0.43\)

Hence

\(DF _{ BC }=1-0.43=0.57\)

From the symmetry of the frame, \(DF _{ CB }=0.57\) and \(DF _{ CD }=0.43\).

The no-sway moments are determined in the table below. We now assume that the frame sways by an arbitrary amount, δ, as shown in Fig. 16.47(b). Since we are ignoring the effect of axial strains, the horizontal movements of B and C are both δ. The FEMs corresponding to this sway are then (see Table 16.6)

\(M_{ AB }^{ F }=M_{ BA }^{ F }=-\frac{6 E I \delta}{10^{2}}=M_{ DC }^{ F }=M_{ CD }^{ F }\)

 

\(M_{ BC }^{ F }=M_{ CB }^{ F }=0\)

Suppose that \(\delta=100 \times 10^{2} / 6 E I\). Then

\(M_{ AB }^{ F }=M_{ BA }^{ F }=M_{ DC }^{ F }=M_{ CD }^{ F }=-100 kNm\) (a convenient value)

The DFs for the members are the same as those in the no-sway case since they are functions of the member stiffness. We now obtain the member end moments corresponding to the arbitrary sway.
