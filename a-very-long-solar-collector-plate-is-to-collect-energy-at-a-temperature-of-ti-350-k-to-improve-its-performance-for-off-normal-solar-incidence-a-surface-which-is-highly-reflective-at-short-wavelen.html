Guest Question.

A very long solar collector plate is to collect energy at a temperature of \(T_{i} = 350 K.\) To improve its performance for of F-normal solar incidence, a surface, which is highly reflective at short wavelengths, is placed next to the collector as shown in Fig. 7-1. For simplicity you may make the following assumptions: (i) The collector \(A_{1}\) is isothermal and a diffuse reflector; (ii) the mirror \(A_{2}\) is a specular reflector; (iii) the spectral properties of collector and mirror may be approximated as

\(\epsilon _{1} = 1- \rho ^{d}_{1} =\begin{cases} 0.8 & \lambda \lt \lambda _{c} = 4 \mu m \\ 0.1 & \lambda \gt \lambda _{c} \end{cases},\)
\(\epsilon _{2} = 1- \rho ^{s}_{2} =\begin{cases}0.1 & \lambda \lt \lambda _{c} , \\ 0.8 & \lambda \gt \lambda _{c}, \end{cases}\)

and (iv) heat losses from the mirror by convection as well as all losjses from the collector ends may be neglected. How much energy (per unit length) does the collector plate collect for a solar incidence angle of 30째?

Solution

From equation (7.5)

\(\begin{array}{l}\sum\limits_{j=1}^{N}{\left[\frac{\delta _{ij} }{\epsilon ^{(1)}_{j} } – \frac{\rho ^{d(1)}_{j} }{\epsilon ^{(1)}_{j}} F^{s(1)}_{i-j} \right] } q^{(1)}_{j} + H^{s}_{oi}=0, (7.5a) \\ \sum\limits_{j=1}^{N}{\left[\frac{\delta _{ij} }{\epsilon ^{(2)}_{j} } – \frac{\rho ^{d(2)}_{j} }{\epsilon ^{(2)}_{j}} F^{s(2)}_{i-j} \right] } q^{(2)}_{j} = \sum\limits_{j=1}^{N}{\left[\delta _{ij} – (1- \rho ^{s(2)}_{j}) F^{s(2)}_{i-j}\right] } E_{bj}, (7.5b) \\ q_{i}=q^{(1)}_{i}+ q^{(2)}_{i} , i= 1,2,…., N, (7.5c) \end{array}\)

we find, with \(F^{s}_{1-2} = F_{1-2} , F^{s}_{2-1}= F_{2-1} , and F^{s}_{1-1}=F^{s}_{2-2}=0\), for range (1),

\(\frac{q^{(1)}_{1} }{\epsilon ^{(1)}_{1}} + H^{s}_{o1} = 0 ,\)
\(– \left(\frac{1}{\epsilon ^{(1)}_{1}}-1 \right) F_{2-1}q^{(1)}_{1} + \frac{q^{(1)}_{2}}{\epsilon ^{(1)}_{2}} + H^{s}_{o2}=0,\)

and for range (2),

\(\frac{q^{(2)}_{1} }{\epsilon ^{(2)}_{1}} = E_{b1} – \epsilon ^{(2)}_{2} F_{1-2} E_{b2},\)
\(– \left(\frac{1}{\epsilon ^{(2)}_{1}}-1 \right) F_{2-1}q^{(2)}_{1} + \frac{q^{(2)}_{2}}{\epsilon ^{(2)}_{2}} = – F_{2-1} E_{b1} +E_{b2}.\)

Eliminating \(E_{b2}\) from the last two equations, we find

\(\left[\frac{1}{\epsilon ^{(2)}_{1}}- \left(\frac{1}{\epsilon ^{(2)}_{1}}-1 \right) \epsilon ^{(2)}_{2} F_{1-2} F_{2-1} \right] q^{(2)}_{1} + F_{1-2}q^{(2)}_{2} = (1- \epsilon ^{(2)}_{2} F_{1-2} F_{2-1}) E_{b1} .\)

Multiplying the second equation for range (1) by \(\epsilon ^{(1)}_{2} F_{1-2}\) results in

\(– \left(\frac{1}{\epsilon ^{(1)}_{1}}-1 \right) \epsilon ^{(1)}_{2} F_{1-2} F_{2-1} q^{(1)}_{1} + F_{1-2}q^{(1)}_{2} = -\epsilon ^{(1)}_{2} F_{1-2} H^{s}_{o2}.\)

Adding the last two equations and using \(q_{2}=q^{(1)}_{2}+ q^{(2)}_{2}=0\) then leads to

\(\left[\frac{1}{\epsilon ^{(2)}_{1}}- \left(\frac{1}{\epsilon ^{(2)}_{1}}-1 \right) \epsilon ^{(2)}_{2} F_{1-2} F_{2-1} \right] q^{(2)}_{1} + = \left(\frac{1}{\epsilon ^{(1)}_{1}}-1 \right) \epsilon ^{(1)}_{2} F_{1-2} F_{2-1} q^{(1)}_{1} -\epsilon ^{(1)}_{2} F_{1-2} H^{s}_{o2} + (1- \epsilon ^{(2)}_{2} F_{1-2} F_{2-1}) E_{b1},\)

or with \(q^{(1)}_{1}= – \epsilon ^{(1)}_{1}H^{s}_{o1},\)

\(q_{1}= q^{(1)}_{1}+ q^{(2)}_{1} = \frac{(1- \epsilon ^{(2)}_{2} F_{1-2} F_{2-1}) E_{b1} – (1-\epsilon ^{(1)}_{1} ) \epsilon ^{(1)}_{2} F_{1-2} F_{2-1} H^{s}_{o1} – \epsilon ^{(1)}_{2} F_{1-2} H^{s}_{o2} }{1/\epsilon ^{(2)}_{1} – (1/\epsilon ^{(3)}_{1}-1) \epsilon ^{(2)}_{2} F_{1-2} F_{2-1}} – \epsilon ^{(1)}_{1} H^{s}_{o1}\) .

From we have

\( H^{s}_{o2} = q_{sun}\sin \varphi = 1000 \times \sin 30째 = 500 W/m^{2},\)
\(H^{s}_{o1} = q_{sun}\left[\cos \varphi + \rho ^{s(1)}_{2} \sin \varphi (l_{2}/l_{1} )\right] = 1000 [\cos 30째 + 0.9\times \sin 30째 (60/80)] = 1203.5 W/m^{2}\)

With \(F_{1-2}=\frac{1}{4} , F_{2-1}=\frac{1}{3} , F_{1-2}F_{2-1}=\frac{1}{12}\) , and\(E_{b1} = 5.670\times 10^{-8}\times 350^{4} = 850.9 W/m^{2}\) , \(q_{1}\) may now be evaluated as

\( q_{1} = \frac{\left(1- \frac{0.8}{12} \right) \times 850.9 – \frac{0.2\times 0.1}{12}\times 1203.5 – \frac{0.1}{4}\times 500 }{\frac{1}{0.1} – \left(\frac{1}{0.1}-1 \right) \times \frac{0.8}{12} } – 0.8 \times 1203.5 = 82.9 – 962.8 = -880.1 W/m^{2} ,\)

or a collection efficiency of 88%! In addition, surface \(A_{2}\) remains much cooler than for the gray case (); from the first equation for region (2)

\(E_{b2}= \left(E_{b1} – \frac{q^{(2)}_{1} }{\epsilon ^{(2)}_{1} } \right) / \epsilon ^{(2)}_{2} F_{1-2} = \left(850.9 – \frac{82.9}{0.1} \right) / \frac{0.8}{4}= 109.5 W/m^{2},\)

or

\(T_{2}= (E_{b2}/\sigma )^{1/4} = \left[109.5/5.670\times 10^{-8} \right] ^{1/4} = 209 K.\)

Obviously, surface \(A_{2}\) would heat up by convection from the surroundings. Surface emission from \(A_{2}\) would then further improve the collection efficiency.
