Guest Question.

A three-phase induction motor with sort circuited Y-connected rotor has the following parameters:

2 hp, line-to-line rms voltage 311 V, 50 Hz, four poles, \(R_s\) = 0.277 Ω, \(R_r\) = 0.14 Ω, \(L_m\) = 0.054 H, \(L_s\) = 0.055 H, \(L_r\) = 0.057 H, \(\frac{N_s}{N_r}\)= windings turns ratio = 4, symmetrical ac supply.

Calculate the voltages and currents of the motor when the rotor is stationary.

Use the induction motor model referred to the stator.

Admin
Solution

The input phase voltages of the induction motor are given by:

\(v_{as} = \frac{311\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{2}}\sin \omega t = 253.93 \sin \omega t\) (1)

\(v_{bs} = \frac{311\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{2}} \sin(\omega t – \frac{2\pi}{3}) = 253.93 \sin (\omega t – \frac{2\pi}{3})\) (2)

\(v_{cs} = \frac{311\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{2}} \sin(\omega t – \frac{4\pi}{3}) = 253.93 \sin (\omega t – \frac{4\pi}{3})\) (3)

where \(\omega\) = ac supply angular frequency = \(2\pi f = 2\pi (50) = 314.16 \frac{rad}{s}\).

Knowing that the ac supply is symmetrical and using Eq. (\(T_e = \frac{3}{2} (\frac{P}{2})\frac{L_m}{L_rL^′_s}|\overrightarrow{\psi_s}| |\overrightarrow{\psi_r} |\sin \delta_\psi \)

where

\(L^′_s = L_sL_r – L^2_m\)

 

\(\delta_\psi\)=angle between the magnetic flux vectors \(\overrightarrow{\psi_s}\) and \(\overrightarrow{\psi_r}\)), then the \(ds^s-qs^s\) axes voltage components of the stationary reference frame are given by:

\(\begin{bmatrix} v_{qs^s} \\ v_{ds^s} \end{bmatrix} = \frac{2}{3}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{2}\\ 0 & -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} &\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \\ 0.5 &0.5 & 0.5 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_{as} \\ v_{bs} \\ v_{cs} \end{bmatrix}\) (4)

\(v_{qs^s} =\frac{2}{3}(v_{as} – \frac{1}{2}(v_{bs} + v_{cs})\) (5)

\(v_{ds^s} = \frac{2}{3}(-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} v_{bs} + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} v_{cs}) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(v_{cs} – v_{bs})\) (6)

For symmetrical ac voltage supply, the following equation holds:

\(v_{as} + v_{bs} + v_{cs} =0\) or \(v_{bs} + v_{cs} = -v_{as}\) (7)

Substituting (7) into (5) and (6) yields:

\(v_{qs^s} =\frac{2}{3}(v_{as} + \frac{1}{2}v_{as}) = \frac{2}{3}(\frac{3}{2}v_{as}) = 253.93 \sin \omega t = 253.93 \angle 0°\) (8)

\(v_{ds^s} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(v_{cs} – v_{bs}) = 253.93 \sin \omega t = 253.93 \angle 90°\) (9)

Since the rotor of the motor is stationary \(\frac{d\theta_r}{dt}=0\) , the voltages vqr and vdr have zero value and, therefore, using the parameters of the motor and Eq. (\(\begin{bmatrix} v_{qs^e} \\ v_{ds^e} \\v_{qr^e} \\v_{dr^e} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} R_s + SL_s & \omega_e L_s & SL_m & \omega_e L_m \\ -\omega_e L_s & R_s + SL_s &-\omega_e L_m & SL_m \\ SL_m & (\omega_e – \omega_r)L_m & R_r + SL_r & (\omega_e – \omega_r)L_r \\ -(\omega_e – \omega_r)L_m &SL_m & -(\omega_e – \omega_r)L_r & R_r + SL_r \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} i_{qs^s} \\ i_{ds^s} \\i_{dr^s} \\i_{dr^s} \end{bmatrix}\)

where S is the Laplace operator.) the equations of the induction motor are given by:

\(\begin{bmatrix} v_{qs^s} \\ v_{ds^s} \\0 \\0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0.277 + j314.16 & 0 & j16.96 &0 \\ 0 & 0.277 + j314.16 &0 & j16.96 \\ j16.96 & 0 & 0.277 + j314.16 & 0 \\ 0 & j16.96 & 0 & 0.277 + j314.16 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} i_{qs^s} \\ i_{ds^s} \\i_{dr^s} \\i_{dr^s} \end{bmatrix}\) (10)

or

\(\left. \begin{matrix} v_{qs^s} = i_{qs^s} (0.277 + j314.16) + j16.96 i_{qr^s} = 253.93 \angle 0° \\ v_{ds^s} = i_{ds^s} (0.277 + j314.16) + j16.96 i_{dr^s} = 253.93 \angle 90°\\ 0 = j16.96 i_{qs^s} + i_{qr^s} (0.277 + j314.16) \\0 = j16.96 i_{ds^s} + i_{dr^s} (0.277 + j314.16) \end{matrix} \right\}\) (11)

Solving the system of Eq. (11) the current components \(i_{qs^s} , i_{ds^s} , i_{qr^s}\) , and \(i_{dr^s}\) can be found.
