Guest Question.

In the slab shown in Fig. P.19.3 Area 1 carries an ultimate load of intensity \(12 kN / m ^{2}\) while Area 2 carries an ultimate load of intensity \(8 kN / m ^{2}\). Determine the value of the moment parameter m assuming the yield line pattern shown.
Ans. 14.73 kNm/m.

Admin
Solution

Referring to Fig. S.19.3

the work absorbed in the yield lines is:
for panel A,

\(0.4 m \times 5\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)=\frac{2 m}{x}\)

for panel B,

\(2(m+m) \times 10\left(\frac{1}{2.5}\right)=16 m\)

The total work absorbed by the yield lines is therefore m[16+(2/x)].

The work done by the load is \(=12\left[5 x\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)+5(7-x)\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)\right]+8\left[3 \times 5\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)\right]\)

= 270 – 10x
Therefore

\(m\left(16+\frac{2}{x}\right)=270-10 x\)

i.e.

\(m=\frac{270-10 x}{\left(16+\frac{2}{x}\right)}\)

Differentiating the above with respect to x and simplifying gives

\(8 x^{2}+2 x-27=0\)

which gives

x = 1.716 m

Then

m = 14.73 kNm/m.
