Guest Question.

Let us consider the cable, which is presented in Fig. 5.11. The cable has following parameters: \(L_0 =117.7m; D_0 = 100m; h_0 =57.7m; q_0 = 0.014 kN/m\). Determine the shape of the cable, thrust, and tension.

Admin
Solution

Parameter a of the catenary can be calculated from (5.33a)

\(\sinh \frac{D_0}{2a}=\frac{1}{2a}\sqrt{L_0^2 -h^2_0}\) (5.33a)

\(\sinh\frac{100}{2a}= \frac{1}{2a}\sqrt{117.7^2 -57.7^2} \rightarrow a= 127.41 m.\)

Coordinate x of the support A is \(x_A = 127.41 \tanh^{-1}(57.7/117.7) – (100/2)= 18.34 m.\)

Slopes at supports A and B

\(\tan\theta _A = \sinh\frac{18.34}{127.34}= 0.1446;\) \( \sin \theta _A=0.1431;\) \( \cos\theta _A= 0.9897\)

 

\(\tan\theta _B = \sinh\frac{18.34 +100}{127.34}= 1.0694;\) \( \sin \theta _B=0.7304;\) \( \cos\theta _B= 0.6830.\)

Thrust of the cable is \(H = q_0a = 0.014\times 127.41 = 1.7837kN.\)

Expressions (5.36)

\(N_A= H\sqrt{1 + \sinh^2\frac{x_A}{a} };\) \(N_B= H\sqrt{1 + \sinh^2\frac{x_B}{a} }.\) (5.36)

allows calculating the tension at supports A and B

\(N_A= 1.7834\sqrt{1 + \sinh^2\frac{18.34}{127.41} }=1.802 kN;\)

 

\(N_B= 1.7834\sqrt{1 + \sinh^2\frac{18.34+100}{127.41} }=2.609 kN.\)

Control. Ordinates of support points are

\(y_A =a \cosh\frac{x_A}{a}= 127.41 \cosh\frac{18.34}{127.41}= 128.73 m;\)

 

\(y_B = 127.41 \cosh\frac{18.34+100}{127.41}= 186.43 m.\)

Vertical distance between points A and B equals \(h_0 = y_B -y_A = 186.43m – 128.73 m = 57.7 m.\)

Equilibrium equation \(âˆ‘ {Y} =0\) for cable in whole leads to the following result

\(– N_A\sin\theta _A + N_B\sin\theta _B – L_0q = – 1.802 \times 0.1431+ 2.609 \times 0.7304 – 117.7 \times 0.014\)

 

\( = -1.9057 + 1.9056 \cong 0.\)

Pay attention that vertical reaction of the support A is directed downward as shown in Fig. 5.11.

Trust can be calculated by formulas

\( H =N_A \cos\theta _A = 1.802 \times 0.9897 = 1.783,\)

 

\( H =N_B \cos\theta _B = 2.609 \times 0.6830 = 1.782.\)
