Guest Question.

Calculate the deflection at the mid-span point of the beam of by assuming a deflected shape function of the form

\(v=v_{1} \sin \frac{\pi x}{L}+v_{3} \sin \frac{3 \pi x}{L}\)

in which \(v_{1}\) and \(v_{3}\) are unknown displacement parameters. Note:

\(\int_{0}^{L} \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{n \pi x}{L}\right) d x=\frac{L}{2} \int_{0}^{L} \sin \left(\frac{m \pi x}{L}\right) \sin \left(\frac{n \pi x}{L}\right) d x=0\)

Ans. \(0.02078 WL ^{3} / EI\) .

Admin
Solution

This problem may be solved using either complementary energy or potential energy. The strain energy U due to the bending of the beam is given by Eq. (9.21),

\(U=\int_{L} \frac{M^{2}}{2 E I_{z}} d x\) (9.21)

i.e.

\(U=\int_{0}^{L}\left(M^{2} / 2 E I\right) d x\)

and the potential energy of the applied load is, from

\(V=-W v_{ B }\)

The total potential energy is then

\(TPE =U+V=\int_{0}^{L}\left(\frac{M^{2}}{2 E I}\right) d x-W v_{ B }\)

which, from Eq. (13.3)

\(\frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=\frac{M}{E I}\) (13.3)

may be written

\(TPE =\frac{E I}{2} \int_{0}^{L}\left(\frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}\right)^{2} d x-W v_{ B }\)

Substituting for \(d ^{2} v / d x^{2}\) from the given deflected shape function

\(TPE =\frac{E I}{2} \int_{0}^{L}\left[\left(\frac{\pi^{2} v_{1}}{L^{2}}\right) \sin \left(\frac{\pi x}{L}\right)+\left(\frac{9 \pi^{2} v_{3}}{L^{2}}\right) \sin \left(\frac{3 \pi x}{L}\right)\right]^{2} d x-W v_{ B }\)

or

\(TPE =\left(\frac{\pi^{4} E I}{2 L^{4}}\right)\left[v_{1}^{2} \int_{0}^{L} \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{\pi x}{L}\right) d x+18 v_{1} v_{3} \int_{0}^{L} \sin \left(\frac{\pi x}{L}\right) \sin \left(\frac{3 \pi x}{L}\right) d x\right.\)

 

\(\left.+81 v_{3}^{2} \int_{0}^{L} \sin ^{2}\left(\frac{3 \pi x}{L}\right) d x\right]\)

Using the values of the integrals given

\(TPE =\left(\frac{\pi^{4} E I}{4 L^{3}}\right)\left(v_{1}^{2}+81 v_{3}^{2}\right)-W v_{ B }\)

From the given deflected shape function, at mid-span when x=L/2

\(v_{ B }=v_{1}-v_{3}\)

so that

\(TPE =\left(\frac{\pi^{4} E I}{4 L^{3}}\right)\left(v_{1}^{2}+81 v_{3}^{2}\right)-W\left(v_{1}-v_{3}\right)\)

Then, from the principle of the stationary value of the total potential energy

\(\frac{\partial( TPE )}{\partial v_{1}}=\left(\frac{\pi^{4} E I}{2 L^{3}}\right) v_{1}-W=0\)

 

\(\frac{\partial( TPE )}{\partial v_{3}}=\left(\frac{81 \pi^{4} E I}{2 L^{3}}\right) v_{3}+W=0\)

Solving gives

\(v_{1}=\frac{2 W L^{3}}{\pi^{4} E I}, \quad v_{3}=\frac{-2 W L^{3}}{81 \pi^{4} E I}\)

so that

\(v_{ B }=\left(\frac{2 W L^{3}}{\pi^{4} E I}\right)\left(1+\frac{1}{81}\right)\)

i.e.

\(v_{ B }=\frac{0.02078 WL ^{3}}{E I}\)

Note that Eq. (v) of Ex. 13.5

\(v_{\text {mid-span }}=-\frac{W L^{3}}{48 E I}\) Eq. (v) of Ex. 13.5

gives \(v_{ B }=0.02083 W L^{3} / E I\) .
