Guest Question.

What mass of sodium hydroxide, NaOH, would be required to produce 8.00 g of the antacid milk of magnesia, \(Mg(OH)_{2}\), by the reaction of \(MgCl_{2}\) with NaOH?

Admin
Solution

Step 1. Write and balance the equation:

\(MgCl_{2}(aq) + 2NaOH(aq) → Mg(OH)_{2}(s) + 2NaCl(aq)\)

Step 2. Determine the strategy:

The equation tells us that 2 mol of NaOH form 1 mol of \(Mg(OH)_{2}\). If we calculate the number of mol of \(Mg(OH)_{2}\) in 8.00 g of Mg(OH)2, we can determine the number of mol of NaOH necessary and then the mass of NaOH required:

g mol mol g

\(Mg(OH)_{2}\) → \(Mg(OH)_{2}\) → \(NaOH\) → \(NaOH\)

Step 3. The conversion factor to convert g \(Mg(OH)_{2}\) to mol \(Mg(OH)_{2}\) is:

\(58.3 g Mg(OH)_{2} = 1 mol Mg(OH)_{2}\)

Therefore, using the molar mass of \(Mg(OH)_{2}\),

\(8.00 \cancel{g Mg(OH)_{2}} × \frac{1 mol Mg(OH)_{2}}{58.3 \cancel{g Mg(OH)2}} = 0.137 mol Mg(OH)_{2}\)

Step 4. Two moles of NaOH react to give 1 mol of \(Mg(OH)_{2}\). Therefore, using the molar ratio,

\(0.137 \cancel{mol Mg(OH)_{2}} × \frac{2 mol NaOH}{1 \cancel{mol Mg(OH)_{2}}} = 0.274 mol NaOH\)

Step 5. The conversion factor to convert mol to mass NaOH is: 40.0 g of NaOH = 1 mol of NaOH. Therefore, using
the molar mass of NaOH,

\(0.274 \cancel{mol NaOH} × \frac{40.0 g NaOH}{1 \cancel{mol NaOH}} =11.0 g NaOH \)

This calculation may also be done in a single step:

\(8.00 \cancel{g Mg(OH)_{2}} × \frac{1 \cancel{mol Mg(OH)_{2}}}{58.3 \cancel{g Mg(OH)_{2}}} × \frac{2 \cancel{mol NaOH}}{1 \cancel{mol Mg(OH)_{2}}} × \frac{40.0 g NaOH}{1 \cancel{mol NaOH}} = 11.0 g NaOH \)

Data Given × Conversion Factor × Conversion Factor × Conversion Factor = Desired Result

Helpful Hint: Mass is a laboratory unit, whereas mol is a calculation unit. The laboratory balance is calibrated in units of mass (g). Although mol are essential for this type of calculation, the starting point and objective are often in mass units. As a result, our path is often g ⟶ mol ⟶ mol ⟶ g
