Guest Question.

Let L : R² → R³ be the linear transformation defined by

\(L(x) = (x_{2}, x_{1} + x_{2}, x_{1}− x_{2})^{T}\)

Find the matrix representations of L with respect to the ordered bases {\(u_{1}, u_{2}\)} and {\(b_{1}, b_{2}, b_{3}\)}, where

\(u_{1} = (1, 2)^{T} , u_{2} = (3, 1)^{T}\)

and

\(b_{1} = (1, 0, 0)^{T} , b_{2} = (1, 1, 0)^{T} , b_{3} = (1, 1, 1)^{T}\)
Admin
Solution

We must compute \(L(u_{1})\) and \(L(u_{2})\) and then transform the matrix \((b_{1}, b_{2}, b_{3} | L(u_{1}),L(u_{2}))\) to reduced row echelon form:

\(L(u_{1}) = (2, 3, −1)^{T}\) and \(L(u_{2}) = (1, 4, 2)^{T}\)

\( \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & | &2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1& |& 3 & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & 1&|& -1 & 2 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup \longrightarrow \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & | &-1 & -3 \\ 0 & 1 & 0& |& 4 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 1&|& -1 & 2 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup \)

The matrix representing L with respect to the given ordered bases is

\(A = \left\lgroup\begin{matrix}-1 & -3 \\ 4 & 2 \\ -1 & 2 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup \)

The reader may verify that
\(L(u_{1}) = −b_{1} + 4b_{2} − b_{3}\)

\(L(u_{2}) = −3b_{1} + 2b_{2} + 2b_{3}\)
