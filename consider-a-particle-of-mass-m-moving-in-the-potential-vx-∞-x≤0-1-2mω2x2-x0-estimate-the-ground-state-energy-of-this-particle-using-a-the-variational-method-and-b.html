Guest Question.

Consider a particle of mass m moving in the potential \(V(x)= \begin{cases}+\infty, & x \leq 0, \\ \frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} x^{2}, & x>0.\end{cases}\)

Estimate the ground state energy of this particle using

(a) the variational method and (b) the WKB method.

Admin
Solution

(a) As seen in , page 266, the ground state wave function of this potential mustbe selected from the harmonic oscillator wave functions that vanish at x = 0. Only the oddwave functions vanish at x = 0. So a trial function that, besides being zero at x = 0, is finite as \(x \longrightarrow+\infty\) is given by

\(\psi_{0}(x, \alpha)=x e^{-\alpha x^{2}}\). (9.421)

Using the results

\(\left\langle\psi_{0}\mid\psi_{0}\right\rangle=\int_{0}^{+\infty} x^{2} e^{-2 \alpha x^{2}} d x=\frac{1}{8 \alpha} \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2\alpha}}\), (9.422)

 

\(\left\langle\psi_{0}\left|\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} x^{2}\right| \psi_{0}\right\rangle=\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} \int_{0}^{+\infty} x^{4} e^{-2 \alpha x^{2}} d x=\frac{3 m \omega^{2}}{64 \alpha^{2}} \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2 \alpha}},\) (9.423)

 

\(\left\langle\psi_{0}\left|-\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \frac{d^{2}}{d x^{2}}\right| \psi_{0}\right\rangle=\frac{\hbar^{2}}{2 m} \int_{0}^{+\infty}\left(3 \alpha x^{2}-2 \alpha^{2} x^{4}\right) e^{-2 \alpha x^{2}} d x=\frac{3 \hbar^{2}}{16 m} \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2 \alpha}}\), (9.424)

we obtain the ground state energy

\(E_{0}(\alpha)=\frac{\left\langle\psi_{0}(\alpha)|\hat{H}| \psi_{0}(\alpha)\right\rangle}{\left\langle\psi_{0}\mid\psi_{0} \right\rangle}=\frac{3 \hbar^{2}}{2 m} \alpha+\frac{3 m \omega^{2}}{8 \alpha}\). (9.425)

The minimization of \(E_{0}(\alpha)\) with respect to α yields \(\alpha_{0}=m \omega /(2 \hbar)\) and hence \(E_{0}\left(\alpha_{0}\right)=\frac{3}{2} \hbar \omega\).

This energy is identical to the exact value obtained in Chapter 4.

(b) This potential contains a single rigid wall at x = 0. Thus the proper quantization rule for this potential is given by (9.224): \(\int_{0}^{a} p d x=\left(n+\frac{3}{4}\right) \pi \hbar\); the turning point occurs at x = a with \(E=\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} a^{2}\) and hence \(a=\sqrt{2 E /\left(m \omega^{2}\right)}\)

The calculation of \(\int_{0}^{a} p d x\) goes as follows:

\(\int_{0}^{a} p d x=\int_{0}^{a} \sqrt{2 m Em^{2}\omega^{2} x^{2}} d x=m \omega \int_{0}^{a} \sqrt{a^{2}-x^{2}} d x .\) (9.426)

The change of variable x = a sin θ leads to

\(\int_{0}^{a} \sqrt{a^{2}-x^{2}} d x=a^{2} \int_{0}^{\pi / 2} \cos ^{2} \theta d \theta=\frac{a^{2}}{2} \int_{0}^{\pi / 2}(1+\cos 2 \theta) d \theta=\frac{\pi a^{2}}{4}\); (9.427)

hence

\(\int_{0}^{a} p d x=m \omega \frac{\pi a^{2}}{4}=\frac{\pi E}{2 \omega}\). (9.428)

Since\(\int_{0}^{a} p d x=\left(n+\frac{3}{4}\right) \pi \hbar\), that is, \(\pi E /(2 \omega)=\left(n+\frac{3}{4}\right) \pi \hbar\), we obtain

\(E_{n}^{W K B}=\left(2 n+\frac{3}{2}\right)\hbar\omega \), n = 0, 1, 2, 3, …. (9.429)

This relation is identical with the exact expression obtained in Chapter 4. The WKB ground state energy is thus given by \(E_{0}^{W K B}=\frac{3}{2} \hbar \omega\).
