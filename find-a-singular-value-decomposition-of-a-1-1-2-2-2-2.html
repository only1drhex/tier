Guest Question.

Find a singular value decomposition of \( A = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & -1 \\ -2 & 2 \\ 2 & -2 \end{matrix} \right ] \) .

Admin
Solution

First, compute \( A^{T}A = \left [ \begin{matrix} 9 & -9 \\ -9 & 9 \end{matrix} \right ] \) . The eigenvalues of \( A^{T}A \) are 18 and 0, with corresponding unit eigenvectors

\( v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1/\sqrt{2} \\ -1/\sqrt{2} \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1/\sqrt{2} \\ 1/\sqrt{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \)

These unit vectors form the columns of V :

\( V = \left [ \begin{matrix} v_{1} & v_{2} \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1/\sqrt{2} & 1/\sqrt{2} \\ -1/\sqrt{2} & 1/\sqrt{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \)

The singular values are \( \sigma_{1} = \sqrt{18} = 3\sqrt{2} \text{and } \sigma_{2} = 0 \). Since there is only one nonzero singular value, the “matrix” D may be written as a single number. That is, \( D = 3\sqrt{2} \). The matrix Σ is the same size as A, with D in its upper left corner:

\( \Sigma = \left [ \begin{matrix} D & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 3\sqrt{2} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

To construct U, first construct \( Av_{1} \) and \( Av_{2} \):

\( Av_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2/\sqrt{2} \\ -4/\sqrt{2} \\ 4/\sqrt{2} \end{matrix} \right ] , Av_{2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

As a check on the calculations, verify that \( \left\|Av_{1}\right\| = \sigma_{1} = 3\sqrt{2} \). Of course, \( Av_{2} = 0 \) because \( \left\|Av_{2}\right\| = \sigma_{2} = 0 \). The only column found for U so far is

\( u_{1} = \frac{1}{3\sqrt{2}}Av_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1/3 \\ -2/3 \\ 2/3 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

The other columns of U are found by extending the set {\( u_{1} \)} to an orthonormal basis for \( \mathbb{R} ^{3} \). In this case, we need two orthogonal unit vectors \( u_{2} \) and \( u_{3} \) that are orthogonal to \( u_{1} \). (See Figure 3.) Each vector must satisfy \( u_{1}^{T}x = 0 \), which is equivalent to the equation \( x_{1} – 2x_{2} + 2x_{3} = 0 \). A basis for the solution set of this equation is

\( w_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] , w_{2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} -2 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

(Check that \( w_{1} \) and \( w_{2} \) are each orthogonal to \( u_{1} \).) Apply the Gram–Schmidt process (with normalizations) to {\( w_{1} , w_{2} \)}, and obtain

\( u_{2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2/\sqrt{5} \\1/\sqrt{5} \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] , u_{3} = \left [ \begin{matrix} -2/\sqrt{45} \\ 4/\sqrt{45} \\ 5/\sqrt{45} \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Finally, set \( U = \left [ \begin{matrix} u_{1} & u_{2} & u_{3} \end{matrix} \right ] \), take Σ and \( V^{T} \) from above, and write

\( A = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & -1 \\ -2 & 2 \\ 2 & -2 \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1/3 & 2/\sqrt{5} & -2/\sqrt{45} \\ -2/3 & 1/\sqrt{5} & 4/\sqrt{45} \\ 2/3 & 0 & 5/\sqrt{45} \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} 3\sqrt{2} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} 1/\sqrt{2} & -1/\sqrt{2} \\ 1/\sqrt{2} & 1/\sqrt{2} \end{matrix} \right ] \)
