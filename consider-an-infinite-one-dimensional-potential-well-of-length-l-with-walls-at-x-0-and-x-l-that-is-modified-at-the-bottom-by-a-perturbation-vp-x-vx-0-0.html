Guest Question.

Consider an infinite, one-dimensional potential well of length L, with walls at x = 0 and x = L, that is modified at the bottom by a perturbation \(V_{p} (x)\):

\(V(x)=\begin{cases} 0, & 0<x<L, \\ \infty , & elsewhere,\end{cases}\) \(V_{p} (x)=\begin {cases} V_{0}, & 0\leq x\leq L/2, \\ 0, & elsewhere,\end{cases} \)

where \(V_{0}\ll 1\).

(a) Using first-order perturbation theory, calculate the energy \(E_{n}\).

(b) Calculate the energy \(E_{n}\) in the WKB approximation. Compare this energy with the expression obtained in (a).

Admin
Solution

The exact energy \(E^{exact}_{n}\) and wave function \( \phi _{n} (x)\) for a potential well are given by

\(E^{exact}_{n} =\frac{\pi ^{2} \hbar ^{2} }{2mL^{2} } n^{2} , \phi _{n} (x)=\sqrt{\frac{2}{L} } \sin \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) \)

(a) Since the first-order correction to the energy caused by the perturbation \(V_{p} (x)\) is given by

\(E^{(1)} _{n} =〈\phi _{n}|V_{p} |\phi _{n} 〉 =\frac{2}{L} V_{0} ∫^{L/2}_{0} \sin ^{2} \left(\frac{n\pi x}{L} \right) dx \)

 

\(=\frac{1}{L} V_{0} ∫^{L/2}_{0} \left[1-\cos \left(\frac{2n\pi x}{L} \right) \right] dx=\frac{V_{0}}{2} \); (9.355)

hence the energy is given to first-order perturbation by

\(E^{P T} _{n} =\frac{\pi ^{2} \hbar ^{2} }{2mL^{2} } n^{2}+ \frac{V_{0}}{2}\). (9.356)

(b) Since this potential has two rigid walls, the energy within theWKB approximation needs to be extracted from the quantization condition \(\int_{0}^{L} p(E_{n},x)dx=n\pi \hbar \), where

\(∫^{L}_{0} p(E_{n},x)dx=\sqrt{2m(E_{n}-V_{0})} ∫^{L/2}_{0} dx+\sqrt{2mE_{n}} ∫^{L}_{L/2} dx \)

 

\(=\frac{L}{2} \sqrt{2m} \left(\sqrt{E_{n}-V_{0}}+\sqrt{E_{n}} \right) \); (9.357)

hence \(L\sqrt{2} (\sqrt{E_{n}-V_{0}}+\sqrt{E_{n}})=2n\pi \hbar \) or

\(\sqrt{E_{n}-V_{0}}+\sqrt{E_{n}}=\frac{2n\pi \hbar }{L\sqrt {2m} } \). (9.358)

Squaring both sides of this equation and using the notation \( \alpha _{n} =2n^{2} \pi ^{2} \hbar ^{2} /(mL^{2})\), we have

\(2\sqrt{E_{n}(E_{n}-V_{0})} =\alpha _{n} -2E_{n}+V_{0}\). (9.359)

Squaring both sides of this equation, we obtain

\(4E^{2}_{n} -4E_{n} V_{0} =\alpha ^{2}_{n} +4E^{2}_{n} +V^{2}_{0} -4\alpha _{n}E_{n}+2\alpha _{n}V_{0}-4E_{n} V_{0}\), (9.360)

which, solving for \(E_{n}\), leads to

\(E_{n} =\frac{\alpha _{n}}{4} +\frac{V_{0}}{2} +\frac{V^{2} _{0} }{4\alpha _{n}} \) (9.361)

or

\(E^{W K B}_{n} =\frac{\pi ^{2} \hbar ^{2} }{2mL^{2} } n^{2} +\frac{V_{0}}{2}+\frac{mL^{2} V^{2}_{0}}{8\pi ^{2} \hbar ^{2}} \frac{1}{n^{2}} \). (9.362)

When \(n\gg 1\), and since \(V_{0}\) is very small, the WKB energy relation (9.362) gives back the expression (9.356) that was derived from a first-order perturbative treatment:

\(E^{W K B}_{n} \simeq E^{P T}_{n} = \frac{\pi ^{2} \hbar ^{2} }{2mL^{2} } n^{2}+\frac{V_{0}}{2}\).
