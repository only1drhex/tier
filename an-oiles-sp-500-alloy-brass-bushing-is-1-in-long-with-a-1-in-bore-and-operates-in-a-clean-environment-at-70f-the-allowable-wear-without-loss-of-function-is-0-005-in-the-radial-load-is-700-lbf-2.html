Guest Question.

An Oiles SP 500 alloy brass bushing is 1 in long with a 1-in bore and operates in a clean environment at 70°F. The allowable wear without loss of function is 0.005 in. The radial load is 700 lbf. The peripheral velocity is 33 ft/min. Estimate the number of revolutions for radial wear to be 0.005 in. See Fig. 12–40 and Table 12–12 from the manufacturer.

\(V =\frac {πDN}{12}\) (12–29)

\(PV =\frac {F}{DL} \frac {πDN}{12} =\frac {π}{12} \frac {FN}{L}\) (12–30)

\(P =\frac {F}{DL} =\frac{700}{(1)(1)} = 700 psi\)

PV = 700(33) = 23 100 psi · ft/min < 46 700 psi · ft/min (OK)

Equation (12–32) with Eq. (12–29) is

\(w = f_{1} f_{2}K \frac {4}{π} \frac {F}{DL} \frac {πDN t}{12} =\frac {f_{1} f_{2}K FN t}{3L}\) (12–32)

\(w = f_{1} f_{2}K \frac {4}{π} \frac {F}{DL} \frac {πDN t}{12}=f_{1} f_{2}K \frac {4}{π} \frac {F}{DL} Vt\)

Solving for t gives

\(t =\frac {πDLw}{4 f_{1} f_{2}KV F} =\frac {π(1)(1)0.005}{4(1.3)(1)0.6(10^{−10})33(700) }= 2180 h = 130 770 min\)

The rotational speed is

\(N =\frac {12V}{πD} =\frac {12(33)}{π(1)} = 126 r/min\)

Cycles = Nt = 126(130 770) = 16.5(\(10^{6}\)) rev
