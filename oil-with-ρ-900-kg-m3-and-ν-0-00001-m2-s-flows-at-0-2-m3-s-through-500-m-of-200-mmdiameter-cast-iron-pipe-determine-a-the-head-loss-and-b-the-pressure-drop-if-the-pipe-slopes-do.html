Guest Question.

Oil, with ρ = 900 kg/\(m^3\) and ν = 0.00001 \(m^2\)/s, flows at 0.2 \(m^3\)/s through 500 m of 200-mmdiameter cast iron pipe. Determine (a) the head loss and (b) the pressure drop if the pipe slopes down at 10° in the flow direction.

Admin
Solution

First compute the velocity from the known flow rate:

\(V = \frac{Q}{\pi R^2} = \frac{0.2 m^3/s}{\pi (0.1 m)^2} = 6.4 m/s\)

Then the Reynolds number is

\(Re_d = \frac{Vd}{\nu} = \frac{(6.4 m/s)(0.2 m)}{0.00001 m^2/s} = 128,000\)

From Table 6.1, ε = 0.26 mm for cast iron pipe. Then

\(\frac{\epsilon}{d} = \frac{0.26 mm}{200 mm} = 0.0013\)

Stainless, new 7E-06 0.002 ±50

Wrought, new 0.0002 0.046 ±20
Galvanized, new 0.0005 0.15 ±40

Brass Drawn, new 7E-06 0.002 ±50

Enter the Moody chart on the right at ε/d = 0.0013 (you will have to interpolate), and move to the left to intersect with Re = 128,000. Read f ≈ 0.0225 [from Eq. (6.48) for these values we could compute f = 0.0227]. Then the head loss is

\(h_f = f \frac{L}{d}\frac{V^2}{2g} = (0.0225)\frac{500 m}{0.2 m} \frac{(6.4 m/s)^2}{2(9.81 m/s^2)} = 117 m\)

\(\frac{1}{f^{1/2}}=-2.0\log \left(\frac{\epsilon /d}{3.7}+\frac{2.51}{Re_d f^{1/2}}\right)\) (6.48)

From Eq. (6.9) for the inclined pipe,

\(h_f = \frac{\Delta p}{\rho g} + z_1 – z_2 = \frac{\Delta p}{\rho g} + L \sin 10^{\circ}\)

or \(\Delta p = \rho g[h_f (500 m) \sin 10^{\circ}] = \rho g(117 m – 87 m) = (900 kg/m^3)(9.81 m/s^2)(30 m) = 265,000 kg/(m \cdot s^2) = 265,000 Pa\)

\(\sum{F_x} = \Delta p (\pi R^2) + \rho g(\pi R^2)L \sin \phi – \tau_w(2 \pi R)L = \dot{m}(V_2 – V_1) = 0\) (6.9a)

\(\Delta z + \frac{\Delta p}{\rho g} = h_f = \frac{2 \tau_w}{\rho g}\frac{L}{R} = \frac{4 \tau_w}{\rho g}\frac{L}{d}\) (6.9b)
