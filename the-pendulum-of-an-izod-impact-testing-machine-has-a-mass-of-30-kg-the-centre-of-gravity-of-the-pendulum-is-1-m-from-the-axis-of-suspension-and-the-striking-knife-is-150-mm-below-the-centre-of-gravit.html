Guest Question.

The pendulum of an Izod impact testing machine has a mass of 30 kg. The centre of gravity of the pendulum is 1 m from the axis of suspension and the striking knife is 150 mm below the centre of gravity. The radius of gyration about the point of suspension is 1.1 m, and about the centre of gravity is 350 mm. In making a test, the pendulum is released from an angle of \(60^{\circ}\) to the vertical. Determine :
1. striking velocity of the pendulum, 2. impulse on the pendulum and sudden change of axis reaction when a specimen giving an impact value of 54 N-m is broken, 3. angle of swing of the pendulum after impact, and 4. average force exerted at the pivot and at the knife edge if the duration of impact is assumed to be 0.005 second.

Admin
Solution

Given : m = 30 kg ; AG = a = 1 m ; GB = b = 150 mm = 0.15 m ; \(k_{1}=1.1 m\) ; \(k_{2}=350 mm =0.35 m ; \theta=60^{\circ} ; t=0.005 s\)

We know that mass moment of inertia of the pendulum about the point of suspension A,

\(I_{ A }=m\left(k_{1}\right)^{2}=30(1.1)^{2}=36.3 kg – m ^{2}\)

and mass moment of inertia of the pendulum about centre of gravity G,

\(I_{ G }=m\left(k_{2}\right)^{2}=30(0.35)^{2}\) \(=3.675 kg – m ^{2}\)

1. Striking velocity of the pendulum

Let v = Striking velocity of the pendulum, and

ω = Angular velocity of the pendulum.

Since the potential energy of the pendulum is converted into angular kinetic energy of the pendulum, therefore,

\(m \cdot g \cdot h_{1}=\frac{1}{2} I_{ A } \cdot \omega^{2}\)

\(30 \times 9.81\left(1-1 \cos 60^{\circ}\right)=\frac{1}{2} \times 36.3 \omega^{2}\) \(\ldots\left(\because h_{1}=a-a \cos 60^{\circ}\right)\)

or 147.15 = 18.15 \(\omega ^{2}\)

\(\therefore\) \(\omega^{2}=147.15 / 18.15=8.1 \text { or } \omega=2.85 rad / s\)

and v = \(\omega \) × A B = \(\omega \) (a + b) = 2.85 (1 + 0.15) = 3.28 m/s

2. Impulse on the pendulum

Let \(F_{1} \) = Impulse at the pivot A,

\(F_{2} \) = Impulse at the knife edge B,

\(\omega \) = Angular velocity of the pendulum just before the breakage of the specimen, and

\(\omega_{1} \) = Angular velocity of the pendulum just after the breakage of the specimen.

Since the loss in angular kinetic energy of the pendulum is equal to the energy used for breaking the specimen (which is 54 N-m ), therefore

\(\frac{1}{2} I_{ A }\left(\omega^{2}-\omega_{1}^{2}\right)=54 \quad \text { or } \quad \frac{1}{2} \times 36.3\left(2.85^{2}-\omega_{1}^{2}\right)=54\)

\(\therefore\) \(\omega_{1}^{2}=(2.85)^{2}-\frac{54 \times 2}{36.3}=5.125 \text { or } \omega_{1}=2.26 rad / s\)

Let \(v_{ G } \text { and } v_{ G }^{\prime}\) be the linear velocities of G just before and just after the breakage of specimen.

\(v_{ G }=\omega \times O G=2.85 \times 1=2.85 m / s\)

and \(v_{ G }{ }^{\prime}=\omega_{1} \times O G=2.26 \times 1=2.26 m / s\)

We know that Impulse = Change of linear momentum

\(F_{1}+F_{2}=m\left(v_{G}-v_{G}^{\prime}\right)=30(2.85-2.26)=17.7 N\) …(i)

Taking moments about G, we get

Impulsive torque = Change of angular momentum

\(F_{2} \times b-F_{1} \times a=I_{ G }\left(\omega-\omega_{1}\right)\)

\(F_{2} \times 0.15-F_{1} \times 1=3.675(2.85-2.26)=2.17\) ..(ii)

From equations (i) and (ii),

\(F_{2}=17.3 N ; \text { and } F_{1}=0.4 N\)

3. Angle of swing of the pendulum after impact

Let \(\theta \) = Angle of swing of the pendulum after impact.

Since work done in raising the pendulum is equal to angular kinetic energy of the pendulum, therefore

\(m \cdot g \cdot h_{1}=\frac{1}{2} I_{ A }\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2}\)

 

\(30 \times 9.81(1-1 \cos \theta)=\frac{1}{2} \times 36.3(2.26)^{2}=92.7\)

 

\(1-1 \cos \theta=92.7 / 30 \times 9.81=0.315 \quad \text { or } \quad \cos \theta=1-0.315=0.685\)

\(\therefore\) \(\theta=46.76^{\circ}\)

4. Average force exerted at the pivot and at the knife edge

We know that average force exerted at the pivot

\(=\frac{F_{1}}{t}=\frac{0.4}{0.005}=80 N\)

and average force exerted at the knife edge

\(=\frac{F_{2}}{t}=\frac{17.3}{0.005}=3460 N\)
