Guest Question.

Find the singular value decomposition of

\(A=\left[\begin{array}{cc}0 & -1 \\-2 & 1 \\1 & 0\end{array}\right]\)
Admin
Solution

The first step is to write down the transpose of this matrix, which is

 

\(A^{T}=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}0 & -2 & 1 \\-1 & 1 & 0\end{array}\right]\)

 

Now we compute

 

\(W=A A^{T}=\left[\begin{array}{cc}0 & -1 \\-2 & 1 \\1 & 0\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{ccc}0 & -2 & 1 \\-1 & 1 & 0\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & -1 & 0 \\-1 & 5 & -2 \\0 & -2 & 1\end{array}\right]\)

 

The next step is to find the eigenvalues of W. These are (exercise) {0, 1, 6}. Only positive eigenvalues are important. The singular values are the square roots, and so

 

\(\sigma_{1}=1, \quad \sigma_{2}=\sqrt{6}\)

 

D is constructed by placing these elements on the diagonal. They are arranged from greatest to lowest in value. There are two singular values, and so D is a 2 Ã— 2 matrix

 

\(D=\left[\begin{array}{cc}\sqrt{6} & 0 \\0 & 1\end{array}\right]\)

 

Next we find the eigenvectors of W that correspond to the eignvalues {1, 6}. These must be normalized. We demonstrate with the second eigenvalue. The equation is

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & -1 & 0 \\-1 & 5 & -2 \\0 & -2 & 1\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}a \\b \\c\end{array}\right]=6\left[\begin{array}{l}a \\b \\c\end{array}\right]\)

 

This eigenvector equation leads to the relationships (check)

 

\(\begin{array}{l}b=-5 a \\c=2 a\end{array}\)

 

Therefore we can write the eigenvector as

 

\(v=\left[\begin{array}{c}-a \\5 a \\-2 a\end{array}\right]\)

 

Now we normalize the eigenvector

 

\(1=v^{T} v=\left[\begin{array}{lll}a & -5 a & 2 a\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c}a \\-5 a \\2 a\end{array}\right]=a^{2}+25 a^{2}+4 a^{2}=30 a^{2}\)

 

and so we have

 

\(1=30 a^{2} \Rightarrow a=\frac{1}{\sqrt{30}}\)

 

And the normalized eigenvector is

 

\(v=\left[\begin{array}{c}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{30}} \\\frac{5}{\sqrt{30}} \\-\frac{2}{\sqrt{30}}\end{array}\right]\)

 

The other normalized eigenvector, corresponding to the eigenvalue {1}, is (exercise)

 

\(w=\left[\begin{array}{c}-\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}} \\0 \\\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\end{array}\right]\)

 

Now we construct U. The columns of U are the eigenvectors

 

\(U=\left[\begin{array}{cc}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{30}} & -\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}} \\\frac{5}{\sqrt{30}} & 0 \\-\frac{2}{\sqrt{30}} & \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\end{array}\right]\)

 

Now we compute

 

\(W^{\prime}=A^{T} A=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}0 & -2 & 1 \\-1 & 1 & 0\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{cc}0 & -1 \\-2 & 1 \\1 & 0\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc}5 & -2 \\-2 & 2\end{array}\right]\)

 

The eigenvalues of this matrix are {1, 6} (why?). The normalized eigvectors are

 

\(v_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{c}-\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}} \\\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\end{array}\right], \quad v_{2}=\left[\begin{array}{c}\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \\\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}\end{array}\right]\)

 

respectively. We construct V by mapping these eigenvectors to the columns of the matrix

 

\(V=\left[\begin{array}{cc}-\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}} & \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \\\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} & \frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}\end{array}\right]\)

 

(Are these columns orthogonal?). In this case the transpose is equal to V.

We have found the singular value decomposition of the matrix A. Recalling that \(A=U D V^{T}\), we verify the result. First we have

 

\(D V^{T}=\left[\begin{array}{cc}\sqrt{6} & 0 \\0 & 1\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{cc}-\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}} & \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} \\\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} & \frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc}-\frac{2 \sqrt{6}}{\sqrt{5}} & \frac{\sqrt{6}}{\sqrt{5}} \\\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} & \frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}\end{array}\right]\)

 

and so we obtain

 

\(U D V^{T}=\left[\begin{array}{cc}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{30}} & -\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}} \\\frac{5}{\sqrt{30}} & 0 \\-\frac{2}{\sqrt{30}} & \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{cc}-\frac{2 \sqrt{6}}{\sqrt{5}} & \frac{\sqrt{6}}{\sqrt{5}} \\\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} & \frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc}0 & -1 \\-2 & 1 \\1 & 0\end{array}\right]=A\)
