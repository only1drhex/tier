Guest Question.

The thin-walled cantilever box beam shown in Fig. P.14.10 carries a vertically downward load of 100 kN at its free end. Calculate the principal strains at the point A which lies at the edge of the top cover of the beam at the built-in end. Take \(E=200000 N / mm ^{2}\) and ν = 0.3.

Ans. \(\varepsilon_{ I }=9.33 \times 10^{-4}, \varepsilon_{ II }=-2.87 \times 10^{-4}\).

Admin
Solution

The applied load of 100 kN is equivalent to a vertically downward load of 100 kN in the vertical plane of symmetry together with a torque of 100 × 75 = 7500 kNmm. The direct stress due to bending at the point A is calculated using Eq. (9.9)

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I}\) (9.9)

in which the second moment of area about the horizontal axis of symmetry of the beam section is

\(I_{z}=2 \times 150 \times 10 \times 150^{2}+2 \times 12 \times 300^{3} / 12=1.22 \times 10^{8} mm ^{4}\)

Then

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{100 \times 10^{3} \times 1.5 \times 10^{3} \times 150}{1.22 \times 10^{8}}=184.4 N / mm ^{2}\)

The shear stress at the point A is due to the applied torque and to the shear produced by the vertically applied load. From Eq. (11.21)

\(\tau=\frac{q}{t}=\frac{T}{2 A t}\) (11.21)

the shear stress due to the applied torque is

\(\tau=\frac{7500 \times 10^{3}}{2 \times 150 \times 300 \times 10}=8.33 N / mm ^{2}\)

The shear stress produced by the shear load is given by Eq. (10.24)

\(q_{s}=\frac{S_{y} I_{z y}-S_{z} I_{z}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s+\frac{S_{z} I_{z y}-S_{y} I_{y}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s+q_{s, 0}\) (10.24)

in which \(S_{y}=-100 kN\), \(S_{z}=0, I_{z y}=0\) and \(I_{z}\) has been determined above. Also, if s is measured from the vertical plane of symmetry, \(q_{s, 0}=0\) and Eq. (10.24) reduces to

\(q_{s}=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} t y s\) (i)

On the top cover of the beam, t = 10 mm, y = 150 mm and at the point A, s = 75 mm. From Eq. (i) the shear stress at A is then

\(\tau_{ A }=\frac{100 \times 10^{3} \times 10 \times 150 \times 75}{1.22 \times 10^{8}}=9.22 N / mm ^{2}\)

The total shear stress at A is then \(8.33+9.22=17.6 N / mm ^{2}\).

The direct strains in the x and z directions are, from Eqs (7.7) and (7.12)

\(\sigma=E \varepsilon\) (7.7)

\(\varepsilon_{y}=-v \frac{\sigma_{x}}{E} \quad \varepsilon_{z}=-v \frac{\sigma_{x}}{E}\) (7.12)

\(\varepsilon_{z}=\frac{184.4}{200000}=9.22 \times 10^{-4}\)

 

\(\varepsilon_{z}=-0.3 \times 9.22 \times 10^{-4}=-2.77 \times 10^{-4}\)

From Eq. (7.21)

\(G=\frac{E}{2(1+v)} \text { or } E=2 G(1+v)\) (7.21)

\(G=\frac{200000}{2(1+0.3)}=76923 N / mm ^{2}\)

Then, from Eq. (7.9)

\(G=\frac{\tau}{\gamma}\) (7.9)

\(\gamma_{z y}=\frac{17.6}{76923}=2.29 \times 10^{-4}\)

Substituting the above values of strain in Eqs. (14.21) and (14.22)

\(\varepsilon_{ I }=\frac{\varepsilon_{x}+\varepsilon_{y}}{2}+\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\varepsilon_{x}-\varepsilon_{y}\right)^{2}+\gamma_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.21)

\(\varepsilon_{ II }=\frac{\varepsilon_{x}+\varepsilon_{y}}{2}-\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\varepsilon_{x}-\varepsilon_{y}\right)^{2}+\gamma_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.22)

gives

\(\varepsilon_{ I }=9.33 \times 10^{-4}, \quad \varepsilon_{ II }=-2.87 \times 10^{-4}\)
