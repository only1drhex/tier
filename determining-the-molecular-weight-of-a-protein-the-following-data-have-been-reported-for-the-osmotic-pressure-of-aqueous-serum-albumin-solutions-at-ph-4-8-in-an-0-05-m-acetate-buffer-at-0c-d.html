Guest Question.

Determining the Molecular Weight of a Protein

The following data have been reported for the osmotic pressure of aqueous serum albumin solutions at pH = 4.8 in an 0.05 M acetate buffer at 0°C.

Data:

Albumin Concentration Osmotic Pressure
C (g/cc) Π (cm \(H _{2} O\))
0.78 2.39
1.25 4.01
2.79 8.53
3.38 10.8
4.18 13.01
8.98 27.84
12.45 37.69
Admin
Solution

From the experimental data, we have

 

Albumin Concentration Molecular Weight
C (g/cc) C/Π (g)
0.78 0.3264 75 600
1.25 0.3117 72 400
2.79 0.3271 75 700
3.38 0.3127 72 600
4.18 0.3213 74 400
8.98 0.3226 74 700
12.45 0.3303 76 600

 

The average of all these values is approximately 74 500. (Note that in the biological literature, this would be referred to as 74 500 daltons.)
