Guest Question.

Using the transform technique, we wish to determine \(v_{o}(t)\) in Fig. 15.16 if (a) \(v_{i}(t)=5 e^{-2 t} u(t) \mathrm{V}\) and (b) \(v_{i}(t)=5 \cos 2 t \mathrm{~V}\).

Admin
Solution

a. In this case since \(v_{i}(t)=5 e^{-2 t} u(t) \mathrm{V}\), then

 

\(\mathbf{V}_{i}(\omega)=\frac{5}{2+j \omega} \mathrm{V}\)

 

\(\mathbf{H}(\omega)\) for the network is

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{H}(\omega) &=\frac{R}{R+j \omega L} \\\\&=\frac{10}{10+j \omega}\end{aligned}\)

 

From Eq. (15.45),

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{V}_{o}(\omega) &=\mathbf{H}(\omega) \mathbf{V}_{i}(\omega) \\\\&=\frac{50}{(2+j \omega)(10+j \omega)} \\\\&=\frac{50}{8}\left(\frac{1}{2+j \omega}-\frac{1}{10+j \omega}\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

Hence, from Table 15.3, we see that

 

\(v_{o}(t)=6.25\left[e^{-2 t} u(t)-e^{-10 t} u(t)\right] \mathrm{V}\)

 

 

b. In this case, since \(v_{i}(t)=5 \cos 2 t\),

 

\(\mathbf{V}_{i}(\omega)=5 \pi \delta(\omega-2)+5 \pi \delta(\omega+2) \mathrm{V}\)

 

The output voltage in the frequency domain is then

 

\(\mathbf{V}_{o}(\omega)=\frac{50 \pi[\delta(\omega-2)+\delta(\omega+2)]}{(10+j \omega)}\)

 

Using the inverse Fourier transform gives us

 

\(v_{o}(t)=\mathcal{F}^{-1}\left[\mathbf{V}_{o}(\omega)\right]=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} 50 \pi \frac{\delta(\omega-2)+\delta(\omega+2)}{10+j \omega} e^{j \omega t} d \omega\)

 

Employing the sampling property of the unit impulse function, we obtain

 

\(\begin{aligned}v_{o}(t) &=25\left(\frac{e^{j 2 t}}{10+j 2}+\frac{e^{-j 2 t}}{10-j 2}\right) \\\\&=25\left(\frac{e^{j 2 t}}{10.2 e^{j 11.31^{\circ}}}+\frac{e^{-j 2 t}}{10.2 e^{-j 1.31^{\circ}}}\right) \\\\&=4.90 \cos \left(2 t-11.31^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{V}\end{aligned}\)

 

This result can be easily checked using phasor analysis.
