Calculating Power in a Three-Phase Wye-Delta Circuit
a) Calculate the total complex power delivered to the Δ-connected load of .
b) What percentage of the average power at the sending end of the line is delivered to the load?

Admin
Solution

a) Using the a-phase values from the solution of , we obtain

\(V_{\phi } = V_{AB} = 202.72 \angle 29.04^\circ \) V,
\(I_{\phi } =I_{AB} = 1.39 \angle -6.87^\circ \) A.

Using Eqs. 11.52 and 11.53, we have

\(S_{\phi } = P_{\phi } + jQ_{\phi } = V_{\phi }I^*_{\phi } ; \) (11.52)

\(S_T = 3S_{\phi } = \sqrt{3} V_LI_L\theta_{\phi }. \) (11.53)

\(S_T = 3(202.72\angle29.04^\circ)(1.39 \angle6.87^\circ)\)
\(= 682.56 + j494.21\) VA.

b) The total power at the sending end of the distribution line equals the total power delivered to the load plus the total power lost in the line;
therefore

\(P_{input}= 682.56 + 3(2.4)^2(0.3)\)

\(= 687.74\) W.

The percentage of the average power reaching the load is 682.56/687.74, or 99.25% .Nearly 100% of the average power at the input is delivered to the load because the impedance of the line is quite small compared to the load impedance.
