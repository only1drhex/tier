Guest Question.

Determine the dimension of the subspace H of \( \mathbb{R} ^3 \) spanned by the vectors \( v_{1} \), \( v_{2} \), and \( v_{3} \). (First, find a basis for H.)

\( v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 \\ -8 \\ 6 \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 3 \\ -7 \\ -1 \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{3} = \left [ \begin{matrix} -1 \\ 6 \\ -7 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Admin
Solution

Construct A = [\( v_{1} \) \( v_{2} \) \( v_{3} \)] so that the subspace spanned by \( v_{1} \), \( v_{2} \), \( v_{3} \) is the column space of A. A basis for this space is provided by the pivot columns of A.

\( A = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & 3 & -1 \\ -8 & -7 & 6 \\ 6 & -1 & -7 \end{matrix} \right ] \sim \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & 3 & -1 \\ 0 & 5 & 2 \\ 0 & -10 & -4 \end{matrix} \right ] \sim \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & 3 & -1 \\ 0 & 5 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

The first two columns of A are pivot columns and form a basis for H. Thus dim H = 2 .
