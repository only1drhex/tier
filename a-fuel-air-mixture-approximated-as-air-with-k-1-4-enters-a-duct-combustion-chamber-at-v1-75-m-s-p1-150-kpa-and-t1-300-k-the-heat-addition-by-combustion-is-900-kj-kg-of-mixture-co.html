Guest Question.

A fuel–air mixture, approximated as air with k = 1.4, enters a duct combustion chamber at \(V_1 = 75 m/s, p_1 = 150 kPa, and T_1 = 300 K\). The heat addition by combustion is 900 kJ/kg of mixture. Compute (a) the exit properties \(V_2, p_2, and T_2\) and (b) the total heat addition that would have caused a sonic exit flow.

Admin
Solution

Part (a)

First compute \(T_{01} = T_1 + V^2_1/(2c_p) = 300 + (75)^2/[2(1005)] = 303 K\). Then compute the change in stagnation temperature of the gas:

\(q = c_p(T_{02} – T_{01})\)

or \(T_{02} = T_{01} + \frac{q}{c_p} = 303 K + \frac{900,000 J/kg}{1005 J/(kg \cdot K)} = 1199 K\)

We have enough information to compute the initial Mach number:

\(a_1 = \sqrt{kRT_1} = [1.4(287)(300)]^{1/2} = 347 m/s Ma_1 = \frac{V_1}{a_1} = \frac{75}{347} = 0.216\)

For this Mach number, use Eq. (9.78a) or Table B.4 to find the sonic value \(T^*_0\):

\(\frac{T_0}{T^*_0} = \frac{(k + 1) Ma^2 [2 + (k – 1) Ma^2]}{(1 + k Ma^2)^2}\) (9.78a)

At \(Ma_1\) = 0.216: \(\frac{T_{01}}{T^*_0} \approx 0.1992 or T^*_0 = \frac{303 K}{0.1992} \approx 1521 K\)

Then the stagnation temperature ratio at section 2 is \(T_{02}/T^*_0\) = 1199/1521 = 0.788, which corresponds in Table B.4 to a Mach number \(Ma_2\) ≈ 0.573.

Now use Eqs. (9.78) at \(Ma_1 and Ma_2\) to tabulate the desired property ratios.

Section Ma \(V/V^*\) \(p/p^*\) \(T/T^*\)
1 0.216 0.1051 2.2528 0.2368
2 0.573 0.5398 1.6442 0.8876

\(\frac{T_0}{T^*_0} = \frac{(k + 1) Ma^2 [2 + (k – 1) Ma^2]}{(1 + k Ma^2)^2}\) (9.78a)

\(\frac{T}{T^*} = \frac{(k + 1)^2 Ma^2}{(1 + k Ma^2)^2}\) (9.78b)

\(\frac{p}{p^*} = \frac{k + 1}{1 + k Ma^2}\) (9.78c)

\(\frac{V}{V^*} = \frac{\rho^*}{\rho} = \frac{(k + 1) Ma^2}{1 + k Ma^2}\) (9.78d)

\(\frac{p_0}{p^*_0} = \frac{k + 1}{1 + k Ma^2} \left[\frac{2 + (k – 1) Ma^2}{k + 1}\right]^{k/(k-1)}\) (9.78e)

The exit properties are computed by using these ratios to find state 2 from state 1:

\(V_2 = V_1 \frac{V_2/V^*}{V_1/V^*} = (75 m/s) \frac{0.5398}{0.1051} = 385 m/s\) \(p_2 = p_1 \frac{p_2/p^*}{p_1/p^*} = (150 kPa) \frac{1.6442}{2.2528} = 109 kPa\) \(T_2 = T_1 \frac{T_2/T^*}{T_1/T^*} = (300 K) \frac{0.8876}{0.2368} = 1124 K\)

Part (b)

The maximum allowable heat addition would drive the exit Mach number to unity:

\(T_{02} = T^*_0 = 1521 K\) \(q_{max} = c_p(T^*_0 – T_{01}) = [1005 J/(kg \cdot K)](1521 – 303 K) \approx 1.22 E6 J/kg\)
