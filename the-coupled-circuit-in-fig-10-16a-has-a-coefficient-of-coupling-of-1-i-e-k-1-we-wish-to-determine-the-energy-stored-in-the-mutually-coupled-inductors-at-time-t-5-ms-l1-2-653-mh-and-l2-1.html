Guest Question.

The coupled circuit in Fig. 10.16a has a coefficient of coupling of 1 (i.e., k = 1). We wish to determine the energy stored in the mutually coupled inductors at time t = 5 ms. \(L_{1}\) = 2.653 mH and \(L_{2}\) = 10.61 mH.

Admin
Solution

From the data the mutual inductance is

M = \(\sqrt{L_{1} L_{2}}\) = 5.31 mH

The frequency-domain equivalent circuit is shown in Fig. 10.16b, where the impedance values for \(X_{L_{1}}, X_{L_{2}}\), and \(X_{M}\) are 1, 4, and 2, respectively. The mesh equations for the network are then

(2 + j1)\(I_{1} – j2I_{2} = 24\underline{/0^\circ}\)
-j2\(I_{1} + (4 + j4)I_{2}\) = 0

Solving these equations for the two mesh currents yields

\(I_{1} = 9.41\underline{/-11.31^\circ}\) A and \(I_{2} = 3.33\underline{/+33.69^\circ}\) A

and therefore,

\(i_{1}\)(t) = 9.41 cos (377t – 11.31°) A
\(i_{2}\)(t) = 3.33 cos (377t + 33.69°) A

At t = 5 ms, 377t = 1.885 radians or 108°, and therefore,

\(i_{1}\)(t = 5 ms) = 9.41 cos (108° – 11.31°) = -1.10 A
\(i_{2}\)(t = 5 ms) = 3.33 cos (108° + 33.69°) = -2.61 A

Therefore, the energy stored in the coupled inductors at t = 5 ms is

\(w(t)|_{t=0.005 s} = \frac{1}{2}(2.653)(10^{-3})(-1.10)^{2} + \frac{1}{2} (10.61)(10^{-3})(-2.61)^{2}\)
-(5.31)\((10^{-3})\)(-1.10)(-2.61)
= (1.61)\((10^{-3})\) + (36.14)\((10^{-3})\) – (15.25)\((10^{-3})\)
= 22.5 mJ
