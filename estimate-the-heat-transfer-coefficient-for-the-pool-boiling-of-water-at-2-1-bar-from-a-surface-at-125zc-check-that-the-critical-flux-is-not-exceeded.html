Guest Question.

Estimate the heat-transfer coefficient for the pool boiling of water at 2.1 bar, from a surface at 125\(^o\)C. Check that the critical flux is not exceeded.

Admin
Solution

Physical properties, from steam tables:

 

\(\text { Saturation temperature, } T_{s}=121.8^{\circ} C\)

 

\(\rho_{L}=941.6 kg / m ^{3}, \rho_{v}=1.18 kg / m ^{3}\)

 

\(C_{p L}=4.25 \times 10^{3} J / kg ^{\circ} C\)

 

\(k_{L}=687 \times 10^{-3} W / m ^{\circ} C\)

 

\(\mu_{L}=230 \times 10^{-6} Ns / m ^{2}\)

 

\(\lambda=2198 \times 10^{3} J / kg\)

 

\(\sigma=55 \times 10^{-3} N / m\)

 

\(p_{w} \text { at } 125^{\circ} C =2.321 \times 10^{5} N / m ^{2}\)

 

\(p_{s}=2.1 \times 10^{5} N / m ^{2}\)

 

Use the Foster-Zuber correlation, equation 12.62:

 

\(h_{n b}=0.00122\left[\frac{k_{L}^{0.79} C_{p L}^{0.45} \rho_{L}^{0.49}}{\sigma^{0.5} \mu_{L}^{0.29} \lambda^{0.24} \rho_{v}^{0.24}}\right]\left(T_{w}-T_{s}\right)^{0.24}\left(p_{w}-p_{s}\right)^{0.75}\) (12.62)

 

\(\begin{aligned}h_{b}=& 1.22 \times 10^{-3}\left[\frac{\left(687 \times 10^{-3}\right)^{0.79}\left(4.25 \times 10^{3}\right)^{0.45}(941.6)^{0.49}}{\left(55 \times 10^{-3}\right)^{0.5}\left(230 \times 10^{-6}\right)^{0.29}\left(2198 \times 10^{3}\right)^{0.24} 1.18^{0.24}}\right] \\& \times(125-121.8)^{0.24}\left(2.321 \times 10^{5}-2.10 \times 10^{5}\right)^{0.75} \\=& 3738 W / m ^{2}{ }^{\circ} C\end{aligned}\)

 

Use the Zuber correlation, equation 12.65:

 

\(q_{c}=3.67 \times 10^{4} P_{c}\left(\frac{P}{P_{c}}\right)^{0.35}\left[1-\left(\frac{P}{P_{c}}\right)\right]^{0.9}\) (12.65)

 

\(\begin{aligned}q_{c} &=0.131 \times 2198 \times 10^{3}\left[55 \times 10^{-3} \times 9.81(941.6-1.18) 1.18^{2}\right]^{1 / 4} \\&=1.48 \times 10^{6} W / m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\text { Actual flux }=(125-121.8) 3738=11,962 W / m ^{2} \text {, }\)

 

well below critical flux.
