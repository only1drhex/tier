Guest Question.

Determine the horizontal distance from O of the shear centre of the thin-walled beam section shown in Fig. P.10.13. Note that the position of the centroid of area, G, is given.

Admin
Solution

Since we are only required to find the horizontal position of the shear centre we apply an arbitrary shear load \( S_{y} \) through the shear centre S as shown in Fig. S.10.13. Then, since \( S_{z}=0 \) Eq. (10.22) becomes

\( q_{s}=\frac{\left(S_{y} I_{z y}-S_{z} I_{z}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s+\frac{\left(S_{z} I_{z y}-S_{y} I_{y}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s \) (10.22)

 

\( q_{s}=\frac{S_{y} I_{z y}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s-\frac{S_{y} I_{y}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s\) (i)

From Fig. S.10.13

\(\begin{gathered}I_{z}=2 r t r^{2}+2 r t r^{2}+\int_{0}^{\pi} t r^{2} \cos ^{2} \theta r d \theta=5.6 t r^{3} \\I_{y}=\frac{t(2 r)^{3}}{12}+2 r t(0.86 r)^{2}+\frac{2 t r^{3}}{12}+2 r t(0.36 r)^{2}+\int_{0}^{\pi} t(r \sin \theta+0.14 r)^{2} r d \theta=4.8 t r^{3} \\I_{z y}=2 r t(-0.86 r)(+r)+r 2 t (-0.36 r)(-r)+0=-t r^{3}\end{gathered}\)

Substituting for \( I_{z} \) etc in Eq. (i)

\( q_{s}=-\frac{0.04 S_{y}}{t r^{3}} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s-\frac{0.19 S_{y}}{t r^{3}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s \) (ii)

On the flange 12, \( z=-0.86+s_{1}, y=-r \) and the thickness is 2t. Substituting in Eq. (ii)

\( q_{12}=\frac{S_{y}}{r^{3}}\left(0.45 r_{1}-0.04 s_{1}^{2}\right) \) (iii)

so that at 2 where \( s_{1}=r \),

\( q_{2}=\frac{0.41 S_{y}}{r} \)

In the semicircular portion 23, \( z=0.14 r+r \sin \theta, y=-r \cos \theta \) and the thickness is t.
Then from Eq. (ii)

\( q_{23}=-\frac{0.04 S_{y}}{t r^{3}} \int_{0}^{\theta} t(+0.14 r+r \sin \theta) r d \theta-\frac{0.19 S_{y}}{t r^{3}} \int_{0}^{\theta} t(-r \cos \theta) r d \theta+\frac{0.41 S_{y}}{r} \)

which gives

\( q_{23}=\frac{S_{y}}{r}(0.19 \sin \theta+0.04 \cos \theta-0.006 \theta+0.37) \) (iv)

and

\( q_{3}=\frac{0.31 S_{y}}{r} \)

In the flange 34, \( z=-s_{2}+0.14 r, y=r \) and the thickness is t. Then

\( q_{34}=-\frac{0.04 S_{y}}{t r^{3}} \int_{0}^{s_{2}} t\left(-s_{2}+0.14 r\right) d s_{2}-\frac{0.19 S_{y}}{t r^{3}} \int_{0}^{s_{2}} t r d s_{2} \)

which gives

\( q_{34}=\frac{S_{y}}{r^{3}}\left(0.31 r^{2}-0.196 s_{2} r+0.02 s_{2}^{2}\right) \) (v)

Now taking moments about O

\( S_{y} z_{S}=\int_{0}^{r} q_{12} r d s_{1}+\int_{0}^{\pi} q_{23} r r d \theta+\int_{0}^{2 r} q_{34} r d s_{2} \) (vi)

Substituting in Eq. (vi) for \( q_{12} \) etc from Eqs (iii), (iv) and (v) and carrying out the integration gives

\( z_{S}=2.0 r \)
