Guest Question.

Neglect viscous effects and determine the velocity profile just downstream of the contraction at section 2 in the plane flow shown in Fig. P5.48. Hint: In an inviscid flow, the fluid does not stick to the wall.

Admin
Solution

If viscous effects are negligible, as they are in a short section, Eq. 5.3.25 reduces to

 

\(\frac{D \omega_{z}}{D t}=\nu \nabla^{2} \omega_{z}\) (5.3.25)

 

\(\frac{D \omega_{z}}{D t}=0\)

 

that is, there is no change in vorticity (along a streamline) between sections 1 and 2. Since (see Eq. 3.2.21), at section 1,

 

\(\begin{aligned}\epsilon_{x x} &=\frac{u_{B}-u_{A}}{d x} \\&=\left[u+\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} \frac{d x}{2}-\left(u-\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} \frac{d x}{2}\right)\right] / d x=\frac{\partial u}{\partial x}\end{aligned}\) (3.2.21)

 

\(\omega_{z}=\frac{\partial v}{\partial x}-\frac{\partial u}{\partial y}=-10\)

 

we conclude that, for the lower half of the flow at section 2, \(\frac{\partial u}{\partial y}=10\). This means the velocity profile at section 2 is a straight line with the same slope of the profile at section 1. Since we are neglecting viscosity, the flow can slip at the wall with a slip velocity \(u_{0}\); hence, the velocity distribution at section 2 is \(u_{2}(y)=u_{0}+10 y\). Continuity then allows us to calculate the profile:

 

\(V_{1} A_{1}=V_{2} A_{2}\)

 

\(\frac{1}{2}(10 \times 0.04)(0.04 w)=\left(u_{0}+10 \times 0.02 / 2\right)(0.02 w) . \quad \therefore u_{0}=0.3 m / s\).

 

Finally,

 

\(u_{2}(y)=\underline{0.3+10 y}\)
