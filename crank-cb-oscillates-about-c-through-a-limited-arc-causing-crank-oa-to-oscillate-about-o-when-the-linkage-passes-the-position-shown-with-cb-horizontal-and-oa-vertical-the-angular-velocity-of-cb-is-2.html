Guest Question.

Crank CB oscillates about C through a limited arc, causing crank OA to oscillate about O. When the linkage passes the position shown with CB horizontal and OA vertical, the angular velocity of CB is 2 rad /s counterclockwise. For this instant, determine the angular velocities of OA and AB.

Admin
Solution

I (Vector). The relative-velocity equation \(v _{A}= v _{B}+ v _{A / B}\) is rewritten as

 

1 \(\omega_{O A} \times r _{A}=\omega_{C B} \times r _{B}+\omega_{A B} \times r _{A / B}\)

 

where \(\omega_{O A}=\omega_{O A} k \quad \omega_{C B}=2 k rad / s \quad \omega_{A B}=\omega_{A B} k\)

 

\(r _{A}=100 j mm \quad r _{B}=-75 i mm \quad r _{A / B}=-175 i +50 j mm\)

 

Substitution gives

 

\(\omega_{O A} k \times 100 j =2 k \times(-75 i )+\omega_{A B} k \times(-175 i +50 j )\)

 

\(-100 \omega_{O A} i =-150 j -175 \omega_{A B} j -50 \omega_{A B} i\)

 

Matching coefficients of the respective i- and j-terms gives

 

\(-100 \omega_{O A}+50 \omega_{A B}=0 \quad 25\left(6+7 \omega_{A B}\right)=0\)

 

the solutions of which are

 

2 \(\omega_{A B}=-6 / 7 rad / s \quad \text { and } \quad \omega_{O A}=-3 / 7 rad / s\) Ans.

 

II (Scalar-Geometric). Solution by the scalar geometry of the vector triangle is particularly simple here since \(v _{A} \text { and } v _{B}\) are at right angles for this special position of the linkages. First, we compute \(v_{B}\), which is

 

\([v=r \omega] \quad v_{B}=0.075(2)=0.150 m / s\)

 

and represent it in its correct direction as shown. The vector \(v _{A / B}\) must be perpendicular to AB, and the angle Î¸ between \(v _{A / B} \text { and } v _{B}\) is also the angle made by AB with the horizontal direction. This angle is given by

 

\(\tan \theta=\frac{100-50}{250-75}=\frac{2}{7}\)

 

3 The horizontal vector \(v _{A}\) completes the triangle for which we have

 

\(v_{A / B}=v_{B} / \cos \theta=0.150 / \cos \theta\)

 

\(v_{A}=v_{B} \tan \theta=0.150(2 / 7)=0.30 / 7 m / s\)

 

The angular velocities become

 

\([\omega=v / r] \quad \omega_{A B}=\frac{v_{A / B}}{\overline{A B}}=\frac{0.150}{\cos \theta} \frac{\cos \theta}{0.250-0.075}\)

 

= 6/7 rad/s CW Ans.

 

\([\omega=v / r] \quad \omega_{O A}=\frac{v_{A}}{\overline{O A}}=\frac{0.30}{7} \frac{1}{0.100}=3 / 7 rad / s CW\) Ans.
