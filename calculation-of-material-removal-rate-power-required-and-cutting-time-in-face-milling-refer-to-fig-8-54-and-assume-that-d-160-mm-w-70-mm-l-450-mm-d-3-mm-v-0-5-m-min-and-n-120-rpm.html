Guest Question.

Calculation of material-removal rate, power required, and cutting time in face milling

Refer to Fig. 8.54, and assume that D = 160 mm, \(w\) = 70 mm, \(l\)= 450 mm, d = 3 mm, \(v\) =0.5 m/min, and N = 120 rpm. The cutter has 10 inserts and the workpiece material is a high strength aluminum alloy. Calculate the material- removal rate, cutting time, and feed per tooth, and estimate the power required.

Admin
Solution

The cross section of the cut is \( wd\) = \((70)(3) = 210 mm^2.\) Since the workpiece speed \( v\) is 0.5 m/min = 500 mm/min, the material-removal rate is

MRR=\( (210)(0.5 × 1000) = 105,000 \ mm^{3}\)/min.

The cutting time is given by Eq. (8.44) as

\(t=\frac{l+2l_{c}}{v} . \)

Note from Fig. 8.54 that for this problem,

\(l_{c}=\frac{D}{2} \) or 80 mm

so that, thus, the cutting time is

\(t=\frac{(450+2\times 80)}{0.5\times 1000} =1.22 \) min

We obtain the feed per tooth from Eq. (8.43).

\(f=\frac{v}{Nn}\) (8.43)

Noting that N = 120 rpm,

\(f=\frac{0.5\times 1000}{(360)(10)} =0.138 \ mm /tooth. \)

For this material, the unit power can be taken from Table 8.3

Table 8.3,

Approximate Specific-Energy Requirements in Machining Operations
Material Specific energy* (W-s/mm\(^{3}\))
Aluminum alloys 0.4–1.1
Cast irons 1.6–5.5
Copper alloys 1.4–3.3
High-temperature alloys 3.3–8.5

to be 1.1 W-s/\( mm^{3} \); hence, the power can be estimated as

Power = \((1.1)\left\lgroup\frac{105,000}{60} \right\rgroup= 1925 \) W = 1. 925 kW.
