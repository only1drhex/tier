Guest Question.

Figure 10.6 illustrates a graph that has a variable load torque and constant drive torque. Each cycle lasts three revolutions (6π radians). In addition,

\(ω_{0} = 180 rpm = 18.85 rad/sec; I_{machine} = 125 kg-m^{2}\)

Assuming steady-state conditions, determine

(a) the average power required
(b) the maximum and minimum rotational speeds throughout a cycle
(c) the mass of a 0.6-meter-diameter solid disc flywheel to produce \(C_{S} = 0.025\)

Admin
Solution

Since the averages of the absolute values of the drive and load torques must be equal, the con-stant drive torque is

\( T_{D} = – \frac{area \ under \ load \ torque \ curve}{cycle \ of \ operation}\)

 

\(= \left\lgroup\frac{400 × 2π + 550 × π}{6 π} \right\rgroup N-m = 225 N-m \)
TABLE 10.3 ENERGY VALUES AT THE INTERSECTION POINTS IN FIGURE 10.6
Point Energy Level (N-m)
1 \(E_{1}\)
2 \(E_{2} = E_{1} –(400–225)×2π = E_{1} –350π\)
3 \(E_{3} = E_{2} +225×2π= E_{1} +100 π\)
4 \(E_{4} = E_{3} –(550–225)×π = E_{1} –(550–225)×π\)

Energies at the intersection points between the load and drive torque curves are listed in Table 10.3.
Examination of Table 10.3 shows that the maximum energy level corresponds to point 3, and the minimum energy level is at point 2. Thus, \(∆E_{max}\) is calculated as

\(∆E_{max} = E_{3} – E_{2} = 450π N-m\)

Based on the above, the solutions are

(a)

\(T_{D}ω_{0} = 225 N-m × 18.85 rad/sec = 4240 N-m/sec = 4.24 kW \)

(b)

\( I = \frac{∆E_{max} }{ω_{0}^{2} C_{S}}; I = I_{machine}\)

and therefore

\(C_{S} = \frac{∆E_{max} }{ω_{0}^{2} I } = \frac{450 π}{18.85^{2} × 125} = 0.0318 \)

However,

\(C_{S} = \frac{ω_{max} – ω_{min}}{ω_{0}}\)

Therefore

\(ω_{max} – ω_{min} = C_{S} × ω_{0} = (0 0318 × 18.85) rad/sec = 0.600 rad/sec\)

and

\( ω_{min} = ω_{0} – \frac{ω_{max} – ω_{min}}{2} = ( 18.85 – 0.300) rad/sec = 18.55 rad/sec \)

 

\(ω_{max} = ( 18.85 + 0.300) rad/sec = 19.15 rad/sec \)

(c)

\( I = \frac{∆E_{max} }{ω_{0}^{2} C_{S}} = \left\lgroup\frac{450\pi }{18.85^{2} \times 0.025} \right\rgroup kg-m^{2} = 159 kg-m^{2} \)

Therefore, using Equation (10.2-14), we obtain

\(I_{flywheel} = I – I_{machine} = (159 – 125 ) kg-m^{2} = 34 kg-m^{2} \)

But, using Equation (C.9.9)

\(I_{flywheel} = \frac{1}{2} mass r^{2} = 34 kg-m^{2} = \frac{1}{2} mass 0.3 m^{2}\)

 

mass = 756 kg
