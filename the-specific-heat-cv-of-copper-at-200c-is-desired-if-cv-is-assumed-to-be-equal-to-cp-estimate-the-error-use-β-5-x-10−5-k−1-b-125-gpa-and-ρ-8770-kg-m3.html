Guest Question.

The specific heat \( C_{v} \text { of copper at } 200^{\circ} C \text { is desired. If } C_{v} \text { is assumed to be equal to } C_{p} \) estimate the error. Use \( \beta=5 \times 10^{-5} K ^{-1}, B=125 GPa , \text { and } \rho=8770 kg / m ^{3} \).

Admin
Solution

Equation (11.59) provides the relation

\( C_{p}-C_{v}=v T \beta^{2} B \) (11.59).

\( C_{P}-C_{v}=v T \beta^{2} B=\left(\frac{1}{8770}\right)(473)\left(5 \times 10^{-5}\right)^{2}\left(125 \times 10^{9}\right)=16.85 J / kg \cdot K \).

From Table B.4, the specific heat of copper is approximated at 200°C to be about 0.40 kJ/kg·K. Hence,

\( C_{v}=C_{p}-0.01685=0.4-0.01685=0.383 kJ / kg \cdot K \).
