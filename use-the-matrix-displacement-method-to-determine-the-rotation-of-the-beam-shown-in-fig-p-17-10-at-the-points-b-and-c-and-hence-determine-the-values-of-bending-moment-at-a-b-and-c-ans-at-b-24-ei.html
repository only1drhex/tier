Guest Question.

Use the matrix displacement method to determine the rotation of the beam shown in Fig. P.17.10 at the points B and C and hence determine the values of bending moment at A, B, and C.
Ans. At B: -24/EI. At C: 12/EI.
\(M_{ A }=-106 kNm , M_{ B }=-58 kNm , M_{ C }=-40 kNm\).

Admin
Solution

Initially the fixed end moments in the spans AB and BC are calculated. From Table 16.6

FEMs
Local case \(M_{A B}^{F}\) \(M_{B A}^{F}\)
\(-\frac{W L}{8}\) \(+\frac{W L}{8}\)
\(-\frac{W a b^{2}}{L^{2}}\) \(+\frac{W a^{2} b}{L^{2}}\)
\(-\frac{w L^{2}}{12}\) \(+\frac{W L^{2}}{12}\)
\(-\frac{W}{L^{2}}\left[\frac{L^{2}}{2}\left(b^{2}-a^{2}\right)-\frac{2}{3} L\left(b^{3}-a^{3}\right)\right.\)

 

\(\left.+\frac{1}{4}\left(b^{4}-a^{4}\right)\right]\)

 \(+\frac{w b^{3}}{L^{2}}\left(\frac{L}{3}-\frac{b}{4}\right)\)
\(+\frac{M_{0} b}{L^{2}}(2 a-b)\) \(+\frac{M_{0} a}{L^{2}}(2 b-a)\)
\(-\frac{6 E / \delta}{L^{2}}\) \(-\frac{6 E / \delta}{L^{2}}\)
0 \(-\frac{3 E / \delta}{L^{2}}\)
\(M_{ AB }{ }^{ F }=-M_{ BA }{ }^{ F }=\frac{-30 \times 6^{2}}{12}=-90 kNm\)

and

\(M_{ BC }{ }^{ F }=-M_{ CB }{ }^{ F }=\frac{-80 \times 4}{8}=-40 kNm\)

Therefore, at B, there will be an out-of-balance moment of 50 kNm causing a rotation \(\theta_{ B }\). The fixed end moment in BC at C will be balanced by the moment from the cantilever overhang so that the resultant out-of-balance moment at C may be regarded as zero although there will be a rotation \(\theta_{ C }\). Further, the horizontal and vertical displacements at A, B, and C are zero so that for the span AB Eq. (17.31)

\(\left\{\begin{array}{c}F_{y, i} \\M_{i} \\F_{y, j} \\M_{j}\end{array}\right\}=E I\left[\begin{array}{cccc}12 / L^{3} & -6 / L^{2} & -12 / L^{3} & -6 / L^{2} \\-6 / L^{2} & 4 / L & 6 / L^{2} & 2 / L \\-12 / L^{3} & 6 / L^{2} & 12 / L^{3} & 6 / L^{2} \\-6 / L^{2} & 2 / L & 6 / L^{2} & 4 / L\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}v_{i} \\\theta_{i} \\v_{j} \\\theta_{j}\end{array}\right\}\) (17.31)

reduces to

\(\left\{\begin{array}{c}M_{ A } \\M_{ B }\end{array}\right\}=E(2 I)\left[\begin{array}{cc}4 / L_{ AB } & 2 / L_{ AB } \\2 / L_{ AB } & 4 / L_{ AB }\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}\theta_{ A }=0 \\\theta_{ B }\end{array}\right\}\) (i)

Similarly for the span BC

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}M_{ B } \\M_{ C }\end{array}\right\}=E I\left[\begin{array}{ll}4 / L_{ BC } & 2 / L_{ BC } \\2 / L_{ BC } & 4 / L_{ BC }\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}\theta_{ B } \\\theta_{ C }\end{array}\right\}\) (ii)

Substituting the relevant values in these two matrices and combining the stiffness matrices for the two spans gives

\(\left\{\begin{array}{c}-50 \\0\end{array}\right\}=E I\left[\begin{array}{cc}2.33 & 0.5 \\0.5 & 1\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}\theta_{ B } \\\theta_{ C }\end{array}\right\}\) (iii)

From Eq. (iii)

\(0=0.5 \theta_{B}+\theta_{C}\)

so that

\(\theta_{B}=-2 \theta_{C}\)

Again from Eq. (iii)

\(-50=E I(-2 \times 2.33+0.5) \theta_{C}\),

which gives

\(\theta_{C}=12 / E I\)

Then

\(\theta_{B}=-24 / E I\)

From Eq. (i) in which \(\theta_{ A }=0 \text { and } \theta_{ B }=-24 / E I, M_{ AB }=-16 kNm\). Then, the total moment at A is \(M_{ A }=-90-16=-106 kNm\). Similarly, the total moment at B is \(M_{ B }=90-32=58 kNm\).
Finally, the total moment at C is \(M_{ C }=40 kNm\).
