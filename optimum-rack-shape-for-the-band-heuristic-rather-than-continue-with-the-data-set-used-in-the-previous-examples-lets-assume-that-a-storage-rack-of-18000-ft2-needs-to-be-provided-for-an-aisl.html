Guest Question.

Optimum rack shape for the band heuristic

Rather than continue with the data set used in , let’s assume that a storage rack of \(18,000 \mathrm{ft}^{2}\) needs to be provided for an aisle. Since a storage rack is provided on either side of the aisle, \(9000 \mathrm{ft}^{2}\) of rack space is needed on each side. Recall that the P/D station is located at the lower lefthand corner of the rack, randomized storage is used, and the \(S / R\) machine follows the Chebyshev metric.

Admin
Solution

Suppose the S/R machine travels at \(300 \mathrm{fpm}\) and \(60 \mathrm{fpm}\) in the horizontal and vertical directions, respectively. Further suppose that 30 picks will be performed on each pick trip and that the rack is divided into two bands, that is, \(n=30\) and \(k=2\). As shown in Table \(10.22\),

one alternative is to provide a \(15^{\prime}\)-tall rack that is \(600^{\prime}\) long, for which the expected travel time is equal to \(4.3951\) minutes.

Given other configurations shown in Table 10.22, in this particular example, the optimum rack height is approximately \(25^{\prime} 3^{\prime \prime}\), plus or minus \(3^{\prime \prime}\), and the minimum expected travel time is \(3.5634\) minutes. Note that the “optimum” shape factor for the rack is equal to \(0.3542\), which is far from SIT. We believe this is due to the band heuristic (with \(k=2\) ), which tends to yield less zigzagging when the band width is small. Taller racks lead to wider bands, which cause zigzagging.

According to \([10]\), the most desirable rack shape depends on the heuristic used for sequencing the pick points. With certain heuristics, the expected trip length decreases as the rack becomes SIT. Some of these heuristics generate sequences that require less travel time than those obtained from the band heuristic; the reader may refer to [10] for details. Also, the results in [22] suggest that, for sufficiently large \(n\) values, the exact shape of the region has little or no impact on the expected length of the optimum picking sequence. Furthermore, we remind the reader that the above “optimum” rack shape is determined only with respect to the expected travel time for the \(\mathrm{S} / \mathrm{R}\) machine. When the rack cost is included, the “optimum” rack shape may be different from the one we computed in Table 10.22.
