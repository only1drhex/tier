Guest Question.

A single-thread steel worm rotates at 1800 rev/min, meshing with a 24-tooth worm gear transmitting 3 hp to the output shaft. The worm pitch diameter is 3 in and the tangential diametral pitch of the gear is 4 teeth/in. The normal pressure angle is 14.5°. The ambient temperature is 70°F. The application factor is 1.25 and the design factor is 1; gear face width is 2 in, lateral case area 600 \( in^{2}\) , and the gear is chill-cast bronze.
(a) Find the gear geometry.
(b) Find the transmitted gear forces and the mesh efficiency.
(c) Is the mesh sufficient to handle the loading?
(d) Estimate the lubricant sump temperature.

Admin
Solution

(a) \( m_{G} = N_{G}∕N_{W}\) = 24∕1 = 24, gear: D =\( N_{G}∕P_{t} =\) 24∕4 = 6.000 in, worm: d = 3.000 in. The axial circu-lar pitch px is \(p_{x} = π∕P_{t} =\) π∕4 = 0.7854 in. C = (3 + 6)∕2 = 4.5 in

Eq. (15–39):\( a = \frac{px}{π} = 0.3183p_{x}\) a = \(p_{x}∕π \)= 0.7854∕π = 0.250 in

Eq. (15–40)\( b = \frac{1.157p_{x}}{π} = 0.3683p_{x} \): b = 0.3683\(p_{x} \)= 0.3683(0.7854) = 0.289 in

Eq. (15–41) \( h_{t} = \begin{cases} \frac{2.157p_{x}} {π} = 0.6866p_{x} &p_{x} ≥ 0.16 in \\ \frac{2.200p_{x}}{π} + 0.002 = 0.7003p_{x} + 0.002 &p_{x} < 0.16 in \end{cases} \): \(h_{t} = 0.6866p_{x} = 0.6866(0.7854) = 0.539 \)in

Eq. (15–42) do = d + 2a : \(d_{o} = 3 + 2(0.250) = 3.500 \)in

Eq. (15–43) \(d_{r} = d − 2b\): \(d_{r} = 3 − 2(0.289) = 2.422\) in

Eq. (15–44) \( D_{t} = D + 2a \): \(D_{t} = 6 + 2(0.250) = 6.500\) in

Eq. (15–45) \( D_{r} = D − 2b \): \( D_{r} = 6 − 2(0.289) = 5.422\) in

Eq. (15–46) c = b − a: \(c = 0.289 − 0.250 = 0.039 \)in

Eq. (15–47) \( (F_{W}) _{max} = 2 \sqrt{(\frac{D_{t}}{2})^{2}− (\frac{D}{2} − a )^{2} }= 2\sqrt{2Da}\): \((F_{W})_{max} = 2\sqrt{2(6)0.250} = 3.464 \)in

The tangential speeds of the worm, \(V_{W}\), and gear, \(V_{G},\) are, respectively,

\(V_{W} = π(3)1800∕12 =\) 1414 ft/min \( V_{G} =\frac{ π(6)1800∕24}{12} = 117.8 \)ft/min

The lead of the worm, from Eq. (13–27), is \(L = p_{x} N_{W} = 0.7854(1)\) = 0.7854 in. The lead angle λ, from Eq. (13–28) \(\tan \lambda =\frac{L}{\pi d_{w}} \), is

\( λ = tan^{−1} \frac{L}{πd} = tan^{−1}\frac{ 0.7854}{π(3)} = 4.764° \)

The normal diametral pitch for a worm gear is the same as for a helical gear, which from Eq. (13–18), with ψ = λ is

\( P_{n} = \frac{P_{t}}{cos λ} =\frac{ 4}{cos 4.764°} = 4.014\)

 

\(p_{n} = \frac{π}{P_{n}}= \frac{π}{ 4.014 }= 0.7827 in \)

The sliding velocity, from Eq. (15–62), is

\( V_{s} = \frac{πdn_{W}}{12 cos λ} = \frac{π(3)1800}{12 cos 4.764°} = 1419 ft/min \)

(b) The coefficient of friction, from Eq. (15–38) \( f= \begin{cases} 0.15 &V_{s} = 0\\0.124 exp(−0.074V^{0.645}_{ s} ) &0 < V_{s} ≤ 10 ft/min\\ 0.103 exp(−0.110V^{0.450}_ {s} ) + 0.012 &V_{s} > 10 ft/min \end{cases} \), is

f = 0.103 exp\([−0.110(1419)^{0.450}] + \)0.012 = 0.0178

The efficiency e, from Eq. (13–46), is

\( e = \frac{cos ϕ_{n} − f tan λ}{ cos ϕ_{n} + f cot λ }= \frac{cos 14.5° − 0.0178 tan 4.764°}{cos 14.5° + 0.0178 cot 4.764°} = 0.818 \)

The designer used\( n_{d} = 1, K_{a} = \)1.25 and an output horsepower of \(H_{0}\) = 3 hp. The gear tangential force com-ponent\( W^{t}_{G}, \)from Eq. (15–58), is

\( W^{t}_{G} = \frac{33 000n_{d} H_{0} K_{a}}{VG_{e}} =\frac{ 33 000(1)3(1.25)}{117.8(0.818) }= 1284 lbf \)

The tangential force on the worm is given by Eq. (15–57):

\( W^{t}_{W} = W^{t}_{G}\frac{cos ϕ_{n} sin λ + f cos λ}{cos ϕ_{n} cos λ − f sin λ}\)

 

\(= 1284\frac{cos 14.5° sin 4.764° + 0.0178 cos 4.764°}{ cos 14.5° cos 4.764° − 0.0178 sin 4.764° }= 131 lbf \)

(c)

Eq. (15–34) \( C_{s} = \begin{cases} 1000 &C > 3 & D_{m} ≤ 8 in\\ 1412 − 456 log D_{m} &C > 3 &D_{m} > 8 in \end{cases} \) : \( C_{s} \)= 1000

Eq. (15–36) \( C_{m}= \begin{cases} 0.02\sqrt{−m^{2}_{G} + 40m_{G} − 76} +0.46 &3 < m_{G} ≤ 20 \\ 0.0107 \sqrt{−m^{2} _{G} + 56m_{G} + 5145} & 20 < m_{G} ≤ 76\\ 1.1483 − 0.006 58m_{G} & m_{G} > 76 \end{cases} \) : \(C_{m} = 0.0107\sqrt{−24^{2} + 56(24) + 5145 }= 0.823\)

Eq. (15–37) \( C_{v}= \begin{cases} 0.659 exp (−0.0011V_{s})&V_{s} < 700 ft/min \\ 13.31 V^{−0.571}_{s} & 700 ≤ V_{s} < 3000 ft/min \\ 65.52 V^{−0.774}_{s} & V_{s} > 3000 ft/min \end{cases}\) : \( C_{v} = 13.31(1419)^{−0.571} = 0.211\)

Eq. (15–28): \( (W ^{t})_{all} = C_{s} D^{0.8} (F_{e})_{G} C_{m} C_{v}\)

 

\(= 1000(6)^{0.8}(2)0.823(0.211) = 1456 \)lbf

The friction force\( W_{f} \)is given by Eq. (15–61):

\( W_{f} = \frac{fW^{t}_{G}}{f sin λ − cos ϕ_{n} cos λ} = \frac{0.0178(1284)}{0.0178 sin 4.764° − cos 14.5° cos 4.764°}\)

=-23.7 lbf

The power dissipated in frictional work \(H_{f}\) is given by Eq. (15–63):

\( H_{f} = \frac{∣W_{f} ∣V_{s}}{33 000 }hp=\frac{∣W_{f} ∣V_{s}}{33 000 }= \frac{∣−23.7∣1419}{33 000 }= 1.02 hp \)

The worm and gear transmitted powers,\( H_{W} \)and \(H_{G}]\), are given by

\(H_{W} =\frac{ W ^{t}_{W} V_{W}}{33 000} =\frac{ 131(1414)}{33 000} = 5.61 hp H_{G} = \frac{W ^{t}_{G} V_{G}}{33 000} = \frac{1284(117.8)}{33 000} = 4.58 hp \)

Gear power is satisfactory. Now,

\(P_{n} = P_{t}∕cos λ = 4∕cos 4.764° = 4.014\)

\(p_{n} = π∕P_{n} = π∕4.014 = 0.7827 \)in

The bending stress in a gear tooth is given by Buckingham’s adaptation of the Lewis equation, Eq. (15–53), as

\((σ)_{G} =\frac{ W^{t}_{G}}{p_{n} F_{G} y} =\frac{ 1284}{0.7827(2)(0.1)} = 8200 psi \)

Stress in gear is satisfactory.

(d)

Eq. (15–52): \(A_{min} = 43.2C^{1.7} = 43.2(4.5)^{1.7} = 557 in^{2}\)

The gear case has a lateral area of 600\( in^{2} .\)

Eq. (15–49): \(H_{loss} = 33 000(1 − e)H_{in} = 33 000(1 − 0.818)5.61\)

= 33 690 ft · lbf/min

Eq. (15–50)\( \hbar _{CR}=\begin{cases}\frac{n_{w}}{6494} +0.13 & no fan on worm shaft \\\frac{n_{w}}{3939} +0.13 & fan on worm shaft\end{cases} \):

\( \hbar _{CR} = \frac{n_{W}}{3939 }+ 0.13 =\frac{ 1800}{3939} + 0.13 = 0.587 ft · lbf/(min · in^{2} · °F) \):

Eq. (15–51) \(t_{s}=t_{a}+\frac{H_{loss}}{\hbar _{CR}A} =\frac{33000(1-e)(H)_{in}}{\hbar _{CR}A} +t_{a}\):

\( t_{s} = t_{a} +\frac{H_{loss}}{_{CR} A} = 70 +\frac{33 690}{ 0.587(600)}\) = 166°F
