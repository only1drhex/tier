Guest Question.

A thin-walled beam has the cross section shown in Fig. P.13.15 and is simply supported over a span of 2 m. If the beam carries a horizontal uniformly distributed load of 10 kN/m applied in the plane of its flange together with a vertical uniformly distributed load of 20 kN/m applied in the plane of its leg, both loads being over the complete span, calculate the magnitude and direction of the deflection of the mid-span point. Take \(E=200000 N / mm ^{2}\).
Ans. 24.4 mm at 64.8° to the right of vertical.

Admin
Solution

Referring to Fig. P.13.15 the second moments of area of the beam section are given by

\(I_{z}=100 \times 3 \times 37.5^{2}+\frac{2 \times 150^{3}}{12}+2 \times 150 \times 37.5^{2}=1.41 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\)

 

\(I_{y}=\frac{3 \times 100^{3}}{12}+3 \times 100 \times 12.5^{2}+150 \times 2 \times 12.5^{2}=0.34 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\)

 

\(I_{z y}=3 \times 100(-12.5)(+37.5)+2 \times 150(+12.5)(-37.5)=-0.28 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\)

The bending moments at any section of the beam due to the applied uniformly distributed loads are, in general terms

\(M_{z}=\frac{w_{y} L}{2} x-\frac{w_{y} x^{2}}{2}\)

see Eq. (i) of Ex. 13.4 and the sign conventions in Section 9.5)

\(M=\frac{w L}{2} x-\frac{w x^{2}}{2}\) Eq. (i) of Ex. 13.4
That is

\(M_{z}=\frac{w_{y}}{2}\left(L x-x^{2}\right)\) (i)

Similarly

\(M_{ y }=\frac{w_{z}}{2}\left(L x-x^{2}\right)\) (ii)

Substituting in Eq. (13.14)

\(\frac{ d ^{2} u}{ d x^{2}}=\frac{M_{y} I_{z}-M_{z} I_{z y}}{E\left(I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}\right)}\) (13.14)

gives

\(\frac{ d ^{2} u}{ d x^{2}}=\frac{1}{2 E\left(I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}\right)}\left[w_{z}\left(L x-x^{2}\right) I_{z}-w_{y}\left(L x-x^{2}\right) I_{z y}\right]\) (iii)

Then

\(\frac{ d u}{ d x}=\frac{1}{2 E\left(I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}\right)}\left[w_{z}\left(\frac{L x^{2}}{2}-\frac{x^{3}}{3}\right) I_{z}-w_{y}\left(\frac{L x^{2}}{2}-\frac{x^{3}}{3}\right) I_{z y}+C_{1}\right]\) (iv)

and

\(u=\frac{1}{2 E\left(I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}\right)}\left[w_{z}\left(\frac{L x^{3}}{6}-\frac{x^{4}}{12}\right) I_{z}-w_{y}\left(\frac{L x^{3}}{6}-\frac{x^{4}}{12}\right) I_{z y}+C_{1} x+C_{2}\right]\) (v)

When x = 0, u = 0 so that from Eq. (v), \(C_{2}=0\). Also, at the mid-span point (du/dx) = 0 from symmetry. Then, from Eq. (iv)

\(0=w_{z}\left(\frac{L^{3}}{8}-\frac{L^{3}}{24}\right) I_{z}-w_{y}\left(\frac{L^{3}}{8}-\frac{L^{3}}{24}\right) I_{z y}+C_{1}\)

from which

\(C_{1}=\frac{L^{3}}{12}\left(-w_{z} I_{z}+w_{y} I_{z y}\right)\)

Eq. (v) then becomes

\(u=\frac{1}{24 E\left(I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}\right)}\left[w_{z}\left(2 L x^{3}-x^{4}\right) I_{z}-w_{y}\left(2 L x^{3}-x^{4}\right) I_{z y}+2 L^{3}\left(-w_{z} I_{z}+w_{y} I_{z y}\right) x\right]\) (vi)

At the mid-span point where x = L/2 Eq. (vi) gives

\(u_{ m . s }=\frac{5 L^{4}}{384 E\left(I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}\right)}\left(w_{y} I_{z y}-w_{z} I_{z}\right)\) (vii)

The expression for the vertical displacement of the mid-span point may be deduced directly from Eq. (vii) and is

\(v_{ m . s }=\frac{5 L^{4}}{384 E\left(I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}\right)}\left(w_{z} I_{z y}-w_{y} I_{y}\right)\) (viii)

(Note the benefit of working in general terms).
Substituting the relevant values in Eqs (vii) and (viii) gives

\(u_{ m . s }=-22.1 mm , \quad v_{ m . s }=-10.4 mm\)

The resultant deflection, δ, is then

\(\delta=\sqrt{\left(22.1^{2}+10.4^{2}\right)}=24.4 mm\)

at an angle of \(\tan ^{-1}(22.1 / 10.4)=64.8^{\circ}\) downwards and to the right of vertical.
