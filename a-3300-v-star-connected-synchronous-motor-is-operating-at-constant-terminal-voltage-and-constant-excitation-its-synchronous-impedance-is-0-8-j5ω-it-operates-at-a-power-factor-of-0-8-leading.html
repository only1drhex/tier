Guest Question.

A 3300 V, star-connected synchronous motor is operating at constant terminal voltage and constant excitation. Its synchronous impedance is \( 0.8 + j5\Omega\) . It operates at a power factor of 0.8 leading when drawing 800 kW from the mains. Find its power factor when the input is increased to 1200 kW, excitation remaining constant

Admin
Solution

Figure 8.60 gives the circuit model of the motor

\( Z_{s}=0.8+j 5=5.06 \angle 81^{\circ} ; \quad \alpha=9^{\circ}\)

 

\( V_{t}=3300 / \sqrt{3}=1905 V\)

 

\( P_{e}(\text { in })=800 / 3=266.7 kW\) (per phase)

\( \cos \phi=0.8\) leading

\( \therefore\) \( Q_{e} \text { (in) }=-P_{e} \text { (in) } \tan \phi=-200 kVAR\)

Note that \( Q_{e}\) (in) is negative because the power factor is leading.

From Eqs (8.87a) \( P_{e} \text { (in) }=-P_{e} \text { (out) } =\frac{V_{t}^{2}}{Z_{s}^{2}} R_{a}+\frac{V_{t} E_{f}}{Z_{s}} \sin (\delta-\alpha)\) and (8.87b) \( Q_{e} \text { (in) }=-Q_{e} \text { (out) } =\frac{V_{t}^{2}}{Z_{s}^{2}} X_{s}-\frac{V_{t} E_{f}}{Z_{s}} \cos (\delta-\alpha)\)

\( P_{e}(\text { in })=\frac{V_{t}^{2}}{Z_{S}^{2}} R_{a}+\frac{V_{t} E_{f}}{Z_{S}} \sin (\delta-\alpha)\) (i)

 

\( Q_{e}(\text { in })=\frac{V_{t}^{2}}{Z_{S}^{2}} X_{S}-\frac{E_{f} V_{t}}{Z_{S}} \cos (\delta-\alpha)\) (ii)

Substituting the values

 

\( 266.7 \times 1000=\frac{(1905)^{2} \times 0.8}{(5.06)^{2}}+\frac{(1905) E_{f}}{5.06} \sin (\delta-\alpha)\)

 

\( -200 \times 1000=\frac{(1905)^{2} \times 5}{(5.06)^{2}}-\frac{(1905) E_{f}}{5.06} \cos (\delta-\alpha)\)

These equations simplify as

\( E_{f} \sin (\delta-\alpha)=407.2\)

 

\( E_{f} \cos (\delta-\alpha)=2413.6\)

from which \( E_{f}\) is obtained as

\( E_{f}=2447.7 V\)

Under new operating conditions

\( P_{e}(\text { in })=1200 / 3=400 kW\)

Substituting in (i),

\( 400 \times 1000=\frac{(1905)^{2} \times 0.8}{(5.06)^{2}}+\frac{(1905) \times(2447.7)}{5.06} \sin (\delta-\alpha)\)

Or \( \sin (\delta-\alpha)=0.31\) or \( \delta-\alpha=18^{\circ}\)

Now Eq. (ii) is used to find \( Q_{e}\) for \( (\delta-\alpha)=18^{\circ}\)

 

\( Q_{e}=\frac{(1905)^{2} \times 5}{(5.06)^{2}}-\frac{1905 \times 2447.7}{5.06} \cos 18^{\circ}=-167.7 kVAR\)

Power factor \( =\cos \left(\tan ^{-1} Q_{e} / P_{e}\right)=\cos \left(\tan ^{-1} 167.7 / 400\right)=0.92\) leading
