Guest Question.

Given the state-space equations \(\dot{x}=Ax+B\eta \)

where

\(A=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & -1 &-3 \\ -6 & 0 & -2 \\ 5 & -2 & -4 \end{matrix} \right] \)

 

\(B=\left[\begin{matrix}0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right] \)

determine the transformation matrix P so that the new state equations are in the Jordan canonical form.

Admin
Solution

The transformation matrix P is determined from the eigenvectors of the A matrix:

\(\left|\lambda I -A\right|=0 \)

 

\(\left|\begin{matrix} \lambda & 1 & 3 \\ 6 & \lambda & 2 \\ -5 & 2 & \lambda +4 \end{matrix} \right|=\lambda ^3+4\lambda ^2+5\lambda +2 \)

The roots of the characteristic equation are \(\lambda =-2 , \lambda =-1 ,and \lambda =-1\). We have a repeated eigenvalue \(\lambda =-1\) The eigenvectors for the repeated roots are determined using equation (9.69):

\((\lambda _i -A)P_1 =0\)

 

\((\lambda _i -A)P_2 =-P_1\)

The eigenvector \(P_1\) is determined from the following equations:

\(\left[\begin{matrix} -1 & 1 & 3 \\ 6 & -1 & 2 \\ -5 & 2 & 3 \end{matrix} \right]\left[\begin{matrix} P_{11} \\ P_{21} \\ P_{31} \end{matrix} \right] =0\) \(-P_{11}+P_{21}+3P_{31}=0\)

 

\(6P_{11}-P_{21}+2P_{31}=0\)

 

\(-5P_{11}+2P_{21}+3P_{31}=0\)

From the first two equations we can eliminate\(P_{21}\):

\(5P_{11}+5P_{31}=0\)

Let \(P_{11}=1 then P_{31}= -1\).

From the first equation:

\(-P_{11}+P_{21}+3P_{31}=0\)

or
\(P_{21}=P_{11}-3P_{31}=4\)

The eigenvector \(P_1\) is as follows:

\(P_1=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 4 \\ -1 \end{matrix} \right] \)

The second eigenvector for \(\lambda =-1\) is determined from the equation \((\lambda _i -A)P_2 =-P_1\)

\(-P_{12}+P_{22}+3P_{32}=-1\)

 

\(6P_{12}-P_{22}+2P_{32}=-4\)

 

\(-5P_{12}+2P_{22}+3P_{32}=1\)

Eliminating \(P_{22}\) from the first two equations yields:

\(5P_{12}+5P_{32}=-5\)

Let \(P_{12}=1, therefore P_{32}= -2\) Substituting \(P_{12} and P_{32} \) into the first equation yields \(P_{22}\):

\(P_{22}-1 +P_{12}-3 P_{32}=6\)

The second eigenvector is

\(P_2=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 6 \\ -2 \end{matrix} \right] \)

The eigenvector for the distinct eigenvalue \(\lambda =-2\) is found in the usual way:

\(P_3=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2.75 \\ -0.25 \end{matrix} \right] \)

The transformation matrix P is formed by stacking the eigenvectors:

\(P=\left[\begin{matrix} P_1 & P_2 & P_3\end{matrix} \right] \)

or

\(P=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 4 & 6 & 2.75 \\ -1 & -2 & -0.25 \end{matrix} \right] \)
