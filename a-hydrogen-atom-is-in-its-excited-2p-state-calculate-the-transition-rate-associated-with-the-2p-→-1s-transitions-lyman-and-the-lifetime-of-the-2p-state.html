Guest Question.

A hydrogen atom is in its excited 2p state. Calculate the transition rate associated with the 2p → 1s transitions (Lyman) and the lifetime of the 2p state.

Admin
Solution

The first expression of the total transition rate is given by (10.141):

\(W_{i \rightarrow f}^{e m i}=\frac{4}{3} \frac{\omega^{3}}{\hbar c^{3}}\left|\vec{d}_{f i}\right|^{2}=\frac{4}{3} \frac{\omega^{3} e^{2}}{\hbar c^{3}}\left|\left\langle\psi_{f}|\vec{r}| \psi_{i}\right\rangle\right|^{2},\) (10.141)

\(W_{2 p \rightarrow 1 s}=\frac{4 \omega_{2 p}^{3} \rightarrow 1 s}{3 \hbar c^{3}}\left|\vec{d}_{2 p} \rightarrow 1 s\right|^{2}\), (10.215)

where

\(\left|\vec{d}_{2 p \rightarrow 1 s }\right|^{2}=e^{2}|\langle 2 p |\vec{\varepsilon} \cdot \vec{r}| 1 s \rangle|^{2}=e^{2}\left|\int_{0}^{\infty} r^{3} R_{21}^{*} R_{10}(r) d r \int d \Omega Y_{1 m}^{*} \vec{\varepsilon} \cdot \hat{r} Y_{00}\right|^{2} .\) (10.216)

First, we need to calculate \(\langle 2 p |\vec{\varepsilon} \cdot \vec{r}| 1 s \rangle\). The radial integral is given by

\(\int_{0}^{\infty} r^{3} R_{21}^{*}(r) R_{10}(r) d r=\frac{1}{a_{0}^{4} \sqrt{6}} \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{4} e^{-3 r / 2 a_{0}} d r=\frac{2^{8} a_{0}}{3^{4} \sqrt{6}}\). (10.217)

The angular part can be calculated from (10.127) as follows:

\(\int d \Omega Y_{1 m}^{*}(\Omega) \vec{\varepsilon} . \hat{r} Y_{00}(\Omega)=\sqrt{\frac{4 \pi}{3}} \int Y_{1 m}^{*}\left(\frac{-\epsilon_{x}+i \epsilon_{y}}{\sqrt{2}} Y_{11}+\frac{\epsilon_{x}+i \epsilon_{y}}{\sqrt{2}} Y_{1-1}+\epsilon_{z} Y_{10}\right) Y_{00} d \Omega\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \int Y_{1 m}^{*}\left(\frac{-\epsilon_{x}+i \epsilon_{y}}{\sqrt{2}} Y_{11}+\frac{\epsilon_{x}+i \epsilon_{y}}{\sqrt{2}} Y_{1-1}+\epsilon_{z} Y_{10}\right) d \Omega\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\left(\frac{-\epsilon_{x}+i \epsilon_{y}}{\sqrt{2}} \delta_{m, 1}+\frac{\epsilon_{x}+i \epsilon_{y}}{\sqrt{2}} \delta_{m,-1}+\epsilon_{z} \delta_{m, 0}\right)\), (10.218)

since \(\int Y_{1 m}^{*}(\theta, \phi) Y_{l_{i} m_{i}}(\theta, \phi) d \Omega=\delta_{l_{i}, 1} \delta_{m, m_{i}}\). An insertion of (10.217) and (10.218) into (10.216) leads to

\(\left|\vec{d}_{2 p \rightarrow 1 s }\right|^{2}=32\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{10} e^{2} a_{0}^{2}\left[\frac{1}{2}\left(\epsilon_{x}^{2}+\epsilon_{y}^{2}\right)\left(\delta_{m,-1}+\delta_{m, 1}\right)+\epsilon_{z}^{2} \delta_{m, 0}\right]\), (10.219)

which, when inserted into (10.215), leads to the total transition rate corresponding to a certain value of the azimuthal quantum number m:

\(W_{2 p} \rightarrow 1 s =\frac{4 \omega_{2 p}^{3} \rightarrow 1 s }{3 \hbar c^{3}}\left|\vec{d}_{f i}\right|^{2}=\frac{128 e^{2} a_{0}^{2} \omega^{3}}{3 \hbar c^{3}}\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{10}\left[\frac{1}{2}\left(\epsilon_{x}^{2}+\epsilon_{y}^{2}\right)\left(\delta_{m,-1}+\delta_{m, 1}\right)+\epsilon_{z}^{2} \delta_{m, 0}\right]\). (10.220)

Summing over the three possible m-states, m = -1, 0, 1,

\(\sum_{m=-1}^{1}\left[\frac{1}{2}\left(\epsilon_{x}^{2}+\epsilon_{y}^{2}\right)\left(\delta_{m,-1}+\delta_{m, 1}\right)+\epsilon_{z}^{2} \delta_{m, 0}\right]=\epsilon_{x}^{2}+\epsilon_{y}^{2}+\epsilon_{z}^{2}=1\), (10.221)

and since, as shown in (10.213), \(\omega_{2 p \rightarrow 1 s }=\left(E_{2 p }-E_{1 s }\right) / \hbar=3 R_{y} /(4 \hbar)=3 e^{2} /\left(8 \hbar a_{0}\right)\) (because the Rydberg constant \(R_{y}\) is equal to \(\left.e^{2} /\left(2 \hbar a_{0}\right)\right)\), we can reduce (10.220) to

\(W_{2 p \rightarrow 1 s }=\frac{128}{3 \hbar c^{3}}\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{10} e^{2} a_{0}^{2} \omega_{2 p \rightarrow 1 s }^{3}=\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{8}\left(\frac{e^{2}}{\hbar c}\right)^{4} \frac{c}{a_{0}}=\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{8} \frac{c \alpha^{4}}{a_{0}}\), (10.222)

where \(\alpha=e^{2} /(\hbar c)=1 / 137\) is the fine structure constant and \(a_{0}=0.529 \times 10^{-10}\) m is the Bohr radius. The numerical value of the transition rate is

\(W_{2 p \rightarrow 1 s }=\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{8} \frac{c \alpha^{4}}{a_{0}} \simeq\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{8} \frac{3 \times 10^{8} m s ^{-1}}{137^{4} \times 0.529 \times 10^{-10} m }=0.628 \times 10^{9} s ^{-1} .\) (10.223)

The lifetime of the 2p state is then given by

\(\tau=\frac{1}{W_{2 p \rightarrow 1 s }}=\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^{8} \frac{a_{0}}{c \alpha^{4}}=\frac{1.5^{8} \times 137^{4} \times 0.529 \times 10^{-10} m }{3 \times 10^{8} m s ^{-1}}=1.6 \times 10^{-9} s\). (10.224)

This value is in very good agreement with experimental data.

Remark
Another way of obtaining (10.222) is to use the relation

\(W_{2 p \rightarrow 1 s }=\frac{4 e^{2} \omega_{2 p \rightarrow 1 s }^{3}}{3 \hbar c^{3}} \frac{1}{3} \sum_{m=-1}^{1}|\langle 21 m|\vec{r}| 100\rangle|^{2}\)

 

\(=\frac{4 e^{2} \omega_{2 p \rightarrow 1 s}^{3}}{9 \hbar c^{3}} \sum_{m=-1}^{1}\left[|\langle 21 m|\hat{x}| 100\rangle|^{2}+|\langle 21 m|\hat{y}| 100\rangle|^{2}+|\langle 21 m|\hat{z}| 100\rangle|^{2}\right]\), (10.225)

where we have averaged over the various transitions. Using the relations \(x=r \sin \theta \cos \phi=-\sqrt{2 \pi / 3} r\left(Y_{11}-Y_{1-1}\right), y=r \sin \theta \sin \phi=i \sqrt{2 \pi / 3} r\left(Y_{11}+Y_{1-1}\right)\), and \(z=r \cos \theta=\sqrt{4 \pi / 3} r Y_{10}\), we can showthat

\(\langle 21 m|\hat{x}| 100\rangle=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{4 \pi}} \sqrt{\frac{2 \pi}{3}} \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{3} R_{21}^{*}(r) R_{10}(r) d r \int Y_{1 m}^{*}(\Omega)\left(Y_{11}-Y_{1-1}\right) d \Omega\)

 

\(=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}\left[\frac{24}{\sqrt{6}}\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{5} a_{0}\right]\left(\delta_{m, 1}-\delta_{m,-1}\right)\), (10.226)

 

\(\langle 21 m|\hat{y}| 100\rangle=\frac{i}{\sqrt{4 \pi}} \sqrt{\frac{2 \pi}{3}} \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{3} R_{21}^{*}(r) R_{10}(r) d r \int Y_{1 m}^{*}\left(Y_{11}+Y_{1-1}\right) d \Omega\)

 

\(=\frac{i}{\sqrt{6}}\left[\frac{24}{\sqrt{6}}\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{5} a_{0}\right]\left(\delta_{m, 1}+\delta_{m,-1}\right)\), (10.227)

 

\(\langle 21 m|\hat{z}| 100\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{4 \pi}} \sqrt{\frac{4 \pi}{3}} \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{3} R_{21}^{*}(r) R_{10}(r) d r \int Y_{1 m}^{*} Y_{10} d \Omega\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\left[\frac{24}{\sqrt{6}}\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{5} a_{0}\right] \delta_{m, 0}\). (10.228)

A combination of the previous three relations leads to

\(\sum_{m=-1}^{1}|\langle 21 m|\vec{r}| 100\rangle|^{2}=96 a_{0}^{2}\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{10} \sum_{m}\left[\frac{1}{6}\left(\delta_{m, 1}-\delta_{m,-1}\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{6}\left(\delta_{m, 1}+\delta_{m,-1}\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3} \delta_{m, 0}^{2}\right]\)

 

\(=96 a_{0}^{2}\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{10} \sum_{m}\left[\frac{1}{6}\left(\delta_{m, 1}+\delta_{m,-1}\right)+\frac{1}{6}\left(\delta_{m, 1}+\delta_{m,-1}\right)+\frac{1}{3} \delta_{m, 0}\right]\)

 

\(=\frac{96 a_{0}^{2}}{3}\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{10} \sum_{m=-1}^{1}\left(\delta_{m,-1}+\delta_{m, 1}+\delta_{m, 0}\right)=96\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{10} a_{0}^{2}\). (10.229)

Finally, substituting (10.229) into (10.225) and using \(\omega_{2 p \rightarrow 1 s} =3 e^{2} /\left(8 \hbar a_{0}\right)\), we obtain

\(W_{2 p \rightarrow 1 s }=\frac{128 e^{2} a_{0}^{2}}{3 \hbar c^{3}} \omega^{3}\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{10}=\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{8}\left(\frac{e^{2}}{\hbar c}\right)^{4} \frac{c}{a_{0}}=\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{8} \frac{c \alpha^{4}}{a_{0}}\). (10.230)
