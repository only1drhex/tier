Guest Question.

Let V be the vector space of all (2 × 2) matrices, and let the subset S of V be defined by \(S=\left\{A_{1}, A_{2}, A_{3}, A_{4}\right\}\), where
\(A_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{rr}1 & 2 \\-1 & 3\end{array}\right], \quad A_{2}=\left[\begin{array}{rr}0 & -1 \\1 & 4\end{array}\right]\),
\(A_{3}=\left[\begin{array}{rr}-1 & 0 \\1 & -10\end{array}\right], \text { and } A_{4}=\left[\begin{array}{rr}3 & 7 \\-2 & 6\end{array}\right]\).
Use the corollary to Theorem 5 and the techniques of Section 3.4 to obtain a basis for Sp(S).

Admin
Solution

If B is the natural basis for V, \(B=\left\{E_{11}, E_{12}, E_{21}, E_{22}\right\}\), then
\(\left[A_{1}\right]_{B}=\left[\begin{array}{r}1 \\2 \\-1 \\3\end{array}\right], \quad\left[A_{2}\right]_{B}=\left[\begin{array}{r}0 \\-1 \\1 \\4\end{array}\right]\),
\(\left[A_{3}\right]_{B}=\left[\begin{array}{r}-1 \\0 \\1 \\-10\end{array}\right], \quad \text { and } \quad\left[A_{4}\right]_{B}=\left[\begin{array}{r}3 \\7 \\-2 \\6\end{array}\right]\).
Let \(T=\left\{\left[A_{1}\right]_{B},\left[A_{2}\right]_{B},\left[A_{3}\right]_{B},\left[A_{4}\right]_{B}\right\}\). Several techniques for obtaining a basis for Sp(T) were illustrated in Section 3.4. For example, using the method demonstrated in of Section 3.4, we form the matrix
\(C=\left[\begin{array}{rrrr}1 & 0 & -1 & 3 \\2 & -1 & 0 & 7 \\-1 & 1 & 1 & -2 \\3 & 4 & -10 & 6\end{array}\right]\).
The matrix \(C^{T}\) can be reduced to the matrix
\(D^{T}=\left[\begin{array}{rrrr}1 & 2 & -1 & 3 \\0 & -1 & 1 & 4 \\0 & 0 & 2 & 1 \\0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]\).
Thus
\(D=\left[\begin{array}{rrrr}1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\2 & -1 & 0 & 0 \\-1 & 1 & 2 & 0 \\3 & 4 & 1 & 0\end{array}\right]\),
and the nonzero columns of D constitute a basis for Sp(T). Denote the nonzero columns of D by \(w _{1}, w _{2}, \text { and } w _{3}\), respectively. Thus
\(w _{1}=\left[\begin{array}{r}1 \\2 \\-1 \\3\end{array}\right], \quad w _{2}=\left[\begin{array}{r}0 \\-1 \\1 \\4\end{array}\right], \quad \text { and } \quad w _{3}=\left[\begin{array}{r}0 \\0 \\2 \\1\end{array}\right]\),
and \(\left\{ w _{1}, w _{2}, w _{3}\right\}\) is a basis for Sp(T). If \(B_{1}, B_{2} \text {, and } B_{3}\) are (2 × 2) matrices such that \(\left[B_{1}\right]_{B}= w _{1},\left[B_{2}\right]_{B}= w _{2}, \text { and }\left[B_{3}\right]_{B}= w _{3}\), then it follows from Theorem 5 that \(\left\{B_{1}, B_{2}, B_{3}\right\}\) is a basis for Sp(S). If
\(B_{1}=E_{11}+2 E_{12}-E_{21}+3 E_{22}\),
then clearly \(\left[B_{1}\right]_{B}= w _{1} . B_{2} \text { and } B_{3}\) are obtained in the same fashion, and
\(B_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{rr}1 & 2 \\-1 & 3\end{array}\right], \quad B_{2}=\left[\begin{array}{rr}0 & -1 \\1 & 4\end{array}\right], \quad \text { and } \quad B_{3}=\left[\begin{array}{ll}0 & 0 \\2 & 1\end{array}\right].\)
