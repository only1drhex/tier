Guest Question.

Figure 7-8 depicts a streamline for two-dimensional flow and its associated equipotential lines 1 to 10, each normal to the streamline. The spacing between the equipotential lines is given in the second column of the table on page 115. If the average velocity between 1 and 2 is 0.500 ft/sec, calculate (a) the average velocities of the streamline between each pair of equipotential lines and (b) the time it will take for a particle to move from 1 to 10 along the streamline.

Admin
Solution

(a) Referring to the relation between velocity and Δn in ,

\(V_{1-2} \Delta n_{1-2}=V_{2-3} \Delta n_{2-3}=V_{3-4} \Delta n_{3-4}=\ldots\).

Also

\(\Delta S_{1-2} \cong \Delta n_{1-2}, \quad \Delta S_{2-3} \cong \Delta n_{2-3}, \ldots\).

\(\text { Thus } V_{2-3} \cong V_{1-2}\left(\Delta S_{1-2} / \Delta S_{2-3}\right)=(0.500)(0.500 / 0.400)=0.625 ft / sec . \text { Similarly, } V_{3-4}=\)\((0.500)(0.500 / 0.300)=0.833 ft / sec \text {, etc. The values of the average velocities are tabulated below. }\)

(b) The time to travel from 1 to 2 equals distance 1-2 divided by the average velocity from 1 to 2, or \(t_{1-2}=0.500 / 0.500=1.000 sec . \text { Similarly, } t_{2-3}=0.400 / 0.625=0.640 sec\). The total time to travel from 1 to 10 is the sum of the last column, 2.234 sec.
