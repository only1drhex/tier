Guest Question.

Saturated-vapor steam at 100 kPa \((t^{sat} = 99.63^{\circ}C)\) is compressed adiabatically to 300 kPa. If the compressor efficiency is 0.75, what is the work required and what are the properties of the discharge stream?

Admin
Solution

For saturated steam at 100 kPa,

\( S_{1} = 7.3598 kJ \cdot kg^{−1}\cdot K^{−1} H_{1} = 2675.4 kJ \cdot kg^{−1}\)

For isentropic compression to 300 kPa, \(S^{\prime }_{2} = S_{1} = 7.3598 kJ \cdot kg^{−1} \cdot K^{−1} .\) Interpo-lation in the tables for superheated steam at 300 kPa shows that steam with this entropy has the enthalpy: \(H^{\prime }_{2} = 2888.8 kJ\cdot kg^{−1}.\)

Thus, \( (\Delta H)_{S}=2888.8-2675.4=213.4 kJ\cdot kg^{-1}\)

By Eq. (7.17),\( (\Delta H)=\frac{(\Delta H)_{S}}{\eta }=\frac{213.4}{0.75}=284.5kJ\cdot kg^{-1}\)

\( \eta \equiv \frac{(\Delta H)_{S}}{\Delta H}\) (7.17)

and \( H_{2}=H_{1}+\Delta H=2675.4+284.5=2959.9kJ\cdot kg^{-1}\)

For superheated steam with this enthalpy, interpolation yields:

\( T_{2} = 246.1^{\circ}C S_{2} = 7.5019 kJ\cdot kg^{−1}\cdot K^{−1}\)

Moreover, by Eq. (7.14), the work required is:

\( W_{s}=\Delta H=H_{2}-H_{1}\) (7.14)

\( W_{s}=\Delta H=284.5 kJ\cdot kg^{-1}\)
