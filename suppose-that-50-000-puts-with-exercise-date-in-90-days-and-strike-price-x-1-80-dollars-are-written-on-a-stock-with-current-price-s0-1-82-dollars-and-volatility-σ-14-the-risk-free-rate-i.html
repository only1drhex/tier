Guest Question.

Suppose that 50, 000 puts with exercise date in 90 days and strike price \(X = 1.80\) dollars are written on a stock with current price \(S(0) = 1.82\) dollars and volatility \(σ = 14\%. \)The risk-free rate is \(r = 5\%\). Construct a delta neutral portfolio and compute its value after one day if the stock price drops to \(S(\frac{1}{365})= 1.81\) dollars.

Admin
Solution

The premium for a single put is 0.031648 dollars (from the Black–Scholes formula), so the bank will receive 1, 582.40 dollars by writing and selling 50, 000 puts. The delta of a single put is −0.355300, so the delta-hedging portfolio requires shorting 17, 765.00 shares, which will raise 32, 332.29 dollars. This gives a total of 33, 914.69 dollars received to be invested at 5%. The value of the delta neutral portfolio consisting of the shored stock, invested cash and sold options will be −32, 332.29 + 33, 914.69 − 1, 582.40 = 0.00 dollars. One day later the shorted shares will be worth \( 17, 765 × 1.81 = 32, 154.64 \) dollars, whereas the cash investment will grow to \(33, 914.69e^{0.05/365} \cong 33, 919.34\) dollars. The put price will increase to 0.035182 dollars, so the price of 50, 000 puts will be 1, 759.11 dollars. The value of the delta neutral portfolio will be \( −32, 154.64 + 33, 919.34 − 1, 759.11 ≅ 5.59\) dollars.
