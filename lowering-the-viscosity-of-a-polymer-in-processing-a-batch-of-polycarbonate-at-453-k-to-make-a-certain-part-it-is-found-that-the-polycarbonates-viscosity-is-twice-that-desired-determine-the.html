Guest Question.

Lowering the viscosity of a polymer

In processing a batch of polycarbonate at 453 K to make a certain part, it is found that the polycarbonate’s viscosity is twice that desired. Determine the temperature at which this polymer should be processed.

Admin
Solution

From Table 10.2,

Glass-Transition and Melting Temperatures of Selected Polymers
Material \(T_{g}\) (K) \(T_{m}\) (K)
Nylon 6,6 330 538
Polycarbonate 423 538
Polyester 346 538
Polyethylene
high density 183 410
low density 163 388
Polymethylmethacrylate 378 —
Polypropylene 259 449
Polystyrene 373 512
Polytetrafluoroethylene (Teflon) 183 600
Polyvinyl chloride 360 485
Rubber 200 —

the \( T_{g}\) for polycarbonate is 423 K. Its viscosity at 453 K is determined by Eq. (10.8):

\(\log \eta =12-\frac{17.5 \ \Delta T }{52+\Delta T}.\) (10.8)

\(\log \eta =12-\frac{17.5(30)}{52+30}=5.6\)

Hence, \(\eta =0.396\) MPa-s, and because this magnitude is 2.2 times what is desired, the new viscosity should be 0.18 MPa-s. We substitute this new value to find the new temperature:

\(\log (0.18\times 10^6) =12-\frac{17.5(\Delta T)}{52+\Delta T}.\)

Therefore, \(\Delta T = 32.6 \), or the new temperature is 423 + 32.6 = 455.6 K Note that in this solution the numbers for temperature have been rounded, and that viscosity is very sensitive to temperature.
