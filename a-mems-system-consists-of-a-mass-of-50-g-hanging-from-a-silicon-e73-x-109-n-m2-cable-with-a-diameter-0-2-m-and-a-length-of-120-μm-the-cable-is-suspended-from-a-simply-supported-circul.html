Guest Question.

A MEMS system consists of a mass of 50 g hanging from a silicon \((E=73 \times 10^{9} N/m^{2})\) cable with a diameter 0.2 m and a length of 120 μm. The cable is suspended from a simply supported, circular silicon beam with a diameter of 1.6 μm and a length of 50 μm, as shown in Figure 3.27. The mass vibrates in a silicone oil such that its damping coefficient is \(1.2\times 10^{-6} N\cdot s/m.\) The mass is given as an initial displacement of 2 m and released. Determine the response of the system.

Admin
Solution

The stiffness of the beam is

\(k_{b}= \frac{48 EI}{L^{3}}=\frac{48(73\times 10^{9} N/m^{2})(0.8\mu m)^{4}\pi /4}{(50 \mu m)^{3}}= 9.018 N/m\) (a)

The stiffness of the cable is

\(k_c= \frac{AE}{L} = \frac{\pi (0.1 \mu m)^{2}(73\times 10^{9 }N/m^{2})}{120 \mu m}= 19.11 N/m\) (b)

The springs are in series with an equivalent stiffness as

\(k_{eq}=\frac{1}{\frac{1}{9.08 N/m}+\frac{1}{19.11}n/m } =6.13 N/m\) (c)

The undamped natural frequency is

\(\omega _n= \sqrt{\frac{k_{eq}}{m} }= \sqrt{\frac{6.14 N/m}{50 \mu g} }= 1.10 \times 10^{4} rad/s\) (d)

The damping ratio is

\(\zeta = \frac{c}{2m\omega_{n}} = =\frac{1.2 \times 10^{-6 N\cdot s/m}}{2(50 \mu g)(1.10 \times 10^{4} rad/s)}= 0.0011\) (e)

The damped natural frequency is

\(\omega _{d}= (1.10 \times 10^{4} rad/s)\sqrt{1-(0.0011)^{2}}= 1.10 \times 10^{4} rad/s\) (f)

The response of an underdamped system with an initial displacement is

\(x(t)= (2 \mu m)\sqrt{1+ \left[\frac{0.0011}{\sqrt{1- (0.0011)^{2}} } \right]^{2} }e^{-(0.0011)(1.10 \times 10^{4} rad/s)t}\)

\(\sin (1.10 \times 10^{4}t +1.57)\)

\(=2e^{-12t}\sin (1.10 \times 10^{4}t+ 1.57) \mu m\) (g)
