Guest Question.

For the problem defined in and extended in Example 6–13, all of the fatigue criteria predicted finite life. For the fatigue criteria of Goodman, Morrow, SWT, and Walker, estimate the equivalent completely reversed stress and the predicted life. Compare the results.

Admin
Solution

From , \(σ_{a} = 48 kpsi, σ_{m} = 24 kpsi, S_{ut} = 100 kpsi, and S_{e} = 40 kpsi\).

Equation (6–59): Goodman \( σ_{ar} =\frac{\sigma _{a}}{1-σ_{m}∕S_{ut}} =\frac{48}{1-24/100}= 63 kpsi \)

Equation (6–44): \(σ^{′}_{f} = S_{ut} + 50 kpsi = 150 kpsi\)

Equation (6–60): Morrow \( σ_{ar} =\frac{\sigma _{a}}{1-σ_{m}∕σ^{\prime }_{f}} =\frac{48}{1-24/150}= 57 kpsi \)

Equation (6–61): SWT \( σ_{ar}=\sqrt{(σ_{m} + σ_{a})σ_{a}} =\sqrt{(24 + 48)48} = 59 kpsi \)

From, γ = 0.74

Equation (6–62): Walker \( σ_{ar} = σ^{1−γ}_ {max}σ^{γ}_{a} = (σ_{m} + σ_{a})^{1−γ}σ^{γ}_{a} = (24 + 48)^{1−0.74}48^{0.74} = 53 kpsi \)

Use the S-N diagram equations with these equivalent completely reversed stresses to estimate the life based on each criterion.
Figure 6–23: f = 0.84

Equation (6–13): \(a=\frac{(fS_{ut})^{2}}{S_{e}} =\frac{((0.84)100)^{2}}{40}= 176.4 \)

Equation (6–14): \( b=-\frac{1}{3}\log \left(\frac{fS_{ut}}{S_{e}} \right)=-\frac{1}{3}\log \left(\frac{(0.84)(100)}{40} \right) = −0.1074 \)

Equation (6–15): \( N=\left(\frac{σ_{ar}}{a} \right) ^{1∕b}\)

Calculate the estimated life for each criterion. Results are reported in order of lowest life to highest life.

Goodman \( σ_{ar} = 63 kpsi\) N = 15 000 cycles
SWT \(σ_{ar} = 59 kpsi\) N = 27 000 cycles
Morrow \(σ_{ar} = 57 kpsi\) N = 37 000 cycles
Walker \(σ_{ar} = 53 kpsi \) N = 73 000 cycles
A higher equivalent completely reversed stress on an S-N diagram will predict a shorter life. From a design perspective, a prediction of a shorter life is more conservative. The Walker estimate is expected to be the most typical of reality. The Morrow result is an improvement on Goodman, even with just the estimate for \(σ^{′}_{f}\) . As expected, Goodman is conservative. Though Goodman is in the correct order of magnitude, it is substantially less than the presumably better value from Walker
