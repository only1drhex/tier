Guest Question.

A piston cylinder shown in Fig. P3.169 contains 0.5 \(m ^{3}\) of R-410A at 2 MPa,150°C. The piston mass and atmosphere gives a pressure of 450 kPa that will float the piston. The whole setup cools in a freezer maintained at -20°C. Find the heat transfer and show the P-v diagram for the process when \(T _{2}\) = -20°C.

 

Admin
Solution

C.V.: R-410A. Control mass.

Continuity: m = constant,

Energy Eq.3.5: \(m \left( u _{2}- u _{1}\right)={ }_{1} Q _{2}-{ }_{1} W _{2}\)

Process: \(F \downarrow= F \uparrow= P A = P _{\text {air }} A + F _{\text {stop }}\)

\(\text { if } V < V _{\text {stop }} \Rightarrow F _{\text {stop }}=0\)

This is illustrated in the P-v diagram shown below.

State 1: \(v _{1}=0.02247 m ^{3} / kg , u _{1}=373.49 kJ / kg\)

\(\Rightarrow \quad m = V / v =22.252 kg\)

State 2: \(T _{2}\) and on line ⇒ compressed liquid, see figure below.

\(v _{2} \cong v _{ f }=0.000803 m ^{3} / kg \Rightarrow V _{2}=0.01787 m ^{3} ; \quad u _{2}= u _{ f }=27.92 kJ / kg\)

\({ }_{1} W _{2}=\int PdV = P _{ lift }\left( V _{2}- V _{1}\right)=450 kPa (0.01787-0.5) m ^{3}=-217.0 kJ\);

Energy eq. ⇒

\({ }_{1} Q _{2}=22.252 kg \times(27.92-373.49) kJ / kg -217.9 kJ =-7906.6 k J\)
