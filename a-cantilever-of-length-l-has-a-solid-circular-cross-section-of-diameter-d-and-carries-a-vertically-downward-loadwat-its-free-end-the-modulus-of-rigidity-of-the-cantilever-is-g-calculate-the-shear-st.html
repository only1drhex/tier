Guest Question.

A cantilever of length L has a solid circular cross section of diameter D and carries a vertically downward loadWat its free end. The modulus of rigidity of the cantilever is G. Calculate the shear stress distribution across a section of the cantilever and hence determine the deflection due to shear at its free end.
Ans. \(\tau=16 W\left(1-4 y^{2} / D^{2}\right) / 3 \pi D ^{2}, 40 W L / 9 \pi G D^{2}\) (downwards).

Admin
Solution

The shear stress distribution has been calculated in and is given by Eq. (10.19),

\(\tau=-\frac{16 S_{y}}{3 \pi D^{2}}\left(1-\frac{4 y^{2}}{D^{2}}\right)\) (10.19)

i.e.

\(\tau=\left(\frac{16 W}{3 \pi D^{2}}\right)\left(1-\frac{4 y^{2}}{D^{2}}\right)\)

Now substituting in Eq. (13.20)

\(\beta=\frac{A}{S_{y}^{2}} \int_{y_{1}}^{y_{2}} \tau^{2} b_{0} d y\) (13.20)

\(\beta=\left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4 W^{2}}\right)\left(\frac{16 W}{3 \pi D^{2}}\right)^{2} \int_{-D / 2}^{D / 2}\left(1-\frac{4 y^{2}}{D^{2}}\right)^{2} b_{0} d y\) (i)

Changing to angular coordinates, (see Fig. 10.10)

\(y=\left(\frac{D}{2}\right) \sin \theta, \quad d y=\left(\frac{D}{2}\right) \cos \theta d \theta, \quad b_{0}=D \cos \theta\)

Substituting in Eq. (i) and simplifying

\(\beta=\frac{32}{9 \pi} \int_{-\pi / 2}^{\pi / 2} \cos ^{6} \theta d \theta\)

The value of the integral may be found using a reduction formula. Then

\(\beta=\frac{10}{9}\)

The shear force at any section of the cantilever is W. Substituting in Eq. (13.22)

\(v_{s}=\frac{\beta}{G} \int_{L}\left(\frac{S_{y}}{A}\right) d x\) (13.22)

\(v_{s}=\left(\frac{10}{9 G}\right) \int_{0}^{L} \frac{W}{\left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right)} d x\)

i.e.

\(v_{ s }=\frac{40 W L}{9 \pi G D^{2}}\).
