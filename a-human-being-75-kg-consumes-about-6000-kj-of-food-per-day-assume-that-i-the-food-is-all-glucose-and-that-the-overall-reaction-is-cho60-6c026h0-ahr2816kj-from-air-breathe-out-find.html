Guest Question.

A human being ( \(75 \mathrm{~kg}\) ) consumes about \(6000 \mathrm{~kJ}\) of food per day. Assume that the food is all glucose and that the overall reaction is

\(\begin{array}{c}\mathrm{C}_{6} \mathrm{H}_{12} \mathrm{O}_{6}+6 \mathrm{O}_{2} \rightarrow 6 \mathrm{CO}_{2}+6 \mathrm{H}_{2}\mathrm{O},-\Delta \mathrm{H}_{r}=2816 \mathrm{~kJ} \\\text { from air }\end{array}\)

Find man’s metabolic rate (the rate of living, loving, and laughing) in terms of moles of oxygen used per \(\mathrm{m}^{3}\) of person per second.

 

Admin
Solution

We want to find

\(-r_{\mathrm{O}_{2}}^{m}=-\frac{1}{V_{\text {petsan }}} \frac{d N_{\mathrm{O}_{2}}}{d t}=\frac{\operatorname{mol} \mathrm{O}_{2} \text { used }}{\left(\mathrm{m}^{3} \text { of person }\right) \mathrm{s}}\)

Let us evaluate the two terms in this equation. First of all, from our life experience we estimate the density of man to be

\(\rho=1000 \frac{\mathrm{kg}}{\mathrm{m}^{3}}\)

Therefore, for the person in question

\(V_{\text {penso }}=\frac{75 \mathrm{~kg}}{1000 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}}=0.075 \mathrm{~m}^{3}\)

Next, noting that each mole of glucose consumed uses 6 moles of oxygen and releases \(2816 \mathrm{~kJ}\) of energy, we see that we need

\(\frac{d N_{\mathrm{O}_{2}}}{d t}=\left(\frac{6000 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{day}}{2816 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{mol} \text { glucose }}\right)\left(\frac{6 \mathrm{~mol} \mathrm{O}_{2}}{1 \mathrm{~mol} \mathrm{~g} \text { lucose }}\right)=12.8 \frac{\mathrm{mol} \mathrm{O}_{2}}{\mathrm{day}}\)

 

\(-r_{\mathrm{O}_{2}}^{\mathrm{m}}=\frac{1}{0.075 \mathrm{~m}^{3}} \cdot \frac{12.8 \mathrm{~mol} \mathrm{O}_{2} \text { used }}{\text { day }} \frac{1 \mathrm{day}}{24 \times 3600 \mathrm{~s}}=0.002 \frac{\mathrm{mol} \mathrm{O}_{2} \text { used }}{\mathrm{m}^{3} \cdot \mathrm{s}}\)
