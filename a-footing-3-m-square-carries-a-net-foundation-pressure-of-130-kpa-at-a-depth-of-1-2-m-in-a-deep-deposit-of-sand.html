Guest Question.

A footing 3 m square carries a net foundation pressure of 130 kPa at a depth of 1.2 m in a deep deposit of sand. The water table is at a depth of 3 m and the unit weights of the sand above and below the water table are 16 and \( 19 kN/m^{3} \), respectively. The variation of cone penetration resistance (\( q_{c} \)) with depth (z) is as follows:

 

z (m) 1.2 1.6 2.0 2.4 2.6 3.0 3.4 3.8 4.2
\(q_{c} \left ( MN/m^{2} \right ) \) 3.2 2.1 2.8 2.3 6.1 5.0 6.6 4.5 5.5
z (m) 4.6 5.0 5.5 5.8 6.2 6.6 7.0 7.4 8.0
\(q_{c} \left ( MN/m^{2} \right ) \) 4.5 5.4 10.4 8.9 9.9 9.0 15.1 12.9 14.8
Admin
Solution

The CPT results show that the sand deposit below the foundation level may be divided into four layers as shown in

Check safety factor against bearing capacity failure.
For each layer, select a suitable value for \(\varnothing \) using the various approximate correlations between \(\varnothing \) and \( q_{c} \). If more than one correlation is considered, the average of the results will be taken as a design value for \(\varnothing \); in this solution the following two correlations are considered:

 

Equation : \( {\varnothing }'_{1} = \tan^{-1} \left [ 0.1 + 0.38 log \left ( \frac{q_{c}}{{\sigma}'_{o}} \right ) \right ] \)

 

An approximate correlation (Bowles, 2001) for \({\varnothing }' \) is

 

\( {\varnothing }' = 29^{\circ} + \sqrt{q_{c}} + 5^{\circ} \) for gravel; \(– 5^{\circ} \) for silty sand, where \( q_{c} \) is in MPa. As a second correlation, assume \({\varnothing }'_{2} = 29^{\circ} + \sqrt{q_{c}}\) ( ).

 

In bearing capacity analysis, the influence depth for square footings is about 2B below the foundation level and therefore, the \({\varnothing }' \) values of the first three layers need to be taken into consideration only. Accordingly, the design \({\varnothing }' \) is

 

\( {\varnothing }' = \frac{35 + 36 + 37}{3} = 36^{\circ} \)

 

: \( gross q_{ult} = cN_{c}s_{c}d_{c}i_{c}g_{c}b_{c} + qN_{q}s_{q}d_{q}i_{q}g_{q}b_{q} + \frac{1}{2} {y}'B N_{y}s_{y}d_{y}i_{y}g_{y}b_{y} \)

 

The \(N_{c} \) term is 0, since the cohesion c = 0

Table 4.8: for the conditions of this problem all \(i_{c}g_{c}b_{c} \) and \(i_{q}g_{q}b_{q} \) are 1.

 

\( gross q_{ult} = \overline{q}N_{q}s_{q}d_{q} + \frac{1}{2} {y}' B N_{y}s_{y}d_{y}\)

 

: for \( \varnothing = 36^{\circ}: N_{q} = 37.7, N_{y} = 56.2 \)

 

\( s_{q} = 1 + \frac{B}{L} \tan \varnothing = 1.73; s_{y} = 1 – 0.4 \frac{B}{L} = 0.6 \)

 

\( d_{q} = 1 + 2 \tan \varnothing \left ( 1 – \sin \varnothing \right )^{2} \left ( \frac{D}{B} \right ) \)

 

\( = 1 + 2 \tan 36 \left ( 1 – \sin 36 \right )^{2} \left ( 0.4 \right ) = 1.1 \)

 

\( d_{y} = 1 \)

 

Equation : \( {y}' = y_{b} + \left ( \frac{z_{w}}{B} \right )\left ( y_{wet} – y_{b}\right ) = \left ( 19 – 10 \right ) + \left ( \frac{1.8}{3} \right ) \left ( 16 – 9 \right ) = 13.2 kN/m^{3} \)

 

\( gross q_{ult} = 16 \times 1.2 \times 37.7 \times 1.73 \times 1.1 \)

 

\( + 0.5 \times 13.2 \times 3 \times 56.2 \times 0.6 \times 1 \)

 

\( gross q_{ult} = 1252 + 668 = 1920 kPa \)

 

Equation : \( net q_{ult} = gross q_{ult} – {\sigma}'_{o} = 1920 – 1.2 \times 16 = 1901 Pa \)

 

The safety factor against bearing capacity failure is

\( SF = \frac{ net q_{ult}}{ net foundation pressure } = \frac{1901}{130} = 14.6 \gg 3 \)

 

The required safety against bearing capacity failure is satisfied.

Check allowable settlement using the Buismanâ€“DeBeer method:

\( C = 1.9 \frac{q_{c}}{{\sigma}'_{o}} \) ( Recommended by Meyerhof, 1965 )

 

\( S_{i} = \frac{H}{C} ln \frac{{\sigma}'_{o} + {\sigma}'_{z}}{{\sigma}'_{o}} = \frac{2.3 H {\sigma}'_{o}}{1.9 q_{c}} log \frac{{\sigma}'_{o} + {\sigma}'_{z}}{{\sigma}'_{o}} \) or \( \sum S_{i} = \sum_{0}^{H} \frac{2.3 {\sigma}'_{o}}{1.9 q_{c}} \sum z log \frac{{\sigma}'_{o} + \sigma_{z}}{{\sigma}'_{o}} \)

 

The stress increment \( \sigma_{z} \) at the centre of each layer will be calculated using either Figure or Table 2.3. The above settlement equations are applied to obtain the total settlement, as in .
\( \sum S_{i} = 24.5 mm \cong 25 mm \)

 

The required safety against excessive settlement is satisfied.
Check the allowable settlement using the Schmertmann method:

 

Equation : \( S_{i} = C_{1} C_{2} C_{3} q_{net} \sum_{z = 0}^{z = z_{2}} \frac{I_{z}}{E_{s}} \Delta z \)

 

According to Schmertmann, for sand soils supporting square footings:

 

\( E_{s} = 2.5 q_{c}\) ( see Section 3.3.3 )

 

Also, the influence depth under square footings is 2 B (Figure ). Hence, in this case, only the first three layers need to be considered in the settlement computations. The thickness of layer III should be increased by 0.4 m so that the required 2B depth is maintained.
Considering square footings:

Equation :

 

For z = 0 to \( \frac{B}{2}: I_{z} = 0.1 + \left ( \frac{z}{B} \right ) \left ( 2 I_{zp} – 0.2 \right ) \)

 

Equation :

 

For \(z \frac{B}{2} \) to 2B : \( I_{z} = 0.667 I_{zp} \left ( 2 – \frac{z}{B} \right ) \)

 

Equation :

 

\( I_{zp} = 0.5 + 0.1 \sqrt{\frac{q_{net}}{{\sigma}'_{zp}}} \)

 

\( q_{net} = 130 kPa \)

 

For square footings, \( {\sigma}'_{zp} \) is calculated at depth B/2 below foundation level; hence, \( {\sigma}'_{zp} = 2.7 \times 16 = 43.2 kPa \)

 

\( I_{zp} = 0.5 + 0.1 \sqrt{\frac{130}{43.2}} = 0.673 \)

 

Determine the value of \(\left ( I_{z} \Delta z / E_{s} \right ) \) at the midpoint of each layer, as shown in .

 

\( C_{1} = 1 – \frac{0.5 {\sigma}'_{0}}{q_{net}} = 1 – \frac{0.5 \left ( 1.2 \times 16 \right )}{130} = 0.926 \)

 

\( C_{2} = 1 + 0.2 log \frac{t}{0.1}. \) Assume t = 0.1 year; hence, \( C_{2} = 1 \)

 

\( C_{3} = 1.0 \) ( for square footings )

 

\( S_{i} = C_{1} C_{2} C_{3} q_{net} \sum_{z = 0}^{z = z_{2}} \frac{I_{z}}{E_{s}} \Delta z = 0.926 \times 1 \times 1 \times 130 \times 18.55 \times 10^{-5} \)

 

\( = 0.0223 m = 22.3 mm < 25 mm\)

The required safety against excessive settlement is satisfied.
