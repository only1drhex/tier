Guest Question.

The circuit in Fig. 12.85a is an inexpensive “bass boost” amplifier that amplifies only lowfrequency audio signals, as illustrated by the Bode plot sketch in Fig. 12.85b. We wish to derive the transfer function \(V_{o}/V_{in}\) when the switch is open. Then, from this transfer function and Fig. 12.85b, select appropriate values for \(R_{1}\) and \(R_{2}\). What is the resulting value of \(f_{P}\)?

Admin
Solution

With the switch open, we can use the classic noninverting op-amp configuration expression to write the transfer function as

\(\frac{V_{o}}{V_{in}}= 1 + \frac{Z_{2}}{Z_{1}}\)

where \(Z_{1} = R_{1}\) and \(Z_{2}\) is the parallel combination of the \(R_{2}\) and 1/jωC. \(Z_{2}\) can be written as

\(Z_{2} = R_{2} //\frac{1}{jωC} = \frac{R_{2}}{1 + jωR_{2}C}\)

and the transfer function as

\(\frac{V_{o}}{V_{in}}= 1 + \frac{\frac{R_{2}}{1 + jωR_{2} C}}{R_{1}}=\frac{R_{1} + R_{2} + jωR_{1}R_{2} C}{R_{1}(1 + jωR_{2} C)} = [\frac{R_{1}+ R_{2}}{R_{1}}] [\frac{1 + jωR_{P}C}{1 + jωR_{2}C}]\) 12.92

where \(R_{P} = R_{1}//R_{2}\). The network has one zero at 1/\(R_{P}\)C and one pole at 1/\(R_{2}\) C. Since \(R_{P}\) must be less than \(R_{2}\), the zero frequency must be greater than the pole frequency, and the sketch in Fig. 12.85b is appropriate.
Now let us determine the component values. At dc (ω = 0) the gain must be 6 dB or a factor of 2. From Eq. (12.92).

\(\frac{V_{o}}{V_{in}}(j0) = \frac{R_{1} + R_{2}}{R_{1}}\) = 2

Thus, \(R_{1} = R_{2}\) and \(R_{P} = R_{1}/2\). From Fig. 12.85b, the zero frequency is 500 Hz, and given this information we can determine \(R_{P}\) as

\(\frac{1}{R_{P}C} = 2 \pi (500) ⇒ R_{P} = \frac{1}{1000 \pi C}\) = 3.18 kΩ

Of course, \(R_{1} = R_{2} = 2R_{P}\) = 6.37 kΩ. Finally, the pole frequency is

\(\frac{1}{2 \pi R_{2} C}\) = 250 Hz
