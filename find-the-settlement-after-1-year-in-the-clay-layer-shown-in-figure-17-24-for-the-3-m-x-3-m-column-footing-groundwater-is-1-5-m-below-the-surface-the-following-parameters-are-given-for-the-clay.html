Guest Question.

Find the settlement after 1 year in the clay layer shown in Figure 17.24 for the \(3 m \times 3 m\) column footing. Groundwater is 1.5 m below the surface. The following parameters are given for the clay layer: \(\gamma = 18 kN/m^{3}, C_{r} = 0.032, C_{c} = 0.38, e_{0} = 0.85, p_{c}^{\prime }= 50 kPa, c_{v} = 0.011 m^{2}/day.\) Description of terms: \(\Delta H,\) total primary consolidation settlement; H, thickness of the drainage layer; \(C_{c},\) compression index of the clay layer in the virgin zone; \(C_{r},\) recompression index of the clay layer in the recompression zone; \(e_{0},\) void ratio of the clay layer at mid point of the clay layer prior to loading; \(p_{0}^{\prime },\) effective stress at the mid point of the clay layer prior to loading; \(p_{c}^{\prime },\) preconsolidation pressure; \(p_{f}^{\prime },\) pressure after the footing is placed; \(c_{v},\) consolidation coefficient.

Figure 17.24 Consolidation in clay (single drainage).

Admin
Solution

Step 1: The first step is to find the percent consolidation \((U\%)\) after 1 year.

\(t=\frac{H^{2}T_{v}}{c_{v}}\)

t = 1 year = 365 days
H = Thickness of the drainage layer. In this case, drainage is possible only from top. Drainage is not possible from the bottom due to bedrock. This is a single drainage situation. Hence, H = 6 m. It should be mentioned here that in reality, the longest drainage path is 7 m, since the footing is placed 1 m below the surface.
\(T_{v} =\) Time coefficient.

Find \(T_{v}\)

\(T_{v}=\frac{c_{v}t}{H^{2}}=\frac{0.011\times 365}{6^{2}}=0.111\)

Step 2: From \(U\%\) versus \(T_{v}\) (Table 17.1);

\(T_{v}\) \(U\%\) (percent consolidation)
0.00 0
0.048 10
0.090 20
0.115 30
0.207 40
0.281 50
0.371 60
0.488 70
0.652 80
0.933 90
1.0 (approximately) 100

for \((U\%) = 20\%, T_{v} = 0.009,\)
for \((U\%) = 30\%, T_{v} = 0.115,\)
Through interpolation, find the \(U\%\) for \(T_{v} = 0.111\)

\(\frac{U-20}{0.111-0.009}=\frac{30-20}{0.115-0.009}\)

\(U = 29.6,\) for \(T_{v} = 0.111\)
When t (time) = 1 year \(= T_{v} = 0.111,\) and \(U\% = 29.6\)

Step 3: Find the total settlement due to consolidation.

\(\Delta H=H\frac{C_{r}}{1+e_{0}}\log \frac{p_{0}^{\prime }+\Delta p_{1}}{p_{0}^{\prime }}+H\frac{C_{c}}{1+e_{0}}\log \frac{p_{c}^{\prime }+\Delta p_{2}}{p_{c}^{\prime }}\)

The clay layer is 7 m thick and the footing is placed 1 m below the surface. The top 1 m is not subjected to consolidation. The compressible portion of the clay layer is 6 m thick. Find the effective stress at the mid layer of the compressible clay stratum \((p_{0}^{\prime });\)

\(p_{0}^{\prime }=1.5\times \gamma _{clay}+2.5\times (\gamma _{clay}-\gamma _{w})\)

 

\(p_{0}^{\prime }=1.5\times 18+2.5\times (18-9.807)=47.5 kN/m^{2}.\)

Step 4: Find \(\Delta p\)
\(\Delta p =\) Increase of stress at the mid point of the clay layer due to the footing.
Total load of 350 kN is distributed at a larger area at the mid section of the clay layer.
Area of the mid section of the clay layer \(= (3 + 1.5 + 1.5) \times (3 + 1.5 + 1.5) = 36 m^{2}\)
\(\Delta p = 350/36 = 9.72 kN/m^{2}.\)

Step 5: Find the final pressure after application of the footing load;
\(p_{f}^{\prime } =\) Initial pressure + pressure increase due to footing load at mid point
\(p_{f}^{\prime } = p_{0}^{\prime } + \Delta p = 47.5 + 9.72 = 57.22 kN/m^{2}.\)

Step 6: Apply values in the consolidation equation.

\(\Delta H=H\frac{C_{r}}{1+e_{0}}\log \frac{p_{0}^{\prime }+\Delta p_{1}}{p_{0}^{\prime }}+H\frac{C_{c}}{1+e_{0}}\log \frac{p_{c}^{\prime }+\Delta p_{2}}{p_{c}^{\prime }}\)

\((\Delta p_{1}) =\) Stress increase from initial stress \((p^{\prime }_{0})\) to preconsolidation pressure \(p_{c}^{\prime }.\)
Since \(p_{0}^{\prime }\) is 47.5 and \(p_{c}^{\prime }\) was given to be \(50 kN/m^{2},\) \(\Delta p_{1}\) would be \((50 − 47.5) kN/m^{2}.\)

\(\Delta p_{1}=2.5 kN/m^{2}.\)

\((\Delta p_{2}) =\) Stress increase from preconsolidation pressure \(p_{c}^{\prime }\) to final pressure \(p_{f}^{\prime }.\)
Since \(p_{c}^{\prime }\) is \(50 kN/m^{2}\) and \(p_{f}^{\prime }\) is was found to be 57.2, \(\Delta p_{2}\) would be \((57.22 − 50) kN/m^{2}.\)

\(\Delta p_{2}=7.22 kN/m^{2}.\)

 

\(\Delta H=6\frac{0.032}{1+0.85}\log \frac{p_{0}^{\prime }+\Delta p_{1}}{p_{0}^{\prime }}+6\frac{0.38}{1+0.85}\log \frac{p_{c}^{\prime }+\Delta p_{2}}{p_{c}^{\prime }}\)

 

\(\Delta H=0.1037\log \frac{47.5+2.5}{47.5}+1.23\log \frac{50+7.22}{50}\)

 

\(\Delta H=0.0023+0.072\)

 

\(\Delta =0.0743 m\) (2.92in.).

Step 7: Find the settlement after 1 year;
Percent consolidation \((U\%)\) after 1 year is \(29.6\%.\) (see Step 2)
Settlement after 1 year = total settlement \(\times\) percent consolidation
Settlement after 1 year \(= 0.0743 \times 0.296 = 0.022 m\) (0.87 in.)
