Guest Question.

Given:

Data in for San Francisco Bay mud. The initial water content of the specimen is 105.7% and the \(C_{c}\) is 1.23.

Required:

From the data in Table 9.4 and Fig. 9.17, estimate the (a) \(C_{\alpha}\) and (b) \(C_{\alpha \varepsilon}\). (c) Compare with the values calculated in .

TABLE 9.4 Values of \(C_{\alpha} / C_{c}\) for Natural Geotechnical Materials
Material \(C_{\alpha} / C_{c}\)
Granular soils in Cluding rockfill \(0.02 \pm 0.01\)
Shale and mudstone \(0.03 \pm 0.01\)
Inorganic clays and silts \(0.04 \pm 0.01\)
Organic clays and silts \(0.05 \pm 0.01\)
Peat and muskeg \(0.06 \pm 0.01\)
Admin
Solution

a. Use an average value of \(C_{\alpha} / C_{c}\) of 0.04. Therefore

 

\(C_{\alpha}=0.04\left(C_{c}\right)=0.04(1.23)=0.05\)

 

b. From Eq. (9.29), \(C_{\alpha \varepsilon}=C_{\alpha} / 1+e_{o}\) From Fig. Ex. 9.11b, \(e_{o}=2.855\). Therefore

 

\(C_{\alpha \varepsilon}=\frac{C_{\alpha}}{1+e_{o}}\) (9.29)

 

\(C_{\alpha \varepsilon}=\frac{0.05}{1+2.855}=0.013\)

 

A second way to estimate the modified secondary compression index is to use Fig. 9.17, where \(C_{\alpha \varepsilon}\) is plotted versus natural water content. For our example, the initial water content was 105.7%. From Fig. 9.17, a value of \(C_{\alpha \varepsilon}\) of about 0.01 (or higher) is obtained if you use the dashed line.

c. Compare with the calculated values. From , \(C_{\alpha}=0.052 \text { and } C_{\alpha \varepsilon}=0.0135\). agreement using the approximate values is acceptable for preliminary design estimates.
