Guest Question.

Explain the acidity order in Table 10.4 for butanoic acid and its 2- and 3-chloro derivatives.

Table 10.4 The Ionization Constants of Some Acids
Name Formula \(K_{a}\) \( pK_{a}\)
formic acid HCOOH \(2.1 × 10^{-4}\) 3.68
acetic acid \(CH_{3}COOH\) \(1.8 × 10^{-5}\) 4.74
propanoic acid \(CH_{3}CH_{2}COOH\) \(1.4 × 10^{-5}\) 4.85
butanoic acid \(CH_{3}CH_{2}CH_{2}COOH\) \(1.6 × 10^{-5}\) 4.80
chloroacetic acid \(ClCH_{2}COOH\) \(1.5 × 10^{-3}\) 2.82
dichloroacetic acid \(Cl_{2}CHCOOH\) \(5.0 × 10^{-2}\) 1.30
trichloroacetic acid \(Cl_{3}CCOOH\) \(2.0 × 10^{-1}\) 0.70
2-chlorobutanoic acid \(CH_{3}CH_{2}CHClCOOH\) \(1.4 × 10^{-3}\) 2.85
3-chlorobutanoic acid \( CH_{3}CHClCH_{2}COOH\) \(8.9 × 10^{-5}\) 4.05
benzoic acid \(C_{6}H_{5}COOH\) \(6.6 × 10^{-5}\) 4.18
o-chlorobenzoic acid \(o-Cl-C_{6}H_{4}COOH\) \(12.5 × 10^{-4}\) 2.90
m-chlorobenzoic acid \(m-Cl-C_{6}H_{4}COOH\) \(1.6 × 10^{-4}\) 3.80
p-chlorobenzoic acid \(p-Cl-C_{6}H_{4}COOH\) \(1.0 × 10^{-4}\) 4.00
p-nitrobenzoic acid \(p-NO_{2}-C_{6}H_{4}COOH\) \(4.0 × 10^{-4}\) 3.40
phenol \(C_{6}H_{5}OH\) \(1.0 × 10^{-10}\) 10.00
ethanol \(CH_{3}CH_{2}OH\) \(1.0 × 10^{-16}\) 16.00
water HOH \(1.8 × 10^{-16}\) 15.74
Admin
Solution

The 2-chloro substituent increases the acidity of butanoic acid substantially, due to its inductive effect. In fact, the effect is about the same as for chloroacetic and acetic acids. The 3-chloro substituent exerts a similar but much smaller effect, because the C-Cl bond is now farther away from the carboxylate group. Inductive effects fall off rapidly with distance.
