Guest Question.

A button-pad disk brake uses dry sintered metal pads. The pad radius is \(\frac{1}{2}\) in, and its center is 2 in from the axis of rotation of the \(3\frac{1}{2}\)-in-diameter disk. Using half of the largest allowable pressure, \(p_{max} \)= 350 psi, find the actuating force and the brake torque. The coefficient of friction is 0.31.

 

Admin
Solution

Since the pad radius R = 0.5 in and eccentricity e = 2 in,

\(\frac{R}{e} = \frac{0.5}{2} = 0.25 \)

From Table 16–1, by interpolation, δ = 0.963 and \(p_{max}∕p_{av}\) = 1.290. It follows that the effective radius e is found from Equation (16–41):

\(r_{e} = δe = 0.963(2)\) = 1.926 in

and the average pressure is

\(p_{av} = \frac{p_{max}∕2}{1.290} =\frac{ 350∕2}{1.290} = 135.7 psi \)

The actuating force F is found from Equation (16–42) to be

\(F = πR^{2}p_{av} = π(0.5)^{2}135.7 = 106.6 \) lbf (one side)

The brake torque T is

\(T = fFr_{e} = 0.31(106.6)1.926 = 63.65 \)lbf · in (one side)
