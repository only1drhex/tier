Guest Question.

A simply supported beam has a span of 4 m, a rectangular cross section 100 mm wide by 200 mm deep and carries a uniformly distributed load of intensity w N/mm over its complete span. A rectangular strain gauge rosette positioned at mid-span on the upper surface of the beam and in the vertical plane of symmetry recorded the following values of strain:

\(\varepsilon_{ a }=-900 \times 10^{-6}, \varepsilon_{ b }=-200 \times 10^{-6}, \varepsilon_{ c }=+300 \times 10^{-6}\)

If Young’s modulus E is \(200000 N / mm ^{2}\) calculate the value of Poisson’s ratio, the principal stresses at the point and hence the load intensity.
Ans. \(\nu=0.33, \sigma_{ I }=0, \sigma_{ II }=-180 N / mm ^{2}, w=60 N / mm\) .

Admin
Solution

From Section 7.8 Poisson’s ratio is given by

\(v=\frac{\varepsilon_{ c }}{\varepsilon_{ a }}=\frac{300 \times 10^{-6}}{900 \times 10^{-6}}=0.33\)

Substituting the strain gauge readings in Eqs (14.34) and (14.35)

\(\varepsilon_{ I }=\frac{1}{2}\left(\varepsilon_{ a }+\varepsilon_{ c }\right)+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \sqrt{\left(\varepsilon_{ a }-\varepsilon_{ b }\right)^{2}+\left(\varepsilon_{ c }-\varepsilon_{ b }\right)^{2}}\) (14.34)

\(\varepsilon_{ II }=\frac{1}{2}\left(\varepsilon_{ a }+\varepsilon_{ c }\right)-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \sqrt{\left(\varepsilon_{ a }-\varepsilon_{ b }\right)^{2}+\left(\varepsilon_{ c }-\varepsilon_{ b }\right)^{2}}\) (14.35)

gives the principal strains at the point.

\(\varepsilon_{ I }=308.6 \times 10^{-6}, \quad \varepsilon_{ II }=-908.3 \times 10^{-6}\)

The principal stresses follow from Eqs (14.32) and (14.33)

\(\sigma_{ I }=\frac{E}{1-v^{2}}\left(\varepsilon_{ I }+v \varepsilon_{ II }\right)\) (14.32)

\(\sigma_{ II }=\frac{E}{1-v^{2}}\left(\varepsilon_{ II }+v \varepsilon_{ I }\right)\) (14.33)

and are

\(\sigma_{ I }=0, \quad \sigma_{ II }=-180.0 N / mm ^{2}\)

Note that the above values of principal stress may be slightly different from the calculated values due to rounding off errors. Now adding Eqs (14.8) and (14.9)

\(\sigma_{ I }=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2}+\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right)^{2}+4 \tau_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.8)

\(\sigma_{ II }=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2}-\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right)^{2}+4 \tau_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.9)

and noting that \(\sigma_{y}=0\)

\(\sigma_{ I }+\sigma_{ II }=\sigma_{x}=0-180=-180.0 N / mm ^{2}\)

Then, from Eq. (9.9)

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I}\) (9.9)

\(-180=-\frac{w \times\left(4 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2} \times 100}{8 \times\left(100 \times 200^{3} / 12\right)}\)

from which
w = 60N/mm
Note: In this example the value of \(\sigma_{x}\left(-180.0 N / mm ^{2}\right)\) could have been obtained directly from the strain gauge reading \(\varepsilon_{ a }\left(-900 \times 10^{-6} \times 200000=-180.0 N / mm ^{2}\right)\) .
