Guest Question.

Determine \(\Delta E_{c}, \Delta E_{v}\), and \(V_{b i}\) for an \(\mathrm{n}-\mathrm{Ge}\) to \(\mathrm{P}-\mathrm{GaAs}\) heterojunction using the electron affinity rule.

Consider n-type Ge doped with \(N_{d}=10^{16} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}\) and P-type GaAs doped with \(N_{a}=10^{16} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}\). Let \(T=300 \mathrm{~K}\) so that \(n_{i}=2.4 \times 10^{13} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}\) for Ge.

Admin
Solution

From Equation (9.34a), we have

\(\Delta E_{c}=e\left(\chi_{n}-\chi_{P}\right)\) (9.34a)

\(\Delta E_{c}=e\left(\chi_{n}-\chi_{P}\right)=e(4.13-4.07)=0.06 \mathrm{eV}\)

and from Equation (9.34b), we have

\(\begin{aligned}\Delta E_{c}+\Delta E_{v}=& E_{g P}-E_{g n}=\Delta E_{g} \\ \end{aligned}\) (9.34b)

\(\begin{aligned} \Delta E_{v}=\Delta E_{g}-\Delta E_{c}=(1.43-0.67)-0.06=0.70 \mathrm{eV}\end{aligned}\)

To determine \(V_{b i}\) using Equation (9.39), we need to determine \(p_{n o}\) in Ge, or

\(\begin{array}{r}e V_{b i}=\Delta E_{v}+k T \ln \left(\frac{p_{p o}}{p_{n o}} \cdot \frac{N_{v n}}{N_{v P}}\right) \\ \end{array}\) (9.39)

\(\begin{array}{r}p_{n o}=\frac{n_{i}^{2}}{N_{d}}=\frac{\left(2.4 \times 10^{13}\right)^{2}}{10^{16}}=5.76 \times 10^{10} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}\end{array}\)

Then

\(e V_{b i}=0.70+(0.0259) \ln \left[\frac{\left(10^{16}\right)\left(6 \times 10^{18}\right)}{\left(5.76 \times 10^{10}\right)\left(7 \times 10^{18}\right)}\right]\)

or, finally,

\(V_{b i} \approx 1.0 \mathrm{~V}\)

Comment

There is a nonsymmetry in the \(\Delta E_{c}\) and \(\Delta E_{v}\) values that will tend to make the potential barriers seen by electrons and holes different. This nonsymmetry does not occur in homojunctions.
