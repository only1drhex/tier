Guest Question.

A caliper brake is used 24 times per hour to arrest a machine shaft from a speed of 250 rev/min to rest. The ventilation of the brake provides a mean air speed of 25 ft/s. The equivalent rotary inertia of the machine as seen from the brake shaft is 289 lbm · in · s. The disk is steel with a density \(\gamma=0.282 lbm / in ^{3}\), a specific heat capacity of 0.108 Btu/(lbm · \(\left.{ }^{\circ} F \right)\), a diameter of 6 in, a thickness of \(\frac{1}{4}\) in. The pads are dry sintered metal. The lateral area of the brake surface is 50 \(\text { in }^{2}\). Find \(T_{\max } \text { and } T_{\min }\) for the steady-state operation.

Admin
Solution
\(t_{1}=60^{2} / 24=150 s\)

 

Assuming a temperature rise of \(T_{\max }-T_{\infty}=200^{\circ} F\), from Fig. 16–24a,

 

\(h_{r}=3.0\left(10^{-6}\right) Btu /\left( in ^{2} \cdot s \cdot{ }^{\circ} F \right)\)

 

\(h_{c}=2.0\left(10^{-6}\right) Btu /\left( in ^{2} \cdot s \cdot{ }^{\circ} F \right)\)

 

Fig. 16–24b

 

\(\hbar_{ CR }=h_{r}+f_{v} h_{c}=3.0\left(10^{-6}\right)+4.8(2.0) 10^{-6}=12.6\left(10^{-6}\right) Btu /\left( in ^{2} \cdot s \cdot{ }^{\circ} F \right)\)

 

The mass of the disk is

 

\(W=\frac{\pi \gamma D^{2} h}{4}=\frac{\pi(0.282) 6^{2}(0.25)}{4}=1.99 lbm\)

 

Eq. (16–58):

 

\(E=\frac{1}{2} \frac{I}{9336}\left(\omega_{o}^{2}-\omega_{f}^{2}\right)\) (16–58)

 

\(E=\frac{1}{2} \frac{I}{9336}\left(\omega_{o}^{2}-\omega_{f}^{2}\right)=\frac{289}{2(9336)}\left(\frac{2 \pi}{60} 250\right)^{2}=10.6 Btu\)

 

\(\beta=\frac{\hbar_{ CR } A}{W C_{p}}=\frac{12.6\left(10^{-6}\right) 50}{1.99(0.108)}=2.93\left(10^{-3}\right) s ^{-1}\)

 

Eq. (16–59):

 

\(\Delta T=\frac{E}{W C}\) (16–59)

 

\(\Delta T=\frac{E}{W C_{p}}=\frac{10.6}{1.99(0.108)}=49.3^{\circ} F\)

 

Eq. (16–60):

 

\(T_{\max }=T_{\infty}+\frac{\Delta T}{1-\exp \left(-\beta t_{1}\right)}\) (16–60

 

\(T_{\max }=70+\frac{49.3}{1-\exp \left[-2.93\left(10^{-3}\right) 150\right]}=209^{\circ} F\)

 

\(T_{\min }=209-49.3=160^{\circ} F\)

 

The predicted temperature rise here is \(T_{\max }-T_{\infty}=139^{\circ} F\). Iterating with revised values of \(h_{r} \text { and } h_{c}\) from Fig. 16–24a, we can make the solution converge to \(T_{\max }=220^{\circ} F\) and \(T_{\min }=171^{\circ} F\).

Table 16–3 for dry sintered metal pads gives a continuous operating maximum temperature of 570–660◦F. There is no danger of overheating.
