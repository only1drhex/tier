Guest Question.

A large tank (860 liters) is used as a gas-liquid contactor. Gas bubbles up through the vessel and out the top, liquid flows in at one part and out the other at 5 litersls. To get an idea of the flow pattern of liquid in this tank a pulse of tracer (M = 150 gm) is injected at the liquid inlet and measured at the outlet, as shown in Fig. E11.2a.

(a) Is this a properly done experiment?

(b) If so, find the liquid fraction in the vessel.

(c) Determine the E curve for the liquid.

(d) Qualitatively what do you think is happening in the vessel?

Admin
Solution

(a) Check the material balance against the tracer curve. From the material balance, Eq. 3, we should have

\(\text { Area }=\frac{M}{v}=\frac{150 \mathrm{gm}}{5 \text { liters/s }}=30 \frac{\mathrm{gm} \cdot \mathrm{s}}{\text { liter }}=0.5 \frac{\mathrm{gm} \cdot \mathrm{min}}{\text { liter }}\)

From the tracer curve

\(\text { Area }=A_{1}\left(1+\frac{1}{4}+\frac{1}{16}+\cdots\right)=0.375\left(\frac{4}{3}\right)=0.5 \frac{\mathrm{gm} \cdot \mathrm{min}}{\text { liter }}\)

These values agree. The results are consistent.

(b) For the liquid, Eq. 4 gives

\(\bar{t}_{l}=\frac{\int t C d t}{\int C d t}=\frac{1}{0.5}\left[2 A_{1}+4 \times \frac{A_{1}}{4}+6 \times \frac{A_{1}}{16}+8 \times \frac{A_{1}}{64}+\cdots\right]=2.67 \mathrm{~min}\)

Thus the liquid volume in the vessel is

\(V_{l}=\bar{t}_{l} v_{l}=2.67(5 \times 60)=800 \text { liters }\)

and the volume fraction of phases is

\(\left.\begin{array}{c}\text { Fraction of liquid }=\frac{800}{860}=93 \% \\\text { Fraction of gas }=7 \%\end{array}\right\}\)

(c) Finally, from Eq. 5 we find the \(\mathbf{E}\) curve, of

\(\mathbf{E}=\frac{C_{\text {pulse }}}{M / v}=\frac{0.75}{0.5} \mathrm{C}=1.5 \mathrm{C}\)

Thus the \(\mathbf{E}\) curve for the liquid is as shown in Fig. \(E 11.2 b\)

(d) The vessel has a strong recirculation of liquid, probably induced by the rising bubbles.
