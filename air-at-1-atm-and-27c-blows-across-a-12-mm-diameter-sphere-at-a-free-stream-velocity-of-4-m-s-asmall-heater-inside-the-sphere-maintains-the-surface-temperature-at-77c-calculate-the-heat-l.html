Guest Question.

Air at 1 atm and \(27 ^{◦} C \) blows across a 12-mm-diameter sphere at a free-stream velocity of 4 m/s.Asmall heater inside the sphere maintains the surface temperature at \( 77 ^{◦} C \). Calculate the heat lost by the sphere.

Admin
Solution

Consulting Equation (6-30)

\(Nu=2+(0.4Re^{1/2} _{d} +0.06 Re^{2/3} _{d} )Pr^{0.4}\enspace (μ_{∞} /μ_{w} )^{1/4 }\quad [6-30] \)

which is valid for the range \(3.5<Red <8×10^{4} \enspace and\enspace 0.7<Pr <380 \) Properties in Equation (6-30) are evaluated at the free-stream temperature.

we find that the Reynolds number is evaluated at the free-stream
temperature.We therefore need the following properties: at \(T_{∞} =27^{◦} C=300 K\) ,

\(ν=15.69×10^{−6} m^{2} /s\quad k =0.02624 \enspace W/m· ^{◦} C,\)

 

\(Pr =0.708\qquad μ_{∞} =1.8462×10^{−5} kg/m· s\)

 

\(At \enspace T_{w} =77^{◦} C=350\> K,\)

 

\(μ_{w} =2.075×10^{−5} \)

The Reynolds number is thus

\(Re_{d} =\frac{(4)(0.012)}{15.69×10^{−6} }=3059 \)

From Equation (6-30),

 

\(\overline{Nu}=2+[(0.4)(3059)^{1/2} +(0.06)(3059)^{2/3} ](0.708)^{0.4} (\frac{1.8462}{2.075})^{1/4}= 31.40 \)

and

\(\overline{h}=\overline{Nu}\left(\frac{k}{d} \right)=\frac{(31.4)(0.02624)}{0.012}=68.66 \> W/m^{2} · ^{◦} C \)

The heat transfer is then

\(q=\overline{h} A(T_{w} −T_{∞} )=(68.66)(4π)(0.006)^{2} (77−27)=1.553\; W \).

For comparison purposes let us also calculate the heat-transfer coefficient using Equation (6-25).

\(\frac{hd}{kf}=0.37\left(\frac{u_{∞} d}{ν_{f} } \right) ^{0.6}\; for\; 17<Re_{d} <70,000 \hspace{30 pt} [6-25] \)

 

The film temperature is \(T_{f} =(350+300)/2=325\; K\) so that

\(ν_{f} =18.23×10^{−6 } m^{2} /s\qquad k_{f} =0.02814 \quad W/m· ^{◦} C \)

and the Reynolds number is

\(Re_{d}=\frac{(4)(0.012)}{18.23×10^{−6} } =2633 \)

From Equation (6-25)

\(\frac{hd}{kf}=0.37\left(\frac{u_{∞} d}{ν_{f} } \right) ^{0.6}\; for\; 17<Re_{d} <70,000 \hspace{30 pt} [6-25] \)

 

\(Nu_{f}=(0.37)(2633)^{0.6} =41.73 \)

 

and \(\overline{h} \) is calculated as

\(\overline{h} =Nu\left(\frac{k_{f} }{d} \right) =\frac{(41.73)(0.02814)}{0.012} =97.9 \> W/m^{2} · ^{◦} C\)

or about 42 percent higher than the value calculated before.
