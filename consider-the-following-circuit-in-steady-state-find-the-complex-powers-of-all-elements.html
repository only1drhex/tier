Guest Question.

Consider the following circuit in steady state.

Find the complex powers of all elements.

Admin
Solution

In this case, we have \(\omega=2 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}\), leading to the following conversions:

  • \(\cos (2 t) \longrightarrow \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \underline{/0} \mathrm{~V}\)
  • \(2 \mathrm{~F} \longrightarrow-j / 4 \Omega\),
  • \(1 \mathrm{H} \longrightarrow j 2 \Omega\).

Hence, in the phasor domain, the circuit is as follows.

The complex power of the \(4 \Omega\) resistor can be found as

\(s_{4 \Omega}=\underline{v}_{4 \Omega} \times \underline{i}_{4 \Omega}^{*} =\frac{\left|\underline{v}_{4 \Omega}\right|^{2}}{Z_{4 \Omega}^{*}}=1 / 8 \mathrm{~W} .\)

Next, the current across the capacitor and \(1 / 4 \Omega\) resistor can be obtained as

\(\underline{i}_{C}=\underline{i}_{1 / 4 \Omega}=\frac{\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \underline{/0} }{\frac{1}{4}-\frac{j}{4}}=\frac{\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \underline{/0} }{\frac{\sqrt{2}}{4} \underline{/(-\pi/4)}}=2 \underline{/\pi/4} ,\)

leading to

\(\begin{aligned}s_{C} &=\underline{v}_{C} \times \underline{i}_{C}^{*}=Z_{C}\left|\underline{i}_{C}\right|^{2}=(-j / 4) \times 4=-j \mathrm{VA}, \\s_{1 / 4 \Omega} &=\underline{v}_{1 / 4 \Omega} \times \underline{i}_{1 / 4 \Omega}^{*}=Z_{1 / 4 \Omega}\left|\underline{i}_{1 / 4 \Omega}\right|^{2}=1 \mathrm{~W}.\end{aligned}\)

Finally, the current across the inductor and \(2 \Omega\) resistor is obtained similarly as

\(\underline{i}_{L}=\underline{i}_{2 \Omega}=\frac{\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \underline{/0} }{2+j 2}=\frac{\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \underline{/0}}{2 \sqrt{2} \underline{/\pi/4}}=\frac{1}{4} \underline{/(-\pi/4)} . \)

Therefore, the corresponding complex powers can be calculated as

\(\begin{aligned}s_{L} &=\underline{v}_{L} \times \underline{i}_{L}^{*}=Z_{L}\left|\underline{i}_{L}\right|^{2}=j 2 \times \frac{1}{16}=j / 8 \mathrm{VA} \\s_{2 \Omega} &=\underline{v}_{2 \Omega} \times \underline{i}_{2 \Omega}^{*}=Z_{2 \Omega}\left|\underline{i}_{2 \Omega}\right|^{2}=1 / 8 \mathrm{~W} .\end{aligned}\)

Using the conservation of energy, we can also obtain the power of the voltage source as

\(\begin{aligned}s_{s} &=-s_{4 \Omega}-s_{C}-s_{1 / 4 \Omega}-s_{L}-s_{2 \Omega} \\&=-1 / 8+j-1-j / 8-1 / 8=-10 / 8+j 7 / 8=(-10+j 7) / 8 \mathrm{VA}.\end{aligned}\)
