Guest Question.

A converging nozzle has a throat area of 6 \(cm^2\) and stagnation air conditions of 120 kPa and 400 K. Compute the exit pressure and mass flow if the back pressure is (a) 90 kPa and (b) 45 kPa. Assume k = 1.4.

Admin
Solution

From Eq. (9.32) for k = 1.4 the critical (sonic) throat pressure is

\(\frac{p^*}{p_0} = 0.5283 or p^* = (0.5283)(120 kPa) = 63.4 kPa\)

 

\(\frac{p^*}{p_0} = \left(\frac{2}{k + 1}\right)^{k/(k-1)} = 0.5283 \frac{\rho^*}{\rho_0} = \left(\frac{2}{k + 1}\right)^{1/(k-1)} = 0.6339\)

\(\frac{T^*}{T_0} = \frac{2}{k + 1} = 0.8333 \frac{a^*}{a_0} = \left(\frac{2}{k + 1}\right)^{1/2} = 0.9129\) (9.32)

If the back pressure is less than this amount, the nozzle flow is choked.

Part (a)

For \(p_b = 90 kPa \gt p^*\), the flow is subsonic, not choked. The exit pressure is \(p_e = p_b\). The throat Mach number is found from the isentropic relation (9.35) or Table B.1:

\(Ma^2 = 5\left(\frac{T_0}{T} – 1\right) = 5 \left[\left(\frac{\rho_0}{\rho}\right)^{2/5} – 1\right] = 5 \left[\left(\frac{p_0}{p}\right)^{2/7} – 1\right]\) (9.35)

\(Ma_e^2 = 5 \left[\left(\frac{p_0}{p_e}\right)^{2/7} – 1\right] = 5 \left[\left(\frac{120}{90}\right)^{2/7} – 1\right] = 0.4283 Ma_e = 0.654\)

To find the mass flow, we could proceed with a serial attack on \(Ma_e, T_e, a_e, V_e, and \rho_e\), hence to compute \(\rho_e A_e V_e\). However, since the local pressure is known, this part is ideally suited for the dimensionless mass flow function in Eq. (9.47). With \(p_e/p_0\) = 90/120 = 0.75, compute

Mass flow function = \(\frac{\dot{m}}{A} \frac{\sqrt{RT_0}}{p_0} = \sqrt{\frac{2k}{k – 1} \left(\frac{p}{p_0}\right)^{2/k} \left[1 – \left(\frac{p}{p_0}\right)^{(k-1)/k}\right]}\) (9.47)

\(\frac{\dot{m} \sqrt{RT_0}}{Ap_0} = \sqrt{\frac{2(1.4)}{0.4} (0.75)^{2/1.4} [1 – (0.75)^{0.4/1.4}]} = 0.6052\)

hence \(\dot{m} = 0.6052 \frac{(0.0006)(120,000)}{\sqrt{287(400)}} = 0.129 kg/s\)

for \(p_e = p_b = 90 kPa\)

Part (b)

For \(p_b = 45 kPa \lt p^*\), the flow is choked, similar to condition d in Fig. 9.11b. The exit pressure is sonic:

\(p_e = p^* = 63.4 kPa\)

The (choked) mass flow is a maximum from Eq. (9.46b):

\(\dot{m}_{max} = 0.6847A^* \rho_0(RT_0)^{1/2} = \frac{0.6847p_0 A^*}{(RT_0)^{1/2}}\) (9.46b)

\(\dot{m} = \dot{m}_{max} = \frac{0.6847p_0 A_e}{(RT_0)^{1/2}} = \frac{0.6847(120,000)(0.0006)}{[287(400)]^{1/2}} = 0.145 kg/s\)

Any back pressure less than 63.4 kPa would cause this same choked mass flow. Note that the 50 percent increase in exit Mach number, from 0.654 to 1.0, has increased the mass flow only 12 percent, from 0.128 to 0.145 kg/s.
