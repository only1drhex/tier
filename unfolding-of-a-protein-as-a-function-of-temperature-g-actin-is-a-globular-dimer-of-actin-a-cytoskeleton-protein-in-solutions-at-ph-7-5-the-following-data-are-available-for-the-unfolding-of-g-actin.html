Unfolding of a Protein as a Function of Temperature

G-actin is a globular dimer of actin, a cytoskeleton protein. In solutions at pH = 7.5 the following data are available for the unfolding of G-actin at 25ºC:

\(\Delta_{ unf } G=27 \frac{ kJ }{ mol } \quad \Delta_{ unf } \underline{H}=197 \frac{ kJ }{ mol } \quad \text { and } \quad \Delta_{ unf } C_{ P }=27 \frac{ J }{ mol K }\)

Determine the temperature at which half of the G-actin is unfolded (that is, find its “melting temperature”).

Admin
Solution

The apparent equilibrium constant for this unimolecular chemical reaction is

 

\(K(T)=\frac{M_{\text {unfolded }}(T)}{M_{\text {folded }}(T)}=\frac{\alpha(T) \cdot M_{ o } \cdot \gamma_{\text {unfolded }}(T)}{\left(M_{ o }-\alpha(T) \cdot M_{ o }\right) \cdot \gamma_{\text {folded }}(T)}=\frac{\alpha(T)}{(1-\alpha(T))}\)

 

where α(T) is the fraction of the initial G-actin that has been denatured at the temperature T, and since there is only a conformational change between the folded and unfolded protein, we have assumed that their activity coefficients are equal, and therefore cancel from the equation. Also, we are interested in the melting temperature, that is, the temperature \(T_{ m }\) at which α = 1/2, so that K = 1 and ln K = 0. Therefore, the equation to be solved is

 

\(\ln K\left(T_{ m }\right)=0\)

 

However

 

\(\begin{aligned}\ln K\left(T_{ m }\right)=-\frac{\triangle_{ unf } G\left(25^{\circ} C \right)}{R \cdot 298.15}-\frac{\triangle_{ unf } H\left(25^{\circ} C \right)}{R}\left(\frac{1}{T_{ m }}-\frac{1}{298.15}\right) \\&+\frac{\triangle_{ unf } C_{ P }}{R}\left[\ln \left(\frac{T_{ m }}{298.15}\right)+\frac{298.15}{T_{ m }}-1\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

or

 

\(\begin{aligned}0=-\frac{27000}{8.314 \cdot 298.15}-\frac{197000}{8.314} P\left(\frac{1}{T_{ m }}-\frac{1}{298.15}\right) & \\&+\frac{54000}{R}\left[\ln \left(\frac{T_{ m }}{298.15}\right)+\frac{298.15}{T_{ m }}-1\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

which has the solution of \(T_{ m }\) = 330.0 K = 56.8ºC, which is close to the measured value of 57.1ºC

It is also of interest to determine the fraction of G-actin that is unfolded at any temperature. To do this we solve the equation

 

\(K(T)=\frac{\alpha}{1-\alpha} \quad \text { or } \quad \alpha=\frac{K(T)}{1+K(T)}\)

 

where

 

\(\begin{aligned}K(T)=\exp \left[-\frac{27000}{8.314 \cdot 298.15}-\frac{197000}{8.314}\left(\frac{1}{T}-\frac{1}{298.15}\right)\right.\\&\left.+\frac{54000}{R}\left[\ln \left(\frac{T}{298.15}\right)+\frac{298.15}{T}-1\right]\right]\end{aligned}\)
