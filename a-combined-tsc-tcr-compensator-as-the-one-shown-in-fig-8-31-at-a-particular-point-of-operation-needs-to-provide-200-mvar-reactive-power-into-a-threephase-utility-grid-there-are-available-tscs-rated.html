Guest Question.

A combined TSC-TCR compensator as the one shown in Fig. 8.31 at a particular point of operation needs to provide 200 MVAR reactive power into a threephase utility grid. There are available TSCs rated at 60 MVAR and TCRs rated at 60 MVAR. The magnitude of the SVC and the utility grid line-to-line voltage is 400 kV. Perform the following:

a) How many TSCs and TCRs are needed to handle the rated reactive power?

b) What is the firing angle of the TCR if it is connected in Y?

Admin
Solution

a) Since the rated reactive power to be injected into the utility is \(200 MVAR\), four TSC compensator units will be used injecting into the grid \(Q_{TSC(total)} = 240 MVAR\) and the excess \(40 MVAR\) will be absorbed by the TCR unit. Since the TCR unit is rated to absorb from the utility grid \(Q_{TCR(total)} = 60 MVAR\) the thyristors of TCR have to be controlled through their firing angle so that the required reactive power to be absorbed to be \(Q_{TCR(\alpha)} = 40 MVAR\). Therefore, using these SVC units the net injected capacitive reactive power into the utility grid will be:

\(Q_{injected(total)} = Q_{TSC(total)} – Q_{TCR(\alpha)}= 4 \times 60 – 40 \times 1 = 200 MVAR\)

Therefore, using the above specifications the following results are found:

\(X_{L(total per phase)}\) = total available TCR reactance of each phase

\(=\frac{\widetilde{V}^2_{i(phase)} }{Q_{TCR(total per phase)}} = \frac{(\frac{400 \times 10^3}{\sqrt{3}})^2}{\frac{60 \times 10^6}{3}} = 2666.67 \Omega\)

However, the required reactance in each phase for wye connection is:

\(X_{L}(α)_{(total per phase)} = \frac{\widetilde{V}^2_{i(phase)} }{Q(\alpha)_{TCR(required per phase)}} = \frac{(\frac{400 \times 10^3}{\sqrt{3}})^2}{\frac{40 \times 10^6}{3}} = 4000\Omega\)

Also, is known that the inductive susceptance of a TCR is given by:

\(B_L(\alpha) = \frac{1}{X_L(\alpha)} = \frac{1}{X_{L(total)}} (1 – \frac{2}{\pi} \alpha – \frac{1}{\pi} \sin 2\alpha) S(siemens)\)

and, consequently:

\(X_L(\alpha) = \frac{X_{L(total)}}{1 – \frac{2}{\pi} \alpha – \frac{1}{\pi} \sin 2\alpha}\) or \(4000 = \frac{2666.67}{1 – \frac{2}{\pi} \alpha – \frac{1}{\pi} \sin 2\alpha}\)

Using numerical analysis methods (e.g., Newton Raphson method or other) the value of \(\alpha\) is calculated from the above equation and is given as:

\(\alpha = 15.36°\)

Therefore, for the TCR of each phase to absorb \(\frac{40\times 10^6}{3}\) VAR from the utility grid, the thyristor firing angle should be adjusted to 15.36° with respect to the origin of the phase voltage. Fig. 8.33 shows the required SVC system.
