Guest Question.

Show that the differential operator \(T: P _{2} \rightarrow P _{2}\) defined by \(T(p)=x^{2} p^{\prime \prime}+(2 x-1) p^{\prime}+ 3p\) is diagonalizable.

Admin
Solution

With respect to the basis \(B=\left\{1, x, x^{2}\right\}\), T has the matrix representation

\(Q=\left[\begin{array}{rrr}3 & -1 & 0 \\0 & 5 & -2 \\0 & 0 & 9\end{array}\right]\).

Since Q is triangular, we see that the eigenvalues are 3, 5, and 9; and since Q has distinct eigenvalues, Q can be diagonalized where the matrix S of eigenvectors will diagonalize Q.
We calculate the eigenvectors \(u _{1}, u _{2}, u _{3}\) for Q and form \(S=\left[ u _{1}, u _{2}, u _{3}\right]\), which yields

\(S=\left[\begin{array}{rrr}1 & 1 & 1 \\0 & -2 & -6 \\0 & 0 & 12\end{array}\right]\).

In this case it follows that

\(S^{-1} Q S=\left[\begin{array}{lll}3 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 5 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 9\end{array}\right]=R\).

In view of our remarks above, R is the matrix representation for T with respect to the basis \( C=\left\{ v _{1}, v _{2}, v _{3}\right\}, \text { where }\left[ v _{i}\right]_{B}= S _{i}\), or

\(\left[ v _{1}\right]_{B}=\left[\begin{array}{c}1 \\0 \\0\end{array}\right], \quad\left[ v _{2}\right]_{B}=\left[\begin{array}{r}1 \\-2 \\0\end{array}\right], \quad \text { and } \quad\left[ v _{3}\right]_{B}=\left[\begin{array}{r}1 \\-6 \\12\end{array}\right]\).

Therefore, the basis C is given precisely as \(C=\left\{1,1-2 x, 1-6 x+12 x^{2}\right\}\). Moreover, it is easy to see that \(T\left( v _{1}\right)=3 v _{1}, T\left( v _{2}\right)=5 v _{2}, \text { and } T\left( v _{3}\right)=9 v _{3}, \text { where } v _{1}=1, v _{2}=1-2 x\), and \(v _{3}=1-6 x+12 x^{2}\).
