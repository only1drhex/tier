Guest Question.

A strip footing, 1.5 m wide, rests on the surface of a dry cohesionless soil having \(\phi=20^{\circ} \text { and } \gamma=19 kN / m ^{3}\). If the water table rises temporarily to the surface due to flooding, calculate the percentage reduction in the ultimate bearing capacity of the soil. Assume \(N_{\gamma}=5.0\).

Admin
Solution
\(\phi=20^{\circ} \quad N_{\gamma}=5.0 \quad b=1.5 m \quad D_{f}=0\)

Dry cohesionles soil, ∴ c = 0

\(\begin{aligned}q_{\text {ult }} &=c N_{c}+\frac{1}{2} \gamma b N_{\gamma}+\gamma D_{f} N_{q}=\frac{1}{2} \gamma b N_{\gamma} \text { in this case. } \\&=\frac{1}{2} \times 19 \times 1.5 \times 5.0=71.3 kN / m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

If the water table rises temporarily to the surface due to flooding, reduction factors \(R_{\gamma}\) and \(R_{q}\) shall be applied as the maximum values for the \(N_{\gamma} \text { and } N_{q}\) terms respectively.
In this case, \(R_{\gamma}=0.5 \text { is applied for } N_{\gamma}\)-term.

\(\therefore \quad q_{\text {ult }}=\frac{1}{2} \gamma b N_{\gamma} \cdot R_{\gamma}=\frac{1}{2} \times 19 \times 1.5 \times 5.0 \times 0.5=35.6 kN / m ^{2}\)

The percentage reduction in the ultimate bearing capacity is thus 50 due to flooding and consequent complete submergence.

(\(\text { Note: } \gamma_{\text {sat }} \text { is assumed to be } \gamma \text { itself here, and } \gamma^{\prime}=\frac{1}{2} \gamma_{\text {sat }}\))
