Guest Question.

A low-speed single-stage rig is required to simulate a repeating stage of an air compressor with a flow coefficient of 0.5, a stage loading of 0.45 and 25° of preswirl at stage inlet. The motor that will drive the rig runs at 500 rpm. The aspect ratio of the rotor blade is to be 1.3 and its hub-to-tip radius ratio is 0.8.
a. If the Reynolds number needs to be \(3\times 10^{5}\) , determine the mean radius for the rig, the rotor blade height and the motor power required. Use the following definition of Reynolds number based on midspan rotor chord:

\(Re=\frac{\rho c_{x} l}{\mu } \), where \( \mu =1.8\times 10^{-5} kg/ms\) and \(\rho =1.2 kg/m^{3} \)

b. Calculate the stage reaction, the rotor relative flow angles, and the stator inlet flow angle. If the Lieblein diffusion factor for the rotor at the design point cannot exceed 0.55 and for the stator cannot exceed 0.5, determine the number of rotor and stator blades required. Assume that the stator aspect ratio is 1.5.
c. Confirm that the rig is low speed by estimating the rotor tip relative Mach number.

Admin
Solution

a. Use the hub-to-tip ratio to relate the mean radius to the blade height:

\(\frac{r_{h} }{r_{t} }=\frac{r_{m}-H/2 }{r_{m}+H/2} =0.8 \therefore 0.2r_{m}=0.9H \Rightarrow r_{m}=4.5H\)

Expressing the Re in terms of H:

\(Re=\frac{\rho c_{x} l}{\mu }=\frac{\rho \phi r_{m} \Omega l}{\mu} =\frac{\rho \phi r_{m} \Omega H}{\mu \left(H/l\right)} =\frac{4.5\rho \phi \Omega H^{2} }{\mu \left(H/l\right)} \)

 

\(\therefore H=\sqrt{\frac{\mu \left(H/l\right)Re}{4.5 \rho \phi \Omega} }=\sqrt{\frac{1.8\times 10^{-5}\times 1.3\times 3\times 10^{5}}{4.5\times 1.2\times 0.5\times 100\times \pi /6} } =0.223 m \)

 

\( \Rightarrow r_{m}=4.5 H=4.5\times 0.223=1.003 m\)

The motor power can be found from the stage loading and the rig mass flow:

\( \dot{W} =\dot{m} \Delta h_{0}=\dot{m}\psi U^{2} \)

\(\therefore \dot{W}=\left(2\pi r_{m}H\rho c_{x}\right)\psi \left(r_{m}\Omega \right) ^{2} = \left(2\pi \rho r_{m}H\phi r_{m} \Omega \right)\psi \left(r_{m}\Omega \right) ^{2} =2\pi \rho \phi \psi Hr^{4}_{m}\Omega ^{3} \)

\( \dot{W}=2\pi \times 1.2\times 0.5\times 0.45\times 0.223\times 1.003^{4}\times \left(100\pi /6\right) ^{3} =54.96 kW \)

This power requirement is reasonable for a large low-speed rig. The large blade size (0.223 m span) would enable high resolution measurements to be made at the correct Re number.
b. From Eq. (5.23):

\(\psi =2\left(1-R-\phi \tan \alpha _{1} \right) \Rightarrow R=1-\psi /2-\phi \tan \alpha _{1}=1-0.45/2-0.5\tan 25°=0.542 \)

\(\tan \beta _{1} =\frac{1}{\phi } – \tan \alpha _{1}=\frac{1}{0.5}-\tan 25°\Rightarrow \beta _{1}=56.9° \)

From Eq (5.17b):

\(\psi =\phi \left(\tan \beta _{1}-\tan \beta _{2}\right)\) (5.17b)

\(\tan \beta _{2}=\tan \beta _{1}-\frac{\psi }{\phi } =1.5337-\frac{0.45}{0.5} \Rightarrow \beta _{2}=32.4° \)

 

\(\tan \alpha _{2}=\frac{1}{\phi } -\tan \beta _{2}=\frac{1}{0.5}-\tan 32.4° \Rightarrow \alpha _{2}=53.8°\)

 

For the rotor, the pitch-to-chord ratio is found from Eq. (3.36), which is the incompressible version of Eq. (5.18),

 

\( DF=\left(1-\frac{\cos \alpha _{1} }{\cos \alpha _{2}} \right) +\frac{s}{l}\frac{\cos \alpha _{1}}{2} \left(\tan \alpha _{1}-\tan \alpha _{2}\right)\) (3.36)

 

\( DF=\left(1-\frac{w _{2} }{w_{1}} \right) +\frac{\Delta c_{\theta }}{2w_{1} } \frac{s}{l} \) (5.18)

 

This must be applied to the relative angles of the rotor:

\(\frac{s}{l}=2 \left(\frac{\cos\beta _{1} }{\cos\beta _{2}}-1+DF\right) /\cos\beta _{1} \left(\tan \beta _{1}-\tan \beta _{2}\right)\)

 

\(\frac{s}{l}\leq 2\left(\frac{\cos 56.9}{\cos 32.4}-1+0.55 \right) /\cos 56.9\left(\tan 56.9-\tan 32.4\right)=0.801 \)

The inequality is used so that the DF used is a maximum. The number of rotor blades is then,

 

\(Z_{rotor} =\frac{2\pi r_{m} }{s}=\frac{2\pi \left(H/l\right)r_{m} /H}{s/l} \Rightarrow Z_{rotor}\geq \frac{2\pi \times 1.3\times 4.5}{0.801} =45.87 \therefore Z_{rotor}=46 \)

 

For the stator,

\(\frac{s}{l}=2 \left(\frac{\cos\alpha _{2} }{\cos\alpha _{3}}-1+DF\right) /\cos\alpha _{2} \left(\tan \alpha _{2}-\tan \alpha _{3}\right)\)

 

\(\frac{s}{l} \leq 2\left(\frac{\cos 53.8}{\cos 25}-1+0.5 \right) /\cos 53.8\left(\tan 53.8-\tan 25\right)=0.571 \)

 

The number of stator blades is then,

 

\(Z_{stator} =\frac{2\pi r_{m} }{s}=\frac{2\pi \left(H/l\right)r_{m} /H}{s/l} \Rightarrow Z_{stator}\geq \frac{2\pi \times 1.5\times 4.5}{0.571} =74.3 \therefore Z_{stator}=75 \)

 

c. The rotor relative Mach number is given by

 

\( M_{1,rel} =\frac{w_{1} }{\sqrt{\gamma RT_{1}}}=\frac{c_{x}/\cos \beta _{1}}{\sqrt{\gamma RT_{1}}} =\frac{\phi r\Omega }{\cos \beta _{1}\sqrt{\gamma RT_{1}}}\)

Assuming the flow angles are the same at the tip, this will simply scale with the radius.
Hence, the tip relative Mach number is

 

\( M_{1t,rel}=\frac{\phi r_{m} \Omega }{\cos \beta _{1}{\sqrt{\gamma RT_{1}}}}\frac{r_{t}}{r_{m}} =\frac{0.5\times 1.003\times \left(100\pi /6\right) }{\cos 56.9\sqrt{1.4\times 287\times 288} } \frac{5}{4.5} =0.16 \)

 

This will be close to the highest Mach number in the rig, and therefore the flow can be treated as incompressible throughout.
