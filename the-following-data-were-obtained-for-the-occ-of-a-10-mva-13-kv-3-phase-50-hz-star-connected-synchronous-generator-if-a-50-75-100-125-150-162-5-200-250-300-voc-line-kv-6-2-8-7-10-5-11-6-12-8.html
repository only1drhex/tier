Guest Question.

The following data were obtained for the OCC of a 10 MVA, 13 kV, 3-phase, 50 Hz, star-connected synchronous generator:

\(I_{f} (A)\) 50 75 100 125 150 162.5 200 250 300
\(V_{OC} (line) (kV)\) 6.2 8.7 10.5 11.6 12.8 13.7 14.2 15.2 15.9
Admin
Solution
\(I_{a}(\text { rated })=\frac{10 \times 10^{6}}{\sqrt{3} \times 13 \times 10^{3}}=444 A \)

(a) The OCC and SCC are plotted in Fig. 8.24 from which the short-circuit armature current corresponding to \( V_{t}\) (rated) = 13 kV (line) on the OCC is

\( I_{SC}\) = 688 A

\( \therefore\) \( X_{s} \text { (adjusted) }=\frac{V_{t} \text { (rated) }}{\sqrt{3} \times I_{S C} \text { (corresponding to } V_{t} \text { (rated)) }}\)

 

\( =\frac{13 \times 1000}{\sqrt{3} \times 688}=10.9 \Omega\)

(b) To find the leakage reactance, the Potier triangle must be constructed. Figure 8.25 shows the plot of the OCC and the location of the point \( P\) corresponding to ZPF at rated current and voltage and the point \( P^{\prime}\) corresponding to short-circuit at rated current.

At P draw parallel to the horizontal axis \( P R=O P^{\prime}\). At R draw RS parallel to the initial slope of the OCC thereby locating the point S on the OCC. Draw SQ perpendicular to RP. Then

\( S Q=\sqrt{3} I_{a} \text { (rated) } X_{l}=1200 V \)

\( \therefore\) \( X_{l}=\frac{1200}{\sqrt{3} \times 444}=1.56 \Omega\)

(c) From Fig. 8.22, the armature reaction in the equivalent field current is

\( I_{f}^{a r}=Q P=90 A\)

The phasor diagram is drawn in Fig. 8.26. Proceeding computationally

\( \bar{E}_{r}=13 \angle 0^{\circ}+j \sqrt{3} \times 444 \times 1.56 \times 10^{-3}=13.75 \angle 4^{\circ} kV \text { (line) }\)

From the OCC corresponding to \( E_{r}=13.75 kV\)

 

\( I_{f}^{r}=185 A\) leads \( E_{r} \text { by } 90^{\circ}\)

From the phasor diagram

\( I_{f}=\left[\left(185+90 \sin 40.9^{\circ}\right)^{2}+\left(90 \cos 40.9^{\circ}\right)^{2}\right]^{1 / 2}=253 A\)

corresponding to which the value of \( E_{f}\) from the OCC is

\( E_{f}=15.2 kV \text { (line) }\)

 

\( \therefore \) Voltage regulation \( =\frac{E_{f}-V_{t}}{V_{t}} \times 100\)

 

\( =\frac{15.2-13}{13} \times 100=16.9 \% \)

Voltage regulation using \( X_{s}\) (adjusted):

\( \bar{E}_{f}=\bar{V}_{t}+j \sqrt{3} \quad \bar{I}_{a}\) (rated) \( X_{s} \) (adjusted)

\( =13000+j \sqrt{3} \times 444(0.8-j 0.6) \times 10.9\)

= 18029 + j 6706

Or \( E_{f}=19.24 kV \text { (line) }\)

 

\( I_{f} \) (from modified air-gap line) \( =\frac{150}{13} \times 19.24=222 A\)

 

\( \left.V_{t}(O C)\right|_{I_{f}=222 A }=14.8 kV\)

 

\( \therefore\) Voltage regulation \( =\frac{14.8-13}{13} \times 100=13.85 \% \)
