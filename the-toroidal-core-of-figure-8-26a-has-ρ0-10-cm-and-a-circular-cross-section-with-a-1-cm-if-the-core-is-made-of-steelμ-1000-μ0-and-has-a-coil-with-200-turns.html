Guest Question.

The toroidal core of Figure 8.26(a) has \(ρ_{0} \)= 10 cm and a circular cross section with a = 1 cm. If the core is made of steel(\(μ= 1000 μ_{0} \)) and has a coil with 200 turns, calculate the amount of current that will produce a flux of 0.5 m Wb in the core.

Admin
Solution

This problem can be solved in two different ways: by using the magnetic field approach (direct), or by using the electric circuit analog (indirect).
Method 1: Since\( ρ_{0}\) is large compared with a, from ,

\(B=\frac{\mu NI}{\ell} =\frac{\mu _{0}\mu _{r}NI}{2\pi \rho _{0}}\)

Hence,
\(\Psi =BS=\frac{\mu _{0}\mu _{r}NI\pi a^{2}}{2\pi \rho _{0}}\)
or
\(I=\frac{2\rho _{0}\Psi }{\mu _{0}\mu _{r}Na^{2}}=\frac{2(10\times 10^{-2})(0.5\times 10^{-3})}{4\pi \times 10^{-7}(1000)(200)(1\times 10^{-4})}\)
\(=\frac{100}{8\pi }=\)3.979 A

Method 2: The toroidal core in Figure 8.26(a) is analogous to the electric circuit of Figure 8.26(b). From the circuit and Table 8.4.
\(\mathscr{F}=NI=\Psi \Re =\Psi \frac{\ell}{\mu S}=\Psi \frac{2\pi \rho _{0}}{\mu _{0}\mu _{r}\pi a^{2}}\)
or
\(I=\frac{2\rho _{0}\Psi }{\mu _{0}\mu _{r}Na^{2}}=\)3.979 A

as obtained with Method 1.
