Guest Question.

The system acetone(1)/acetonitrile(2)/nitromethane(3) at \(80^{\circ}C\) and 110 kPa has the overall composition \(z_{1} = 0.45, z_{2} = 0.35, z_{3} = 0.20.\) Assuming that Raoult‚Äôs law is appropriate to this system, determine \(\iota, v , \left\{x_{i}\right\}, \)and \(\left\{y_{i}\right\}.\) The vapor pressures of the pure species at \(80^{\circ}C\) are:

\( P_{1}^{sat}=195.75 P_{2}^{sat}=97.84 P_{3}^{sat}=50.32 kPa\)
Admin
Solution

First, do a BUBL P calculation with \(\left\{z_{i}\right\} = \left\{x_{i}\right\} \)to determine \(P_{bubl}:\)

\( \begin{aligned} &P_{\text {bubl }}=x_{1} P_{1}^{\text {sat }}+x_{2} P_{2}^{\text {sat }}+x_{3} P_{3}^{\text {sat }} \\ &P_{\text {bubl }}=(0.45)(195.75)+(0.35)(97.84)+(0.20)(50.32)=132.40 kPa \end{aligned} \)

Next, do a DEW P calculation with \(\left\{z_{i}\right\} = \left\{x_{i}\right\} \) to find \(P_{dew}:\)

\( P_{\text {dew }}=\frac{1}{y_{1} / P_{1}^{\text {sat }}+y_{2} / P_{2}^{\text {sat }}+y_{3} / P_{3}^{\text {sat }}}=101.52 kPa \)

Because the given pressure lies between \(P_{bubl}\) and \(P_{dew},\) the system is in the two-phase region, and a flash calculation is possible.
From Raoult‚Äôs law, Eq. (13.16), we have \(K_{i}=y_{i} /x_{i} = P_{i}^{sat}/ P,\) from which:

\( K_{1}=1.7795 K_{2}=0.8895 K_{3}=0.4575\)

 

\( y_{i} P=x_{i} P_{i}^{\text {sat }} \quad (i=1,2, \ldots, N) \) (13.16)

Substituting known values into Eq. (13.106) gives:

\( \sum_{i} \frac{z_{i} K_{i}}{1+ V \left(K_{i}-1\right)}=1 \) (13.106)

 

\( \frac{(0.45)(0.7795)}{1+0.7795 V }+\frac{(0.35)(0.8895)}{1-0.1105 V }+\frac{(0.20)(0.4575)}{1-0.5425 V }=1 \)

Trial-and-error or iterative solution for V followed by evaluation of the other unknowns yields:

\( V =0.7364 mol \quad L =1- V =0.2636 mol \)

 

\(\begin{aligned} &y_{1}=\frac{(0.45)(1.7795)}{1+(0.7795)(0.7634)}=0.5087 \quad y_{2}=0.3389 \quad y_{3}=0.1524\\&x_{1}=\frac{y_{1}}{K_{1}}=\frac{0.5087}{1.7795}=0.2859 \quad x_{2}=0.3810 \quad x_{3}=0.3331 \end{aligned}\)

Reassuringly, \( \sum_{i} x_{i}=\sum_{i} y_{i}=1 \)
