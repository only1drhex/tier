Guest Question.

A uniform plane wave in air with

\(E=8 \cos(\omega t-4x-3z)a_{y} \)V/m

is incident on a dielectric slab (z≥0) with \(μ_{r}= 1.0, ε_{r}= 2.5, \)σ= 0. Find
(a) The polarization of the wave
(b) The angle of incidence
( c) The reflected E field
(d) The transmitted H field

Admin
Solution

(a) From the incident E field, it is evident that the propagation vector is
\(k_{i}=4a_{x}+3a_{z}\rightarrow k_{i}=5=\omega \sqrt{\mu _{0}\varepsilon _{0}}=\frac{\omega }{c}\)
Hence,
\(\omega =5c=15\times 10^{8}\) rad/s

A unit vector normal to the interface (z = 0) is \(a_{z}\). The plane containing k and \(a_{z}\) is y = constant, which is the xz-plane, the plane of incidence. Since \(E_{i}\) is normal to this plane, we have perpendicular polarization (similar to Figure 10.18).
(b) The propagation vectors are illustrated in Figure 10.19 where it is clear that
\(\tan \theta _{i}=\frac{k_{ix}}{k_{iz}}=\frac{4}{3}\rightarrow \theta _{i}=53.13^{\circ }\)

Alternatively, without Figure 10.19, we can obtain \(\theta _{i}\) from the fact that \(\theta _{i}\) is the angle between k and \(a_{n}\), that is,
\(\cos \theta _{i}=a_{k}.a_{n}=(\frac{4a_{x}+3a_{z}}{5} ).a_{z}=\frac{3}{5}\)
or
\(\theta _{i}=53.13^{\circ }\)

(c) An easy way to find \(E_{r}\) is to use eq. (10.126a) because we have noticed that this problem is similar to that considered in Section 10.10B. Suppose we are not aware of this. Let

\(E_{rs}=E_{ro}e^{-j\beta _{1}(x \sin \theta _{r}-z \cos \theta _{r})}a_{y}\) (10.126a)
\(E_{r}=E_{ro}\cos (\omega t-k_{r}.r)a_{y}\)

which is similar to form to the given \(E_{i}\). The unit vector \(a_{y}\) is chosen in view of the fact that the tangential component of E must be continuous at the interface. From Figure 10.19,
\(k_{r}=k_{rx}a_{x}-k_{rz}a_{z}\)
where
\(k_{rx}=k_{r}\sin \theta _{r}, k_{rz}=k_{r}\cos \theta _{r}\)

But \(\theta _{r}=\theta _{i}\) and \(k_{r}= k_{i} = 5\) because both \(k_{r} \)and \(k_{i}\) are in the same medium. Hence,
\(k_{r}=4a_{x}-3a_{z}\)

To find \(E_{ro}\), we need \(\theta _{t}\)· From Snell’s law
\(\sin \theta _{t}=\frac{n_{1}}{n_{2}} \sin \theta _{i}=\frac{c\sqrt{\mu _{1}\varepsilon _{1}} }{c\sqrt{\mu _{2}\varepsilon _{2}} }\sin \theta _{i}\)
\(=\frac{\sin 53.13^{\circ }}{\sqrt{2.5} }\)
or
\(\theta _{t}=30.39^{\circ }\)
\(\Gamma _{\bot }=\frac{E_{ro}}{E_{io}}\)
\(=\frac{\eta _{2}\cos \theta _{i}-\eta _{1}\cos \theta _{t}}{\eta _{2}\cos \theta _{i}+\eta _{1}\cos \theta _{t}}\)
where \(\eta _{1}=\eta _{0}=377 \Omega , \eta _{2}=\sqrt{\frac{\mu _{0}\mu _{r_{2}}}{\varepsilon _{0}\varepsilon _{r_{2}}} }=\frac{377}{\sqrt{2.5} }=238.4\Omega\)
\(\Gamma _{\bot }=\frac{238.4 \cos 53.13^{\circ }-377 \cos 30.39^{\circ } }{238.4 \cos 53.13^{\circ }+377 \cos 30.39^{\circ } } =-0.389\)
Hence,
\(E_{ro}=\Gamma _{\bot }\) \(E_{io}=-0.389(8)=-3.112\)
and
\(E_{r}=-3.112 \cos (15\times 10^{8}t-4x+3z)a_{y}\) V/m

(d) Similarly, let the transmitted electric field be
\(E_{t}=E_{to} \cos (\omega t-k_{t}.r)a_{y}\)
where
\(k_{t}=\beta _{2}=\omega \sqrt{\mu _{2}\varepsilon _{2}}=\frac{\omega }{c}\sqrt{\mu_{r_{2}}\varepsilon _{r_{2}}}\)
\(=\frac{15\times 10^{8}}{3\times 10^{8}}\sqrt{1\times 2.5}=7.906\)
From Figure 10.19,
\(k_{tx}=k_{t} \sin \theta _{t}=4\)
\(k_{tz}=k_{t} \cos \theta _{t}=6.819\)
or
\(k_{t}=4a_{x}+6.819 a_{z}\)

Notice that \(k_{ix}= k_{rx}=k_{tx}\) as expected.
\(\tau _{\bot }=\frac{E_{to}}{E_{io}}=\frac{2\eta _{2}\cos \theta _{i}}{\eta _{2}\cos \theta _{i}+\eta _{1} \cos \theta _{t}}\)
\(=\frac{2\times 238.4 \cos 53.13^{\circ } }{ 238.4 \cos 53.13^{\circ } +377 \cos 30.39^{\circ }}\)
=0.611

The same result could be obtained from the relation \(\tau _{\bot }=1+\Gamma _{\bot }\). Hence,
\(E_{to}=\tau _{\bot }E_{io}=0.611\times 8=4.888\)
\(E_{t}=4.888 \cos (15\times 10^{8}t-4x-6.819z)a_{y}\) V/m

From \(E_{t}, H_{t}\) is easily obtained as
\(H_{i}=\frac{1}{\mu _{2}\omega }k_{t}\times E_{t}=\frac{a_{k_{t}}\times E_{t}}{\eta _{2}}\)
\(=\frac{4a_{x}+6.819a_{z}}{7.906(238.4)}\times 4.888 a_{y} \cos (\omega t-k.r)\)
\(H_{t}=(-17.69a_{x}+10.37a_{z})\cos (15\times 10^{8}t-4x-6.819z) \)mA/m
