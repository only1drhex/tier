Guest Question.

Let Q denote the House holder matrix of the form (1), where \(u =[1,2,0,1]^{T}\). Calculate Qx and QA, where

\(x =\left[\begin{array}{l}1 \\1 \\4 \\3\end{array}\right] \quad \text { and } \quad A=\left[\begin{array}{rr}1 & 6 \\2 & 0 \\1 & 5 \\-2 & 3\end{array}\right]\).

Admin
Solution

By Eq. (1) \(Q=I-\frac{2}{ u ^{T} u } u u ^{T}\), Q is the (4 × 4) matrix \(Q=I-b u u ^{T}\), where \(b=2 /\left( u ^{T} u \right)=2 / 6=1 / 3\). In detail, Qx is given by

\(Q x =\left(I-(1 / 3) u u ^{T}\right) x = x -\frac{ u ^{T} x }{3} u = x -\frac{6}{3} u = x -2 u\).

Thus Qx is the vector

\(Q x = x -2 u =\left[\begin{array}{c}1 \\1 \\4 \\3\end{array}\right]-2\left[\begin{array}{c}1 \\2 \\0 \\1\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{r}-1 \\-3 \\4 \\1\end{array}\right]\).

The matrix QA is found by forming \(Q A=\left[Q A _{1}, Q A _{2}\right]\). Briefly, the calculations are

\(Q A _{1}= A _{1}-\left(\frac{ u ^{T} A _{1}}{3}\right) u = A _{1}- u\)
\(Q A _{2}= A _{2}-\left(\frac{ u ^{T} A _{2}}{3}\right) u = A _{2}-3 u\).

Thus QA is the (4 × 2) matrix given by

\(Q A=\left[\begin{array}{rr}0 & 3 \\0 & -6 \\1 & 5 \\-3 & 0\end{array}\right]\).
