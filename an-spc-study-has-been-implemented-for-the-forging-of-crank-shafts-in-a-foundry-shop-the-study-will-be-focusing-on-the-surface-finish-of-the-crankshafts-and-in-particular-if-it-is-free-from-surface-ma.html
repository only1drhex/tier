Guest Question.

An SPC study has been implemented for the forging of crank-shafts in a foundry shop. The study will be focusing on the surface finish of the crankshafts and in particular if it is free from surface marks. This will be car-ried out by a simple visual inspection. The sample size is 5 and the frequency of sampling is per batch, which is approximately 1000. The initial data .gath-ered are given in Table 8.15. Using these data, compile an attribute control chart for the forging process using a p chart .

Solution

The first step is to calculate the proportion of defectives p for each sample as follows:

\( p = \frac{f}{n} = \frac{65}{998} = 0.065\)

For simplicity, this has been carried out for only the first sample and the rest are shown in the control chart in Fig. 8.15. The process mean and the aver-age sample size must now be calculated as follows:

\( \bar{ p} = \frac{\sum{p} }{\sum{n}} = \frac{1.221}{25} = 0.049\) \( \bar{ n} = \frac{\sum{n}}{N} = \frac{25 281}{25} =1011\)

The control limits are calculated using both the process mean and the average sample size as follows:

\( UCL_{p}= \bar{ p} + 3 \sqrt{\frac{\bar{ p} (1 – \bar{ p} )} {n} } \) \( LCL_{p}= \bar{ p} – 3 \sqrt{\frac{\bar{ p} (1 – \bar{ p} )} {n} } \)

\( UCL_{p}= 0.049 + 3 \sqrt{\frac{0.049 (1 – 0.049 )} {1011} } \) \( LCL_{p}= 0.049 – 3 \sqrt{\frac{0.049 (1 – 0.049 )} {1011} } \)

\( UCL_{p}= 0.049 + 0.020 \) \( LCL_{p}= 0.049 – 0.020 \)

\( UCL_{p}= 0.069 \) \( LCL_{p}= 0.029 \)

All of the above should be plotted on the p chart as illustrated in Fig. 8.15.
