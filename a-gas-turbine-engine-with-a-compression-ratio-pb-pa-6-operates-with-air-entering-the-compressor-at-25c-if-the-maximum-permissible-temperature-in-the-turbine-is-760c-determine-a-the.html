Guest Question.

A gas-turbine engine with a compression ratio \(P_{B}/P_{A} = 6\) operates with air entering the compressor at \(25^{\circ}C.\) If the maximum permissible temperature in the turbine is \(760^{\circ}C,\) determine:
(a) The thermal efficiency \(\eta\) of the reversible air-standard cycle for these conditions if \(\gamma = 1.4.\)
(b) The thermal efficiency for an air-standard cycle for the given conditions if the compressor and turbine operate adiabatically but irreversibly with efficiencies \(\eta_{c}= 0.83\) and \(\eta_{t} = 0.86.\)

Admin
Solution

(a) Direct substitution in Eq. (8.12) gives the efficiency:

\( \eta=1-\left(\frac{P_{A}}{P_{B}} \right)^{(\gamma -1)/\gamma }\) (8.12)

\( \eta =1-(1/6)^{(1.4-1)/1.4}=1-0.60=0.40\)

(b) Irreversibilities in both the compressor and turbine reduce the thermal effi-ciency of the engine because the net work is the difference between the work required by the compressor and the work produced by the turbine. The temper-ature of air entering the compressor \(T_{A}\) and the temperature of air entering the turbine, the specified maximum for \(T_{C},\) are the same as for the reversible cycle of part (a). However, the temperature after irreversible compression in the com-pressor \(T_{B}\) is higher than the temperature after isentropic compression \(T^{\prime }_{B},\) and the temperature after irreversible expansion in the turbine \(T_{D}\) is higher than the temperature after isentropic expansion \(T^{\prime }_{D}.\)

The thermal efficiency of the engine is given by:

\( \eta =\frac{-(W_{turb}+W_{comp})}{Q_{BC}}\)

The two work terms are found from the expressions for isentropic work:

\( W_{turb}=\eta _{t}C_{P}^{ig}(T_{D}^{\prime }-T_{C})\)

\( W_{comp}=\frac{C_{p}^{ig}(T^{\prime }_{B}-T_{A})}{\eta _{c}}\) (A)

The heat absorbed to simulate combustion is:

\( Q_{BC}=C_{P}^{ig}(T_{C}−T_{B})\)

These equations combine to yield:

\( \eta =\frac{\eta_{t}(T_{C}-T_{D}^{\prime })-(1/\eta _{c})(T_{B}^{\prime }-T_{A})}{T_{C}-T_{B}}\)

An alternative expression for the compression work is:

\( W_{comp}=C_{P^{ig}}(T_{B}− T_{A})\) (B)

Equating this and Eq. (A) and using the result to eliminate \(T_{B}\) from the equation for \(\eta\) gives, after simplification:

\( \eta =\frac{\eta _{t}\eta _{c}(T_{C}/T_{A}-T_{D}^{\prime }/T_{A})-(T_{B}^{\prime }/T_{A}-1)}{\eta _{c}(T_{C}/T_{A}-1)-(T_{B}^{\prime }/T_{A}-1)}\) (C)

The ratio \(T_{C}/T_{A}\) depends on given conditions. The ratio \(T_{B}^{\prime } /T_{A}\) is related to the pressure ratio by Eq. (8.10). In view of Eq. (8.11), the ratio \(T_{D}^{\prime } /T_{A}\) can be expressed as:

\(\frac{T_{A}}{T_{B}}=\left(\frac{P_{A}}{P_{B}} \right)^{(\gamma -1)/\gamma }\) (8.10)

\(\frac{T_{D}}{T_{C}}=\left(\frac{P_{D}}{P_{C}} \right)^{(\gamma -1)/\gamma }=\left(\frac{P_{A}}{P_{B}} \right)^{(\gamma -1)/\gamma }\) (8.11)

 

\( \frac{T_{D}^{\prime }}{T_{A}}=\frac{T_{C}T_{D}^{\prime }}{T_{A}T_{C}}=\frac{T_{C}}{T_{A}}\left(\frac{P_{A}}{P_{B}} \right)^{(\gamma -1)/\gamma }\)

Substituting these expressions in Eq. (C) yields:

\( \eta =\frac{\eta _{t}\eta _{\eta }(T_{C}/T_{A})(1-1/\alpha )-(\alpha -1)}{\eta _{c}(T_{C}/T_{A}-1)-(\alpha -1)}\) (8.13)

where \(\alpha =\left(\frac{P_{B}}{P_{A}} \right)^{(\gamma -1)/\gamma }\)

One can show by Eq. (8.13) that the thermal efficiency of the gas-turbine engine increases as the temperature of the air entering the turbine \((T_{C})\) increases, and as the compressor and turbine efficiencies \(\eta _{c}\) and \(\eta _{t}\) increase.
The given efficiency values are here:

\( \eta _{t} = 0.86\) and \(\eta _{c} = 0.83\)

Other given data provide:

\( \frac{T_{C}}{T_{A}}=\frac{760+273.15}{25+273.15}=3.47\)

and \( \alpha =(6)^{(1.4-1)/1.4}=1.67\)

Substituting these quantities in Eq. (8.13) gives:

\( \eta =\frac{(0.86)(0.83)(3.47)(1-1/1.67)-(1.67-1)}{(0.83)(3.47-1)-(1.67-1)}=0.235\)

This analysis shows that even with rather high compressor and turbine efficiencies, the thermal efficiency \((23.5\%)\) is considerably reduced from the reversible-cycle value of part (a) \((40\%).\)
