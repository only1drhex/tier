Determination of an Equivalent Force System

A table supports the vertical forces shown.
(a) Determine an equivalent force system at the center of the table, point O.
(b) Determine an equivalent force system consisting of a single force, and specify the x and y coordinates of the point where the forceâ€™s line of action intersects the table.

Admin
Solution

Road Map Both scalar and vector approaches are effective for Part (a) of this problem, and we will use a vector approach. For Part (b), a straightforward scalar evaluation will provide the necessary location of an equivalent force system that consists of a single force only.

Part (a)

Governing Equations & Computation With the following force and position vectors

\(\vec{F} _{A} = -260 \hat{k} N, \vec{r} _{OA} = \left(0.5\hat{\imath} + 0.2\hat{\jmath} \right) m\) (1)

\(\vec{F} _{B} = -120 \hat{k} N, \vec{r} _{OB} = 0.4 \hat{\jmath} m\) (2)

\(\vec{F} _{C} = – 100 \hat{k} N \vec{r} _{OC} = \left(-0.4\hat{\imath} – 0.3\hat{\jmath} \right) m\) (3)

we use Eq. (4.15) on p. 223 to evaluate the resultant force and moment at point O as

\(\vec{F} _{R} = \sum\limits_{i =1}^{n}{\vec{F} _{i} } ,\)

\(\vec{M} _{R} = \sum\limits_{i =1}^{n}{\vec{r} _{i} } \times \vec{F} _{i} + \sum\limits_{i =1}^{m}{\vec{M} _{i} }\) (4.15)

\(\vec{F} _{R} = \vec{F} _{A} + \vec{F} _{B} + \vec{F} _{C}\)

\(= – 480 \hat{k}N\) (4)

\(\vec{M} _{RO} = \vec{r} _{OA}\times \vec{F} _{A} + \vec{r} _{OB}\times \vec{F} _{B} + \vec{r} _{OC}\times \vec{F} _{C}\)

\(= \left(- 70 \hat{\imath} + 90 \hat{\jmath} \right) N.m\) (5)

\(M_{RO} = \sqrt{\left(- 70\right) ^{2} + \left(90\right) ^{2} } N. m= 114 N.m\) (6)

This force system is illustrated in Fig. 2.

Part (b)

Governing Equations & Computation As shown in Fig. 4.32 on p. 225, an equivalent force system consisting of a single force is obtained by moving \(\vec{F} _{R}\) to a new position, point D, where D is located a distance d perpendicular to the plane containing \(\vec{F} _{R}\) and \(\vec{M} _{RO}\)

\(d = \frac{M_{RO} }{F_{R} } = \frac{114 N.m}{480N} = 0.238 m\) (7)

This force system is shown in Fig. 3. To determine the coordinates of D, similar triangles with the geometry shown in Fig. 4 may be used to write

\(x= \left(0.238 m\right) \frac{90}{114} = 0.188 m\) (8)

\(y= \left(0.238 m\right) \frac{70}{114} = 0.146 m\) (9)

Discussion & Verification The force systems shown in Figs. 1 through 3 are all equivalent. When developing an equivalent force system that consists of a single force only, as in Part (b), sometimes you may find that the line of action of the force does not intersect the structure.
