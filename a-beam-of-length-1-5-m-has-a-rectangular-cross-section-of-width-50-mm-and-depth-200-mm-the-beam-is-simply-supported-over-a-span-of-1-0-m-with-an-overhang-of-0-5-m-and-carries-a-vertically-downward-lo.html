Guest Question.

A beam of length 1.5 m has a rectangular cross section of width 50 mm and depth 200 mm. The beam is simply supported over a span of 1.0 m with an overhang of 0.5 m and carries a vertically downward load of 200 kN at the free end of the overhang. Calculate the deflection of the beam midway between the supports allowing for the effects of both bending and shear. Take \(E=200000 N / mm ^{2}\) and \(G=70000 N / mm ^{2}\). What percentage of the total deflection is due to shear?
Ans. 1.03 mm upwards, 8.7%.

Admin
Solution

The second moment of area of the beam cross section about its Gz axis is

\(I_{z}=\frac{50 \times 200^{3}}{12}=33.3 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\)

Referring now to Fig. S.13.19
and taking moments about B

\(R_{ A } \times 1.0+200 \times 0.5=0\)

which gives

\(R_{ A }=-100 kN\)

From Eq. (13.3)

\(\frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=\frac{M}{E I}\) (13.3)

\(E I \frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=R_{ A } x\)

Then

\(E I \frac{ d v}{ d x}=R_{ A } \frac{x^{2}}{2}+C_{1}\)

and

\(E I v=R_{ A } \frac{x^{3}}{6}+C_{1} x+C_{2}\) (i)

When x = 0, v = 0 and when x = 1.0 m, v = 0. From the first of these boundary conditions \(C_{2}=0\) and from the second, \(C_{1}=-R_{ A } / 6=100 \times 10^{3} / 6\). Eq. (i) then becomes

\(E I v=-\frac{100 \times 10^{3}}{6}\left(x^{3}-x\right)\) (ii)

At the mid-point of AB the vertical displacement due to bending is, from Eq. (ii)

\(v_{ m , ptAB (\text { bend })}=-\frac{100 \times 10^{3}}{\left(200000 \times 10^{6}\right) \times\left(33.3 \times 10^{6} / 10^{12}\right)}\left(0.5^{3}-0.5\right)\)

which gives

\(v_{\text {m.pt AB (bend) }}=0.00094 m =0.94 mm\)

The deflection due to shear is given by Eq. (13.22)

\(v_{s}=\frac{\beta}{G} \int_{L}\left(\frac{S_{y}}{A}\right) d x\) (13.22)

and, at the mid-point of the span AB is

\(v_{\text {m.pt AB (shear) }}=\frac{1.2}{70000} \int_{0}^{500} \frac{100 \times 10^{3}}{50 \times 200} d x\)

from which

\(v_{ m , pt AB (\text { shear })}=0.09 mm\)

The total deflection due to both bending and shear is then 0.94 + 0.09 = 1.03 mm. The
percentage due to shear is (0.09/1.03) ×100 = 8.7%.
