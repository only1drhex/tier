Guest Question.

Choose an Oiles 500 bushing to give a maximum wear of 0.001 in for 800 h of use with a 300 rev/min journal and 50 lbf radial load. Use \(h_{ CR }=2.7 Btu /\left( h \cdot ft ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} F \right), T_{\max }=\) 300°F, \(f_{s}=0.03\), and a design factor \(n_{d}=2\). Table 12–13 lists the available bushing sizes from the manufacturer.

Admin
Solution

With a design factor \(n_{d}\), substitute \(n_{d}\) F for F. First, estimate the bushing length using Eq. (12–32) with \(f_{1}=f_{2}=1\), and \(K=0.6\left(10^{-10}\right)\) from Table 12–8:

 

\(w=f_{1} f_{2} K \frac{4}{\pi} \frac{F}{D L} \frac{\pi D N t}{12}=\frac{f_{1} f_{2} K F N t}{3 L}\) (12–32)

 

\(L=\frac{f_{1} f_{2} K n_{d} F N t}{3 w}=\frac{1(1) 0.6\left(10^{-10}\right) 2(50) 300(800)}{3(0.001)}=0.48 in\) (1)

From Eq. (12–38) with \(f_{s}=0.03\) from Table 12–9, \(\hbar_{ CR }=2.7 Btu /\left( h \cdot ft ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} F \right)\), and \(n_{d}\) F for F,

 

\(T_{f} \doteq T_{\infty}+\frac{10 \pi f_{s} F D N}{J \hbar_{ CR }(2 \pi D L / 144)}=T_{\infty}+\frac{720 f_{s} F N}{J \hbar_{ CR } L}\) (12–38)

 

\(L \doteq \frac{720 f_{s} n_{d} F N}{J h_{ CR }\left(T_{f}-T_{\infty}\right)}=\frac{720(0.03) 2(50) 300}{778(2.7)(300-70)}=1.34 \text { in }\)

 

The two results bracket L such that 0.48 \(\leq L \leq 1.34\) in. As a start let L = 1 in. From Table 12–13, we select D = 1 in from the midrange of available bushings.

Trial 1: D = L = 1 in.

Eq. (12–31):

 

\(P_{\max }=\frac{4}{\pi} \frac{F}{D L}\) (12–31)

 

\(P_{\max }=\frac{4}{\pi} \frac{n_{d} F}{D L}=\frac{4}{\pi} \frac{2(50)}{1(1)}=127 psi <3560 psi\) (OK)

 

\(P=\frac{n_{d} F}{D L}=\frac{2(50)}{1(1)}=100 psi\)

 

Eq. (12–29):

 

\(V=\frac{\pi D N}{12}\) (12–29)

 

\(V=\frac{\pi D N}{12}=\frac{\pi(1) 300}{12}=78.5 ft / min <100 ft / min\) (OK)

 

\(P V=100(78.5)=7850 psi \cdot ft / min <46700 psi \cdot ft / min\) (OK)

Our second estimate is \(L \geq 0.48(1.64)=0.787\) From Table 12–13, there is not much available for \(L=\frac{7}{8}\) in. So staying with L = 1 in, try \(D=\frac{1}{2}\) in.

Trial 2: D = 0.5 in, L = 1 in.

 

\(P_{\max }=\frac{4}{\pi} \frac{n_{d} F}{D L}=\frac{4}{\pi} \frac{2(50)}{0.5(1)}=255 psi <3560 psi\) (OK)

 

\(P=\frac{n_{d} F}{D L}=\frac{2(50)}{0.5(1)}=200 psi\)

 

\(V=\frac{\pi D N}{12}=\frac{\pi(0.5) 300}{12}=39.3 ft / min <100 ft / min\) (OK)

 

Note that PV is not a function of D, and since we did not change L, PV will remain the same:

 

\(P V=200(39.3)=7860 psi \cdot ft / min <46700 psi \cdot ft / min\) (OK)

 

From Table 12–9, \(f_{1}=1.34, L \geq 1.34(0.48)=0.643\) in. There are many \(\frac{3}{4}\) -in bushings to select from. The smallest diameter in Table 12–13 is \(D=\frac{1}{2}\) in. This gives an L/D ratio of 1.5, which is acceptable according to Eq. (12–33).

 

\(0.5 \leq L / D \leq 2\) (12–33)

 

Trial 3: D = 0.5 in, L = 0.75 in. From trial 2, V = 39.3 ft/min does not change.

 

\(P_{\max }=\frac{4}{\pi} \frac{n_{d} F}{D L}=\frac{4}{\pi} \frac{2(50)}{0.5(0.75)}=340 psi <3560 psi\) (OK)

 

\(P=\frac{n_{d} F}{D I}=\frac{2(50)}{05(075)}=267 psi\)

 

\(P V=267(39.3)=10490 psi \cdot ft / min <46700 psi \cdot ft / min\) (OK)

 

Select any of the bushings from the trials, where the optimum, from trial 3, is \(D=\frac{1}{2}\) in and \(L=\frac{3}{4}\) in. Other factors may enter in the overall design that make the other bushings more appropriate.
