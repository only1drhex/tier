Guest Question.

(a)A sample of carbon dioxide gas from the atmosphere fills a vessel of volume 200.0 \(cm^{3}\) to a pressure of \(2.00 \times 10^{4}\) Pa (1 Pa = 1 \(N/m^{2}\), about \(10^{-5}\) atm) at a temperature of 295 K. Assuming that all of the \(^{14}C\) beta decays were counted, how many counts would be accumulated in one week? (b) An old sample of wood is burned, and the resulting carbon dioxide is placed in an identical vessel at the same pressure and temperature. After one week, 1420 counts have been accumulated. What is the age of the sample?

Admin
Solution

(a) We first find the number of atoms present in the vessel, using the ideal gas law:

\(N = \frac{PV}{kT} = \frac{(2.00 \times 10^{4} N/m^{2})(2.00 \times 10^{-4} m^{3})}{ (1.38 \times 10^{-23} J/K)(295 K)}\)
\(= 9.82 \times 10^{20} atoms \)

If the fraction of \(^{14}C\) atoms is \(10^{-12}\), there are \(9.82 \times 10^{8}\) atoms of \(^{14}C\) present. The activity is

\( a = \lambda N = \frac{0.693}{(5730 y)(3.16 \times 10^{7} s/y)} 9.82 \times 10^{8}\)
\( = 3.76 \times 10^{3} decays/s\)

In one week the number of decays is 2280.
(b) An identical sample that gives only 1420 counts must be old enough for only 1420/2280 of its original activity to remain. With 1420 = \(2280e^{−λt}\) , we have

\( t = \frac{1}{\lambda } \ln \left(\frac{2280}{1420} \right) = \frac{5730 y}{0.693} \ln \left(\frac{2280}{1420} \right) = 3920 y \)
