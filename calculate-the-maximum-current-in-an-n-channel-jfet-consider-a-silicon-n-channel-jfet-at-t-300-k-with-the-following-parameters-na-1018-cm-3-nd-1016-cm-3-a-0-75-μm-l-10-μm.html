Guest Question.

Calculate the maximum current in an n-channel JFET.

Consider a silicon n-channel JFET at \(T=300 \mathrm{~K}\) with the following parameters: \(N_{a}=\) \(10^{18} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}, N_{d}=10^{16} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}, a=0.75 \mu \mathrm{m}, L=10 \mu \mathrm{m}, W=30 \mu \mathrm{m}\), and \(\mu_{n}=1000 \mathrm{~cm}^{2} / \mathrm{V}-\mathrm{s}\).

Admin
Solution

The pinchoff current from Equation (13.28) becomes

\(\begin{aligned}I_{P 1} \equiv & \frac{\mu_{n}\left(e N_{d}\right)^{2} W a^{3}}{6 \epsilon_{s} L} \\ \end{aligned}\) (13.28)

\(\begin{aligned}I_{P 1} &=\frac{(1000)\left[\left(1.6 \times 10^{-19}\right)\left(10^{16}\right)\right]^{2}\left(30 \times 10^{-4}\right)\left(0.75 \times 10^{-4}\right)^{3}}{6(11.7)\left(8.85 \times 10^{-14}\right)\left(10 \times 10^{-4}\right)}=0.522 \mathrm{~mA}\end{aligned}\)

We also have from that \(V_{b i}=0.814 \mathrm{~V}\) and \(V_{p 0}=4.35 \mathrm{~V}\). The maximum current occurs when \(V_{G S}=0\), so from Equation (13.35)

\(\begin{array}{l}I_{D 1}=I_{D 1}(\mathrm{sat})=I_{P 1}\left\{1-3\left(\frac{V_{b i}-V_{G S}}{V_{p 0}}\right)\left[1-\frac{2}{3} \sqrt{\frac{V_{b i}-V_{G S}}{V_{p 0}}}\right]\right\} \\ \end{array}\) (13.35)

\(\begin{array}{l}I_{D 1}(\max )=I_{P 1}\left\{1-3\left(\frac{V_{b i}}{V_{p 0}}\right)\left[1-\frac{2}{3} \sqrt{\frac{V_{b i}}{V_{p 0}}}\right]\right\}\end{array}\) (13.36)

or

\(I_{D 1}(\max )=(0.522)\left\{1-3\left(\frac{0.814}{4.35}\right)\left[1-\frac{2}{3} \sqrt{\frac{0.814}{4.35}}\right]\right\}=0.313 \mathrm{~mA}\)

Comment

The maximum current through the JFET is less than the pinchoff current \(I_{P 1}\).
