Solution

From Eq. (20–10),

 

\(s_{x}=\sqrt{\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{k} f_{i} x_{i}^{2}-\left[\left(\sum_{i=1}^{k} f_{i} x_{i}\right)^{2} / N\right]}{N-1}}=\sqrt{\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{k} f_{i} x_{i}^{2}-N \bar{x}^{2}}{N-1}}\) (20–10)

 

\(s_{x}=\sqrt{\frac{4054864-63625^{2} / 1000}{1000-1}}=2.594245=2.594 kpsi\)

 

\(C_{x}=\frac{s_{x}}{\bar{x}}=\frac{2.594245}{63.625}=0.040773=0.0408\)

 

From Eq. (20–14) the probability density function for a normal distribution with a mean of 63.625 and a standard deviation of 2.594 245 is

 

\(f(x)=\frac{1}{\hat{\sigma}_{x} \sqrt{2 \pi}} \exp \left[-\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{x-\mu_{x}}{\hat{\sigma}_{x}}\right)^{2}\right]\) (20–14)

 

\(f(x)=\frac{1}{2.594245 \sqrt{2 \pi}} \exp \left[-\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{x-63.625}{2.594245}\right)^{2}\right]\)

 

For example, f (63.625) = 0.1538. The probability density f (x) is evaluated at class midpoints to form the column of normal density in Table 20–5.
