Guest Question.

A \(2.00-\mathrm{cm}^{3}\) sample of aluminum (symbolized \(\mathrm{Al}\) ) is found to weigh \(5.40 \mathrm{~g}\). Calculate the density of \(\mathrm{Al}\) in units of \(\mathrm{g} / \mathrm{cm}^{3}\) and \(\mathrm{g} / \mathrm{mL}\).

Admin
Solution

The density expression is:
\(d=\frac{m}{V}\), in which mass is usually in \(\mathrm{g}\), and volume is in either \(\mathrm{mL}\) or \(\mathrm{cm}^{3}\).
Substituting the information given in the problem,

\(d=\frac{5.40 \mathrm{~g}}{2.00 \mathrm{~cm}^{3}}=2.70 \mathrm{~g} / \mathrm{cm}^{3}\)

According to Table 1.2,

mega M 10\(^{6}\) 1,000,000.00 1Mx=10\(^{6}\)x
kilo k 10\(^{3}\) 1000 1kx=10\(^{3}\)x
deka da 10\(^{1}\) 10 1dax=10\(^{1}\)x
deci d 10\(^{-1}\) 0.1 1 dx=10\(^{-1}\)x
centi c 10\(^{-2}\) 0.01 1cx=10\(^{-2}\)x
milli m 10\(^{-3}\) 0.001 1 mx=10\(^{-3}\)x
micro μ 10\(^{-6}\) 0.000001 1μx=10\(^{-6}\)x
nano n 10\(^{-9}\) 0.000000001 1nx=10\(^{-9}\)x

\(1 \mathrm{~mL}=1 \mathrm{~cm}^{3}\). Therefore, we can use this relationship as a conversion factor to obtain the answer in \(\mathrm{g} / \mathrm{mL}\). The \(\mathrm{cm}^{3}\) unit is placed in the numerator so that it cancels, and the \(\mathrm{mL}\) unit is placed in the denominator because it will provide the correct unit for the product.

\(\frac{2.70 \mathrm{~g}}{1 \cancel{~cm}^{3}} \times \frac{1 \cancel{~cm}^{3}}{1 \mathrm{~mL}}=2.70 \mathrm{~g} / \mathrm{mL}\)

Initial Data Result \(\times\) Conversion Factor \(=\) Desired Result
