Guest Question.

The truss seen in Figure P17.65/66/67 is subjected to concentrated loads of P = 160 kN and 2P = 320 kN. All members are made of steel [E = 200 GPa], and each has a cross-sectional area of A = 3,500 \(mm^{2}\) . Use the virtualwork method to determine:
(a) the horizontal displacement of joint A.
(b) the vertical displacement of joint A.

Admin
Solution

Real Truss Reaction Forces

\(B_{x}=0.000 kN \quad B_{y}=640.000 kN \uparrow \quad F_{y}=640.000 kN \uparrow\)

 

(a) Horizontal Displacement of Joint A
Virtual Load Assume that a 1-kN virtual load is applied in the –x direction at A. The reaction forces for the virtual load are:

\(B_{x}=1.000 kN \rightarrow \quad B_{y}=0.375 kN \uparrow \quad F_{y}=0.375 kN \downarrow\)

 

\(\begin{array}{ccccc}\hline \text { Member } & \begin{array}{c}L \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}F \\( kN )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}f \\( kN )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}f(F L) \\\left( kN ^{2}- mm \right)\end{array} \\\hline A B & 10,000 & -266.67 & 0.0000 & -2,666,666.70 \\A C & 8,000 & 213.33 & 1.0000 & 1,706,666.64 \\B C & 6,000 & -480.00 & -0.3750 & -2,880,000.00 \\B D & 8,000 & -213.33 & -1.0000 & -1,706,666.64 \\C D & 10,000 & 266.67 & 0.6250 & 2,666,666.70 \\C E & 8,000 & 0.00 & 0.5000 & 0.00 \\ D E & 6,000 & -320.00 & 0.0000 & -1,920,000.00 \\D F & 8,000 & -213.33 & 0.0000 & -1,706,666.64 \\D G & 10,000 & 266.67 & -0.6250 & 2,666,666.70 \\E G & 8,000 & 0.00 & 0.5000 & 0.00 \\F G & 6,000 & -480.00 & 0.3750 & -2,880,000.00 \\F H & 10,000 & -266.67 & 0.0000 & -2,666,666.70 \\G H & 8,000 & 213.33 & 0.0000 & 1,706,666.64 \\\hline && & \sum f(F L)= & 3,413,333.28 \\\hline\end{array}\)

 

Equation (17.30) can now be applied.

\(1 \cdot \Delta=\sum_{j} f_{j}\left(\frac{F_{j} L_{j}}{A_{j} E_{j}}\right)=\frac{1}{A E} \sum_{j} f_{j}\left(F_{j} L_{j}\right)\)

From the tabulated results:

\(\begin{aligned}(1 kN ) \cdot \Delta_{A} &=\frac{\left(3,413,333.28 kN ^{2}- mm \right)(1,000 N / kN )}{\left(3,500 mm ^{2}\right)\left(200,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)} \\\Delta_{A} &=4.88 mm \leftarrow\end{aligned}\)

Since the virtual load was applied in the –x direction at A, the positive value of the result confirms that joint A does displace to the left.

 

(b) Vertical Displacement of Joint A
Virtual Load Assume that a 1-kN virtual load is applied in the –y direction at A. The reaction forces for the virtual load are:

\(\begin{array}{cc}B_{x}=0.00 kN & B_{y}=1.50 kN \uparrow & F_{y}=0.50 kN \downarrow\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{ccccc}\hline \text { Member } & \begin{array}{c}L \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}F \\( kN )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}f \\( kN )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}f(F L) \\\left( kN ^{2}- mm \right)\end{array} \\\hline A B & 10,000 & -266.67 & -1.6667 & 4,444,453.39 \\A C & 8,000 & 213.33 & 1.3333 & 2,275,549.83 \\B C & 6,000 & -480.00 & -0.5000 & 1,440,000.00 \\B D & 8,000 & -213.33 & -1.3333 & 2,275,549.83 \\C D & 10,000 & 266.67 & 0.8333 & 2,222,213.36 \\C E & 8,000 & 0.00 & 0.6667 & 0.00 \\D E & 6,000 & -320.00 & 0.0000 & 0.00 \\D F & 8,000 & -213.33 & 0.0000 & 0.00 \\D G & 10,000 & 266.67 & -0.8333 & -2,222,213.36 \\E G & 8,000 & 0.00 & 0.6667 & 0.00 \\F G & 6,000 & -480.00 & 0.5000 & -1,440,000.00 \\F H & 10,000 & -266.67 & 0.0000 & 0.00 \\G H & 8,000 & 213.33 & 0.0000 & 0.00 \\\hline & & & \sum f(F L)= & 8,995,553.05 \\\hline\end{array}\)

 

Equation (17.30) can now be applied.

\(1 \cdot \Delta=\sum_{j} f_{j}\left(\frac{F_{j} L_{j}}{A_{j} E_{j}}\right)=\frac{1}{A E} \sum_{j} f_{j}\left(F_{j} L_{j}\right)\)

From the tabulated results:

\(\begin{aligned}(1 kN ) \cdot \Delta_{A} &=\frac{\left(8,995,553.05 kN ^{2}- mm \right)(1,000 N / kN )}{\left(3,500 mm ^{2}\right)\left(200,000 N / mm ^{2}\right)} \\\Delta_{A} &=12.85 mm \downarrow\end{aligned}\)

Since the virtual load was applied in the –y direction at A, the positive value of the result confirms that joint A does displace downward.
