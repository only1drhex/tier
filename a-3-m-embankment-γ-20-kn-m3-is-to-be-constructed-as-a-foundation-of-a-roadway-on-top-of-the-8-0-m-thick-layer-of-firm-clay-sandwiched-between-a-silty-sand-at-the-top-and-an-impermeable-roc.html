Guest Question.

A \(3 \mathrm{~m}\) embankment \(\left(\gamma=20 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\right)\) is to be constructed as a foundation of a roadway, on top of the \(8.0 \mathrm{~m}\) thick layer of firm clay, sandwiched between a silty sand at the top and an impermeable rock layer at the bottom.

To accelerate drainage, it is proposed to install a series of prefabricated vertical drains (PVDs)in sufficient number with a vacuum preloading of \(60 \mathrm{kPa}\) so that the time needed for \(95 \%\) overall consolidation to occur is reduced to just 6 months. The PVDs (100 mm wide band drains) to be installed have an equivalent diameter of \(50 \mathrm{~mm}\) (see Eq. 15.10) and will be inserted throughout the clay layer using the triangular pattern. The coefficients of horizontal and vertical consolidation are \(\cdot c_{h}=0.288 \mathrm{~m}^{2} / \mathrm{month}, c_{v}=0.187 \mathrm{~m}^{2} / \mathrm{month}\)

\(r_{w}=\frac{a+b}{4}\) (15.10)

A preliminary drain installation trial to evaluate the effect of smear caused by the mandrel during the installation of the PVDs revealed that the ratio of horizontal permeability in the smear zone \(\left(k_{h} / k_{s}\right)\) is 5 , whereas the diameter of the smear zone smear zone \(\left(D_{s}\right)\) is found to be \(0.15 \mathrm{~m}\). Calculate the spacing between two consecutive drains needed to achieve the stated objective.

Admin
Solution

Based on , the following parameters can be obtained:

\(\begin{aligned}&\xi=44 \\&\alpha=0.463 \\&\beta=-0.649\end{aligned}\)

The average excess pore pressure due to vertical loading \(\left(u^{*}\right)\) can be computed using Fig.15.12 and considering \(T_{v}=0.02\) (from the calculation below).

\(T_{v}=\frac{C_{v} t}{H^{2}}=\frac{0.187 \times 6}{8^{2}}=0.02\)

Then, \(u^{*}=0.83\) (Fig. 15.12).

For vacuum pressure and surcharge preloading, the general equation for \(\gamma\) is modified as follows:

\(\gamma=-\frac{8 T_{h}^{\prime}}{\ln \left(\frac{1-U_{t, \text { vac }}}{u^{*}}\right)}\)

where \(U_{t, v a c}\) is the equivalent degree of consolidation incorporating the combined effect of vacuum pressure \(\left(u_{\text {vac }}\right)\) and the vertical surcharge load \(\left(u_{0}\right)\).

The vertical surcharge load can be computed considering the unit weight of the embankment fill as follows:

\(u_{0}=20 \times 3=60 \mathrm{kPa}\)

Then,

\(U_{t, \text { vac }}=\frac{U_{t} \times u_{0}}{u_{0}+\left|u_{\text {vac }}\right|}=\frac{0.95 \times 60}{60+60}=0.48\)

The numerator of the previous equation assumes that the maximum pore pressure for this case will be obtained by multiplying the expected degree of consolidation and maximum surcharge load. Furthermore, in this approach, it is assumed the surcharge pressure is applied all at once, which does not reflect construction practice where surcharge load is increased incrementally. Then,

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{h}^{\prime}=\frac{c_{h} t}{D_{w}{ }^{2}}=\frac{0.288 \times 6}{(0.05)^{2}}=691.2 \\ &\gamma=-\frac{8 T_{h}^{\prime}}{\ln \left(\frac{1-U_{t, v a c}}{u^{*}}\right)}=-\frac{8 \times 691.2}{\ln \left(\frac{1-0.48}{0.83}\right)}=11825.57\end{aligned}\)

and

\(n=\exp (\alpha \ln \gamma+\beta)=\exp (0.463 \ln (11825.57)-0.649)=40\)

Then, \(n=\frac{D_{e}}{D_{w}}\), or \(D_{e}=40 \times 0.05=2 \mathrm{~m}\).

For a triangular pattern, \(D_{e}=1.05 S\), thus drain spacing \((S)\) is:

\(\mathrm{S}=2 / 1.05=1.92 \approx 1.9 \mathrm{~m}\)

The value for drain spacing obtained considering vacuum pressure is larger compared with the case considering surcharge only, which clarifies the role of suction effecting consolidation.

In practical situations, considering possible vacuum loss in the ground, the drain spacing can then be adjusted to \(1.5 \mathrm{~m}\).
