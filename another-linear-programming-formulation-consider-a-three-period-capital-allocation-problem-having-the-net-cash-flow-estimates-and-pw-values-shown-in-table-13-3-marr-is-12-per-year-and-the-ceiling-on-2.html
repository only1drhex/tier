Solution

First, the net PW of each investment opportunity at \(12 \%\) per year is calculated (Table 13-3). The objective function then becomes

\(\begin{aligned}\text { Maximize } P W=& 135.3 X_{A 1}+146.0 X_{A 2}+119.3 X_{A 3}+164.1 X_{B 1} \\&+151.9 X_{B 2}+8.1 X_{C 1}-13.1 X_{C 2}+2.3 X_{C 3} .\end{aligned}\)

The budget constraints are the following:

Investment funds constraint:

\(\begin{aligned}&225 X_{A 1}+290 X_{A 2}+370 X_{A 3}+600 X_{B 1}+1,200 X_{B 2} \\&+160 X_{C 1}+200 X_{C 2}+225 X_{C 3} \leq 1,200\end{aligned}\)

First year’s operating cost constraint:

\(\begin{aligned}&60 X_{A 1}+180 X_{A 2}+290 X_{A 3}+100 X_{B 1}+250 X_{B 2} \\&+80 X_{C 1}+65 X_{C 2}+100 X_{C 3} \leq 400\end{aligned}\)

Interrelationships among the investment opportunities give rise to these constraints on the problem:

\(X_{A 1}+X_{A 2}+X_{A 3} \quad \leq 1 \quad A 1, A 2, A 3 \text { are mutually exclusive }\)

\(\left.\begin{array}{ll}X_{B 1} & \leq 1 \\ X_{B 2} & \leq 1\end{array}\right\}\quad B 1, B 2\) are independent

\(X_{C 1}+X_{C 2}+X_{C 3} \leq X_{A 1}+X_{A 2}\quad \) accounts for dependence of C1, C2, C3 (which are mutually exclusive) on \(A 1\) or \(A 2\)

Finally, if all decision variables are required to be either zero (not in the optimal solution) or one (included in the optimal solution), the last constraint on the problem would be written

\(X_{j}=0,1 \quad \text { for } j=A 1, A 2, A 3, B 1, B 2, C 1, C 2, C 3 \text {. }\)

As can be seen in , a fairly simple problem such as this would require a large amount of time to solve by listing and evaluating all mutually exclusive combinations. Consequently, it is recommended that a suitable computer program be used to obtain solutions for all but the most simple capital allocation problems.
