Guest Question.

The following interpolation formula is suggested as a displacement function for deriving the stiffness matrix of a plane stress rectangular element of uniform thickness t shown in Fig. P.17.21

\(u=\frac{1}{4 a b}\left[(a-x)(b-y) u_{1}+(a+x)(b-y) u_{2}+(a+x)(b+y) u_{3}+(a-x)(b+y) u_{4}\right]\)

Form the strain matrix and obtain the stiffness coefficients \(K_{11} \text { and } K_{12}\) in terms of the material constants c, d and e where, in the elasticity matrix [D], \(D_{11}=D_{22}=c, D_{12}=d, D_{33}=e\) and \(D_{13}=D_{23}=0\).

Ans. \(K_{11=} t(4 c+e) / 6, K_{12}=t(d+e) / 4\)

Admin
Solution

For a=1 and b=2

\(u=\frac{1}{8}\left[(1-x)(2-y) u_{1}+(1+x)(2-y) u_{2}+(1+x)(2+y) u_{3}+(1-x)(2+y) u_{4}\right]\)

The expression for ν is identical in format to the above with v replacing u.
Then

\(\frac{\partial u}{\partial x}=\frac{1}{8}\left[-(2-y) u_{1}+(2-y) u_{2}+(2+y) u_{3}-(2+y) u_{4}\right]\)

 

\(\frac{\partial \nu}{\partial y}=\frac{1}{8}\left[-(1-x) \nu_{1}-(1+x) \nu_{2}+(1+x) \nu_{3}+(1-x) \nu_{4}\right]\)

 

\(\frac{\partial u}{\partial y}+\frac{\partial \nu}{\partial x}=\frac{1}{8}\left[-(1-x) u_{1}-(2-y) \nu_{1}-(1+x) u_{2}+(2-y) \nu_{2}\right.\)

 

\(\left.+(1+x) u_{3}+(2+y) v_{3}+(1-x) u_{4}-(2+y) v_{4}\right]\)

The strain matrix [B] follows and is (see Eq. (ix) of Ex. 17.5)

\([B]\left\{\delta^{e}\right\}=\left[\begin{array}{c}\frac{\partial w}{\partial x} \\\frac{\partial v}{\partial y} \\\frac{\partial w}{\partial y}+\frac{\partial v}{\partial x}\end{array}\right]=\frac{1}{4}\left[\begin{array}{rrrrrr}-1 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\0 & -1 & 0 & -1 & 0 & 2 \\-1 & -1 & -1 & 1 & 2 & 0\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}w_{1} \\v_{1} \\w_{2} \\v_{2} \\w_{3} \\v_{3}\end{array}\right\}\) Eq. (ix) of Ex. 17.5

\((1/8)\left[\begin{matrix} -(2-y) & 0 & (2-y) & 0 & (2+y) & 0 & -(2+y) & 0 \\ 0 & -(1-x) & 0 & -(1+x) & 0 & (1+x) & 0 & (1-x) \\ -(1-x) & -(2-y) & -(1+x) & (2-y) & (1+x) & (2+y) & (1-x) & -(2+y) \end{matrix} \right]\)

Now

\([D]=\left[\begin{array}{lll}c & d & 0 \\d & c & 0 \\0 & 0 & e\end{array}\right]\)

so that

\([D][B]=(1 / 8)\left[\begin{array}{cccccccc}-c(2-y) & -d(1-x) &c(2-y) & -d(1+x) & c(2+y) & d(1+x) & -c(2+y) & d(1-x) \\-d(2-y) & -c(1-x) & d(2-y) & -c(1+x) & d(2+y) & c(1+x) & -d(2+y) & c(1-x) \\-e(1-x) & -e(2-y) & -e(1+x) & e(2-y) & e(1+x) & e(2+y) & e(1-x) & -e(2+y)\end{array}\right]\)

Then

\([B]^{ T }[D][B]=(1 / 64)\left[\begin{array}{ccc}-(2-y) & 0 & -(1-x) \\0 & -(1-x) & -(2-y) \\\vdots & \vdots & \vdots\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{ccc}-c(2-y) & -d(1-x) & \cdots \\-d(2-y) & -c(1-x) & \cdots \\-e(1-x) & -e(2-y) & \cdots\end{array}\right]\)

Then

\(K_{11}=\frac{t}{64} \int_{-2}^{2} \int_{-1}^{1}\left[c(2-y)^{2}+e(1-x)^{2}\right] d x d y\)

so that

\(K_{11}=\frac{t}{6}(4 c+e)\)

Also

\(K_{12}=\frac{t}{64} \int_{-2}^{2} \int_{-1}^{1}[d(2-y)(1-x)+e(1-x)(2-y)] d x d y\)

which gives

\(K_{12}=\frac{t}{4}(d+e)\)
