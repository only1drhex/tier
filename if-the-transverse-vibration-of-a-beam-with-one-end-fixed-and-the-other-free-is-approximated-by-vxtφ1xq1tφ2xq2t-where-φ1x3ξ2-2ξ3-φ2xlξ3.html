Guest Question.

If the transverse vibration of a beam with one end fixed and the other free is approximated by

\(v(x,t)= \bar{\phi}_{1}(x)q_{1}(t) + \bar{\phi}_{2}(x)q_{2}(t) \)

where

\( \bar{\phi}_{1}(x)=3\xi ^{2}-2\xi ^{3}, \bar{\phi}^{2}(x)=l\left(\xi ^{3}-\xi ^{2}\right) \)

in which l is the length of the beam and \(\xi=x/ l\), obtain the matrix equation of motion of this system.

Admin
Solution

The displacement and velocity of the beam can be expressed as

\(v(x,t)=\left[\bar{\phi}_{1}(x) \bar{\phi}_{2}(x)\right] \left[\begin{matrix} q_{1}(t) \\ q_{2}(t) \end{matrix} \right] \)

\(\dot{v} (x,t)=\left[\bar{\phi}_{1}(x) \bar{\phi}_{2}(x)\right] \left[\begin{matrix} \dot{q}_{1}(t) \\\dot{q}_{2}(t) \end{matrix} \right] \)

Also

\(v^{\prime \prime }(x,t)=\left[\bar{\phi}^{\prime \prime }_{1}(x) \bar{\phi}^{\prime \prime }_{2}(x)\right] \left[\begin{matrix} q_{1}(t) \\ q_{2}(t) \end{matrix} \right] \)

\(=\left[\frac{6}{l^{2}}(1-2\xi) \frac{2}{l}(3\xi-1)\right] \left[\begin{matrix} q_{1}(t) \\ q_{2}(t) \end{matrix} \right] \)

Therefore, the kinetic energy and strain energy are given by

\(T=\frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{l}{\rho A \dot{v}^{2}dx} = \frac{1}{2}\dot{q}^{T}M\dot{q}, U=\frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{l}{EIv^{\prime \prime 2}dx} = \frac{1}{2}q^{T}K q\)

where \(q=\left[q_{1} q_{2}\right]^{T}\) and M and K are the mass and stiffness matrices defined as

\(M=\int_{0}^{l}{\rho A \left[\begin{matrix} \bar{\phi }_{1} \\ \bar{\phi }_{2} \end{matrix} \right] \left[\bar{\phi }_{1} \bar{\phi }_{2}\right] dx} = \int_{0}^{l}{\rho A\begin{bmatrix} \bar{\phi }_{1}^{2} & \bar{\phi }_{1}\bar{\phi }_{2} \\ \bar{\phi }_{2}\bar{\phi }_{1} & \bar{\phi }_{2}^{2} \end{bmatrix}dx}\)

\(= \rho Al\begin{bmatrix} 13/ 35 & -11l/210 \\ -11l/210 & l^{2}/ 105 \end{bmatrix}\)

\(K=\int_{0}^{l}{EI \left[\begin{matrix} \bar{\phi }_{1}^{\prime \prime } \\ \bar{\phi }_{2}^{\prime \prime } \end{matrix} \right] \left[\bar{\phi }_{1}^{\prime \prime } \bar{\phi }_{2}^{\prime \prime }\right] dx} = \int_{0}^{l}{EI\begin{bmatrix} \bar{\phi }_{1}^{\prime \prime 2} & \bar{\phi }_{1}^{\prime \prime }{\phi }_{2}^{\prime \prime } \\ \bar{\phi }_{2}^{\prime \prime }{\phi }_{1}^{\prime \prime } & \bar{\phi }_{2}^{\prime \prime 2} \end{bmatrix}dx}\)

\(= \frac{EI}{l} \begin{bmatrix} 12/ l^{2} & -6/l \\ -6/l & 4 \end{bmatrix}\)

The virtual work of the external force is

\(\delta W=\int_{0}^{l}{F(x,t)\delta vdx} =\int_{0}^{l}{F(x,t)\left[\bar{\phi }_{1} \bar{\phi }_{2}\right]\left[\begin{matrix} \delta q_{1} \\ \delta q_{2} \end{matrix} \right] dx} = \left[Q_{1} Q_{2}\right]\left[\begin{matrix} \delta q_{1} \\ \delta q_{2} \end{matrix} \right] \)

Where

\(Q_{1}=\int_{0}^{l}{F(x,t)\bar{\phi }_{1}dx}\), \(Q_{2}=\int_{0}^{l}{F(x,t)\bar{\phi }_{2}dx}\)

Therefore, the matrix equation for the equivalent two degree of freedom system can be written as

\(\rho Al\begin{bmatrix} 13/ 35 & -11l/210 \\ -11l/210 & l^{2}/ 105 \end{bmatrix}\left[\begin{matrix} \ddot{q}_{1} \\\ddot{q}_{2} \end{matrix} \right] + \frac{EI}{l} \begin{bmatrix} 12/ l^{2} & -6/l \\ -6/l & 4 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} q_{1} \\ q_{2} \end{matrix} \right] = \left[\begin{matrix} Q_{1} \\ Q_{2} \end{matrix} \right] \)
