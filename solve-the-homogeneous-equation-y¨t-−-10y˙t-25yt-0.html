Guest Question.

Solve the homogeneous equation

 

\(\ddot{y}(t)-10 \dot{y}(t)+25 y(t)=0\)
Admin
Solution

The characteristic equation is found to be \(r^{2}\) – \(10 r+25=0\) with two equal roots \(r_{1,2}=5\) The solution is then

 

\(y(t)=c_{1} e^{5 t}+c_{2} t e^{5 t}\)

 

which is unbounded since \(y(t)\) increases without bound. (No initial conditions were provided, meaning the specific values of \(c_{1}\) and \(c_{2}\) cannot be found. However, since the solution is unbounded, it would not be physically meaningful.)
