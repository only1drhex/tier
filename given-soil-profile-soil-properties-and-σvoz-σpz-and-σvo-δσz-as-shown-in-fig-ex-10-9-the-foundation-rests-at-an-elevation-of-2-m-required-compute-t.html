Guest Question.

Given:

Soil profile, soil properties, and \(\sigma_{v o}^{\prime}(z), \sigma_{p}^{\prime}(z), \text { and } \sigma_{v o}^{\prime}+\Delta \sigma(z)\), as shown in Fig. Ex. 10.9. The foundation rests at an elevation of -2 m.

Required:

Compute the settlement.

Admin
Solution

Figure Ex. 10.9 shows that the soil is overconsolidated and that down to a depth of 10 m, the addition of \(\Delta \sigma\) leads to a final vertical effective stress that exceeds the preconsolidation pressure. Further, the groundwater table is at the top of the compressible layer and the external loading is shown as the resulting \(\Delta \sigma(z)\) curve.

Break the compressible layer up into seven layers; the top layer has a thickness of 1 m and the rest of the layers are 2 m thick. Prepare a table to facilitate computations. For each layer, record the stresses at the mid depth of each layer to represent the “average” conditions. (This problem is similar to the compound compressibility Case 3 in Sec. 10.5.) We have filled in the top portion of Table Ex. 10.9 with the layers above a depth of 10 m as they all have terms using \(C_{c}\). At the average depth of 2.5 m for the first layer, determine from the graph \(\sigma_{v o}^{\prime}(=48 kPa ), \sigma_{v o}^{\prime}+\Delta \sigma_{v}(=217 kPa )\) and \(\sigma_{p}^{\prime}(=117 kPa )\). These numbers are used in Columns (6) \(\left[C_{r} \log \left(\sigma_{p}^{\prime} / \sigma_{v v}^{\prime}\right)\right]\) and (7) to determine the recompression and virgin compression portions of the change in void ratio, respectively, which are then summed and divided by \(\left(1+e_{o}\right)\) in Column (8) to get the vertical strain. \(s_{c}=C_{r} \frac{H_{o}}{1+e_{o}}\left(\log \sigma_{p}^{\prime}-\log \sigma_{v o}^{\prime}\right)+C_{c} \frac{H_{o}}{1+e_{o}}\left(\log \sigma_{f}^{\prime}-\log \sigma_{p}^{\prime}\right)\) (8.18a)

 

For the layers below a depth of 10 m in this problem, the influence of the foundation stress is so small that the final effective stress, \(\sigma_{v o}^{\prime}+\Delta \sigma_{v}\), does not reach the preconsolidation pressure, so that all settlement is from recompression strains only. In this case, rather than using the ratio \(C_{r} \log \left(\sigma_{p}^{\prime} / \sigma_{v o}^{\prime}\right)\), we use \(C_{r} \log \left(\sigma_{v f}^{\prime} / \sigma_{v o}^{\prime}\right)\) to compute the change in void ratio, and that is the only contribution to settlement. When all layer contributions to settlement are summed, we obtain the total settlement of 0.43 m.
