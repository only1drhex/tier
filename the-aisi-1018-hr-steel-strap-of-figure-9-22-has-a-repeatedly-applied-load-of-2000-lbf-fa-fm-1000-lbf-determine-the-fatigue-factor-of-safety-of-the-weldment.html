Guest Question.

The AISI 1018 HR steel strap of Figure 9–22 has a repeatedly applied load of 2000 lbf (\(F_{a} = F_{m} = 1000 lbf)\). Determine the fatigue factor of safety of the weldment

Admin
Solution

From Table 6–2, \(k_{a} = 12.7(58)^{−0.758} = 0.58\). From case 2 of Table 9–2
\(A = 1.414(0.375)(2) = 1.061 in^{2}\)
For uniform shear stress on the throat \(k_{b} = 1\).
Equation (6–25)\(k_{c} = \begin{cases} 1 &bending \\ 0.85 & axial\\ 0.59&torsion \end{cases} \),

: \(k_{c} = 0.59\)
Equations (6–10)\(\begin{cases} 0.5S_{ut} & S_{ut} ≤ 200 kpsi (1400 MPa) \\ 100 kpsi & S_{ut} > 200 kpsi\\ 700 MPa &S_{ut} > 1400 MPa \end{cases} \) and (6–17),\(S_{e} = k_{a}k_{b}k_{c}k_{d} k_{e}S^{′}_{e} \): \(S_{se}\) = (0.58)(0.59)(0.5)(58) = 9.92 kpsi

From Table 9–5,\( K_{fs} = 2\). Only primary shear is present:

\( τ^{′}_{a} = τ^{′}_{m} =\frac{K_{fs}F_{a}}{A}= \frac{2(1000)}{1.061}= 1885 psi \)

Estimate the shear modulus of rupture with Equation (6–59) \(\sigma _{ar}=\frac{\sigma _{a}}{1-{\sigma _{m}}/{S_{ut}}} \),
\(S_{su} = 0.67 S_{ut} = 0.67 (58) = 38.9 kpsi\)
Choosing the Goodman fatigue failure criterion of Equation (6–41) \(n_{f}=(\frac{\sigma _{a}}{S_{e}} +\frac{\sigma _{m}}{S_{ut}} )^{-1} \sigma _{m}\geq 0\) , adapted for shear, gives

\( n_{f}= \left(\frac{τ^{′}_{a}}{S_{se}} +\frac{τ^{′}_{m}}{S_{su}} \right)^{-1}= (\frac{1.885}{9.92}+\frac{1.885}{38.9})^{-1}=4.2 \)
