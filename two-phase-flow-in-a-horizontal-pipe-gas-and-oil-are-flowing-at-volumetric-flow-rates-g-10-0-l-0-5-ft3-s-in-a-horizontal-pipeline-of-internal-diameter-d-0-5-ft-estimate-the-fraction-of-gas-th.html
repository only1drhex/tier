Two-Phase Flow in a Horizontal Pipe

Gas and oil are flowing at volumetric flow rates \(G=10.0, L=0.5 \mathrm{ft}^{3} / \mathrm{s}\) in a horizontal pipeline of internal diameter \(D=0.5 \mathrm{ft}\). Estimate the fraction of gas, the pressure gradient, and the mean velocities of each phase. The physical properties are \(\rho_{g}=0.15, \rho_{l}=50.0 \mathrm{lb}_{\mathrm{m}} / \mathrm{ft}^{3}, \mu_{g}=0.025, \mu_{l}=20.0 \mathrm{lb}_{\mathrm{m}} / \mathrm{ft} \mathrm{hr}\); the Fanning friction factor is \(f_{\mathrm{F}}=0.0045\).

Admin
Solution

The following quantities are first calculated:

Cross-sectional area of pipe

\(A=\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}=\frac{\pi \times 0.5^{2}}{4}=0.196 \mathrm{ft}^{2}.\)

Superficial velocities

\(j_{g}=\frac{G}{A}=\frac{10.0}{0.196}=51.0, \quad j_{l}=\frac{L}{A}=\frac{0.5}{0.196}=2.55 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s}.\)

Reynolds numbers

\(\begin{array}{l}\operatorname{Re}_{g}=\frac{0.15 \times 51.0 \times 0.5 \times 3,600}{0.025}=5.51 \times 10^{5}, \\ \operatorname{Re}_{l}=\frac{50.0 \times 2.55 \times 0.5 \times 3,600}{20.0}=11,475 .\end{array}\)

Thus, the flow is turbulent for the liquid and turbulent for the gas, with \(n=4.12\) from Table 10.5.

The quantity \(X^{2}\), being the ratio of the liquid-only and gas-only pressure gradients, is next calculated from Eqns. (10.23), (10.17), and a similar one is calculated for the liquid pressure gradient. The friction factor and the diameters are the same in each case and cancel, giving:

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{g o}=-\frac{2 f_{\mathrm{F}} \rho_{g} j_{g}^{2}}{D}.\) (10.17)

\(X^{2}=\frac{(d p / d z)_{l o}}{(d p / d z)_{g o}}=\frac{\phi_{g}^{2}}{\phi_{l}^{2}}.\) (10.23)

\(X^{2}=\frac{(d p / d z)_{l o}}{(d p / d z)_{g o}}=\frac{\rho_{l} j_{l}^{2}}{\rho_{g} j_{g}^{2}}=\frac{50 \times 2.55^{2}}{0.15 \times 51.0^{2}}=0.833, \quad \text { or } \quad X=0.913.\)

From Eqn. (10.26) (or Table 10.4), the value of \(\phi_{g}\) is:

\(\phi_{g}=\left(1+X^{2 / n}\right)^{n / 2}\) (10.26)

\(\phi_{g}=\left(1+X^{2 / n}\right)^{n / 2}=\left(1+0.913^{2 / 4.12}\right)^{4.12 / 2}=3.99 .\)

From Eqn. (10.25) (or Table 10.4) the fraction of gas is:

\(\varepsilon=\frac{1}{\left(1+0.0904 X^{0.548}\right)^{2.82}}.\) (10.25)

\(\varepsilon=\frac{1}{\left(1+0.0904 X^{0.548}\right)^{2.82}}=\frac{1}{\left(1+0.0904 \times 0.913^{0.548}\right)^{2.82}}=0.792 .\)

The relevant pressure drops are obtained from Eqns. (10.17) and (10.20):

Gas only

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{g o}=-\frac{2 f_{\mathrm{F}} \rho_{g} j_{g}^{2}}{D}.\) (10.17)

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{t p}=\phi_{g}^{2}\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{g o}, \quad\) in which \(\quad \phi_{g}^{2}=\frac{1}{\varepsilon^{5 / 2}}.\) (10.20)

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{g o}=-\frac{2 f_{\mathrm{F}} \rho_{g} j_{g}^{2}}{D}=-\frac{2 \times 0.0045 \times 0.15 \times 51.0^{2}}{0.5 \times 32.2 \times 144}=-1.51 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{psi} / \mathrm{ft}. \)

Two-phase flow

\(\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{t p}=\phi_{g}^{2}\left(\frac{d p}{d z}\right)_{g o}=3.99^{2} \times\left(-1.51 \times 10^{-3}\right)=-0.0241 \mathrm{psi} / \mathrm{ft} .\)

Finally, the individual mean velocities are obtained:

Gas

\(v_{g}=\frac{G}{\varepsilon A}=\frac{10.0}{0.792 \times 0.196}=64.4 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s}.\)

Liquid

\(v_{l}=\frac{L}{(1-\varepsilon) A}=\frac{0.5}{(1-0.792) \times 0.196}=12.3 \mathrm{ft} / \mathrm{s}.\)
