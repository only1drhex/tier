Guest Question.

Given a uniform plane wave in air as

\(E_{i}=40 \cos (\omega t-\beta z)a_{x}+30 \sin (\omega t-\beta z)a_{y}\) V/m

(a) Find \(H_{i}.\)
(b) If the wave encounters a perfectly conducting plate normal to the z-axis at z = 0, find the reflected wave \(E_{r}\) and \(H_{r}.\)

(c) What are the total E and H fields for z ≤0?
(d) Calculate the time-average Poynting vectors for z≤0 and z ≥0.

Admin
Solution

(a) This is similar to the problem in . We may treat the wave as consisting of two waves \(E_{i1}\) and \(E_{i2}\), where

\(E_{i1}=40 \cos (\omega t-\beta z)a_{x}, E_{i2}=30 \sin (\omega t-\beta z)a_{y}\)

At atmospheric pressure, air has \(ε_{r} = 1.0006 \simeq 1.\) Thus air may be regarded as free space.
Let \(H_{i}= H_{i1}+H_{i2}·\)
\(H_{i1}=H_{i1o} \cos(\omega t-\beta z)a_{H_{1}}\)
where
\(H_{i1o}=\frac{E_{i1o}}{\eta _{0}}=\frac{40}{120\pi }=\frac{1}{3\pi }\)
\(a_{H_{1}}=a_{k}\times a_{E}=a_{z}\times a_{x}=a_{y}\)
Hence
\(H_{i1}=\frac{1}{3\pi } \cos(\omega t-\beta z)a_{y}\)
Similarly,
\(H_{i2}=H_{i2o} \sin (\omega t-\beta z)a_{H_{2}}\)
where
\(H_{i2o}=\frac{E_{i2o}}{\eta _{0}}=\frac{30}{120\pi }=\frac{1}{4\pi }\)
\(a_{H_{2}}=a_{k}\times a_{E}=a_{z}\times a_{y}=-a_{x}\)
Hence
\(H_{i2}=-\frac{1}{4\pi } \sin(\omega t-\beta z)a_{x}\)
and
\(H_{i}=H_{i1}+H_{i2}\)
\(=-\frac{1}{4\pi } \sin(\omega t-\beta z)a_{x}+\frac{1}{3\pi } \cos(\omega t-\beta z)a_{y}\) mA/m

This problem can also be solved using Method 2 of .
(b) Since medium 2 is perfectly conducting,
\(\frac{\sigma _{2}}{\omega \varepsilon _{2}}\gg 1\rightarrow \eta _{2} \ll \eta _{1}\)
that is,
\(\Gamma \simeq -1 , \tau =0\)

showing that the incident E and H fields are totally reflected:
\(E_{ro}=\Gamma E_{io}=-E_{io}\)
Hence,
\(E_{r}=-40 \cos(\omega t+\beta z)a_{x}-30 \sin (\omega t+\beta z)a_{y}\) V/m

We can find \(H_{r}\) from \(E_{r}\) just as we did in part (a) of this example or by using Method 2 of , starting with \(H_{i}\). Whichever approach is taken, we obtain
\(H_{r}=\frac{1}{3\pi } \cos(\omega t+\beta z)a_{y}-\frac{1}{4\pi } \sin(\omega t+\beta z)a_{x}\) A/m

( c) The total fields in air
\(E_{1}=E_{i}+E_{r}\) and \(H_{1}=H_{i}+H_{r}\)

can be shown to be standing waves. The total fields in the conductor are
\(E_{2}=E_{t}=0, H_{2}=H_{t}=0\)

(d) For z ≤0,
\(\mathscr{P}_{1ave}=\frac{|E_{1s}|^{2}}{2\eta _{1}}a_{k}=\frac{1}{2\eta _{0}}[E_{io}^{2}a_{z}-E_{ro}^{2}a_{z}]\)
\(=\frac{1}{240\pi }[(40^{2}+30^{2})a_{z}-(40^{2}+30^{2})a_{z}]\)

=0
For \(z\geq 0,\)
\(\mathscr{P}_{2ave}=\frac{|E_{2s}|^{2}}{2\eta _{2}}a_{k}=\frac{E_{to}^{2}}{2\eta _{2}}a_{z}=0\)

because the whole incident power is reflected.
