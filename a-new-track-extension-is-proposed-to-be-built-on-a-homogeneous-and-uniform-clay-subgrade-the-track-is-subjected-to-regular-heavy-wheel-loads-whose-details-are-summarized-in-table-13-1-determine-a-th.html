Guest Question.

A new track extension is proposed to be built on a homogeneous and uniform clay subgrade. The track is subjected to regular heavy wheel loads whose details are summarized in Table 13.1. Determine a thickness of granular layer for track foundation. (Note: this example is similar to the one presented by Li and Selig, 1998a.)

Table 13.1 Input parameters f or determining the thickness of the granular layer based on
the performance of the subgrade (Li and Selig, 1998b).
Design parameters Values
Design criteria Allowable subgrade plastic strain for the design period, \(\varepsilon _{pa} =2\%\)
Allowable settlement of subgrade in design period, \(ρ _{a} = 25 mm\)
Minimum granular layer height = 0.45 m
Impact factor method = AREA (1974)
Subgrade capacity method = Li and Selig (1998b)
Rail and sleeper Not needed
properties
Traffic conditions Static wheel load, \(P _{s} = 173 kN\)
Velocity, V = 64 km/h
Design tonnage, T = 64 MGT
Wheel diameter, D = 0.97 m
Granular material Resilient modulus, \(E _{b} = E _{c} =E _{s} = 276 MPa\)
characteristics
Subgrade soil Soil type, CH (fat clay)
characteristics Soil compressive strength, \(σ _{s} = 90 kPa\)
Subgrade modulus, \(E _{s} = 14 MPa\)
Thickness = 1.5 m

Solution

Step 1: Dynamic amplification factor (IF) calculation based on AREA (1974) method. The American Railroad Engineering Association (AREA, 1974) introduced a simple mathematical expression to calculate the value of \(I F\) based on the results of in-situ measurements of dynamic wheel loads from train cars with known static wheel loads. All the measurements were undertaken on standard-gauge tracks \((1435 \mathrm{~mm})\) in the U.S. The value of \(I F\) is set to be a function of vehicle speed \(V(\mathrm{~km} / \mathrm{h})\) and wheel diameter \(D(\mathrm{~mm})\).

\(I F=1+5.21 \frac{V}{D}\)

 

\(I F=1+5.21 \frac{V}{D}=1+5.21 \frac{64}{970}=1.34\)

Step 2: Dynamic wheel load \(\left(P_{d}\right)\) calculation:

\(P_{d}=1.34 \times 173=231.82 \mathrm{kN}\)

Step 3: Number of load cycle \((N)\) calculation:

\(N=\frac{T}{8 P_{s}}=\frac{\left(60 \times 10^{6}\right) \times 9.81}{8 \times 173}=425,289\)

Step 4: Select the values of \(a, m\) and \(b\) for \(\mathrm{CH}\) soil from Table 13.2:

\(a=1.2 ; m=2.4 ; b=0.18\)
Table 13.2 Values of soil parameters a, b and m for calculating the plastic shear strain of
subgrade for four types of soil (Li and Selig, 1998a).
Soil type a b m
CH (fat clay) 1.20 0.18 2.40
CL (lean clay) 1.10 0.16 2.00
MH (elastic silt) 0.84 0.13 2.00
ML (silt) 0.64 0.10 1.70

Step 5: Calculation for the first design procedure (preventing local shear failure of subgrade):

Step 5.1: Calculate allowable deviator stress on subgrade \(\left(\sigma_{\mathrm{da}}\right)\) using

\(\sigma_{d a}=\sigma_{s}\left[\left(\frac{\varepsilon_{p a}}{a N^{b}}\right)^{(1 / m)}\right]\)

\(\varepsilon_{p}=2 \%\) and \(\sigma_{s}=90 \mathrm{kPa}\) is used from the input parameters.

\(\sigma_{\mathrm{da}}=\sigma_{s}\left[\left(\frac{\varepsilon_{p}}{a N^{b}}\right)^{(1 / m)}\right]=90\left[\left(\frac{2}{1.2 \times 425289^{0.18}}\right)^{(1 / 2.4)}\right]=42.1 \mathrm{kPa}\)

Step 5.2: Calculate the strain influence factor \(\left(I_{\varepsilon}\right)\) from \(I_{\varepsilon}=\frac{\sigma_{d} A}{P_{d}}\)

\(I_{\varepsilon}=\frac{42.1 \times 0.645}{232.47}=0.117\)

Step 5.3: Determine the \((\mathrm{H} / \mathrm{L})\) from Figure \(13.6\) corresponding to the granular material modulus \(\mathrm{E}_{\mathrm{s}}=276 \mathrm{MPa}\) and subgrade modulus \(14 \mathrm{MPa}\) :

\(\begin{aligned}&\mathrm{H} / \mathrm{L}=5.07 \\ &\mathrm{H}=5.07 \times 0.152=0.771 \mathrm{~m}\end{aligned}\)
