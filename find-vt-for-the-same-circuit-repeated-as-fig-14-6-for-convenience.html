Guest Question.

Find v(t) for the same circuit, repeated as Fig. 14.6 for convenience.

Admin
Solution

This time we write a single nodal equation,

 

\(\frac{v(t)-u(t)}{4}+\frac{1}{16} \frac{d v}{d t}=0\)

 

Taking the Laplace transform, we obtain

 

\(\frac{ V ( s )}{4}-\frac{1}{4 s }+\frac{1}{16} s V ( s )-\frac{v\left(0^{-}\right)}{16}=0\)

 

or

 

\(V ( s )\left(1+\frac{ s }{4}\right)=\frac{1}{ s }+\frac{9}{4}\)

 

Thus,

 

\(V ( s )=\frac{4}{ s ( s +4)}+\frac{9}{ s +4}\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{ s }-\frac{1}{ s +4}+\frac{9}{ s +4}\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{ s }+\frac{8}{ s +4}\)

 

and taking the inverse transform,

 

\(v(t)=\left(1+8 e^{-4 t}\right) u(t) V\)

 

To check this result, we note that \(\left(\frac{1}{16}\right) d v / d t\) should yield the previous expression for i (t). For t > 0,

 

\(\frac{1}{16} \frac{d v}{d t}=\frac{1}{16}(-32) e^{-4 t}=-2 e^{-4 t}\)

 

which is in agreement with what was found in .
