Guest Question.

Obtain the Boltzmann probability distribution for our model system that corresponds to an average energy per oscillator of hν.

Admin
Solution

The Boltzmann distribution of Eq. (22.5-1):(Probability of a state of energy E) \(∝e^{−E/k_{B}T}\) is not normalized. The normalized Boltzmann probability distribution is given by

(Probability of a state i) \(= p_{i}= \frac{1}{Z}e^{−ε_{i}/k_{B}T} \) (25.1-10)

where the divisor Z is chosen to give normalization:

\(Z=\sum\limits_{i=0}^{\infty }{e^{−ε_{i}/k_{B}T}}\) (25.1-11)

Note that the Boltzmann probability distribution is not restricted to the states that we have listed, so that the upper limit of the sum is infinite. The formula for Z is the sum of a geometric progression, a well-known sum:

\(\sum\limits_{v=0}^{\infty }{a^{v}}=\frac{1}{1-a}\) (25.1-12)

where we must require that a < 1. Since \( hν/k_{B}T > 0, e^{−hν/k_{B}T} < 1\) and our sum is:

\(Z=\sum\limits_{v=0}^{\infty }{e^{−vhv/k_{B}T}}=\sum\limits_{v=0}^{\infty }{\bigl(e^{−hv/k_{B}T}\bigr)^{v}}=\frac{1}{1-e^{−hv/k_{B}T}}\) (25.1-13)

so that the normalized Boltzmann probability distribution is

\(p_{v}=(1-e^{−hv/k_{B}T}) e^{−vhv/k_{B}T}\) (25.1-14)

We will show later in the chapter that the formula for the mean energy is given by

\(〈ε〉=\frac{hν}{e^{hv/k_{B}T}−1}\) (25.1-15)

In order to have 〈ε〉 equal to hνs we must have a value of the temperature given by

\(e^{hv/k_{B}T}−1 = 1 or \frac{hν}{k_{B}T}=\ln(2)\) (25.1-16)

which gives the normalized Boltzmann distribution for our model system of four harmonic oscillators:

\(p_{v}(Boltzmann) =\frac{1}{2}e^{−v \ln(2)} =\frac{1}{2}\bigl(e^{−\ln(2)}\bigr)^{v} =\frac{1}{2}2^{−v}\) (25.1-17)
