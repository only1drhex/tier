Guest Question.

A steel shaft loaded in bending is 32 mm in diameter, abutting a filleted shoulder 38 mm in diameter. Estimate the Marin size factor \(k_{b}\) if the shaft is used in
(a) A rotating mode.
(b) A nonrotating mode.

Admin
Solution

(a) From Equation (6–19)
\( k_{b} = (\frac{d}{7.62})^{−0.107}= (\frac{32}{7.62})^{−0.107}= 0.86\)

(b) From Table 6–3,
\(d_{e}\) = 0.37d = 0.37(32) = 11.84 mm
From Equation (6–19) \(k_{b}=\begin{cases} (d/0.3)^{-0.107}=0.879d^{-0.107} & 0.3\leq d\leq2in \\ 0.91d^{-0.157} & 2\lt d\leq 10 in \\(d/7.62)^{-0.107}=1.24d^{-0.107} &7.62\leq d\leq51mm \\ 1.51d^{-0.157}&51<d\leq254mm\end{cases} \),

\( k_{b} = (\frac{11.84}{7.62})^{−0.107}= 0.95\)
