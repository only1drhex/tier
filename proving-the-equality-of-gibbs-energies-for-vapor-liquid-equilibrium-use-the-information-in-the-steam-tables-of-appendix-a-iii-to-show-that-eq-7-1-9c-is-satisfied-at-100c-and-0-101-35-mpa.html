Guest Question.

Proving the Equality of Gibbs Energies for Vapor-Liquid Equilibrium
Use the information in the steam tables of to show that Eq. 7.1-9c is satisfied at 100°C and 0.101 35 MPa.

 

\(\left(\frac{\partial S}{\partial N^{\mathrm{I}}}\right)_{U^\mathrm{I}, V^{\mathrm{I}}}=0 \quad \text { so that } \quad \frac{\underline{G}^{\mathrm{I}}}{T^{\mathrm{I}}}=\frac{\underline{G}^{\mathrm{II}}}{T^{\mathrm{II}}} \quad \text { or } \quad \underline{G}^{\mathrm{I}}=\underline{G}^{\mathrm{II}}\) (7.1.9c)

Admin
Solution

From the saturated steam temperature table, we have at 100°C and 0.10135 MPa

 

\(\begin{aligned}\hat{H}^{\mathrm{L}} &=419.04 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} & \hat{H}^{\mathrm{V}} &=2676.1 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \\\\\hat{S}^{\mathrm{L}} &=1.3069 \mathrm{~kJ} /(\mathrm{kg} \mathrm{K}) & \hat{S}^{\mathrm{V}} &=7.3549 \mathrm{~kJ} /(\mathrm{kg} \mathrm{K})\end{aligned}\)

 

Since \(\hat{G}=\hat{H}-T \hat{S}\), we have further that

 

\(\hat{G}^{\mathrm{L}}=419.04-373.15 \times 1.3069=-68.6 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}\)

 

and

 

\(\hat{G}^{\mathrm{V}}=2676.1-373.15 \times 7.3549=-68.4 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}\)

 

which, to the accuracy of the calculations here, confirms that \(\hat{G}^{\mathrm{L}}=\hat{G}^{\mathrm{V}}\) (or, equivalently, that \(\underline{G}^{\mathrm{L}}=\underline{G}^{\mathrm{V}}\) ) at this vapor-liquid phase equilibrium state.
