Guest Question.

The concentration readings in Table Ell.l represent a continuous response to a pulse input into a closed vessel which is to be used as a chemical reactor. Calculate the mean residence time of fluid in the vessel t, and tabulate and plot the exit age distribution E.

Tracer Output Concentration, \(C_{pulse}\) gm/liter fluid Time t, min
0 0
3 5
5 10
5 15
4 20
2 25
1 30
0 35

 

Admin
Solution

The mean residence time, from Eq. \(4,\) is

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{t} &=\frac{\sum t_{i} C_{i} \Delta t_{i}}{\sum C_{i} \Delta t_{i}}=\frac{\Delta t=\text { constant }}{\sum C_{i}} \frac{\sum t_{i} C_{i}}{\sum} \\&=\frac{5 \times 3+10 \times 5+15 \times 5+20 \times 4+25 \times 2+30 \times 1}{3+5+5+4+2+1}=15 \mathrm{~min}\end{aligned}\)

The area under the concentration-time curve,

\(\text { Area }=\sum C \Delta t=(3+5+5+4+2+1) 5=100 \mathrm{gm} \cdot \text { min/liter }\)

gives the total amount of tracer introduced. To find \(\mathbf{E},\) the area under this curve must be unity; hence, the concentration readings must each be divided by the total area, giving

\(\mathbf{E}=\frac{C}{\text { area }}\)

Thus we have

t,mine 0 5 10 15 20 25 30
\(E=\frac{C}{area},mine^{-1}\) 0 0.03 0.05 0.05 0.04 0.02 0.01

Figure Ell.l is a plot of this distribution.
