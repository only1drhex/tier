Guest Question.

A 400 V, 1450 rpm, 50 Hz wound-rotor induction motor has the following circuit model parameters.

\( R_{1} = 0.3 \Omega \) \( R_{2}^{\prime}=0.25 \Omega \)

\( X_{1}=X_{2}^{\prime}=0.6 \Omega \Omega \) \( X_{m} = 35 \Omega\)

Rotational loss = 1500 W

(a) Calculate the starting torque and current when the motor is started direct on full voltage.

(b) Calculate the full-load current, pf and torque (net).

Also find internal efficiency and overall efficiency. Internal efficiency is defined as

\( P_{o u t}(\text { gross }) / P_{i n} ; P_{o u t}(\text { gross })=(1-s) P_{G}\)

(c) Find the slip for maximum torque and the value of the maximum torque.

Admin
Solution

The motor circuit model is drawn in Fig. 9.27(a) and its Thevenin equivalent version is drawn in Fig. 9.27(b). Thevenin equivalent quantities are calculated below

\( \bar{Z}_{T H}=\frac{j 35(0.3+j 0.6)}{0.3+j 35.60}=0.29+j 059 \Omega\)

 

\( \bar{V}_{T H}=\frac{231 \angle 0^{\circ} \times j 35}{0.3+j 35.60}=227 \angle 0.5^{\circ} V\)

 

\( n_{s}=1500 rpm \quad \omega_{s}=157.1 rad / s\)

 

(a) Starting on full voltage (s = 1)

From the circuit of Fig. 9.27(a)

\( \bar{Z}_{f}=\frac{j 35(0.25+j 0.6)}{0.25+j 35.6}=0.24+j 0.59 \Omega\)

 

\( R_{f}=0.24 \Omega\)

 

\( I_{1}(\text { start })=\frac{231}{1.31}=176.3 A\)

 

\( T(\text { start })=\frac{3 I_{1}^{2} R_{f}}{\omega_{s}}\)

 

\( \frac{3 \times(176.3)^{2} \times 0.24}{157.1}=142.4 Nm\)

 

(b) \( s_{f 1}=\frac{1500-1450}{1500}=1 / 30\)

 

\( R_{2}^{\prime} / s_{f l}=0.25 \times 30=7.5 \Omega\)

From the circuit of Fig. 9.27(a)

\( \bar{Z}_{f}=\frac{j 35(75+j 0.6)}{7.5+j 35.6}\)

 

\( =6.94+j 2.05 \Omega\)

 

\( R_{f}=6.94 \Omega\)

 

\( \bar{Z}_{i n}=(0.3+j 0.6)+(6.94+j 2.05)\)

 

\( =7.71 \angle 20.1^{\circ} \Omega\)

 

\( I_{1}=\frac{231}{7.71}=30 A\)

 

\( p_{f}=\cos 20.1^{\circ}=0.94\) lagging

 

\( P_{G}=3 \times(30)^{2} \times 6.94=18.74 kW\)

Power output (gross \( =(1-s) P_{ G }\)

 

\( (1-1 / 30) \times 18.74=18.12 kW\)

 

Rotational loss = 1.5 kW

Power output (net) = 18.12 – 1.5 = 16.62 kW

Torque (net) \( =\frac{16.62 \times 1000}{157.1(1-1 / 30)}=109.4 Nm\)

Power input \( =\sqrt{3} \times 400 \times 30 \times 0.94=19.54 kW\)

 

\( \eta=\frac{16.62}{19.54} \times 100=85.06 \% \)

Internal efficiency \( =\frac{P_{G}(1-s)}{P_{i n}}=\frac{18.74(1-1 / 30)}{19.54}=92.71 \% \)

(c) We shall use the Thevenin equivalent circuit of Fig. 9.27(b). For maximum torque

\( \frac{0.25}{s_{\max , T}}=\sqrt{(0.29)^{2}+(0.59+0.6)^{2}}\)

Or \( s_{\max , T}=0.204\)

 

\( R_{2}^{\prime} / s_{\max , T}=0.25 / 0.204=1.225\)

 

\( \bar{Z}(\text { total })=(0.29+j 0.59)+(1.225+j 0.6)=1.93 \angle 38.1^{\circ}\)

 

\( I_{2}^{\prime}=\frac{227}{1.93}=117.6\)

 

\( T_{\max }=3 \times \frac{(117.6)^{2} \times 1.225}{157.1}=323.5 Nm\)
