Guest Question.

A thin liquid film of ammonia \((NH_{3})\), which has formed on the inner surface of a tube of diameter D = 10 mm and length L = 1 m, is removed by passing dry air through the tube at a flow rate of \(3 × 10^{-4} kg/s\). The tube and the air are at 25°C. What is the average mass transfer convection coefficient?

Admin
Solution

Known: Liquid ammonia on the inner surface of a tube is removed by evaporation into an airstream.

Find: Average mass transfer convection coefficient for the tube.

Assumptions:
1. Thin ammonia film with smooth surface.
2. Heat and mass transfer analogy is applicable.

Properties: , air (25°C): \(\nu = 15.7 × 10^{-6} m^{2}/s, \mu = 183.6 × 10^{-7} N .s/m^{2}\). , ammonia–air (25°C): \(D_{AB} = 0.28 × 10^{-4} m^{2}/s, Sc = (\nu /D_{AB}) = 0.56\).

Analysis: From Equation 8.6

\( Re_D = \frac{4\dot m}{\pi D\mu }\) (8.6)

\( Re_{D} = \frac{4 × 3 × 10^{-4} kg/s}{π × 0.01 m × 183.6 × 10^{-7} N . s/m} = 2080\)

in which case the flow is laminar. From Equation 8.3, the hydrodynamic entrance length is

\( \left( \frac{x_{fd,h}}{D} \right)_{lam} ≈ 0.05 \ Re_D \) (8.3)

\(x_{fd,h} ≈ 0.05 D_{h} Re_{D} = 0.05 × 0.01m × 2080 = 1.04 m\)

The concentration entrance length may be obtained from the mass transfer analog of Equation 8.23, yielding

\( \left( \frac{x_{fd,t}}{D} \right)_{lam} ≈ 0.05 \ Re_D \ Pr \) (8.23)

\( x_{fd,c} ≈ x_{fd,h} Sc = 1.04 m × 0.56 = 0.58 m\)

Since \(x_{fd,h} > L\), the combined entry length condition holds even though \( x_{fd,c} < L \). A constant ammonia vapor concentration is maintained at the surface of the film, which is analogous to a constant surface temperature. The mass transfer analog of Equation 8.58 is appropriate, with \( Gz_{D}= (D/L) Re_{D} Sc = (0.01 m/1 m) × 2080 × 0.56 = 11.6.\) Thus

\(\overset{-}{Sh_{D}}= \frac{\frac{3.66}{\tanh[2.264 \ Gz_{D}^{-1/3} + 1.7 \ Gz_{D}^{-2/3}]} +0.0499 \ Gz_{D} \tanh(Gz_{D}^{-1}) }{\tanh(2.432 \ Sc^{1/6} \ Gz^{-1/6}_{D} )} \)

 

\(= \frac{\frac{3.66}{\tanh[2.264× 11.6^{-1/3} + 1.7 × 11.6^{-2/3}]} +0.0499 × 11.6 × tanh(11.6^{-1}) }{\tanh(2.432 ×0.56^{1/6} × 11.6^{-1/6} )} = 4.74\)

Finally,

\(\overset{-}{h_{m}} = \overset{-}{Sh_{D}}\frac{D_{AB}}{D} = \frac{4.74 × 0.28 × 10^{-4}m^{2}/s}{0.01 m} = 0.013 m/s \)

Comments: An assumption of fully developed conditions over the entire tube would provide a value of \(\overset{-}{Sh_{D}} = 3.66\) , which is 23% less than the preceding result.
