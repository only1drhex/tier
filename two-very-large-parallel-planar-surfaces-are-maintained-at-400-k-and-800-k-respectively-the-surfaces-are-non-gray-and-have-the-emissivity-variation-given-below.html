Guest Question.

Two very large parallel planar surfaces are maintained at 400 K and 800 K, respectively. The surfaces are non-gray and have the emissivity variation given below:

Surface 1: \(\varepsilon_{1,1}=0.6\) for \(0<\lambda<3 \mu m\) ; \(\varepsilon_{1,2}=0.2\) for \(\lambda>3 \mu m\)

Surface 2: \(\varepsilon_{2,1}=0.2\) for \(0<\lambda<3 \mu m\) ; \(\varepsilon_{2,2}=0.6\) for \(\lambda>3 \mu m\)

Determine the heat transfer between the two planes per unit area using a non-gray analysis. Compare this with a gray analysis based on the equivalent gray emissivities of the two surfaces at their respective temperatures.

Admin
Solution

Step 1 There are two common bands in this problem. The first band spans the wavelength region 0 to \(3 \mu m\) and the second band spans the wavelength region from \(3 \mu m\) to âˆž. Let \(f_{j, i}\) represent the fraction of black body power for the jth surface and the ith band.

Step 2

Surface 1:

With \(T_{1}=800 K\) and \(\lambda_{1}=3 \mu m\), we have \(\lambda_{1} T_{1}=2400 \mu m K\) From Table 8.1, we then have \(f_{0-2400}=0.150256\) and hence \(f_{1,1}=0.150256\). With \(\lambda_{2}=\infty\) we then have \(f_{1,2}=1-f_{1,1}=1-0.150256=0.859744\) .

Surface 2:

With \(T_{2}=400 K\) and \(\lambda_{1}=3 \mu m\), we have \(\lambda_{1} T_{2}=1200 \mu m K\) From Table 8.1, we then have \(f_{0-2400}=0.150256\) and hence \(f_{2,1}=0.002134\). With \(\lambda_{2}=\infty\) we then have \(f_{2,2}=1-f_{2,1}=1-0.002134=0.997866\).

Step 3 Heat transfer from surface 1 to surface 2 per unit area:

It is calculated using expression 10.42 for each band. For band 1, we have

\(q_{1-2}=\frac{\varepsilon_{1} \varepsilon_{2} \sigma\left(T_{1}^{4}-T_{2}^{4}\right)}{1-\left(1-\varepsilon_{1}\right)\left(1-\varepsilon_{2}\right)}=\frac{\sigma\left(T_{1}^{4}-T_{2}^{4}\right)}{\left(\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{1}}+\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{2}}-1\right)}\) (10.42)

\(q_{1}=\frac{\sigma\left(f_{1,1} T_{1}^{4}-f_{2,1} T_{2}^{4}\right)}{\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{1,1}}+\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{2,1}}-1}\) \(=\frac{5.67 \times 10^{-8}\left(0.150256 \times 800^{4}-0.002134 \times 400^{4}\right)}{\frac{1}{0.6}+\frac{1}{0.2}-1}=581.7 W / m ^{2}\)

Similarly, for band 2 we have

\(q_{2}=\frac{\sigma\left(f_{1,2} T_{1}^{4}-f_{2,2} T_{2}^{4}\right)}{\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{1,2}}+\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{2,2}}-1}\) \(=\frac{5.67 \times 10^{-8}\left(0.859744 \times 800^{4}-0.997866 \times 400^{4}\right)}{\frac{1}{0.2}+\frac{1}{0.6}-1}=3260.6 W / m ^{2}\)

Hence, the net heat transfer from surface 1 to surface 2 is

\(q=581.7+3260.6=3842.3 W / m ^{2}\)

Heat transfer under the gray model:

The gray model calculation is based on the equivalent gray body emissivities of the two surfaces at the respective surface temperatures.

\(\varepsilon_{1}=f_{1,1} \varepsilon_{1,1}+f_{1,2} \varepsilon_{1,2}=0.150256 \times 0.6+0.002134 \times 0.2=0.257\) \(\varepsilon_{2}=f_{2,1} \varepsilon_{2,1}+f_{2,2} \varepsilon_{2,2}=0.859744 \times 0.2+0.997866 \times 0.6=0.599\)

The gray calculation thus yields

\(q=\frac{\sigma\left(T_{1}^{4}-T_{2}^{4}\right)}{\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{1}}+\frac{1}{\varepsilon_{2}}-1}=\frac{5.67 \times 10^{-8}\left(800^{4}-400^{4}\right)}{\frac{1}{0.257}+\frac{1}{0.599}-1}=4771.8 W / m ^{2}\)

The example shows that a gray analysis is grossly in error.
