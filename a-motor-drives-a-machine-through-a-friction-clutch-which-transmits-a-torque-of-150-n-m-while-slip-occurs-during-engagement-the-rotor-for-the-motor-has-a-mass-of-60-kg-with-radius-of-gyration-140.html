Guest Question.

A motor drives a machine through a friction clutch which transmits a torque of 150 N-m, while slip occurs during engagement. The rotor, for the motor, has a mass of 60 kg, with radius of gyration 140 mm and the inertia of the machine is equivalent to a mass of 20 kg at the driving shaft with radius of gyration 80 mm. If the motor is running at 750 r.p.m. and the machine is at rest, find the speed after the engagement of the clutch and the time taken. What will be the kinetic energy lost during the operation ?

Admin
Solution

Given : T = 150 N-m ; \(m_{1}=60 kg ; k_{1}=140 mm =0.14 m ; m_{2}=20 kg\) ; \(k_{2}=80 mm =0.08 m ; N_{1}=750 \text { r.p.m. or } \omega_{1}=2 \pi \times 750 / 60=78.55 rad / s ; N_{2}=0 \text { or } \omega_{2}=0 \)

We know that mass moment of inertia of the rotor on motor,

\(I_{1}=m_{1}\left(k_{1}\right)^{2}=60(0.14)^{2}=1.176 kg – m ^{2}\)

and mass moment of inertia of the parts attached to machine,

\(I_{2}=m_{2}\left(k_{2}\right)^{2}=20(0.08)^{2}=0.128 kg – m ^{2}\)

Speed after the engagement of the clutch and the time taken

Let \(\omega \) = Speed after the engagement of the clutch in rad/s,

t = Time taken in seconds, and

\(\alpha \) = Angular acceleration during the operation in rad/\(s^{2}\).

We know that the impulsive torque = change of angular momentum

\(\therefore\) \(T . t=I_{1}\left(\omega_{1}-\omega\right) \quad \text { or } \quad t=\frac{I_{1}\left(\omega_{1}-\omega\right)}{T}=\frac{1.176(78.55-\omega)}{150} s\) …(i)

Also \(T . t=I_{2}\left(\omega_{2}-\omega\right) \quad \text { or } \quad t=\frac{I_{2}\left(\omega-\omega_{2}\right)}{T}=\frac{0.128 \times \omega}{150} s\) …(ii)

Equating equations (i) and (ii), \(\ldots\left(\because \omega_{2}=0\right)\)

\(\frac{1.176(78.55-\omega)}{150}=\frac{0.128 \omega}{150} \quad \text { or } \quad 92.4-1.176 \omega=0.128 \omega\) \(1.304 \omega=92.4 \quad \text { or } \quad \omega=92.4 / 1.304=70.6 rad / s\)

Substituting the value of Ï‰ in equation (ii),

\(t=\frac{0.128 \times 70.6}{150}=0.06 s\)

Kinetic energy lost during the operation

We know that the kinetic energy lost during the operation,

\(E=\frac{I_{1} \cdot I_{2}\left(\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}\right)^{2}}{2\left(I_{1}+I_{2}\right)}=\frac{I_{1} \cdot I_{2} \cdot \omega_{1}^{2}}{2\left(I_{1}+I_{2}\right)}\) \(\ldots\left(\because \omega_{2}=0\right)\)

\(=\frac{1.176 \times 0.128(78.55)^{2}}{2(1.176+0.128)}=\frac{928.8}{2.61}=356 N – m\)
