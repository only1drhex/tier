Guest Question.

An air-filled rectangular waveguide of dimensions a = 4 cm, b = 2 cm transports energy in the dominant mode at a rate of 2 mW. If the frequency of operation is 10 GHz, determine the peak value of the electric field in the waveguide.

Admin
Solution

The dominant mode for \(a\gt b\) is \(TE_{10}\) mode. The field expressions corresponding to this mode \((m=1, n=0)\) are in eq. (12.36):

\(E_{xs}=\frac{j\omega\mu}{h^{2}}\left(\frac{n\pi}{b}\right)H_{o}\cos\left(\frac{m\pi x}{a}\right)\sin\left(\frac{n\pi y}{b}\right)e^{-\gamma z}\)

\(E_{ys}=-\frac{j\omega\mu}{h^{2}}\left(\frac{m\pi}{a}\right)H_{o}\sin\left(\frac{m\pi x}{a}\right)\cos\left(\frac{n\pi y}{b}\right)e^{-\gamma z}\)

\(H_{xs}=\frac{\gamma}{h^{2}}\left(\frac{m\pi}{a}\right)H_{o}\sin\left(\frac{m\pi x}{a}\right)\cos\left(\frac{n\pi y}{b}\right)e^{-\gamma z}\)

\(H_{ys}=\frac{\gamma}{h^{2}}\left(\frac{n\pi}{b}\right)H_{o}\cos\left(\frac{m\pi x}{a}\right)\sin\left(\frac{n\pi y}{b}\right)e^{-\gamma z}\)

or (12.43):

\(E_{y}=\frac{\omega\mu a}{\pi}H_{o}\sin\left(\frac{\pi x}{a}\right)\sin(\omega t-\beta z)\)

\(H_{x}=-\frac{\beta a}{\pi}H_{o}\sin\left(\frac{\pi x}{a}\right)\sin(\omega t-\beta z)\)

\(E_{z}=E_{x}=H_{y}=0\)

namely

\(E_{xs}=0, E_{ys}=-jE_{o}\sin\left(\frac{\pi x}{a}\right)e^{-j\beta z}\), where \(E_{o}=\frac{\omega\mu a}{\pi}H_{o}\)

\(f_{c}=\frac{u^{'}}{2a}=\frac{3\times10^{8}}{2(4\times10^{-2})}=3.75 GHz\)

\(\eta=\eta_{TE}=\frac{\eta^{'}}{_{\square}\overline{1-\left[\frac{f_{c}}{f}\right]^{2}}}=\frac{377}{_{\square}\overline{1-\left[\frac{3.75}{10}\right]^{2}}}=406.7\Omega\)

From eq. (12.53):

\(P_{ave}=\int?_{ave}\cdot dS=\int_{x=0}^{a}\int_{y=0}^{b}\frac{|E_{xs}|^{2}+|E_{ys}|^{2}}{2\eta}dydx\)

the average power transmitted is

\(P_{ave}=\int_{y=0}^{b}\int_{x=0}^{a}\frac{|E_{ys}|^{2}}{2\eta}dxdy=\frac{E_{o}^{2}}{2\eta}\int_{0}^{b}dy\int_{0}^{a}\sin^{2}\left(\frac{\pi x}{a}\right)dz=\frac{E_{o}^{2}ab}{4\eta}\)

Hence

\(E_{o}^{2}=\frac{4\eta P_{ave}}{ab}=\frac{4(406.7)\times2\times10^{-3}}{8\times10^{-4}}=4067\)

\(E_{o}=63.77 V/m\)
