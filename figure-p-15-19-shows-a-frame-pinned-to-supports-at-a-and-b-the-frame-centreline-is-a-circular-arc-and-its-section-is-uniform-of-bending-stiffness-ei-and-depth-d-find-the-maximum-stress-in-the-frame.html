Guest Question.

Figure P.15.19 shows a frame pinned to supports at A and B. The frame centreline is a circular arc and its section is uniform, of bending stiffness EI and depth d. Find the maximum stress in the frame produced by a uniform temperature gradient through the depth, the temperature on the outer and inner surfaces being raised and lowered by an amount T. The coefficient of linear expansion of the material of the frame is α. (Hint. Treat half the frame as a curved cantilever builtin on its axis of symmetry and determine the horizontal reaction at a support by equating the horizontal deflection produced by the temperature gradient to the horizontal deflection produced by the reaction).
Ans. 1.29 ETα.

Admin
Solution

The frame is symmetrical about a vertical plane through its centre so that only half need be considered. Also, from symmetry the frame will act as though fixed at C (Fig. S.15.19).
If B is released the displacement at B due to the temperature rise is obtained from Eq. (15.43)

\(\Delta_{ Te , B }=-\int_{0}^{L} M_{1} \frac{\alpha t}{h} d x\) (15.43)

in which

\(M_{1}=1\left(r \sin 30^{\circ}+r \sin \theta\right)=r(0.5+\sin \theta)\)

Then

\(\delta_{ B , T }=\int_{-\pi / 6}^{\pi / 2} r(0.5+\sin \theta)\left(\frac{2 \alpha T}{d}\right) r d \theta\)

which gives

\(\delta_{ B , T }=\frac{3.83 \alpha T r^{2}}{d}\)

The displacement at B due to the horizontal reaction \(H_{ B }\) at B is obtained using the unit load method. From Eq. (iii) of Ex. 15.14

\(v=\int_{0}^{L} \frac{M_{ A } M_{1}}{E I} d x\) Eq. (iii) of Ex. 15.14

in which

\(M_{ A }=-H_{ B } r(0.5+\sin \theta)\) and \(M_{1}\) is given above

\(\delta_{ B , H }=-\left(\frac{H_{ B }}{E I}\right) \int_{-\pi / 6}^{\pi / 2}(0.5+\sin \theta)^{2} r^{3} d \theta\)

from which

\(\delta_{ B , H }=\frac{-2.22 H_{ B } r^{3}}{E I}\)

The sum of the displacement of B due to the temperature rise and the displacement of B due to the support reaction is zero since B is a support point. Then

\(\frac{3.83 \alpha T r^{2}}{d}-\frac{2.22 H_{ B } r^{3}}{E I}=0\)

from which

\(H_{ B }=\frac{1.73 E I T \alpha}{d r}\)

The maximum bending moment occurs at C and is \(H_{ B } \times 1.5 r=2.6 E I T \alpha / d\). Then, from Eq. (9.9),

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I}\) (9.9)

the maximum direct stress is

\(\sigma_{\max }=\frac{2.6 E I T \alpha \times 0.5 d}{I d}=1.3 E T \alpha\) .
