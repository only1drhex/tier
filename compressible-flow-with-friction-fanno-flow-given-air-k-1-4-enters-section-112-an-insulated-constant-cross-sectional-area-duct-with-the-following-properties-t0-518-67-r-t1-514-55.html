Guest Question.

Compressible Flow with Friction (Fanno Flow)

GIVEN Air (k = 1.4) enters [section 112] an insulated, constant cross-sectional area duct with the following properties:

\(\begin{array}{l}T_{0}=518.67^{\circ} R \\T_{1}=514.55^{\circ} R \\p_{1}=14.3 psia\end{array}\)

FIND For Fanno flow, determine corresponding values of fluid temperature and entropy change for various values of downstream pressures and plot the related Fanno line.

Admin
Solution

To plot the Fanno line we can use Eq. 11.75

 

\(T+\frac{(\rho V)^{2} T^{2}}{2 c_{p}\left(p^{2} / R^{2}\right)}=T_{0}=\text { constant }\) (11.75)

 

\(T+\frac{(\rho V)^{2} T^{2}}{2 c_{p} p^{2} / R^{2}}=T_{0}=\text { constant }\) (1)

 

and Eq. 11.76

 

\(s-s_{1}=c_{p} \ln \frac{T}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{p}{p_{1}}\) (11.76)

 

\(s-s_{1}=c_{p} \ln \frac{T}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{p}{p_{1}}\) (2)

 

to construct a table of values of temperature and entropy change corresponding to different levels of pressure in the Fanno flow.

We need values of the ideal gas constant and the specific heat at constant pressure to use in Eqs. 1 and 2. From Table 1.7 we read for air

 

\(R=1716( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( slug \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)=53.3( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\)

 

From Eq. 11.14 we obtain

 

\(c_{p}=\frac{R k}{k-1}\) (11.14)

 

\(c_{p}=\frac{R k}{k-1}\) (3)

 

or

 

\(\begin{aligned}c_{p} &=\frac{\left[53.3( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\right](1.4)}{1.4-1} \\&=187( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\end{aligned}\)

 

From Eqs. 11.1 and 11.69 we obtain

 

\(\rho=\frac{p}{R T}\) (11.1)

 

\(V= Ma \sqrt{R T k}\) (11.69)

 

\(\rho V=\frac{p}{R T} Ma \sqrt{R T k}\)

 

and \(\rho V\) is constant for this flow

 

\(\rho V=\rho_{1} V_{1}=\frac{p_{1}}{R T_{1}} Ma _{1} \sqrt{R T_{1} k}\) (4)

 

But

 

\(\frac{T_{1}}{T_{0}}=\frac{514.55^{\circ} F }{518.67^{\circ} R }=0.992\)

 

and from Eq. 11.56

 

\(\frac{T}{T_{0}}=\frac{1}{1+[(k-1) / 2] Ma ^{2}}\) (11.56)

 

\(Ma _{1}=\sqrt{\left(\frac{1}{0.992}-1\right) / .02}=0.2\)

 

Thus, with

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sqrt{R T_{1} k} &=\sqrt{(1.4)\left[53.3( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\right]\left(514.55{ }^{\circ} R \right)} \\&=196[( ft \cdot lb ) / lbm ]^{1 / 2}\left[\left(32.2 lbm \cdot ft / s ^{2}\right) / lb \right]^{1 / 2} \\&=1112 ft / s\end{aligned}\)

 

Eq. 4 becomes

 

\(\rho V=\frac{(14.3 psia )\left(144 in ^{2} / ft ^{2}\right) 0.2(1112 ft / s )}{\left[53.3( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\right]\left(514.55^{\circ} R \right)}\)

 

or

 

\(\rho V=16.7 lbm /\left( ft ^{2} \cdot s \right)\)

 

For p = 7 psia we have from Eq. 1

 

\(\begin{aligned}T &+\frac{\left[16.7 lbm /\left( ft ^{2} \cdot s \right)\right]^{2} T^{2}}{2\left[187( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\right] \frac{(7 psia )^{2}\left(144 in ^{2} \cdot ft ^{2}\right)^{2}}{\left[53.3( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\right]^{2}}} \\&=518.67{ }^{\circ} R\end{aligned}\)

 

or

 

\(2.08 \times 10^{-3}\left[\left( lbm \cdot ft / s ^{2}\right) /\left( lb \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\right] T^{2}+T-518.67{ }^{\circ} R =0\)

 

Thus, since \(1 lb =32.2 lbm \cdot ft / s ^{2}\) we obtain

 

\(6.5 \times 10^{-5} T^{2}+T-518.67=0\)

 

Hence,

 

\(T=502.3^{\circ} R\)

 

where T is in \({ }^{\circ} R\).

From Eq. 2, we obtain

 

\(\begin{aligned}s-s_{1}=& {\left[187( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\right] \ln \left(\frac{502.3{ }^{\circ} R }{514.55{ }^{\circ} R }\right) } \\&-\left[53.3( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\right] \ln \left(\frac{7 psia }{14.3 psia }\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

or

 

\(s-s_{1}=33.6( ft \cdot lb ) /\left( lbm \cdot{ }^{\circ} R \right)\)

 

Proceeding as outlined above, we construct the table of values shown below and graphed as the Fanno line in Fig. E11.11. The maximum entropy difference occurs at a pressure of 2.62 psia and a temperature of \(432.1^{\circ} R\).

COMMENT Note that for Fanno flow the entropy must increase in the direction of flow. Hence, this flow can proceed either from subsonic conditions upstream to a sonic condition (Ma = 1) downstream or from supersonic conditions upstream to a sonic condition downstream. The arrows in Fig. 11.11 indicate in which direction a Fanno flow can proceed.
