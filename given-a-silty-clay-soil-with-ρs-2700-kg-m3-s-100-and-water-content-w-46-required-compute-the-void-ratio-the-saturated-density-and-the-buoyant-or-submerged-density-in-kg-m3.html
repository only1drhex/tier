Guest Question.

Given:

A silty clay soil with \(\rho_{s}=2700 kg / m ^{3}, S=100 \%\), and water content w = 46%.

Required:

Compute the void ratio, the saturated density, and the buoyant or submerged density in \(kg / m ^{3}\).

Admin
Solution

Place the given information on a phase diagram (Fig. Ex. 2.7).

Assume \(V_{s}=1 m ^{3} \text {; therefore } M_{s}=V_{s} \rho_{s}=2700 kg\). From Eq. (2.15a), we can solve fore directly:

 

\(\rho_{w} S_{e}=w \rho_{s}\) (2.15a)

 

\(e=\frac{w \rho_{s}}{\rho_{w} S}=\frac{0.46 \times 2700 kg / m ^{3}}{1000 kg / m ^{3} \times 1.0}=1.242\)

 

But e also equals \(V_{v} \text {, since } V_{s}=1.0 \text {; likewise } M_{w}=1242 kg \text {, since } M_{w} \text { is numerically equal to } V_{w}\), because \(\rho_{w}=1000 kg / m ^{3}\). Now that all the unknowns have been found, we may readily calculate the saturated density [Eq. (2.10)]:

 

\(\rho_{ sat }=\frac{M_{s}+M_{w}}{V_{t}}\left(V_{a}=0, S=100 \%\right)\) (2.10)

 

\(\rho_{ sat }=\frac{M_{t}}{V_{t}}=\frac{M_{w}+M_{s}}{1+e}=\frac{(1242+2700) kg }{(1+1.24) m ^{3}}=1758 kg / m ^{3}\)

 

We could also use Eq. (2.17) directly:

 

\(\rho_{ sat }=\frac{\rho_{s}+\rho_{w} e}{1+e}\) (2.17)

 

The buoyant density \(\rho^{\prime}\) from Eq. (2.11) is:

 

\(\rho^{\prime}=\rho_{\text {sat }}-\rho_{w}\) (2.11)

 

\(\rho^{\prime}=\rho_{\text {sat }}-\rho_{w}=1758 kg / m ^{3}-1000 kg / m ^{3}=758 kg / m ^{3}\)

 

In this example, \(\rho^{\prime}\) is less than the density of water. Go back and look at Table 2.1 for typical values of \(\rho^{\prime}\). The submerged or buoyant density of soil will be very important later on in our discussion of consolidation, settlement, and strength properties of soils.
