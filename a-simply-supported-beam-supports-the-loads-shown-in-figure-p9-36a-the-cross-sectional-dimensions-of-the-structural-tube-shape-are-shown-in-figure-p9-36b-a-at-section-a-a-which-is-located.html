Guest Question.

A simply supported beam supports the loads shown in Figure P9.36a. The cross-sectional dimensions of the structural tube shape are shown in Figure P9.36b.
(a) At section a–a, which is located 4 ft to the right of pin support B, determine the bending stress and the shear stress at point H, which is located 3 in. below the top surface of the tube shape.
(b) Determine the magnitude and the location of the maximum horizontal shear stress in the tube shape at section a–a.

Admin
Solution

Shear force magnitude at a–a:
V = 27.60 kips

Bending moment at a–a:
M = 60.90 kip-ft

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}I &=\frac{(12 in. )(16 in. )^{3}}{12}-\frac{(11.25 in. )(15.25 in. )^{3}}{12} \\&=771.0830 in .^{4}\end{aligned}\)

(a) Bending stress at H:

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{H} &=-\frac{M y}{I} \\&=-\frac{(60,900 lb – ft )(5 in .)(12 in . / ft )}{771.0830 in. ^{4}} \\&=-4,738.79 psi =4,740 psi ( C )\end{aligned}\)

Shear stress at H:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{H}=(12 \text { in. })(0.375 \text { in. })(7.8125 \text { in. })+2(0.375 \text { in. })(2.625 \text { in. })(6.3125 in .)=47.5840 \text { in. }^{3} \\&\tau_{H}=\frac{(27,600 lb )\left(47.5840 in .{ }^{3}\right)}{\left(771.0830 in .{ }^{4}\right)(2 \times 0.375 in .)}=2,270 psi\end{aligned}\)

(b) Maximum shear force magnitude:
V = 39.60 kips (at pin B)

Maximum horizontal shear stress:
At neutral axis:

\(\begin{aligned}&Q_{\max }=(12 in .)(0.375 in. )(7.8125 in .)+2(0.375 in .)(7.625 in. )(3.8125 in .)=56.9590 in .{ }^{3} \\&\tau_{\max }=\frac{(39,600 lb )\left(56.9590 in .{ }^{3}\right)}{\left(771.0830 in .{ }^{4}\right)(2 \times 0.375 in .)}=3,900 psi\end{aligned}\)
