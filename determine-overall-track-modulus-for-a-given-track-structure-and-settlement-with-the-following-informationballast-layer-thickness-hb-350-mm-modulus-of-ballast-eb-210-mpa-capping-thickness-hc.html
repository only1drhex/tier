Guest Question.

Determine overall track modulus for a given track structure and settlement with the following information:

Ballast layer: thickness, \(\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{b}}=350 \mathrm{~mm}\); modulus of ballast: \(\mathrm{E}_{\mathrm{b}}=210 \mathrm{MPa}\)

Capping: thickness, \(\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{c}}=150 \mathrm{~mm}\); modulus of capping: \(\mathrm{E}_{\mathrm{b}}=110 \mathrm{MPa}\)

Structure fill: thickness, \(\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{f}}=700 \mathrm{~mm}\); modulus of structural fill: \(\mathrm{E}_{\mathrm{b}}=40 \mathrm{MPa}\)

Admin
Solution

Calculation procedure:

Equivalent modulus of granular layer \((\bar{E})\) is calculated as:

\(\bar{E}=\frac{H_{b}+H_{c}+H_{f}}{\left(\frac{H_{b}}{E_{b}}+\frac{H_{c}}{E_{c}}+\frac{H_{f}}{E_{f}}\right)}\)

where \(\mathrm{E}_{\mathrm{b}}, \mathrm{E}_{\mathrm{c}}\) and \(\mathrm{E}_{\mathrm{f}}\) are the elastic modulus of the ballast, capping and structural fill. \(\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{b}}, \mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{c}}\) and \(\mathrm{H}_{\mathrm{f}}\) are the thicknesses of ballast, capping and structural fill, respectively.

\(\bar{E}=\frac{0.35+0.15+0.7}{\frac{0.35}{210}+\frac{0.15}{110}+\frac{0.7}{40}}=58.45 \mathrm{MPa}\)

Assume the equivalent dynamic stress at the sleeper/ballast is \(550 \mathrm{kPa}\) (Indraratna and Ngo 2018). The average strain \(\left(\varepsilon_{\text {ave }}\right)\) of the equivalent granular medium is calculated as the stress at sleeper/ballast interface divided by the equivalent modulus \((\bar{E})\), determined as:

\(\varepsilon_{\text {ave }}=\frac{\sigma_{d y n}}{\bar{E}}=\frac{550}{58.45 \times 10^{3}} \times 100=0.941 \%\)

The elastic settlement of granular medium is then predicted using the total height \((\mathrm{H}=1.2 \mathrm{~m})\) and determined as:

\(S_{\text {total }}=\varepsilon_{\text {ave }} \times \mathrm{H}=0.941 / 100 \times 1.2=0.01129(\mathrm{~m})=11.29 \mathrm{~mm}\)
