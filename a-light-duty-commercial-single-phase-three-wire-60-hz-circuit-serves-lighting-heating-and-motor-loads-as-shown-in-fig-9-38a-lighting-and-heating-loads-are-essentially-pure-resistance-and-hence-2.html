Guest Question.

A light-duty commercial single-phase three-wire 60 -Hz circuit serves lighting, heating, and motor loads, as shown in Fig. 9.38a. Lighting and heating loads are essentially pure resistance and, hence, unity power factor (pf), whereas motor loads have lagging pf.

We wish to design a balanced configuration for the network and determine its economic viability using the following procedure.
a. Compute the phase and neutral currents, and the complex power and pf for each source.
b. Now move the heating load (panel H) to phase b, as shown in Fig. 9.38b. This is called “balancing” the load. Repeat the analysis of (a).
c. Assume that the phase and neutral conductor resistances are each 0.05 Î© and have negligible effect on the results of (a). Evaluate the system line losses for (a) and (b). If the loads in question operate 24 hours per day and 365 days per year, at $0.08/kWh, how much energy (and money) is saved by operating in the balanced mode?

Admin
Solution

a. The magnitudes of the rms currents are

 

\(I_{L}=I_{H}=\frac{P}{V}=\frac{5000}{120}=41.67 \mathrm{~A} \mathrm{rms} \)

 

and

\(I_{m}=\frac{10,000}{240}=41.67 \mathrm{~A} \mathrm{rms}\)

 

 

In addition,

 

\(\theta_{m}=\cos ^{-1}(0.8)=-36.9^{\circ}\)

 

Therefore,

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{I}_{a} &=\mathbf{I}_{L}+\mathbf{I}_{H}+\mathbf{I}_{m} \\\\&=41.67 \underline{/ 0^{\circ}}+41.67 \underline{/ 0^{\circ}}+41.67 \underline{/-36.9^{\circ}} \\\\&=119.4 \underline{/-12.1^{\circ}} \mathrm{A} \mathrm{rms}\end{aligned}\)

 

The currents in the neutral and phase b lines are

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\mathbf{I}_{n}=\mathbf{I}_{L}+\mathbf{I}_{H}=83.34 \angle 0^{\circ} \mathrm{A} \mathrm{rms} \\\\&\mathbf{I}_{b}=41.67\underline{/-36.9^{\circ}} \mathrm{A} \mathrm{rms}\end{aligned}\)

 

The complex power and power factor for each source are

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\mathbf{S}_{a}=\mathbf{V}_{a n} \mathbf{I}_{a}^{*}=\left(120 \underline{/ 0^{\circ}}\right)\left(119.4 \underline{/+12.1^{\circ}}\right)=14+j 3 \mathrm{kVA} \\\\&\mathrm{pf}_{a}=\cos \left(12.1^{\circ}\right)=0.9778 \text { lagging }\end{aligned}\)

 

and in a similar manner

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{S}_{b} &=\mathbf{V}_{b n} \mathbf{I}_{b}^{*}=4+j 3 \mathrm{kVA} \\\\\mathrm{pf}_{b} &=0.8 \text { lagging }\end{aligned}\)

 

b. Under the balanced condition

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{I}_{a} &=\mathbf{I}_{L}+\mathbf{I}_{m}=41.67 \underline{/ 0^{\circ}}+41.67 \underline{/-36.9^{\circ}} \\\\&=79.06\underline{/-18.4^{\circ}} \mathrm{A} \mathrm{rms}\end{aligned}\)

 

and

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\mathbf{I}_{b}=79.06-18.4^{\circ} \mathrm{A} \mathrm{rms} \\\\&\mathbf{I}_{n}=0\end{aligned}\)

 

Therefore,

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{S}_{a} &=\mathbf{V}_{n a} \mathbf{I}_{a}^{*}=9+j 3 \mathrm{kVA} \\\\\mathrm{pf}_{a} &=0.9487 \text { lagging }\end{aligned}\)

 

and

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{S}_{b} &=\mathbf{V}_{b n} \mathbf{I}_{b}^{*}=9+j 3 \mathrm{kVA} \\\\\mathrm{pf}_{b} &=0.9487 \text { lagging }\end{aligned}\)

 

c. The power loss in the lines in kW is

 

\(\begin{aligned}P_{\text {loss }} &=R_{a} I_{a}^{2}+R_{b} I_{b}^{2}+R_{n} I_{c}^{2} \\\\&=0.05\left(I_{a}^{2}+I_{b}^{2}+I_{n}^{2}\right) / 1000\end{aligned}\)

 

The total energy loss for a year is

 

\(W_{\text {loss }}=(24)(365) P_{\text {loss }}=8760 P_{\text {loss }}\)

 

and the annual cost is

 

\(\text { Cost }=\$ 0.08 W_{\text {loss }}\)

 

A comparison of the unbalanced and balanced cases is shown in the following table.

UNBALANCED CASE BALANCED CASE
\(P_{\text {loss }}(\mathrm{kW})\) 1.147 0.625
\(W_{\text {loss }}(\mathrm{kW}-\mathrm{hr})\) 10,034 5,475
Cost($) 804 438

 

Therefore, the annual savings obtained using the balanced configuration is
Annual energy savings = 10,048 – 5,475 = 4,573 kWh
Annual cost savings = 804 – 438 = $ 366
