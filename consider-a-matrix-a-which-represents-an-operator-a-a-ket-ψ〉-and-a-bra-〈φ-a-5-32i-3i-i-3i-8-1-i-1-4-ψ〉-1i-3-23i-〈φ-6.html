Guest Question.

Consider a matrix A (which represents an operator Â), a ket | ψ〉, and a bra 〈φ |:

\(A=\left(\begin{matrix} 5 & 3+2i & 3i \\ -i & 3i & 8 \\ 1-i & 1 & 4 \end{matrix} \right), | \psi 〉=\left(\begin{matrix} -1+i \\ 3 \\ 2+3i \end{matrix} \right) , 〈 \phi |=(6 -i 5). \)

(a) Calculate the quantities \( A | ψ〉,〈\phi | A, 〈\phi | A | ψ〉, and | ψ〉〈\phi |. \)

(b) Find the complex conjugate, the transpose, and the Hermitian conjugate of A, | ψ〉, and 〈φ |.

(c) Calculate 〈φ | ψ〉 and 〈ψ | φ〉; are they equal? Comment on the differences between the complex conjugate, Hermitian conjugate, and transpose of kets and bras.

Admin
Solution

(a) The calculations are straightforward:

\(A | \psi 〉=\left(\begin{matrix} 5 & 3+2i & 3i \\ -i & 3i & 8 \\ 1-i & 1 & 4 \end{matrix} \right) \left(\begin{matrix} -1+i \\ 3 \\ 2+3i \end{matrix} \right)=\left(\begin{matrix} -5+17i \\ 17+34i \\ 11+14i \end{matrix} \right), \) (2.220)

\(〈\phi | A =(6 -i 5)\left(\begin{matrix} 5 & 3+2i & 3i \\ -i & 3i & 8 \\ 1-i & 1 & 4 \end{matrix} \right)=(34-5i 26+12i 20+10i ),\) (2.221)

\(〈\phi | A | \psi 〉 =(6 -i 5)\left(\begin{matrix} 5 & 3+2i & 3i \\ -i & 3i & 8 \\ 1-i & 1 & 4 \end{matrix} \right)\left(\begin{matrix} -1+i \\ 3 \\ 2+3i \end{matrix} \right)=59 + 155i,\) (2.222)

\(| \psi 〉〈\phi |=\left(\begin{matrix} -1+i \\ 3 \\ 2+3i \end{matrix} \right)(6 -i 5)=\left(\begin{matrix} -6+6i & 1+i & -5+5i \\ 18 & -3i & 15 \\ 12+18i & 3-2i & 10+15i \end{matrix} \right) .\) (2.223)

(b) To obtain the complex conjugate of A, | ψ〉, and 〈φ |, we need simply to take the complex conjugate of their elements:

\(A^*=\left(\begin{matrix} 5 & 3-2i & -3i \\ i & -3i & 8 \\ 1+i & 1 & 4 \end{matrix} \right), | \psi 〉^*=\left(\begin{matrix} -1-i \\ 3 \\ 2-3i \end {matrix} \right ), 〈\phi |^*=(6 i 5).\) (2.224)

For the transpose of A, | ψ〉, and 〈φ |, we simply interchange columns with rows:

\(A^T=\left(\begin{matrix} 5 & -i & 1-i \\ 3+2i & 3i & 1 \\ 3i & 8 & 4 \end{matrix} \right), | \psi 〉^T=( -1+i 3 2+3i ), 〈\phi |^T=\left(\begin{matrix} 6 \\ -i \\ 5 \end{matrix}\right) .\) (2.225)

The Hermitian conjugate can be obtained by taking the complex conjugates of the transpose expressions calculated above: \(A^\dagger =(A^T)^*, | \psi 〉^\dagger=(| \psi 〉^T)^*=〈\psi |,〈\phi |^\dagger=(〈\phi |^T)^*=| \phi 〉:\)

\(A^\dagger =\left(\begin{matrix} 5 & i & 1+i \\ 3-2i & -3i & 1 \\ -3i & 8 & 4 \end{matrix} \right), 〈\psi |=( -1-i 3 2-3i ), | \phi 〉=\left(\begin{matrix} 6 \\ i \\ 5 \end{matrix} \right).\) (2.226)

(c) Using the kets and bras above, we can easily calculate the needed scalar products:

\(〈\phi | \psi 〉=( 6 -i 5 )\left(\begin{matrix} -1+i \\ 3 \\ 2+3i \end{matrix} \right)=6(-1+i)+(-i)(3)+5(2+3i)=4+18i, \) (2.227)

\(〈\psi | \phi 〉=( -1-i 3 2-3i )\left(\begin{matrix} 6 \\ i \\ 5 \end{matrix} \right)=6(-1-i)+(i)(3)+5(2-3i)=4-18i, \) (2.228)

We see that 〈φ | ψ〉 and 〈ψ | φ〉 are not equal; they are complex conjugates of each other:

\(〈\psi | \phi 〉=〈\phi | \psi 〉^*=4-18i.\) (2.229)

Remark

We should underscore the importance of the differences between \(| ψ〉^*,| ψ〉^T ,and | ψ〉^\dagger \). Most notably, we should note (from equations (2.224)–(2.226)) that \( | ψ〉^*\) a ket, while \( | ψ〉^T\) and \( | ψ〉^\dagger\) are bras. Additionally, we should note that \(〈\phi |^* \) is a bra, while \(〈\phi |^T \) and \(〈\phi |^\dagger \) are kets.
