Guest Question.

Consider the reaction:

\(S \rightarrow P\)

catalyzed by enzyme E. Michaelis-Menten rate parameters are \(r_{\max }=0.15 min ^{-1} \text { and } K_{ m }\) = 0.01 mol/L when the enzyme loading 0.1 g/L. CSTR has been selected to produce 100 moles of P per hour from a stream of substrate containing 1 mole-S/L. S costs $0.1/mole, P sells for $1/mole, and the enzyme E cost $100/g. The cost of operating the reactor is $0.1/L/h. Assume no value or cost of disposal of unreacted S (i.e., separation or recovery cost to S is identical to the fresh S cost).

a. What is the relationship between rate of formation of P and enzyme loading?

b. Perform a mole balance on the reactor to relate the concentration of exiting S with reactor size.

c. What is the optimum concentration of S at the outlet of the reactor? What is the optimum enzyme loading?

d. What is the cash flow per mole of product from the process?

 

 

Admin
Solution

A sketch of the reactor system is shown in Fig. E7.4. Substrate and enzyme are fed to the CSTR. The enzyme is let out unchanged, while some substrate is turned into product P.

(a) The maximum rate is proportional to the enzyme loading, that is,

\(r_{\max }=k E_{0}\) (E7.4.1)

Thus the rate of formation of P is given by

\(r_{P}=\frac{k E_{0} S}{K_{m}+S}\) (E7.4.2)

 

\(K_{ m }=0.01 mol / L \text { and } k=0.15 min ^{-1} /(0.1 g / L )=1.5 L \cdot min ^{-1} \cdot g ^{-1}\).

(b) It is understood that the CSTR is operating under constant temperature (isothermal) and steady state conditions, that is, no accumulation of any sort of materials inside the reactor. Mole balance over the entire reactor for P yields

\(0-F_{ P }+r_{ P } V=0\) (E7.4.3)

Substituting Eq. (E7.4.2) \(r_{P}=\frac{k E_{0} S}{K_{m}+S}\) into Eq. (E7.4.3) \(0-F_{ P }+r_{ P } V=0\) , we obtain

\(V=\frac{F_{P}}{r_{P}}=F_{P} \frac{K_{m}+S}{k E_{0} S}\) (E7.4.4)

(c) Based on the economic information at hand, we can estimate the gross profit for the reaction process as

\(GP \$=\$_{ P } F_{ P }-\$_{ S } F_{ S 0}-\$_{ E } F_{ E 0}-\$_{V } V\) (E7.4.5)

where \(\$_{ P }, \$_{ S }, \$_{ E } \text { and } \$ V\) are the molar value of product P, molar cost of substrate S, unit cost of enzyme, and the unit operating cost of reactor based on the reactor volume and time; respectively. Based on the stoichiometry, we have

\(Q=\frac{F_{P}}{Q}=\frac{F_{P}}{S_{0}-S}\) (E7.4.6)

which leads to

\(F_{S 0}=\frac{F_{P} S_{0}}{S_{0}-S}\) (E7.4.7a)

 

\(F_{E 0}=\frac{F_{P} E_{0}}{S_{0}-S}\) (E7.4.7b)

Substituting Eqs. (E7.4.7) \(F_{S 0}=\frac{F_{P} S_{0}}{S_{0}-S}\) \(F_{E 0}=\frac{F_{P} E_{0}}{S_{0}-S}\) and (E7.4.4) \(V=\frac{F_{P}}{r_{P}}=F_{P} \frac{K_{m}+S}{k E_{0} S}\) into Eq. (E7.4.5) \(GP \$=\$_{ P } F_{ P }-\$_{ S } F_{ S 0}-\$_{ E } F_{ E 0}-\$_{V } V\) , we obtain

\(C P \$=\$_{P} F_{P}-\$_{S} \frac{F_{P} S_{0}}{S_{0}-S}-\$_{E} \frac{F_{P} E_{0}}{S_{0}-S}-\$_{V} F_{P} \frac{K_{m}+S}{k E_{0} S}\) (E7.4.8)

To find the optimum reactor conditions, we maximize the gross profit by varying the effluent substrate concentration and enzyme loading. This task can easily be achieved by an optimizer like the Excel Solver. Substituting the known parameters into Eq. (E7.4.8) \(C P \$=\$_{P} F_{P}-\$_{S} \frac{F_{P} S_{0}}{S_{0}-S}-\$_{E} \frac{F_{P} E_{0}}{S_{0}-S}-\$_{V} F_{P} \frac{K_{m}+S}{k E_{0} S}\) , we obtain

\(G P \$=1 \times 100-0.1 \times \frac{100}{1-S}-100 \times \frac{100 E_{0}}{1-S}-0.1 \times \frac{100(0.01+S)}{1.5 \times 60 E_{0} S} \$ / h \)

which is reduced to

\(G P \$=100-\frac{10}{1-S}-\frac{10000 E_{0}}{1-S}-\frac{0.01+S}{9 E_{0} S} \$ / h\) (E7.4.9)

Maximizing the gross profit GP$, we obtain: S = 0.075969 mol/L, \(E_{0}\) = 0.0024102 g/L, and GP$ = $10.9256/h

Therefore, we have found the optimum enzyme loading and the effluent substrate concentration.

(d) The cash flow based on Eq (E7-4.8) \(C P \$=\$_{P} F_{P}-\$_{S} \frac{F_{P} S_{0}}{S_{0}-S}-\$_{E} \frac{F_{P} E_{0}}{S_{0}-S}-\$_{V} F_{P} \frac{K_{m}+S}{k E_{0} S}\) ,

\(\frac{G P \$}{F_{P}}=\$_{P}-\$_{S} \frac{S_{0}}{S_{0}-S}-\$_{E} \frac{E_{0}}{S_{0}-S}-\$_{V} \frac{K_{m}+S}{k E_{0} S}\) (E7.4.10)

Or since we have GP$ value already,

\(\frac{G P \$}{F_{P}}=\frac{10.9256}{100} \frac{\$}{m o l-P}=\frac{\$ 0.109256}{m o l-P}\)

This concludes the example. One must be careful of the units whenever numbers are inserted into the equation.
Example 7.4 shows that enzyme cost can be significant. Therefore, separate and reuse of enzyme is very important for bioprocess systems.
