Guest Question.

A 25 kW, 230 V has an armature resistance of \( 0.12 \Omega \) and a field resistance of \( 120 \Omega \). Its speed at full load is 2000 rpm. The field current may be neglected.

(a) For a four step (resistance steps) calculate the resistances of the steps if the allowable maximum armature current is 1.5 times the full load current. What is the lower current limit?

(b) Calculate the motor speed at each stud when the current reaches the lower limit and starting handle is to be moved to the next stud.

Admin
Solution
\( I_{a}(fl)=\frac{25\times 10^{3}}{230} =108.7 A\)

(a) Four resistance steps require five studs (k = 5) upper current limit,

\( I_{1} =108.7 \times 1.5 =163 A\)

 

\( R_{1}=\frac{230}{I_{1}} = \frac{230}{163} =1.41 \Omega\)

 

\( R_{a} = 0.12 \Omega \) (given)

 

\( \frac{R_{1}}{R_{a}} =\gamma ^{k-1}\)

 

\( \frac{1.41}{0.12} =\gamma ^{4}\) or \( \gamma =\left(\frac{1.41}{0.12} \right) ^{1/4}\)

 

\( \gamma = 1.851\)

 

\( \frac{I_{1}}{I_{2}} =\gamma =1.851, 1/\gamma =0.54\)

 

or \( I_{2}\) (lower limit) \( = \frac {1.63} {1.85} = 88.1 A\)

 

Resistance steps \( r_{1} = (1 – 0.54) \times 1.41 = 0.6486 \Omega = 0.649 \Omega\)

 

\( r_{2} = 0.6486 \Omega 0.54 = 0.3502 \Omega = 0.350 \Omega \)

 

\( r_{3} = 0.1891 \Omega = 0.189\Omega \)

 

\( r_{4} = 0.1021 \Omega = 0.102 \Omega \)

 

(b) At full load

\( I_{a}(fl) = 103.7 A\)

 

\( E_{a} = 230 – 103.7 \times 0.12 = 217.6 V\)

 

Speed n = 2000 rpm

 

\( E_{a} = K'_{a} \Phi n = K''_{a} n\); filed current is constant, armature reaction effect ignored.

or \( 217.6 = K''_{a} \times 2000\) or \( K''_{a} = 0.1088\)

At stud 1 when armature current reduces to \( I_{2} = 88.1\) A

\( E_{a} (1) = V_{t} – I_{2} \times R_{1} = 230 – 88.1 \times 1.41 = 105.8 \) V

\( \therefore\) \( n_{1}=\frac{105.8}{0.1088} =971 rpm\)

At stud 2

\( E_{a} (2) = 230 – 88.1 \times (1.41 – 0.649) = 230 – 88.1 \times 0.761 \)

= 162.96 V

\( n_{2}=\frac{162.96}{0.1088} =1498 rpm\)

At stud 3

\( E_{a}(3) = 230 – 88.1 \times (0.761 – 0.35) = 230 – 88.1 \times 0.411\)

= 193.8 V

\( n_{3}=\frac{210.4}{0.1088} =1934 rpm\)

 

At stud 5 (ON)

The armature current instantaneously rises to \( I_{1} = 163 A\). The speed increases from 1934 rpm to 2000 rpm and armature current falls to 108.7 A to match the full load on the motor of the load is any different the armature current and speed adjust accordingly.

Note: To determine the times at which speeds \( n_{1},…, n_{4}\) are reaches needs electromagnetic study; Section 7.21.
