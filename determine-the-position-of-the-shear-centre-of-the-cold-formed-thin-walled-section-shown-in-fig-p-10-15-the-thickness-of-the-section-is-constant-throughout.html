Guest Question.

Determine the position of the shear centre of the cold-formed, thin-walled section shown in Fig. P.10.15. The thickness of the section is constant throughout.

Admin
Solution

In this problem the axis of symmetry is the vertical y axis and the shear centre will lie on this axis so that only its vertical position is required. Therefore we apply a horizontal shear load \( S_{z} \) through the shear centre, S, as shown in Fig. S.10.15.

The thickness of the section is constant and will not appear in the answer for the shear centre position (see S.10.14), therefore assume the section has unit thickness.
Eq. (10.22), since \( I_{z y}=0, t=1 \text { and only } S_{z} \) is applied, reduces to

\( q_{s}=-\frac{S_{z}}{I_{y}} \int_{0}^{s} z d s \) (i)

where

\( I_{y}=2\left[\frac{25^{3}}{12}+25 \times 62.5^{2}+50 \times 50^{2}+\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}}(50 \cos \theta)^{2} 50 d \theta\right] \)

i.e.

\( I_{y}=6.44 \times 10^{5} mm ^{4} \)

In the flange 12, \( z=75-s_{1} \) and

\( q_{12}=-\frac{S_{z}}{I_{y}} \int_{0}^{s_{1}}\left(75-s_{1}\right) d s=-\frac{S_{z}}{I_{y}}\left(75 s_{1}-\frac{s_{1}^{2}}{2}\right) \)

and when \( s_{1}=25 mm , q_{2}=-1562.5 S_{z} / I_{y} \)
In the wall 23, z=50 mm, then

\( q_{23}=-\frac{S_{z}}{I_{y}}\left(\int_{0}^{s_{2}} 50 d s+1562.5\right)=-\frac{S_{z}}{I_{y}}\left(50 s_{2}+1562.5\right) \)

When \( s_{2}=50 mm , q_{3}=-4062.5 S_{z} / I_{y} \)
In the wall 34, z=50 cos Î¸, therefore

\( q_{34}=-\frac{S_{z}}{I_{y}}\left(\int_{0}^{\theta} 50 \cos \theta 50 d \theta+4062.5\right)=-\frac{S_{z}}{I_{y}}(2500 \sin \theta+4062.5) \)

Now taking moments about O

\( S_{z} y_{S}=-2\left(\int_{0}^{25} q_{12} 50 d s_{1}-\int_{0}^{50} q_{23} 50 d s_{2}-\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}} q_{34} 50^{2} d \theta\right) \)

Note that the moments due to the shear flows in the walls 23 and 34 are opposite in sign to the moment produced by the shear flow in the wall 12. Substituting for \( q_{12} \), etc. gives

\( y_{ S }=87.5 mm \)
