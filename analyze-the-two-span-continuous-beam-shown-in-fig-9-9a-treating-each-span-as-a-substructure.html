Guest Question.

Analyze the two-span continuous beam shown in Fig. 9.9(a), treating each span as a substructure.

Solution

Analytical Model: The structure is modeled as being composed of two substructures and three joints, as shown in Fig. 9.9(b). It has one degree of freedom and five restrained coordinates. As each substructure consists of two beam members connected together by a hinged joint, we will use the expressions of stiffnesses and fixed-joint forces for such substructures, derived in , in the present example.

\(\bar{F}^{*}_{fE1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 25 \\ 125 \\25\\-125 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 2 \\ 3 \\ 4\\1 \end{matrix}\)

Substructure Joint Displacements, and Member End Displacements and End Forces:

Substructure 1 The substructure’s external joint displacements \(\bar{v}_{E}\) can be obtained by simply comparing the substructure’s external degree of freedom numbers with its code numbers, as follows.

\(\bar{v}_{E1}=\left[\begin{matrix} \bar{v}_{1} \\ \bar{v}_{2} \\ \bar{v}_{3} \\ \bar{v}_{4} \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 2 \\ 3 \\4\\1 \end{matrix}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\\d_{1} \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ -0.011905 \end{matrix}\right]\)

The substructure’s internal joint displacements can now be calculated, using the relationship (Eq. (9.25)) \(\bar{v}_{1}=\bar{K}^{-1}_{II}(\bar{F}_{I}-\bar{F}_{fI}-\bar{K}_{IE}\bar{v}_{E} )\) . Substitution of the numerical values of E, I, \(L_{1}, L_{2}\), and W into Eqs. (1) and (3) of yields

\(\bar{K}^{-1}_{II}=[0.0014881]\)

and

\(\bar{F}_{I1}=[-50]\)

Similarly, by substituting the appropriate numerical values into the expressions of \(\bar{K}_{IE}\) and \(\bar{F}_{fI}\) given in Figs. 9.7(c) and (d), respectively, of , we obtain

\(\bar{K}_{IE1}\)=[-336 -1,680 -336 1,680]

and

\(\bar{F}_{fI1}=0\)

By substituting the numerical values of the foregoing submatrices and subvectors into Eq. (9.25) \(\bar{v}_{1}=\bar{K}^{-1}_{II}(\bar{F}_{I}-\bar{F}_{fI}-\bar{K}_{IE}\bar{v}_{E} )\), we determine the internal joint displacements for substructure 1 to be

\(\bar{v}_{I1}=\bar{K}^{-1}_{II1}(\bar{F}_{I1}-\bar{F}_{fI1}-\bar{K}_{IE1}\bar{v}_{E1} )=[-0.044643]\)

Thus, the complete joint displacement vector for substructure 1 is

\(\bar{v}_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} \bar{v}_{E1} \\ \overset{……….}{\bar{v}_{I1} } \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ -0.011905 rad \\ -0.044643 m \end{matrix} \right]\)

With the displacements of all the joints of substructure 1 now known, we can determine the end displacements u, and end forces Q, for its two members (Fig. 9.9(c)) in the usual manner.

Member 1 (MT = 2) From Fig. 9.9(c), we can see that the code numbers for member 1 are 1, 2, 5, 6. Thus,

\(u_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} u_{1} \\ u_{2} \\ u_{3}\\u_{4} \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 5\\6 \end{matrix}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ \bar{v}_{5}\\0 \end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ -0.044643\\0 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Substituting the numerical values of E and I and L = 5 m into Eq. (7.18) \(k=\frac{EL}{L^{3}}\left[\begin{matrix} 3 & 3L & -3&0 \\ 3L & 3L^{2} & -3L & 0\\ -3 & -3L & 3&0 \\0&0&0&0 \end{matrix} \right]\) , we obtain the member stiffness matrix,

\(k_{1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 336 & 1,680 & -336&0 \\ 1,680 & 8,400 & -1,680 & 0\\ -336 & -1,680 & 336&0 \\0&0&0&0 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Substitution of \(k_{1}\) and \(Q_{f1} = 0\) into the member stiffness relationship, Q = ku + \(Q_{f}\), yields the following end forces for member 1 of substructure 1.

\(Q_{1}=k_{1}{u_{1}}+Q_{f1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 15 kN \\ 75 kN.m \\ -15 kN \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Member 2 (MT = 1)

\(u_{2}=\left[\begin{matrix} -0.044643 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ -0.011905 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{matrix} 5 \\6 \\ 3 \\ 4 \end{matrix}\)

Support Reactions: The reaction vector R can be assembled either directly from the member end force vectors Q, or from the external joint force vectors, \(\bar{F}_{E}\) , of the substructures. To use the latter option, we first apply Eq. (9.26)\(\bar{F}_{E}=\bar{K}^{*}_{EE}\bar{v}_{E}+\bar{F}^{*}_{fE}\) to calculate \(\bar{F}_{E}\) . Thus, by substituting the previously calculated numerical values of K∗EE, F∗f E, and ¯vE into Eq. (9.26)\(\bar{F}_{E}=\bar{K}^{*}_{EE}\bar{v}_{E}+\bar{F}^{*}_{fE}\), we obtain

\(\bar{F}_{E1}=\bar{K}^{*}_{EE1}\bar{v}_{E1}+\bar{F}^{*}_{fE1}=\left[\begin{matrix} 15 \\75 \\ 35\\-175 \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 2 \\ 3 \\ 4\\1 \end{matrix}\)

and

\(\bar{F}_{E2}=\bar{K}^{*}_{EE2}\bar{v}_{E2}+\bar{F}^{*}_{fE2}= \left[\begin{matrix} 80 \\175 \\ 100\\-275 \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 4 \\ 1 \\ 5\\6 \end{matrix}\)

The foregoing substructure forces are depicted in Fig. 9.9(e). Finally, we calculate the support reaction vector R by storing the pertinent elements of \(\bar{F}_{E1}\) and \(\bar{F}_{E2}\) in their proper positions in R, using the substructure code numbers. This yields,

\(R=\left[\begin{matrix} 15 kN \\75 kN.m \\ 115 kN\\100 kN \\-275 kN.m \end{matrix} \right]\begin{matrix} 2 \\ 3 \\ 4\\5\\6 \end{matrix}\)

The support reactions are shown in Fig. 9.9(f ).
