Finding the Kernel of a Linear Transformation
Find the kernel of the linear transformation \(T: R^3\rightarrow R^2 \) defined by \(T\left(x\right) =Ax\) , where

\(\left [\begin{matrix} 1 & -1 & -2 \\ -1 & 2 & 3 \end{matrix} \right ]\)
Admin
Solution

The kernel of T is the set of all \(x = \left(x_1, x_2, x_3\right) \ in \ R^3 \) such that\( T\left(x_1, x_2, x_3\right) = \left(0, 0\right)\) .
From this equation, you can write the homogeneous system

\(\left [\begin{matrix} 1 & -1 & -2 \\ -1 & 2 & 3 \end{matrix} \right ]\left [\begin{matrix} x_1 \\x_2\\x_3\end{matrix} \right ]=\left [\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

–>

\(x_1-x_2-2x_3=0\\-x_1+2x_2+3x_3=0\)

Writing the augmented matrix of this system in reduced row-echelon form produces

\(\left [\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & -1&0 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 &0 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

–>

\(x_1=x_3\\x_2=-x_3\).

Using the parameter\(t = x_3 \) produces the family of solutions

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} t \\ -t \\ t \end{matrix} \right ] =t\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ] =\)

So, the kernel of T is
\(ker\left(T\right) = {\left\{t\left(1, −1, 1\right) : t \ is \ a \ real \ number\right\} } = span{\left(1, −1, 1\right) }\). (See Figure 6.2.)
