Guest Question.

Differentiating Unequally Spaced Data

As in Fig. 23.4, a temperature gradient can be measured down into the soil. The heat flux at the soil-air interface can be computed with Fourier’s law,

\(q(z=0)=-\left.k \rho C \frac{d T}{d z}\right|_{z=0}\)

where q = heat flux (W/m²), k 5 coefficient of thermal diffusivity in soil \((≅ 3.5 × 10^{-7 }m^2/s)\), r = soil density ( \(\cong 1800 kg / m ^{3}\)), and C = soil specific heat ( ≅ 840 J/(kg · °C)). Note that a positive value for flux means that heat is transferred from the air to the soil. Use numerical differentiation to evaluate the gradient at the soil-air interface and employ this estimate to determine the heat flux into the ground.

Admin
Solution

Equation (23.9)

\(f^{\prime}(x)=f\left(x_{i-1}\right) \frac{2 x-x_{i}-x_{i+1}}{\left(x_{i-1}-x_{i}\right)\left(x_{i-1}-x_{i+1}\right)}+f\left(x_{i}\right) \frac{2 x-x_{i-1}-x_{i+1}}{\left(x_{i}-x_{i-1}\right)\left(x_{i}-x_{i+1}\right)}+f\left(x_{i+1}\right) \frac{2 x-x_{i-1}-x_{i}}{\left(x_{i+1}-x_{i-1}\right)\left(x_{i+1}-x_{i}\right)}\) (23.9)

can be used to calculate the derivative as

\(f^{\prime}(x)=13.5 \frac{2(0)-1.25-3.75}{(0-1.25)(0-3.75)}+12 \frac{2(0)-0-3.75}{(1.25-0)(1.25-3.75)}+10 \frac{2(0)-0-1.25}{(3.75-0)(3.75-1.25)}\)

= -14.4 + 14.4 – 1.333333 = -1.333333°C/cm

which can be used to compute (note that 1 W = 1 J/s),

\(q(z=0)=-3.5 \times 10^{-7} \frac{ m ^{2}}{ s }\left(1800 \frac{ kg }{ m ^{3}}\right)\left(840 \frac{ J }{ kg \cdot{ }^{\circ} C }\right)\left(-133.3333 \frac{{ }^{\circ} C }{ m }\right)\)

 

\(=70.56 W / m ^{2}\)
