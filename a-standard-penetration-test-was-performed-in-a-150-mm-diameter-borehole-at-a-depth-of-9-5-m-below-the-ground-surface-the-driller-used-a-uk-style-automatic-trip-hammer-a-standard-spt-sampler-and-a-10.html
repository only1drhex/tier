Guest Question.

A standard penetration test was performed in a 150 mm diameter borehole at a depth of 9.5 m below the ground surface. The driller used a UK-style automatic trip hammer, a standard SPT sampler and a 10-m drill rod. The actual blow count \(N_{field}\) was 19. The soil is normally consolidated fine sand with a unit weight of \(18.0 kN/m^{3} \) and \(D_{50} \) = 0.4 mm. The ground water table is at a depth of 15 m. Compute (a) \(N_{60} \), (b) \({N}'_{60} \), (c) \(D_{r}\% \), (d)\(\varnothing \), and (e) denseness of the fine sand.

Admin
Solution

(a) From Table, for the UK automatic trip hammer, \(E_{r} \) = 73%

From Table, for 150 mm diameter borehole, \(\eta _{b} \) = 1.05

From Table, for a standard sampler, \(\eta _{s} \) = 1.0

From Table, for a 10-m drill rod, \(\eta _{r} \) = 0.95

\(N_{60 = \frac{E_{r} \cdot \eta _{b}\cdot \eta _{s} \cdot \eta _{r} }{60}} = \frac{73 \times 1.05 \times 1 \times 0.95 \times 19}{60} = 23 \)

 

(b) \( {N}'_{60} = C_{N}N_{60} \)

 

\(C_{N} = \sqrt{\frac{Pa}{{\sigma }_{O}'}} = \sqrt{\frac{100}{9.5 \times 18}} = 0.76 \)

 

\( {N}'_{60}= 0.76 \times 23 = 17 \)

 

(c) \( D_{r}\% = [\frac{N_{60}\left ( 0.23 + \frac{0.06}{D_{50}} \right )^{1.7}}{9} \left ( \frac{1}{\frac{{\sigma}_{O}'}{Pa}} \right )]^{1/2} \)

 

\( = [\frac{23\left ( 0.23 + \frac{0.06}{0.4} \right )^{1.7}}{9} \left ( \frac{1}{\frac{9.5 \times 18}{100}} \right )]^{1/2} \left ( 100 \right) = 54 \% \)

 

(d) \( {\varnothing}' = \tan^{-1} [\frac{N_{60}}{12.2 + 20.3 \left ( \frac{{\sigma}_{O}'}{Pa} \right )}]^{0.34} \)

 

\( = \tan^{-1} [\frac{23}{12.2 + 20.3 \left ( \frac{9.5 \times 18}{100} \right )}]^{0.34} = \tan^{-1}\left ( 0.785 \right) \rightarrow {\varnothing}' = 38^{\circ} \)
