Solution

According to the theory this circuit should be implemented using \(m – 1 = 5 – 1 = 4\) dc bus capacitors, \((m – 1)(m – 2)/2 = (5 – 1)(5 – 2)/2 = 6\) flying capacitors, and \((2m – 2) = (2 \times 5 – 2) = 8\) semiconductor switches. The voltage across each dc bus capacitor is \(V_{in}/(m – 1) = V_{in}/(5 – 1) = V_{in}/4\) and the voltage across each flying capacitor is \(V_{in}/(m – 1) = V_{in}/(5 – 1) = V_{in}/4\). Since the dc bus has four capacitors the second leg of capacitors will have three, the third leg two, and the last only one. Therefore, the equivalent circuit of the five-level FCMI using only capacitors and switches will be as shown in Fig. 6.56. Simplifying the circuit of Fig. 6.56 the final equivalent circuit is presented in Fig. 6.57.

Next, using the equivalent circuit of Fig. 6.57, the switching states equivalent circuits are presented in Fig. 6.58.

As can be seen from Fig. 6.58(a)e(e), using this equivalent circuit the results are as that of Table 6.10.
