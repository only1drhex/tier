Guest Question.

The block diagram of a feedback system is shown in Fig. 7.91. The system state is,

\( x =\left[\begin{array}{l} x _{p} \\ x _{f}\end{array}\right]\),

and the dimensions of the matrices are as follows:

F = n × n, L = n × 1,

G = n × 1, x = 2n × 1,

H = 1 × n, r = 1 × 1,

K = 1 × n, y = 1 × 1

a) Write state equations for the system.

b) Let x = Tz , where

\(T =\left[\begin{array}{cc} I & 0 \\ I & - I\end{array}\right]\).

Show that the system is not controllable.

c) Find the transfer function of the system from r to y

Admin
Solution

(a) We have,

\(\left[\begin{array}{c} \dot{ x } \\ \dot{ x }_{f}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{cc} F & – G K \\ L H & F – L H – G K\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} x \\ x _{f}\end{array}\right]+\left[\begin{array}{c} G \\ G\end{array}\right] r\).

(b) In order to apply our transformation of coordinates, we need \(T^{- 1}\),

\(T =\left[\begin{array}{cc} I & 0 \\ I & – I\end{array}\right] \Longrightarrow T ^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{cc}I & 0 \\ I & – I\end{array}\right]\).

Thus,

\(F _{c l}= T ^{-1}\left[\begin{array}{cc}F & – GK \\ LH & F – LH – GK\end{array}\right] T =\left[\begin{array}{cc} F – GK & – GK \\ 0 & F – LH\end{array}\right]\),

\(G _{c l}=\left[\begin{array}{cc} I & 0 \\ I & – I\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} G \\ G\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c} G \\ 0\end{array}\right]\),

\(H _{c l}=\left[\begin{array}{ll} H & 0\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{cc} I & 0 \\ I & – I\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ll}H & 0\end{array}\right]\).

In the new coordinate system, we have,

\(\dot{ z }=\left[\begin{array}{cc}F – GK & – GK \\0 & F – LH\end{array}\right] z +\left[\begin{array}{c}G \\ 0\end{array}\right] r\),

\(y=\left[\begin{array}{ll}H & 0\end{array}\right] z\).

Observe that the system is now decomposed into controllable and uncontrollable parts. Hence, we have shown that it is an uncontrollable system.

(c) The transfer function is,
\(T(s)= H [sI -( F – GK )]^{-1} G\).
