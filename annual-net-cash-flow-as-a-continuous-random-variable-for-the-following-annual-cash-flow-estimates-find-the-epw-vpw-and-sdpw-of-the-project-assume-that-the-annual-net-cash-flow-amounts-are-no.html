Annual Net Cash Flow as a Continuous Random Variable

For the following annual cash-flow estimates, find the \(E(\mathrm{PW}), V(\mathrm{PW})\), and \(\mathrm{SD}(\mathrm{PW})\) of the project. Assume that the annual net cash-flow amounts are normally distributed with the expected values and standard deviations as given and statistically independent and that the MARR \(=15 \%\) per year.

Solution

The expected PW, based on Equation \((12-14)\), is calculated as follows, where \(E\left(F_{k}\right)\) is the expected net cash flow in year \(k(0 \leq k \leq N)\) and \(c_{k}\) is the single-payment

\(E(\mathrm{PW})=\sum_{k=0}^{N} c_{k} E\left(F_{k}\right).\) (12-14)

PW factor \((P / F, 15 \%, k)\) :

\(\begin{aligned}E(P W)=& \sum_{k=0}^{3}(P / F, 15 \%, k) E\left(F_{k}\right) \\=&-\$ 7,000+\$ 3,500(P / F, 15 \%, 1)+\$ 3,000(P / F, 15 \%, 2) \\&+\$ 2,800(P / F, 15 \%, 3) \\=& \$ 153 .\end{aligned}\)

To determine \(V(\mathrm{PW})\), we use the relationship in Equation (12-13). Thus,

\(V(\mathrm{PW})=\sum_{k=0}^{N} c_{k}^{2} V\left(F_{k}\right)\) (12-13)

\(\begin{aligned}V(\mathrm{PW})=& \sum_{k=0}^{3}(P / F, 15 \%, k)^{2} V\left(F_{k}\right) \\=& 0^{2} 1^{2}+600^{2}(P / F, 15 \%, 1)^{2}+500^{2}(P / F, 15 \%, 2)^{2} \\&+400^{2}(P / F, 15 \%, 3)^{2} \\=& 484,324 \$^{2}\end{aligned}\)

and

\(\mathrm{SD}(\mathrm{PW})=[V(\mathrm{PW})]^{1 / 2}=\$ 696.\)
