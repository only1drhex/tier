Guest Question.

For the pipes in series in , what flow will be produced for a total lost head of 70.0 ft, using (a) the equivalent pipe method and (b) the percentage method?

Admin
Solution

(a) From , 14,600 ft of 16″ pipe is equivalent to the pipes in series. For a lost head of 70.0 ft,

\(S_{16}=70.0 / 14.6=4.8 ft / 1000 ft \text {, and from Diagram B-1, } Q_{100}=3.4 mgd\).

Hence

\(Q_{130}=(130 / 100)(3.4)=4.4 mgd\).

(b) The percentage method requires the calculation of lost head values for an assumed flow Q. Although values are available from , an additional calculation will be made to serve as a check on the solution. Assuming \(Q_{130}=3.9 mgd , \text { then } Q_{100}=100 / 130 \times 3.9=3.0\) mgd, and, from Diagram B-l,

\(\begin{aligned}&S_{20}=1.35 ft / 1000 ft \text { and lost head }=1.35 \times 6=8.1 ft (14.0 \%)\\&S_{16}=4.0 ft / 1000 ft \text { and lost head }=4.0 \times 4=16.0 ft \quad(27.5 \%)\\&S_{12}=17.0 ft / 1000 ft \text { and lost head }=17.0 \times 2=34.0 ft \quad(58.5 \%)\\&\text { For } Q=3.9 mgd : \quad \text { total lost head }=58.1 ft (100.0 \%) \end{aligned}\)

The same percentages appear here as are shown in . Apply these percentages to the given total lost head of 70 ft, i.e.,

\(\begin{aligned}&LH _{20}=70 \times 14.0 \%=9.8 ft , \quad S=9.8 / 6=1.63 ft / 1000 ft , \quad Q=130 / 100 \times 3.3=4.3 mgd\\&LH _{16}=70 \times 27.5 \%=19.2 ft , \quad S=19.2 / 4=4.80 ft / 1000 ft , \quad Q=130 / 100 \times 3.4=4.4 mgd\\&LH _{12}=70 \times 58.5 \%=41.0 ft , \quad S=41.0 / 2=20.50 ft / 1000 ft , \quad Q=130 / 100 \times 3.4=4.4 mgd\end{aligned}\)

The calculation for one size is sufficient to obtain flow Q, but the check calculations give assurance that no mistakes have been made.
