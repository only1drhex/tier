Simple Statistics of a Sample

Compute the mean, variance, standard deviation, and coefficient of variation for the data in Table PT5.1.

TABLE PT5.1 Measurements of the coefficient of thermal expansion of structural steel \([× 10^{-6} in/(in ·°F)]\)

6.495 6.595 6.615 6.635 6.485 6.555
6.665 6.505 6.435 6.625 6.715 6.655
6.755 6.625 6.715 6.575 6.655 6.605
6.565 6.515 6.555 6.395 6.775 6.685
Admin
Solution

These data are added (Table PT5.2), and the results are used to compute [Eq. (PT5.1)]

\(\overline{y} = \frac{\sum{y_i} }{n}\) (PT5.1)

 

\(\overline{y} = \frac{158.4 }{24} = 6.6\)

As in Table PT5.2, the sum of the squares of the residuals is 0.217000, which can be used to compute the standard deviation [Eq. (PT5.2)]:

\(s_y = \sqrt{\frac{S_t}{n -1} }\) (PT5.2)

 

\(s_y = \sqrt{\frac{0.217000}{24 -1} } = 0.097133\)

the variance [Eq. (PT5.4)]:

\(s^2_y= \frac{\sum{(y_i – \overline{y})^2 } }{n – 1}\)

 

\(s^2y = 0.009435\)

and the coeffi cient of variation [Eq. (PT5.5)]:

\(c.v. = \frac{s_y}{y}100 \%\)

 

\(c.v. = \frac{0.097133}{6.6}100 \% = 1.47\% \)
