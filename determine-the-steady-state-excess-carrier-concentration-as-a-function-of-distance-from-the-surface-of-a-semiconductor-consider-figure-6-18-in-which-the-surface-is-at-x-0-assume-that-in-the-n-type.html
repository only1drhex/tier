Guest Question.

Determine the steady-state excess carrier concentration as a function of distance from the surface of a semiconductor.

Consider Figure \(6.18\), in which the surface is at \(x=0\). Assume that in the n-type semiconductor \(\delta p_{B}=10^{14} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}\) and \(\tau_{p 0}=10^{-6} \mathrm{~s}\) in the bulk, and \(\tau_{p 0 s}=10^{-7} \mathrm{~s}\) at the surface. Assume zero applied electric field and let \(D_{p}=10 \mathrm{~cm}^{2} / \mathrm{s}\).

Admin
Solution

From Equations (6.106) and (6.107), we have

\(\begin{array}{l} R=\frac{\delta p}{\tau_{p 0}} \equiv \frac{\delta p_{B}}{\tau_{p 0}} \\ \end{array}\) (6.106)

\(\begin{array}{l}R_{s}=\frac{\delta p_{s}}{\tau_{p 0 s}} \end{array}\) (6.107)

\(\frac{\delta p_{B}}{\tau_{p 0}}=\frac{\delta p_{s}}{\tau_{p 0 s}}\)

so that

\(\delta p_{s}=\delta p_{B}\left(\frac{\tau_{p 0 s}}{\tau_{p 0}}\right)=\left(10^{14}\right)\left(\frac{10^{-7}}{10^{-6}}\right)=10^{13} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}\)

From Equation (6.56), we can write

\(D_{p} \frac{\partial^{2}(\delta p)}{\partial x^{2}}-\mu_{p} \mathrm{E} \frac{\partial(\delta p)}{\partial x}+g^{\prime}-\frac{\delta p}{\tau_{p 0}}=\frac{\partial(\delta p)}{\partial t}\) (6.56)

\(D_{p} \frac{d^{2}(\delta p)}{d x^{2}}+g^{\prime}-\frac{\delta p}{\tau_{p 0}}=0\) (6.108)

The generation rate can be determined from the steady-state conditions in the bulk, or

\(g^{\prime}=\frac{\delta p_{B}}{\tau_{p 0}}=\frac{10^{14}}{10^{-6}}=10^{20} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}-\mathrm{s}^{-1}\)

The solution to Equation (6.107) is of the form

\(\delta p(x)=g^{\prime} \tau_{p 0}+A e^{x / L_{p}}+B e^{-x / L_{p}}\) (6.109)

As \(x \rightarrow+\infty, \delta p(x)=\delta p_{B}=g^{\prime} \tau_{p 0}=10^{14} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}\), which implies that \(A=0\). At \(x=0\), we have

\(\delta p(0)=\delta p_{s}=10^{14}+B=10^{13} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}\)

so that \(B=-9 \times 10^{13}\). The entire solution for the minority carrier hole concentration as a function of distance from the surface is

\(\delta p(x)=10^{14}\left(1-0.9 e^{-x / L_{p}}\right)\)

where

\(L_{p}=\sqrt{D_{p} \tau_{p 0}}=\sqrt{(10)\left(10^{-6}\right)}=31.6 \mu \mathrm{m}\)

Comment

The excess carrier concentration is smaller at the surface than in the bulk.
