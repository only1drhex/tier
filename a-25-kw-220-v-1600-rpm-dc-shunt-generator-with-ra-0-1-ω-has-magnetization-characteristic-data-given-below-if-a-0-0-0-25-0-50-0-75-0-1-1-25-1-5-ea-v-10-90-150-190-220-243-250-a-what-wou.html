Guest Question.

A 25 kW, 220 V, 1600 rpm dc shunt generator with Ra = 0.1\( \Omega\) has magnetization characteristic data given below:

\( I_{f} (A)\)

 0.0 0.25 0.50 0.75 0.1 1.25 1.5
\( E_{a} (V)\) 10 90 150 190 220 243

250
Admin
Solution

The magnetization characteristic drawn in Fig. 7.66

(a) No-load voltage, \( V_{0} = 250 V\)

Corresponding, \( I_{f} = 1.5 A\)

\( R_{f} = 250/1.5 = 167 \Omega\)

(b) \( V_{t}\) = 220 V

\( I_{L}= \frac{25\times 10^{3}}{220} = 113.6 A\)

As \( I_{f}\) is small, we can assume

\( I_{a} =I_{L} \)= 113.6 A

\( E_{a} = 220 + 113.6 \times 0.1 = 231.4 V\)

Ignoring armature reaction, we find from magnetization characteristic

\( I_{f} = 1.1 A\) (about 1% of \( I_{L}\))

\( R_{f} = 220/1.11 = 198.2\Omega\)

(c) Electromagnetic power = \( E_{a}I_{a} = 231.4 \times \) (113.6 – 1.1)

\( = 231.4 \times 112.5 = 26.033 kW\)

Electromagnetic torque = \( \frac{26.033\times 10^{3}}{1600\times \frac{2\pi }{60} } = 155.37 Nm\)

(d) Under load condition as in part (b)

\( I_{f} = 1.1 A\) , armature reaction ignored

Actual \( I_{f} \)= 1.25 A

\( I_{f} \) needed to counter effect of armature reaction = 1.25 – 1.1 = 0.15 A
