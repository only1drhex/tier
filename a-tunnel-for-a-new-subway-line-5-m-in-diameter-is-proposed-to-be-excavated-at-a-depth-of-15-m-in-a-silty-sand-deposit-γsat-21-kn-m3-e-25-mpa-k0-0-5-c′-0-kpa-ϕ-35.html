Guest Question.

A tunnel for a new subway line \(5 \mathrm{~m}\) in diameter is proposed to be excavated at a depth of \(15 \mathrm{~m}\) in a silty sand deposit \(\left(\gamma_{\text {sat }}=21 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{3}, E=25 \mathrm{MPa}, k_{0}=0.5\right.\), \(\left.c^{\prime}=0 \mathrm{kPa}, \phi=35^{\circ}\right)\).

a. Derive the Terzaghi (1943) general relationship for computing vertical stresses on a tunnel considering arching theory applied to tunnels in sands.

\(\sigma _{v}=\frac{yB_{1} }{k_{0}\tan \phi } (1-e^{(-k_{0}\tan \phi {D_{1} }/{B_{1} } )} )+yD_{2}e^{(-k_{0}\tan \phi {D_{1} }/{B_{1} } )}\)

b. Compute the vertical stress at the tunnel crown considering

i. Arching extends to the surface and

ii. Arching extends only to \(5 \mathrm{~m}\) above the tunnel crown.

Admin
Solution

a. Terzaghi made the following assumptions based on experimental observations:

  • Sliding surfaces are assumed to be vertical (sections ae and bf of Fig. 12.14).
  • Pressure on the yielding strip is equal to the difference between the weight of the sand located above the strip and at the section (ab) and the shear resistance along the vertical sliding surfaces.

Figure \(12.14\) shows a section through the space between two vertical surfaces of sliding. The shearing resistance of the soil is determined as follows:

\(\tau=c^{\prime}+\sigma \tan \phi^{\prime}\)

The unit weight of the soil is \(\gamma_{\text {sat }}\), and the surface of the soil carries a uniform surcharge \(q\) per unit of area. The vertical stress on a horizontal section at any depth \(z\) below the surface is \(\sigma_{v}\), and the corresponding horizontal stress \(\left(\sigma_{h}\right)\) is

\(\sigma_{h}=\sigma_{v} \times K_{0}\)

where \(K_{0}\) is the earth pressure coefficient.

The weight of the slice with a thickness \(d z\) at a depth z below the surface is \(2 B \gamma d z\) per unit of length perpendicular to the plane of the drawing. The slice is acted upon by the forces indicated in Figure 12.14. The sum of the vertical components that act on the slice must be equal to zero for equilibrium as follows:

\(2 B \gamma d z=2 B\left(\sigma_{v}+d \sigma_{v}\right)-2 B \sigma_{v}+2 c^{\prime} d z+2 K_{0} \sigma_{v} \times d z \times \tan \phi^{\prime}\)

or

\(\frac{d \sigma_{v}}{d z}=\gamma-\frac{c^{\prime}}{B}-K_{0} \sigma_{v} \frac{\tan \phi^{\prime}}{B}\)

Considering \(\sigma_{v}=q\) for \(z=0\) and solving this equation, we can obtain

\(\sigma_{v}=\frac{B\left(\gamma-c^{\prime} / B\right)}{K \tan \phi^{\prime}}\left(1-e^{\left(-K \tan \phi^{\prime} z / B\right)}\right)+q e^{\left(-K \tan \phi^{\prime} z / B\right)}\)

Substituting \(B=B_{1}, q=\gamma D_{2}\) and \(z=D_{1}\) for application to square tunnels in sand \(\left(c^{\prime}=0\right)\) (Fig. 12.5) gives:

\(\sigma_{v}=\frac{B\left(\gamma-c^{\prime} / B\right)}{k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime}}\left(1-e^{\left(-k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime} z / B\right)}\right)+q e^{\left(-k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime} z/ B\right)}\)

This relationship can be simplified further for different cases, considering \(c^{\prime}=0\) and \(q=0\) as follows:

\(c^{\prime}>0\) and \(q=0: \sigma_{v}=\frac{B_{1}\left(\gamma-c^{\prime} / B_{1}\right)}{k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime}}\left(1-e^{\left(-k_{0} \tan \phi D_{1} / B_{1}\right)}\right)\)

\(c^{\prime}=0\) and \(q>0: \sigma_{v}=\frac{B_{1} \gamma}{k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime}}\left(1-e^{\left(-k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime} D_{1} / B_{1}\right)}\right)+q e^{\left(-k_{0} \tan \phi D_{1} / B_{1}\right)}\)

\(c^{\prime}=0\) and \(q=0: \sigma_{v}=\frac{B \gamma}{k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime}}\left(1-e^{\left(-k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime} D_{1} / B_{1}\right)}\right)\)

b. The relationship derived may be adopted to compute the vertical stress at the tunnel crown \(\left(c^{\prime}=0\right)\) :

\(\sigma_{v}=\frac{\gamma B_{1}}{k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime}}\left(1-e^{\left(-k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime} D_{1} / B_{1}\right)}\right)+\gamma D_{2} e^{\left(-k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime} D_{1} / B_{1}\right)}\)

Making a reasonable assumption of a square geometry for the tunnel, then,

\(\begin{aligned}&2 B_{1}=2\left[B_{0}+H \tan \left(45+\frac{\phi}{2}\right)\right] \\ &B_{1}=\left[2.5+2.5 \tan \left(45+\frac{35}{2}\right)\right]=7.3 \mathrm{~m}\end{aligned}\)

i. arching extends to the surface, then \(D_{2}=0\) and

\(\sigma_{v}=\frac{\gamma B_{1}}{k_{0} \tan \phi}\left(1-e^{\left(-k_{0} \tan \phi D_{1} / B_{1}\right)}\right)\) then

\(\sigma_{v}=\frac{21 \times 7.3}{0.5 \tan 35}\left(1-e^{(-0.5 \tan 35 15 / 7.3)}\right)=224.6 \mathrm{kPa}\)

ii. if aching extends only to \(5 \mathrm{~m}\) above the tunnel crown, then \(D_{1}=5 \mathrm{~m}\) and \(D_{2}=10 \mathrm{~m}\).

\(\begin{aligned}&\sigma_{v}=\frac{\gamma B_{1}}{k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime}}\left(1-e^{\left(-k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime} D_{1} / B_{1}\right)}\right)+\gamma D_{2} e^{\left(-k_{0} \tan \phi^{\prime} D_{1} / B_{1}\right)} \text { then } \\ &\sigma_{v}=\frac{21 \times 7.3}{0.5 \tan 35}\left(1-e^{(-0.5 \tan 35 5 / 7.3)}\right)+21 \times 10 \times e^{(-0.5 \tan 35 5 / 7.3)} \\ &\sigma_{v}=91.95+165.27=257.22 \mathrm{kPa}\end{aligned}\)
