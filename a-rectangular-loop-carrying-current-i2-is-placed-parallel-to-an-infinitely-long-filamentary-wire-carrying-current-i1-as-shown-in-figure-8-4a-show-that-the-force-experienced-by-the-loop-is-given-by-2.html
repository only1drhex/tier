Guest Question.

A rectangular loop carrying current \(I_{2}\) is placed parallel to an infinitely long filamentary wire carrying current \(I_{1}\) as shown in Figure 8.4(a). Show that the force experienced by the loop is given by

\(F_{\ell }=-\frac{\mu _{0}I_{1}I_{2}b}{2\pi } [\frac{1}{\rho _{0}}-\frac{1}{\rho _{0}+a} ]a_{\rho }N\)
Admin
Solution

Let the force on the loop be

\(F_{\ell }=F_{1}+F_{2}+F_{3}+F_{4}=I_{2}\oint\limits_{L}^{}{dl_{2}\times B_{1}}\)

where \(F_{1}, F_{2}, F_{3},\) and \(F_{4}\) are, respectively, the forces exerted on sides of the loop labeled 1, 2, 3, and 4 in Figure 8.4(b). Owing to the infinitely long wire
\(B_{1}=\frac{\mu _{0}I_{1}}{2\pi \rho _{0}}a_{\phi }\)
Hence,
\(F_{1}=I_{2}\oint\limits_{L}^{}{dl_{2}\times B_{1}}=I_{2}\int\limits_{z=0}^{b}{dz a_{z}\times \frac{\mu _{0}I_{1}}{2\pi \rho _{0}}a_{\phi }} \)
\(=-\frac{\mu _{0}I_{1}I_{2}b}{2\pi \rho _{0}}a_{\rho }\) (attractive)

\(F_{1}\) is attractive because it is directed toward the long wire; that is, \(F_{1}\) is along \(– a_{ρ}\) because loop side 1 and the long wire carry currents along the same direction. Similarly,
\(F_{3}=I_{2}\oint\limits_{L}^{}{dl_{2}\times B_{1}}=I_{2}\int\limits_{z=b}^{0}{dz a_{z}\times \frac{\mu _{0}I_{1}}{2\pi (\rho _{0}+a)}a_{\phi }}\)
\(=\frac{\mu _{0}I_{1}I_{2}b}{2\pi (\rho _{0}+a)}a_{\rho }\) (repulsive)
\(F_{2}=I_{2}\int\limits_{\rho =\rho _{0}}^{\rho _{0}+a}{d\rho a_{\rho }\times \frac{\mu _{0}I_{1}a_{\phi }}{2\pi \rho }}\)
\(=\frac{\mu _{0}I_{1}I_{2}}{2\pi }\ln \frac{\rho _{0}+a}{\rho _{0}}a_{z}\) (parallel)

\(F_{4}=I_{2}\int\limits_{\rho =\rho _{0}+a}^{\rho _{0}}{d\rho a_{\rho }\times \frac{\mu _{0}I_{1}a_{\phi }}{2\pi \rho }}\)
\(=-\frac{\mu _{0}I_{1}I_{2}}{2\pi }\ln \frac{\rho _{0}+a}{\rho _{0}}a_{z}\) (parallel)

The total force \(F_{\ell }\)on the loop is the sum of \(F_{1}, F_{2}, F_{3}\), and \(F_{4};\) that is,
\(F_{\ell }=\frac{\mu _{0}I_{1}I_{2}b}{2\pi } [\frac{1}{\rho _{0}}-\frac{1}{\rho _{0}+a} ](-a_{\rho })\)

which is an attractive force trying to draw the loop toward the wire. The force \(F_{w}\) on the wire, by Newton’s third law, is –\( F_{\ell }\); see Figure 8.4(b ).
