Guest Question.

An electricity-generating station needs to deliver energy at a rate of 20 MW to a city 1.0 km away.

(A) If the resistance of the wires is 2.0 Ω and the energy costs about 10¢/kWh, estimate what it costs the utility company for the energy converted to internal energy in the wires during one day. A common voltage for commercial power generators is 22 kV, but a step-up transformer is used to boost the voltage to 230 kV before transmission.

(B) Repeat the calculation for the situation in which the power plant delivers the energy at its original voltage of 22 kV.

Admin
Solution

Conceptualize by noting that the resistance of the wires is in series with the resistance representing the load (homes and businesses). Thus, there will be a voltage drop in the wires, which means that some of the transmitted energy is converted to internal energy in the wires and never reaches the load. Because this is an estimate, let us categorize this as a problem in which the power factor is equal to 1. To analyze the problem, we begin by calculating \( I_{ rms } \) from Equation 33.31:

\( P _{ av }=I_{ rms } \Delta V_{ rms } \cos \phi \) (33.31).

\( I_{ rms }=\frac{ P _{ av }}{\Delta V_{ rms }}=\frac{20 \times 10^{6} W }{230 \times 10^{3} V }=87 A \).

Now, we determine the rate at which energy is delivered to the resistance in the wires from Equation 33.32:

\( P _{ av }=I_{ rms }^{2} R \) (33.32).

\( P _{ av }=I_{ rms }^{2} R=(87 A )^{2}(2.0 \Omega)=15 kW \).

Over the course of a day, the energy loss due to the resistance of the wires is (15 kW)(24 h) = 360 kWh, at a cost of \( \$ 36 \).

(B) Repeat the calculation for the situation in which the power plant delivers the energy at its original voltage of 22 kV.

Again using Equation 33.31, we find

\( P _{ av }=I_{ rms } \Delta V_{ rms } \cos \phi \) (33.31).

\( I_{ rms }=\frac{ P _{ av }}{\Delta V_{ rms }}=\frac{20 \times 10^{6} W }{22 \times 10^{3} V }=910 A \).

and, from Equation 33.32,

\( P _{ av }=I_{ rms }^{2} R \) (33.32).

\( P _{ av }=I_{ rms }^{2} R=(910 A )^{2}(2.0 \Omega)=1.7 \times 10^{3} kW \).

\( \text { Cost per day }=\left(1.7 \times 10^{3} kW \right)(24 h )(\$ 0.10 / kWh ) \).

\( =\$ 4100 \).

To finalize the example, note the tremendous savings that are possible through the use of transformers and highvoltage transmission lines. This, in combination with the efficiency of using alternating current to operate motors, led to the universal adoption of alternating current instead of direct current for commercial power grids.
