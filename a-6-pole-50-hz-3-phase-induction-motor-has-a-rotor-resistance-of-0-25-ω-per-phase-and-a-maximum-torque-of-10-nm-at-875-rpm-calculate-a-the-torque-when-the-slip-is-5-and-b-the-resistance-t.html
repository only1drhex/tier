Guest Question.

A 6-pole, 50 Hz, 3-phase induction motor has a rotor resistance of \( 0.25 \Omega\) per phase and a maximum torque of 10 Nm at 875 rpm. Calculate (a) the torque when the slip is 5%, and (b) the resistance to be added to the rotor circuit to obtain 60% of the maximum torque at starting. Explain why two values are obtained for this resistance. Which value will be used? The stator impedance is assumed to be negligible.

Admin
Solution
\( n_{s}=\frac{120 \times 50}{6}=1000 rpm ; \quad \omega_{s}=\frac{2 \pi \times 1000}{60}=104.7 rad / s\)

 

\( s_{\max , T}=\frac{1000-875}{1000}=0.125\)

 

\( s_{\max , T}=\frac{R_{2}^{\prime}}{X_{2}^{\prime}}=\frac{R_{2}}{X_{2}}\)

 

\( X_{2}=\frac{R_{2}}{s_{\max , T}}=\frac{0.25}{0.125}=2 \Omega\)

 

\( T_{\max }=\frac{3}{\omega_{s}} \cdot\left(\frac{0.5 V^{2}}{X_{2}^{\prime}}\right)\)

Or \( 10=\frac{3}{104.7} \cdot\left(\frac{0.5 V^{2}}{X_{2}^{\prime}}\right)=\frac{3}{104.7} \cdot\left(\frac{0.5 V^{2} / a^{2}}{X_{2}}\right)\)

Or \( V^{\prime}=V / a=\sqrt{1396} V\)

(a) \( T=\frac{3}{\omega_{s}} \cdot \frac{V_{2}^{2}\left(R_{s}^{\prime} / s\right)}{\left(R_{2}^{\prime} / s\right)^{2}+X_{2}^{\prime 2}}=\frac{3}{\omega_{s}} \cdot \frac{V^{\prime 2}\left(R_{2} / s\right)}{\left(R_{2} / s\right)^{2}+X_{2}^{2}}\)

s = 0.05

\( T=\frac{3}{104.7} \cdot \frac{1396 \times 0.25 / 0.05}{(0.25 / 0.05)^{2}+(2)^{2}}=6.9 Nm\)

(b) From Eqs (9.30) \( T_{\max }=\frac{3}{\omega_{s}} \cdot\left(\frac{0.5 V^{2}}{X_{2}^{\prime}}\right)\) and (9.31) \( T_{ start }=\frac{3}{\omega_{s}} \cdot \frac{V^{2} R_{2}^{\prime}}{R_{2}^{\prime 2}+X_{2}^{\prime 2}}\)

\( \frac{T_{\text {start }}}{T_{\max }}=\frac{\left(R_{2}+R_{\text {ext }}\right)}{\left(R_{2}+R_{\text {ext }}\right)^{2}+X_{2}^{2}} \cdot \frac{X_{2}}{0.5}\)

 

\( 0.6=\left(\frac{R_{t}}{R_{t}^{2}+4}\right) \frac{2}{0.5}\)

Where \( R_{t}=R_{2}+R_{\text {ext }}\)

 

\( R_{t}^{2}-6.67 R_{t}+4=0\)

 

\( R_{t}=0.667,6.0 \Omega\)

 

\( R_{ ext }=R_{t}-r_{2}=0.417,5.75 \Omega\)

The former value will be used, i.e

\( R_{\text {ext }}=0.417 \Omega\)

It may be noted that \( R_{\text {ext }}=5.75 \Omega\) will correspond to \( T_{\max }\) lying in the region. \( s>1\) as shown in Fig. 9.16.
