Guest Question.

The water table in a granular soil is 3.5 m below the ground level. It is required to excavate the top 2.5 m. How deep would you drive the sheet pile providing a safety factor of 2 against passive resistance? The unit weights of the granular soil above and below the water table are 17 \(kN / m ^{3} \text { and } 20 kN / m ^{3}\) respectively, and the friction angle is 35°.

Admin
Solution
\(\phi^{\prime}=35^{\circ} \rightarrow K_{A}=0.271 \text { and } K_{P}=3.690\)

 

Let’s assume that the sheet pile has to be driven to a depth of x below the water table as shown in the figure. At any depth, the active earth pressure and passive earth pressure in a granular soil are given by:

 

\(\sigma_{h a}^{\prime}=K_{A} \sigma_{v}^{\prime} \text { and } \sigma_{h p}^{\prime}=K_{P} \sigma_{v}^{\prime}\)

 

\(\text { where } K_{P} \text { will be replaced by } K_{P} / F \text {, with } F=2 \text {. }\)

 

\(\text { The values of } \sigma_{h a}^{\prime} \text { and } \sigma_{h p}^{\prime} \text { thus are computed as follows. }\)

 

\(\text { On the right (active) side, at the water table, } \sigma_{v}^{\prime}=3.5 \times 17=59.5 kPa\)

 

\(\therefore \sigma_{h a}^{\prime}=K_{A} \sigma_{v}^{\prime}=0.271 \times 59.5=16.12 kPa\)

 

\(\text { On the left (passive) side, at the water table, } \sigma_{v}^{\prime}=1.0 \times 17=17.0 kPa\)

 

\(\therefore \sigma_{h p}^{\prime}=K_{p} \sigma_{v}^{\prime} / F=3.690 \times 17.0 / 2=31.37 kPa\)

 

On the left (passive) side, at the bottom of the sheet pile,

 

\(\sigma_{v}^{\prime}=1.0 \times 17+x \times(20-9.81)=17.0+10.19 x kPa\)

 

\(\therefore \sigma_{h p}^{\prime}=K_{P} \sigma_{v}^{\prime} / F=31.37+18.80 x kPa\)

 

The lateral pressure diagram is divided into six triangular and rectangular blocks as shown in the figure on page 411. The horizontal load contribution from each block, the height of its location above the base, and the moment about O are summarized below. The pore water pressure acts equally on both sides, and hence is not considered in the analysis.

 

Block Force (kN) per m Height (m) above O Moment (kN-m) per m
1 \(0.5 \times 16.12 \times 3.5=28.21\) \(x+1.17\) \(28.21 x+32.92\)
2 \(16.12 \times x=16.12 x\) \(0.5 x\) \(8.06 x^{2}\)
3 \(0.5 \times 2.76 x \times x=1.38 x^{2}\) \(0.33 x\) \(0.46 x^{3}\)
4 \(0.5 \times 31.37 \times 1=15.69\) \(x+0.333\) \(15.69 x+5.23\)
5 \(31.37 \times x=31.37 x\) \(0.5 x\) \(15.69 x^{2}\)
6 \(0.5 \times 18.80 x \times x=9.4 x^{2}\) \(0.33 x\) \(3.13 x^{3}\)

 

Taking moments about O:

 

\(\begin{array}{c}28.21 x+32.92+8.06 x^{2}+0.46 x^{3}-15.69 x-5.23-15.69 x^{2}-3.13 x^{3}=0 \\2.67 x^{3}+7.63 x^{2}-12.52 x-27.69=0\end{array}\)

 

Solving the above equation by trial and error, x = 2.02 m.

\(\therefore \text { The }\) sheet pile has to be driven to 5.5 m to provide a safety factor of 2 on passive resistance.
