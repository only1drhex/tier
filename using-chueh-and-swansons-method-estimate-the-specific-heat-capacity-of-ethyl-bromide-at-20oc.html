Guest Question.

Using Chueh and Swanson’s method, estimate the specific heat capacity of ethyl bromide at \(20^{\circ} C\).

Admin
Solution
\(\text { Ethyl bromide } CH _{3} CH _{2} Br\)

 

Group Contribution No. of
\(– CH _{3}\) 36.84 1 = 36.84
\(– CH _{2}-\) 30.4 1 = 30.40
\(– Br\) 37.68 1 = 37.68
Total 104.92 \(kJ / kmol ^{\circ} C\)

 

Add 18.84 for any carbon group which fulfils the following criterion: a carbon group which is joined by a single bond to a carbon group connected by a double or triple bond with a third carbon group. In some cases a carbon group fulfils the above criterion in more ways than one; 18.84 should be added each time the group fulfils the criterion.

Exceptions to the above 18.84 rule:

1. No such extra 18.84 additions for \(– CH _{3}\) groups.

2. For a \(– CH _{2}-\) group fulfilling the 18.84 addition criterion add 10.47 instead of 18.84. However, when the \(– CH _{2}-\) group fulfils the addition criterion in more ways than one, the addition should be 10.47 the first time and 18.84 for each subsequent addition.

3. No such extra addition for any carbon group in a ring.

mol. wt. = 109

 

\(\text { Specific heat capacity }=\frac{104.92}{100}=0.96 kJ / kg ^{\circ} C\)

 

Experimental value 0.90 \(kJ / kg ^{\circ} C\)
