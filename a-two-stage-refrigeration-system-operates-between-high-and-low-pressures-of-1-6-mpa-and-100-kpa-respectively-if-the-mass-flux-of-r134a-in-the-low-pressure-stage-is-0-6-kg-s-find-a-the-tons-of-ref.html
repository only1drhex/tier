Guest Question.

A two-stage refrigeration system operates between high and low pressures of 1.6 MPa and 100 kPa, respectively. If the mass flux of R134a in the low-pressure stage is 0.6 kg/s, find (a) the tons of refrigeration, (b) the coefficient of performance, and (c) the mass flux of cooling water used to cool the R134a in the condenser if \( \Delta T_{w}=15^{\circ} C \).

Admin
Solution

\( \text { The intermediate pressure is } P_{i}=\left(P_{H} P_{L}\right)^{1 / 2}=[(1.6)(0.1)]^{1 / 2}=0.4 MPa \text {. Referring to Fig. } 10.5 \text {, the R134a tables } \) provide us with

\( h_{1}=231.35 kJ / kg \quad h_{5}=252.32 kJ / kg \quad h_{7}=h_{8}=134.2 kJ / kg \).

\( h_{3}=h_{4}=62.0 kJ / kg \quad s_{1}=0.9395 kJ / kg \cdot K \quad s_{5}=0.9145 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

Assuming the compressors to be isentropic, the enthalpies of states 2 and 6 are found by extrapolation as follows:

\( \left.\begin{array}{l} s_{2}=s_{1}=0.9395 kJ / kg \cdot K \\ P_{2}=0.4 MPa \end{array}\right\} h_{2}=259.5 kJ / kg \).

\( \left.\begin{array}{l} s_{6}=s_{5}=0.9145 kJ / kg \cdot K \\ P_{2}=1.6 MPa \end{array}\right\} h_{6}=280.8 kJ / kg \).

The mass flux of the R134a in the high-pressure stage is

\( \dot{m}_{H}=\dot{m}_{L} \frac{h_{2}-h_{3}}{h_{5}-h_{8}}=(0.6)\left(\frac{259.5-62.0}{252.32-134.02}\right)=1.00 kg / s \).

\( \text { (a) } \dot{Q}_{E}=\dot{m}_{L}\left(h_{1}-h_{4}\right)=(0.6)(231.35-62.0)=101.6 kW =28.9 \text { tons } \).

\( \text { (b) } \quad \dot{W}_{m}=\dot{m}_{L}\left(h_{2}-h_{1}\right)+\dot{m}_{H}\left(h_{6}-h_{5}\right)=(0.6)(259.5-231.35)+(1.0)(280.8-252.3)=45.4 kW \).

\( \operatorname{COP}_{R}=\frac{\dot{Q}_{E}}{\dot{W}_{m}}=\frac{101.6}{45.4}=2.24 \).

(c) Cooling water is used to cool the R134a in the condenser. As energy balance on the condenser provides

\( \dot{m}_{w} C_{p} \Delta T_{w}=\dot{m}_{H}\left(h_{6}-h_{7}\right) \quad \dot{m}_{w}=\frac{(1.00)(280.8-134.02)}{(4.18)(15)}=2.34 kg / s \).
