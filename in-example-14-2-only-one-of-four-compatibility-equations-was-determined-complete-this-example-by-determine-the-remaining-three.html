Guest Question.

In example 14.2 only one of four compatibility equations was determined. Complete this example by determine the remaining three.

Admin
Solution

The complete left eigenvector is

 

\(L =\left[\begin{array}{cccc}\rho u & 0 & \frac{-1}{\sqrt{\left(u^{2}+v^{2}\right)-a^{2}}} & \frac{1}{\sqrt{\left(u^{2}+v^{2}\right)-a^{2}}} \\\rho v & 0 & \frac{u / v}{\sqrt{\left(u^{2}+v^{2}\right)-a^{2}}} & \frac{-u / v}{\sqrt{\left(u^{2}+v^{2}\right)-a^{2}}} \\1 & -1 / a^{2} & 1 / \rho v a & 1 / \rho v a \\0 & 1 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]\)

 

The compatibility equations are established by application of

 

\(l _{\lambda i }^{ T } d w =0\)

 

for each left eigenvector corresponding to a particular eigenvalue.

Along the characteristic given by \(dy / dy =\lambda_{2}\).

 

\(l _{\lambda 2}^{ T } d w =\left[\begin{array}{llll} l _{1}^{2} & l_{2}^{2} & l_{3}^{2} & l_{4}^{2}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} du \\dv \\dp \\d \rho\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ccc}0 & 0 & \frac{-1}{ a ^{2}} & 1\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} du \\dv \\dp \\d \rho\end{array}\right]=-\frac{ dp }{ a ^{2}}+ d \rho=0\)

 

So we obtain the speed sound expression

 

\(\frac{ dp }{ d \rho}= a ^{2}\)

 

Along the characteristics given by \(dy / dy =\lambda_{3} \text { and } \lambda_{4}\) we have

 

\(l _{\lambda 3,4}^{ T } d w =\left[\begin{array}{llll}l_{1}^{3,4} & l_{2}^{3,4} & l_{3}^{3,4} & l_{4}^{3,4}\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} du \\dv \\dp \\d \rho\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{llll}\frac{\mp 1}{ a \beta} & \frac{\pm u / v }{ a \beta} & \frac{1}{\rho va } & 0\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} du \\dv \\dp \\d \rho\end{array}\right]=0\)

 

where \(\beta=\sqrt{ M ^{2}-1}=\sqrt{\left( u ^{2}+ v ^{2}\right) / a ^{2}-1}\). Expanding and canceling terms yields

 

\(\mp vdu \pm udv +\frac{\beta}{\rho} dp =0\)

 

Now according to Bernoulli’s equation

 

\(\frac{d p}{\rho}=-(u d u+v d v)\)

 

Uniting the expressions and rearranging produces

 

\(\frac{ dv }{ du }=\frac{ v \pm \beta u }{ u \mp \beta v }\)

 

Multiply both numerator and denominator by u ± βv. The numerator simplifies as follows

 

\(( v \pm \beta u )( u \pm \beta v )= uv \pm \beta u ^{2} \pm \beta v ^{2}+ uv \beta^{2}\)

 

\(=u v\left(1+\beta^{2}\right) \pm \beta\left(u^{2}+v^{2}\right)=\frac{\left(u^{2}+v^{2}\right)}{a^{2}}\left[u v \pm \sqrt{a^{2}\left(u^{2}+v^{2}\right)-a^{4}}\right]\)

 

Whereas the denominator simplifies as follows

 

\(( u \mp \beta u )( u \pm \beta v )= u ^{2}-\beta^{2} v ^{2}= u ^{2}-\frac{\left( u ^{2}+ v ^{2}\right)}{ a ^{2}} v ^{2}+ v ^{2}=\frac{\left( u ^{2}+ v ^{2}\right)}{ a ^{2}}\left( a ^{2}- v ^{2}\right)\)

 

Hence,

 

\(\frac{d v}{d u}=\frac{u v \pm \sqrt{a^{2}\left(u^{2}+v^{2}\right)-a^{4}}}{a^{2}-v^{2}}\)
