Guest Question.

The Weibull is used extensively for expressing the reliability of rolling-contact bearings (see Sec. 11–4). Here, the variate x is put in dimensionless form as \(x = L/L_{10}\) where L is bearing life, in say, number of cycles; and \(L_{10}\) is the manufacturer’s rated life of the bearing where 10 percent of the bearings have failed (90 percent reliability).
Construct the distributional properties of a 02–30 mm deep-groove ball bearing if the Weibull parameters are \(x_{0} = 0.0200, θ = 4.459, and b = 1.483\). Find the mean, median, \(L_{90}\), and standard deviation.

Admin
Solution

From Eq. (20–28) the mean dimensionless life is

\(μ_{x }= x_{0} + (θ − x_{0})Γ(1 + 1/b)\)
\(= 0.0200 + (4.459 − 0.0200)Γ(1 + 1/1.483) = 4.033\)

This says that the average bearing life is 4.033 \(L_{10}\). The median dimensionless life corresponds to R = 0.5, or \(L_{50}\), and from Eq. (20–26) is

\(x = x_{0} + (θ − x_{0})\left(ln \frac{1}{R}\right)^{1/b}\) (20-26)

\(x_{0.5} = x_{0} + (θ − x_{0})\left(ln \frac{1}{0.5}\right)^{1/b}= 0.0200 + (4.459 − 0.0200)\left(ln \frac{1}{0.5}\right)^{1/1.483}\)
= 3.487

For \(L_{90}\), R = 0.1, the dimensionless life x is

\(x_{0.90} = 0.0200 + (4.459 − 0.0200) \left(ln \frac{1}{0.1}\right)^{1/1.483}= 7.810\)

The standard deviation of the dimensionless life is given by Eq. (20–29):

\( \hat{σ}_{x} = (θ − x_{0})\sqrt{Γ(1 + 2/b) −Γ^{2}(1 + 1/b)}\)
\(= (4.459 − 0.0200) \sqrt{Γ(1 + 2/1.483) − Γ^{2}(1 + 1/1.483)} = 2.753\)
