Guest Question.

Use the energy method to determine the buckling load of the column shown in Fig. 21.20.

Admin
Solution

An approximate shape may be deduced from the deflected shape of a cantilever loaded at its free end. Thus, from Eq. (iv) of Ex. 13.1

\(v=-\frac{W}{6 E I}\left(3 L x^{2}-x^{3}\right)\) Eq. (iv) of Ex. 13.1

\(v=\frac{v_{0} x^{2}}{2 L^{3}}(3 L-x)\)

This expression satisfies the end conditions of deflection, viz. υ = 0 at x = 0 and \(v=v_{0}\) at x = L.

In addition, it satisfies the conditions that the slope of the column is zero at the built-in end and that the bending moment, i.e. \(d ^{2} v / d x^{2}\), is zero at the free end. The bending moment at any section is \(M=P_{ CR }\left(v_{0}-v\right)\) so that substitution for M and υ in Eq. (21.66)

\(U+V=\int_{0}^{L} \frac{M^{2}}{2 E I} d x-\frac{P_{ CR }}{2} \int_{0}^{L}\left(\frac{ d v}{ d x}\right)^{2} d x\) (21.66)

gives

\(U+V=\frac{P_{ CR }^{2} v_{0}^{2}}{2 E I} \int_{0}^{L}\left(1-\frac{3 x^{2}}{2 L^{2}}+\frac{x^{3}}{2 L^{3}}\right)^{2} d x-\frac{P_{ CR }}{2} \int_{0}^{L}\left(\frac{3 v_{0}}{2 L^{3}}\right)^{2} x^{2}(2 L-x)^{2} d x\)

Integrating and substituting the limits we have

\(U+V=\frac{17}{35} \frac{P_{ CR }^{2} v_{0}^{2} L}{2 E I}-\frac{3}{5} P_{ CR } \frac{v_{0}^{2}}{L}\)

Hence

\(\frac{\partial(U+V)}{\partial v_{0}}=\frac{17}{35} \frac{P_{ CR }^{2} v_{0} L}{E I}-\frac{6 P_{ CR } v_{0}}{5 L}=0\)

from which

\(P_{ CR }=\frac{42 E I}{17 L^{2}}=2.471 \frac{E I}{L^{2}}\)

This value of critical load compares with the exact value (see Eq.(21.15))

\(P_{ CR }=\frac{\pi^{2} E I}{4 L^{2}}\) (21.15)

of \(\pi^{2} E I / 4 L^{2}=2.467 E I / L^{2}\); the error, in this case, is seen to be extremely small. Approximate values of critical load obtained by the energy method are always greater than the correct values. The explanation lies in the fact that an assumed deflected shape implies the application of constraints in order to force the column to take up an artificial shape. This, as we have seen, has the effect of stiffening the column with a consequent increase in critical load.
