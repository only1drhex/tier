Guest Question.

Designing an ECL OR/NOR gate

(a) Design the ECL OR/NOR gate of the circuit in Fig. 15.50. It has two inputs. The desired collector currents are \( I_{ C1 }=3 mA \text { and } I_{ C 4}=I_{ C 5}=3 mA \text {. At logic high, } V_{ OR }=-0.7 V , V_{ NOR }=-1.63 V , \text { and } v_{ A }\left(\text { or } v_{ B }\right)=V_{ OR } = -0.7 V\). Assume \( V_{ BE }=0.7 V , V_{ EE }=-5.2 V , V_{ BE }=0.7 V , \text { and } \beta_{ F }=100 \).

(b) Calculate the maximum fan-out with similar ECL gates if \( V_{\text {OR }} \) is allowed to fall from \( V_{ OR (\max )}=-0.7 V \) to \( V_{ OR (\min )}=-0.75 V \).
(c) Use PSpice/SPICE to check your design by plotting the transfer function. Calculate \( NM _{ L } \text { and } NM _{ H } \).
Use PSpice/SPICE to find \( P_{\text {static }} \text { if } v_{ A }=v_{ B }=-0.7 V \).

Admin
Solution

(a) Assuming \( v_{ A }\left(\text { or } v_{ B }\right)=V_{ OR }=-0.7 V \), the steps to complete the design are as follows:

Step 1. Calculate \( V_{ CC }, v_{ A }, \text { and } V_{ E } \). The value of \( V_{ CC } \) is

\( V_{ CC }=V_{ OR }+0.7=-0.7+0.7=0 \)

The input voltage for logic high is

\( v_{ A }=V_{ OH }=V_{ CC }-0.7=0-0.7=-0.7 V \)

The emitter voltage is

\( V_{ E }=v_{ A }-V_{ BE 1}=-0.7-0.7=-1.4 V \)

Step 2. Calculate the emitter resistance \( R_{ E } \):

\( R_{ E }=\frac{V_{ E }-V_{ EE }}{I_{ E } \simeq I_{ C }}=\frac{-1.4 V -(-5.2 V )}{3 mA }=\frac{(-1.4+5.2) V }{3 mA }=1.27 k \Omega \)

Step 3. Calculate the voltage \( v_{ O 1} \), the collector resistance \( R_{ C1 }, R_{4}, \text { and } R_{5} \):

\(\begin{aligned}&v_{ O 1}=V_{ NOR }+V_{ BE 5}=-1.63+0.7=-0.93 V \\&R_{ C 1}=\frac{V_{ CC }-v_{ O 1}}{I_{ C 1}}=\frac{V_{ CC }-v_{ O 1}}{I_{ E }}=\frac{0-(-0.93 V )}{3 mA }=\frac{0.93 V }{3 mA }=310 \Omega \\&R_{4}=\frac{V_{ OR }-V_{ EE }}{I_{ E 4} \simeq I_{ C 3}}=\frac{-0.7 V -(-5.2 V )}{3 mA }=1.5 k \Omega \\&R_{5}=\frac{V_{ NOR }-V_{ EE }}{I_{ E 5} \simeq I_{ C 3}}=\frac{-1.63 V -(-5.2 V )}{3 mA }=1.19 k \Omega\end{aligned}\)

Step 4. Since the input voltages \( v_{ A } \text { and } v_{ B } \text { are greater than } V_{\text {ref }} \) when the circuit is in the logic high state and less than \( V_{ ref } \) when it is in the logic low state, set \( V_{ ref } \) at the midpoint between the logic low and logic high levels and calculate the voltage at the base of \( Q _{6} \):

\(\begin{aligned}&V_{ ref }=\frac{V_{ NOR }+V_{ OR }}{2}=\frac{-1.63-0.7}{2}=-1.165 V \\&V_{ B 6}=V_{ ref }+V_{ BE 6}=-1.165+0.7=-0.465 V\end{aligned}\)

Step 5. Calculate the values of \( R_{1}, R_{2}, \text { and } I_{1} \). Using KVL, we can write

\(\begin{aligned}I_{1}\left(R_{1}+R_{2}\right) &=-V_{ EE }-2 V_{ D } \\I_{1} R_{1} &=-V_{ B 6}\end{aligned}\)

which gives

\(\begin{aligned}& \frac{I_{1}\left(R_{1}+R_{2}\right)}{I_{1} R_{1}}=\frac{-V_{ EE }-2 V_{ D }}{-V_{ B 6}} \\\text { or } \quad & 1+\frac{R_{2}}{R_{1}}=\frac{-V_{ EE }-2 V_{ D }}{-V_{ B 6}}=\frac{-(-5.2)-2 \times 0.7}{-(-0.465)}=8.172\end{aligned}\)

Choose \( R_{1}=300 \Omega \). Then

\( R_{2}=(8.172-1) R_{1}=(8.172-1) \times 300=2.15 k \Omega \)

Calculate the value of \( I_{1} \) as

\( I_{1}=I_{2}=-\frac{V_{ B 6}}{R_{1}}=-\frac{-0.465}{300}=1.55 mA \)

Step 6. Calculate the value of \( R_{3} \). For good temperature compensation, the current through the emitter of \( Q_{6} \) should be the same as the current through diodes \( D _{1} \text { and } D _{2} \); that is,

\( I_{3}=I_{2}=\frac{V_{ ref }-V_{ BE }}{R_{3}}=\frac{-1.165-(-5.2)}{R_{3}}=1.55 mA \)

or \( R_{3}=2.6 k \Omega .\)

Step 7. Choose \( R_{ C 2} \) to be slightly more than \( R_{ C 1} \) (approximately 3% more for resistors of 1% tolerance)â€” that is, 2% more than the tolerance value. Thus,

\( R_{ C 2}=1.03 \times R_{ C 1}=1.03 \times 310=319.3 \Omega \)

Let \( R_{ C 2}=320 \Omega.\)

(b) The fan-out can be determined from Fig. 15.51, which shows the emitter-follower output stage of an ECL circuit driving a difference amplifier input stage of an ECL load. This circuit is shown for \( V_{ OR } \) at the logic high level. The load transistor \( Q _{1} \) is on, and the load emitter current is given by

\(\begin{aligned}I_{ E } &=\frac{V_{ OR (\max )}-V_{ BE }-V_{ EE }}{R_{ E }} \\&=\frac{-0.7 V -0.7 V -(-5.2 V )}{1.27 k \Omega}=2.99 mA\end{aligned}\)

The input base current for each fan-out is given by

\( I_{ N 1}=\frac{I_{ E }}{1+\beta_{ F }}=\frac{2.99 mA }{1+100}=29.6 \mu A \)

Thus, the total load current is

\( I_{ L 1}=N I_{ N 1}=N \times 29.6 \mu A \)

The emitter current of \( Q_{4} \) is

\( I_{ E 4}=\frac{V_{ OR (\min )}-V_{ EE }}{R_{4}}=\frac{-0.75 V -(-5.2 V )}{1.5 k \Omega}=2.97 mA \)

The base current \( I_{ B 4} \) required to supply the load current \( I_{ L } \text { and the current } I_{ E 4} \) is

\( I_{ B 4}=\frac{I_{ E 4}+I_{ L }}{1+\beta_{ F }}=\frac{V_{ CC }-V_{ BE 4}-V_{ OR ( min )}}{R_{ C 2}} \)

which gives

\( \frac{2.97 mA +(N \times 29.6 \mu A )}{1+100}=\frac{0-0.7-(-0.75)}{320} \)

 

or N = 432.

(c) The PSpice schematic is shown in Fig. 15.52. The PSpice plots shown in Fig. 15.53 give \( V_{ OH }=-0.81 V \) (expected value is -0.7 V), \( V_{ IL }=0.95 V \text { at } v_{ O }=-0.855 V , V_{ OL }=-1.59 V \text { at } V_{ I }=2 V , \text { and } V_{ IH }=1.33 V \) at \( V_{ O }=-1.55 V \). Note that V(11) and V(12) are the voltages at the OR and NOR terminals, respectively.

\(\begin{aligned}& NM _{ L }=V_{ IL }-\left|V_{ OL }\right|=0.95-1.59=-0.64 V \\& NM _{ H }=\left|V_{ OH }\right|-V_{ IH }=0.81-1.33=-0.52 V\end{aligned}\)

 

\( t_{ pHL }=1.05 ns , t_{ pLH }=1.22 ns , \text { and } t_{ pd }=1.13 ns . \text { The PSpice output file gives } P_{\text {static }}=62 mW \)
