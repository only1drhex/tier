Guest Question.

A doubly symmetrical I-section beam is reinforced by a flat plate attached to the upper flange as shown in Fig. P.10.3. If the resulting compound beam is subjected to a vertical shear load of 200 kN, determine the distribution of shear stress in the portion of the cross section that extends from the top of the plate to the neutral axis. Calculate also the shear force per unit length of beam resisted by the shear connection between the plate and the flange of the I-section beam.

Admin
Solution

The area of cross section of the reinforced beam is given by

\( A=400 \times 40+2 \times 200 \times 30+25 \times 540=41500 mm ^{2} \)

Referring to Fig. S.10.3(a) and taking moments of areas about the top edge of the plate

\( 41500 \bar{y}=400 \times 40 \times 20+200 \times 30 \times 55+25 \times 540 \times 340+200 \times 30 \times 625 \)

i.e.

\( \bar{y}=216.6 mm \)

Then

\(\begin{aligned}I_{z}=& \frac{400 \times 40^{3}}{12}+400 \times 40 \times 196.6^{2}+\left(\frac{200 \times 600^{3}}{12}-\frac{175 \times 540^{3}}{12}\right) \\&+(200 \times 600-175 \times 540) \times 123.4^{2}\end{aligned}\)

i.e.

\( I_{z}=2.31 \times 10^{9} mm ^{4} \)

In the plate, \( A^{\prime}=400(216.6-y), \bar{y}=(216.6+y) / 2, b_{0}=400 mm \). Then from Eq. (10.4)

\(\tau=-\frac{S_{y} A^{\prime} \bar{y}}{b_{0} I_{z}} \) (10.4)

 

\( \tau=4.33 \times 10^{-5}\left(216.6^{2}-y^{2}\right) \)

When \( y=216.6 mm , \tau=0 \text { and when } y=176.6 mm , \tau=0.68 N / mm ^{2}\).
In the flange of the I-beam, \( A^{\prime} \bar{y}=[400 \times 40 \times 196.6+200(176.6-y)(176.6+y) / 2] \text { and } b_{0}=200 mm \). Substituting in Eq. (10.4)

\( \tau=4.33 \times 10^{-7}\left(6.26 \times 10^{6}-100 y^{2}\right) \)

When \( y=176.6 mm , \tau=1.36 N / mm ^{2} \text { and when } y=146.6 mm , \tau=1.78 N / mm ^{2} \). In the web of the I-beam, \( A^{\prime} \bar{y}=[400 \times 40 \times 196.6+200 \times 30 \times 161.6+25(146.6-y) (146.6+y) / 2] \text { and } b_{0}=25 mm \).

Substituting in Eq. (10.4)

\( \tau=3.46 \times 10^{-6}\left(4.38 \times 10^{6}-12.5 y^{2}\right) \)

When \( y=146.6 mm , \tau=14.22 N / mm ^{2} \text { and when } y=0, \tau=15.15 N / mm ^{2} \) .
The required distribution is shown in Fig. S.10.3(b).
The shear stress at the top of the flange is 1.36 N/mm² so that the shear force/unit length of beam is \( 1.36 \times 200=272 N / mm \text { or } 272 kN / m \).
