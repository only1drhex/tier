Guest Question.

Find the control law that minimizes the performance index \(J=\int_{0}^{\infty }{(x_1^2+x_2^2+u^2)dt} \)

for the system

\(\left[\begin{matrix} \dot{x_1} \\ \dot{x_2} \end{matrix} \right]=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\left[\begin{matrix} {x_1} \\ {x_2} \end{matrix} \right] +\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right][u] \)
Admin
Solution

First we need to determine the weighting matrices Q and R. The performance index is expressed as

\(J=\int_{0}^{\infty }{(x^TQx+\eta ^TR\eta )dt}\)

The first part of the integrand can be expanded in the following manner.

\(x^TQx=\left[\begin{matrix} x_1 & x_2 \end{matrix} \right]\begin{bmatrix} Q_{11} & Q_{12} \\ Q_{21} & Q_{22} \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} {x_1} \\ {x_2} \end{matrix} \right] \)

 

\(=x_1^2Q_{11}+x_2x_1Q_{21}+x_2x_1Q_{12}+x_2^2Q_{22}\)

But that part of the integrand related to the state variables is given as \(x_1^2+x_2^2\) therefore \(Q_{11}=Q_{22}=1 and Q_{12}=Q_{21}=0\). In a similar manner one can show that \(R=[1]\). The weighting matrices Q and R follow:

\(Q=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \)

 

\(R=[1]\)

The reduced Riccati equations can be rearranged as follows:

\(A^TS+SA-SBR^{-1}B^TS+Q=0\)

We will perform the indicated matrix operations for first two terms in the Riccati equation:

\(A^TS=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} S_{11} & S_{12} \\ S_{21} & S_{22} \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ S_{11} & S_{12} \end{bmatrix}\)

 

\(SA=\begin{bmatrix} S_{11} & S_{12} \\ S_{21} & S_{22} \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & S_{11} \\ 0 & S_{21} \end{bmatrix}\)

The third term \(SBR^{-1}B^TS\) will be calculated in step

\(SBR^{-1}B^TS=\begin{bmatrix} S_{11} & S_{12} \\ S_{21} & S_{22} \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}R^{-1}B^TS\)

 

\(=\begin{bmatrix} S_{12} \\ S_{22} \end{bmatrix}R^{-1}B^TS\)

 

\(=\begin{bmatrix} S_{12} \\ S_{22} \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}S\)

 

\(=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & S_{12}\\ 0 & S_{22} \end{bmatrix}S\)

 

\(=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & S_{12}\\ 0 & S_{22} \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} S_{11} & S_{12} \\ S_{21} & S_{22} \end{bmatrix}\)

 

\(=\begin{bmatrix} S_{12} S_{21}& S_{12} S_{22} \\ S_{22} S_{21} & S^2_{22} \end{bmatrix}\)

 

Note that in this problem \(R^{-1}=1\) Substituting matrices into the Riccati equation yields

\( \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ S_{11} & S_{12} \end{bmatrix}+\begin{bmatrix} 0 & S_{11} \\ 0 & S_{21} \end{bmatrix}-\begin{bmatrix} S_{12} S_{21}& S_{12} S_{22} \\ S_{22} S_{21} & S^2_{22} \end{bmatrix} +\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\)

Combining the matrices yields

\(\begin{bmatrix} -S_{12}S_{21}+1 & S_{11}-S_{12} S_{22}\\ S_{11}-S_{22} S_{21} & S_{12} + S_{21}-S^2_{22}+1\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\)

We now must solve the nonlinear algebraic equations for the unknown Riccati gains:

\(-S_{12}S_{21}+1=0\)

 

\(S_{11}-S_{12} S_{22}=0\)

 

\(S_{12} + S_{21}-S^2_{22}+1=0\)

From symmetry

\(S_{12}=S_{21}\)

 

 

\(-S^2_{12}+1=0\Rightarrow S_{12}=\pm \sqrt{1}= \pm1 \)

 

\(2S_{12}-S^2_{22}+1=0\)

For \(S_{12}=1\),

\(-S^2_{22}+3=0\Rightarrow S_{22}=\pm \sqrt{3} \)

 

\(S_{11}-S_{12} S_{22}=0 \Rightarrow S_{11}=\pm \sqrt{3}\)

The Riccati matrix S follows:

\(S=\begin{bmatrix} \sqrt{3} & 1 \\ 1 & \sqrt{3} \end{bmatrix} \)

he control law \(\eta =-K^Tx\) where

\(K=R^{-1}B^T\)

 

\(K=[1]\left[\begin{matrix} 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right] \begin{bmatrix} \sqrt{3} & 1 \\ 1 & \sqrt{3} \end{bmatrix}=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & \sqrt{3} \end{matrix} \right] \)

The control law can now be written

\(\eta =-K^Tx=-\left[\begin{matrix} 1 & \sqrt{3} \end{matrix} \right]\left[\begin{matrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{matrix} \right] \)

 

\(-x_1-\sqrt{3}x_2 \)

For a higher-order system numerical techniques are required. The MATLAB control system toolbox has numerical algorithms for solving optimal control problems.
