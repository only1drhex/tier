Guest Question.

For the circuit shown below, find the required MOV that will protect the IGBT from an overvoltage failure due to the series inductance of 1 mH. The circuit conditions are (1) \(V_{in}\) = 900 V dc and (2) input peak current during the overvoltage is 200 A.

Admin
Solution

To find the voltage rating of the MOV, a 20% tolerance is allowed to account for voltage swell and power supply tolerances. Therefore, 900 V dc × 1.2 = 1080 V dc will be the rated operating voltage of the required MOV.

The maximum energy that will be stored in the series inductance is given by:

\(W_{(stored)max}\) = energy =\(\frac{1}{2}LI^2 =\frac{1}{2}(1 \times 10^{-3}) (200)^2 = 20 J\) (10.69)

Therefore, the required MOV must have a rated operating continuous voltage of 1080 V, must be able to dissipate 20 J of energy per current surge pulse and to be able to withstand the circuit current of 200 A. The MOV type V1000LA80AP (VMDC = 1200 V and WTM = 220 J) satisfies these requirements. Fig. 10.110 shows the technical data sheets characteristics of the selected MOV.

As can be seen from Fig. 10.110(a), when the peak current of the selected MOV is 200 A the maximum clamping voltage \(V_c\) is approximately 3200 V which will appear across the IGBT when the IGBT is turned off or during a discontinuity fault. Therefore, for this application the rated voltage of the IGBT (i.e., \(V_{CE(max)}\)) must be higher than 3200 V (e.g., 3500 V). Fig. 10.111 shows the waveforms of the IGBT and MOV. The energy that will be absorbed by each MOV is given by:

\(W_{MOV}\) = energy absorbed by one MOV = \(W_{(stored)max} = kV_cI_{pk}t_p\) (10.70)

where k ¼ 1 (when the current impulse is a square wave); k = 0.5 (when the current impulse is a triangular wave); \(V_c\) = clamping voltage; \(I_{PK}\) = peak current; \(t_p\) = current impulse duration time.

Therefore, using Eqs. (10.69) and (10.70) and knowing that the current through the MOV is triangular, the current impulse duration time is found to be:

\(t_p = \frac{W_{MOV}}{KV_cI_{pk}}=\frac{20}{(0.5)(3200)(200)} = 62.6 \mu s\)

Next, using Fig. 10.110(b) for \(t_p = 62.6 \mu s\) and current peak surge value of 200 A, a rate repetition of 1000 is found. This means that the MOV can withstand 1000 current surge events before replacement.
