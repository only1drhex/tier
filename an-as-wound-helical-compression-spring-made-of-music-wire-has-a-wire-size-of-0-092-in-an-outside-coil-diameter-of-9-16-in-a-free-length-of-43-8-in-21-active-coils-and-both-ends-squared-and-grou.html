Guest Question.

An as-wound helical compression spring, made of music wire, has a wire size of 0.092 in, an outside coil diameter of \(\frac{9}{16} \) in, a free length of 4\(\frac{3}{8}\) in, 21 active coils, and both ends squared and ground. The spring is unpeened. This spring is to be assembled with a preload of 5 lbf and will operate with a maximum load of 35 lbf during use.
(a) Estimate the factor of safety guarding against fatigue-failure using a torsional Gerber fatigue-failure criterion with Zimmerli data.
(b) Repeat part (a) using the Sines torsional fatigue criterion (steady stress component has no effect), with Zimmerli data.
(c) Repeat using a torsional Goodman failure criterion with Zimmerli data.
(d) Estimate the critical frequency of the spring.

Admin
Solution

The mean coil diameter is D = 0.5625 − 0.092 = 0.4705 in. The spring index is C = D∕d = 0.4705∕0.092 = 5.11. Then

\(K_{B}=\frac{4C+2}{4C-3}=\frac{4(5.11)+2}{4(5.11)-3}=1.287\)

From Equations (10–31), \( F_{a}= \frac{F_{max} − F_{min}}{2} \) \( F_{m}= \frac{F_{max} + F_{min}}{2} \)

\(F_{a}=\frac{35-5}{2}= 15lbf \) \( F_{m}= \frac{35+5}{2}=20 lbf\)

The alternating shear-stress component is found from Equation (10–32) to be

\(τ_{a} = K_{B}\frac{8F_{a}D}{πd^{3}}=(1.287)\frac{ 8(15)0.4705}{π(0.092)^{3}}(10^{−3}) = 29.7 kpsi\)

Equation (10–33) gives the mean shear-stress component

\(τ_{m} = K_{B} \frac{8F_{m} D}{πd^{3}} = 1.287 \frac{8(20)0.4705}{π(0.092)^{3}} (10−3)= 39.6 kpsi\)

From Table 10–4 we find A = 201 kpsi ·\( in^{m}\) and m = 0.145. The ultimate tensile strength is estimated from Equation (10–14) as

\(S_{ut} = \frac{A}{d^{m}} = \frac{201}{0.092^{0.145}} = 284.1 kpsi\)

The shear modulus of rupture is estimated from Equation (10–30)

\(S_{su} = 0.67S_{ut}\) = 0.67(284.1) = 190.3 kpsi

(a) The endurance limit based on the Gerber criterion, Equation (10–29b) \(S_{se} =\frac{S_{sa}}{1 − (S_{sm}∕S_{su})^{2}} \), and the Zimmerli endurance
strength components, Equation (10–28a) \( S_{sa} = 35 kpsi (241 MPa) S_{sm }= 55 kpsi (379 MPa) \), is

\(S_{se} =\frac{S_{sa}}{1 − (S_{sm}∕S_{su})^{2}} = \frac{35}{1 − (55∕190.3)^{2}} = 38.2 kpsi\)

The Gerber fatigue criterion from Equation (6–48), adapted for shear, is

\(n_{f} = \frac{1}{2}(\frac{S_{su}}{τm} )^{2} (\frac{τ_{a}}{S_{se}}) [−1 + \sqrt{1 +(\frac{2τ_{m}S_{se}} {S_{su}τ_{a}})^{2}}] \) \(= \frac{1}{2}(\frac{190.3}{ 39.6} )^{2}(\frac{29.7}{38.2} )[−1 + \sqrt{ 1 +(\frac{(2(39.6)(38.2)}{(190.3)(29.7))}})^{2}] = 1.21\)

(c) The endurance limit based on the Goodman criterion, Equation (10–29a) \( S_{se} =\frac{S_{sa}}{1 − \frac{S_{sm}}{S_{su}}} \), and the Zimmerli endurance strength components, Equation (10–28a), is

\( S_{se} =\frac{S_{sa}}{1 − (S_{sm}∕S_{su})}= \frac{35}{1− (55∕190.3)}=49.2 kpsi \)

The Goodman fatigue criterion from Equation (6–41) \(n_{f}=(\frac{\sigma _{a}}{S_{e}} +\frac{\sigma _{m}}{S_{ut}} )^{-1} \sigma_{m}\geq 0\), adapted for shear, is

\(n_f = (\frac{τ_{a}}{S_{se}} + \frac{τ_{m}}{S_{su}} )^{−1}= (\frac{29.7}{49.2} +\frac{39.6}{190.3} )^{−1}= 1.23 \)

(d) Using Equation (10–9) and Table 10–5, we estimate the spring rate as

\(k = \frac{d^{4}G}{8D^{3}N_{a}} =\frac{0.092^{4}[11.75(10^{6})]}{8(0.4705)^{3}21} = 48.1 lbf/in \)

From Equation (10–27) \( W = ALγ = \frac{πd^{2}}{4} (πDN_{a})(γ) =\frac{ π^{2}d^{2}DN_{aγ}}{4} \) we estimate the spring weight as

\(W = \frac{π^{2}(0.092^{2})0.4705(21)0.284}{4} = 0.0586 lbf\)

and from Equation (10–25) \( f=\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\frac{kg}{W}} \) the frequency of the fundamental wave is

\(f_{n} = \frac{1}{2} [\frac{48.1(386)}{0.0586} ]^{1/2} = 281 Hz\)

If the operating or exciting frequency is more than 281∕20 = 14.1 Hz, the spring may have to be redesigned.
