Determining the Molecular Weight and Osmotic Virial Coefficients of a Protein

The following data have been reported for the osmotic pressure of α-chymotrypsin in water at pH = 4.0 in an 0.01 M potassium sulfate solution at \(25^{\circ} C .^{19}\)

Data

\(C_{ S }( g / L )\) II (Pa) \(\frac{II}{C_{ S }}\left(\frac{ PaL }{ g }\right)\)
0.902 74.8 82.92
1.798 142.3 79.14
3.574 276.2 77.29
5.331 398 74.65
7.027 513.3 73.05
8.835 625.3 70.77
Admin
Solution

As shown in the figure, these data can be fit with the following equation:

 

\(\frac{II}{C_{ S }}=83.961-2.168 \cdot C_{ S }+0.077 \cdot C_{ S }^{2}\)

 

Therefore,

 

\(\lim _{C_{ S } \rightarrow 0} \frac{\Pi}{C_{ S }}=83.961 \frac{ Pa L }{ g }=\frac{R T}{m_{ S }}\)

 

so that the molecular weight is

 

\(m_{ S }=\frac{8.314 \times 10^{-3} \frac{ kPa { }^{3}}{ mol K } \times 298.15 K \times 10^{3} \frac{ Pa }{ kPa } \times 10^{3} \frac{ L }{ m ^{3}}}{83.961 \frac{ Pa L }{ g }}=29528 \frac{ g }{ mol }\)

 

Next,

 

\(\frac{II}{R T}=\frac{C_{ S }}{m_{ S }}\)

 

\(\frac{II}{C_{ S }}=\frac{R T}{m_{ S }}\left\{1+B_{2} \frac{C_{ S }}{m_{ S }}+\cdots\right\}\)

 

so that

 

\(\lim _{C_{ S } \rightarrow 0} \frac{d\left(\frac{II}{C_{ S }}\right)}{d C_{ S }}=\frac{R T B_{2}}{m^{2}}=-2.168 \frac{ Pa L ^{2}}{ g ^{2}}\)

 

and

 

\(B_{2}=-\frac{2.168 \frac{ Pa L ^{2}}{ g } \times\left(2.9528 \times 10^{4} \frac{ g }{ mol }\right)^{2}}{8.314 \times 10^{3} \frac{ Pa L }{ mol K } \times 298.15 K }=-762.4 \frac{ L }{ mol }\)

 

Note: When the protein (or other membrane-impermeable solute) has an appreciable net charge, and salts that can pass through the membrane are present, the analysis of osmotic equilibrium can be more complicated than discussed here as a result of the uneven partitioning of the ions. This phenomenon, referred to as Gibb-Donnan equilibrium, is discussed in Sec. 15.7.
