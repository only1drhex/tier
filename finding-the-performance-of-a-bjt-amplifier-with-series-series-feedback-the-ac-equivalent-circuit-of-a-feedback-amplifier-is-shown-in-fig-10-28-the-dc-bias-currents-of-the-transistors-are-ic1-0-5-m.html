Guest Question.

Finding the performance of a BJT amplifier with series-series feedback The AC equivalent circuit of a feedback amplifier is shown in Fig. 10.28. The DC bias currents of the transistors are \( I_{ C 1}=0.5 mA , I_{ C 2}=1 mA \text {, and } I_{ C 3}=5 mA \). The transistor parameters are \( h_{ fe }=h_{ fe 1}=h_{ fe 2}=h_{ fe 3}=100 \text { and } r_{\mu}=r_{ o }=\infty.\)

(a) Use the techniques of feedback analysis to calculate the input resistance \( R_{ if } \), the output resistance \( R_{\text {of }} \), and the closed-loop transconductance gain \( A_{ f }=i_{ o } / v_{ S } \).

(b) Use PSpice/SPICE to check your results.

NOTE: The following analysis can also be applied to MOSFET amplifiers by substituting a very large value of the B-E resistance \( r_{\pi} \) tending to infinity, \( 1 / r_{\pi} \simeq 0.\)

Admin
Solution

For \( I_{ C 1}=0.5 mA, \) Eqs. (8.40) and (8.42) give

\(\frac{1}{r_{\pi}}=\frac{i_{ b }}{v_{ be }}=\left.\frac{d i_{ B }}{d v_{ BE }}\right|_{ at Q \text {-point }}=\frac{I_{ B }}{V_{ T }}=\frac{I_{ B }}{25.8 mV }\) (8.40)

 

\(g_{ m }=\frac{i_{ c }}{v_{ be }}=\left.\frac{\beta_{ F } d i_{ B }}{d v_{ BE }}\right|_{\text {at } Q \text {-point }}=\frac{\beta_{ f } I_{ B }}{V_{ T }}=\frac{I_{ C }}{V_{ T }}=\frac{\beta_{ f }}{r_{\pi}}\) (8.42)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&r_{\pi 1}=\frac{h_{ fe } \times 25.8 mV }{I_{ C 1}}=\frac{100 \times 25.8 mV }{0.5 mA }=5.16 k \Omega \\&g_{ m 1}=\frac{I_{ Cl }}{V_{ T }}=\frac{0.5 mA }{25.8 mV }=19.38 mA / V\end{aligned}\)

 

For \( I_{ C 2}=1 mA, \)

\(\begin{aligned}&r_{\pi 2}=\frac{h_{ fe } \times 25.8 mV }{I_{ C 2}}=\frac{100 \times 25.8 mV }{1 mA }=2.58 k \Omega \\&g_{ m 2}=\frac{I_{ C 2}}{V_{ T }}=\frac{1 mA }{25.8 mV }=38.76 mA / V\end{aligned}\)

 

For \( I_{ C 3}=5 mA ,\)

\(\begin{aligned}&r_{\pi 3}=\frac{h_{ fe } \times 25.8 mV }{I_{ C 3}}=\frac{100 \times 25.8 mV }{5 mA }=516 \Omega \\&g_{ m 3}=\frac{I_{ C 3}}{V_{ T }}=\frac{5 mA }{25.8 mV }=193.8 mA / V\end{aligned}\)

 

(a) The steps in analyzing the feedback network are as follows:

Step 1. \( R_{3}, R_{ F } \text {, and } R_{4} \) constitute the feedback network. The input voltage \( v_{ i } \) is compared with the feedback signal \( v_{f} \). An error voltage \( v_{ e }=v_{ i }-v_{ f } \) is the input to the base of the transistor \( Q _{3} \). The block diagram representing the feedback mechanism is shown in Fig. 10.29(a). The feedback network is shown in Fig. 10.29(b).

Step 2. The amplifier uses series-series feedback. Thus, the transconductance gain A must be expressed in A/V.

Step 3. The effect of the feedback network is taken into account at the input side by removing the network from the emitter of \( Q _{1} \) at side 2, as shown in Fig. 10.29(b), and at the output side by removing the network from the emitter of \( Q _{1} \) at side 1. These modifications are shown in the small-signal equivalent circuit in Fig. 10.29(c).

Step 4. If we represent the amplifier in Fig. 10.29(c) by an equivalent transconductance amplifier, the input resistance is

\(\begin{aligned}R_{ i } &=r_{\pi 1}+\left(1+g_{ m 1} r_{\pi 1}\right)\left[R_{4} \|\left(R_{ F }+R_{3}\right)\right]=5.16 k +101 \times(100 \| 750)=14.07 k \Omega \\R_{ ie } &=R_{ i }+R_{ s }=14.07 k +150=14.22 k \Omega\end{aligned}\)

The output resistance seen at the collector of \( Q _{3} \) is

\( R_{ o }=r_{ o }=\infty \)

From Fig. 10.29(c), we get

\(V_{ bel }=\left(\frac{r_{\pi 1}}{R_{ i }}\right) v_{ i }=\left(\frac{5.16 k }{14.07 k }\right) v_{ i }=0.367 v_{ i }\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}v_{ b 2} &=v_{ be 2}=-\left(R_{1} \| r_{\pi 2}\right) g_{ m 1} v_{ bel }=-(9 k \| 2.58 k ) \times 19.38 m \times 0.367 v_{ i }=-14.25 v_{ i } \\v_{ b 3} &=-\left\{R_{2} \|\left[r_{\pi 3}+\left(1+g_{ m 3} r_{\pi 3}\right)\left(R_{3} \|\left(R_{ F }+R_{4}\right)\right)\right]\right\} g_{ m 2} v_{ be 2} \\&=-\{5 k \|[516+(1+193.8 m \times 516)(100 \| 750)]\} \times 38.76 m \times\left(-14.25 v_{ i }\right) \\&=1.805 \times 10^{3} v_{ i }\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}v_{ o } &=\frac{v_{ b 3} \times r_{\pi 3}}{\left\{r_{\pi 3}+\left(1+g_{ m 3} r_{\pi 3}\right)\left[R_{3} \|\left(R_{ F }+R_{4}\right)\right]\right\}} \\&=\frac{\left(1.825 \times 10^{3} \times v_{ i }\right) \times 516}{[516+(1+193.8 m \times 516)(100 \| 750)]}=98.766 v_{ i } \\i_{ o } &=g_{ m 3} v_{ be 3}=193.8 m \times 98.766 v_{ i }=19.14 v_{ i }\end{aligned}\)

Therefore, the open-loop transconductance A is given by \( A=i_{ o } / v_{ i }=19.14 A / V.\)

Step 5. From Eq. (10.55), the feedback factor Î² is given by

\(\beta=\left.\frac{v_{ f }}{i_{ o }}\right|_{i_{ f }=0}\) (open-circuited input side) (10.55)

 

\( \beta=\left.\frac{v_{ f }}{i_{ o }}\right|_{i_{ f }=0}=\frac{R_{3} R_{4}}{R_{3}+R_{4}+R_{ F }}=\frac{100 \times 100}{100+100+650}=11.76 V / A \)

 

Step 6. The input resistance with feedback is

\( R_{ if }=\frac{v_{ s }}{i_{ s }}=R_{ ie }\left(1+\beta A_{ e }\right)=14.22 k \Omega \times(1+11.76 \times 18.94)=3.18 M \Omega \)

Step 7. The output resistance with feedback is

\( R_{ of }=R_{ o }(1+\beta A)=\infty \)

Since the output is taken across \( R_{ C }, \) the output resistance of the amplifier is

\( R_{\text {out }}=R_{\text {of }} \| R_{ C }=R_{ C }=500 \Omega \)

Step 8. The closed-loop transconductance gain \( A_{ f } \) is

\( A_{ f }=\frac{i_{ o }}{v_{ s }}=\frac{18.94}{1+11.76 \times 18.94}=84.66 mA / V \)

(b) The series-series feedback amplifier for PSpice simulation is shown in Fig. 10.30. The results of PSpice simulation (.TF analysis) are shown below, with hand calculations to the right.

\(\begin{array}{ll}\text { I(VU) } / \text { VS }=8.379 E -02=83.79 mA / V & A_{ f }=84.66 mA / V \\\text { INPUT RESISTANCE AT VS }=3.215 E +06=3.215 M \Omega & R_{\text {if }} \approx 3.18 M \Omega \\\text { OUTPUT RESISTANCE AT I }( VU )=8.639 E +13 \Omega & R_{\text {of }}=\infty\end{array}\)

The PSpice results are very close to the hand-calculated values.
