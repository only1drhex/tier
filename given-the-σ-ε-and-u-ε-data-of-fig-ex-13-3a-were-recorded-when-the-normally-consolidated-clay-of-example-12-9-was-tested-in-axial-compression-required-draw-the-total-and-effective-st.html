Guest Question.

Given:

The \(\sigma-\varepsilon \text { and } u-\varepsilon\) data of Fig. Ex 13.3a were recorded when the normally consolidated clay of was tested in axial compression.

Required:

Draw the total and effective stress paths for this test. Determine the Mohr-Coulomb strength parameters.

Admin
Solution

Using Eqs. (13.1) and (13.2), we have to determine p, \(p^{\prime}\) and q for several strains in order to plot the stress paths. Usually five of six points are sufficient. Sometimes, to keep things in order, a table is helpful. Then just fill in the appropriate columns. It may also be helpful to know that

 

\(q=\frac{\sigma_{v}-\sigma_{h}}{2}\) (13.1)

 

\(p=\frac{\sigma_{v}+\sigma_{h}}{2}\) (13.2)

 

\(\sigma_{1}+\sigma_{3}=\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)+2 \sigma_{3}\) (13.20)

 

and

 

\(\frac{\sigma_{1}+\sigma_{3}}{2}=\frac{\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}}{2}+\sigma_{3}\) (13.21)

 

Also, since \(\sigma^{\prime}=\sigma-u, p^{\prime}=p-u\). And finally,

 

\(\frac{\sigma_{1}^{\prime}+\sigma_{3}^{\prime}}{2}=\frac{\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}}{2}+\sigma_{3}^{\prime}\) (13.22)

 

because \(\left(\sigma_{1}^{\prime}-\sigma_{3}^{\prime}\right)=\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)\).

Now just choose the values of \(\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)\) and \(\Delta u\) at several convenient strains, and fill in the table (Table Ex. 13.3) by using the above equations. Note that \(\sigma_{3}\) in was 150 kPa.

Total and effective stress paths are shown in Fig. Ex. 13.3b. The failure lines are also drawn, assuming \(a^{\prime}=a=0\).

From Eq. (13.7)

 

\(\sin \phi=\tan \psi\) (13.7)

 

\(\psi^{\prime}=24.1^{\circ}, \text { then } \phi^{\prime}=26.6^{\circ}\)

 

and

 

\(\psi_{T}=14.1^{\circ}, \quad \text { so } \phi_{T}=14.5^{\circ}\)

 

Note that the problem could be solved graphically by plotting the TSP first, then scaling off the corresponding \(\Delta u\) values horizontally to the left of the TSP; one point done this way is shown in Fig. Ex. 13.3b.
