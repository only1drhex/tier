Guest Question.

Please refer to the previous Fig. 3.17 that shows an insulated wire.We have a copper #12 AWG wire with PTFE insulation. The bare wire has a diameter of 2.052 mm and the insulation is 15 mils thick. The thermal conductivity of the insulation is k=0.25 W/m C. The temperature \(T_{1}\) at the interface between the wire and the insulation is kept at 100 C, and the surrounding air is at 20 C. The convective coefficient h at the outer surface of the insulation is 10 W/\(m^{2}\) C.
(a) What is the heat transfer rate from the wire per meter length before additional insulation is added?
(b) If more insulation is added to the wire, will the heat transfer rate increase or decrease?
(c) If the answer to Part (b) is that the heat transfer will increase, then how much insulation has to be added for the heat transfer to reach a maximum? Also, what is the value of this maximum heat transfer rate?

Admin
Solution

(a) For the original wire with the original insulation, \(r_{i}\) = 0.001026 m and \(r_{0}\) = 0.001407 m. (Note: 1 mil is 1/1000
inch)

Putting the values of this problem into Eq. (3.75), we

\(q= \frac{T_{1} -T_{\infty } }{(\frac{1}{h(2\pi r_{0} L)} +\frac{\ln(\frac{ r_{0}}{ r_{i}} )}{2\pi KL} )} \)…………(3.75) get that the original heat transfer is q/L =6.95 W/m.

(b) Maximum heat transfer occurs when \(r_{o}\)= \(r_{oc}\) = k=h.

\(r_{oc} = \frac{0.25 W/m C}{10 W/m^{2}C} = 0.025 m\)

Because our initial \(r_{o}\) is 0.001407 m, which is less than \(r_{oc}\), addition of insulation will increase the heat transfer until \(r_{o}\) reaches \(r_{oc}\).

(c) The thickness of insulation that has to be added to reach \(r_{oc}\) is 0.025e0-001407 = 0.0236 m=23.6 mm. Using \(r_{oc}\) = 0.025 m in Eq. (3.75), it is found that the maximum heat transfer rate is 29.97 W/m.

\(r_{oc}\) = 0.025 m and the maximum heat flow is 29.97 W/m.

The below graph shows q=L versus \(r_o\) for this example. It is seen that the maximum heat flow indeed occurs at \(r_o = 0.025\) m and the maximum heat flow is 29.97 W/m.

One final note on this example: The inner surface of the insulation had a temperature boundary condition. The temperature
was held constant at 100 C. As insulation was added to the wire, up to \(r_oc \), the heat transfer from the wire increased. To keep the temperature constant as the heat transfer increased, the electrical current would have to be increased to increase the heat generation of the wire. If, instead of the constant temperature boundary condition, we had a constant power boundary condition, then, as the insulation was added, the increased heat transfer would have caused a drop in temperature at the inner surface of the insulation.
