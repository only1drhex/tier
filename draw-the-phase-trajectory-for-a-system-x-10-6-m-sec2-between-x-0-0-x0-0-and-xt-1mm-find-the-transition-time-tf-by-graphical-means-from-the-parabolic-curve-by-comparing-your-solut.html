Guest Question.

Draw the phase trajectory for a system

\(\ddot{x}=10^{-6} m / sec ^{2}\)

between \(\dot{x}(0)=0\), x(0) = 0 and x(t) = 1mm. Find the transition time, \(t_{f}\) , by graphical means from the parabolic curve by comparing your solution with several different interval sizes and the exact solution.

Admin
Solution

The phase portrait is shown in the figure below.

\(\ddot{x}=a, \quad \frac{\dot{v}}{\dot{x}}=\frac{a}{v}\),

\(\frac{v^{2}}{2}=a x\) and \(a=10^{-6}\),

\(v=\sqrt{2 a x}=a t+v_{o}=a t\),

\(x(t)=\frac{a}{2} t^{2}+v_{o} t+x_{o}=5 \times 10^{-7}t^{2}=1 \times 10^{-3}\),

\(t^{2}=\left(\frac{1}{5}\right) \times 10^{4} \Rightarrow t_{f}=45 sec\).

\(v=\frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}\).

To obtain t graphically, i.e., by graphical integration, we write,

\(\Delta t=\frac{\Delta x}{\hat{v}}\),

where \(\hat{v}\) is the “average” v in a given interval of \(\Delta x\). In the figure above, we divide x into five intervals and find (going from left to the right),

\(t_{f}=\Delta t_{1}+\Delta t_{2}+\Delta t_{3}+\Delta t_{4}+\Delta t_{5}\)

= \(\frac{0.2}{0.01}+\frac{0.2}{0.025}+\frac{0.2}{0.0315}+\frac{0.2}{0.037}+\frac{0.2}{0.0425}\)

= \(20+8+6+5.4+4.7=44.1 sec\).

which compares well with the exact answer of t = 45 sec. Better approximation can be found by finer division of x. Alternatively we can compute the time from,

\(t_{f}=\int_{0}^{x} \frac{1}{v} d x\)

which means the time can also be found by finding the area under the \(v(x)^{-1}\) plot.
