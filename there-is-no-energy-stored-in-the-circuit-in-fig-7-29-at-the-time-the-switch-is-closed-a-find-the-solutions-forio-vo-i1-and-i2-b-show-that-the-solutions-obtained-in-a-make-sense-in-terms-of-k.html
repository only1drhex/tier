Guest Question.

There is no energy stored in the circuit in Fig. 7.29 at the time the switch is closed.

a) Find the solutions for \(i_{o},v_{o},i_{1}, \) and\(i_{2}\).

b) Show that the solutions obtained in (a) make sense in terms of known circuit behavior.

Admin
Solution

a) For the circuit in Fig. 7.29, the magnetically coupled coils can be replaced by a single inductor having an inductance of

 

\(L_{eq}=\frac{L_{1}L_{2}-M^{2}}{L_{1}+L_{2}-2M}=\frac{45-36}{18-12}=1.5 H\).

 

(See Problem 6.41.) It follows that the circuit in Fig. 7.29 can be simplified as shown in Fig. 7.30.By hypothesis the initial value of \(i_{o} \)is zero. From Fig. 7.30 we see that the final value of \(i_{o} \) will be\(\frac{120}{7.5}\) or 16 A. The time constant of the circuit is\(\frac{1.5}{7.5}\) or 0.2 s. It follows directly from Eq. 7.60 that

 

\(i_{o}=16 -16e^{-5t} A ,t\geq 0\).

 

The voltage \(v_{o} \) follows from Kirchhoff’s voltage law. Thus,

 

\(v_{o}=120-7.5i_{o}\).

 

\(=120e^{-5t}V , t\geq 0^{+}\).

 

To find \(i_{1} \) and\(i_{2}\)we first note from Fig. 7.29 that

 

\(3\frac{di_{1}}{dt}+6\frac{di_{2}}{dt}=6\frac{di_{1}}{dt}+15\frac{di_{2}}{dt}\)

 

or

 

\(\frac{di_{1}}{dt}=-3\frac{di_{2}}{dt}\).

 

It also follows from Fig. 7.29 that because

 

\(i_{o}=i_{1}+i_{2}\),

 

\(\frac{di_{o}}{dt}=\frac{di_{1}}{dt}+\frac{di_{2}}{dt}\).

 

Therefore

 

\(80e^{-5t}=-2\frac{di_{2}}{dt}\).

 

Because \(i_{2}\left(0 \right)\) is zero we have

 

\( i_{2}=\int_{0}^{t}{-40e^{-5x} dx}\)

 

\(=-8 +8e^{-5t} A , t\geq 0\) .

 

Using Kirchhoff’s current law we get

 

\(i_{1} =24-24e^{-5t} A , t\geq 0\) .

 

b) First we observe that \(i_{o}\left(0 \right) , i_{1}\left(0 \right),\) and\( i_{2}\left(0 \right)\) are all zero, which is consistent with the statement that no energy is stored in the circuit at the instant the switch is closed. Next we observe \(v_{o}\left(0^{+} \right)=120 V\) which is consistent with the fact that \(i_{o}\left(0 \right)=0\). Now we observe the solutions for \( i_{1}\)and \( i_{2}\) are consistent with the solution for \(v_{o}\) by observing

 

\(v_{o}=3\frac{di_{1}}{dt}+6\frac{di_{2}}{dt}\).

 

\(=360e^{-5t} -240e^{-5t} \)

 

\(=120e^{-5t} V ,t\geq 0^{+} \) .

 

The final values of \( i_{1}\)and \( i_{2}\) can be checked using flux linkages. The flux linking the 3 H coil \(\left(\lambda _{1}\right)\) must be equal to the flux linking the 15 H coil \(\left(\lambda _{2}\right)\) , because

 

\(v_{o}=\frac{d_{\lambda _{1}}}{dt}\)

 

\(=\frac{d_{\lambda _{2}}}{dt}\).

 

Now

 

\(\lambda _{1}=3i_{1}+6i_{2}\)Wb-turns

 

and

 

\(\lambda _{2}=6i_{1}+15i_{2}\)Wb-turns.

 

Regardless of which expression we use, we obtain

 

\(\lambda _{1}=\lambda _{2}=24-24e^{-5t} \)Wb-turns.

 

Note the solution for \(\lambda _{1}\) or \(\lambda _{2}\) is consistent with the solution for \(v_{o}\). The final value of the flux linking either coil 1 or coil 2 is 24 Wb-turns, that is,

 

\(\lambda _{1}\left(\infty\right)=\lambda _{2}\left(\infty\right)=24\)Wb-turns.

 

The final value of \(i_{1}\) is

 

\(i_{1}\left(\infty\right)=24A\).

 

and the final value of \(i_{2}\) is

 

\(i_{2}\left(\infty\right)=-8A\).

 

The consistency between these final values for \( i_{1}\)and \( i_{2}\) and the final value of the flux linkage can be seen from the expressions:

 

\(\lambda_{1}\left(\infty\right)=3i_{1}\left(\infty\right)+6i_{2}\left(\infty\right)\)

 

\(= 3\left(24\right) + 6\left(-8\right) =\) 24Wb-turns,

 

\(\lambda_{2}\left(\infty\right)=6i_{1}\left(\infty\right)+15i_{2}\left(\infty\right)\)

 

\(= 6\left(24\right) + 15\left(-8\right) = 24\) Wb-turns.

 

It is worth noting that the final values of \( i_{1}\)and \( i_{2}\) can only be checked via flux linkage because at t= ∞ the two coils are ideal short circuits. The division of current between ideal short circuits cannot be found from Ohm’s law.
