Guest Question.

A cohesive infinite soil slope has 2.5 m soil overburden above the underlying stiff stratum. The slope is inclined at 20° to the horizontal and there is no water table within the overburden soil. The soil properties are \(\gamma_{m}=19.0 kN / m ^{3}, \phi^{\prime}=15^{\circ}, \text { and } c^{\prime}=35 kPa\).

Determine the following:

a. Safety factor against sliding

b. Maximum shear stress developed within the overburden soil

c. Shear strength along the potential failure plane above

d. Critical height of overburden that would have caused sliding

Admin
Solution

a. Substituting m = 0 in Equation 15.15:

 

\(F=\frac{c^{\prime}}{\gamma_{m} z \sin \beta \cos \beta}+\frac{\tan \phi^{\prime}}{\tan \beta}\) (15.15)

 

\(F=\frac{c^{\prime}}{\gamma_{m} z \sin \beta \cos \beta}+\frac{\tan \phi^{\prime}}{\tan \beta}=\frac{35}{19 \times 2.5 \times \sin 20 \times \cos 20}+\frac{\tan 15}{\tan 20}=3.03\)

 

b. Substituting m = 0 in the expression for mobilized shear stress at depth z:

 

\(\tau_{ mob }=\gamma_{m} z \sin \beta \cos \beta=19 \times 2.5 \times \sin 20 \cos 20=15.3 kPa\)

 

c. Substituting m = 1 in the expression for shear strength at depth z:

 

\(\tau_{f}=c^{\prime}+\gamma_{m} z \cos ^{2} \beta \tan \phi^{\prime}=35+19 \times 2.5 \times \cos ^{2} 20 \times \tan 15=46.2 kPa\)

 

d. Substituting F = 1 in Equation 15.15:

 

\(1=\frac{35}{19 \times H \times \sin 20 \cos 20}+\frac{\tan 15}{\tan 20} \rightarrow H=21.7 m\)
