Guest Question.

The homogeneous door shown in Fig. 6-83a has a mass of 25 kg and is supported in a horizontal position by two hinges and a bar. The hinges have been properly aligned; therefore, they exert only force reactions on the door. Assume that the hinge at B resists any force along the axis of the hinge pins. Determine
(a) The reactions at supports A, B, and D.
(b) The axial stress and deformation in bar CD if it is made of structural steel and has a 25- × 8-mm rectangular cross section.
(c) The shearing stress on a cross section of the pin at D if the diameter of the pin is 6 mm.

Admin
Solution

(a) A free-body diagram of the door is shown in Fig. 6-83b. The weight, the hinge reactions at A and B, and the bar reaction at D can be written as

\( W = -mg k = -25(9.81) k = -245.3 k \quad N \)

 

\( A=A_y j+A_z k \quad N\)

 

\( B=B_x i+B_y j +B_z k \quad N \)

 

\( D=0.5647D j+0.8253D k\quad N \)

For the door to be in equilibrium, \( R= \sum{F_xi}+\sum{F_yj}+\sum{F_zk} \) must be satisfied. Thus,

\( R=\sum{F_xi}+\sum{F_yj}+\sum{F_zk}=0 \)

 

\( (B_x)i+(A_y+B_y+0.5647D)j+(A_z+B_z+0.8253D-245.3)k=0 \) (a)

Summing moments about B to eliminate the maximum number of unknowns

\( C=\sum{C_xi}+\sum{C_yj}+\sum{C_zk} =0 \)

 

\( (r_{A/B}\times A)+(r_{W/B}\times W)+(r_{D/B}\times D)=0 \)

 

 

\( \left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ 1.2 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & A_y & A_z \end{matrix} \right| +\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ 0.6 & 0.5 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 &- 245.3\end{matrix} \right| +\left|\begin{matrix} i & j & k \\ 1.6 & 0.65 & 0 \\ 0 & 0.5647D & 0.8253D \end{matrix} \right| \)

 

\( =(-122.65+0.5364D)i +(-1.2A_z+147.18-1.3205D)j +(1.2A_y+0.9035D)k=0 \) (b)

Equating the coefficients of i, j, and k to zero in Eqs. (a) and (b) and solving yields

\( B_x=0 \quad\quad B_x=0 \)

 

\(A_y+B_y+0.5647D=0 \quad\quad B_y=43.03N \)

 

\(A_z+B_z+0.8253D-245.3=0 \quad \quad B_z=185.56N \)

 

\(-122.65+0.5364D=0 \quad\quad D=228.65N \)

 

\(-1.2A_z+147.18-1.3205D=0 \quad\quad A_z=-128.96N \)

 

\(1.2A_y+0.9035D=0 \quad\quad A_y=-172.15N \)

The reactions at hinges A and B and the force exerted by the bar at D are

\( A=-172.2j-129.0k \quad N \quad\quad |A|=215N \)

 

\(B=43.0j+185.6k \quad N \quad\quad |B|=190.5N \)

 

\( D=129.1j+188.7k \quad N \quad\quad |D|=229N \)

A scalar analysis can frequently be used in three-dimensional problems when a single unknown is the only quantity required. For example, the force exerted by the bar at support D can be determined by summing moments about the axis of the hinges. Thus,

\(+\searrow \sum{M_x}=0 \quad\quad D(0.95 sin\theta _D)-W(0.5)=0 \)

 

\(D(0.95 sin34.38^\circ )-245.3(0.5)=0 \)

 

\(D=228.6≅229N\)

(b) The axial stress and deformation in bar CD is given by \(\sigma _{avg} =\frac{F}{A}\) and \(\delta =\frac{PL}{EA} \) as

\(\sigma =\frac{F_{CD}}{A_{CD}}=\frac{228.6}{0.025(0.008)}=1.143(10^6)N/m^2=1.143MPa \)

 

\(L_{CD}=\sqrt{(0.65)^2+(0.95)^2}=1.151m \)

 

\(E_{CD} =200GPa (see \quad Table A-18)\)

 

\(\delta =\frac{F_{CD}L_{CD}}{E_{CD}A_{CD}}=\frac{228.6(1.151)}{200(10^9)(0.025)(0.008)}=0.00658(10^{-3})m=0.00658mm \)

(c) The shearing stress on a cross section of the pin at D, which is in single shear, is given by \(\tau_{avg} =\frac{V}{A_s}\) as

\(\tau =\frac{F_{CD}}{A_s}=\frac{228.6}{(\pi /4)(0.006)^2}=8.085(10^6)N/m^2\cong 8.09MPa \)
