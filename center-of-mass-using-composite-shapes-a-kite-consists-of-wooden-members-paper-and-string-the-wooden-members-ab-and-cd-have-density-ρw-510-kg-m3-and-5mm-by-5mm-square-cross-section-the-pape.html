Guest Question.

Center of Mass Using Composite Shapes

A kite consists of wooden members, paper, and string. The wooden members AB and CD have density \(\rho_w = 510 kg/m^3\) and 5mm by 5mm square cross section. The paper ADBC has density \(\rho_p = 0.039 kg/m^2\). Around the perimeter of the kite (between points A, D, B, C, and A) is taut string with density \(\rho_s = 2.6(10)^{-5} kg/m\). Determine the weight of the kite and the center of mass.

Admin
Solution

Road Map The kite consists of an arrangement of objects having simple shapes. Thus it will be easiest to determine the center of mass by using composite shapes with Eq. (\(\overline{x} = \frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{\widetilde{x}_i m_i} }{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{m_i}} = \frac{\widetilde{x}_1 \rho V + \widetilde{x}_2 \rho_A A + \widetilde{x}_3 \rho_L L}{\rho V + \rho_A A + \rho_L L}\)) on p. 414. The kite is symmetric about the x axis, thus its center of mass must lie on the x axis, and hence \(\overline{y} = 0\).

Governing Equations & Computation In Fig. 2 the kite is subdivided into eight composite shapes, or objects. The mass of each composite shape and the position of its center of mass are collected in Table 1.

Table 1. Masses and center of mass positions for the composite objects in Fig. 2. Note: “w,” “p,” and “s” stand for wood, paper, and string, respectively.
Object no. \(m_i\) (kg) \(\widetilde{x}_i\) (m)
1 (w AB) 510\(\frac{kg}{m^3}\)(0.005m)(0.005m)(0.7m) = 0.008925 0.15
2 (w CD) 510\(\frac{kg}{m^3}\)(0.005m)(0.005m)(0.6m) = 0.007650 0
3 (p ADC) 0.039\(\frac{kg}{m^2} \frac{1}{2}\)(0.6m)(0.2m) = 0.002340 -0.06667
4 (p BCD) 0.039\(\frac{kg}{m^2} \frac{1}{2}\)(0.6m)(0.5m) = 0.005850 0.1667
5 (s AD) \(2.6(10)^{-5}\sqrt{(0.2m)^2 + (0.3m)^2}= 9.374(10)^{-6}\) -0.1
6 (s AC) (. . . ”. . . ) = 9.374(10)\(^{-6}\) -0.1
7 (s BD) \(2.6(10)^{-5}\sqrt{(0.5m)^2 + (0.3m)^2}= 1.516(10)^{-5}\) 0.25
8 (s BC) (. . . ”. . . ) = 1.516(10)\(^{-5}\) 0.25

Using Eq. (\(\overline{x} = \frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{\widetilde{x}_i m_i} }{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{m_i}} = \frac{\widetilde{x}_1 \rho V + \widetilde{x}_2 \rho_A A + \widetilde{x}_3 \rho_L L}{\rho V + \rho_A A + \rho_L L}\)) provides the x position of the center of mass as

\(\overline{x} = \frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{\widetilde{x}_i m_i} }{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{m_i}} = \frac{(0.15m)(0.008925 kg) + \cdot \cdot \cdot + (0.25m)(1.516(10)^{-5} kg)}{0.008925 kg + \cdot \cdot \cdot + 1.516(10)^{-5} kg} \) (1)

\(= \frac{0.002163 kg\cdot m}{0.02481 kg} = 0.0872 m\). (2)

For brevity, numbers for only the first and eighth objects are shown in Eq. (1), and you should verify the results that are given. The location of the center of mass is shown in Fig. 3. In Eq. (2), the denominator is the total mass of the kite, \(m = 0.02481 kg\). Hence, the weight of the kite is

\(w = mg = (0.02481 kg)(9.81 m/s^2) = 0.243N\). (3)

Discussion & Verification We do not have a definitive check of our solution’s accuracy. Nonetheless, the center of mass has a reasonable location, and the weight of the kite is reasonable (it may be helpful to express the weight in U.S. Customary units— you should find that the kite weighs slightly less than 1 ounce).
