Guest Question.

(a) Obtain the Fourier transform of the decaying exponential signal

\( x(t)=u(t)e^{-\alpha t} \)

where α is a positive constant and

\( u(t)=\begin{cases} 1 & for t ≥0 \\ 0 & for t < 0 \end{cases} \)

is known as the unit-step function. (b) Obtain and plot the amplitude and phase spectrums of x(t).

Admin
Solution

(a) We can write

\( X(j\omega )=\int_{-\infty }^{\infty }{u(t)e^{-\alpha t}e^{-j\omega t}dt }=\int_{0}^{\infty }{x^{-(\alpha +j\omega )t} }dt \)

 

\( =[-\frac{e^{-(\alpha +j\omega )t} }{\alpha +j\omega } ]^{\infty }_{0} \)

For α>0, we note that

\( \underset{t\rightarrow \infty}{\lim}e^{-\alpha t}\rightarrow 0 \)

and as a result

\( \underset{t\rightarrow \infty}{\lim}e^{(-\alpha-j\omega )t}=\underset{t\rightarrow \infty}{\lim}(e^{-\alpha t}\cdot e^{-j\omega t} )\rightarrow 0 \)

Thus

\( X(j\omega )=\frac{1}{\alpha +j\omega } \)

or

\( u(t)e^{-\alpha t} \leftrightarrow \frac{1}{\alpha +j\omega } \)

(b) The amplitude and phase spectrums of the signal are given by

\( A(\omega )=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\alpha ^{2}+\omega ^{2} } } \) and \( \phi (\omega )=-\tan ^{-1}\frac{\omega }{\alpha } \)

respectively. Note that certain ambiguities can arise in the evaluation of the phase spectrum as the above equation has an inﬁnite number of solutions due to the periodicity of the tangent function (see Sec. A.3.7).
