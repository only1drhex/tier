Guest Question.

Figure 9.16(a) illustrates a four-bar mechanism having the following parameters:

\(r_{1} = 6.0 cm \)

 

\(r_{2} = 2.0 cm; m_{2}^{*} = 250 gr; b_{2}^{*} = 0.70 cm; ϕ_{2}^{*} = 5°\)

 

\(r_{3} = 10.0 cm; m_{3} = 500 gr; b_{3} = 3.0 cm; ϕ_{3}= 10°\)

 

\(r_{4} = 7.0 cm; m_{4}^{*} = 250 gr; b_{4}^{*} = 2.5 cm; ϕ_{4}^{*} = 15°\)

 

\(I_{G_{3}} = 250 gr-cm^{2}; I_{O_{4}}^{*} = 500 gr-cm^{2} \)

The constant rotational speed of link 2 is ω = 120 rad/sec CCW. Figure 9.16(b) shows a polar plot of the shaking force.
Determine the magnitudes and locations of balance masses to be added to links 2 and 4 that will provide complete force balance. Add the masses at the following radial distances from the base pivots:

\((r_{c})_{2} = 1.0 cm; (r_{c})_{4} = 3.5 cm\)

 

Admin
Solution

From Equations (9.5-12) \( m_{2} (b_{2})_{ξ_{2}} = m_{3} \left\lgroup b_{3} \frac{r_{2}}{r_{3} }\cos \varphi _{3} -r_{3} \right\rgroup\) \(m_{2} (b_{2})_{η_{2}} = m_{3} b_{3} \frac{r_{2}}{r_{3}} \sin \varphi _{3}\) and (9.5-13) \( m_{4} (b_{4})_{ξ_{4}} = – m_{3} b_{3} \frac{r_{4}}{r_{3} }\cos \varphi _{3}\) \(m_{4} (b_{4})_{η_{4}} = – m_{3} b_{3} \frac{r_{4}}{r_{3}} \sin \varphi _{3} \)

\( m_{2} (b_{2})_{ξ_{2}} = -705 gr-cm; m_{2} (b_{2})_{η_{2}} = 52.1 gr-m\)

 

\( m_{4} (b_{4})_{ξ_{4}} = -1000 gr-cm; m_{4} (b_{4})_{η_{4}} = -182 gr-m\)

To provide force balance of the mechanism, balance masses \((m_{c})_{2}\) and \((m_{c})_{4}\) are added to links 2 and 4, respectively. The combined effect caused by the given links, and that of the added balance masses, must provide the above calculated values. That is, for link 4

\( m_{4} (b_{4})_{ξ_{4}} = m_{4}^{*} (b^{*}_{4})_{ξ_{4}} + (m_{c})_{4} ((r_{c})_{4})_{ξ_{4}}\) (9.5-14a)

 

\( m_{4} (b_{4})_{η_{4}} = m_{4}^{*} (b^{*}_{4})_{η_{4}} + (m_{c})_{4} ((r_{c})_{4})_{η_{4}} \) (9.5-14b)

where

\( (b^{*}_{4})_{ξ_{4}} = b_{4}^{*} \cos \varphi _{4}^{*} = 2.42 cm; (b^{*}_{4})_{η_{4}} = b_{4}^{*} \sin \varphi _{4}^{*} = 0.65 cm \)

Using Equations (9.5-14a) and (9.5-14b) and substituting known values, we obtain

\( (\varphi _{c})_{4} = tan 2^{-1} [(m_{c})_{4} ((r_{c})_{4})_{ξ_{4}} , (m_{c})_{4}((r_{c})_{4})_{η_{4}} ]\)

 

\(= tan 2^{-1} [m_{4} (b_{4})_{ξ_{4}} – m_{4}^{*} (b^{*}_{4})_{ξ_{4}} , m_{4} (b_{4})_{η_{4}} – m_{4}^{*} (b^{*}_{4})_{η_{4}} ]\)

 

\( = -168.1° \)

The coordinates of the balance mass for link 4 are

\(((r_{c})_{4})_{ξ_{4}} = (r_{c})_{4}) \cos (\varphi _{c})_{4} = -3.4 cm\)

 

\(((r_{c})_{4})_{η_{4}} = (r_{c})_{4}) \sin (\varphi _{c})_{4} = -0.72 cm\)

From Equation (9.5-14a), the balance mass required on link 4 is

\((m_{c})_{4} = \frac{m_{4} (b_{4})_{ξ_{4}} – m_{4}^{*} (b^{*}_{4})_{ξ_{4}}}{((r_{c})_{4})_{ξ_{4}}} = 478 gr \)

The mass of link 4 after adding the balance mass is

\(m_{4} = m_{4}^{*} + (m_{c})_{4} = 728 gr\)

The polar mass moment of inertia of link 4 about its base pivot after addition of the balance mass is

\(I_{O_{4}} = I_{O_{4}} ^{*} + (m_{c})_{4} (r_{c})_{4}^{2}= 6.360 gr-cm^{2}\)

A similar analysis for link 2 provides

\((\varphi _{c}) = 177.6°; (m_{c})_{2} = 880 gr; m_{2} = 1130 gr\)

The force-balanced mechanism is shown in Figure 9.17. After force balancing, the polar plot of the shaking force is shown as a dot at the origin.
