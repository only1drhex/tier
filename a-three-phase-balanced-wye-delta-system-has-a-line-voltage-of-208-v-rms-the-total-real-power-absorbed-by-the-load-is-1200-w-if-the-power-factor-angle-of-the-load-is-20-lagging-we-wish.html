Guest Question.

A three-phase balanced wye–delta system has a line voltage of 208 V rms. The total real power absorbed by the load is 1200 W. If the power factor angle of the load is 20° lagging, we wish to determine the magnitude of the line current and the value of the load impedance per phase in the delta.

Admin
Solution

The line current can be obtained from Eq. (11.26). Since the real power per phase is 400 W,

Eq. (11.26): \(P_{p} =\frac{V_{L} I_{L}}{\sqrt{3}}cos\,θ \quad Q_{p} =\frac{V_{L} I_{L}}{\sqrt{3}}sin\,θ \)

400 = \(\frac{208I_{L}}{\sqrt{3}}\) cos 20°
\(I_{L}\) = 3.54 A rms

The magnitude of the current in each leg of the delta-connected load is

\(I_{Δ} = \frac{I_{L}}{\sqrt{3}}\)
= 2.05 A rms

Therefore, the magnitude of the delta impedance in each phase of the load is

\(|Z_{Δ}| =\frac{V_{L}}{I_{Δ}}\)
= \(\frac{208}{2.05}\)
= 101.46 Ω

Since the power factor angle is 20° lagging, the load impedance is

\(Z_{Δ} = 101.46\underline{/20^\circ}\)
= 95.34 + j34.70 Ω
