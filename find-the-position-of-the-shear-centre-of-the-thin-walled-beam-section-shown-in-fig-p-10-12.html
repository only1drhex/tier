Guest Question.

Find the position of the shear centre of the thin-walled beam section shown in Fig. P.10.12.

Admin
Solution

The shear centre S lies on the horizontal axis of symmetry so that we apply an arbitrary shear load \( S_{y} \) through S to determine its horizontal position. Further since Gz is an axis of symmetry \( I_{z y}=0 \) and Eq. (10.22) reduces to

\( q_{s}=\frac{\left(S_{y} I_{z y}-S_{z} I_{z}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s+\frac{\left(S_{z} I_{z y}-S_{y} I_{y}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s \) (10.22)

\( q_{s}=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s \) (i)

in which, referring to Fig. S.10.12

\( I_{z}=2\left[\int_{0}^{r} t y^{2} d s_{1}+\int_{0}^{\pi / 4} t(r \sin \theta)^{2} r d \theta\right] \)

so that

\( I_{z}=2\left[\int_{0}^{r} t\left(2 r \sin 45^{\circ}-s_{1} \sin 45^{\circ}\right)^{2} d s_{1}+\int_{0}^{\pi / 4} \operatorname{tr}^{3} \sin ^{2} \theta d \theta\right] \)

Integrating gives

\( I_{z}=t\left\{\left[4 r^{2} s_{1}-2 r s_{1}^{2}+\frac{s_{3}}{3}\right]_{0}^{r}+r^{3}\left[\theta-\frac{\sin 2 \theta}{2}\right]_{0}^{\pi / 4}\right\} \)

from which

\( I_{z}=2.62 tr ^{3} \)

From Eq. (i)

\( q_{12}=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \int_{0}^{s_{1}} t\left(2 r \sin 45^{\circ}-s_{1} \sin 45^{\circ}\right) d s_{1} \)

which gives

\( q_{12}=-\frac{0.27 S_{y}}{r^{3}}\left(2 r s_{1}-\frac{s_{1}^{2}}{2}\right) \) (ii)

From Eq. (ii) at the point 2 when \( s_{1}=r \)

\(q_{2}=\frac{-0.4 S_{y}}{r}\)

Further

\(q_{23}=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \int_{0}^{\varphi} t r \sin \left(45^{\circ}-\varphi\right) r d \varphi-\frac{0.4 S_{y}}{r}\)

which simplifies to

\( q_{23}=-\frac{S_{y}}{2.62 r} \cos \left(45^{\circ}-\varphi\right)-\frac{0.13 S_{y}}{r} \) (iii)

Now taking moments about O

\( S_{y} z_{S}=-2\left[\int_{0}^{r} q_{12} r d s_{1}+\int_{0}^{\pi / 4} q_{23} r^{2} d \varphi\right] \) (iv)

Substituting for \( q_{12} \text { and } q_{23} \) from Eqs (ii) and (iii) in Eq. (iv) and integrating

\( z_{S}=-2\left\{\frac{-0.27}{r^{2}}\left[r s_{1}^{2}-\frac{S_{1}^{3}}{6}\right]_{0}^{r}-r\left[-\frac{\sin \left(45^{\circ}-\varphi\right)}{2.62}+0.13 \varphi\right]_{0}^{\pi / 4}\right\} \)

which gives

\( z_{S}=1.2 r \)
