Guest Question.

In a laboratory investigation, acetylene is catalytically hydrogenated to ethylene at \(1120^{\circ}C\) and 1 bar. If the feed is an equimolar mixture of acetylene and hydrogen, what is the composition of the product stream at equilibrium?

Admin
Solution

The required reaction is obtained by the addition of the two formation reactions written as follows:

\( \begin{aligned} C _{2} H _{2} & \rightarrow 2 C + H _{2}(I) \\ 2 C +2 H _{2} & \rightarrow C _{2} H _{4}(II) \end{aligned} \)

The sum of reactions (I) and (II) is the hydrogenation reaction:

\( C _{2} H _{2}+ H _{2} \rightarrow C _{2} H _{4} \)

Also, \( \Delta G^{\circ}=\Delta G_{ I }^{\circ}+\Delta G_{ II }^{\circ} \)

By Eq. (14.11b),

\( \ln K=\frac{-\Delta G^{\circ}}{R T} \) (14.11b)

 

\( -R T \ln K=-R T \ln K_{ I }-R T \ln K_{ II } \quad \text { or } \quad K=K_{ I } K_{ II } \)

Data for both reactions (I) and (II) are given in Fig. 14.2. For \(1120^{\circ}C(1393.15 K), 10^{4}/T = 7.18, \) the following values are read from the graph:

\( \begin{aligned} \ln K_{ I } &=12.9 & K_{ I }=4.0 \times 10^{5} \\ \ln K_{ II } &=-12.9 & K_{ II }=2.5 \times 10^{-6} \end{aligned} \)

Therefore, \( K=K_{ I } K_{ II }=1.0 \)

At this elevated temperature and for a pressure of 1 bar, we can safely assume ideal gases. Application of Eq. (14.28) leads to the expression:

\( \prod_{i}\left(y_{i}\right)^{\nu_{i}}=\left(\frac{P}{P^{\circ}}\right)^{-\nu} K \) (14.28)

 

\( \frac{y_{ C _{2} H _{4}}}{y_{ H _{2}} y_{ C _{2} H _{2}}}=1 \)

On the basis of one mole of each reactant, Eq. (14.5) gives:

\( y_{i}=\frac{n_{i}}{n}=\frac{n_{i_{0}}+\nu_{i} \varepsilon}{n_{0}+\nu \varepsilon} \) (14.5)

 

\( y_{ H _{2}}=y_{ C _{2} H _{2}}=\frac{1-\varepsilon_{e}}{2-\varepsilon_{e}} \quad \text { and } \quad y_{ C _{2} H _{4}}=\frac{\varepsilon_{e}}{2-\varepsilon_{e}} \)

Therefore, \( \frac{\varepsilon_{e}\left(2-\varepsilon_{e}\right)}{\left(1-\varepsilon_{e}\right)^{2}}=1 \)

The smaller root of this quadratic expression (the larger is \(\gt 1,\) corresponding to negative mole fractions for the reactants) is: \(\varepsilon _{e} = 0.293.\)
The equilibrium composition of the product gas is then:

\( y_{ H _{2}}=y_{ C _{2} H _{2}}=\frac{1-0.293}{2-0.293}=0.414 \quad \text { and } \quad y_{ C _{2} H _{4}}=\frac{0.293}{2-0.293}=0.172 \)
