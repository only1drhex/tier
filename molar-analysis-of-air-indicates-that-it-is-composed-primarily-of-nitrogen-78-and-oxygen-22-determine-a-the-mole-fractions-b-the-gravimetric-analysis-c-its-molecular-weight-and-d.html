Guest Question.

Molar analysis of air indicates that it is composed primarily of nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (22%). Determine (a) the mole fractions, (b) the gravimetric analysis, (c) its molecular weight, and (d) its gas constant. Compare with values from Appendix B.

Admin
Solution

\( \text { (a) The mole fractions are given as } y_{1}=0.78 \text { and } y_{2}=0.22 \text {, where the subscript } 1 \text { refers to nitrogen and the subscript } 2 \text { refers } \) to oxygen.

(b) If there are 100 mol of the mixture, the mass of each component is

\( \left.\begin{array}{l} m_{1}=N_{1} M_{1}=(78)(28)=2184 kg \\ m_{2}=N_{2} M_{2}=(22)(32)=704 kg \end{array}\right\} \quad \therefore m=2888 kg \).

Gravimetric analysis yields

\( x_{1}=\frac{m_{1}}{m}=\frac{2184}{2888}=0.756 \quad x_{2}=\frac{m_{2}}{m}=\frac{704}{2888}=0.244 \).

\( \text { or, by mass, the mixture is } 75.6 \% N _{2} \text { and } 24.4 \% O _{2} \text {. } \)

(c) The molecular weight of the mixture is M = m /N = 2888/100 = 28.9 kg / kmol. This compares with 28.97 kg/ kmol from the Appendix, an error of − 0.24%.

\( \text { (d) The gas constant for air is calculated to be } R=R_{u} / M=8.314 / 28.9=0.288 kJ / kg \cdot K \text {. This compares with } 0.287 kJ / kg \cdot K \) from the Appendix, an error of 0.35%.
By including argon as a component of air, the above calculations could be improved. However, it’s obvious that the above analysis is quite acceptable.
