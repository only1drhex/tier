Guest Question.

It is necessary to ensure that a certain part supplied by a foundry always meets or exceeds ASTM No. 20 specifications for cast iron (see Table A–24). What hardness should be specified?

Solution

From Eq. (2–18), with \((S_{u})_{m}\) in = 20 kpsi, we have

\(S_{u}= \begin{cases} 0.23H_{B} − 12.5 kpsi \\1.58H_{B} − 86 MPa \end{cases}\) (2–18)

\(H_{B} =\frac{S_{u} + 12.5}{0.23} =\frac{20 + 12.5}{0.23} = 141\)

If the foundry can control the hardness within 20 points, routinely, then specify 145 < \(H_{B}\) < 165. This imposes no hardship on the foundry and assures the designer that ASTM grade 20 will always be supplied at a predictable cost.
