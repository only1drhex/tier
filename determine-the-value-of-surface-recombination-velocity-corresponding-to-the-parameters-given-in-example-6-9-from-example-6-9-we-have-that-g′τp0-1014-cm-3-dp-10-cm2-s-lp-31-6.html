Guest Question.

Determine the value of surface recombination velocity corresponding to the parameters given in .

From , we have that \(g^{\prime} \tau_{p 0}=10^{14} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}, D_{p}=10 \mathrm{~cm}^{2} / s, L_{p}=31.6 \mu \mathrm{m}\), and \(\delta p(0)=10^{13} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3} .\)

Admin
Solution

Writing Equation (6.114) at the surface, we have

\(\begin{array}{r} \delta p(x)=g^{\prime} \tau_{p 0}\left(1-\frac{s L_{p} e^{-x / L_{p}}}{D_{p}+s L_{p}}\right) \\ \end{array}\) (6.114)

\(\begin{array}{r}\delta p(0)=g^{\prime} \tau_{p 0}\left[1-\frac{s}{\left(D_{p} / L_{p}\right)+s}\right] \end{array}\)

Solving for the surface recombination velocity, we find that

\( s=\frac{D_{p}}{L_{p}}\left(\frac{g^{\prime} \tau_{p 0}}{\delta p(0)}-1\right) \)

which becomes

\(s=\frac{10}{31.6 \times 10^{-4}}\left[\frac{10^{14}}{10^{13}}-1\right]=2.85 \times 10^{4} \mathrm{~cm} / \mathrm{s}\)

Comment

This example shows that a surface recombination velocity of approximately \(s=3 \times 10^{4} \mathrm{~cm} / \mathrm{s}\) could seriously degrade the performance of semiconductor devices, such as solar cells, since these devices tend to be fabricated close to a surface.
