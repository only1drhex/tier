Guest Question.

The balcony shown in Fig. 13.35 comprises a concrete slab 1.5 m wide and 100 mm thick. The slab is supported at intervals of 3 m by steel joists having a self-weight of 17 kg/m and a second moment of area about a horizontal axis of \(800 \times 10^{4} mm ^{4}\). Each joist is built-in at its wall end and supported at its outer end by a hollow circular section steel column of external diameter 80 mm and having walls 5 mm thick. The connection between a joist and the top of a column allows rotation of the joist at its outer end. If the density of concrete is \(2000 kg / m ^{3}\) and the balcony must be designed for an imposed load of \(2 kN / m ^{2}\), calculate the maximum bending moment in a joist allowing for the effect of the compressive load in the supporting column.

Admin
Solution

Take E for steel as \(200000 N / mm ^{2}\).

Weight of the concrete slab/joist \(=2000 \times 9.81 \times 1.5 \times 3.0 \times 0.1 \times 10^{-3}=8.83 kN\).

Weight of a joist \(=17 \times 9.81 \times 1.5 \times 10^{-3}=0.25 kN\).

Imposed load/joist \(=2 \times 1.5 \times 3.0=9.0 kN\).

Total load/joist = 8.83 + 0.25 + 9.0 = 18.08 kN.

Then the uniformly distributed load/joist \(=\frac{18.08}{1.5}=12.05 kN / m\).

From , the vertical reaction between a column and a joist is given by

Vertical reaction \(=\frac{3}{8} \times 12.05 \times 1.5=6.78 kN\)

The shortening of the column is then \(=\frac{6.78 \times 10^{3} \times 2.5 \times 10^{3}}{(\pi / 4)\left(80^{2}-70^{2}\right) \times 200000}=0.072 mm\)

From Table 16.6,

this shortening will induce an anticlockwise moment at the built-in end of a joist of

Moment \(=\frac{3 \times 200000 \times 800 \times 10^{4} \times 0.072}{\left(1.5 \times 10^{3}\right)^{2}}=0.154 \times 10^{6} Nmm\)

i.e.

Moment = 0.154 kNm.

The bending moment at the built-in end due to the applied load and self-weight is, from

Moment \(=\frac{1}{8} \times 12.05 \times 1.5^{2}=3.39 kNm\),

which is also anticlockwise. The total bending moment at the built-in end of a joist is then 0.154 + 3.39 = 3.544 kNm, which will be the maximum value.
