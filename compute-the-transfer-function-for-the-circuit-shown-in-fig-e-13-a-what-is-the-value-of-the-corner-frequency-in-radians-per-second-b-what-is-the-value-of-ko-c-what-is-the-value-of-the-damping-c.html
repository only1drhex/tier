Guest Question.

Compute the transfer function for the circuit shown in Fig. E.13.
a) What is the value of the corner frequency in radians per second?

b) What is the value of \(K_o\) ?

c) What is the value of the damping coefficient?

d) Make a straight-line amplitude plot ranging from 10 to 500 rad/s .

e) Calculate and sketch the actual amplitude in decibels at \(ω_n/2,ω_p,ω_n\) , and \(ω_0.\)

f) From the straight-line amplitude plot, describe the type of filter represented by the circuit in Fig. E.13 and estimate its cutoff frequency, \(ω_c.\)

Admin
Solution

Transform the circuit in Fig. E.13 to the s-domain and then use s-domain voltage division to get

\(H(s) =\frac{\frac{1}{LC} }{s^2 +\left(\frac{R}{L} \right)s+\frac{1}{LC} }. \)

Substituting the component values,

\(H(s) =\frac{2500 }{s^2 +20s+2500 }. \)

a) From the expression for H(S), \(ω^2_n = 2500;\) there fore,

\(ω_n\) = 50 rad/s.

b) By definition \(K_o\) is \(2500/ω^2_n \), or 1.

c) The coefficient of s equals \(2\zeta ω_n\); therefore

\(\zeta =\frac{20}{2\omega _n}=0.20. \)

d) See Fig. E.14.

e) The actual amplitudes are

\(A_{dB}(\omega _n/2) = -10\log _{10}(0.6025) = 2.2 \ dB,\)

\(\omega _p = 50\sqrt{0.92} = 47.96 \ rad/s,\)

\(A_{dB}(\omega _p) = -10 \log _{10}(0.16)(0.96) = 8.14 \ dB,\)

\(A_{dB}(\omega _n) = -20 \log _{10}(0.4) = 7.96 \ dB,\)

\(\omega _o = \sqrt{2} \omega _p = 67.82 \ rad/s,\)

\(A_{dB}(\omega _o) = 0 \ dB\)

Figure E.14 shows the corrected plot.
f) It is clear from the amplitude plot in Fig. E.14 that this circuit acts as a low-pass filter. At the cutoff frequency, the magnitude of the transfer
function, , \(|H( jω_c)|,\) is 3 dB less than the maximum
magnitude. From the corrected plot, the cutoff frequency appears to be about 55 rad/s, almost the same as that predicted by the straight-line
Bode diagram.
