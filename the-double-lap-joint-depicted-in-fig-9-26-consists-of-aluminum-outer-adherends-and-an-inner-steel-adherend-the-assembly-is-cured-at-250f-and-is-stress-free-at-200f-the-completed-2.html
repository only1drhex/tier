Guest Question.

The double-lap joint depicted in Fig. 9–26 consists of aluminum outer adherends and an inner steel adherend.

The assembly is cured at 250°F and is stress-free at 200°F.The completed bond is subjected to an axial load of 2000 lbf at a service temperature of 70°F. The width b is 1 in, the length of the bond l is 1 in. Additional information is tabulated below:

G, psi E, psi \(\alpha \ , \ in/(in .°F) \) Thickness, in
Adhesive \(0.2 \left(10^6\right)\) \(55 \left(10^{−6}\right)\) 0.020
Outer adherend \(10 \left(10^6\right)\) \(13.3 \left(10^{−6}\right)\) 0.150
Inner adherend \(30 \left(10^6\right)\) \(6.0 \left(10^{−6}\right)\) 0.100

Solution

In Eq. (9–7)

\(\tau \left(x\right) =\frac{P\omega }{4b\sinh \left({\omega l}/{2}\right) } \cosh \left(\omega x\right) +\left[\frac{P\omega }{4b\cosh \left({\omega l}/{2}\right) } \left(\frac{2E_ot_o-E_it_i}{2E_ot_o+E_it_i} \right)+ \\ \frac{\left(\alpha _i-\alpha _o\right)\Delta T\omega }{\left({1}/{E_o t_o}+{2}/{E_it_i}\right)\cosh \left({\omega l}/{2}\right) } \right] \sinh \left(\omega x\right) \)
the parameter \(\omega \) is given by
\(\omega =\sqrt{\frac{G}{h}\left(\frac{1}{E_o t_o} +\frac{2}{E_i t_i} \right) } \\ =\sqrt{\frac{0.2\left(10^6\right) }{0.020}\left[\frac{1}{10\left(10^6\right)0.15} + \frac{2}{30\left(10^6\right)0.10 } \right] } =3.62 \ in^{-1}\)

(a) For the thermal component, \( \alpha_i− \alpha _o= 6 \left(10^{−6}\right) − 13.3 \left(10^{−6}\right) = −7.3 \left(10^{−6} \right) \ in/(in.°F) \ , \Delta T = 70 − 200 = −130◦F\),
\( \tau_{th} \left(x\right) =\frac{\left(\alpha_i− \alpha_o \right) \Delta T \omega \sinh \left({\omega x}\right)}{\left({1}/{E_o t_o}+{2}/{E_i t_i}\right)\cosh \left({\omega l}/{2}\right)} \\ \)

\(\tau _{th}\left(x\right) =\frac{-7.3\left(10^{-6}\right)\left(-130\right)3.65 \sinh \left(3.65x\right) }{\left[\frac{1}{10\left(10^6\right)0.150 }+\frac{2}{30\left(10^6\right)0.100 } \right] \cosh \left[\frac{3.65}{2} \right] } \\ = 816.4 \sinh \left(3.65x\right) \)

The thermal stress is plotted in Fig. (9–27) and tabulated at \(x = −0.5 \ , \ 0 \ , and \ 0.5\) in the table below.
(b) The bond is “balanced” (\({E_o t_o= E_i t_i}/{2}\)), so the load-induced stress is given by \(\tau_P \left(x\right) = \frac{P \omega \cosh \left(\omega x\right)}{4b \sinh \left({\omega l}/{2}\right)}= \frac{2000 \left(3.65\right) \cosh \left(3.65 x\right)}{4 \left(1\right)3.0208} = 604.1 \cosh \left(3.65 x \right)]\) (1)

The load-induced stress is plotted in Fig. (9–27) and tabulated at \(x = −0.5 \ , \ 0\ , \ and 0.5 \)
in the table below.
(c) Total stress table (in psi):

\(\tau \left(-0.5\right)\) \(\tau \left(0\right)\) \(\tau \left(0.5\right)\)
Thermal only −2466 0 2466 0 2466
Load-induced only 1922 604 1922
Combined −544 604 4388

(d) The maximum shear stress predicted by the shear-lag model will always occur at the ends. See the plot in Fig. 9–27. Since the residual stresses are always present, significant shear stresses may already exist prior to application of the load. The large stresses present for the combined-load case could result in local yielding of a ductile adhesive or failure of a more brittle one. The significance of the thermal stresses serves as a caution against joining dissimilar adherends when large temperature changes are involved. Note also that the average shear stress due to the load is
\( \tau_{avg}= {P}/{ \left(2bl\right)} = 1000 \ psi\).
Equation (1) produced a maximum of 1922 psi, almost double the average.
