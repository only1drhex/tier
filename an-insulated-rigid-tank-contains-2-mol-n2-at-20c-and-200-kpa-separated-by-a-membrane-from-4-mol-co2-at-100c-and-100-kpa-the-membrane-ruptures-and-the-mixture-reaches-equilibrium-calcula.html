Guest Question.

An insulated, rigid tank contains 2 mol \( N _{2} \text { at } 20^{\circ} C \text { and } 200 kPa \text { separated by a membrane from } 4 mol CO _{2} \) at 100°C and 100 kPa. The membrane ruptures and the mixture reaches equilibrium. Calculate the final temperature and pressure.

Admin

This equation can be solved to yield the equilibrium temperature as T = 78.7°C. The initial volumes occupied by the gases are

\( V_{1}=\frac{N_{1} R_{u} T_{1}}{P_{1}}=\frac{(2)(8.314)(293)}{200}=24.36 m ^{3} \quad V_{2}=\frac{N_{2} R_{u} T_{2}}{P_{2}}=\frac{(4)(8.314)(373)}{100}=124 m ^{3} \).

The total volume remains fixed at 124.0 + 24.4 = 148.4 m³. The pressure is then

\( P=\frac{N R_{u} T}{V}=\frac{(6)(8.314)(273+78.7)}{148.4}=118.2 kPa \).
