Guest Question.

Use state feedback to improve the Dutch roll characteristics of an airplane. For this example we use a 2-degrees-of-freedom model to approximate the Dutch roll motion. The equations used to approximate the Dutch roll motion follow:

\(\left[\begin{matrix}\Delta \dot{\beta } \\ \Delta \dot{r } \end{matrix} \right]=\begin{bmatrix}Y_\beta /u_0 & -(1-Y_r /u_0) \\ N_\beta & N_r \end{bmatrix}\left[\begin{matrix}\Delta {\beta } \\ \Delta {r } \end{matrix} \right] + \begin{bmatrix}0 & Y_{\delta _r}/u_0) \\ N_{\delta _a} & N_{\delta _r} \end{bmatrix}\left[\begin{matrix}\Delta {\delta _a } \\ \Delta {\delta _r } \end{matrix} \right] \)

Assume that the stability derivatives have the following numerical values.

\(Y_\beta= - 19.5 ft/s^2 Y_{\delta _r}= 4.8 ft/s\)

 

\(Y_r= 1.3 ft/s N_{\delta _r}= -0.082 l/s^2 \)

 

\(N_\beta = 1.5 l/s^2 N_{\delta _a}= -0.008 l/s^2\)

 

\(N_r= -.21 l/s u_0 = 400 ft/s\)

The desired damping ratio for the Dutch roll motion is \(\zeta _{DR}=0.3\) and the undamped natural frequency is \(\omega _n= 1.0 rad/s.\)
 Substituting the numerical values of the stability derivatives into the Dutch roll equations yields

\(\left[\begin{matrix}\Delta \dot{\beta } \\ \Delta \dot{r } \end{matrix} \right]=\begin{bmatrix} - 0.049 & - 0.997 \\ 1.5 & - 0.21 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix}\Delta {\beta } \\ \Delta {r } \end{matrix} \right] +\begin{bmatrix}0 & 0.012 \\ - 0.008 & - 0.082\end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix}\Delta {\delta _a } \\ \Delta {\delta _r } \end{matrix} \right] \)
Admin
Solution

For a multiple-input system the control law is modified as follows:

\(\eta =-gk^Tx\)

where

\(\eta = \left[\begin{matrix}\Delta {\delta _a } \\ \Delta {\delta _r } \end{matrix} \right]\)

 

 

\(g= \left[\begin{matrix}g_1 \\ g_2 \end{matrix} \right]\)

 

 

\(k= \left[\begin{matrix}k_1 \\ k_2 \end{matrix} \right]\)

 

 

\(x=\left[\begin{matrix}\Delta {\beta } \\ \Delta {r } \end{matrix} \right]\)

The elements of the constant vector g represent a weighting of the relative authority of the ailerons and rudder for augmentation. For this problem we assume that \(g_1=1\) and \(g_2=\Delta \delta _r/\delta a\) is taken as the ratio of the maximum control deflection angles. With this assumption the maximum authority of the rudder and aileron would be achieved simultaneously. We assume that \(g_2=\Delta \delta _a/\Delta \delta r = 0.25\) for this problem. Substituting the control law into the state equation yields

\(\dot{x}=(A-Bgk^T)x \)

The augmented matrix \(A^\ast \) is given as

\(A^\ast = A-Bgk^T\)

The characteristic equation of the augmented system can now be determined:

\(\left|\lambda I-A^\ast \right|=0 \)

or

\(\left|\begin{bmatrix} \lambda & 0 \\ 0 & \lambda \end{bmatrix}- \begin{bmatrix} – 0.049 & – 0.997 \\ 1.5 & – 0.21 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}0 & 0.012 \\ – 0.008 & – 0.082\end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 0.25 \end{matrix} \right]\left[\begin{matrix} k_{1} & k_{2} \end{matrix} \right] \right| =0\)

 

\(\left|\begin{matrix} \lambda + 0.049 + 0.003k_1 &0.997 + 0.003k_2 \\- 1.5 – 0.0285k_1 & \lambda + 0.21 — 0.285k_2 \end{matrix} \right|=0 \)

Expanding this determinant yields the characteristic equation of the augmented matrix in terms of the unknown gains \(k_1 and k_2\)

\(\lambda ^4 + (0.259 + 0.003k_1 – 0 0285k_2)\lambda +1.51 + 0.029k_1 + 0.0031k_2 = 0\)

The desired characteristic equation can be expressed in terms of the Dutch roll damping ratio and frequency:

\(\lambda ^2 + 2\zeta _{DR}\omega _{n_{DR}} + \omega^2 _{n_{DR}}= 0\)

Substituting in the values for \(\zeta _{DR} and \omega _{n_{DR}}\) yields

\(\lambda ^2 + 0.6\lambda + 1.0 = 0\)

Equating the coefficients of the two polynomials yields a set of linear algebraic equations in terms of the unknown gains:

\(\begin{bmatrix} 0.003 & – 0.0285 \\ 0.029 & 0.0031 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix}k_1 \\ k_2 \end{matrix} \right]= \left[\begin{matrix}0.341 \\ – 0.51 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Solving for the gains yields

\(k=\left[\begin{matrix} – 16.1 \\ – 13.7 \end{matrix} \right]\)

Substituting the gain vector back into the control law yields

\(\eta =-gk^Tx\)

or

\(\left[\begin{matrix}\Delta {\delta _a } \\ \Delta {\delta _r } \end{matrix} \right]=-\left[\begin{matrix}g_1 \\ g_2 \end{matrix} \right] \left[\begin{matrix}k_1 & k_2 \end{matrix} \right]\left[\begin{matrix}\Delta {\beta } \\ \Delta {r } \end{matrix} \right]\)

 

 

\(\Delta {\delta _a } =-g_1k_1\Delta {\beta }- g_1 k_2\Delta {r }\)

 

 

\(\Delta {\delta _r } =-g_2k_1\Delta {\beta }- g_2 k_2\Delta {r }\)

For the selected values of g and the feedback gains determined here, we have the following control law:

\(\Delta {\delta _a } =16.1\Delta {\beta }+ 13.7\Delta {r }\)

 

 

\(\Delta {\delta _r } =4.0\Delta {\beta }+ 3.42\Delta {r }\)

This problem could have been solved using the Bass-.Gura method once the g matrix was selected. The augmented system for a single- and multiple-input system follow:

\(A^\ast = A – Bk^T \quad \quad \quad \text{Single-input system}\)

 

 

\(A^\ast = A – Bgk^T \quad \quad \quad \text{ Multiple-input system}\)

For a multiple-input system the control matrix B is \(n\times m\) where n is the order of the system and m is the number of control input signals. The constant matrix g is \(n\times 1\), therefore the matrix product Bg is \(n\times 1\).The Bass-Gura method can be used if we replace the control matrix by \(\bar{B} \) where

\(\bar{B}=Bg \)

The constant vector reduces the problem to determining n gains instead of \(n\times m\) gains. Now let us apply the Bass-Gura method to this problem. First we determine the modified control matrix \(\bar{B} \):

\(\bar{B}=Bg =\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0.01 2 \\ – 0.008 & – 0.082 \end{bmatrix}\left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 0.25 \end{matrix} \right] \)

 

\(\bar{B}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0.003 \\ – 0.0285 \end{matrix} \right] \)

 

The vector \(\bar{a}\) is determined from the desired characteristic equation

\(\lambda ^2+0.6\lambda + 1.0 = 0\)

or

\(\lambda ^2+\bar{a_1}\lambda + \bar{a_2} = 0\)

therefore

\(\bar{a}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0.6 \\ 1.0 \end{matrix} \right]\)

The vector a is determined from the characteristic equation of the plant matrix A. The characteristic equation for the A matrix can be calculated easily:

\(\lambda ^2+0.259\lambda + 1.5058 = 0\)

or

\(\lambda ^2+{a_1}\lambda + {a_2} = 0\)

therefore

\({a}=\left[\begin{matrix} 0.259 \\ 1.506 \end{matrix} \right]\)

The controllability matrix is defined in terms of \(\bar{B} and A\):

\(V=[\begin{matrix} \bar{B} & A \bar{B} \end{matrix} ] \)

 

\(=\begin{bmatrix}0.003 & 0.028 \\ -0.0285 & 0.011 \end{bmatrix} \)

Because the plant matrix is not in companion form we need to determine the transformation matrix W:

\(W=\begin{bmatrix}1 & a_1 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0.259 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\)

Finally the feedback gains can be calculated by:

\(k=[(VW)^T]^{-1}[\bar{a}-a]\)

where

\([(VW)^T]^{-1}=\begin{bmatrix} 3.71 & 3 4 .05 \\ – 34.70 & 3.58 \end{bmatrix} \)

and

\(k=\left[\begin{matrix}- 16.0 \\ – 13.7 \end{matrix} \right]\)
