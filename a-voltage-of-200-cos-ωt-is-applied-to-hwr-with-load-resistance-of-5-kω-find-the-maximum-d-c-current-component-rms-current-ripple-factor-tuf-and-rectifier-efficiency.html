Guest Question.

A voltage of 200 cos ωt is applied to HWR with load resistance of 5 kΩ. Find the maximum d.c. current component, rms current, ripple factor, TUF and rectifier efficiency,

Admin
Solution

Given Applied voltage = 200 cos ωt, \(V_{m}\) = 200 V, \(R_{L}\) = 5 kΩ
(a) To find d.c. current:

\(I_{m} =\frac{V_{m}}{R_{L}}=\frac{200}{5 × 10^{3}}\) = 40 mA

Therfore, \(I_{d.c.} =\frac{I_{m}}{\pi}=\frac{40 × 10^{– 3}}{\pi}= 12.7 × 10^{– 3}\) A = 12.73 mA

(b) To find rms current:

\(I_{rms} =\frac{I_{m}}{2}=\frac{40 × 10^{– 3}}{2}\) = 20 mA

(c) Ripple factor: Γ = \(\sqrt{( \frac{I_{rms}}{I_{d.c.}})^{2}– 1} = \sqrt{(\frac{20 × 10^{– 3}}{12.73 × 10^{– 3}})^{2} –1}\) = 1.21

(d) To determine TUF:

TUF = \(\frac{P_{d.c.}}{P_{a.c. (rated)}}\)

\(P_{d.c.} = I_{d.c.}^{2}R_{L} = (12.73 × 10^{– 3})^{2} × 5 × 10^{3}\) = 0.81 W

\(P_{a.c.(rated)} =\frac{V_{m}}{\sqrt{2}}×\frac{I_{m}}{2}=\frac{200}{\sqrt{2}}×\frac{40 × 10^{– 3}}{2}\) = 2.828

Therefore, TUF = \(\frac{P_{d.c.}}{P_{a.c.(rated)}}=\frac{0.81}{2.828}\)= 0.2863

(e) Rectifier Efficiency: η = \(\frac{P_{d.c.}}{P_{a.c.}}\)

\(P_{d.c.}\) = 0.81 W
\(P_{a.c.} = I_{rms}^{2}R_{L} = (20 × 10^{– 3})^{2} × 5 × 10^{3}\) = 2 W

Therefore, η = \(\frac{P_{d.c.}}{P_{a.c.}}× 100 =\frac{0.81}{2}\) × 100 = 40.5%
