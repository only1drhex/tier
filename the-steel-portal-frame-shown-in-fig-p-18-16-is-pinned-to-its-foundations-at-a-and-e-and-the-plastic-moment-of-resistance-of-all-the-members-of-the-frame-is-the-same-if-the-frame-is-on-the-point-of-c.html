Guest Question.

The steel portal frame shown in Fig. P.18.16 is pinned to its foundations at A and E and the plastic moment of resistance of all the members of the frame is the same. If the frame is on the point of collapse under the loading shown calculate the actual plastic moment of resistance. Sketch the bending moment diagram of the frame at the onset of collapse giving the value of the bending moment at each joint.

Ans. \(M_{ P }=47.83 kNm . M_{ B }=27.15 kNm . M_{ C }=21.57 kNm , M_{ D }=47.83 kNm\).

Admin
Solution

The collapse mechanism for the rafter is shown in Fig. S.18.16(a).

Then

\(M_{ P }(\theta+2 \theta+\theta)=30 \times 3 \theta\)

which gives

\(M_{ P }=22.5 kNm\)

The collapse mechanism for the sway case is shown in Fig. S.18.16(b).

Then

\(M_{ P }(\theta+\theta)=15 \times 5 \theta\)

that is

\(M_{ P }=37.5 kNm\)

The gable mechanism is shown in Fig. S.18.16(c).

Then

\(M_{ P }(0.5 \theta+1.5 \theta+1.9 \theta)=30 \times 3 \theta / 2\)

from which

\(M_{ P }=11.54 kNm\)

For the combined rafter plus gable case with a hinge cancellation at C and for a rotation of 2θ at B

\(6 M_{ P } \theta+3.9 M_{ P } \theta-2 \times 1.5 M_{ P } \theta=135 \theta+45 \theta\)

which gives

\(M_{ P }=26.09 kNm\)

For the combined rafter plus gable plus sway case with a hinge cancellation at B and for a rotation of 2θ at B

\(6 M_{ P } \theta+3.9 M_{ P } \theta-2 \times 1.5 M_{ P } \theta+4 M_{ P } \theta-2 \times 2 M_{ P } \theta=135 \theta+45 \theta+150 \theta\)

from which

\(M_{ P }=47.83 kNm\)

The last is therefore the critical case with hinges at the mid-point of BC and at D. Then, considering the right hand column as shown in Fig. S.18.16(d)

\(R_{ E , H } \times 5=47.83\)

so that

\(R_{ E , H }=9.57 kN\)

Now taking moments about A

\(R_{ E , V } \times 9=30 \times 3+15 \times 5\)

which gives

\(R_{ E , V }=18.33 kN\)

The bending moment diagram for the frame is then as shown in Fig. S.18.16(e).
