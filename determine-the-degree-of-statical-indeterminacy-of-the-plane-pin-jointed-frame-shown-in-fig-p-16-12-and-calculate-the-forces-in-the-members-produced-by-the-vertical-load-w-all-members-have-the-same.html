Guest Question.

Determine the degree of statical indeterminacy of the plane pin-jointed frame shown in Fig. P.16.12 and calculate the forces in the members produced by the vertical load, W. All members have the same length L and axial rigidity AE. Determine also the lack of fit required in the member 45 to reduce the force in the member 41 to zero.

Ans. 14 = -0.81 W, 24 = -0.92W, 25 = +0.23W, 53 = +0.35W, 45 = -0.23W,
46 = -0.58W, 56 = +0.58W, 67 = +0.58W, 47 = -1.15W.
1.73 WL/AE.

Admin
Solution

In this case the test for statical determinacy, Eq. (16.5),

\(n_{ s }=M-2 N+3\) (16.5)

breaks down since the number of members is 11 (counting the foundation as two members, 12 and 23) and the number of joints is 7. This gives \(n_{ s }=0\) but inspection reveals that the member 45 could be omitted without destabilising the truss (see Section 4.4 and the method of building a truss given with reference to Fig. 4.9). Further, the analysis by the method of joints could not proceed past joints 4 and 5. It is therefore concluded that the truss has a degree of statical indeterminacy equal to 1.
Choosing member 45 as the redundant member (note that members 47, 76, 46 and 56 could not be selected since this would result in parts of the truss becoming a mechanism) the \(F_{0}\) system is as shown in Fig. S.16.12(a) and the \(F_{1}\) system is as shown in Fig. S.16.12(b).

Table S.16.12 lists the member forces due to the two systems, these may be calculated by the method of joints.

The compatibility condition is

\(\Delta_{45}+a_{11} X_{1}=0\) (see Eq.(ii) of Ex.16.8) (i)

\(\Delta_{ BD }+X_{ BD } a_{ BD }=0\) Eq.(ii) of Ex.16.8

in which

\(\Delta_{45}=1.15 WL / AE\)

and

\(a_{11}=5 L / A E\)

Substituting in Eq. (i) gives

\(X_{1}=-0.23 W\)

Hence the forces \(F_{ a }\) in the members.
For \(F_{ a }\) in the member 41 to be zero

\(F_{0,41}+X_{1}^{\prime} F_{1,41}=0\)

Then

\(X_{1}^{\prime}=0.58 W\)

Therefore

\(\frac{1.15 W L}{A E}+\frac{0.58 W L}{A E}=\lambda\) (the initial lack of fit)

which gives

\(\lambda=\frac{1.73 WL }{A E}\)
