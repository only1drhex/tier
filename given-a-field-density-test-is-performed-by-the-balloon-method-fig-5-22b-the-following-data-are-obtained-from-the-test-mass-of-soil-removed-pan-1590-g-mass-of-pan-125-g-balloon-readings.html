Guest Question.

Given:

A field density test is performed by the balloon method [Fig. 5.22(b)]. The following data are obtained from the test:

\(\begin{aligned}\text { Mass of soil removed }+\text { pan } &=1590 g \\\text { Mass of pan } &=125 g\end{aligned}\)

Balloon readings:

\(\begin{array}{lr}\text { Final } & =1288 cm ^{3} \\\text { Initial } & =538 cm ^{3}\end{array}\)

Water-content information:

\(\begin{aligned}&\text { Mass of wet soil }+\text { pan }=404.9 g \\&\text { Mass of dry soil }+\text { pan }=365.9 g \\&\text { Mass of pan }=122.0g\end{aligned}\)

Required:

a. Compute the dry density and water content of the soil.

b. Using curve B of Fig. 5.1 as the laboratory standard, compute the relative compaction.

Admin
Solution

a. Compute the wet density, \(\rho=\frac{M_{t}}{V_{t}}\)

 

\(\rho=\frac{1590-125 g }{1288-538 cm ^{3}}=\frac{1465 g }{750 cm ^{3}}=1.95 g / cm ^{3}=1.95 Mg / m ^{3}\)

 

Water content determination:

1. Mass of wet soil + pan = 404.9 g

2. Mass of dry soil + pan = 365.9 g

3. Mass of water \(M_{w}( 1 – 2 )=39.0 g\)

4. Mass of pan = 122.0 g

5. Mass of dry soil \(M_{s}(2-4)=243.9 g\)

6. Water content \(\left(M_{w} / M_{s}\right) \times 100(3 \div 5)=16 \%\)

For calculation of dry density, use Eq. (2.14):

 

\(\rho_{d}=\frac{\rho}{1+w}\) (2.14)

 

\(\rho_{d}=\frac{\rho}{1+w}=\frac{1.95 Mg / m ^{3}}{1+0.16}=1.68 Mg / m ^{3}\)

 

b. ·For calculation of relative compaction, use Eq. ( 5.9):

 

\(\text { relative or percent compaction }(R C)=\frac{\rho_{d \text { field }}}{\rho_{d \max }} \times 100(\%)\) (5.9)

 

\(\text { R.C. }=\frac{\rho_{d \text { field }}}{\rho_{d \max }}=\frac{1.68}{1.86} \times 100=90.3 \%\)
