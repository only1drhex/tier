Guest Question.

A basement of a building requires a \(60 \times 60 \mathrm{~m}\) excavation \(2 \mathrm{~m}\) deep in a bed of sand \(25 \mathrm{~m}\) thick. The minimum drawdown expected at the center of the excavation is \(3 \mathrm{~m}\), and permeability of sand is \(k_{\text {sand }}=4.8 \times 10^{-4} \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\). Prior to the dewatering system being installed, the groundwater level (GWL) was found to be at ground surface. The proposed dewatering system is to be set back from the excavation \(5 \mathrm{~m}\), with each well having a nominal diameter of \(200 \mathrm{~mm}\) and pump operating capacity of \(3000 \mathrm{~L} / \mathrm{min}\).

a. Determine the drawdown level expected at the wells.

b. Select the most appropriate well system.

c. Determine the number of wells and spacing (assume \(\left.C=2,000(\mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s})^{-1 / 2}\right)\).

Admin
Solution

a. The profile of the ground is shown in Figure 15.7.

For an unconfined aquifer, we have:

\(q=\frac{k \pi\left(H_{2}^{2}-H_{1}^{2}\right)}{\ln \left(\frac{R_{2}}{R_{1}}\right)}\)

To calculate the expected drawdown at the wells, we can use the previous relationship, considering \(R_{1}=0.1 \mathrm{~m}\) (radius of the well) and \(R_{2}=35 \mathrm{~m}\) (center of the excavation) and \(h_{2}=(25-3)=\) \(22 \mathrm{~m}\) (center of excavation) and pump capacity of \(3000 \mathrm{~L} / \mathrm{min}\) or \(0.05 \mathrm{~m} 3 / \mathrm{s}\).

\(\begin{aligned}&0.05=\frac{4.8 \times 10^{-4} \pi\left(22^{2}-H_{1}^{2}\right)}{\ln \left(\frac{35}{0.1}\right)} \\ &\mathrm{H}_{1}=17.01 \mathrm{~m}\end{aligned}\)

Therefore, expected drawdown at the wells is \(25-17.02=7.98 \mathrm{~m}\).

Using Figure 15.1 (reproduced as Fig. 15.8), based on the expected drawdown and ground permeability, the most suitable dewatering system is the use of two stage well-points or deep wells.

For the design of the dewatering system (no. of wells and spacing), the total amount of water flow that needs to be pumped out from the excavation has to be computed.

For this, we can consider the excavation as a single well with an equivalent radius:

\(B \times L=\pi R_{w}^{2}\) or \(R_{w}=\sqrt{\frac{B \times L}{\pi}}\)

For this excavation \((60 \times 60 \mathrm{~m})\) with wells set back \(5 \mathrm{~m}\) away, then:

\(R_{w}=\sqrt{\frac{70 \times 70}{\pi}}=39.5 \mathrm{~m}\)

For an unconfined aquifer we have:

\(q=\frac{k \pi\left(H_{2}^{2}-H_{1}^{2}\right)}{\ln \left(\frac{R_{2}}{R_{1}}\right)}\)

In this problem, we can use the previous relationship, considering \(R_{w}=R_{1}\) and radius of influence of the pumped zone \(=R_{2}\) and \(H_{1}=(25-3)=22 \mathrm{~m}\) (center of excavation) and \(H_{2}=25 \mathrm{~m}\) (GWL at ground surface).

To compute the radius of influence of the pumped zone, Sichardt’s formula can be used (minimum drawdown \((\mathrm{H})\) at the center of the excavation \(=3 \mathrm{~m}\) ).

\(R_{0}=C \times H \times \sqrt{k}=2000 \times 3 \times \sqrt{4.8 \times 10^{-4}}=131.5 \mathrm{~m}\)

Then, substituting \(R_{0}=R_{2}\), the total amount of water flow can be computed as follows:

\(q=\frac{4.8 \times 10^{-4} \pi\left(25^{2}-22^{2}\right)}{\ln \left(\frac{131.5}{39.5}\right)}=0.177 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{s}\)

The principle of superposition can be used to calculate the combined drawdown of multiple wells. If each well pump has an operating capacity of \(3000 \mathrm{~L} / \mathrm{min}\) or \(0.05 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{s}\), then the required number of wells can be calculated as follows:

No. of wells \(=0.177 / 0.05=3.54\) wells

The number of wells required is 4 .

Well location on the excavation perimeter is \(70 \times 4=280 \mathrm{~m}\), then well spacing is approximately \(280 / 4=70 \mathrm{~m}\).
