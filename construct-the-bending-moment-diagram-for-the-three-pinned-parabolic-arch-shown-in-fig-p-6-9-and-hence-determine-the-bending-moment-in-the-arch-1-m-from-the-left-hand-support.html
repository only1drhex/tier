Guest Question.

Construct the bending moment diagram for the three-pinned parabolic arch shown in Fig. P.6.9 and hence determine the bending moment in the arch 1 m from the left-hand support.

Admin
Solution

Referring to Fig. S.6.9(a), the equation of the arch is

\( y^{2}=\frac{4 x}{9}(12-x) \) (i)

 

Taking moments about B to obtain the simply supported beam reactions

\( R_{ A , V } \times 12-6 \times 8-8 \times 2=0 \)

which gives

\( R_{ A , V }=5.33 kN \)

so that

\(R_{ B , V }=14-5.33=8.67 kN\)

The simply supported beam bending moment at C is given by

\( M_{ C }=5.33 \times 6-6 \times 2=20.0 kNm \)

Also the simply supported beam bending moment at D is given by

\( M_{ D }=5.33 \times 4=21.3 kNm \)

Now construct a parabola (see Fig. S.6.9(b)) using Eq. (i) in which the vertical height to the crown C represents 20.0 kNm. That is 4 m of height represents 20.0 kNm of bending moment, a scale of 1:5.

From Eq. (i)

\(\begin{array}{cccccc}x( m ) & 2 & 4 & 6 & 8 & 10 \\y( m ) & 2.98 & 3.77 & 4.0 & 3.77 & 2.98\end{array}\)

Using the scale of 1:5, superimpose the simply supported bending moment diagram on the arch diagram; the final bending moment at C must be zero and the simply supported beam bending moment at D is 21.3 kNm. This gives the final bending moment diagram for the arch as shown in Fig. S.6.9(b). Then the bending moment in the arch at 1 m from A is scaled from the final bending moment diagram and is 6.0 kNm.
