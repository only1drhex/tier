Guest Question.

A new extension for a port infrastructure is proposed. To reclaim the land for the new extension, a \(2.5 \mathrm{~m}\) thick layer of hydraulic fill \(\left(\gamma_{\text {sat }}=15 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\right)\) is placed over a \(6 \mathrm{~m}\) thick deposit of normally consolidated clay, resting on impermeable shale (Fig. 15.9). To accelerate drainage and consolidation of the clay layer, it is proposed to install a series of radial sand drains in sufficient number so that the time needed for \(90 \%\) overall consolidation to occur is reduced to just 2 years. The sand drains to be installed are \(200 \mathrm{~mm}\) in diameter and will be inserted throughout the clay layer using the triangular pattern. Laboratory tests revealed that the clay has a coefficient for vertical and horizontal consolidation of \(c_{v}=2.2 \mathrm{~m}^{2} /\) year and \(c_{h}=3 \mathrm{~m}^{2} /\) year, respectively.

Calculate the spacing between two consecutive drains needed to achieve the stated objective.

Admin
Solution

Calculate the time factor for vertical drainage only.

\(T_{v}=\frac{C_{v}}{H^{2}} t=\frac{2.2 \times 2}{6^{2}}=0.122\)

Then calculate the degree of consolidation due to vertical drainage only for the 2-year period using the Terzaghi chart (Fig. 15.3) reproduced from \(U_{v}\) versus \(T_{v}\) (Fig. 15.10).

From the graph:

\(U_{v} \approx 0.40\)

As the required overall degree of consolidation for 2 years is \(U=90 \%\) :

\((1-U)=\left(1-U_{v}\right)\left(1-U_{r}\right)\)

Therefore, in order to have an overall consolidation of \(90 \%\), the required radial degree of consolidation \(U_{r}\) for 2 years is:

\(\begin{aligned}&(1-0.9)=(1-0.4)\left(1-U_{r}\right) \\&U_{r}=0.83\end{aligned}\)

The sand drain must be designed so that the system reaches a minimum of \(0.83\) degrees of consolidation due to radial drainage.

To determine the drain spacing required for reaching the degree of consolidation of \(0.83\), an iterative process between an initial assumed \(n\) compared to an obtained \(n\) must be used.

Use Barron charts (Fig. 15.4, reproduced in Fig. 15.11) for radial consolidation.

Iteration 1: For \(U_{r}=0.83\) and \(n_{1}=10\), from the graph (Fig. 15.4, reproduced in Fig. 15.11, noting the relevant \(U_{r}\) ), \(T_{r} \approx 0.34\) (as drain diameter is \(200 \mathrm{~mm}, r_{w}=0.1 \mathrm{~m}\) ).

\(T_{r}=\frac{C_{h}}{4 r_{e}^{2}} t\) solving for \(r_{e}\) we have \(r_{e}=\left[\frac{C_{h}}{4 T_{r}} t\right]^{1 / 2}=\left[\frac{3}{4 \times 0.34} \times 2\right]^{1 / 2}=2.1\)

For this calculated \(r_{e}\), a new value of \(n=n_{2}\) can be found:

\(n_{2}=\frac{r_{e}}{r_{w}}=\frac{1}{r_{w}}\left[\frac{C_{h}}{4 T_{r}} t\right]^{1 / 2}=\frac{2.1}{0.1}=21\)

 

\(n_{1}=10 \neq n_{2}=21\)

Iteration 2: A new \(n\) is assumed, \(n_{3}=15\) can be considered.

Using the Barron chart for \(U_{r}=0.83\) and \(n_{3}=15\), then \(T_{r} \approx 0.43\).

A \(n\) value of \(n_{4}\) is:

\(n_{4}=\frac{r_{e}}{r_{w}}=\frac{1}{r_{w}}\left[\frac{C_{h}}{4 T_{r}} t\right]^{1 / 2}=\frac{1}{0.1}\left[\frac{3}{4 \times 0.43} \times 2\right]^{1 / 2}=18.7\)

\(n_{3} \neq n_{4}\) (i.e. different is too high)

Iteration 3: A new \(n\) is assumed, \(n_{5}=17\) can be considered.

Using the Barron chart for \(U_{r}=0.83\) and \(n_{5}=17\), then \(T_{r} \approx 0.46\).

A new \(n\) value of \(n_{6}\) is:

\(n_{6}=\frac{r_{e}}{r_{w}}=\frac{1}{r_{w}}\left[\frac{C_{h}}{4 T_{r}} t\right]^{1 / 2}=\frac{1}{0.1}\left[\frac{3}{4 \times 0.46} \times 2\right]^{1 / 2}=18\)

 

\(n_{6} \approx n_{5}\)

Since the drains are installed in a triangular pattern, then:

\(\begin{aligned}&n_{6}=\frac{r_{e}}{r_{w}}=18 \\&r_{e}=18 \times 0.1=1.8 \mathrm{~m} \\&R=r_{e}=1.8=0.525 \times S \\&S=3.4 \mathrm{~m}\end{aligned}\)

For improved efficiency and added reliability, keep the spacing of sand drains at \(3 \mathrm{~m}\).
