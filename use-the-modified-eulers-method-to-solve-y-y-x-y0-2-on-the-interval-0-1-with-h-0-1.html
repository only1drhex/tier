Guest Question.

Use the modified Euler’s method to solve y’ = y – x; Y(0) = 2 on the interval [0, 1] with h = 0.1.

Admin
Solution

Here \(f(x, y)=y-x, x_{0}=0, \text { and } y_{0}=2\). From Eq. (19.2) we have \(y_{0}^{\prime}=f(0,2)=2-0=2\). Then using Eqs. (19.4) and (19.3), we compute

\(\begin{array}{ll}n=0: & x_{1}=0.1 \\& p y_{1}=y_{0}+h y_{0}^{\prime}=2+0.1(2)=2.2 \\& p y_{1}^{\prime}=f\left(x_{1}, p y_{1}\right)=f(0.1,2.2)=2.2-0.1=2.1 \\& y_{1}=y_{0}+\frac{h}{2}\left(p y_{1}^{\prime}+y_{0}^{\prime}\right)=2+0.05(2.1+2)=2.205 \\& y_{1}^{\prime}=f\left(x_{1}, y_{1}\right)=f(0.1,2.205)=2.205-0.1=2.105\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}n=1: & x_{2}=0.2 \\p y_{2} &=y_{1}+h y_{1}^{\prime}=2.205+0.1(2.105)=2.4155 \\p y_{2}^{\prime} &=f\left(x_{2}, p y_{2}\right)=f(0.2,2.4155)=2.4155-0.2=2.2155 \\y_{2} &=y_{1}+\frac{h}{2}\left(p y_{2}^{\prime}+y_{1}^{\prime}\right)=2.205+0.05(2.2155+2.105)=2.421025 \\y_{2}^{\prime} &=f\left(x_{2}, y_{2}\right)=f(0.2,2.421025)=2.421025-0.2=2.221025\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{ll}n=2: & x_{3}=0.3 \\& p y_{3}=y_{2}+h y_{2}^{\prime}=2.421025+0.1(2.221025)=2.6431275 \\& p y_{3}^{\prime}=f\left(x_{3}, p y_{3}\right)=f(0.3,2.6431275)=2.6431275-0.3=2.3431275 \\& y_{3}=y_{2}+\frac{h}{2}\left(p y_{3}^{\prime}+y_{2}^{\prime}\right)=2.421025+0.05(2.3431275+2.221025)=2.6492326 \\& y_{3}^{\prime}=f\left(x_{3},y_{3}\right)=f(0.3,2.6492326)=2.6492326-0.3=2.3492326\end{array}\)
