Guest Question.

A simply supported beam has a span and loads as shown in Fig. 14.33. Select the lightest wide flange (W) section that can safely support the loads, including the weight of the beam. The allowable stresses are 24 ksi in bending and 14.5 ksi in shear.

Admin
Solution

The reactions and the shear-force and bending-moment diagrams are shown in . The maximum bending moment is at point B and is equal to 31.5 kip-ft or 378 kip-in., and the maximum shear force acts between A and B and is equal to 10.5 kip. We obtain a tentative beam selection by neglecting the weight of the beam. From Eq. (14.13),

\(S_{req} \geq \frac{M_{max}}{\sigma _a} = \frac{378}{24} = 15.75 in.^3 \)

The following shapes from of the Appendix have section moduli larger than 15.75 \(in.^3 \) and are the lightest shape in each depth group that satisfies the weight requirement.

W16 \(\times 26\) 38.4 \(in.^3 \)

W14 \( \times 22\) 29.0 \(in.^3 \)

W12 \( \times 16\) 17.1 \(in.^3 \lt \) lightest section

W10 \( \times 22\) 23.2 \(in.^3 \)

W8 \( \times 24\) 20.9 \(in.^3 \)

W6 \( \times 25\) 16.7 \(in.^3 \)

Check of W 12 \( \times\) 16 Beam: The beam weighs 16 lb/ft or 0.016 kip/ft. To find the additional bending moment at B(x = 3 ft) from the weight of the beam, we use Eq. (b) from .

\( M_{x=3 ft}= \frac{0.016(3)}{2} \) (12 – 3) = 0.216 kip-ft = 2.59 kip-in.

and the additional shear force at A from the weight of the beam is found from Eq. (c) of .

\(V_{max}=\frac{wl}{2} = \frac{0.016(12)}{2}\) = 0.096 = 0.1 kip

Accordingly, \(M_{max}\) = 378 + 2.59 = 380.6 kip-in. and \(V_{max}\) = 10.5 + 0.1 = 10.6 kip. From Eq. (14.13),

\(S_{req} \geq \frac{M_{max}}{\sigma _a} = \frac{380.6}{24} = 15.9 in.^3 \lt S_x = 17.1 in.^3 \) OK and from Eq. (14.14),

\(\tau _{max} =\frac{V_{max}}{dt} =\frac{10.6}{11.99(0.220)}=4.02 ksi \lt \tau _a = 14.5 ksi\) OK

In this example, the weight of the beam added about 1.0 percent to the required section modulus. It is usually safe to neglect the weight of a steel beam unless the required section modulus is close to the section modulus of the selected beam. However, the weight of a beam should be included in the design of a member that supports that beam.

Use the W12 \( \times\) 16 beam.
