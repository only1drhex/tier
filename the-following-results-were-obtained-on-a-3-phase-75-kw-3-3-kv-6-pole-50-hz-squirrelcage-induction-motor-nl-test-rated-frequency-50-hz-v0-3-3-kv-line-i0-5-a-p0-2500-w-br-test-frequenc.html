Guest Question.

The following results were obtained on a 3-phase, 75 kW, 3.3 kV, 6-pole, 50 Hz squirrelcage induction motor.

NL test: Rated frequency, 50 Hz

\( V_{0}\) = 3.3 kV (line), \( I_{0}\) = 5 A, \( P_{0}\) = 2500 W

BR test: Frequency 15 Hz

\( V_{BR}\) = 400 V (line), \( I_{BR}\) = 27 A, \( P_{BR}\) = 15000 W

DC test on stator Resistance/phase \( = 3.75 \Omega \)

(a) Determine the parameters of the circuit model (exact version).

(b) Find the parameters of Thevenin equivalent as seen from the rotor circuit.

(c) Calculate the maximum torque and the slip at which it occurs.

(d) For a slip of 4%, calculate the stator current, its pf and motor efficiency.

Calculate also internal motor torque

Admin
Solution

NL test

\( Z_{0}=\frac{3300 / \sqrt{3}}{5}=381 \Omega\)

 

\( R_{0}=\frac{2500 / 3}{(5)^{2}}=33.33 \Omega\)

 

\( X_{0}=\sqrt{(381)^{2}-(33.33)^{2}}=379.6 \Omega\)

 

\( X_{m}=X_{0}-X_{1}=? \)

BR test

\( Z_{B R}=\frac{400 / \sqrt{3}}{27}=8.55 \Omega\)

 

\( R_{B R}=\frac{15000 / 3}{(27)^{2}}=6.86 \Omega\)

 

\( X_{B R}=\sqrt{(8.55)^{2}-(6.86)^{2}}=5.10 \Omega\)

 

\( X_{1}=X_{2}^{\prime}=X_{B R} / 2=2.55 \Omega \text { (at } 15 Hz \text { ); } 8.50 \Omega\) (at 50 Hz)

\( \therefore\) \( X_{m}=379.6-8.50=371 \Omega\)

 

\( R_{2}^{\prime}=\left(R_{B R}-R_{1}\right)\left[\frac{X_{m}+X_{2}^{\prime}}{X_{m}}\right]^{2}=(6.86-3.75) \times\left[\frac{371+8.50}{371}\right]^{2}=3.25 \Omega\)

(a) The circuit model is drawn in Fig. 9.26(a).

(b) \( \bar{V}_{T H}=(3300 / \sqrt{3}) \angle 0^{\circ} \times \frac{j 371}{3.75 j(371+8.5)}=1862 \angle 0.6^{\circ} V\)

 

\( \bar{Z}_{T H}=\frac{j 371 \times(3.75+j 8.5)}{3.75+j(371+8.5)}=9.08 \angle 66.8^{\circ}=3.58+j 8.35=R_{T H}+j X_{T H}\)

The corresponding circuit is drawn in Fig. 9.26(b)

(c) For maximum torque to be developed

\( \frac{3.25}{s_{\max , T}}=\sqrt{(3.58)^{2}+(8.35+8.50)^{2}}\)

Or \( s_{\max , T}=\frac{3.25}{17.22}=0.189\)

Or \( R_{2}^{\prime} / s_{\max , T}=\frac{3.25}{0.189}=17.2 \Omega\)

 

\( n_{s}=1000 rpm , \omega_{s}=104.72\)

 

\( \bar{Z}_{\text {total }}=(3.58+17.22)+j 16.85=20.80+j 16.85=26.77 \angle 39^{\circ} \Omega\)

 

\( I_{2}^{\prime}=\frac{1862}{26.77}=69.56 A\)

 

\( T_{\max }=\frac{3 \times(69.56)^{2} \times 17.2}{104.72}=2.387 Nm\)

(d) As the stator current and its pf are required, we shall proceed by using the circuit of Fig. 9.26(a)

\( \bar{Z}_{f}=\frac{j 371(81.25+j 8.50)}{81.25+j 379.50}=78.11 \angle 18.1^{\circ}\)

 

\( =74.24+j 24.26=R_{f}+j X_{f}\)

 

\( \bar{Z}_{i n}=(3.75+j 8.5)+(74.26+j 24.26)=84.60 \angle 22.8^{\circ}\)

 

\( I_{1}=\frac{3300 / \sqrt{3}}{84.60}=22.52 A\)

 

\( p f=\cos 22.8^{\circ}=0.922\) lagging

 

Gross mechanical output \( =(1-s) P_{G}=(1-0.04) \times 3 \times(22.52)^{2} \times 74.24=108.43 kW\)

Rotational loss \( =2.500-(5)^{2} \times 3.75=2.41 kW\)

Net mechanical output = 108.42 – 2.41 = 106.1 kW

\( \eta=\frac{106.21}{118.42}=89.1 \% \)

Internal torque developed \( =(108.43 \times 1000) /[104.72(1-0.04)]=1078.6 Nm\)
