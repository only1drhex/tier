Guest Question.

Solve the system

\(2x_{1} + 3x_{2} + x_{3} = −4\)

 

\( 4x_{1} + x_{2} + 4x_{3} = 9\)

 

\(3x_{1} + 4x_{2} + 6x_{3} = 0\)
Admin
Solution

The coefficient matrix for this system is the matrix A in Example 1. Since L and U

have been determined, the system can be solved by forward and back substitution:

\(\left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 0& 0& | & -4 \\ 2 &1 & 0 &| & 9 \\ \frac{3}{2} & \frac{1}{10} &1&| & 0 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup\) \(\begin{matrix} y_{1} = −4\\y_{2} = 9 − 2y_{1} = 17\\ y_{3} = 0 − \frac{3}{2} y_{1} − \frac{1}{10} y_{2} = 4.3\end{matrix} \)

 

\(\left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 2 & 3& 1& | & -4 \\ 0 &-5 & 2 &| & 17 \\ 0 &0 &4.3&| & 4.3 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup \) \(\begin{matrix}2x_{1} + 3x_{2} + x_{3} = −4& x_{1} = 2\\− 5x_{2} + 2x_{3} = 17& x_{2} = −3 \\4.3x_{3} = 4.3& x_{3} = 1\end{matrix} \)

The solution of the system is \(x = (2, −3, 1)^{T}\) .
