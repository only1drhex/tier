Guest Question.

Linear Momentum—Thrust

GIVEN A static thrust stand as sketched in Fig. E5.15 is to be designed for testing a jet engine. The following conditions are known for a typical test: Intake air velocity = 200 m/s; exhaust gas velocity = 500 m/s; intake cross-sectional area \(=1 m ^{2}\); intake static pressure = -22.5 kPa = 78.5 kPa (abs); intake static temperature = 268 K; exhaust static pressure = 0 kPa = 101 kPa (abs).

FIND Estimate the nominal anchoring force for which to design.

Admin
Solution

The cylindrical control volume outlined with a dashed line in Fig. E5.15 is selected. The external forces acting in the axial direction are also shown. Application of the momentum equation (Eq. 5.22) to the contents of this control volume yields

 

\(\frac{\partial}{\partial t} \int_{ cv } V \rho d \forall+\int_{ cs } V \rho V \cdot \hat{ n } d A=\sum F _{\substack{\text { contents of the } \\ \text { control volume }}}\) (5.22)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\int_{ cs } u \rho V \cdot \hat{ n } d A=& p_{1} A_{1}+F_{\text {th }}-p_{2} A_{2} \\&-p_{ atm }\left(A_{1}-A_{2}\right)\end{aligned}\) (1)

 

where the pressures are absolute. Thus, for one-dimensional flow, Eq. 1 becomes

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left(+u_{1}\right)\left(-\dot{m}_{1}\right)+\left(+u_{2}\right)\left(+\dot{m}_{2}\right)=&\left(p_{1}-p_{\text {atm }}\right) A_{1} \\&-\left(p_{2}-p_{\text {atm }}\right) A_{2}+F_{\text {th }}\end{aligned}\) (2)

 

The positive direction is to the right. The conservation of mass equation (Eq. 5.12) leads to

 

\(\dot{m}=\rho_{1} A_{1} \bar{V}_{1}=\rho_{2} A_{2} \bar{V}_{2}\) (5.12)

 

\(\dot{m}=\dot{m}_{1}=\rho_{1} A_{1} u_{1}=\dot{m}_{2}=\rho_{2} A_{2} u_{2}\) (3)

 

Combining Eqs. 2 and 3 and using gage pressure we obtain

 

\(\dot{m}\left(u_{2}-u_{1}\right)=p_{1} A_{1}-p_{2} A_{2}+F_{\text {th }}\) (4)

 

Solving Eq. 4 for the thrust force, \(F_{\text {th }},\) we obtain

 

\(F_{\text {th }}=-p_{1} A_{1}+p_{2} A_{2}+\dot{m}\left(u_{2}-u_{1}\right)\) (5)

 

We need to determine the mass flowrate, \(\dot{m} \text {, to calculate } F_{\text {th }} \text {, }\) and to calculate \(\dot{m}=\rho_{1} A_{1} u_{1}\), we need \(\rho_{1}\). From the ideal gas equation of state

 

\(\begin{aligned}\rho_{1} &=\frac{p_{1}}{R T_{1}}=\frac{(78.5 kPa )(1000 Pa / kPa )\left[1\left( N / m ^{2}\right) / Pa \right]}{(286.9 J / kg \cdot K )(268 K )(1 N \cdot m / J )} \\&=1.02 kg / m ^{3}\end{aligned}\)

 

Thus,

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{m} &=\rho_{1} A_{1} u_{1}=\left(1.02 kg / m ^{3}\right)\left(1 m ^{2}\right)(200 m / s ) \\&=204 kg / s\end{aligned}\) (6)

 

Finally, combining Eqs. 5 and 6 and substituting given data with \(p_{2}=0\), we obtain

 

\(\begin{aligned}F_{\text {th }}=&-\left(1 m ^{2}\right)(-22.5 kPa )(1000 Pa / kPa )\left[1\left( N / m ^{2}\right) / Pa \right] \\&+(204 kg / s )(500 m / s -200 m / s )\left[1 N /\left( kg \cdot m / s ^{2}\right)\right]\end{aligned}\)

 

or

 

\(F_{\text {th }}=22,500 N +61,200 N =83,700 N\) (Ans)

 

COMMENT The force of the thrust stand on the engine is directed toward the right. Conversely, the engine pushes to the left on the thrust stand 1or aircraft2.
