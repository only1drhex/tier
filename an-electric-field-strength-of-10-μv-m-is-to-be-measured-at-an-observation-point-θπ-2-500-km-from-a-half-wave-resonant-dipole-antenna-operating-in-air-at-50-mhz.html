Guest Question.

An electric field strength of 10 μV /m is to be measured at an observation point θ=π/2, 500 km from a half-wave (resonant) dipole antenna operating in air at 50 MHz.
(a) What is the length of the dipole?
(b) Calculate the current that must be fed to the antenna.
( c) Find the average power radiated by the antenna.
(d) If a transmission line with \(Z_{0}\)=75 Ω is connected to the antenna, determine the standing wave ratio.

Admin
Solution

(a) The wavelength \(\lambda =\frac{c}{f}=\frac{3\times 10^{8}}{50\times 10^{6}}=6m.\)

Hence, the length of the half-dipole is \(\ell =\frac{\lambda }{2}=3m.\)

(b) From eq. (13.19),

\(H_{\phi s}=\frac{jI_{0}e^{-j\beta r}\cos (\frac{\pi}{2} \cos \theta )}{2\pi r \sin \theta }, E_{\theta s}=\eta H_{\phi s} \)(13.19)
\(|E_{\theta s}|=\frac{\eta _{0}I_{0}\cos(\frac{\pi}{2} \cos\theta )}{2\pi r \sin \theta }\)
or
\(I_{0}=\frac{|E_{\theta s}|2\pi r \sin \theta}{\eta _{0}\cos(\frac{\pi}{2} \cos\theta )}\)
\(=\frac{10\times 10^{-6}2\pi (500\times 10^{3}).(1)}{120 \pi (1)}\)
=83.33 mA
(c) \(R_{rad}\simeq 37 \Omega\)
\(P_{rad }=\frac{1}{2}I_{0}^{2}R_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}(83.33)^{2}\times 10^{-6}\times 73\)
=253.5 mW
(d) \(\Gamma =\frac{Z_{L}-Z_{0}}{Z_{L}+Z_{0}}(Z_{L}=Z_{in}\) in this case)
\(=\frac{73+j42.5-75}{73+j42.5-75}=\frac{-2+j42.5}{148+j42.5}\)
\(=\frac{42.55\angle 92.69^{\circ }}{153.98\angle 16.02^{\circ }}=0.2763\angle 76.67^{\circ }\)
\(s=\frac{1+|\Gamma |}{1-|\Gamma |}=\frac{1+0.2763}{1-0.2763}=1.763\)
