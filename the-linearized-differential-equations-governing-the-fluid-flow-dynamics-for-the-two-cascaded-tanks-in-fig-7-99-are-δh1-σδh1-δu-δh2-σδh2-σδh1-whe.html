Guest Question.

The linearized differential equations governing the fluid-flow dynamics for the two cascaded tanks in Fig. 7.99 are

\(\delta \dot{h}_{1}+\sigma \delta h_{1}=\delta u\),

\(\delta \dot{h}_{2}+\sigma \delta h_{2}=\sigma \delta h_{1}\),

where,

\(\delta h_{1}\) = deviation of depth in tank 1 from the nominal level,

\(\delta h_{2}\) = deviation of depth in tank 2 from the nominal level,

\(\delta u\) = deviation in fluid in ow rate to tank 1 (control).

a) Level Controller for Two Cascaded Tanks: Using state feedback of the form

\(\delta u=-K_{1} \delta h_{1}-K_{2} \delta h_{2}\),

choose values of \(K_{1}\) and \(K_{2}\) that will place the closed-loop eigenvalues at,

\(s=-2 \sigma(1 \pm j)\).

b) Level Estimator for two Cascaded Tanks: Suppose that only the deviation in the level of tank 2 is measured (that is, \(y=\delta h_{2}\)). Using this measurement, design an estimator that will give continuous, smooth estimates of the deviation in levels of tank 1 and tank 2, with estimator error poles at – 8σ(1 ± j).

c) Estimator/Controller for Two Cascaded Tanks: Sketch a block diagram (showing individual integrators) of the closed-loop system obtained by combining the estimator of part (b) with the controller of part (a).

d) Using MATLAB, compute and plot the response at y to an initial offset in \(\delta h_{1}\). Assume σ = 1 for the plot.

Admin
Solution

(a) Comparing coefficients of like powers of s,

\(\operatorname{det}\left[\begin{array}{cc} s+\sigma+K_{1} & K_{2} \\ -\sigma & s+\sigma\end{array}\right]=s^{2}+\left(2 \sigma+K_{1}\right) s+\sigma^{2}+\sigma\left(K_{1}+K_{2}\right)=0 \).

 

\(=(s+2 \sigma+2 \sigma j)(s+2 \sigma-2 \sigma j)=s^{2}+4 \sigma s+8 \sigma^{2}=0\),

gives \(K_{1}=2 \sigma\) and \(K_{2}=5 \sigma\).

(b) Comparing coefficients of like powers of s,

\(\operatorname{det}\left[\begin{array}{cc} s+\sigma & l_{1} \\- \sigma & s+\sigma+l_{2}\end{array}\right]=s^{2}+\left(2 \sigma+l_{1}\right) s+\sigma\left(l_{1}+l_{2}\right)+\sigma^{2}=0 \)

 

\(=(s+8 \sigma+8 \sigma j)(s+8 \sigma-8 \sigma j)=s^{2}+16 \sigma s+128 \sigma^{2}=0\).

gives \(l_{1}=113 \sigma\) and \(l_{2}=14 \sigma\).
