Guest Question.

(a) Calculate the transition rate from the first excited state to the ground state for an isotropic (three-dimensional) harmonic oscillator of charge q.

(b) Find a numerical value for the rate calculated in (a) as well as the lifetime of the first excited state for the case of an electron \(\text { (i.e., } m_{e} c^{2}=0.511 MeV \text { ) }\) oscillating with a frequency of an optical radiation \(\omega \simeq 10^{15} rad s ^{-1}\).

Admin
Solution

As mentioned in Chapter 6, the ground state of an isotropic harmonic oscillator is a 1s state, (n, l, m) = (0, 0, 0) , whose energy and wave function are \(E_{0}=3 \hbar \omega / 2\) and

\(\psi_{000}(r, \theta, \phi)=R_{00}(r) Y_{00}(\theta, \phi)=\frac{2}{\sqrt{\sqrt{\pi}}}\left(\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}\right)^{3 / 4} e^{-m \omega r^{2} / 2 \hbar} Y_{00}(\theta, \phi)\), (10.231)

and the first excited state is a 1p state (n, l, m) = (1, 1, m) whose energy and wave function are \(E_{1}=5 \hbar \omega / 2\) and

\(\psi_{11 m}(r, \theta, \phi)=R_{11}(r) Y_{1 m}(\theta, \phi)=\sqrt{\frac{8}{3 \sqrt{\pi}}}\left(\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}\right)^{5 / 4} r e^{-m \omega r^{2} / 2 \hbar} Y_{1 m}(\theta, \phi)\). (10.232)

Using \(\int_{0}^{\infty} x^{4} e^{-x^{2}} d x=\frac{3}{8} \sqrt{\pi}\) along with a change of variable \(x=\sqrt{m \omega / \hbar} r\), we have

\(\int_{0}^{\infty} r^{3} R_{11}^{*}(r) R_{10}(r) d r=4 \sqrt{\frac{2}{3 \pi}}\left(\frac{m \omega}{\hbar}\right)^{2} \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{4} e^{-m \omega r^{2} / \hbar} d r=\sqrt{\frac{3 \hbar}{2 m \omega}}\). (10.233)

(a) The transition rate for a 1p â†’ 1s transition is given by

\(W_{1 p \rightarrow 1 s }=\frac{4 q^{2} \omega_{1 p \rightarrow 1 s }^{3}}{3 \hbar c^{3}} \frac{1}{3} \sum_{m=-1}^{1}|\langle 11 m|\vec{r}| 000\rangle|^{2}\)

 

\(=\frac{4 q^{2} \omega_{1 p \rightarrow 1 S }^{3}}{9 \hbar c^{3}} \sum_{m=-1}^{1}\left[|\langle 11 m|\hat{x}| 000\rangle|^{2}+|\langle 11 m|\hat{y}| 000\rangle|^{2}+|\langle 21 m|\hat{z}| 000\rangle|^{2}\right] .\) (10.234)

Since \(x=r \sin \theta \cos \phi=-\sqrt{2 \pi / 3} r\left(Y_{11}-Y_{1-1}\right), y=r \sin \theta \sin \phi=i \sqrt{2 \pi / 3} r\left(Y_{11}+Y_{1-1}\right)\), and \(z=r \cos \theta=\sqrt{4 \pi / 3} r Y_{10}\), and using (10.233), we can show by analogy with (10.226) to (10.228) that

\(\langle 11 m|\hat{x}| 000\rangle=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{4 \pi}} \sqrt{\frac{2 \pi}{3}} \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{3} R_{11}^{*}(r) R_{00}(r) d r \int Y_{1 m}^{*}(\Omega)\left(Y_{11}-Y_{1-1}\right) d \Omega\)

 

\(=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{6}} \sqrt{\frac{3 \hbar}{2 m \omega}}\left(\delta_{m, 1}-\delta_{m,-1}\right)\), (10.235)

 

\(\langle 11 m|\hat{y}| 000\rangle=\frac{i}{\sqrt{4 \pi}} \sqrt{\frac{2 \pi}{3}} \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{3} R_{11}^{*}(r) R_{00}(r) d r \int Y_{1 m}^{*}\left(Y_{11}+Y_{1-1}\right) d \Omega\)

 

\(=\frac{i}{\sqrt{6}} \sqrt{\frac{3 \hbar}{2 m \omega}}\left(\delta_{m, 1}+\delta_{m,-1}\right)\), (10.236)

 

\(\langle 11 m|\hat{z}| 000\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{4 \pi}} \sqrt{\frac{4 \pi}{3}} \int_{0}^{\infty} r^{3} R_{11}^{*}(r) R_{00}(r) d r \int Y_{1 m}^{*} Y_{10} d \Omega=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \sqrt{\frac{3 \hbar}{2 m \omega}} \delta_{m, 0} .\) (10.237)

A combination of the previous three relations leads to

\(\sum_{m=-1}^{1}|\langle 11 m|\vec{r}| 000\rangle|^{2}=\frac{3 \hbar}{2 m \omega} \sum_{m}\left[\frac{1}{6}\left(\delta_{m, 1}-\delta_{m,-1}\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{6}\left(\delta_{m, 1}+\delta_{m,-1}\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3} \delta_{m, 0}^{2}\right]\)

 

\(=\frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega} \sum_{m=-1}^{1}\left(\delta_{m,-1}+\delta_{m, 1}+\delta_{m, 0}\right)=\frac{3 \hbar}{2 m \omega}\). (10.238)

Substituting (10.238) into (10.234), and using \(\omega_{1 p \rightarrow 1 s}=\left(E_{1}-E_{0}\right) / \hbar=\left(\frac{5}{2}-\frac{3}{2}\right) \omega=\omega\), we obtain

\(W_{1 p \rightarrow 1 s }=\frac{4 q^{2} \omega_{1 p \rightarrow 1 s }^{3}}{9 \hbar c^{3}} \frac{3 \hbar}{2 m \omega}=\frac{2 q^{2} \omega^{2}}{3 m c^{3}}\). (10.239)

(b) In the case of an electron (q = -e and \(\left.m_{e} c^{2}=0.511 MeV \right)\) which is oscillating with a frequency of \(\omega \simeq 10^{15} s ^{-1}\), the transition rate is

\(W_{1 p \rightarrow 1 S }=\frac{2 e^{2} \omega^{2}}{3 m_{e} c^{3}}=\frac{2 \alpha}{3}\left(\frac{\hbar c}{m_{e} c^{2}}\right) \frac{\omega^{2}}{c}\)

 

\(=\frac{2}{3} \frac{1}{137}\left(\frac{197 MeV fm }{0.511 MeV }\right) \frac{10^{30} s ^{-2}}{3 \times 10^{8} m s ^{-1}} \simeq 0.64 \times 10^{7} s ^{-1}\), (10.240)

where \(\alpha=e^{2} /(\hbar c)=1 / 137\) is the fine structure constant. The lifetime of the 1p state for the oscillator is given by

\(\tau=\frac{1}{W_{1 p \rightarrow 1 s }}=\frac{3 m_{e} c^{3}}{2 e^{2} \omega^{2}} \simeq \frac{1}{0.64 \times 10^{7} s ^{-1}}=1.56 \times 10^{-7} s\). (10.241)
