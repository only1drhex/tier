Guest Question.

Two 50-cm horizontal square plates are separated by a distance of 1 cm. The lower plate is
maintained at a constant temperature of \(100^{◦} F \) and the upper plate is constant at \(80^{◦} F \) . Water at atmospheric pressure occupies the space between the plates. Calculate the heat lost by the lower plate.

Admin
Solution

We evaluate properties at the mean temperature of \(90^{◦} F \) and obtain, for water,

\(k =0.623 \enspace W/m·^{ ◦} C\hspace{30 pt} \frac{gβ_{ρ} ^{2} c_{p} }{μk}=2.48×10^{10} \)

The Grashof-Prandtl number product is now evaluated using the plate spacing of 1 cm as the characteristic dimension.
\(Gr Pr =(2.48×10^{10} )(0.01)^{3} (100−80)(5/9)=2.76×10^{5} \)

Therefore, Equation (7-64)

\( \frac{k_{e} }{k} =C(Gr_{δ}\; Pr)^{n }(\frac{L}{\delta } )^{m} \hspace{30 pt} [7-64] \)

 

becomes \(C=0.13\qquad n=0.3\qquad m=0 \)

Now, using Equation (7-64)

\( \frac{k_{e} }{k} =C(Gr_{δ}\; Pr)^{n }(\frac{L}{\delta } )^{m} \hspace{30 pt} [7-64] \)

and consulting Table 7-3 we obtain

\( \frac{k_{e} }{k} =(0.13)(2.76×10^{5} )^{0.3}=5.57 \)

The effective thermal conductivity is thus

\( k_{e} =(0.623)(5.57)=3.47\enspace W/m· ^{◦} C \)

and the heat transfer is

\( q=k_{e} A\Delta T/δ=\frac{(3.47)(0.5)^{2} (100−80)(5/9)}{0.01}=964 \; W \)

We see, of course, that the heat transfer across a water gap is considerably larger than for an air
gap because of the larger thermal conductivity.
