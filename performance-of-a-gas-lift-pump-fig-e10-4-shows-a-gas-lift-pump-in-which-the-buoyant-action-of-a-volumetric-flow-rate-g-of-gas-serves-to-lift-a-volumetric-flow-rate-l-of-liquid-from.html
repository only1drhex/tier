Guest Question.

Performance of a Gas-Lift Pump

Fig. E10.4 shows a gas-lift “pump,” in which the buoyant action of a volumetric flow rate \(G\) of gas serves to lift a volumetric flow rate \(L\) of liquid from a height \(H_{0}\) in a reservoir to a height \(H\) in a vertical pipe of diameter \(D\) and cross-sectional area \(A\), in which slug flow may be assumed. Neglect liquid friction in both the supply pipe and the vertical pipe.

(a) Derive an expression for the void fraction \(\varepsilon\) in the column in terms of \(H\) and \(H_{0}\).

(b) If \(L=0\) (that is, the liquid is just on the verge of reaching the top), prove that the height \(H\) is given by:

\(\frac{H}{H_{0}}=\frac{1.2 G / A+u_{b}}{0.2 G / A+u_{b}},\)

where \(u_{b}=0.35 \sqrt{g D}\).

(c) If the value of \(G / A\) is much larger than \(u_{b}\), what is the maximum height \(H_{\max }\) to which the liquid can be raised (still with \(\left.L=0\right) ?\) Give your answer as a multiple of \(H_{0}\).

(d) If the column has a diameter \(D=0.1 \mathrm{~m}\) and operates with a positive liquid flow rate such that \(\varepsilon=0.5\) and \(G / A=2 L / A\), what is the value of \(G / A(\mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s})\) ?

Admin
Solution

(a) From hydrostatics, the pressure at the base of the column is obtained in two ways:

\(\rho_{l} g H_{0}=\rho_{l} g H(1-\varepsilon),\) (E10.4.1)

which gives:

\(\varepsilon=1-\frac{H_{0}}{H}.\) (E10.4.2)

(b) From Eqn. (10.38), with \(L=0\) and \(u_{b}=0.35 \sqrt{g D}\) :

\(\varepsilon=\frac{G}{1.2(G+L)+0.35 A \sqrt{g D}}=\frac{j_{g}^{*} \sqrt{\frac{\rho_{l}}{\rho_{g}}}}{1.2\left(j_{g}^{*} \sqrt{\frac{\rho_{l}}{\rho_{g}}}+j_{l}^{*}\right)+0.35},\) (10.38)

\(\varepsilon=\frac{G / A}{1.2 G / A+u_{b}}=1-\frac{H_{0}}{H} .\) (E10.4.3)

Rearrangement and solution for the height ratio yields the desired result:

\(\frac{H}{H_{0}}=\frac{1.2 G / A+u_{b}}{0.2 G / A+u_{b}}.\) (E10.4.4)

(c) For \(G / A \gg u_{b}\), the velocity \(u_{b}\) in the numerator and denominator of Eqn. (E10.4.4) can be neglected, so that:

\(\frac{H}{H_{0}}=\frac{1.2 G / A}{0.2 G / A}=6.\) (E10.4.5)

Thus, the maximum height attainable is six times the reservoir height \(H_{0}\).

(d) Inserting numerical values:

\(\begin{array}{c}u_{b}=0.35 \sqrt{g D}=0.35 \sqrt{9.81 \times 0.1}=0.347 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}. \\ \end{array}\) (E10.4.6)

\(\begin{array}{c}\frac{G}{\varepsilon A}=1.2\left(\frac{G}{A}+\frac{L}{A}\right)+u_{b} \quad \text { or } \quad 2 \frac{G}{A}=1.2 \times 1.5 \frac{G}{A}+0.347.\end{array}\) (E10.4.7)

The superficial velocity of the gas is therefore:

\(\frac{G}{A}=1.733 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s},\) (E10.4.8)

which then suffices to achieve the desired liquid flow rate.
