Guest Question.

A flyback converter has the following specifications:

\(V_{in} = 24 V, L_p\) = primary leakage inductance = \(10 \mu H, L_s\) = secondary leakage inductance = \(10 \mu H, L_m\) = magnetizing inductance = \(100 \mu H, C = 200 \mu F, f_s = 40 kHz, R = 5 \Omega, \overline{V}_o = 5 V\), and \(N_1/N_2 = 3\).

Calculate: (a) the duty cycle; (b) the average, maximum, and minimum values of the magnetizing current; (c) the switch maximum voltage; (d) the output voltage peak-to-peak ripple; and (e) verify the theoretical results with their respective simulations.

Admin
Solution

a) Since the magnetizing inductance and the output current have low values the magnetizing current is assumed to be discontinuous and, consequently, using Eq. (\(\overline{V}_o = V_{in}D \sqrt{\frac{R}{2L_mf_s}}\)) the duty cycle is:

\(D =\frac{\overline{V}_o }{V_{in}\sqrt{\frac{R}{2L_mf_s}}}= \frac{5}{24\sqrt{\frac{5}{2\cdot 100 \times 10^{-6} \cdot 40 \times 10^3}}} = \frac{5}{24 \times 0.79} = 0.264\)

b) The average, maximum, and minimum values of the magnetizing current are:

\(\overline{I}_{Lm}= \frac{V_{in}D^2T_s}{2L_m} = \frac{24 \times 0.264^2}{2\cdot 100 \times 10^{-6} \times 40 \times 10^3} = 0.2091 A\)

 

\(I_{Lm,max} =\frac{V_{in}DT_s}{L_m}=\frac{24 \times 0.264}{100 \times 10^{-6} \times 40 \times 10^3} =1.584 A\)

 

\(I_{Lm,min} = 0\)

From the value of \(I_{L,min}\) the initial assumption that the inductor current is discontinuous is satisfied.

c) When the switch S is off the drain to source voltage according to Fig. 7.18(b) is:

\(V_{DS} = V_{in} – v_{primary} = V_{in} + v_{secondary}(\frac{N_1}{N_2})\)

 

\(= V_{in} + \overline{V}_o \frac{N_1}{N_2}\)

 

\(= 24 + 5 \times 3 = 39 V\)

d) The output voltage peak-to-peak ripple is given by:

\(\Delta V_o =\frac{D}{f_sRC} =\frac{0.264}{40 \times 10^3 \times 5 \times 200 \times 10^{-6}} =0.066 V\)

e) Fig. 7.23 presents the simulation results which are in close agreement with the respective theoretical ones.
