Guest Question.

The indeterminate beam 11 of Appendix is reproduced in Fig. 4–16. Determine the reactions using procedure 1.

Admin
Solution

The reactions are shown in Fig. 4–16b.Without \(R_{2}\) the beam is a statically determinate cantilever beam. Without \(M_{1}\) the beam is a statically determinate simply supported beam. In either case, the beam has only one redundant support. We will first solve this problem using superposition, choosing \(R_{2}\) as the redundant reaction. For the second solution, we will use Castigliano’s theorem with \(M_{1}\) as the redundant reaction.

1 Choose \(R_{2}\) at B to be the redundant reaction.

2 Using static equilibrium equations solve for \(R_{1}\) and \(M_{1}\) in terms of F and \(R_{2}\). This results in

 

\(R_{1}=F-R_{2} \quad M_{1}=\frac{F l}{2}-R_{2} l\) (1)

 

3 Write the deflection equation for point B in terms of F and \(R_{2}\). Using superposition of beam 1 of with \(F=-R_{2}\), and beam 2 of with α = l/2, the deflection of B, at x = l, is

 

\(\delta_{B}=-\frac{R_{2} l^{2}}{6 E I}(l-3 l)+\frac{F(l / 2)^{2}}{6 E I}\left(\frac{l}{2}-3 l\right)=\frac{R_{2} l^{3}}{3 E I}-\frac{5 F l^{3}}{48 E I}=0\) (2)

 

4 Equation (2) can be solved for R2 directly. This yields

 

\(R_{2}=\frac{5 F}{16}\) (3)

 

Next, substituting \(R_{2}\) into Eqs. (1) completes the solution, giving

 

\(R_{1}=\frac{11 F}{16} \quad M_{1}=\frac{3 F l}{16}\) (4)

 

Note that the solution agrees with what is given for beam 11 in .

1 Choose \(M_{1}\) at O to be the redundant reaction.

2 Using static equilibrium equations solve for \(R_{1} \text { and } R_{2}\) in terms of F and \(M_{1}\). This results in

 

\(R_{1}=\frac{F}{2}+\frac{M_{1}}{l} \quad R_{2}=\frac{F}{2}-\frac{M_{1}}{l}\) (5)

 

3 Since \(M_{1}\) is the redundant reaction at O, write the equation for the angular deflection at point O. From Castigliano’s theorem this is

 

\(\theta_{O}=\frac{\partial U}{\partial M_{1}}\) (6)

 

We can apply Eq. (4–31), using the variable x as shown in Fig. 4–16b. However, simpler terms can be found by using a variable \(\hat{x}\) that starts at B and is positive to the left. With this and the expression for \(R_{2}\) from Eq. (5) the moment equations are

 

\(\delta_{i}=\frac{\partial U}{\partial F_{i}}=\int \frac{1}{E I}\left(M \frac{\partial M}{\partial F_{i}}\right) d x \quad \text { bending }\) (4–31)

 

\(M=\left(\frac{F}{2}-\frac{M_{1}}{l}\right) \hat{x} \quad 0 \leq \hat{x} \leq \frac{l}{2}\) (7)

 

\(M=\left(\frac{F}{2}-\frac{M_{1}}{l}\right) \hat{x}-F\left(\hat{x}-\frac{l}{2}\right) \quad \frac{l}{2} \leq \hat{x} \leq l\) (8)

 

For both equations

 

\(\frac{\partial M}{\partial M_{1}}=-\frac{\hat{x}}{l}\) (9)

 

Substituting Eqs. (7) to (9) in Eq. (6), using the form of Eq. (4–31) where \(F_{i}=M_{1}\), gives

 

\(\begin{array}{c}\theta_{O}=\frac{\partial U}{\partial M_{1}}=\frac{1}{E I}\left\{\int_{0}^{l / 2}\left(\frac{F}{2}-\frac{M_{1}}{l}\right) \hat{x}\left(-\frac{\hat{x}}{l}\right) d \hat{x}+\int_{l / 2}^{l}\left[\left(\frac{F}{2}-\frac{M_{1}}{l}\right) \hat{x}\right.\right. \\\left.\left.-F\left(\hat{x}-\frac{l}{2}\right)\right]\left(-\frac{\hat{x}}{l}\right) d \hat{x}\right\}=0\end{array}\)

 

Canceling \(1 / E I l\), and combining the first two integrals, simplifies this quite readily to

 

\(\left(\frac{F}{2}-\frac{M_{1}}{l}\right) \int_{0}^{l} \hat{x}^{2} d \hat{x}-F \int_{l / 2}^{l}\left(\hat{x}-\frac{l}{2}\right) \hat{x} d \hat{x}=0\)

 

Integrating gives

 

\(\left(\frac{F}{2}-\frac{M_{1}}{l}\right) \frac{l^{3}}{3}-\frac{F}{3}\left[l^{3}-\left(\frac{l}{2}\right)^{3}\right]+\frac{F l}{4}\left[l^{2}-\left(\frac{l}{2}\right)^{2}\right]=0\)

 

which reduces to

 

\(M_{1}=\frac{3 F l}{16}\) (10)

 

4 Substituting Eq. (10) into (5) results in

 

\(R_{1}=\frac{11 F}{16} \quad R_{2}=\frac{5 F}{16}\) (11)

 

which again agrees with beam 11 of .
