Guest Question.

Neglecting the effect of rotary inertia, find the first four natural frequencies of the transverse vibration of a beam with free ends. The beam has length l, crosssectional area A, moment of inertia \(I_{z}\), mass density ρ, and modulus of elasticity E.

Admin
Solution

At the free end of a beam, the bending moment and shear force are equal to zero. Therefore, the boundary conditions in this example are given by

\(\phi ^{\prime \prime }\left(0\right) = 0, \phi ^{\prime \prime \prime }\left(0\right) = 0 , \phi ^{\prime \prime }\left(l\right) = 0 , \phi ^{\prime \prime \prime }\left(l\right) = 0\)

Substituting the first two boundary conditions into Eq. 4.83 yields

Eq. 4.83 \(\phi \left(x\right) = A_{5}\sinh \eta x + A_{6}\cosh \eta x + A_{7}\sin \eta x + A_{8}\cos \eta x\)

\(\phi^{\prime \prime }\left(0\right)=\eta ^{2}A_{6} – \eta ^{2}A_{8} = 0\)

\(\phi^{\prime \prime \prime }\left(0\right)=\eta ^{3}A_{5} – \eta ^{3}A_{7} = 0\)

which imply \(A_{6} = A_{8}\) and \(A_{5} = A_{7}\). Hence,

\(\phi \left(x\right)= A_{5}\left(\sinh \eta x +\sin \eta x\right) + A_{6}\left(\cosh \eta x +\cos \eta x\right)\)

Substituting the third and fourth boundary conditions into this equation, one obtains

\(A_{5}\left(\sinh \eta l -\sin \eta l\right) + A_{6}\left(\cosh \eta l -\cos \eta l\right) = 0\)

\( A_{5}\left(\cosh \eta l -\cos \eta l\right) + A_{6}\left(\sinh \eta l -\sin \eta l\right) = 0\)

That is,

\( \begin{bmatrix} \sinh \eta l -\sin \eta l & \cosh \eta l -\cos \eta l \\ \cosh \eta l -\cos \eta l & \sinh \eta l -\sin \eta l \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} A_{5} \\ A_{6} \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right] \)

This system of homogeneous algebraic equations in the unknowns \( A_{5} and A_{5}\) has a nontrivial solution if and only if the determinant of the coefficient matrix is equal to zero. This leads to the following frequency equation:

\(\left(\sinh^{2} \eta l -\sin^{2} \eta l\right) – \left(\cosh \eta l -\cos \eta l\right)^{2} = 0\)

Since

\( \cosh^{2} \eta l – \sinh^{2} \eta l = 1 and \cos ^{2} \eta l + \sin^{2} \eta l=1\)

the frequency equation can be simplified to

\(\cos \eta l\cosh \eta l = 1 \)

This equation is satisfied if \(\eta l=0 \). Therefore, the first natural frequency is \(w_{1}=0 \). This frequency is associated with a rigid-body motion of the beam. Thus the associated mode shape is given by \(\phi _{1}\left(x\right) =A_{51}+A_{61}x \). One can show that the second, third, and fourth natural frequencies are defined by the equations

\(w_{2}=\eta ^{2}c=\eta ^{2}l^{2}\frac{c}{l^{2}}=\left(4.730\right)^{2}\frac{c}{l^{2}}\)

\(w_{3}=\left(7.853\right)^{2}\frac{c}{l^{2}}\), \(w_{4}=\left(10.996\right)^{2}\frac{c}{l^{2}} \)

The function \(\phi \left(x\right)\) obtained in this example is expressed in terms of the constants \(A_{5} and A_{6}\). The relationship between these two constants is

\(\frac{A_{6}}{A_{5}} = – \frac{\sinh \eta l – \sin \eta l }{\cosh \eta l – \cos \eta l} = – \frac{\cosh \eta l – \cos \eta l}{\sinh \eta l + \sin \eta l} = \bar{D}\)

Therefore, the space-dependent function ϕ can be expressed in terms of one constant as

\(\phi_{j} \left(x\right)=A_{5j}\left[\sinh \eta_{j} x + \sin \eta_{j} x+ \bar{D}_{j}\left(\cosh \eta_{j} x+ \cos \eta_{j} x \right) \right] \), j=1,2,3…..
