Guest Question.

When 0.10 mol of ammonium chloride \((NH_{4} Cl)\) is dissolved in water producing 1.00 × 10² g solution, the water temperature decreases from 25.0°C to 18.0°C. The specific heat of the resulting solution is 1.00 cal/(g ⋅ °C). Calculate the quantity of energy involved in the process. Is the dissolution of ammonium chloride endothermic or exothermic?

Admin
Solution

Step 1. The calorimetry expression is:

\(Q = m_{s} × ΔT_{s} × SH_{s}\)

The reaction is the system; the solution is the surroundings.

Step 2. The change in temperature is

\(ΔT = T_{s final} − T_{s initial}\)

 

\(= 18.0°C − 25.0°C = −7.0°C\)

The temperature of the solution has decreased by 7.0°C; therefore, the dissolution is endothermic.

Step 3. Substituting these values into the calorimetry expression:

\(Q = 1.00 × 10^{2} \cancel{g solution} × (−7.0 \cancel{°C}) × \frac{1.00 cal}{\cancel{g solution} · \cancel{°C}}\)

 

\(= −7.0 × 10^{2} cal\)

we see that 7.0 × 10² cal (or 0.70 kcal) of heat energy were absorbed by the dissolution process because the solution lost 7.0 × 10² cal of heat energy to the system. The dissolution of ammonium chloride is endothermic.
