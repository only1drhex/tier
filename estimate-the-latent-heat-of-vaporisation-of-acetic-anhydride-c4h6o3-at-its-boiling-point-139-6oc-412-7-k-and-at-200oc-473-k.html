Guest Question.

Estimate the latent heat of vaporisation of acetic anhydride, \(C _{4} H _{6} O _{3}\), at its boiling point, \(139.6^{\circ} C\) (412.7 K), and at \(200^{\circ} C\) (473 K).

Admin
Solution

For acetic anhydride \(T_{c}=569.1 K , P_{c}=\) 46 bar,

Antoine constants A = 16.3982

B = 3287.56

C = -75.11

Experimental value at the boiling point 41,242 kJ/kmol.

From Trouton’s rule:

 

\(L_{v, b}=100 \times 412.7=41,270 kJ / kmol\)

 

Note: the close approximation to the experimental value is fortuitous, the rule normally gives only a very approximate estimate.

From Haggenmacher’s equation:

 

\(\text { at the b.p. } P_{r}=\frac{1}{46}=0.02124\)

 

\(T_{r}=\frac{412.7}{569.1}=0.7252\)

 

\(\Delta z=\left[1-\frac{0.02124}{0.7252^{3}}\right]^{0.5}=0.972\)

 

\(L_{v, b}=\frac{8.32 \times 3287.6 \times(412.7)^{2} \times 0.972}{(412.7-75.11)^{2}}=39,733 kJ / mol\)

 

At \(200^{\circ} C\), the vapour pressure must first be estimated, from the Antoine equation:

 

\(\ln P=A-\frac{B}{T+C}\)

 

\(\ln P=16.3982-\frac{32 \mid 87.56}{473-75.11}=8.14\)

 

\(P=3421.35 mmHg =4.5 bar\)

 

\(P_{c}=\frac{4.5}{46}=0.098\)

 

\(T_{c}=\frac{473}{569.1}=0.831\)

 

\(\Delta z=\left[1-\frac{0.098}{0.831^{3}}\right]^{0.5}=0.911\)

 

\(L_{v}=\frac{8.32 \times 3287.6 \times(473)^{2} \times 0.911}{(473-75.11)^{2}}=\underline{\underline{35,211 kJ / kmol }}\)

 

Using Watson’s equation and the experimental value at the b.p.

 

\(L_{v}=41,242\left[\frac{569.1-473}{569.1-412.7}\right]^{0.38}=\underline{\underline{34,260 kJ / kmol }}\)
