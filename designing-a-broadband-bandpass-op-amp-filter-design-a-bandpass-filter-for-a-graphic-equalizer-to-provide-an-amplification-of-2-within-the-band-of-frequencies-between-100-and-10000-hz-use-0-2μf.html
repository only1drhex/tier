Designing a Broadband Bandpass Op Amp Filter
Design a bandpass filter for a graphic equalizer to provide an amplification of 2 within the band of frequencies
between 100 and 10,000 Hz. Use 0.2μF capacitors.

Admin
Solution

We can design each subcircuit in the cascade and meet the specified cutoff frequency values only if Eq. 15.11 holds. In this case, \(ω_{c2} = 100ω_{c1},\) so we can say that \(\omega _{c2}\gg \omega _{c1} \).

Begin with the low-pass stage. From Eq. 15.12,

\(\omega _{c2}=\frac{1}{R_{L}C_{L}} =2\pi (10000), \)
\(R _{L}=\frac{1}{[2\pi (10000)](0.2 \times 10^{-6})} \)

≈ 80 Ω.

Next, we turn to the high-pass stage. From Eq. 15.13,

\(\omega _{c1}=\frac{1}{R_{H}C_{H}} =2\pi (100), \)
\(R _{H}=\frac{1}{[2\pi (10000)](0.2 \times 10^{-6})} \)

≈ 7958 Ω.

Finally, we need the gain stage. From Eq. 15.15, we see there are two unknowns, so one of the resistors can be selected arbitrarily. Let’s select a 1 kΩ resistor for \(R_i\).Then, from Eq. 15.15,

\(\left|H(j\omega _o) \right|=\frac{R_f}{R_i}. \) (15.15)

\(R_f = 2(1000)\)

\(= 2000 Ω = 2 kΩ.\)

The resulting circuit is shown in Fig. 15.11.We leave to you to verify that the magnitude of this circuit’s transfer function is reduced by \(1/\sqrt{2}\) at both cutoff frequencies, verifying the validity of the assumption \(\omega _{c2}\gg \omega _{c1} \).
