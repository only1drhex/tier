Growth of Silicon Thin Films from Chlorosilane

Single crystal, or epitaxial, thin films of silicon are needed to manufacture integrated circuits. These films are grown by chemically reacting a chlorosilane feed gas in the presence of \(H _{2}\) at elevated temperature. Consider the growth of epitaxial Si at 1300 K and 1 bar. Compare how much Si is produced at equilibrium from \(SiCl _{4}\) vs. \(SiCl _{3} H\) as feed gases, at \(H _{2}\) dilutions from 1:1 to 150:1 \(H _{2}\): chlorosilane ratio. Assume the following species are present at equilibrium: Si, \(SiCl _{2}, SiCl _{4}, SiCl _{3} H , SiCl _{2} H _{2}, SiClH _{3}, SiH _{4}, H _{2}, HCl\).

The following data are available:

Gibbs Energies of Formation in [kJ/mol] at 1300 K
Species \(SiCl _{2}\) \(SiCl _{4}\) \(SiCl _{3} H\) \(SiCl _{2} H _{2}\) \(SiClH _{3}\) \(SiH _{4}\) HCl

Solution

We first apply the phase rule to see how many independent reactions we need. Since silicon can be present in either the gas or solid phase, its stoichiometry in the vapor is not constrained to the feed ratio. On the other hand, the Cl:H ratio must remain constant. Thus, we have s = 1 and can write:

 

\(R=m-\Im+2-\pi-s=9-2+2-2-1=6\)

 

Six independent reactions are then constructed as follows (other choices are also possible):

 

\(\begin{array}{rlr} SiCl _{4}+ H _{2} \rightleftarrows SiCl _{2}+2 HCl & & \text { reaction } 1 \\SiCl _{4}+ H _{2} \rightleftarrows SiCl _{3} H + HCl & & \text { reaction } 2 \\SiCl _{3} H + H _{2} \rightleftarrows SiCl _{2} H _{2}+ HCl & & \text { reaction } 3 \\SiCl _{2} H _{2}+ H _{2} \rightleftarrows SiClH _{3}+ HCl & & \text { reaction } 4 \\SiClH _{3}+ H _{2} \rightleftarrows SiH _{4}+ HCl & & \text { reaction } 5 \\SiCl _{4}+2 H _{2} \rightleftarrows Si ( s )+4 HCl & & \text { reaction } 6\end{array}\)

 

We then write each speciesâ€™ concentration in terms of the extents of these six reactions:

 

\(\begin{aligned}n_{ SiCl _{4}} &=n_{ SiCl _{4}}^{o}-\xi_{1}-\xi_{2}-\xi_{6} \\n_{ SiCl _{2}} &=\xi_{1} \\n_{ SiCl _{3} H } &=n_{ SiCl _{3} H }^{o}+\xi_{2}-\xi_{3} \\n_{ SiCl _{2} H _{2}} &=\xi_{3}-\xi_{4} \\n_{ SiClH _{3}} &=\xi_{4}-\xi_{5} \\n_{ SiH _{4}} &=\xi_{5} \\n_{ H _{2}} &=n_{ H _{2}}^{o}-\xi_{1}-\xi_{2}-\xi_{3}-\xi_{4}-\xi_{5}-2 \xi_{6}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}n_{ HCl } &=2 \xi_{1}+\xi_{2}+\xi_{3}+\xi_{4}+\xi_{5}+4 \xi_{6} \\n^{v, o} &=n_{ SiCl _{4}}^{ o }+n_{ SiCl _{3} H }^{o}+n_{ H _{2}}^{o}+\xi_{1}+\xi_{6} \\n_{ Si } &=\xi_{6}\end{aligned}\)

 

The six equilibrium constants are then written assuming ideal gas behavior:

 

\(K_{1}=\frac{y_{ SiCl _{2}} y_{ HCl }^{2}}{y_{ SiCl _{4}} y_{ H _{2}}} P\)

 

\(=\frac{\left(\xi_{1}\right)\left(2 \xi_{1}+\xi_{2}+\xi_{3}+\xi_{4}+\xi_{5}+4 \xi_{6}\right)^{2}}{\left(n_{ SiCl_{4} }^{o}-\xi_{1}-\xi_{2}-\xi_{6}\right)\left(n_{ H _{2}}^{o}-\xi_{1}-\xi_{2}-\xi_{3}-\xi_{4}-\xi_{5}-2 \xi_{6}\right)\left(n_{ SiCl_{4} }^{o}+n_{ SiCl _{3} H }^{o}+n_{ H _{2}}^{o}+\xi_{1}+\xi_{6}\right)} P\)

 

\(K_{2}=\frac{y_{ SiCl _{3} H } y_{ HCl }}{y_{ SiCl _{4}} y_{ H _{2}}}=\frac{\left(n_{ SiCl _{3} H }^{o}+\xi_{2}-\xi_{3}\right)\left(2 \xi_{1}+\xi_{2}+\xi_{3}+\xi_{4}+\xi_{5}+4 \xi_{6}\right)}{\left(n_{ SiCl _{4}}^{o}-\xi_{1}-\xi_{2}-\xi_{6}\right)\left(n_{ H _{2}}^{o}-\xi_{1}-\xi_{2}-\xi_{3}-\xi_{4}-\xi_{5}-2 \xi_{6}\right)}\)

 

\(K_{3}=\frac{y_{ SiCl _{2} H _{2}} y_{ HCl }}{y_{ SiCl _{3} H y_{ H _{2}}}}=\frac{\left(\xi_{3}-\xi_{4}\right)\left(2 \xi_{1}+\xi_{2}+\xi_{3}+\xi_{4}+\xi_{5}+4 \xi_{6}\right)}{\left(n_{ SiCl _{3} H }^{o}+\xi_{2}-\xi_{3}\right)\left(n_{ H _{2}}^{o}-\xi_{1}-\xi_{2}-\xi_{3}-\xi_{4}-\xi_{5}-2 \xi_{6}\right)}\)

 

\(K_{4}=\frac{y_{ SiClH _{3}} y_{ HCl }}{y_{ SiCl _{2} H _{2}} y_{ H _{2}}}=\frac{\left(\xi_{4}-\xi_{5}\right)\left(2 \xi_{1}+\xi_{2}+\xi_{3} \xi_{4}+\xi_{5}+4 \xi_{6}\right)}{\left(\xi_{3}-\xi_{4}\right)\left(n_{ H _{2}}^{o}-\xi_{1}-\xi_{2}-\xi_{3}-\xi_{4}-\xi_{5}-2 \xi_{6}\right)}\)

 

\(K_{5}=\frac{y_{ SiH _{4}} y_{ HCl }}{y_{ SiCl H _{3}} y_{ H _{2}}}=\frac{\left(\xi_{5}\right)\left(2 \xi_{1}+\xi_{2}+\xi_{3}+\xi_{4}+\xi_{5}+4 \xi_{6}\right)}{\left(\xi_{4}-\xi_{5}\right)\left(n_{ H _{2}}^{\circ}-\xi_{1}-\xi_{2}-\xi_{3}-\xi_{4}-\xi_{5}-2 \xi_{6}\right)}\)

 

\(K_{6}=\frac{\left(\frac{\hat{f}_{ Si }}{f_{ Si }^{o}}\right) y_{ HCl }^{4}}{y_{ SiCl _{4}} y_{ H _{2}}^{2}}\)

 

\(=\frac{(1)\left(2 \xi_{1}+\xi_{2}+\xi_{3}+\xi_{4}+\xi_{5}+4 \xi_{6}\right)^{4}}{\left(n_{ SiCl_{4} }^{o}-\xi_{1}-\xi_{2}-\xi_{6}\right)\left(n_{ H _{2}}^{o}-\xi_{1}-\xi_{2}-\xi_{3}-\xi_{4}-\xi_{5}-2 \xi_{6}\right)^{2}\left(n_{ SiCl _{4}}^{o}+n_{ SiCl _{3} H }^{o}+n_{ H _{2}}^{o}+\xi_{1}+\xi_{6}\right)} P\)

 

From the values of Gibbs energy, we can calculate the values of the six equilibrium constants. We must then solve for the six unknown extents of reaction to match the equilibrium constants. Solution is obtained by calculating the equilibrium constant \(K_{i}\) in two ways: (1) from
Gibbs energy data (labeled \(K_{i}\)) and (2) from the extents of reaction (labeled \(K_{i, \text { calc }}\)). The extents are changed until the two values matched within a convergence criteria as determined by \(K_{i}-K_{i, \text { calc }}\). In this case the extent of reaction 6 is negative, indicating that solid Si would actually be removed, or etched, from the substrate. Hence, thermodynamics tells us there is no way to deposit Si under these conditions. We define the equilibrium deposition efficiency, \(\eta\), as:

 

\(\eta=\frac{\text { amount of Si deposited as solid }}{\text { amount of Si in feed gas }}\)

 

In the case above, we actually get an efficiency of 20.0512, since Si was etched. The complete case study is plotted in Figure E9.21.
