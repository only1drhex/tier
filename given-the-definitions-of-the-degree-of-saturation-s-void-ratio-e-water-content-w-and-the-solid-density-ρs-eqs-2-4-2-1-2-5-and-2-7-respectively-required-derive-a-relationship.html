Guest Question.

Given:

The definitions of the degree of saturation S, void ratio e, water content w, and the solid density \(\rho_{s}\) [Eqs. (2.4), (2.1), (2.5), and (2.7), respectively].

\(e=\frac{V_{v}}{V_{s}}\) (2.1)

\(S=\frac{V_{w}}{V_{v}} \times 100(\%)\) (2.4)

\(w=\frac{M_{w}}{M_{s}} \times 100(\%)\) (2.5)

\(\rho_{s}=\frac{M_{s}}{V_{s}}\) (2.7)

Required:

Derive a relationship between \(S, e,w, \text { and } \rho_{s}\)·

Admin
Solution

Look at the phase diagram with \(V_{s}=1\) (Fig. Ex. 2.5).

From Eq. (2.4) and Fig. Ex. 2.5, we know that \(V_{w}=S V_{v}=S e\). From the definitions of water content [Eq. (2.5)] and \(\rho_{s}\) [Eq. (2.7)], we can place the equivalents for \(M_{s} \text { and } M_{w}\) on the phase diagram. Since from Eq. (2.8), \(M_{w}=\rho_{w} V_{w}\), we now can write the following equation:

 

\(\rho_{w}=\frac{M_{w}}{V_{w}}\) (2.8)

 

\(M_{w}=\rho_{w} V_{w}=w M_{s}=w \rho_{s} V_{s}\)

 

or

 

\(\rho_{w} S e=w \rho_{s} V_{s}\)

 

Since \(V_{s}=1 m ^{3}\),

 

\(\rho_{w} S e=w \rho_{s}\) (2.15)

 

Equation (2.15) is among the most useful of all equations for phase pioblems. You can also verify its validity from the fundamental definitions of \(\rho_{w}, S, e, w, \text { and } \rho_{s}\).

 

Note that, using Eq. (2.15), we can write Eq. (2.13) another way:

 

\(\rho=\frac{\rho_{s}(1+w)}{(1+e)}\) (2.13)

 

\(\rho=\frac{\rho_{s}\left(1+\frac{\rho_{w} S e}{\rho_{s}}\right)}{1+e}=\frac{\rho_{s}+\rho_{w} S e}{1+e}\) (2.16)

 

When S = 100%, Eq. (2.16) becomes

 

\(\rho_{ sat }=\frac{\rho_{s}+\rho_{w} e}{1+e}\) (2.17)
