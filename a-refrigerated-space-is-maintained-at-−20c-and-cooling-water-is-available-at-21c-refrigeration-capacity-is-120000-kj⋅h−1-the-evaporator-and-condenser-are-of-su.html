Guest Question.

A refrigerated space is maintained at \(−20^{\circ}C,\) and cooling water is available at \(21^{\circ}C. \)Refrigeration capacity is \(120,000 kJ\cdot h^{−1}.\) The evaporator and condenser are of suffi-cient size that a \(5^{\circ}C\) minimum-temperature difference for heat transfer can be realized in each. The refrigerant is 1,1,1,2-tetrafluoroethane (HFC-134a), for which data are given in Table 9.1 and Fig. F.2 (App. F).
(a) What is the value of \(\omega\) for a Carnot refrigerator?
(b) Calculate \(\omega\) and \(\dot{m}\) for a vapor-compression cycle (Fig. 9.2) if the compressor efficiency is 0.80.

Solution

(a) Allowing \(5^{\circ}C\) temperature differences, the evaporator temperature is \(−25^{\circ}C = 248.15 K,\) and the condenser temperature is \(26^{\circ}C = 299.15 K.\) Thus, by Eq. (9.3) for a Carnot refrigerator,

\( \omega =\frac{T_{C}}{T_{H}-T_{C}}\) (9.3)

 

\( \omega =\frac{248.15}{299.15-248.15}=4.87\)

(b) With HFC-134a as the refrigerant, enthalpies for states 2 and 4 of Fig. 9.2 are read directly from Table 9.1. The entry at \(−25^{\circ}C\) indicates that HFC-134a vaporizes in the evaporator at a pressure of 1.064 bar. Its properties as a saturated vapor at these conditions are:

\( H_{2}=383.45 kJ\cdot kg^{−1} S_{2}=1.746 kJ⋅kg^{−1}\cdot K^{−1}\)

The entry at \(26^{\circ}C\) in Table 9.1 shows that HFC-134a condenses at 6.854 bar; its enthalpy as a saturated liquid at these conditions is:

\( H_{4}=235.97 kJ\cdot kg^{−1}\)

If the compression step is reversible and adiabatic (isentropic) from saturated vapor at state 2 to superheated vapor at state \(3^{\prime},\)

\( S^{\prime }_{3}=S_{2}=1.746 kJ\cdot kg^{-1}\cdot K^{-1}\)

This entropy value and the condenser pressure of 6.854 bar are sufficient to spec-ify the thermodynamic state at point \(3^{\prime}\). One could find the other properties at this state using Fig. F.2, following a curve of constant entropy from the saturation curve to the condenser pressure. However, more precise results can be obtained using an electronic resource such as the NIST WebBook. Varying the temperature at a fixed pressure of 6.854 bar shows that the entropy is \(1.746 kJ\cdot kg^{−1}\cdot K^{−1}\) at T = 308.1 K. The corresponding enthalpy is:

\( H_{3}^{\prime}= 421.97 kJ\cdot kg^{−1}\)

and the enthalpy change is:

\( (\Delta H)_{S}=H_{3}^{\prime }-H_{2}=421.97-383.45=38.52 kJ\cdot kg^{-1}\)

By Eq. (7.17) for a compressor efficiency of 0.80, the actual enthalpy change for step 2 → 3 is:

\( \eta \equiv \frac{(\Delta H)_{S}}{\Delta H}\) (7.17)

 

\( H_{3}-H_{2}=\frac{(\Delta H)_{S}}{\eta }=\frac{38.52}{0.80}=48.15 kJ\cdot kg^{-1}\)

Because the throttling process of step 1 → 4 is isenthalpic, \(H_{1} = H_{4}\). The coefficient of performance as given by Eq. (9.4) therefore becomes:

\( \omega =\frac{H_{2}-H_{1}}{H_{3}-H_{2}}\) (9.4)

 

\( \omega =\frac{H_{2}-H_{4}}{H_{3}-H_{2}}=\frac{383.45-235.97}{48.15}=3.06\)

and the HFC-134a circulation rate as given by Eq. (9.5) is:

\( \dot{m}=\frac{\dot{Q}_{C} }{H_{2}-H_{1}}\) (9.5)

\( \dot{m}=\frac{\dot{Q}_{C} }{H_{2}-H_{4}}=\frac{120,000}{383.45-235.97}=814 kg\cdot h^{-1}\)
