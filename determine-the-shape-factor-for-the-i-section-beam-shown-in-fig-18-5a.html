Guest Question.

Determine the shape factor for the I-section beam shown in Fig. 18.5(a).

Admin
Solution

Again, as in , the elastic and plastic neutral axes coincide with the centroid, G, of the section.
In the fully plastic condition the stress distribution in the beamis that shown in Fig. 18.5(b). The total direct force in the upper flange is

\(\sigma_{ Y } b t_{ f }\) (compression)

and its moment about Gz is

\(\sigma_{ Y } b t_{ f }\left(\frac{d}{2}-\frac{t_{ f }}{2}\right) \equiv \frac{\sigma_{ Y } b t_{ f }}{2}\left(d-t_{ f }\right)\) (i)

Similarly the total direct force in the web above Gz is

\(\sigma_{ Y } t_{ w }\left(\frac{d}{2}-t_{ f }\right)\) (compression)

and its moment about Gz is

\(\sigma_{ Y } t_{ w }\left(\frac{d}{2}-t_{ f }\right) \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{d}{2}-t_{ f }\right) \equiv \frac{\sigma_{ Y } t_{ w }}{8}\left(d-2 t_{ f }\right)^{2}\) (ii)

The lower half of the section is in tension and contributes the same moment about Gz so that the total plastic moment, \(M_{ P }\), of the complete section is given by

\(M_{ P }=\sigma_{ Y }\left[b t_{ f }\left(d-t_{ f }\right)+\frac{1}{4} t_{ w }\left(d-2 t_{ f }\right)^{2}\right]\) (iii)

Comparing Eqs. (18.5)

\(M_{ P }=\sigma_{ Y } Z_{ P }\) (18.5)

and (iii) we see that \(Z_{ P }\) is given by

\(Z_{ P }=b t_{ f }\left(d-t_{ f }\right)+\frac{1}{4} t_{ w }\left(d-2 t_{ f }\right)^{2}\) (iv)

Alternatively we could have obtained \(Z_{ P }\) from Eq. (18.6).

\(Z_{ P }=\frac{A\left(\bar{y}_{1}+\bar{y}_{2}\right)}{2}\) (18.6)

The second moment of area, I, of the section about the common neutral axis is

\(I=\frac{b d^{3}}{12}-\frac{\left(b-t_{ w }\right)\left(d-2 t_{ f }\right)^{3}}{12}\)

so that the elastic modulus \(Z_{ e }\) is given by

\(Z_{ e }=\frac{I}{d / 2}=\frac{2}{d}\left[\frac{b d^{3}}{12}-\frac{\left(b-t_{ w }\right)\left(d-2 t_{ f }\right)^{3}}{12}\right]\) (v)

Substituting the actual values of the dimensions of the section in Eqs (iv) and (v) we obtain

\(Z_{ P }=150 \times 12(300-12)+\frac{1}{4} \times 8(300-2 \times 12)^{2}=6.7 \times 10^{5} mm ^{3}\)

and

\(Z_{ e }=\frac{2}{300}\left[\frac{150 \times 300^{3}}{12}-\frac{(150-8)(300-24)^{3}}{12}\right]=5.9 \times 10^{5} mm ^{3}\)

Therefore from Eq. (18.7)

\(f=\frac{M_{ P }}{M_{ Y }}=\frac{\sigma_{ Y } Z_{ P }}{\sigma_{ Y } Z_{ e }}=\frac{Z_{ P }}{Z_{ e }}\) (18.7)

\(f=\frac{M_{ P }}{M_{ Y }}=\frac{Z_{ P }}{Z_{ e }}=\frac{6.7 \times 10^{5}}{5.9 \times 10^{5}}=1.14\)

and we see that the fully plastic moment is only 14% greater than the moment at initial yielding.
