Guest Question.

The cover glass on a flat-plate solar collector has a low iron content, and its spectral trans-missivity may be approximated by the following distribution.

What is the total transmissivity of the cover glass to solar radiation?

Admin
Solution

Known: Spectral transmissivity of solar collector cover glass.

Find: Total transmissivity of cover glass to solar radiation.

Assumptions: Spectral distribution of solar irradiation is proportional to that of black-body emission at 5800 K.

Analysis: From Equation 12.61 \(\tau = \frac{\int_{0}^{\infty }{G_{\lambda , tr} (\lambda ) d\lambda } }{\int_{0}^{\infty }{G_{\lambda } (\lambda ) d\lambda } } = \frac{\int_{0}^{\infty }{\tau _{\lambda } (\lambda ) G_{\lambda } (\lambda ) d\lambda } }{\int_{0}^{\infty }{G_{\lambda } (\lambda ) d\lambda }} \) the total transmissivity of the cover is

\( \tau = \frac{\int_{0}^{\infty }{\tau _{\lambda } G_{\lambda } d\lambda } }{\int_{0}^{\infty }{G_{\lambda } d\lambda }} \)

where the irradiation \(G_{\lambda }\) is due to solar emission. Having assumed that the sun emits as a blackbody at 5800 K, it follows that

\(G_{\lambda } (\lambda ) \propto E_{\lambda ,b} (5800 K)\)

With the proportionality constant canceling from the numerator and denominator of the expression for \( \tau \), we obtain

\(\tau = \frac{\int_{0}^{\infty }{\tau _{\lambda } E_{\lambda ,b} (5800 K) d\lambda } }{\int_{0}^{\infty }{ E_{\lambda ,b} (5800 K) d\lambda }} \)

or, for the prescribed spectral distribution of \(\tau _{\lambda } (\lambda)\),

\(\tau = 0.90 \frac{\int_{0.3}^{2.5 }{ E_{\lambda ,b} (5800 K) d\lambda } }{E_{b} (5800 K) }\)

\( \lambda _{1} = 0.3 μm, T = 5800 K:\) \(\lambda _{1} T = 1740 μm . K, F_{(0→\lambda _{1})} = 0.0335\)

 

\( \lambda _{2} = 2.5 μm, T = 5800 K:\) \(\lambda _{2}T = 14,500 μm . K, F_{(0→\lambda _{2})} = 0.9664 \)

Hence from Equation 12.35 \(F_{(\lambda _{1} \longrightarrow \lambda _{2})} = \frac{\int_{0}^{\lambda _{2}}{E_{\lambda ,b} d\lambda } – \int_{0}^{\lambda _{1}}{E_{\lambda ,b} d\lambda }}{\sigma T^{4}} = F_{(0 \longrightarrow \lambda _{2})} – F_{(0 \longrightarrow \lambda _{1})}\)

 

\(\tau = 0.90 [F_{(0 \longrightarrow \lambda _{2})} – F_{(0 \longrightarrow \lambda _{1})}] = 0.90(0.9664 – 0.0335) = 0.84\)

Comments: It is important to recognize that the irradiation at the cover plate is not equal to the emissive power of a blackbody at 5800 K, \(G_{\lambda } ≠ E_{\lambda ,b} (5800 K)\) . It is simply assumed to be proportional to this emissive power, in which case it is assumed to have a spectral distribution of the same form. With \(G_{\lambda }\) appearing in both the numerator and denominator of the expression for \(\tau \), it is then possible to replace \(G_{\lambda }\) by \(E_{\lambda ,b}\).
