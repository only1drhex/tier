Guest Question.

Determine the time required for a car to travel 1 km along a road if the car starts from rest, reaches a maximum speed at some intermediate point, and then stops at the end of the road. The car can accelerate or decelerate at \( 1.5 m/s^2 \) .

Admin
Solution

Method I : By using equation of motion

(i) Consider the motion of car from O to A [Refer to Fig. 11.4(a)].

Initial velocity u = 0 ; Final velocity \( ν = ν_{max} \);

Acceleration \( a = 1.5 m/s^2 \) ; Time \( t = t_1 \) ;

Displacement \( s = s_1 \) ;

ν = u + at.

\( ν_{max} = 0 + 1.5 × t_1 \) ∴ \( t_1 = \frac{ ν_{max}}{1.5} \) .

\( s = ut + \frac{1}{2} at^2 \)

\( s_1 = (0)(t_1) + \frac{1}{2} × 1.5 × T^2_1 \).

\( s_1 = 0.75 \bigl(\frac{\nu _{max}}{1.5}\bigr)^2 \).

(ii) Consider the motion of car from A to B.

Initial velocity \( u = \nu _{max} \); Final velocity ν = 0 ;

Acceleration \( a = -1.5 m/s^2 \) ; Time \( t = t_2 \);

Displacement \( s = s_2 \) ;

ν = u + at.

\( 0 =\nu _{max} + (-1.5) × t_2 \) ∴ \( t_2 = \frac{\nu _{max}}{1.5} \) .

\( s = ut + \frac{1}{2} at^2 \).

\( s_2 = \nu _{max}\bigl(\frac{\nu _{max}}{1.5}\bigr) +\frac{1}{2} \times (-1.5)\bigl(\frac{\nu _{max}}{1.5}\bigr)^2 \).

\( s_2 = \nu _{max}\bigl(\frac{\nu _{max}}{1.5}\bigr) -0.75 \bigl(\frac{\nu _{max}}{1.5}\bigr)^2 \).

(iii) Total displacement \( s = s_1 + s_2 \).

\( 1000 = 0.75 \bigl(\frac{\nu _{max}}{1.5}\bigr)^2 + \Bigl[\frac{\nu ^2_{max}}{1.5} -0.75\bigl(\frac{\nu _{max}}{1.5}\bigr)^2 \Bigr] \).

\( 1000 = \frac{\nu ^2_{max}}{1.5} \) .

\( \nu _{max} = 38.73 \) m/s Ans.

(iv) Total time \( t = t_1 + t_2 \).

\( t = \frac{ \nu _{max}}{1.5} + \frac{ \nu _{max}}{1.5}\) .

\( t = \frac{ 38.73}{1.5} + \frac{ 38.73}{1.5} \) .

t = 51.64 sec Ans.

Method II : By using v-t diagram

(i) Consider the ν-t diagram shown in Fig. 11.4(b).

Since the magnitude of acceleration is same, therefore, the slope is same.

∴ \( t_1 = t_2 \) ∴ \( t= 2t_1 \) .

Slope = Acceleration = \( 1.5 = \frac{ \nu _{max}}{1.5}\) .

∴ \( \nu _{max} = 1.5t_1 \) …..(I)

Area under ν-t diagram is displacement

∴ \( 1000 = \frac{1}{2} × 2 t_1 × \nu _{max} \).

\( \nu _{max} = \frac{1000}{t_1} \) ……(II)

(ii) Equating Eqs. (I) and (II)

\( 1.5 t_1 = \frac{1000}{t_1} \) .

\( t_1 = 25.82 \) sec.

∴ \( t = 2 × t_1 = 51.64 \) sec.

\( \nu _{max} = 1.5 × 25.82 \) .

\( \nu _{max} = 38.73 \) m/s Ans.
