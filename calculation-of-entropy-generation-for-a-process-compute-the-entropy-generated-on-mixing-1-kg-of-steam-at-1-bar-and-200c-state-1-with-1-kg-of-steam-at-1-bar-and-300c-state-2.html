Calculation of Entropy Generation for a Process

Compute the entropy generated on mixing 1 kg of steam at 1 bar and 200°C (state 1) with 1 kg of steam at 1 bar and 300°C (state 2).

Admin
Solution

Considering the 2 kg of steam to be a closed system, the mass balance is

\(M_{f}-\left(M_{1}+M_{2}\right)=0 \quad \text { or } \quad M_{f}=M_{1}+M_{2}=2 kg\)

The energy balance is

\(M_{f} \hat{U}_{f}-M_{1} \hat{U}_{1}-M_{2} \hat{U}_{2}=-P\left(M_{f} \hat{V}_{f}-M_{1} \hat{V}_{1}-M_{2} \hat{V}_{2}\right)\)

Since the pressure is constant, the U and PV terms can be combined to give

\(M_{f} \hat{H}_{f}-M_{1} \hat{H}_{1}-M_{2} \hat{H}_{2}=0\)

or

\(\begin{aligned}2 kg \cdot \hat{H}\left(T_{f}=?, P=\right.&1 bar ) \\&=1 kg \cdot \hat{H}\left(T=200^{\circ} C , P=1 bar \right)+1 kg \cdot \hat{H}\left(T=300^{\circ} C , P=1 bar \right)\end{aligned}\)

Therefore,

\(\begin{aligned}\hat{H}\left(T_{f}=?, P=1 \text { bar }\right) &=\frac{1}{2}\left[\hat{H}\left(T=200^{\circ} C , P=1 \text { bar }\right)+\hat{H}\left(T=300^{\circ} C , P=1 \text { bar }\right)\right] \\&=\frac{1}{2}[2875.3+3074.3] \frac{ kJ }{ kg }=2974.8 \frac{ kJ }{ kg }\end{aligned}\)

and from the steam tables we find for this value of the enthalpy at a pressure of 1 bar that \(t_{f}</span><span class="fontstyle3"> </span><span class="fontstyle4">= 250</span><span class="fontstyle4">°C\). Now from the entropy balance, we have

\(M_{f} \hat{S}_{f}-M_{1} \hat{S}_{1}-M_{2} \hat{S}_{2}=S_{\text {gen }}\)

or

\(\begin{aligned}S_{ gen }=& 2 kg \cdot \hat{S}\left(T=250^{\circ} C , P=1 bar \right)-1 kg \cdot \hat{S}\left(T=200^{\circ} C , P=1 bar \right) \\&-1 kg \cdot \hat{S}\left(T=300^{\circ} C , P=1 bar \right) \\=& 2 kg \cdot 8.0333 \frac{ kJ }{ kg K }-1 kg \cdot 7.8343 \frac{ kJ }{ kg K }-1 kg \cdot 8.2158 \frac{ kJ }{ kg K } \\=& 0.0165 \frac{ kJ }{ K }\end{aligned}\)

So we see that mixing two fluids of the same pressure but different temperatures generates entropy and therefore is an irreversible process.
