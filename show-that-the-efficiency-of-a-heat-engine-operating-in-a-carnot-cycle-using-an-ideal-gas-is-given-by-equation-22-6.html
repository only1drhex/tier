Guest Question.

Show that the efficiency of a heat engine operating in a Carnot cycle using an ideal gas is given by Equation 22.6.

\( e_{ C }=1-\frac{T_{c}}{T_{h}} \) (22.6).

Admin
Solution

During the isothermal expansion (process A →B in Fig. 22.10), the temperature of the gas does not change.
Thus, its internal energy remains constant. The work done on a gas during an isothermal process is given by Equation 20.13. According to the first law,

\( W=n R T \ln \left(\frac{V_{i}}{V_{f}}\right) \) (20.13)

\( \left|Q_{h}\right|=\left|-W_{A B}\right|=n R T_{h} \ln \frac{V_{B}}{V_{A}} \).

In a similar manner, the energy transferred to the cold reservoir during the isothermal compression C → D is

\( \left|Q_{c}\right|=\left|-W_{C D}\right|=n R T_{c} \ln \frac{V_{C}}{V_{D}} \).

Dividing the second expression by the first, we find that

(1) \( \frac{\left|Q_{c}\right|}{\left|Q_{h}\right|}=\frac{T_{c}}{T_{h}} \frac{\ln \left(V_{C} / V_{D}\right)}{\ln \left(V_{B} / V_{A}\right)} \).

We now show that the ratio of the logarithmic quantities is unity by establishing a relationship between the ratio of volumes. For any quasi-static, adiabatic process, the temperature and volume are related by Equation 21.20

\( T_{i} V_{i} \gamma-1=T_{f} V_{f}^{\gamma-1} \) (21.20).

\( T_{i} V_{i}^{\gamma-1}=T_{f} V_{f}^{\gamma-1} \).

Applying this result to the adiabatic processes B → C and D → A, we obtain

\( T_{h} V_{B}^{\gamma-1}=T_{c} V_{C}^{\gamma-1} \).

\( T_{h} V_{A}^{\gamma-1}=T_{c} V_{D}^{\gamma-1} \).

Dividing the first equation by the second, we obtain

\( \left(V_{B} / V_{A}\right)^{\gamma-1}=\left(V_{C} / V_{D}\right)^{\gamma-1} \).

(2) \( \frac{V_{B}}{V_{A}}=\frac{V_{C}}{V_{D}} \).

Substituting Equation (2) into Equation (1), we find that the logarithmic terms cancel, and we obtain the relationship

\( \frac{\left|Q_{c}\right|}{\left|Q_{h}\right|}=\frac{T_{c}}{T_{h}} \).

Using this result and Equation 22.2, we see that the thermal efficiency of the Carnot engine is

\( e=\frac{W_{\text {eng }}}{\left|Q_{h}\right|}=\frac{\left|Q_{h}\right|-\left|Q_{c}\right|}{\left|Q_{h}\right|}=1-\frac{\left|Q_{c}\right|}{\left|Q_{h}\right|} \) (22.2).

\( e_{ C }=1-\frac{\left|Q_{c}\right|}{\left|Q_{h}\right|}=1-\frac{T_{c}}{T_{h}} \)

which is Equation 22.6, the one we set out to prove

\( e_{ C }=1-\frac{T_{c}}{T_{h}} \). (22.6)
