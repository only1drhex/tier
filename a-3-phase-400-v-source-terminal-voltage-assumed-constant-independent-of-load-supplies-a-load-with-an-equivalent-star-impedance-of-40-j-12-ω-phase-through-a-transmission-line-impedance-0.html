Guest Question.

A 3-phase, 400 V source (terminal voltage assumed constant independent of load) supplies a load with an equivalent-star impedance of (40 + j 12) \( \Omega\) /phase through a transmission line impedance (0.2 + j 0.8) \( \Omega\) /line. Compute (a) the line current; (b) the load voltage; (c) the power, reactive power and VA consumed by the load; (d) the power and reactive power loss in the line; and (e) the power, reactive power and VA supplied by the source.

Admin
Solution

Figure AII.14 is the per phase circuit of the system (star basis)

\( V_{\text {source }}=400 / \sqrt{3}=231 V\)

 

\( \bar{Z}(\text { total })=(0.2+j 0.8)+(40+j 12)=40.2+j 12.8=42.2 \angle 17.7^{\circ}\)

 

\( I_{L}=\frac{231}{42.2}=5.47 A\)

 

\( \bar{Z}_{ load }=40+j 12=41.8 \angle 16.7^{\circ}\)

 

\( V_{\text {load }}=5.47 \times 41.8=228.6 V\) (phase-to-neutral)

 

= 396 V (line-to-line)

 

\( P_{\text {load }}=3 \times(5.47)^{2} \times 40=3.59 kW\)

 

\( Q_{\text {load }}=3 \times(5.47)^{2} \times 12=1.077 kVAR\)

 

\( S_{\text {load }}=\sqrt{(3590)^{2}+(1077)^{2}}=3.748 kVA\)

 

\( P_{\text {line }}=3 \times(5.47)^{2} \times 0.2=18 W\)

 

\( Q_{\text {line }}=3 \times(5.47) \times 0.8=72 VAR\)

 

\( P_{\text {source }}=3.59+0.018=3.608 kW\)

 

\( Q_{\text {source }}=1.077+0.072=1.149 kVAR\)

 

\( S_{\text {source }}=\sqrt{(3.608)^{2}+(1.149)^{2}}=3.786 kVA\)
