Guest Question.

The arch shown in Fig. P.16.25 is parabolic, the equation of its profile being y = 0.05x (40-x).
If the second moment of area of the cross section of the arch is governed by the secant rule calculate the reactions at the supports and the bending moment at the crown.
Ans. \(R_{ A , H }=R_{ B , H }=3.15 kN , R_{ A , V }=11.6 kN , R_{ B , V }=3.4 kN , M_{ C }=19 kNm\).

Admin
Solution

Since the secant assumption applies the horizontal reaction at a support is given by Eq. (16.15).

\(R_{1}=\frac{\int_{\text {Profile }} M_{0} y d x}{\int_{\text {Profile }} y^{2} d x}\) (16.15)

Release the arch horizontally at B and apply a vertical downward unit load at the point of application of the 15 kN load. Then

\(R_{1( A )} \times 30-1 \times 20=0\)

which gives

\(R_{1( A )}=2 / 3\)

Then

\(M_{0}=(2 / 3) x \quad 0 \leq x \leq 10 m\)

 

\(M_{0}=(2 / 3) x-1(x-10)=10-(x / 3) \quad 10 \leq x \leq 30\)

Therefore

\(\int_{L} M_{0} y d x=\int_{0}^{10}(2 / 3) x[0.05 x(40-x)] d x+\int_{10}^{30}[10-(x / 3)] 0.05 x(40-x) d x\)

Integrating

\(\int_{L} M_{0} y d x=0.033\left[\frac{40 x^{3}}{3}-\frac{x^{4}}{4}\right]_{0}^{10}+0.05\left[200 x^{2}-\frac{70 x^{3}}{9}+\frac{x^{4}}{12}\right]_{10}^{30}\)

That is

\(\int_{L} M_{0} y d x=1579.7\)

Further

\(\int_{L} y^{2} d x=\int_{0}^{30}[0.05(40-x)]^{2} d x=\int_{0}^{30} 0.0025 x^{2}\left(40^{2}-80 x+x^{2}\right) d x=7650\)

Then, from Eq. (16.15)

\(R_{ H }=1579.7 / 7650=0.21\)

For the applied load of 15 kN

\(R_{ A , H }=0.21 \times 15=3.15 kN =R_{ B , H }\)

Now taking moments about B

\(R_{ A , V } \times 30-R_{ A , H } y_{ B }-15 \times 20=0\) (i)

where

\(y_{ B }=0.05 \times 30(40-30)=15 m\)

Substituting in Eq. (i) for \(R_{ A , H }\) and \(y_{ B }\) gives

\(R_{ A , V }=11.6 kN\)

Then

\(R_{ B , V }=15-11.6=3.4 kN\)

At the crown of the arch (dy/dx) = 0.05(40â€“2x) = 0. Therefore \(x_{ C }=20 m\) and \(y_{ C }=0.05 \times 20(40-20)=20 m\). The bending moment at the crown of the arch is then

\(M_{ C }=11.6 \times 20-3.15 \times 20-15 \times 10=19.0 kNm\).
