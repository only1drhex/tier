Carpet Calculations

GOAL Apply the rules for significant figures.

PROBLEM Several carpet installers make measurements for carpet installation in the different rooms of a restaurant, reporting their measurements with inconsistent accuracy, as compiled in Table 1.6. Compute the areas for (a) the banquet hall, (b) the meeting room, and (c) the dining room, taking into account significant figures. (d) What total area of carpet is required for these rooms?

STRATEGY For the multiplication problems in parts (a)–(c), count the significant figures in each number. The smaller result is the number of significant figures in the answer. Part (d) requires a sum, where the area with the least accurately known decimal place determines the overall number of significant figures in the answer.

Table 1.6 Dimensions of Rooms in
Length (m) Width (m)
Banquet hall 14.71 7.46
Meeting room 4.822 5.1
Dining room 13.8 9
Admin
Solution

(a) Compute the area of the banquet hall.

Count significant figures:

14.71 m \(\rightarrow\) 4 significant figures

7.46 m \(\rightarrow\) 3 significant figures

To find the area, multiply the numbers keeping only
three digits:

\(14.71 m\times 7.46 m=109.74m^{2} \rightarrow 1.10\times 10^{2} m^{2}\)

(b) Compute the area of the meeting room.

Count significant figures:

4.822 m \(\rightarrow\) 4 significant figures

5.1 m \(\rightarrow\) 2 significant figures

To find the area, multiply the numbers keeping only two digits:

\(4.822 m\times 5.1 m=24.59 m^{2} \rightarrow 25m^{2}\)

(c) Compute the area of the dining room.

Count significant figures:

13.8 m \(\rightarrow\) 3 significant figures

9 m \(\rightarrow\) 1 significant figures

To find the area, multiply the numbers keeping only one digit:

\(13 m\times 9 m=124.2 m^{2} \rightarrow 100m^{2}\)

(d) Calculate the total area of carpet required, with the proper number of significant figures.

Sum all three answers without regard to significant figures:

\(1.10\times 10^{2} m^{2} + 25m^{2} +100m^{2}=235m^{2}\)

The least accurate number is 100 \(m^{2}\) m2, with one significant figure in the hundred’s decimal place:

\(235m^{2} \rightarrow 2\times 10^{2} m^{2}\)

REMARKS Notice that the final answer in part (d) has only one significant figure, in the hundred’s place, resulting in an answer that had to be rounded down by a sizable fraction of its total value. That’s the consequence of having insufficient information. The value of 9 m, without any further information, represents a true value that could be anywhere in the interval [8.5 m, 9.5 m), all of which round to 9 when only one digit is retained.
