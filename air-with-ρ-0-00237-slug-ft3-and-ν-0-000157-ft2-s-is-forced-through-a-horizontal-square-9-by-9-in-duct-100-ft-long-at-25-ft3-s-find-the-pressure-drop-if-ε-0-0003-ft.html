Guest Question.

Air, with ρ = 0.00237 slug/\(ft^3\) and ν = 0.000157 \(ft^2\)/s, is forced through a horizontal square 9-by 9-in duct 100 ft long at 25 \(ft^3\)/s. Find the pressure drop if ε = 0.0003 ft.

Admin
Solution

Compute the mean velocity and hydraulic diameter:

\(V = \frac{25 ft^3/s}{(0.75 ft)^2} = 44.4 ft/s\)

 

\(D_h = \frac{4A}{\mathscr{P}} = \frac{4(81 in^2)}{36 in} = 9 in = 0.75 ft\)

From Table 6.4, for b/a = 1.0, the effective diameter is

\(D_{eff} = \frac{64}{56.91} D_h = 0.843 ft\)

whence \(Re_{eff} = \frac{VD_{eff}}{\nu} = \frac{44.4(0.843)}{0.000157} = 239,000\)

\(\frac{\epsilon}{D_{eff}} = \frac{0.0003}{0.843} = 0.000356\)

From the Moody chart, read f = 0.0177. Then the pressure drop is

\(\Delta p = \rho g h_f = \rho g \left(f\frac{L}{D_h}\frac{V^2}{2g}\right) = 0.00237(32.2) \left[0.0177\frac{100}{0.75}\frac{44.4^2}{2(32.2)}\right]\)

or Δp = 5.5 lbf/\(ft^2\)

Pressure drop in air ducts is usually small because of the low density.
