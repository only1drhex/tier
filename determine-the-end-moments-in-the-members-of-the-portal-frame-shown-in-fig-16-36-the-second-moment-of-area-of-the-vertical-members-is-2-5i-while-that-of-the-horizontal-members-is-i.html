Guest Question.

Determine the end moments in the members of the portal frame shown in Fig. 16.36; the second moment of area of the vertical members is 2.5I while that of the horizontal members is I.

Admin
Solution

In this particular problem the approach is very similar to that for the continuous beam of . However, due to the unsymmetrical geometry of the frame and also to the application of the 10 kN load, the frame will sway such that there will be horizontal displacements, \(v_{ B }\) and \(v_{ C }\), at B and C in the members BA and CD. Since we are ignoring displacements produced by axial forces then \(v_{ B }=v_{ C }=v_{1}\), say. We would, in fact, have a similar situation in a continuous beam if one or more of the supports experienced settlement. Also we note that the rotation, \(\theta_{ A }\), at A must be zero since the end A of the member AB is fixed.

Initially, as in , we calculate the FEMs in the members of the frame, again using the results of and . The effect of the cantilever CE may be included by replacing it by its end moment, thereby reducing the number of equations to be solved. Thus, from Fig. 16.36 we have

\(M_{ CE }^{ F }=-\frac{3 \times 6^{2}}{2}=-54 kNm\)

 

\(M_{ AB }^{ F }=-M_{ BA }^{ F }=-\frac{10 \times 10}{8}=-12.5 kNm\)

 

\(M_{ BC }^{ F }=-M_{ CB }^{ F }=-\frac{3 \times 20^{2}}{12}=-100 kNm M_{ CD }^{ F }=M_{ DC }^{ F }=0\)

Now, from Eqs (16.28) and (16.30)

\(M_{ AB }=-\frac{2 E I}{L}\left[2 \theta_{ A }+\theta_{ B }+\frac{3}{L}\left(v_{ A }-v_{ B }\right)\right]+M_{ AB }^{ F }\) (16.28)

\(M_{ BA }=-\frac{2 E I}{L}\left[2 \theta_{ B }+\theta_{ A }+\frac{3}{L}\left(v_{ A }-v_{ B }\right)\right]+M_{ BA }^{ F }\) (16.30)

\(M_{ AB }=-\frac{2 \times 2.5 E I}{10}\left(\theta_{ B }-\frac{3}{10} v_{1}\right)-12.5\) (i)

\(M_{ BA }=-\frac{2 \times 2.5 E I}{10}\left(2 \theta_{ B }-\frac{3}{10} v_{1}\right)+12.5\) (ii)

In Eqs (i) and (ii) we are assuming that the displacement, \(v_{1}\), is to the right. Furthermore

\(M_{ BC }=-\frac{2 E I}{20}\left(2 \theta_{ B }+\theta_{ C }\right)-100\) (iii)

\(M_{ CB }=-\frac{2 E I}{20}\left(2 \theta_{ C }+\theta_{ B }\right)+100\) (iv)

\(M_{ CD }=-\frac{2 \times 2.5 E I}{10}\left(2 \theta_{ C }+\theta_{ D }+\frac{3}{10} v_{1}\right)\) (v)

\(M_{ DC }=-\frac{2 \times 2.5 E I}{10}\left(2 \theta_{ D }+\theta_{ C }+\frac{3}{10} v_{1}\right)\) (vi)

From the equilibrium of the member end moments at the joints

\(M_{ BA }+M_{ BC }=0 \quad M_{ CB }+M_{ CD }-54=0 \quad M_{ DC }=0\)

Substituting in the equilibrium equations for \(M_{ BA }, M_{ BC }\), etc., from Eqs (i)–(vi) we obtain

\(1.25 E I \theta_{ B }+0.1 E I \theta_{ C }-0.15 E I v_{1}+87.5=0\) (vii)

\(1.2 E I \theta_{ C }+0.1 E I \theta_{ B }+0.5 E I \theta_{ C }+0.15 E I v_{1}-46=0\) (viii)

\(E I \theta_{ D }+0.5 E I \theta_{ C }+0.15 E I v_{1}=0\) (ix)

Since there are four unknown displacements we require a further equation for a solution. This may be obtained by considering the overall horizontal equilibrium of the frame. Thus

\(S_{ AB }+S_{ DC }-10=0\)

in which, from Eq. (16.29)

\(S_{ AB }=\frac{6 E I}{L^{2}}\left[\theta_{ A }+\theta_{ B }+\frac{2}{L}\left(v_{ A }-v_{ B }\right)\right]+S_{ AB }^{ F }\) (16.29)

\(S_{ AB }=\frac{6 \times 2.5 E I}{10^{2}} \theta_{ B }-\frac{12 \times 2.5 E I}{10^{3}} v_{1}+5\)

where the last term on the right-hand side is \(S_{ AB }^{ F }(=+5 kN )\), the contribution of the 10 kN horizontal load to \(S_{ AB }\). Also

\(S_{ DC }=\frac{6 \times 2.5 E I}{10^{2}}\left(\theta_{ D }+\theta_{ C }\right)-\frac{12 \times 2.5 E I}{10^{3}} v_{1}\)

Hence, substituting for \(S_{ AB }\) and \(S_{ DC }\) in the equilibrium equations, we have

\(E I \theta_{ B }+E I \theta_{ D }+E I \theta_{ C }-0.4 E I v_{1}-33.3=0\) (x)

Solving Eqs (vii)–(x) we obtain

\(E I \theta_{ B }=-101.5 \quad E I \theta_{ C }=+73.2 \quad E I \theta_{ D }=-9.8 \quad E I v_{1}=-178.6\)

Substituting these values in Eqs (i)–(vi) yields

\(M_{ AB }=11.5 kNm M_{ BA }=87.2 kNm \quad M_{ BC }=-87.2 kNm\)

 

\(M_{ CB }=95.5 kNm \quad M_{ CD }=-41.5 kNm\)

 

\(M_{ DC }=0 \quad M_{ CE }=-54 kNm\)
