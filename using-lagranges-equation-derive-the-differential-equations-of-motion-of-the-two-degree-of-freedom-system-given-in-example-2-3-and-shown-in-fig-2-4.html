Guest Question.

Using Lagrange’s equation, derive the differential equations of motion of the two degree of freedom system given in and shown in Fig. 2.4.

Admin
Solution

Since the system has the two degrees of freedom \( q_1=x_1\) and \( q_2=x_2\) as generalized coordinates, the application of Lagrange’s equation yields the two equations

\( \frac{d}{dt} \left(\frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{q_1} }\right) -\frac{\partial T}{\partial q_1 } = Q_1, \frac{d}{dt} \left(\frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{q_2} }\right) -\frac{\partial T}{\partial q_2 } = Q_2 \)

where T is the total kinetic energy of the system and \(Q_1\) and \(Q_2\) are the generalized forces associated, respectively, with the system generalized coordinates. The kinetic energy T is defined as

\(T = \frac{1}{2} m_1\dot{q} ^{2}_{1} + \frac{1}{2} m_2\dot{q} ^{2}_{2} = \frac{1}{2} \left[\begin{matrix} \dot {q_1} & \dot {q_2} \end{matrix} \right]\left[ \begin{matrix} m_{1} & 0 \\ 0 & m_{2} \end{matrix}\right] \left[\begin{matrix} \dot q_{1} \\ \dot q_{2} \end{matrix} \right] \)

It was shown in that the generalized forces \(Q_1\) and \(Q_2\) are given by

\( Q_1 = F_1(t) – (k_1 + k_2)q_1 – (c_1 + c_2) \dot q_1 + k_2q_2 + c_2 \dot q_2 \)

 

\( Q_2 = F_2 (t) – k_2 q_2 – c_2 \dot q_2 + k_2q_1 + c_2 \dot q_1\)

By using the definition of the kinetic energy, one obtains

\(\frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{q_1} }=m_1\dot{q_1}, \frac{d}{dt} \left(\frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{q_1} }\right) =m_1\ddot{q_1},\frac{\partial T}{\partial q_1 }= 0\)

 

\(\frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{q_2} }=m_2\dot{q_2}, \frac{d}{dt} \left(\frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{q_2} }\right) =m_2\ddot{q_2},\frac{\partial T}{\partial q_2 }= 0\)

Substituting into Lagrange’s equation for q1 and q2 yields, respectively, the
following two differential equations:

\(m_{1}\ddot{q} _{1}=F_{1}\left(t\right) – \left(k_{1} + k_{2} \right)q_{1}-\left(c_{1}+c_{2} \right)\dot{q}_{1}+k_{2}q_{2} + c_{2}\dot{q}_{2} \)

 

\(m_{2}\ddot{q} _{2}=F_{2}\left(t\right) -k_{2}q_{2} – c_{2}\dot{q}_{2} +k_{2}q_{1} + c_{2}\dot{q}_{1}\)

or

\(m_1\ddot{q_1} + (c_1+c_2)\dot{q_1}-c_2\dot{q_2} + (k_1+k_2)q_1-k_2q_2 = F_1(t)\)

 

\(m_2\ddot{q_1} + c_2\dot{q_2} – c_2\dot{q_1} + k_2q_2 – k_2q_1 = F_2(t)\)

which can be written in a matrix form as

\( \left[\begin{matrix} m_{1} & 0 \\ 0 & m_{2} \end{matrix}\right] \left[\begin{matrix} \ddot q_{1} \\ \ddot q_{2} \end{matrix} \right] + \left[\begin{matrix} c_1 + c_2 & -c_2 \\ -c_2 & c_2 \end{matrix}\right]\left[\begin{matrix} \dot q_{1} \\ \dot q_{2} \end{matrix} \right] +\left[\begin{matrix} k_1 + k_2 & -k_2 \\ -k_2 & k_2 \end{matrix}\right]\left[\begin{matrix} q_1 \\ q_2 \end{matrix} \right] = \left[\begin{matrix} F_1(t) \\ F_2(t) \end{matrix} \right]\)

which is the same matrix equation that can be obtained for this two degree of freedom system by applying Newton’s second law.
