Guest Question.

What load can a 2 m square column carry in a dense sand (\(\gamma=20 kN / m ^{3}\) and \(\phi\) = 36°) at a depth of 1 m, if the settlement is not to exceed 30 mm ? Assume a factor of safety of 3 against shear and that the ground water table is at a great depth.

Admin
Solution
\(\gamma=20 kN / m ^{3} D_{f}=1 m b=2 m \text { (Square footing) } \phi=36^{\circ}\)

For \(\phi\) = 36, N = 30 (from Fig. 14.18, after Peck, Hanson and Thornburn)
For N = 30, b = 2 m permissible settlement = 40 mm,
Allowable soil pressure = 500 kN/\(m ^{2}\). (from Fig. 14.21 after Terzaghi and Peck)
For a permissible settlement of 30 mm,
Allowable pressure \(e =\frac{500 \times 30}{40}=375 kN / m ^{2}\)

For \(\phi=36^{\circ}, N_{q}=43 \text { and } N_{\gamma}=46\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\therefore \quad q_{\text {net ult }} &=0.4 \gamma b N_{\gamma}+\gamma D_{f}\left(N_{q}-1\right) \\&=0.4 \times 20 \times 2 \times 46+20 \times 1 \times 42=736+840=1576 kN / m ^{2} \\q_{\text {safe }} &=\frac{1576}{3}=525 kN / m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)

∴ Settlement governs the design, and

\(q_{\text {allowable }}=375 kN / m ^{2}\)

Safe load for the column = 375 × 2 × 2 = 1500 kN.
