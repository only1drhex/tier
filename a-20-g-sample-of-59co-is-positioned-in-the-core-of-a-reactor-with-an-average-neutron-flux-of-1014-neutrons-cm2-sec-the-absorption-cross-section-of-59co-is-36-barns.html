Guest Question.

A 20-g sample \(of^{\ \ 59}\)Co is positioned in the core of a reactor with an average neutron flux of \(10^{14}\) neutrons/\(cm^{2}\) sec. The absorption cross section \(of^{\ \ 59}\)Co is 36 barns.

a. What is the activity of the sample after 6.0 years?

b. What is the maximum activity for the sample?

c. When does the sample activity reach 90% of its maximum activity?

Admin
Solution

a.

\(N=\frac{20 g (6.02 × 10^{23} atoms/gram − atomic\ mass}{59 \ grams/gram − atomic \ mass} = 2.04 × 10^{23} \ atoms\)

 

\(A=\varphi N\sigma \left(1- e^{-\left(0.693\right)t/\left(T_{1/2}\right) } \right)\)

 

\(A=(10^{14} neutrons/cm^{2}- sec)(2.04 × 10^{23} atoms)(36 × 10^{-24} cm2)\times \left[1- e^{-\left(0.693\right)6.0yrs/5.3yr } \right] \)

 

\(\ \ \ = 4.00 × 10^{14} Bq\)

b.

\(A_{max}=\varphi N\sigma \)

 

\(A=(10^{14} neutrons/cm^{2}- sec)(2.04 × 10^{23} atoms)(36 × 10^{-24} cm^{2})\)

 

\(\ \ \ =7.33 × 10^{14} Bq\)

c.

\((7.33 × 10^{14} Bq)(0.9) = 6.60 × 10^{14} Bq\)

 

\(6.60 × 10^{14} Bq = 7.33 × 10^{14} Bq\left[1- e^{-\left(0.693\right)t/5.3yr }\right]\)

 

\(6.60 × 10^{14} Ci = 7.33 × 10^{14} − 7.33 × 10^{14} e^{-0.131t}\)

 

\(7.33 × 10^{14} e^{-0.131}= 0.73 × 10^{14}\)

 

\(e^{-0.131t}=0.10\)

 

\(0.131t=2.30\)

t = 17.6 years to reach 90% \(A_{max}\)
