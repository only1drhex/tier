Superposition of a Source and Sink of Equal Strength

Consider an irrotational region of flow composed of a line source of strength \(\dot{V} / L\) at location (-a, 0) and a line sink of the same strength (but opposite sign) at (a, 0), as sketched in Fig. 10‚Äì48. Generate an expression for the stream function in both Cartesian and cylindrical coordinates.

SOLUTION We are to superpose a source and a sink, and generate an expression for ùúì in both Cartesian and cylindrical coordinates.

Assumptions The region of flow under consideration is incompressible and irrotational.

Analysis We use Eq. 10‚Äì44 to obtain ùúì for the source,

Line source at point (a, b):

\(\begin{aligned}\phi &=\frac{\dot{V} / L}{2 \pi} \ln r_{1}=\frac{\dot{V} / L}{2 \pi} \ln \sqrt{(x-a)^{2}+(y-b)^{2}} \\\psi &=\frac{\dot{V} / L}{2 \pi} \theta_{1}=\frac{\dot{V} / L}{2 \pi} \arctan \frac{y-b}{x-a}\end{aligned}\) (10-44)

 

Line source at (‚àía, 0): \(\psi_{1}=\frac{\dot{V} / L}{2 \pi} \theta_{1} \quad \text { where } \quad \theta_{1}=\arctan \frac{y}{x+a}\) (1)

Similarly for the sink,

Line sink at (a, 0): \(\psi_{2}=\frac{-\dot{V} / L}{2 \pi} \theta_{2} \quad \text { where } \quad \theta_{2}=\arctan \frac{y}{x-a}\) (2)

Superposition enables us to simply add the two stream functions, Eqs. 1 and 2, to obtain the composite stream function,

Composite stream function: \(\psi=\psi_{1}+\psi_{2}=\frac{\dot{V} / L}{2 \pi}\left(\theta_{1}-\theta_{2}\right)\) (3)

We rearrange Eq. 3 and take the tangent of both sides to get

\(\tan \frac{2 \pi \psi}{\dot{U} / L}=\tan \left(\theta_{1}-\theta_{2}\right)=\frac{\tan \theta_{1}-\tan \theta_{2}}{1+\tan \theta_{1} \tan \theta_{2}}\) (4)

where we have used a trigonometric identity (Fig. 10‚Äì49).

We substitute Eqs. 1 and 2 for \(\theta_{1} \text { and } \theta_{2}\) and perform some algebra to obtain an expression for the stream function,

\(\tan \frac{2 \pi \psi}{\dot{V} / L}=\frac{\frac{y}{x+a}-\frac{y}{x-a}}{1+\frac{y}{x+a} \frac{y}{x-a}}=\frac{-2 a y}{x^{2}+y^{2}-a^{2}}\)

or, taking the arctangent of both sides,

Final result, Cartesian coordinates: \(\psi=\frac{-\dot{V} / L}{2 \pi} \arctan \frac{2 a y}{x^{2}+y^{2}-a^{2}}\) (5)

We translate to cylindrical coordinates by using Eqs. 10‚Äì38,

Final result, cylindrical coordinates: \(\psi=\frac{-\dot{V} / L}{2 \pi} \arctan \frac{2 a r \sin \theta}{r^{2}-a^{2}}\) (6)

Discussion If the source and sink were to switch places, the result would be the same, except that the negative sign on source strength \(\dot{V} / L\) would disappear.
