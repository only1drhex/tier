Guest Question.

A spring loaded governor of the Hartnell type has arms of equal length. The masses rotate in a circle of 130 mm diameter when the sleeve is in the mid position and the ball arms are vertical. The equilibrium speed for this position is 450 r.p.m., neglecting friction. The maximum sleeve movement is to be 25 mm and the maximum variation of speed taking in account the friction to be 5 per cent of the mid position speed. The mass of the sleeve is 4 kg and the friction may be considered equivalent to 30 N at the sleeve. The power of the governor must be sufficient to overcome the friction by one per cent change of speed either way at mid-position. Determine, neglecting obliquity effect of arms ; 1. The value of each rotating mass : 2. The spring stiffness in N/mm ; and 3. The initial compression of spring

Admin
Solution

Given : x = y ; d = 130 mm or r = 65 mm = 0.065 m ; N = 450 r.p.m. or \(\omega=2 \pi \times 450 / 60\) = 47.23 rad/s ; h = 25 mm = 0.025 m ; M = 4 kg ; F = 30 N

1. Value of each rotating mass

Let m = Value of each rotating mass in kg, and

S = Spring force on the sleeve at mid position in newtons.

Since the change of speed at mid position to overcome friction is 1 per cent either way (i.e.\(\pm 1 \%\)), therefore

Minimum speed at mid position,

\(\omega=\omega-0.01 \omega=0.99 \omega=0.99 \times 47.13=46.66 rad / s\)

and maximum speed at mid-position,

\(\omega_{2}=\omega+0.01 \omega=1.01 \omega=1.01 \times 47.13=47.6 rad / s\)

\(\therefore \) Centrifugal force at the minimum speed,

\(F_{ Cl }=m\left(\omega_{1}\right)^{2} r=m(46.66)^{2} 0.065=141.5 m N\)

and centrifugal force at the maximum speed,

\(F_{ C 2}=m\left(\omega_{2}\right)^{2} r=m(47.6)^{2} 0.0065=147.3 m N\)

We know that for minimum speed at midposition,

\(S+(M \cdot g+F)=2 F_{ Cl } \times \frac{x}{y}\)

or \(S+(4 \times 9.81-30)=2 \times 141.5 m \times 1\) \(\ldots(\because x=y)\)

\(\therefore\) S + 9.24 = 283 m . . . (i)

and for maximum speed at mid-position,

\(S+(M \cdot g+F)=2 F_{ C 2} \times \frac{x}{y}\)

 

\(S+(4 \times 9.81+30)=2 \times 147.3 m \times 1\)

\(\therefore\) S + 69.24 = 294.6 m …(ii)

From equations (i) and (ii),

m = 5.2 kg

 

2. Spring stiffness in N/mm

Let s = Spring stiffness in N/mm.

Since the maximum variation of speed, considering friction is Â± 5% of the mid-position speed, therefore,

Minimum speed considering friction,

\(\omega_{1}{ }^{\prime}=\omega-0.05 \omega=0.95 \omega=0.95 \times 47.13=44.8 rad / s\)

and maximum speed considering friction,

\(\omega_{2}^{\prime}=\omega+0.05 \omega=1.05 \omega=1.05 \times 47.13=49.5 rad / s\)

We know that minimum radius of rotation considering friction,

\(r_{1}=r-h_{1} \times \frac{x}{y}=0.065-\frac{0.025}{2}=0.0525 m\) \(\ldots\left(\because x=y, \text { and } h_{1}=\frac{h}{2}\right)\)

and maximum radius of rotation considering friction,

\(r_{2}=r+h_{2} \times \frac{x}{y}=0.065+\frac{0.025}{2}=0.0775 m\) \(\left(\because x=y, \text { and } h_{2}=\frac{h}{2}\right)\)

\(\therefore \) Centrifugal force at the minimum speed considering friction,

\(F_{ Cl }^{\prime}=m\left(\omega_{1}^{\prime}\right)^{2} r_{1}=5.2(44.8)^{2} 0.0525=548 N\)

and centrifugal force at the maximum speed considering friction,

\(F_{ C 2}^{\prime}=m\left(\omega_{2}\right)^{2} r_{2}=5.2(49.5)^{2} 0.0775=987 N\)

Let \(S_{1}\) = Spring force at minimum speed considering friction, and

\(S_{2}\) = Spring force at maximum speed considering friction.

We know that for minimum speed considering friction,

\(S_{1}+(M \cdot g-F)=2 F_{ Cl }^{\prime} \times \frac{x}{y}\)

\(S_{1}+(4 \times 9.81-30)=2 \times 548 \times 1\) \(\ldots(\because x=y)\)

\(\therefore \) \(S_{1}+9.24=1096 \quad \text { or } \quad S_{1}=1096-9.24=1086.76 N\)

and for maximum speed considering friction,

\(S_{2}+(M \cdot g+F)=2 F_{ C 2}^{\prime} \times \frac{x}{y}\)

\(S_{2}+(4 \times 9.81+30)=2 \times 987 \times 1\) \(\cdots(\because x=y)\)

\(\therefore \) \(S_{2}+69.24=1974 \quad \text { or } \quad S_{2}=1974-69.24=1904.76 N\)

We know that stiffness of the spring,

\(s=\frac{S_{2}-S_{1}}{h}=\frac{1904.76-1086.76}{25}=32.72 N / mm\)

 

3. Initial compression of the spring

We know that initial compression of the spring

\(=\frac{S_{1}}{s}=\frac{1086.76}{32.72}=33.2 mm\)
