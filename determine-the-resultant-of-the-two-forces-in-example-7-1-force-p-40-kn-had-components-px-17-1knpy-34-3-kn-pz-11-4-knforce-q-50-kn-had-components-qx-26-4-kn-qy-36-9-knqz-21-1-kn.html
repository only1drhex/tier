Guest Question.

Determine the resultant of the two forces in . (Force P = 40 kN had components \(P_{x}\) = 17.1kN,\(P_{y}\) = 34.3 kN, \(P_{z}\) = 11.4 kN;force Q = 50 kN had components \(Q_{x}\) = 26.4 kN, \(Q_{y}\) = -36.9 kN,\(Q_{z}\) = 21.1 kN.Both forces act through point A.)

Admin
Solution

Summing forces in the x, y, and z directions, we have

\(R_{x} = ΣF_{x} = P_{x} + Q_{x}\) = 17.1 + 26.4 = 43.5 kN

 

\(R_{y} = ΣF_{y} = P_{y} + Q_{y}\) = 34.3 – 36.9 = -2.6 kN

 

\(R_{z} = ΣF_{z} = P_{z} + Q_{z}\) = 11.4 + 21.1 = 32.5 kN

and the magnitude and direction of the resultant are given by

\(R=\sqrt{R_{x}^{2}+R_{y}^{2}+R_{z}^{2}}\)

 

\(=\sqrt{(43.5)^{2} + (-2.6)^{2} + (32.5)^{2}}=54.4\ kN\)

and

\(\cos \theta _{x}=\frac{R_{x}}{R}=\frac{43.5}{54.4}=0.7996\) \(\theta_{x} = 36.9^{\circ }\)

 

\(\cos \theta _{y}=\frac{R_{y}}{R}=\frac{-2.6}{54.4}=-0.0478\) \(\theta_{y} = 92.7^{\circ }\)

 

\(\cos \theta _{z}=\frac{R_{z}}{R}=\frac{32.5}{54.4}=0.5972\) \(\theta_{z} = 53.3^{\circ }\)

Check:

\(\cos^{2}\theta_{x}+\cos^{2}\theta_{y}+\cos^{2}\theta_{z}=(0.7996)^{2} + (-0.0478)^{2} + (0.5974)^{2} = 1.000\) OK
