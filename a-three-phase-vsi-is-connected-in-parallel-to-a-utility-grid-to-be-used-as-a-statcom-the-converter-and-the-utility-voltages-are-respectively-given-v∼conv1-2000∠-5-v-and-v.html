Guest Question.

A three-phase VSI is connected in parallel to a utility grid to be used as a STATCOM. The converter and the utility voltages are respectively given: \(\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} = 2000 \angle - 5° V\) and \(\widetilde{V}_g = 1900 \angle 0° V\).

If the interconnection impedance is \(X_L = 7 \Omega\), calculate:

a) The active and reactive power supplied or received by each source.

b) The value of the converter output current.

c) If the \(\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} = 2000 \angle - 5° V\) and \(\widetilde{V}_g = 1900 \angle 0° V\), find the operation mode of the converter.

Admin
Solution

a) The values of the active and reactive power at the PCC are given by:

\(P_g = \frac{\widetilde{V}_g\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \sin \delta }{X_{L,1}} = \frac{1900 \cdot 2000 \sin(-5°)}{7} = -47.313 kW\)

 

\(Q_g = \frac{\widetilde{V}_g\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \cos \delta – \widetilde{V}^2_g }{X_{L,1}} = \frac{1900 \cdot 2000 \cos(- 5°) – (1900)^2}{7} = 25.1 kVAR \)

Using Table 6.21 the meaning of the above results is that the grid is feeding active power to the converter and at the same time is absorbing reactive power from the converter (i.e., the utility is acting as a capacitor and the converter as an inductor). According to Fig. 6.138 the system operates in the third quadrant of the \(P_g-Q_g\) plane and, consequently, the phase angle \(\varphi\) must be between -90° and -180°.

b) According to Eq. (\(\overrightarrow{I}_{conv,1}=\frac{\overrightarrow{V}_{conv,1} – \overrightarrow{V}_{g}}{jX_{L,1}} = \frac{\widetilde{V}_{conv,1}(\cos \delta + j \sin \delta) – \widetilde{V}_g}{jX_{L,1}}\)

 

\(=\frac{\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \cos \delta +j \widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \sin \delta – \widetilde{V}_g }{jX_{L,1}}\)

 

\( = \frac{j \widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \cos \delta – \widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \sin \delta – \widetilde{V}_g }{- X_{L,1}}\)

 

\(= \frac{\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \sin \delta + j( \widetilde{V}_g – \widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \cos \delta)}{X_{L,1}}\)

 

\(=\frac{\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \sin \delta}{X_{L,1}} +j \frac{\widetilde{V}_g – \widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \cos \delta)}{X_{L,1}} = I_p + jI_q\)) the rms values of the fundamental component of the line current and its components fed into the utility are given by:

\(I_p\) = real part of \(\overrightarrow{I}_{conv,1} = \frac{\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \sin \delta}{X_{L,1}} = \frac{2000 \sin(-5)}{7} = -24.9 A\)

 

\(I_q\) = imaginary part of \(\overrightarrow{I}_{conv,1} = \frac{\widetilde{V}_g \widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \cos \delta}{X_{L,1}} = \frac{1900 – 2000 \cos(-5)}{7} = -13.2 A\)

 

\(\widetilde{I}_{conv,1} = \sqrt{(-24.9)^2 + (-13.2)^2} = 28.2 A\)

Since the system operates in the third quadrant of the \(P_g-Q_g\) plane, the phase angle \(\varphi\) is given by:

\(\varphi = -180° + \tan^{-1}(\frac{13.2}{24.9}) = -180° + 27.9° = -152°\)

The vector diagram of the system when \(\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \gt \widetilde{V}_g \) and converter phase voltage is lagging with respect to the utility phase voltage by 5° will be as given in Fig. 6.143.

c) The active and reactive power components are given by:

\(P_g = \frac{\widetilde{V}_g\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \sin \delta }{X_{L,1}} = \frac{1900 \cdot 2000 \sin(-5°)}{7} = -47.313 kW\)

 

\(Q_g = \frac{\widetilde{V}_g\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \cos \delta – \widetilde{V}^2_g }{X_{L,1}} = \frac{2000 \times 1900 \cos(-5°) – (2000)^2}{7} = -30.637 kVAR\)

As it was expected since d has the same value as before the active power remains as in part (a). However, since the utility voltage becomes higher than the inverter voltage, the reactive power will have negative value meaning that the utility will feed reactive power to the converter (i.e., the utility is acting as an inductor and the converter as a capacitor). According to Fig. 6.138 for this mode of operation the system operates in the fourth quadrant of the \(P_g-Q_g\) plane.

According to Eq. (

\(\overrightarrow{I}_{conv,1}=\frac{\overrightarrow{V}_{conv,1} – \overrightarrow{V}_{g}}{jX_{L,1}} = \frac{\widetilde{V}_{conv,1}(\cos \delta + j \sin \delta) – \widetilde{V}_g}{jX_{L,1}}\)

 

\(=\frac{\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \cos \delta +j \widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \sin \delta – \widetilde{V}_g }{jX_{L,1}}\)

 

\( = \frac{j \widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \cos \delta – \widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \sin \delta – \widetilde{V}_g }{- X_{L,1}}\)

 

\(= \frac{\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \sin \delta + j( \widetilde{V}_g – \widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \cos \delta)}{X_{L,1}}\)

 

\(=\frac{\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \sin \delta}{X_{L,1}} +j \frac{\widetilde{V}_g – \widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \cos \delta)}{X_{L,1}} = I_p + jI_q\)) the rms values of the fundamental component of the line current and its components fed into the utility are given by:

\(I_p\) = real part of \(\overrightarrow{I}_{conv,1} = \frac{\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \sin \delta}{X_{L,1}} = \frac{1900 \sin(-5)}{7} = -23.7 A\)

 

\(I_q\) = imaginary part of \(\overrightarrow{I}_{conv,1} = \frac{\widetilde{V}_g \widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \cos \delta}{X_{L,1}} = \frac{2000 – 1900 \cos(-5)}{7} = 15.3 A\)

 

\(\widetilde{I}_{conv,1} = \sqrt{(-23.7)^2 + (15.3)^2} = 28.2 A\)

Since the system operates in the fourth quadrant of the \(P_g-Q_g\) plane, the phase angle \(\varphi\) must be in the range of 90° to 180° and is given by:

\(\varphi = 180° – \tan^{-1}(\frac{15.3}{23.7}) = 180° – 32.92° = 147°\)

The vector diagram of the system when \(\widetilde{V}_{conv,1} \lt \widetilde{V}_{g}\) and converter phase voltage is lagging with respect to the utility phase voltage by 5° will be as given in Fig. 6.144.
