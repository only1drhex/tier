Guest Question.

Water at \(45^{\circ }C\) and 10 kPa enters an adiabatic pump and is discharged at a pressure of 8600 kPa. Assume the pump efficiency to be 0.75. Calculate the work of the pump, the temperature change of the water, and the entropy change of the water.

Admin
Solution

The following are properties for saturated liquid water at \(45^{\circ }C\) (318.15 K):

\( V = 1010 cm^{3}\cdot kg^{−1} \beta = 425 \times 10^{−6} K^{−1} C_{P} = 4.178 kJ\cdot kg^{−1}\cdot K^{−1}\)

By Eq. (7.24),

\( W_{s}\)(isentropic)\(=(\Delta H)_{S}=V(P_{2}-P_{1})\) (7.24)

 

\( W_{s}\)(isentropic)\(=(\Delta H)_{S}=(1010)(8600-10)=8.676\times 10^{6}kPa\cdot cm^{3}\cdot kg^{-1}\)

Because \(1 kJ = 106 kPa\cdot cm^{3},\)

\( W_{s}\)(isentropic)\(=(\Delta H)_{S}=8.676 kJ\cdot kg^{-1}\)

By Eq. (7.17), \( \Delta H=\frac{(\Delta H)_{S}}{\eta }=\frac{8.676}{0.75}=11.57 kJ\cdot kg^{-1}\)

 

\( \eta \equiv \frac{(\Delta H)_{S}}{\Delta H}\) (7.17)

and \( W_{s}=\Delta H=11.57 kJ\cdot kg^{-1}\)

The temperature change of the water during pumping, from Eq. (7.25

\( \Delta H=C_{P}\Delta T+V(1-\beta T)\Delta P\) (7.25)

\( 11.57=4.178\Delta T+1010[1-(425\times 10^{-6})(318.15)]\frac{8590}{10^{6}}\)

Solution for \(\Delta T\) gives:

\( \Delta T = 0.97 K\) or \(0.97^{\circ}C\)

The entropy change of the water is given by Eq. (7.26):

\( \Delta S=C_{P}\ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-\beta V\Delta P\) (7.26)

\( \Delta S=4.178\ln \frac{319.12}{318.15}-(425\times 10^{-6})(1010)\frac{8590}{10^{6}}=0.0090 kJ\cdot kg^{-1}\cdot K^{-1}\)
