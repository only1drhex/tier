Guest Question.

Species Fugacity Calculation for a Hydrocarbon Mixture

Compute the fugacities of n-pentane and benzene in their equimolar mixture at 373.15 K and 50 bar.

Admin
Solution

The simplest estimate is obtained using the Lewis-Randall rule and the results of . In this case we obtain

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{f}_{ PE }\left(373.15 K , 50 bar , x_{ PE }=0.5\right) &=0.5 \times f_{ PE }(373.15 K , 50 bar ) \\&=0.5 \times 6.22 bar =3.11 bar\end{aligned}\)

 

and

 

\(\bar{f}_{ BZ }\left(373.15 K , 50 bar , x_{ BZ }=0.5\right)=0.5 \times 1.98 bar =0.99 bar\)

 

A better estimate is obtained using the Peng-Robinson equation of state with a value \(k_{ PE – BZ }=0.018\) from Table 9.4-1. The results are

 

\(\begin{array}{l}\bar{f}_{ PE }\left(373.15 K , 50 bar , x_{ PE }=0.5\right)=3.447 bar \\\bar{f}_{ BZ }\left(373.15 K , 50 bar , x_{ BZ }=0.5\right)=1.129 bar\end{array}\)
