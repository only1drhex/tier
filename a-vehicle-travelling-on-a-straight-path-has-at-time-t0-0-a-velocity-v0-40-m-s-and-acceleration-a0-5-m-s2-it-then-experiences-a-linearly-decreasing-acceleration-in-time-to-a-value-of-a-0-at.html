Guest Question.

A vehicle travelling on a straight path has, at time \(t_{0} = 0\), a velocity \(\ v_{0} = 40 m/s \) and acceleration \(\ a_{0} = 5 m/s^{2} \) . It then experiences a linearly decreasing acceleration in time to a value of a = 0 at t = 6 s. Next, it travels a distance \(\ s_{2} = 550 m \) in uniform motion and finally in a third phase of travel it is uniformly decelerated with an acceleration \(\ |a_{3}| = 11 m/s^{2} \) until it stops.
At what time and in what location does the vehicle come to a stop? Sketch the acceleration-, velocity-, and position-time diagrams.

Admin
Solution

For each of the three time intervals of the motion, we will introduce a new time variable (Fig. 1.7a). Variables at the end of a time interval will be denoted with a star. The position x will be computed relative to the location of the vehicle at time \(\ t_{0}=0 \).

1. Linear acceleration \(\ \left(0\leqq t_{1} \leqq t^{*}_{1} \right) .\)
The time variation of the acceleration can be represented as \(\ a_{1} =a_{0} \left(1-t_{1}/t^{*}_{1} \right)\). Considering the initial conditions \(\ x_{1} \left(t_{1} =0\right) =0 \) and \(\ v_{1} \left(t_{1} =0\right) = v_{0} \) one obtains from (1.11) and (1.12)

\(dv=a(t)dt\rightarrow v=v_{0}+\int_{t_{0}}^{t}{a(\bar{t})d\bar{t}} \) (1.11)

\(dx=v(t)dt\rightarrow x=x_{0}+\int_{t_{0}}^{t}{v(\bar{t})d\bar{t}} \) (1.12)

the velocity

\(\ v_{1} =v_{0}+ \int\limits_{0}^{t_{1}}{{a_{0}\left(1-\frac{\bar{t}_{1} }{t^{*}_{1} } \right) }{d \bar{t}_{1}}}=v_{0}+a_{0}\left(t_{1}-\frac{t^{2}_{1} }{2t^{*}_{1} } \right) \)

and position

\( x_{1} = \int\limits_{0}^{t_{1}}{v_{1}}d{\bar{t}_{1}}=v_{0}t_{1}+a_{0}\left(\frac{t^{2}_{1} }{2}-\frac{t^{3}_{1} }{6t^{*}_{1} } \right) . \)

At the end of this interval of the motion \( \left(t_{1} =t^{*}_{1}=6s\right) \) we have
\(v^{*}_{1}=v_{0}+a_{0}\frac{t^{*}_{1} }{2} =40+5.3=55\frac{m}{s} ,\)

\(x^{*}_{1}=v_{0}t^{*}_{1}+a_{0}\frac{{t^{*}_{1} }^{2}}{3} =40.6+5.\frac{6^{2}}{3} =300 m.\)

2. Uniform motion \( \left(0\leqq t_{2} \leqq t^{*}_{2} \right) .\)
In the second interval of the motion, the velocity is a constant with value \(v_{2}=v^{*}_{1}=55 m/s.\) Thus the position is given by
\( x_{2}=x^{*}_{1}+v_{2}t_{2}.\)

At time \(\ t^{*}_{2} \) the vehicle has traveled a total distance of
\(x^{*}_{2}=x^{*}_{1}+s_{2}=300+550=850 m. \)

From \( s_{2}=v_{2}t^{*}_{2}=550 m, \) follows the time
\(t^{*}_{2}=\frac{s_{2}}{v_{2}}=\frac{550}{55} =10s \)

3. Constant deceleration \(\left(0\leqq t_{3} \leqq t^{*}_{3} \right) .\)

The final conditions of the second time interval \( \left ( x^{*}_{2}, v^{*}_{2}=v_{2} \right) \) are the initial conditions for the third time interval. Thus we find (\(a_{3}\) is the magnitude of the deceleration)

\( v_{3}=v^{*}_{2}-a_{3}t_{3} \) ,

\( x_{3}=x^{*}_{2}+v^{*}_{2}t_{3} -a_{3}\frac{t^{2}_{3}}{2}\).

The time at which the vehicle comes to a stop follows from

\( v^{*}_{3}=v^{*}_{2}-a_{3}t^{*}_{3}=0 \rightarrow t^{*}_{3}=\frac{v^{*}_{2}}{a_{3}} =\frac{55}{11} =5s, \)
and the final position is obtained as

\( \underline{\underline{x_{final} } }= x^{*}_{3}=x^{*}_{2}+v^{*}_{2}t^{*}_{3}-a_{3}\frac{t^{*2}_{3}}{2} =850+55.5-11\frac{5^{2}}{2}=\underline{ \underline{987.5 m}}\).

The total time of travel is

\(\underline{\underline{t_{final} } }= t^{*}_{1}+t^{*}_{2}+t^{*}_{3}=6+10+5=\underline{\underline{21 s } }\) .

Figs. 1.7b-d show the acceleration-, velocity-, and position-time diagrams. At the time where the acceleration has a jump, the velocity has a change in slope. In the position-time curve there is no such change in slope as the vehicle has not experienced any jumps in velocity. (Jumps in v only occur during impacts, cf. Section 2.5)
