Guest Question.

Two bodies (weights \(m_{1}g\gt m_{2}g \) ) are connected by a cable which is passed over a wheel (moment of inertia \(\Theta _{0}\) ). The coefficient of kinetic friction between the body and the inclined plane is \(\mu \).

Determine the velocity of the bodies in dependence of their position. Assume that they are initially at rest and that there is no slip between cable and wheel.

Admin
Solution

The velocity can be determined easiest by using the workenergy theorem

\(T-T_{0}=U\)

With the kinematic relations (constraint by the cable, no slipping)

\(x_{1}=x_{2}=r\varphi =x\) ,

\(\rightsquigarrow \dot{x}_{1}=\dot{x}_{2}=r\dot{\varphi }=\dot{x}=v\) ,

the kinetic energies follow as

\(E_{k0} =0\) (initial position = rest),

\(E_{k} =\frac{1}{2}m_{1}\dot{x}^{2}_{1}+ \frac{1}{2}m_{2}\dot{x}^{2}_{2}+\frac{1}{2}\Theta _{0}\dot{\varphi }^{2}= \frac{1}{2} m_{1}v^{2}\left(1+\frac{m_{2} }{m_{1 } }+\frac{\Theta _{0} }{m_{1}r^{2} } \right) \) .

Using the friction law \(R = \mu N = \mu m_{2}g\cos \alpha \) , the work of the external
forces is given by

\(U=m_{1}gx_{1}-Rx_{2}-\left(m_{2}g\sin \alpha \right)x_{2}=m_{1}gx\left(1-\mu \frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} }\cos \alpha -\frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} }\sin \alpha \right) \) .

Thus, the work-energy theorem leads to

\(\frac{1}{2}m_{1}v^{2}\left(1+\frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} }+\frac{\Theta _{0} }{m_{1}r^{2} } \right)=m_{1}gx\left(1-\mu \frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} }\cos \alpha -\frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} }\sin \alpha \right) \)

 

\(\rightsquigarrow \underline{\underline{ v\left(x\right)=\sqrt{2gx}\sqrt{\frac{1-\mu \frac{m_{2} }{m_{1 } }\cos \alpha -\frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} }\sin \alpha }{1+\frac{m_{2} }{m_{1} }+\frac{\Theta _{0} }{m_{1}r^{2} } } }} } \) .
