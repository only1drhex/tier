Guest Question.

Calculate the forward-bias gate voltage required in an \(\mathrm{n}\)-channel GaAs enhancement mode pn JFET to open up a channel.

Consider a GaAs n-channel pn JFET at \(T=300 \mathrm{~K}\) with \(N_{a}=10^{18} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}, N_{d}=3 \times 10^{15} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}\), and \(a=0.70 \mu \mathrm{m}\). Determine the forward-bias gate voltage required to open a channel region that is \(0.10 \mu \mathrm{m}\) thick with zero drain voltage.

Admin
Solution

The built-in potential barrier is

\(V_{b i}=V_{t} \ln \left(\frac{N_{a} N_{d}}{n_{i}^{2}}\right)=(0.0259) \ln \left[\frac{\left(10^{18}\right)\left(3 \times 10^{15}\right)}{\left(1.8 \times 10^{6}\right)^{2}}\right]=1.25 \mathrm{~V}\)

The internal pinchoff voltage is

\(V_{p 0}=\frac{e a^{2} N_{d}}{2 \epsilon_{s}}=\frac{\left(1.6 \times 10^{-19}\right)\left(0.7 \times 10^{-4}\right)^{2}\left(3 \times 10^{15}\right)}{2(13.1)\left(8.85 \times 10^{-14}\right)}=1.01 \mathrm{~V}\)

which gives a threshold voltage of

\(V_{T}=V_{b i}-V_{p 0}=0.24 \mathrm{~V}\)

The channel depletion width is given by Equation (13.1). Setting \(h=0.60 \mu \mathrm{m}\) will yield an undepleted channel thickness of \(0.1 \mu \mathrm{m}\). Solving for \(V_{G S}\), we obtain

\(\begin{aligned}h=\left[\frac{2 \boldsymbol{\epsilon}_{s}\left(V_{b i}-V_{G S}\right)}{e N_{d}}\right]^{1 / 2} \\ \end{aligned}\) (13.1)

\(\begin{aligned}V_{G S}&=V_{b i}-\frac{e h^{2} N_{d}}{2 \epsilon_{s}} =1.25-\frac{\left(1.6 \times 10^{-19}\right)\left(0.6 \times 10^{-4}\right)^{2}\left(3 \times 10^{15}\right)}{2(13.1)\left(8.85 \times 10^{-14}\right)} \\&= 1.25-0.745=0.50 \mathrm{~V}\end{aligned}\)

Comment

An applied gate voltage of \(0.50 \mathrm{~V}\) is greater than the threshold voltage, so the induced depletion region will be smaller than the metallurgical channel thickness. An n-channel region is then formed between the source and drain contacts. The forward-bias gate voltage must not be too large or an undesirable gate current will be present in the device.
