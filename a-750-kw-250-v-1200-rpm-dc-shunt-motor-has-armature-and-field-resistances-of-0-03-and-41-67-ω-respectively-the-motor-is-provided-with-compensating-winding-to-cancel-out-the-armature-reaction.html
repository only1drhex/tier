Guest Question.

A 750 kW, 250 V, 1200 rpm dc shunt motor has armature and field resistances of 0.03 and \( 41.67 \Omega\) respectively. The motor is provided with compensating winding to cancel out the armature reaction. Iron and rotational losses can be ignored. The motor is carrying constant torque load drawing 126 A and running at 1105 rpm.

(a) Assuming magnetic linearity, what would be the line current and motor speed if the field current is reduced to 5 A. Also calculate the load torque.

(b) The motor has magnetization data as below. Calculate motor speed and line current as in part (a). Compare and comment upon the results of parts (a) and (b)

Speed: 1200 rpm

\(I_{f}(A)\)

 4 5 6 7
\( V_{O C}(V) \) 217 250 267

280
Admin
Solution

(a) Linear magnetization characteristic

\( I_{f 1} =\frac{250}{41.67}=6 \mathrm{~A} \)

 

\( I_{a 1} =126-6=120 \mathrm{~A} \)

 

\( E_{a 1} =250-0.03 \times 120=246.4 \mathrm{~V} \)

 

\( n_{1} =1105 \mathrm{rpm}, \omega_{1}=115.7 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s} \)

 

Now we know

 

\( E_{a} =K_{a} \Phi \omega=K_{a}^{\prime \prime} I_{f} \omega \) (i)

\( T =K_{a} \Phi I_{a}=K_{a}^{\prime \prime} I_{f} I_{a} \) (ii)

From Eq. (i)

\( E_{a 1} =246.4=K_{a}^{\prime \prime} \times 6 \times 115.7 \)

or \( K_{a}^{\prime \prime} =0.355 \)

 

\( T=0.355 \times 6 \times 120=255.6 \mathrm{Nm} \text { (constant) } \)

Field current reduced

\( I_{f 2} =5 \mathrm{~A} \)

 

\( T(\text { constant }) =K_{a}^{\prime \prime} I_{f 1} I_{a 1}=K_{a}^{\prime \prime} I_{f 2} I_{a 2} \)

or \( I_{a 2} =120 \times \frac{6}{5}=144 \mathrm{~A} \)

 

\( I_{L 2} =144+2=146 \mathrm{~A} \)

From Eq. (i)

\( E_{a 2} =250-0.03 \times 144=245.68 \mathrm{~V} \)

 

\( 245.68 =0.355 \times 5 \times \omega_{2} \)

 

\( \omega_{2} =138.4 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s} \text { or } 1322 \mathrm{rpm} \)

(b) Rather than drawing the magnetisation curve, we shall linearly interpolate between data points.

\( I_{f 1} =6 \mathrm{~A} \Rightarrow V_{O C}=267 \mathrm{~V} \text { at } 1200 \mathrm{rpm} \text { or } 125.7 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s} \)

 

\( 267 =K_{a} \Phi_{1} \times 125.7 \)

Or \( K_{a} \Phi_{1} =2.124 \)

 

\( I_{f 2} =5 \Rightarrow V_{O C}=250 \mathrm{~V} \text { at } 1200 \mathrm{rpm} \text { or } 125.7 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s} \)

Or \( 250 =K_{a} \Phi_{2} \times 125.7 \)

 

\( K_{a} \Phi_{2} =\frac{250}{125.7}=1.989 \)

 

\( T(\text { constant }) =K_{a} \Phi_{2} I_{a 2}=K_{a} \Phi_{1} I_{a 1} \)

Or \( I_{a 2} =I_{a 1}\left(\frac{K_{a} \Phi_{1}}{K_{a} \Phi_{2}}\right) \)

 

\( =120 \times \frac{2.124}{1.989}=128.1 \mathrm{~A} \)

 

\( I_{L 2} =128.1+2=130.1 \mathrm{~A} \)

 

\( E_{a 2} =250-0.03 \times 128.1=246.16 \mathrm{~V} \)

 

\( 246.16 =K_{a} \Phi_{2} \omega_{2} \)

 

Or \( \omega_{2} =\frac{246.16}{1.989}=123.76 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s} \text { or } 1182 \mathrm{rpm} \)

 

\( \frac{1322-1182}{1182} \times 100=11.8 \% \)

 

Comparison of results Speed as calculated by linear assumption is \( \frac{1322-1182}{1182} \times 100=11.8 \% \) higher than obtained by consideration of the actual magnetisation characteristic. So the linearization is not very good for speed estimation. As armature voltage drop is very small the armature induced emf is nearly the same in both cases (245.68 V and 246.16 V). Therefore, the speed is mainly governed by the flux/pole. On linear basis the flux reduces by a factor of 5/6 = 0.833 while the actual reduction (obtained from the magnetization characteristic) is 250/267 = 0.936. This is why the actual speed is lower than that calculated on linear basis.
