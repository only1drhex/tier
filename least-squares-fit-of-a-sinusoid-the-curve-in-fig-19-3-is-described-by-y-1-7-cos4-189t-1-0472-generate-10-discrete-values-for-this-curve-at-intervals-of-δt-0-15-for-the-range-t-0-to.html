Guest Question.

Least-Squares Fit of a Sinusoid

The curve in Fig. 19.3 is described by \(y = 1.7 + \cos(4.189t + 1.0472)\). Generate 10 discrete values for this curve at intervals of \(\Delta t = 0.15\) for the range \( to \)1.35. Use this information to evaluate the coefficients of Eq. (19.11) by a least-squares fit.

\(y = A_0 + A_1 \cos(\omega_0 t) + B_1 \sin(\omega_0 t) + e\) (19.11)

Admin
Solution

The data required to evaluate the coefficients with \(\omega = 4.189\) are

t y \( y \cos(\omega_0 t)\) \( y \sin(\omega_0 t)\)
0 2.200 2.200 0.000
0.15 1.595 1.291 0.938
0.30 1.031 0.319 0.980
0.45 0.722 −0.223 0.687
0.60 0.786 −0.636 0.462
0.75 1.200 −1.200 0.000
0.90 1.805 −1.460 −1.061
1.05 2.369 −0.732 −2.253
1.20 2.678 0.829 −2.547
1.35 2.614 2.114 −1.536
\( \sum{=} \) 17.000. 2.502 −4.330.

These results can be used to determine [Eqs. (19.14) through (19.16)]

\(\begin{matrix} A_{0}=\frac{17.000}{10}= 1.7 && A_{1}=\frac{2}{10} 2.502 = 0.500 &&B_{1}=\frac{2}{10}(−4.330) = −0.866 \end{matrix} \)

 

\( A_{0}=\frac{\sum{y} }{N} \) (19.14)

\( B_{1}=\frac{2}{N} \sum{y}\sin(\omega _{0}t) \) (19.16)

Thus, the least-squares fit is

\(y = 1.7 + 0.500 cos(\omega_0 t) − 0.866 sin(\omega_0 t) \)

The model can also be expressed in the format of Eq. (19.2) by calculating [Eq. (19.8)]

\(\theta =arctan\left(-\frac{−0.866}{0.500} \right)= 1.0472 \)

 

\(f(t) = A_0 + C_1 \cos(ω_0 t + \theta ) \) (19.2)

\(\theta =arctan\left(-\frac{B_{1}}{A_{1}} \right) \) (19.8)

and [Eq. (19.9)]

\(C_{1}=\sqrt{(0.5)^2 + (−0.866)^2}= 1.00 \)

 

\(C_{1}=\sqrt{A^2_1 + B^{2}_{1}} \) (19.9)

to give

\(y = 1.7 + \cos(\omega_0 t + 1.0472)\)

or alternatively, as a sine by using [Eq. (19.10)],

\(y= 1.7 + \sin(\omega_0t + 2.618)\)

 

\( \sin(\omega_0t + \delta ) = \cos\left(\omega _{0}t+\delta -\frac{\pi}{2} \right) \)
and (19.10)
\(\cos(\omega_0t + \theta ) = \sin\left(\omega_{0}t+\theta +\frac{\pi}{2} \right) \)
