Guest Question.

The following data have been reported by Shen and Smith for benzene (Bz) adsorption on silica gel [16]:

\(\mu mole\) Bz adsorbed/g \(SiO_{2}\)\(K=e^{-\Delta G^{o}_{ad}/RT} =e^{\Delta S^{o}_{ad}/R}e^{-\Delta H^{o}_{ad} /RT} =K_{o}e^{Q_{ad}/RT}\) and plot \(\ell n \ K\)\(K=e^{-\Delta G^{o}_{ad}/RT} =e^{\Delta S^{o}_{ad}/R}e^{-\Delta H^{o}_{ad} /RT} =K_{o}e^{Q_{ad}/RT}\) and plot \(\ell n \ K\)
\(P_{Bz}\)(atm) 343 K 363 K 383 K 403 K
1\(\times 10^{-3} \) 220 112 45 20
2\(\times 10^{-3} \) 340 180 78 39
5\(\times 10^{-3} \) 680 330 170 86
1\(\times 10^{-2} \) 880 510 270 160

a) Do these data better fit a single site or a dual site Langmuir isotherm?Why?

c) What is saturation coverage at each temperature?

Solution

To test single-site adsorption, plot \(\frac{P}{n}=\frac{1}{K_{n_{m} } }+\frac{P}{n_{m}} \) (Eq 5. 27), whereas to test

dual (double)-site adsorption, plot \( \frac {P^{1/2}}{n} =\frac{1}{K^{1/2}n_{m}}+\frac{P^{1/2}}{n_{m}} \) (Eq. 5.28) . The single-site equation gives a reasonable linear fit, whereas the dual-site equation is curved with negative slopes (See Figures 1 and 2, respectively).
From the slopes and intercepts ofthe plots in Figure 1:

T(K) K(\(atm^{-1})\) \(n_{m}\left(\mu mole \ g^{-1} \right)\) or \(V_{m}\left(cm^{3}_{STP} \ g^{-1}\right)^{a}\)
343 181 1380 30.9
363 87 1180 26.6
383 59 772 17.1
403 28 719 16.1

Use Eq. 5.38 : \(K=e^{-\Delta G^{o}_{ad}/RT} =e^{\Delta S^{o}_{ad}/R}e^{-\Delta H^{o}_{ad} /RT} =K_{o}e^{Q_{ad}/RT}\) and plot \(\ell n \ K\) vs . 1/T (Figure 3)

 

slope=\(4.13\times 10^{3}= – \Delta H^{o}_{ad}/R \) , so \(\Delta H^{o}_{ad}=-8.2 \frac{kcal}{mole}\) or \(-34\frac{kJ}{mole} \)

 

Intercept=\(-6.85 =\Delta S^{o}_{ad}/R\) , so \(\Delta S^{o}_{ad}=-14\frac{cal}{mole\cdot K} \) or \(-59 \frac{J}{mole\cdot K}\)

(a)

\(V_{m}=nRT/P=\left(n \ g \ mole \right) \frac{\left(82.06 \ cm^{3}\cdot atm/g \ mole\cdot K \right) \left(273.2 \ K\right) }{1 \ atm} = n \left(2.242\times 10^{4} \ cm^{3} \right)\)
