Guest Question.

Design a Zener voltage regulator to meet the following specifications: Output voltage = 5 V, Load current = 10 mA, Zener wattage = 400 mW and Input voltage = 10 V ± 2 V.

Admin
Solution

Given \(V_{0}\) = 5 V, \(I_{L}\) = 10 mA
Here, load resistance is \(R_{L} =\frac{V_{0}}{I_{L}}=\frac{5}{10 × 10^{– 3}}\) = 500 Ω
Maximum Zener Current \(I_{z(max)} =\frac{400\,mW}{5\,V}\) = 80 mA
The minimum input voltage required will be when \(I_{z}\) = 0. Under this condition,
I = \(I_{L}\) = 10 mA
Minimum input voltage \(V_{i(min)} = V_{o} \) + IR
Hence, \(V_{i(min)}\) = 10 – 2 = 8 V
or 8 = 5 + (10 × \(10^{– 3}\)) R
Thererfore, \(R_{max} =\frac{3}{10 × 10^{– 3}}\) = 300 Ω
Now, maximum input voltage, \(V_{i(max)} = 5 + [(80 + 10)10^{– 3}]R \)
or 12 = 5 + (90 × \(10^{– 3}\))R
\(R_{min} =\frac{7}{90 × 10^{– 3}}\) = 77.77 Ω
The value of R is chosen between 77.77 Ω and 300 Ω.
