Guest Question.

Determine the channel thickness of a GaAs MESFET to achieve a specified threshold voltage.

Consider an n-channel GaAs MESFET at \(T=300 \mathrm{~K}\) with a gold Schottky barrier contact. Assume the barrier height is \(\phi_{B n}=0.89 \mathrm{~V}\). The \(\mathrm{n}\)-channel doping is \(N_{d}=2 \times 10^{15} \mathrm{~cm}^{-3}\). Design the channel thickness such that \(V_{T}=+0.25 \mathrm{~V}\).

Admin
Solution

We find that

\(\phi_{n}=V_{t} \ln \left(\frac{N_{c}}{N_{d}}\right)=(0.0259) \ln \left(\frac{4.7 \times 10^{17}}{2 \times 10^{15}}\right)=0.141 \mathrm{~V}\)

The built-in potential barrier is then

\(V_{b i}=\phi_{B n}-\phi_{n}=0.89-0.141=0.749 \mathrm{~V}\)

The threshold voltage, from Equation (13.42), is

\(V_{T}=V_{b i}-V_{p 0}\) (13.42)

or

\(V_{p 0}=V_{b i}-V_{T}=0.749-0.25=0.499 \mathrm{~V}\)

Now

\(V_{p 0}=\frac{e a^{2} N_{d}}{2 \epsilon_{s}}\)

or

\(0.499=\frac{a^{2}\left(1.6 \times 10^{-19}\right)\left(2 \times 10^{15}\right)}{2(13.1)\left(8.85 \times 10^{-14}\right)}\)

The channel thickness is then

\(a=0.601 \mu \mathrm{m}\)

Comment

For this enhancement mode \(\mathrm{n}\)-channel MESFET, the internal pinchoff voltage is less than the built-in potential barrier. A smaller channel thickness would result in a larger threshold voltage.
