Guest Question.

Locate the centroid of the composite area shown in Fig. 5-47a.

Admin
Solution

The composite area can be divided into four parts: a rectangle, a triangle, a quarter circle, and a circle, as shown in Fig. 5-47b. Recall that the area of the hole is negative since it must be subtracted from the area of the rectangle. The centroid locations for each of these parts can be determined from the relationships listed in Table 5-1:
For the triangle:

\(x_C=\frac{b}{3}=\frac{50}{3}=16.67mm \)

 

\(y_C=50+\frac{h}{3}=50+\frac{50}{3}=66.67mm \)

For the quarter circle:

\(x_C=-\frac{4r}{3\pi }=-\frac{4(50)}{3\pi }=-21.22mm \)

 

\(y_C=50+\frac{4r}{3\pi }=50+\frac{4(50)}{3\pi }=71.22mm \)

The centroid for the composite area is determined by listing the area, centroid location, and first moments for the individual parts in a table and applying \(x_CA=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{x_{Ci}A_i} \quad\quad A=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{A_i} \). Thus,

Part \(A_i(mm^2)\) \(x_{Ci}(mm)\) \(M_y(mm^3)\) \(y_{Ci}(mm)\) \(M_x(mm^3)\)

From\(x_CA=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{x_{Ci}A_i} \quad\quad A=\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{A_i} \),

 

\(x_C=\frac{M_y}{A}=\frac{-20,817}{7506}=-2.77 mm\)

 

\(y_C=\frac{M_x}{A}=\frac{330,468}{7506}=44.0 mm\)
