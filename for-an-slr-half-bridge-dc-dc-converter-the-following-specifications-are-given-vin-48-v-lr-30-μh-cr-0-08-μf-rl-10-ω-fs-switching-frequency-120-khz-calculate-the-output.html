Guest Question.

For an SLR half-bridge dc-dc converter, the following specifications are given:\(V_{in} = 48 V, L_R = 30 \mu H, C_R = 0.08 \mu F, R_L = 10 \Omega, f_s\) = switching frequency = \(120 kHz\).

Calculate the output voltage and the normalized switching frequency.

Admin
Solution
\(f_R =\frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{L_RC_R}} =\frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{(30 \times 10^{-6})(0.08 \times 10^{-6})}} = 102.7 kHz\)

 

\(R_{eq} =\frac{8}{\pi^2}R_L=\frac{8}{\pi^2} 10 = 8.11 \Omega\)

 

\(X_{LR} = \omega_s L_R = 2\pi (120000)(30 \times 10^{-6}) = 22.6 \Omega\)

 

\(X_{CR} = \frac{1}{\omega_s C_R} = \frac{1}{(2\pi \times 120000)(0.08 \times 10^{-6})} = 16.6 \Omega\)

 

\(Q =\frac{\omega_R L_R}{R_L} = \frac{(2\pi \times 102.7 \times 10^3)(30 \times 10^{-6})}{10} = 1.94\)

 

\(\overline{V}_o = \frac{V_{in}}{2\sqrt{1+(\frac{X_{LR} – X_{CR}}{R_{eq}})^2}} = \frac{48}{2\sqrt{1+(\frac{22.6 – 16.6}{8.11})^2}} = 19.3 V\)

Therefore

\(\frac{\overline{V}_o}{V_{in}} = \frac{19.3}{48} =0.402\)

 

\(\frac{f_s}{f_R} =\frac{120 kHz}{102.7 kHz} = 1.17\)

Also, from Fig. 7.50 for \(\frac{f_s}{f_R} = 1.17\) and \(Q = 1.94\) the value of \(\frac{\overline{V}_o}{V_{in}}= 0.40\) which is in close agreement with the previous result.
