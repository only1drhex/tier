Gauss-Jordan Method

Use the Gauss-Jordan technique to solve the same system as in :

\(3x_1 - 0.1x_2 - 0.2x_3 = 7.85\)

 

\(0.1x_1 + 7x_2 - 0.3x_3 = - 19.3\)

 

\(0.3x_1 - 0.2x_2 + 10x_3 = 71.4\)
Admin
Solution

First, express the coefficients and the right-hand side as an augmented matrix:

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 3 & – 0.1 & – 0.2 & 7.85 \\ 0.1 & 7 & -0.3 & – 19.3 \\ 0.3 & -0.2 & 10 & 71.4 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

Then normalize the first row by dividing it by the pivot element, 3, to yield

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & – 0.0333333 & – 0.066667 & 2.61667 \\ 0.1 & 7 & -0.3 & – 19.3 \\ 0.3 & -0.2 & 10 & 71.4 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

The \(x_1\) term can be eliminated from the second row by subtracting 0.1 times the first row from the second row. Similarly, subtracting 0.3 times the first row from the third row will eliminate the \(x_1\) term from the third row:

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & – 0.0333333 & – 0.066667 & 2.61667 \\ 0 & 7.00333 & -0.293333 & – 19.5617 \\ 0 & -0.190000 & 10.0200 & 70.6150 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Next, normalize the second row by dividing it by 7.00333:

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & – 0.0333333 & – 0.066667 & 2.61667 \\ 0 & 1 & -0.0418848 & – 2.79320 \\ 0 & -0.190000 & 10.0200 & 70.6150 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Reduction of the \(x_2\) terms from the first and third equations gives

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & – 0.0680629 & 2.52356 \\ 0 & 1 & -0.0418848 & – 2.79320 \\ 0 & 0 & 10.0200 & 70.0843 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

The third row is then normalized by dividing it by 10.0120:

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & – 0.0680629 & 2.52356 \\ 0 & 1 & -0.0418848 & – 2.79320 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 7.0000 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Finally, the \(x_3 \) terms can be reduced from the fi rst and the second equations to give

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 3.0000 \\ 0 & 1 &0 & – 2.5000 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 7.0000 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

Thus, as depicted in Fig. 9.9, the coefficient matrix has been transformed to the identity matrix, and the solution is obtained in the right-hand-side vector. Notice that no back substitution was required to obtain the solution.
