Guest Question.

A manager has the option of purchasing one, two, or three machines . Fixed costs and potential volumes are as follows:

Number of Machines Total Annual Fixed Costs Corresponding Range of Output
1 $ 9,600 0 to 300
2 15,000 301 to 600
3 20,000 601 to 900
Admin
Solution

a. Compute the break-even point for each range using the formula \(Q_{BEP} = FC/( R – v )\).

For one machine: \(Q_{BEP} = \frac{\$ 9,600}{\$ 40/unit – \$ 10 unit}=320 \) units [not in range so there is no BEP]

For two machines: \(Q_{BEP} = \frac{\$ 15,000}{\$ 40/unit – \$ 10 unit}=500\) units

For three machines: \(Q_{BEP} = \frac{\$ 20,000}{\$ 40/unit – \$ 10 unit}=666.7 \) units

b. Comparing the projected rang of demand to the two ranges for which a break-even point occurs (see Figure 5.7 B),you can see that the break-even point is 500, which is in the range 301 to 600. This means that even if demand is at the low end of the range, it would be above the break-even point and thus yield a profit. That is not true of range 601 to 900. At the top end of projected demand, the volume would still be less than the break-even point for that range, so there would be no profit. Hence, the manager should choose two machines
