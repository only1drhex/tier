Guest Question.

Consider the following circuit.

The source values are given as follows:

  • \(v_{s}=8 \cos (2000 t) \mathrm{V}\),
  • \(i_{s 1}=18 \sin (2000 t) \mathrm{mA}\),
  • \(i_{s 2}=10 \cos \left(2000 t-180^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{mA}\).

Find \(i_{x}(t)\) in steady state.

Admin
Solution

We again start with the conversions as follows:

  • \(8 \cos (2000 t) \mathrm{V} \longrightarrow 4 \sqrt{2} \underline{/0} =4 \sqrt{2} \mathrm{~V}\),
  • \(18 \sin (2000 t) \mathrm{mA} \longrightarrow 9 \sqrt{2} \underline{/-\pi/2} =-j 9 \sqrt{2} \mathrm{~mA}\),
  • \(10 \cos \left(2000 t-180^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{mA} \longrightarrow 5 \sqrt{2} \underline{/-\pi} =-5 \sqrt{2} \mathrm{~mA}\),
  • \(0.5 \mu \mathrm{F} \longrightarrow 1 /\left(j \times 2000 \times 0.5 \times 10^{-6}\right)=-j 1000 \Omega=-j \mathrm{k} \Omega\).

Note that a zero phase can directly be omitted,

\(\underline{f}=f_{\mathrm{RMS}} \underline{/0}=f_{\mathrm{RMS}},\)

while keeping in mind that \(\underline{f}\) is still a complex number with zero imaginary part. Hence, we have the following phasor-domain circuit.

In the following, the current and impedance units are milliamperes and kilohms, respectively. Applying \(\mathrm{KCL}\) at node 1 , we derive

  • \(\mathrm{KCL}(1): \frac{4 \sqrt{2}-\underline{v}_{1}}{1}-\frac{\underline{v}_{1}}{-j}-\frac{\underline{v}_{1}}{1-j}-\frac{\underline{v}_{1}-\underline{v}_{2}}{1}=0\),

leading to

\(\underline{v}_{1}(5 / 2+j 3 / 2)-\underline{v}_{2}=4 \sqrt{2} .\)

Then, applying \(\mathrm{KCL}\) at node 2 , we have

  • \(\mathrm{KCL}(2): \frac{v_{1}-v_{2}}{1}+j 9 \sqrt{2}-\frac{\underline{v}_{2}}{2}+5 \sqrt{2}=0\),

leading to

\(\underline{v}_{1}-3 \underline{v}_{2} / 2=-5 \sqrt{2}-i 9 \sqrt{2}.\)

Solving the equations, we obtain

\(\begin{aligned}&\underline{v}_{1}=4 \sqrt{2} \mathrm{~V}, \\&\underline{v}_{2}=6 \sqrt{2}(1+j) \mathrm{V},\end{aligned}\)

and

\(\underline{i}_{x}=(-2 \sqrt{2}+j 3 \sqrt{2})=\sqrt{26} \underline{/\pi-\tan ^{-1}(3 / 2)} \mathrm{mA} .\)

Finally, the time-domain \(i_{x}\) can be written as

\(i_{x}(t)=2 \sqrt{13} \cos \left(2000 t+\pi-\tan ^{-1}(3 / 2)\right) \mathrm{mA} .\)
