Guest Question.

A steam power plant is proposed to operate between the pressures of 10 kPa and 2 MPa with a maximum temperature of 400°C, as shown in Fig. 9.3. What is the maximum efficiency possible from the power cycle?

Admin
Solution

Let us include the pump work in the calculation and show that it is negligible. Also, we will assume a unit mass of working fluid since we are only interested in the efficiency. The pump work is [see (4.71)]

\( -\dot{W}_{S}=\dot{m} \frac{P_{2}-P_{1}}{\rho} \quad \text { or } \quad-w_{s}=\frac{P_{2}-P_{1}}{\rho} \) (4.71).

\( w_{P}=\frac{P_{2}-P_{1}}{\rho}=\frac{2000-10}{1000}=1.99 kJ / kg \).

Using (4.67), we find that \( h_{2}=h_{1}+w_{\text {in }}=191.8+1.99=194 kJ / kg . \text { The heat input is found using } q_{B}=h_{3}-h_{2}=3248-194= \) \( 3054 kJ / kg \text {. To locate state } 4 \text { we recognize that } s_{4}=s_{3}=7.1279 \text {. Hence, } \)

\( q-w_{s}=h_{2}-h_{1} \) (4.67).

\( s_{4}=s_{f}+x_{4} s_{f g} \quad \therefore 7.1279=0.6491+7.5019 x_{4} \).

\( \text { giving the quality of state } 4 \text { as } x_{4}=0.8636 \text {. This allows us to find } h_{4} \text { to be } \)

\( h_{4}=192+(0.8636)(2393)=2259 kJ / kg \).

The work output from the turbine is

\( w_{T}=h_{3}-h_{4}=3248-2259=989 kJ / kg \).

Consequently, the efficiency is, including pump work

\( \eta=\frac{w_{T}-w_{P}}{q_{B}}=\frac{989-2}{3054}=0.323 \quad \text { or } \quad 32.3 \% \).

If the pump work is ignored, the efficiency is

\( \eta=\frac{w_{T}}{q_{B}}=\frac{989}{3248-191.8}=0.324 \quad \text { or } \quad 32.4 \% \).

Obviously, the work required in the pumping process is negligible, being only 0.3 percent of the turbine work. In engineering applications, we often neglect quantities that have an influence of less than 3 percent, since invariably there is some quantity in the calculations that is known to only ±3 percent; for example, the mass flux, the dimensions of a pipe, or the density of the fluid.
