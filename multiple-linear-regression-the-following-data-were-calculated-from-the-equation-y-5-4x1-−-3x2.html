Multiple Linear Regression
The following data were calculated from the equation \(y = 5 +4x_1 − 3x_2\):

\(X_{1}\) \(X_{2}\) Y
0 0 5
2 1 10
2.5 2 9
1 3 0
4 6 3
7 2 27

Use multiple linear regression to fit these data.

Admin
Solution

The summations required to develop Eq. (17.22) are computed in Table 17.5. The result is

\(\begin{bmatrix} 6& 16.5 &14\\16.5& 76.25& 48\\14& 48 &54 \end{bmatrix}\begin{Bmatrix} a_{0}\\a_{1}\\a_{2} \end{Bmatrix} =\begin{Bmatrix}54\\243.5\\100 \end{Bmatrix} \)

 

\( \begin{bmatrix} n&\sum{x_{1i}}&\sum{x_{2i}}\\ \sum{x_{1i}}&\sum{x^{2}_{1i}}&\sum{x_{1i}x_{2i}}\\\sum{x_{2i}}&\sum{x_{1i}x_{2i}}&\sum{x^{2}_{2i}} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{Bmatrix} a_{0}\\a_{1}\\a_{2} \end{Bmatrix}=\begin{Bmatrix} \sum{y_{i}}\\\sum{x_{1i}}y_{i}\\\sum{x_{2i}}y_{i} \end{Bmatrix} \) (17.22)
