Guest Question.

A rectangular element used in a plane stress analysis has corners whose coordinates (in metres), referred to an Oxy axes system, are 1(-2, -1), 2(2, -1), 3(2, 1) and 4(-2, 1); the displacements (also in metres) of the corners were

\(w_{1}=0.001 \quad w_{2}=0.003 \quad w_{3}=-0.003 \quad w_{4}=0\)

\(v_{1}=-0.004 \quad v_{2}=-0.002 \quad v_{3}=0.001 \quad v_{4}=0.001\)

If Young’s modulus \(E=200000 N / mm ^{2}\) and Poisson’s ratio υ = 0.3, calculate the stresses at the centre of the element.

Admin
Solution

From the first of Eq. (17.83)

\(\left.\begin{array}{l}w(x, y)=\alpha_{1}+\alpha_{2} x+\alpha_{3} y+\alpha_{4} x y \\v(x, y)=\alpha_{5}+\alpha_{6} x+\alpha_{7} y+\alpha_{8} x y\end{array}\right\}\) (17.83)

\(w_{1}=\alpha_{1}-2 \alpha_{2}-\alpha_{3}+2 \alpha_{4}=0.001\) (i)

\(w_{2}=\alpha_{1}+2 \alpha_{2}-\alpha_{3}-2 \alpha_{4}=0.003\) (ii)

\(w_{3}=\alpha_{1}+2 \alpha_{2}+\alpha_{3}+2 \alpha_{4}=-0.003\) (iii)

\(w_{4}=\alpha_{1}-2 \alpha_{2}+\alpha_{3}-2 \alpha_{4}=0\) (iv)

Subtracting Eq. (ii) from Eq. (i)

\(\alpha_{2}-\alpha_{4}=0.0005\) (v)

Now subtracting Eq. (iv) from Eq. (iii)

\(\alpha_{2}+\alpha_{4}=-0.00075\) (vi)

Then subtracting Eq. (vi) from Eq. (v)

\(\alpha_{4}=-0.000625\) (vii)

whence, from either of Eqs (v) or (vi)

\(\alpha_{2}=-0.000125\) (viii)

Adding Eqs (i) and (ii)

\(\alpha_{1}-\alpha_{3}=0.002\) (ix)

Adding Eqs (iii) and (iv)

\(\alpha_{1}+\alpha_{3}=-0.0015\) (x)

Then adding Eqs (ix) and (x)

\(\alpha_{1}=0.00025\) (xi)

and, from either of Eqs (ix) or (x)

\(\alpha_{3}=-0.00175\) (xii)

The second of Eq. (17.83) is used to determine \(\alpha_{5}, \alpha_{6}, \alpha_{7}\) and \(\alpha_{8}\) in an identical manner to the above.
Thus

\(\alpha_{5}\) =-0.001

\(\alpha_{6}\) =0.00025

\(\alpha_{7}\) =0.002

\(\alpha_{8}\) =-0.00025

Now substituting for \(\alpha_{1}, \alpha_{2}, \ldots, \alpha_{8}\) in Eq. (17.83)

\(w_{i}=0.00025-0.000125 x-0.00175 y-0.000625 x y\)

and

\(v_{i}=-0.001+0.00025 x+0.002 y-0.00025 x y\)

Then, from Eq. (17.75)

\(\varepsilon_{x}=\frac{\partial w}{\partial x} \quad\varepsilon_{y}=\frac{\partial v}{\partial y} \quad \gamma_{xy}=\frac{\partial w}{\partial y}+\frac{\partial v}{\partial x}\) (17.75)

\(\varepsilon_{x}=\frac{\partial w}{\partial x}=-0.000125-0.000625 y\)

 

\(\varepsilon_{y}=\frac{\partial v}{\partial y}=0.002-0.00025 x\)

 

\(\gamma_{x y}=\frac{\partial w}{\partial y}+\frac{\partial v}{\partial x}=-0.0015-0.000625 x-0.00025 y\)

Therefore, at the centre of the element (x=0, y=0)

\(\varepsilon_{x}\) =-0.000125

\(\varepsilon_{y}\) =0.002

\(\gamma_{x y}\) =-0.0015

so that, from Eq. (17.79)

\(\{\sigma\}=\left\{\begin{array}{c}\sigma_{x} \\\sigma_{y} \\\gamma_{x y}\end{array}\right\}=\frac{E}{1-v^{2}}\left[\begin{array}{ccc}1 & v & 0 \\v & 1 & 0 \\0 & 0 & \frac{1}{2}(1-v)\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}\varepsilon_{x} \\\varepsilon_{y} \\\gamma_{x y}\end{array}\right\}\) (17.79)

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{E}{1-v^{2}}\left(\varepsilon_{x}+v \varepsilon_{y}\right)=\frac{200000}{1-0.3^{2}}(-0.000125+(0.3 \times 0.002))\)

i.e.

\(\sigma_{x}=104.4 N / mm ^{2}\)

 

\(\sigma_{y}=\frac{E}{1-\nu^{2}}\left(\varepsilon_{y}+\nu \varepsilon_{x}\right)=\frac{200000}{1-0.3^{2}}(0.002+(0.3 \times 0.000125))\)

i.e.

\(\sigma_{y}=431.3 N / mm ^{2}\)

and

\(\tau_{x y}=\frac{E}{1-\nu^{2}} \times \frac{1}{2}(1-\nu) \gamma_{x y}=\frac{E}{2(1+\nu)} \gamma_{x y}\)

Thus

\(\tau_{x y}=\frac{200000}{2(1+0.3)} \times(-0.0015)\)

i.e.

\(\tau_{x y}=-11.5 N / mm ^{2}\)
