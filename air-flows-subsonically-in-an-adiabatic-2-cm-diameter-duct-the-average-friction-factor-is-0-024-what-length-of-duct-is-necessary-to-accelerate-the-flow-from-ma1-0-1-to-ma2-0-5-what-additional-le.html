Guest Question.

Air flows subsonically in an adiabatic 2-cm-diameter duct. The average friction factor is 0.024. What length of duct is necessary to accelerate the flow from \(Ma_1 = 0.1 to Ma_2 = 0.5\)? What additional length will accelerate it to \(Ma_3\) = 1.0? Assume k = 1.4.

Admin
Solution

Equation (9.67) applies, with values of \(\bar{f}L^*/D\) computed from Eq. (9.66) or read from Table B.3:

\(\frac{\bar{f}L^*}{D} = \frac{1 – Ma^2}{k Ma^2} + \frac{k + 1}{2k} \ln \frac{(k + 1) Ma^2}{2 + (k – 1) Ma^2}\) (9.66)

\(\bar{f} \frac{\Delta L}{D} = \left(\frac{\bar{f}L^*}{D}\right)_1 – \left(\frac{\bar{f}L^*}{D}\right)_2\) (9.67)

\(\bar{f} \frac{\Delta L}{D} = \frac{0.024 \Delta L}{0.02 m} = \left(\frac{\bar{f}L^*}{D}\right)_{Ma=0.1} – \left(\frac{\bar{f}L^*}{D}\right)_{Ma=0.5} = 66.9216 – 1.0691 = 65.8525\)
