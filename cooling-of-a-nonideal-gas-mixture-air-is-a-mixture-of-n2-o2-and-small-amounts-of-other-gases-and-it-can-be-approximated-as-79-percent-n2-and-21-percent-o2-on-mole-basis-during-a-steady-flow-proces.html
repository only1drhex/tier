Cooling of a Nonideal Gas Mixture

Air is a mixture of \(N_2\), \(O_2\), and small amounts of other gases, and it can be approximated as 79 percent \(N_2\) and 21 percent \(O_2\) on mole basis. During a steady-flow process, air is cooled from 220 to 160 K at a constant pressure of 10 MPa (Fig. 13-17). Determine the heat transfer during this process per kmol of air, using (a) the ideal-gas approximation, (b) Kay’s rule, and (c) Amagat’s law.

Admin
Solution

Air at a low temperature and high pressure is cooled at constant pressure. The heat transfer is to be determined using three different approaches.

Assumptions 1 This is a steady-flow process since there is no change with time at any point and thus \(Δm_{cv}\) = 0 and \(ΔE_{cv}\) = 0. 2 The kinetic and potential energy changes are negligible.

Analysis We take the cooling section as the system. This is a control volume since mass crosses the system boundary during the process. We note that heat is transferred out of the system.

The critical properties are \(T_{cr}\) = 126.2 K and \(P_{cr}\) = 3.39 MPa for \(N_2\) and \(T_{cr}\) = 154.8 K and \(P_{cr}\) = 5.08 MPa for 02. Both gases remain above their critical temperatures, but they are also above their critical pressures. Therefore, air will probably deviate from ideal-gas behavior, and thus it should be treated as a real-gas mixture.
The energy balance for this steady-flow system can be expressed on a unit mole basis as

\(e_{\text {in }}-e_{\text {out }}=\Delta e_{\text {system }}^{\nearrow ^{0}}=0 \rightarrow e_{\text {in }}=e_{\text {out }} \rightarrow \bar{h}_{1}=\bar{h}_{2}+\bar{q}_{\text {out }} \)

 

\(\bar{q}_{\text {out }}=\bar{h}_{1}-\bar{h}_{2}=y_{\mathrm{N}_{2}}\left(\bar{h}_{1}-\bar{h}_{2}\right)_{\mathrm{N}_{2}}+y_{\mathrm{O}_{2}}\left(\bar{h}_{1}-\bar{h}_{2}\right)_{O_{2}}\)

 

where the enthalpy change for either component can be determined from the generalized enthalpy departure chart (Fig. A-29) and Eq. 12-58:

 

\(\bar{h}_{1}-\bar{h}_{2}=\bar{h}_{1, \text { ideal }}-\bar{h}_{2, \text { ideal }}-R_{u} T_{\mathrm{cr}}\left(Z_{h_{1}}-Z_{h_{2}}\right)\) (12-58)

 

The first two terms on the right-hand side of this equation represent the ideal-gas enthalpy change of the component. The terms in parentheses represent the deviation from the ideal-gas behavior, and their evaluation requires a knowledge of reduced pressure \(P_{R}\) and reduced temperature \(T_{R}\), which are calculated at the mixture temperature \(T_{m}\) and mixture pressure \(P_{m}\).

(a) If the \(\mathrm{N}_{2}\) and \(\mathrm{O}_{2}\) mixture is assumed to behave as an ideal gas, the enthalpy of the mixture will depend on temperature only, and the enthalpy values at the initial and the final temperatures can be determined from the ideal-gas tables of \(\mathrm{N}_{2}\) and \(\mathrm{O}_{2}\) (Tables and ):

 

\(\begin{aligned}T_{1}=220 \mathrm{~K} \rightarrow \bar{h}_{1, \text { ideal }, \mathrm{N}_{2}} &=639 \mathrm{I} \mathrm{kJ} / \mathrm{kmol} \\\quad\\\bar{h}_{1, \text { ideal }, \mathrm{O}_{2}} &=6404 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}T_{2}=160 \mathrm{~K} \rightarrow \bar{h}_{2 \text { ideal, } \mathrm{N}_{2}} &=4648 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol} \\\quad\\\bar{h}_{2 \text {,ideal. } \mathrm{O}_{2}} &=4657 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol}\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{q}_{\text {out }} &=y_{\mathrm{N}_{2}}\left(\bar{h}_{1}-\bar{h}_{2}\right)_{\mathrm{N}_{2}}+y_{\mathrm{O}_{2}}\left(\bar{h}_{1}-\bar{h}_{2}\right)_{\mathrm{O}_{2}} \\\quad\\&=(0.79)(6391-4648) \mathrm{kJ} / \mathrm{kmol}+(0.21)(6404-4657) \mathrm{kJ} / \mathrm{kmol} \\\quad\\&=1744 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol}\end{aligned}\)

 

 

(b) Kay’s rule is based on treating a gas mixture as a pseudopure substance whose critical temperature and pressure are

\(T_{\mathrm{ct}, m}^{\prime} =\sum y_{i} T_{\mathrm{ct} , i}=y_{\mathrm{N}_{2}} T_{\mathrm{cr}, \mathrm{N}_{2}}+y_{\mathrm{o}_{2}} T_{\mathrm{cr}, \mathrm{O}_{2}} \)

 

\(=(0.79)(126.2 \mathrm{~K})+(0.21)(154.8 \mathrm{~K})=132.2 \mathrm{~K}\)

 

and

\(P_{\mathrm{cr}, m}^{\prime} =\sum y_{i} P_{\mathrm{cr}, i}=y_{\mathrm{N}_2} P_{\mathrm{cr}, \mathrm{N}_{2}}+y_{\mathrm{O}_{1}} P_{\mathrm{cr}, \mathrm{O}_{2}} \)

 

\(=(0.79)(3.39 \mathrm{MPa})+(0.21)(5.08 \mathrm{MPa})=3.74 \mathrm{MPa}\)

 

Then,

 

\(\begin{gathered}T_{R, 1}=\frac{T_{m, 1}}{T_{\mathrm{cr}, m}}=\frac{220 \mathrm{~K}}{132.2 \mathrm{~K}}=1.66 \\\quad\\P_{R}=\frac{P_{m}}{P_{\mathrm{cr}, m}}=\frac{10 \mathrm{MPa}}{3.74 \mathrm{MPa}}=2.67 \\T_{R, 2}=\frac{T_{m, 2}}{T_{\mathrm{cr}, m}}=\frac{160 \mathrm{~K}}{132.2 \mathrm{~K}}=1.21\end{gathered}\)\(\begin{aligned}&\left.\begin{aligned}\quad\\\quad\\\end{aligned}\right\}Z_{h_1, m}=1.0 \\\quad\\&\left.\begin{aligned}\quad\\\quad\\\end{aligned}\right\} Z_{h_{2}, m}=2.6\end{aligned}\)

 

 

Also,

 

\(\bar{h}_{m_{1} \text {,ideal }} =y_{\mathrm{N}_1} \bar{h}_{1, \text { ideal, } \mathrm{N}_{2}}+y_{\mathrm{O}_{2}} \bar{h}_{1, \text { ideal, } \mathrm{O}_{2}} \)

 

\(=(0.79)(6391 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol})+(0.21)(6404 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol}) \)

 

\(=6394 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol} \)

 

 

\(\bar{h}_{m_2{\text {, ideal }}} =y_{\mathrm{N}_{2}} \bar{h}_{2 \text {,ideal, } \mathrm{N}_{2}}+y_{\mathrm{O}_{2}} \bar{h}_{2, \text { ideal, } \mathrm{O}_{2}} \)

 

\(=(0.79)(4648 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol})+(0.21)(4657 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol}) \)

 

\(=4650 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol}\)

 

 

Therefore

 

\(\bar{q}_{\text {out }} =\left(\bar{h}_{m_{l}, \text { ideal }}-\bar{h}_{m_{2} \text {,ideal }}\right)-R_{u} T_{c r}\left(Z_{h_{1}}-Z_{h_{2}}\right)_{m} \)

 

\(=[(6394-4650) \mathrm{kJ} / \mathrm{kmol}]-(8.314 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol} \cdot \mathrm{K})(132.2 \mathrm{~K})(1.0-2.6) \)

 

\(=3503 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol}\)

 

(c) The reduced temperatures and pressures for both \(N_2\) and \(O_2\) at the initial and final states and the corresponding enthalpy departure factors are, from Fig. A-29,

 

\(N_2\):

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{R_{1}, \mathrm{~N}_{2}}=\frac{T_{m, 1}}{T_{\mathrm{cr}, N_{2}}}=\frac{220 \mathrm{~K}}{126.2 \mathrm{~K}}=1.74 \\\quad\\&P_{R, \mathrm{~N}_{2}}=\frac{P_{m}}{P_{\mathrm{cr}, \mathrm{N}_{2}}}=\frac{10 \mathrm{MPa}}{3.39 \mathrm{MPa}}=2.95 \\\quad\\&T_{R_{2}, \mathrm{~N}_{2}}=\frac{T_{m, 2}}{T_{\mathrm{cr}, \mathrm{N}_{2}}}=\frac{160 \mathrm{~K}}{126.2 \mathrm{~K}}=1.27\end{aligned}\)\(\begin{aligned}&\left.\begin{aligned}\quad\\\quad\\\end{aligned}\right\}Z_{h_1, N_2}=0.9 \\\quad\\&\left.\begin{aligned}\quad\\\quad\\\end{aligned}\right\} Z_{h_{2}, N_2}=2.4\end{aligned}\)

 

 

\(O_2\):

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{R_{1}, \mathrm{O}_{2}}=\frac{T_{m, 1}}{T_{\mathrm{cr}, \mathrm{O}_{2}}}=\frac{220 \mathrm{~K}}{154.8 \mathrm{~K}}=1.42 \\\quad\\&P_{R, \mathrm{O}_{2}}=\frac{P_{m}}{P_{\mathrm{cr}, \mathrm{O}_{2}}}=\frac{10 \mathrm{MPa}}{5.08 \mathrm{MPa}}=1.97 \\\quad\\&T_{R_{1}, \mathrm{O}_{2}}=\frac{T_{m, 2}}{T_{\mathrm{cr}, \mathrm{O}_{2}}}=\frac{160 \mathrm{~K}}{154.8 \mathrm{~K}}=1.03\end{aligned}\)\(\begin{aligned}&\left.\begin{aligned}\quad\\\quad\\\end{aligned}\right\}Z_{h_1, O_2}=1.3 \\\quad\\&\left.\begin{aligned}\quad\\\quad\\\end{aligned}\right\} Z_{h_{2}, O_2}=4.0\end{aligned}\)

 

 

 

From Eq. 12-58,

 

\(\left(\bar{h}_{1}-\bar{h}_{2}\right)_{\mathrm{N}_{2}} =\left(\bar{h}_{1, \text { ideal }}-\bar{h}_{2, \text { ideal }}\right)_{\mathrm{N}_{2}}-R_{u} T_{\mathrm{cr}}\left(Z_{h_{1}}-Z_{h_{2}}\right)_{\mathrm{N}_{2}} \)

 

\(=[(6391-4648) \mathrm{kJ} / \mathrm{kmol}]-(8.314 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol} \cdot \mathrm{K})(126.2 \mathrm{~K})(0.9-2.4) \)

 

\(=3317 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol} \)

 

 

\(\left(\bar{h}_{1}-\bar{h}_{2}\right)_{\mathrm{O}_{2}} =\left(\bar{h}_{1, \text { ideal }}-\bar{h}_{2, \text { ideal }}\right)_{\mathrm{O}_{2}}-R_{u} T_{\mathrm{cr}}\left(Z_{h_{1}}-Z_{h_{2}}\right)_{\mathrm{O}_{2}} \)

 

\(=[(6404-4657) \mathrm{kJ} / \mathrm{kmol}]-(8.314 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol} \cdot \mathrm{K})(154.8 \mathrm{~K})(1.3-4.0) \)

 

\(=5222 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol} \)

 

 

Therefore,

 

\(\bar{q}_{\text {out }}=y_{\mathrm{N}_{2}}\left(\bar{h}_{1}-\bar{h}_{2}\right)_{\mathrm{N}_{2}}+y_{\mathrm{O}_{2}}\left(\bar{h}_{1}-\bar{h}_{2}\right)_{\mathrm{O}_{2}} \)

 

\(=(0.79)(3317 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol})+(0.21)(5222 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol}) \)

 

\(=3717 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kmol}\)

 

 

Discussion This result is about 6 percent greater than the result obtained in part (b) by using Kay’s rule. But it is more than twice the result obtained by assuming the mixture to be an ideal gas.
