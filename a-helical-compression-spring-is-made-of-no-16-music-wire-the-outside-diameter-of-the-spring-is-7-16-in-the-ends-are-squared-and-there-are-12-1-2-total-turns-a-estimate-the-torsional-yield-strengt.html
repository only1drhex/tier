Guest Question.

A helical compression spring is made of no. 16 music wire. The outside diameter of the spring is \(\frac{7}{16}\) in. The ends are squared and there are \(12 \frac{1}{2}\) total turns.
(a) Estimate the torsional yield strength of the wire.
(b) Estimate the static load corresponding to the yield strength.
(c) Estimate the scale of the spring.
(d) Estimate the deflection that would be caused by the load in part (b).
(e) Estimate the solid length of the spring.
( f ) What length should the spring be to ensure that when it is compressed solid and then released, there will be no permanent change in the free length?
(g) Given the length found in part ( f ), is buckling a possibility?
(h) What is the pitch of the body coil?

Admin
Solution

(a) From Table A–28, the wire diameter is d = 0.037 in. From Table 10–4, we find A = 201 kpsi \(· in^{m}\) and m = 0.145. Therefore, from Eq. (10–14)

\(S_{ut} =\frac{A}{d_{m}}\) (10–14)

\(S_{ut} =\frac {A}{d_{m}} =\frac {201}{0.037^{0.145}} = 324 kpsi\)

(b) The mean spring coil diameter is \(D = \frac {7}{16} − 0.037 = 0.400\) in, and so the spring index is C = 0.400/0.037 = 10.8. Then, from Eq. (10–6),

\(K_{B} =\frac {4C + 2}{4C − 3}\) (10–6)

\(K_{B} =\frac {4C + 2}{4C − 3}=\frac{4 (10.8) + 2}{4 (10.8) − 3}= 1.124\)

Now rearrange Eq. (10–3) replacing \(K_{s} and τ\) with \(K_{B} and S_{ys}\) , espectively, and solve for F:

\(τ =K_{s} \frac {8FD}{πd^{3}}\) (10–3)

\(F =\frac {πd^{3}S_{sy}}{8K_{B}D} =\frac {π(0.037^{3})146(10^{3})}{8(1.124) 0.400} = 6.46 lbf\)

(c) From Table 10–1, \(N_{a} = 12.5 − 2 = 10.5\) turns. In Table 10–5, G =11.85 Mpsi, and the scale of the spring is found to be, from Eq. (10–9),

\(k\dot{=} \frac {d^{4}G}{8D^{3}N}\) (10–9)

\(k =\frac {d^{4}G}{8D^{3}Na} =\frac {0.037^{4} (11.85)10^{6}}{8(0.400^{3})10.5} = 4.13 lbf/in\)

\(y =\frac {F}{k} =\frac {6.46}{4.13} = 1.56 in\)

(e) From Table 10–1,

\(L_{s} = (N_{t} + 1)d = (12.5 + 1)0.037 = 0.500 in\)

( f )

\(L_{0} = y + L_{s} = 1.56 + 0.500 = 2.06 in.\)

(g) To avoid buckling, Eq. (10–13) and Table 10–2 give

\(L_{0} < 2.63 \frac{D}{α}\) (10–13)

\(L_{0} < 2.63 \frac{D}{α}=2.63\frac {0.400}{0.5} = 2.10 in\)

atically, a free length of 2.06 in is less than 2.10 in, and buckling is unlikely. However, the forming of the ends will control how close α is to 0.5. This has to be investigated and an inside rod or exterior tube or hole may be needed.

(h) Finally, from Table 10–1, the pitch of the body coil is

\(p =\frac {L_{0} − 3d}{N_{a}} =\frac {2.06 − 3(0.037)}{10.5} = 0.186 in\)
