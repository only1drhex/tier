Guest Question.

Define the term ‘shear centre’ of a thin-walled open section and determine the position of the shear centre of the thin-walled open section shown in Fig. P.10.14.

Admin
Solution

The shear centre is the point in a beam cross section through which shear loads must be applied for there to be no twisting of the section.
The z axis is an axis of symmetry so that the shear centre lies on this axis. Its position is found by applying a shear load \( S_{y} \), through the shear centre, determining the shear flow distribution and then taking moments about some convenient point. Eq. (10.22) reduces to

\( q_{s}=\frac{\left(S_{y} I_{z y}-S_{z} I_{z}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t z d s+\frac{\left(S_{z} I_{z y}-S_{y} I_{y}\right)}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s \) (10.22)

 

\( q_{s}=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \int_{0}^{s} t y d s \) (i)

in which, referring to Fig. S.10.14

\( I_{z}=2\left(\frac{t r^{3}}{3}+2 r t r^{2}+\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}} \operatorname{tr}^{2} \cos ^{2} \theta r d \theta\right) \)

i.e.

\( I_{z}=6.22 t r^{3} \)

In the wall 12, \( y=s_{1} \). Therefore substituting in Eq. (i)

\( q_{12}=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \int_{0}^{s_{1}} t s_{1} d s=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \frac{t s_{1}^{2}}{2} \)

Then

\( q_{2}=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \frac{t r^{2}}{2} \)

In the wall 23, y=r, then

\( q_{23}=-\left(\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}}\right)\left(\int_{0}^{s_{2}} \operatorname{tr} d s+\frac{\operatorname{tr}^{2}}{2}\right) \)

i.e.

\( q_{23}=-\left(\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}}\right)\left(\operatorname{trs}_{2}+\frac{\operatorname{tr}^{2}}{2}\right) \)

and

\( q_{3}=-5 \frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \frac{\operatorname{tr}^{2}}{2}\)

In the wall 34, y=r cos θ, then

\(q_{34}=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}}\left(\int_{0}^{\theta} \operatorname{tr}^{2} \cos \theta d \theta+\frac{5 \operatorname{tr}^{2}}{2}\right) \)

i.e.

\( q_{34}=-\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} \operatorname{tr}^{2}\left(\sin \theta+\frac{5}{2}\right) \)

Taking moments about O

\( S_{y} z_{S}=-2\left[\int_{0}^{r} q_{12} 2 r d s+\int_{0}^{2 r} q_{23} r d s+\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}} q_{34} r^{2} d \theta\right] \)

The negative sign arises from the fact that the moment of the applied shear load is in the opposite sense to the moments produced by the internal shear flows. Substituting for \( q_{12}, q_{23} \text { and } 934 \) from the above

\( S_{y} z_{S}=\frac{S_{y}}{I_{z}} t\left[\int_{0}^{r}\left(\frac{s_{1}^{2}}{2}\right) 2 r d s+\int_{0}^{2 r}\left(r_{2}+\frac{r^{2}}{2}\right) r d s+\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}} r^{4}\left(\sin \theta+\frac{5}{2}\right) d \theta\right] \)

which gives

\( z_{S}=2.66 r \)
