Guest Question.

The electric field and the magnetic field in free space are given by

\(E=\frac{50}{\rho } \cos (10^{6}t+\beta z)a_{\phi }\) V/m
\(H=\frac{H_{0}}{\rho } \cos (10^{6}t+\beta z)a_{\rho }\) A/m

Express these in phasor form and determine the constants \(H_{0}\) and β such that the fields satisfy Maxwell’s equations.

Admin
Solution

The instantaneous forms of E and H are written as

\(E=Re(E_{s}e^{j\omega t}), H=Re(H_{s}e^{j\omega t}) \)(9.7.1)

where \(ω= 10^{6}\) and phasors \(E_{s}\) and \(H_{s} \)are given by
\(E_{s}=\frac{50}{\rho }e^{j\beta z}a_{\phi } , \)\(H_{s}=\frac{H_{0}}{\rho }e^{j\beta z} a_{\rho } \)(9.7.2)

For free space, \(ρ_{v}\) = 0, σ= 0, \(ε=ε_{0} \), and \(μ= μ_{0}\) so Maxwell’s equations become
\(\triangledown .D=\varepsilon _{0}\triangledown .E=0\rightarrow \triangledown .E_{s}=0\) (9.7.3)
\(\triangledown .B=\mu _{0}\triangledown .H=0\rightarrow \triangledown .H_{s}=0\) (9.7.4)
\(\triangledown \times H=\sigma E+\varepsilon _{0}\frac{\partial E}{\partial t}\rightarrow \triangledown \times H_{s}=j\omega \varepsilon _{0}E_{s} \)(9.7.5)
\(\triangledown \times E=-\mu _{0}\frac{\partial H}{\partial t}\rightarrow \triangledown \times E_{s}=-j\omega \mu _{0}H_{s}\) (9.7.6)

Substituting eq (9.7.2) into eqs. (9.7.3) and (9.7.4), it is readily verified that two Maxwell’s equations are satisfied; that is,
\(\triangledown .E_{s}=\frac{1}{\rho }\frac{\partial}{\partial \phi } (E_{\phi s})=0\)
\(\triangledown .H_{s}=\frac{1}{\rho }\frac{\partial}{\partial \rho } (\rho H_{\phi s})=0\)
Now
\(\triangledown \times H_{s}=\triangledown \times (\frac{H_{0}}{\rho }e^{j\beta z}a_{\rho } )=\frac{jH_{0}\beta }{\rho } e^{j\beta z}a_{\phi }\) (9.7.7)

Substituting eqs. (9.7.2) and (9.7.7) into eq. (9.7.5), we have
\(\frac{jH_{0}\beta }{\rho } e^{j\beta z}a_{\phi } \)\(=j\omega \varepsilon _{0}\frac{50}{\rho }e^{j\beta z}a_{\phi }\)
or
\(H_{0}\beta =50\omega \varepsilon _{0} \)(9.7.8)

Similarly, substituting eq. (9.7.2) into eq. (9.7.6) gives
\(-j\beta \frac{50}{\rho }e^{j\beta z}a_{\rho }=-j\omega \mu _{0}\frac{H_{0}}{\rho }e^{j\beta z}a_{\rho }\)
or
\(\frac{H_{0}}{\beta } =\frac{50}{\omega \mu _{0}} \)(9.7.9)

Multiplying eq. (9.7.8) by eq. (9.7.9) yields
\(H_{0}^{2}=(50)^{2}\frac{\varepsilon _{0}}{\mu _{0}}\)
or
\(H_{0}=\pm 50\sqrt{\varepsilon _{0}/\mu _{0}}=\pm \frac{50}{120\pi } =\pm 0.1326\)

Dividing eq. (9.7.8) by eq. (9.7.9), we get
\(\beta ^{2}=\omega ^{2}\mu _{0}\varepsilon _{0}\)
or
\(\beta =\pm \omega \sqrt{\mu _{0}\varepsilon _{0}}=\pm \frac{\omega }{c}=\pm \frac{10^{6}}{3\times 10^{8}}\)
\(=\pm 3.33\times 10^{-3}\)

Inviewofeq.(9.7.8),\(H_{0}\)=0.1326,β=\( 3.33×10^{- 3}\) or \(H_{0}\) =-0.1326,β=\(-3.33×10^{-3}\) only these will satisfy Maxwell’s four equations.
