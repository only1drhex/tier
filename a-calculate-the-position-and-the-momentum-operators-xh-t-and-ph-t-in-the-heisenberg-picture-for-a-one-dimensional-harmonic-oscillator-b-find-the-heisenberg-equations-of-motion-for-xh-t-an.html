Guest Question.

(a) Calculate the position and the momentum operators, \(\hat{X}_{H}(t)\) and \(\hat{P}_{H}(t)\), in the Heisenberg picture for a one-dimensional harmonic oscillator.

(b) Find the Heisenberg equations of motion for \(\hat{X}_{H}(t)\) and \(\hat{P}_{H}(t)\).

Admin
Solution

In the Schrödinger picture, where the operators do not depend explicitly on time, the Hamiltonian of a one-dimensional harmonic oscillator is given by

\(\hat{H}=\frac{\hat{P}^{2}}{2 m}+\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2} \hat{X}^{2}\). (10.152)

(a) Using the commutation relations

\([\hat{H}, \hat{X}]=\frac{1}{2 m}\left[\hat{P}^{2}, \hat{X}\right]=-\frac{i \hbar}{m} \hat{P}\), (10.153)

 

\([\hat{H}, \hat{P}]=\frac{1}{2} m \omega^{2}\left[\hat{X}^{2}, \hat{P}\right]=i \hbar m \omega^{2} \hat{X}\), (10.154)

along with

\(e^{\hat{A}} \hat{B} e^{-\hat{A}}=\hat{B}+[\hat{A}, \hat{B}]+\frac{1}{2 !}[\hat{A},[\hat{A}, \hat{B}]]+\frac{1}{3 !}[\hat{A},[\hat{A},[\hat{A}, \hat{B}]]]+\cdots\), (10.155)

we may write (see Eq. (10.11))

\(\hat{A}_{H}(t)=\hat{U}^{\dagger}(t) \hat{A} \hat{U}(t)=e^{i t \hat{H} / \hbar} \hat{A} e^{-i t \hat{H} / \hbar}\). (10.11)

 

\(\hat{X}_{H}(t)=e^{i t \hat{H} / \hbar} \hat{X} e^{-i t \hat{H} / \hbar}=\hat{X}+\frac{i t}{\hbar}[\hat{H}, \hat{X}]+\frac{1}{2 !}\left(\frac{i t}{\hbar}\right)^{2}[\hat{H},[\hat{H}, \hat{X}]]+\cdots\)

 

\(=\hat{X}+\frac{t}{m} \hat{P}-\frac{(\omega t)^{2}}{2 !} \hat{X}-\frac{(\omega t)^{3}}{3 !} \frac{1}{m \omega} \hat{P}+\frac{(\omega t)^{4}}{4 !} \hat{X}+\frac{(\omega t)^{5}}{5 !} \frac{1}{m \omega} \hat{P}+\cdots\)

 

\(=\hat{X}\left[1-\frac{(\omega t)^{2}}{2 !}+\frac{(\omega t)^{4}}{4 !}+\cdots\right]+\frac{1}{m \omega} \hat{P}\left[(\omega t)-\frac{(\omega t)^{3}}{3 !}+\frac{(\omega t)^{5}}{5 !}+\cdots\right]\), (10.156)

or

\(\hat{X}_{H}(t)=\hat{X} \cos (\omega t)+\frac{1}{m \omega} \hat{P} \sin (\omega t)\). (10.157)

A similar calculation yields (see Eq. (10.11))

\(\hat{P}_{H}(t)=e^{i t \hat{H} / \hbar} \hat{P} e^{-i t \hat{H} / \hbar}=\hat{P}+\frac{i t}{\hbar}[\hat{H}, \hat{P}]+\frac{1}{2 !}\left(\frac{i t}{\hbar}\right)^{2}[\hat{H},[\hat{H}, \hat{P}]]+\cdots\)

 

\(=\hat{P}\left[1-\frac{(\omega t)^{2}}{2 !}+\frac{(\omega t)^{4}}{4 !}+\cdots\right]-m \omega \hat{X}\left[(\omega t)-\frac{(\omega t)^{3}}{3 !}+\frac{(\omega t)^{5}}{5 !}+\cdots\right]\), (10.158)

or

\(\hat{P}_{H}(t)=\hat{P} \cos (\omega t)-m \omega \hat{X} \sin (\omega t)\). (10.159)

(b) To find the equations of motion of \(\hat{X}_{H}(t)\) and \(\hat{P}_{H}(t)\), we need to use the Heisenberg equation \(d \hat{A}_{H}(t) / d t=(1 / i \hbar)\left[\hat{A}_{H}(t), \hat{H}\right]\) which, along with (10.153) and (10.154), leads to

\(\frac{d \hat{X}_{H}(t)}{d t}=\frac{1}{i \hbar}\left[\hat{X}_{H}(t), \hat{H}\right]=\frac{1}{i \hbar} e^{i t \hat{H} / \hbar}[\hat{X}, \hat{H}] e^{-i t \hat{H} / \hbar}=\frac{1}{i \hbar} \frac{i \hbar}{m} e^{i t \hat{H} / \hbar} \hat{P} e^{-i t \hat{H} / \hbar}\), (10.160)

 

\(\frac{d \hat{P}_{H}(t)}{d t}=\frac{1}{i \hbar}\left[\hat{P}_{H}(t), \hat{H}\right]=\frac{1}{i \hbar} e^{i t \hat{H} / \hbar}[\hat{P}, \hat{H}] e^{-i t \hat{H} / \hbar}=\frac{\left(-i \hbar m \omega^{2}\right)}{i \hbar} e^{i t \hat{H} / \hbar} \hat{X} e^{-i t \hat{H} / \hbar}\), (10.161)

or

\(\frac{d \hat{X}_{H}(t)}{d t}=\frac{1}{m} \hat{P}_{H}(t), \quad \frac{d \hat{P}_{H}(t)}{d t}=-m \omega^{2} \hat{X}_{H}(t)\). (10.162)
