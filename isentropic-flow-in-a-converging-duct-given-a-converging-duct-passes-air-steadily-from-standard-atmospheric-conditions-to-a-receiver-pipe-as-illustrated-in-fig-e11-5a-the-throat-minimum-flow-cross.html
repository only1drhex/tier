Isentropic Flow in a Converging Duct

GIVEN A converging duct passes air steadily from standard atmospheric conditions to a receiver pipe as illustrated in Fig. E11.5a. The throat (minimum) flow cross-sectional area of the converging duct is \(1 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2}\). The receiver pressure is (a) 80 kPa (abs), (b) 40 kPa (abs).

FIND Determine the mass flowrate through the duct and sketch temperature–entropy diagrams for situations (a) and (b).

Admin
Solution

To determine the mass flowrate through the converging duct we use Eq. 11.40. Thus,

 

\(\dot{m}=\rho A V= constant\) (11.40)

 

or in terms of the given throat area, \(A_{\text {th }}\),

 

\(\dot{m}=\rho_{ th } A_{ th } V_{ th }\) (1)

 

We assume that the flow through the converging duct is isentropic and that the air behaves as an ideal gas with constant \(c_{p}\) and \(c_{v}\) Then, from Eq. 11.60

 

\(\frac{\rho}{\rho_{0}}=\left\{\frac{1}{1+[(k-1) / 2] Ma ^{2}}\right\}^{1 /(k-1)}\) (11.60)

 

\(\frac{\rho_{ th }}{\rho_{0}}=\left\{\frac{1}{1+[(k-1) / 2] Ma _{ th }^{2}}\right\}^{1 /(k-1)}\) (2)

 

The stagnation density, \(\rho_{0}\), for the standard atmosphere is \(1.23 kg / m ^{3}\) and the specific heat ratio is 1.4. To determine the throat Mach number, \(Ma _{ th }\), we can use Eq. 11.59,

 

\(\frac{p}{p_{0}}=\left\{\frac{1}{1+[(k-1) / 2] Ma ^{2}}\right\}^{k /(k-1)}\) (11.59)

 

\(\frac{p_{\text {th }}}{p_{0}}=\left\{\frac{1}{1+[(k-1) / 2] Ma _{\text {th }}^{2}}\right\}^{k /(k-1)}\) (3)

 

The critical pressure, \(p^{*}\), is obtained from Eq. 11.62 as

 

\(\left(\frac{p^{*}}{p_{0}}\right)_{k=1.4}=0.528\) (11.62)

 

\(\begin{aligned}p^{*} &=0.528 p_{0}=0.528 p_{ atm } \\&=(0.528)[101 kPa ( abs )]=53.3 kPa ( abs )\end{aligned}\)

 

If the receiver pressure, \(p_{\text {re }}\), is greater than or equal to \(p^{*}\), then \(p_{ th }=p_{ re } \text {. If } p_{ re }<p^{*}, \text { then } p_{ th }=p^{*}\) and the flow is choked. With \(p_{ th }, p_{0}, \text { and } k \text { known, } Ma _{ th }\) can be obtained from Eq. 3, and \(\rho_{ th }\) can be determined from Eq. 2.

The flow velocity at the throat can be obtained from Eqs. 11.36 and 11.46 as

 

\(c=\sqrt{R T k}\) (11.36)

 

\(Ma =\frac{V}{c}\) (11.46)

 

\(V_{ th }= Ma _{ th } c_{ th }= Ma _{ th } \sqrt{R T_{ th } k}\) (4)

 

The value of temperature at the throat, \(T_{\text {th }}\), can be calculated from Eq. 11.56,

 

\(\frac{T}{T_{0}}=\frac{1}{1+[(k-1) / 2] Ma ^{2}}\) (11.56)

 

\(\frac{T_{ th }}{T_{0}}=\frac{1}{1+[(k-1) / 2] Ma _{ th }^{2}}\) (5)

 

Since the flow through the converging duct is assumed to be isentropic, the stagnation temperature is considered constant at the standard atmosphere value of \(T_{0}=15 K +273 K =288 K\). Note that absolute pressures and temperatures are used.

 

(a) For \(p_{ re }=80 kPa ( abs )>53.3 kPa ( abs )=p^{*}\), we have \(p_{ th }=80 kPa ( abs )\). Then from Eq. 3

 

\(\frac{80 kPa ( abs )}{101 kPa ( abs )}=\left\{\frac{1}{1+[(1.4-1) / 2] Ma _{\text {th }}^{2}}\right\}^{1.4 /(1.4-1)}\)

 

or

 

\(Ma _{ th }=0.587\)

 

From Eq. 2

 

\(\frac{\rho_{ th }}{1.23 kg / m ^{3}}=\left\{\frac{1}{1+[(1.4-1) / 2](0.587)^{2}}\right\}^{1 /(1.4-1)}\)

 

or

 

\(\rho_{ th }=1.04 kg / m ^{3}\)

 

From Eq. 5

 

\(\frac{T_{\text {th }}}{288 K }=\frac{1}{1+[(1.4-1) / 2](0.587)^{2}}\)

 

or

 

\(T_{\text {th }}=269 K\)

 

Substituting \(Ma _{\text {th }}=0.587 \text { and } T_{\text {th }}=269 K\) into Eq. 4 we obtain

 

\(\begin{aligned}V_{ th } &=0.587 \sqrt{[286.9 J /( kg \cdot K )](269 K )(1.4)} \\&=193( J / kg )^{1 / 2}\end{aligned}\)

 

Thus, since \(1 J / kg =1 N \cdot m / kg =1\left( kg \cdot m / s ^{2}\right) \cdot m / kg =( m / s )^{2}\), we obtain

 

\(V_{ th }=193 m / s\)

 

Finally from Eq. 1 we have

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{m} &=\left(1.04 kg / m ^{3}\right)\left(1 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2}\right)(193 m / s ) \\&=0.0201 kg / s\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) For \(p_{ re }=40 kPa ( abs )<53.3 kPa ( abs )=p^{*}\), we have \(p_{\text {th }}=p^{*}=53.3 kPa ( abs ) \text { and } Ma _{\text {th }}=1\). The converging duct is choked. From Eq. 2 (see also Eq. 11.66)

 

\(\left(\frac{\rho^{*}}{\rho_{0}}\right)_{k=1.4}=0.634\) (11.66)

 

\(\frac{\rho_{\text {th }}}{1.23 kg / m ^{3}}=\left\{\frac{1}{1+[(1.4-1) / 2](1)^{2}}\right\}^{1 /(1.4-1)}\)

 

or

 

\(\rho_{ th }=0.780 kg / m ^{3}\)

 

From Eq. 5 (see also Eq. 11.64),

 

\(\left(\frac{T^{*}}{T_{0}}\right)_{k=1.4}=0.833\) (11.64)

 

\(\frac{T_{\text {th }}}{288 K }=\frac{1}{1+[(1.4-1) / 2](1)^{2}}\)

 

or

 

\(T_{ th }=240 K\)

 

From Eq. 4,

 

\(\begin{aligned}V_{ th } &=(1) \sqrt{[286.9 J /( kg \cdot K )](240 K )(1.4)} \\&=310( J / kg )^{1 / 2}=310 m / s\end{aligned}\)

 

since \(1 J / kg =1 N \cdot m / kg =1\left( kg \cdot m / s ^{2}\right) \cdot m / kg =( m / s )^{2}\). Finally from Eq. 1

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{m} &=\left(0.780 kg / m ^{3}\right)\left(1 \times 10^{-4} m ^{2}\right)(310 m / s ) \\&=0.0242 kg / s\end{aligned}\)

 

From the values of throat temperature and throat pressure calculated above for flow situations (a) and (b), we can construct the temperature–entropy diagram shown in Fig. E11.5b.

COMMENT Note that the flow from standard atmosphere to the receiver for receiver pressure, \(p_{ re }\), greater than or equal to the critical pressure, \(p^{*}\), is isentropic. When the receiver pressure is less than the critical pressure as in situation (b) above, what is the flow like downstream from the exit of the converging duct? Experience suggests that this flow, when \(p_{ re }<p^{*}\), is threedimensional and nonisentropic and involves a drop in pressure from \(p_{ th } \text { to } p_{ re }\), a drop in temperature, and an increase of entropy as are indicated in Fig. E11.5c.
