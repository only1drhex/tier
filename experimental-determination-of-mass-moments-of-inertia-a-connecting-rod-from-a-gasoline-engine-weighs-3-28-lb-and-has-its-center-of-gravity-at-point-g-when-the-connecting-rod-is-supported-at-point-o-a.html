Guest Question.

Experimental Determination of Mass Moments of Inertia

A connecting rod from a gasoline engine weighs 3.28 lb and has its center of gravity at point G. When the connecting rod is supported at point O and is allowed to oscillate as a pendulum, 0:816 s is required for one full cycle of motion. Determine the mass moments of inertia about points A and G.

Admin
Solution

Road Map We will use Eq. (\(I_{Oz} =\frac{T^2mgL}{4\pi^2}\)), described in the margin note on this page, to determine \(I_{Oz}\). We will then use the parallel axis theorem to determine the moments of inertia about points A and G. Note that, as discussed in the margin note on p. 557, to determine \(I_{Az}\) it is necessary to first know \(I_{Gz}\).

\(I_{Gz}\)

Governing Equations & Computation Using Eq. (\(I_{Oz} =\frac{T^2mgL}{4\pi^2}\)), the mass moment of inertia of the connecting rod about the z axis through point O is

\(I_{Oz} =\frac{(0.816 s)^2(3.28 lb)(0.45 ft)}{4\pi^2} = 0.02489 ft\cdot lb\cdot s^2\). (2)

To express Eq. (2) in terms of the U.S. Customary mass unit (recall from the definition in Table 1.1 on p. 9, \(1 slug \equiv 1 lb\cdot s^2/ft\)), we carry out the unit conversion

Table 1.1. U.S. Customary and SI unit systems.
Base Dimension System of Units
U.S. Customary SI
force pound (lb) newton\(^a(N) \equiv kg\cdot m/s^2\)
mass slug\(^a \equiv lb\cdot s^2/ft\) kilogram (kg)
length foot (ft) meter (m)
time second (s) second (s)
\(^a\) Derived unit.

\(I_{Oz} = (0.02489 ft\cdot lb\cdot s^2)(\frac{slug}{lb\cdot s^2/ft}) = 0.02489 slug\cdot ft^2\). (3)

The parallel axis theorem, Eq. (\(I_z = I_{z^′} + d^2_z m\)) on p. 556, rearranged for the moment of inertia about the \(z\) axis through the mass center G, is

\(I_{Gz} = I_{Oz} – d^2_z m\)

 

\(= 0.02489 slug\cdot ft^2 – (0.45 ft)^2 (\frac{3.28 lb}{32.2 ft/s^2})\)

 

\(= 0.02489 slug\cdot ft^2 – 0.02063 ft\cdot lb\cdot s^2(\frac{slug}{lb\cdot s^2/ft})\)

 

\(= 4.267 \times 10^{-3} slug\cdot ft^2\). (4)

\(I_{Az}\)

Governing Equations & Computation With the moment of inertia about the mass center known from Eq. (4), the parallel axis theorem can be used again to determine the moment of inertia about the \(z\) axis through point A. Thus,

\(I_{Az} = I_{Gz} + d^2_z m\)

 

\(=4.267 \times 10^{-3} slug\cdot ft^2 + (0.22 ft)^2 (\frac{3.28 lb}{32.2 ft/s^2})\)

 

\(= 4.267 \times 10^{-3} slug\cdot ft^2 + 4.930 \times 10^{-3} ft\cdot lb\cdot s^2(\frac{slug}{lb\cdot s^2/ft})\)

 

\(= 9.20\times 10^{-3} slug\cdot ft^2\). (5)

Discussion & Verification A rough check of accuracy can be obtained by approximating the connecting rod by a slender rod with the same mass and with length approximating that of the connecting rod. Problem 10.76 asks you to carry out this calculation.
