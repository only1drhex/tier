Guest Question.

Estimate the low-to-high propagation delay for the unloaded RTL inverter shown in Figure 5.29.

Admin
Solution

The saturation time constant is

\(\tau _s=\frac{\alpha _{F}(\tau _{F}+\alpha _{R}\tau _{R})}{1-\alpha _{F}\alpha _{R}}=\frac{0.986(0.2 ns+(0.333)10 ns)}{1-(0.986)(0.333)}=5.2 ns\)

The saturated base current is

\(I_{BF}=\frac{V_{CC}-V_{BES}}{R_{B}}=\frac{5V-0.8V}{10 k\Omega } =0.42 mA\).

During the saturation delay, the base current is

\(I_{BR}=\frac{0-V_{BES}}{R_{B}}=\frac{0-0.8V}{10 k\Omega } =-0.08 mA\).

The collector current at the edge of saturation is

\(I_{CEOS}=\frac{V_{CC}-V_{CES}}{R_{C}}=\frac{5 V-0.1 V}{2 k\Omega } =2.45 mA\)

The saturation delay is therefore

\(t_{s}=\tau _{s}\ln\left\lgroup \frac{I_{BF}-I_{BR}}{I_{CEOS}/\beta _{F}-I_{BR}} \right\rgroup =5.2 ns \ln\left\lgroup\frac{0.42 mA -(-0.08 mA)}{2.45 mA/70-(-0.08 mA)} \right\rgroup =7.6 \) ns.

For calculation of the rise time, the base-collector voltage starts at +0.7 V and ends up at â€“4.3 V. Therefore the average value of the base-collector capacitance for the rise time calculation is

\(C_{BC}=\frac{C_{jCO}\phi _{c} }{(V_{BC1}-C_{BC2})(1-m_{c})}\left[\left\lgroup 1-\frac{V_{BC2}}{\phi _{C}}\right\rgroup^{1-m_{C}} -\left\lgroup 1-\frac{V_{BC1}}{\phi _{C}}\right\rgroup^{1-m_{C}} \right]=\frac{(0.15 pF)(0.75 V)}{(0.7 V-(-4.3 V))(0.5)} \left[\left\lgroup 1-\frac{-4.3 V}{0.75 V}\right\rgroup^{0.5} -\left\lgroup 1-\frac{0.7 V}{0.75 V}\right\rgroup ^{0.5} \right]=0.1052 \) pF

The base current during the rise time is

\(I_B(ave)=\frac{0-V_{BEA}}{R_B}=\frac{0-0.7V}{10 k\Omega } =-0.07 mA\)

The rise time is

\(t_{R}=\frac{I_{CEOS} \tau _{F}+\left|\Delta V_{BC}C_{BC}\right| }{\left|I_{B}(ave)\right| }=\frac{(2.45 mA)0.2 ns+\left|(-4.3 V -0.7 V)(0.1052 pF)\right| }{\left|-0.07 mA\right| } =14.51 ns\)

and the low-to-high propagation delay is

\(t_{PLH}=t_{s}+\frac{t_{R}}{3} =7.6 ns+\frac{14.51 ns}{2} =15.3\) ns

Two important points should be noted here. First, the low-to-high propagation delay is many times longer than the high-to-low propagation delay. Second, \(t_{PLH}\) is often dominated by the saturation delay. These results are true in general for RTL circuitry and show that any effort toward speed improvement should be directed toward the prevention of saturation in the switch transistors.
