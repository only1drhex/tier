Guest Question.

An inside cylinder locomotive has its cylinder centre lines 0.8 m apart and has a stroke of 0.6 m. The rotating masses are equivalent to 150 kg at the crank pin and the reciprocating masses per cylinder are 300 kg. The wheel centre lines are 1.8 m apart. The cranks are at right angles. The whole of the rotating and 2/3rd of the reciprocating masses are to be balanced by masses placed at a radius of 0.5 m.
Find (a) the magnitude and direction of the balancing masses, (b) the fluctuation in rail pressure under one wheel, (c) the variation of tractive effort and (d) the magnitude of swaying couple at a crank speed of 300 rpm.

Admin

(c) Each balance mass = 162.5 kg.

Balance mass for the rotating masses \( =150 \times \frac{162.5}{300}=81.25 kg \).

Balance mass for the reciprocating masses, \( B=\left(\frac{2}{3}\right) \cdot\left(\frac{300}{350}\right) \times 162.5=92.86 kg \).

Fluctuation in the rail pressure or hammer blow \( =B \omega^{2} b=92.86 \times\left(2 \pi \times \frac{300}{60}\right)^{2} \times 0.5 \).

= 45824 N.
