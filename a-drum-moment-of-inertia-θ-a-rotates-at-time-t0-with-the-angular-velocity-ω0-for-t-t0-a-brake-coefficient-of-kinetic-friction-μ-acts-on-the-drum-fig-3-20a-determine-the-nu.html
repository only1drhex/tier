Guest Question.

A drum (moment of inertia \(\Theta _{A} \)) rotates at time \(t_{0} \) with the angular velocity \(\omega _{0} \). For t > \(t_{0} \) , a brake (coefficient of kinetic friction μ) acts on the drum (Fig. 3.20a). Determine the number of rotations until the drum comes to a stop. Assume F = const.

Admin
Solution

We separate the drum and the lever; the free-body diagram is shown in Fig. 3.20b (note: R is the friction force). Moment equilibrium of the lever yields the normal force

\(N=\frac{l}{a} F\) .

If we apply the principle of angular momentum (3.12) to the drum, we obtain

\(\Theta _{a}\dot{\omega } =M_{a}\) . (3.12)

\(\overset{\curvearrowright }{A}:\Theta _{A} \ddot{\varphi }=-rR\) .

Using Coulomb’s law of friction \(R = \mu N =\mu \frac{l}{a} F\) leads to \(\ddot{\varphi }=-k\) ,

where the parameter \(k=\frac{r\mu lF}{a\Theta _{A} } \) has been introduced. We use the initial conditions \(\dot{\varphi }\left(0\right)=\omega _{0}, \varphi \left(0\right) =0\) and integrate twice:

\(\dot{\varphi }=-kt+\omega _{0}, \varphi =-\frac{1}{2}kt^{2} +\omega _{0}t\) .

The time \(t_{s} \), the angle \(\varphi _{s} \) and the number of rotations \(n _{s} \) until the drum comes to a stop follow from the condition \(\dot{\varphi }=0 \):

\(t_{s}=\frac{\omega _{0} }{k}, \varphi _{s}=\varphi \left(t_{s} \right)=\frac{\omega ^{2}_{0} }{2k}, n_{s} =\frac{\varphi _{s} }{2\pi } =\frac{\omega ^{2}_{0} }{4\pi k} \) .

The problem can also be solved with the aid of the work-energy theorem. The kinetic energy of the drum at time \(t_{0} \) = 0 (i.e., φ = 0) is given by

\(T_{0} =\frac{1}{2} \Theta _{A}\omega ^{2}_{0}\).

At time ts \(t_{s} \left(i.e.,\varphi =\varphi _{s} \right) \) we have

\(T_{s}=0\) .

The work of the external forces done during the time interval from
0 to \(t_{s}\) is

\(U=\int\limits_{0}^{\varphi _{s} }M_{A} d\varphi =-\int\limits_{0}^{\varphi _{s} }rRd\varphi =-rR\varphi _{s} \) .

Thus, (3.26) yields

\(T-T_{0} =U\) . (3.26)

\(-\frac{1}{2} \Theta _{A} \omega ^{2}_{0}=-rR\varphi _{s} \rightarrow n_{s}=\frac{\varphi _{s} }{2\pi } =\frac{\Theta _{A}\omega ^{2}_{0} }{4\pi rR}=\frac{\omega ^{2}_{0} }{4\pi k}\) .
