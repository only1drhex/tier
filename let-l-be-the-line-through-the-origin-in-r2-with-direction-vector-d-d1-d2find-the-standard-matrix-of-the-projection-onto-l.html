Guest Question.

Let \(\ell\) be the line through the origin in \(\mathbb{R}^{2}\) with direction vector \(\mathbf{d}=\left[\begin{array}{l}d_{1} \\ d_{2}\end{array}\right]\). Find the standard matrix of the projection onto \(\ell\)

Admin
Solution

Let \(T\) denote the projection. There is no harm in assuming that \(\mathrm{d}\) is a unit vector (i.e., \(\left.d_{1}^{2}+d_{2}^{2}=1\right)\), since any nonzero multiple of \(\mathbf{d}\) can serve as a direction vector for \(\ell\). Let \(\mathbf{d}^{\prime}=\left[\begin{array}{c}-d_{2} \\ d_{1}\end{array}\right]\) so that \(\mathbf{d}\) and \(\mathbf{d}^{\prime}\) are orthogonal. Since \(\mathbf{d}^{\prime}\) is also a unit vector, the set \(\mathcal{D}=\left\{\mathbf{d}, \mathbf{d}^{\prime}\right\}\) is an orthonormal basis for \(\mathbb{R}^{2}\).

As Figure 6.13 shows, \(T(\mathbf{d})=\mathbf{d}\) and \(T\left(\mathbf{d}^{\prime}\right)=0\). Therefore,

\([T(\mathbf{d})]_{\mathcal{D}}=\left[\begin{array}{l}1 \\0\end{array}\right] \quad \text { and } \quad\left[T\left(\mathbf{d}^{\prime}\right)\right]_{\mathcal{D}}=\left[\begin{array}{l}0 \\0\end{array}\right]\)

so

\( [T]_{\mathcal{D}}=\left[\begin{array}{ll} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{array}\right] \)

The change-of-basis matrix from \(\mathcal{D}\) to the standard basis \(\mathcal{E}\) is

\( P_{\mathcal{E} \leftarrow \mathcal{D}}=\left[\begin{array}{lr} d_{1} & -d_{2} \\ d_{2} & d_{1} \end{array}\right] \)

so the change-of-basis matrix from \(\mathcal{E}\) to \(\mathcal{D}\) is

\( P_{\mathcal{D} \leftarrow \mathcal{E}}=\left(P_{\mathcal{E} \leftarrow \mathcal{D}}\right)^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{rr} d_{1} & -d_{2} \\ d_{2} & d_{1} \end{array}\right]^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{rr} d_{1} & d_{2} \\ -d_{2} & d_{1} \end{array}\right] \)

By Theorem 6.29, then, the standard matrix of \(T\) is

\( \begin{aligned} [T]_{\mathcal{E}} &=P_{\mathcal{E} \leftarrow \mathcal{D}}[T]_{\mathcal{D}} P_{\mathcal{D} \leftarrow \mathcal{E}} \\ &=\left[\begin{array}{ll} d_{1} & -d_{2} \\ d_{2} & d_{1} \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{ll} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{rr} d_{1} & d_{2} \\ -d_{2} & d_{1} \end{array}\right] \\ &=\left[\begin{array}{cc} d_{1}^{2} & d_{1} d_{2} \\ d_{1} d_{2} & d_{2}^{2} \end{array}\right] \end{aligned} \)

which agrees with part (b) of .

Theorem 6.29 Let \(V\) be a finite-dimensional vector space with bases \(\mathcal{B}\) and \(\mathcal{C}\) and let \(T: V \rightarrow V\) be a linear transformation. Then

\([T]_{\mathcal{C}}=P^{-1}[T]_{\mathcal{B}} P\)

where \(P\) is the change-of-basis matrix from \(\mathcal{C}\) to \(\mathcal{B}\).
