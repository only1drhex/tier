Guest Question.

A single-thread steel worm rotates at 1800 rev/min, meshing with a 24-tooth worm gear transmitting 3 hp to the output shaft. The worm pitch diameter is 3 in and the tangential diametral pitch of the gear is 4 teeth/in. The normal pressure angle is 14.5◦. The ambient temperature is 70◦F. The application factor is 1.25 and the design factor is 1; gear face width is 2 in, lateral case area 600 \(\text { in }^{2}\), and the gear is chill-cast bronze.

(a) Find the gear geometry.

(b) Find the transmitted gear forces and the mesh efficiency.

(c) Is the mesh sufficient to handle the loading?

(d) Estimate the lubricant sump temperature.

Admin
Solution

(a) \(m_{G}=N_{G} / N_{W}=24 / 1=24\), gear: \(D=N_{G} / P_{t}=24 / 4=6.000\) in, worm: \(d=3.000 \text { in }\). The axial circular pitch \(p_{x} \text { is } p_{x}=\pi / P_{t}=\pi / 4=0.7854 \text { in }\). C = (3 + 6)/2 = 4.5 in.

 

Eq. (15–39):

 

\(a=\frac{p_{x}}{\pi}=0.3183 p_{x}\) (15–39)

 

\(a=p_{x} / \pi=0.7854 / \pi=0.250 \text { in }\)

 

Eq. (15–40):

 

\(b=\frac{1.157 p_{x}}{\pi}=0.3683 p_{x}\) (15–40)

 

\(b=0.3683 p_{x}=0.3683(0.7854)=0.289 \text { in }\)

 

Eq. (15–41):

 

\(h_{t}=\left\{\begin{array}{ll}\frac{2.157 p_{x}}{\pi}=0.6866 p_{x} & p_{x} \geq 0.16 in \\\frac{2.200 p_{x}}{\pi}+0.002=0.7003 p_{x}+0.002 & p_{x}<0.16 in\end{array}\right.\) (15–41)

 

\(h_{t}=0.6866 p_{x}=0.6866(0.7854)=0.539 \text { in }\)

 

Eq. (15–42):

 

\(d_{0}=d+2 a\) (15–42)

 

\(d_{0}=3+2(0.250)=3.500 \text { in }\)

 

Eq. (15–43):

 

\(d_{r}=d-2 b\) (15–43)

 

\(d_{r}=3-2(0.289)=2.422 \text { in }\)

 

Eq. (15–44):

 

\(D_{t}=D+2 a\) (15–44)

 

\(D_{t}=6+2(0.250)=6.500 \text { in }\)

 

Eq. (15–45):

 

\(D_{r}=D-2 b\) (15–45)

 

\(D_{r}=6-2(0.289)=5.422 \text { in }\)

 

Eq. (15–46):

c = b − a (15–46)

 

\(c=0.289-0.250=0.039 in\)

 

Eq. (15–47):

 

\(\left(F_{W}\right)_{\max }=2 \sqrt{\left(\frac{D_{t}}{2}\right)^{2}-\left(\frac{D}{2}-a\right)^{2}}=2 \sqrt{2 D a}\) (15–47)

 

\(\left(F_{W}\right)_{\max }=2 \sqrt{2(6) 0.250}=3.464 \text { in }\)

 

The tangential speeds of the worm, \(V_{W}, \text { and gear, } V_{G}\), are, respectively,

 

\(V_{W}=\pi(3) 1800 / 12=1414 ft / min \quad V_{G}=\frac{\pi(6) 1800 / 24}{12}=117.8 ft / min\)

 

The lead of the worm, from Eq. (13–27), is \(L=p_{x} N_{W}=0.7854(1)=0.7854\) in. The lead angle λ, from Eq. (13–28), is

 

\(\frac{C^{0.875}}{3} \leq d \leq \frac{C^{0.875}}{1.6}\) (15–27)

 

\(\left(W^{t}\right)_{\text {all }}=C_{s} D_{m}^{0.8} F_{e} C_{m} C_{v}\) (15–28)

 

\(\lambda=\tan ^{-1} \frac{L}{\pi d}=\tan ^{-1} \frac{0.7854}{\pi(3)}=4.764^{\circ}\)

 

The normal diametral pitch for a worm gear is the same as for a helical gear, which from Eq. (13–18) with \(\psi=\lambda\) is

 

\(\begin{aligned}K_{T} &=\left\{\begin{array}{ll}1 & 32^{\circ} F \leq t \leq 250^{\circ} F \\(460+t) / 710 & t>250^{\circ} F\end{array}\right.\\K_{\theta} &=\left\{\begin{array}{ll}1 & 0^{\circ} C \leq \theta \leq 120^{\circ} C \\(273+\theta) / 393 & \theta>120^{\circ} C\end{array}\right.\end{aligned}\) (13–18)

 

\(P_{n}=\frac{P_{t}}{\cos \lambda}=\frac{4}{\cos 4.764^{\circ}}=4.014\)

 

\(p_{n}=\frac{\pi}{P_{n}}=\frac{\pi}{4.014}=0.7827 \text { in }\)

 

The sliding velocity, from Eq. (15–62), is

 

\(V_{s}=\frac{\pi d n_{W}}{12 \cos \lambda}\) (15–62)

 

\(V_{s}=\frac{\pi d n_{W}}{12 \cos \lambda}=\frac{\pi(3) 1800}{12 \cos 4.764^{\circ}}=1419 ft / \min\)

 

(b) The coefficient of friction, from Eq. (15–38), is

 

\(f=\left\{\begin{array}{ll}0.15 & V_{s}=0 \\0.124 \exp \left(-0.074 V_{s}^{0.645}\right) & 0<V_{s} \leq 10 ft / min \\0.103 \exp \left(-0.110 V_{s}^{0.450}\right)+0.012 & V_{s}>10 ft / min\end{array}\right.\) (15–38)

 

\(f=0.103 \exp \left[-0.110(1419)^{0.450}\right]+0.012=0.0178\)

 

The efficiency e, from Eq. (13–46), is

 

\(e=\frac{\cos \phi_{n}-f \tan \lambda}{\cos \phi_{n}+f \cot \lambda}=\frac{\cos 14.5^{\circ}-0.0178 \tan 4.764^{\circ}}{\cos 14.5^{\circ}+0.0178 \cot 4.764^{\circ}}=0.818\)

 

The designer used \(n_{d}=1, K_{a}=1.25\) and an output horsepower of \(H_{0}=3\) hp. The gear tangential force component \(W_{G}^{t}\) , from Eq. (15–58), is

 

\(W_{G}^{t}=\frac{33000 n_{d} H_{0} K_{a}}{V_{C} e}\) (15–58)

 

\(W_{G}^{t}=\frac{33000 n_{d} H_{0} K_{a}}{V_{G} e}=\frac{33000(1) 3(1.25)}{117.8(0.818)}=1284 lbf\)

 

The tangential force on the worm is given by Eq. (15–57):

 

\(W_{W}^{t}=W_{G}^{t} \frac{\cos \phi_{n} \sin \lambda+f \cos \lambda}{\cos \phi_{n} \cos \lambda-f \sin \lambda}\) (15–57)

 

\(\begin{aligned}W_{W}^{t} &=W_{G}^{t} \frac{\cos \phi_{n} \sin \lambda+f \cos \lambda}{\cos \phi_{n} \cos \lambda-f \sin \lambda} \\&=1284 \frac{\cos 14.5^{\circ} \sin 4.764^{\circ}+0.0178 \cos 4.764^{\circ}}{\cos 14.5^{\circ} \cos 4.764^{\circ}-0.0178 \sin 4.764^{\circ}}=131 lbf\end{aligned}\)

 

(c)

Eq. (15–34):

 

\(C_{s}=\left\{\begin{array}{lll}1000 & C>3 & d_{G} \leq 8 \text { in } \\1412-456 \log d_{G} & C>3 & d_{G}>8 \text { in }\end{array}\right.\) (15–34)

 

\(C_{s}=1000\)

 

Eq. (15–36):

 

\(C_{m}=\left\{\begin{array}{ll}0.02 \sqrt{-m_{G}^{2}+40 m_{G}-76}+0.46 & 3<m_{G} \leq 20 \\0.0107 \sqrt{-m_{G}^{2}+56 m_{G}+5145} & 20<m_{G} \leq 76 \\1.1483-0.00658 m_{G} & m_{G}>76\end{array}\right.\) (15–36)

 

\(C_{m}=0.0107 \sqrt{-24^{2}+56(24)+5145}=0.823\)

 

Eq. (15–37):

 

\(C_{v}=\left\{\begin{array}{ll}0.659 \exp \left(-0.0011 V_{s}\right) & V_{s}<700 ft / min \\13.31 V_{s}^{-0.571} & 700 \leq V_{s}<3000 ft / min \\65.52 V_{s}^{-0.774} & V_{s}>3000 ft / min\end{array}\right.\) (15–37)

 

\(C_{v}=13.31(1419)^{-0.571}=0.211^{4}\)

 

Eq. (15–28):

 

\(\left(W^{t}\right)_{ all }=C_{s} D_{m}^{0.8} F_{e} C_{m} C_{v}\) (15–28)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left(W^{t}\right)_{ all } &=C_{s} D^{0.8}\left(F_{e}\right)_{G} C_{m} C_{v} \\&=1000(6)^{0.8}(2) 0.823(0.211)=1456 lbf\end{aligned}\)

 

Since \(W_{G}^{t}<\left(W^{t}\right)_{\text {all }}\), the mesh will survive at least 25 000 h. The friction force \(W_{f}\) is given by Eq. (15–61):

 

\(W_{f}=\frac{f W_{G}^{t}}{f \sin \lambda-\cos \phi_{n} \cos \lambda}\) (15–61)

 

\(\begin{aligned}W_{f} &=\frac{f W_{G}^{t}}{f \sin \lambda-\cos \phi_{n} \cos \lambda}=\frac{0.0178(1284)}{0.0178 \sin 4.764^{\circ}-\cos 14.5^{\circ} \cos 4.764^{\circ}} \\&=-23.7 lbf\end{aligned}\)

 

The power dissipated in frictional work \(H_{f}\) is given by Eq. (15–63):

 

\(H_{f}=\frac{\left|W_{f}\right| V_{s}}{33000} hp\) (15–63)

 

\(H_{f}=\frac{\left|W_{f}\right| V_{s}}{33000}=\frac{|-23.7| 1419}{33000}=1.02 hp\)

 

The worm and gear powers, \(H_{W} \text { and } H_{C}\), are given by

 

\(H_{W}=\frac{W_{W}^{t} V_{W}}{33000}=\frac{131(1414)}{33000}=5.61 hp \quad H_{G}=\frac{W_{G}^{t} V_{G}}{33000}=\frac{1284(117.8)}{33000}=4.58 hp\)

 

Gear power is satisfactory. Now,

 

\(P_{n}=P_{t} / \cos \lambda=4 / \cos 4.764^{\circ}=4.014\)

 

\(p_{n}=\pi / P_{n}=\pi / 4.014=0.7827 \text { in }\)

 

The bending stress in a gear tooth is given by Buckingham’s adaptation of the Lewis equation, Eq. (15–53), as

 

\(\sigma_{a}=\frac{W_{G}^{t}}{p_{n} F_{e} y}\) (15–53)

 

\((\sigma)_{G}=\frac{W_{G}^{t}}{p_{n} F_{G} y}=\frac{1284}{0.7827(2)(0.1)}=8200 psi\)

 

Stress in gear satisfactory.

(d)

Eq. (15–52):

 

\(A_{\min }=43.20 C^{1.7}\) (15–52)

 

\(A_{\min }=43.2 C^{1.7}=43.2(4.5)^{1.7}=557 in ^{2}\)

 

The gear case has a lateral area of 600 \(\operatorname{in}^{2}\).

 

Eq. (15–49):

 

\(H_{\text {loss }}=33000(1-e) H_{\text {in }}\) (15–49)

 

\(\begin{aligned}H_{ loss } &=33000(1-e) H_{ in }=33000(1-0.818) 5.61 \\&=33690 ft \cdot lbf / min\end{aligned}\)

 

Eq. (15–50):

 

\(\hbar_{ CR }=\left\{\begin{array}{ll}\frac{n_{W}}{6494}+0.13 & \text { no fan on worm shaft } \\\frac{n_{W}}{3939}+0.13 & \text { fan on worm shaft }\end{array}\right.\) (15–50)

 

\(\hbar_{ CR }=\frac{n_{W}}{3939}+0.13=\frac{1800}{3939}+0.13=0.587 ft \cdot lbf /\left( min \cdot in ^{2} \cdot{ }^{\circ} F \right)\)

 

Eq. (15–51):

 

\(t_{s}=t_{a}+\frac{H_{\text {loss }}}{\hbar_{ CR } A}=\frac{33000(1-e)(H)_{ in }}{\hbar_{ CR } A}+t_{a}\) (15–51)

 

\(t_{s}=t_{a}+\frac{H_{\text {loss }}}{h_{ CR } A}=70+\frac{33690}{0.587(600)}=166^{\circ} F\)
