Guest Question.

Find the general solution of x’ = Ax, where

\(A=\left[\begin{array}{rrr}-1 & -8 & 1 \\-1 & -3 & 2 \\-4 & -16 & 7\end{array}\right]\).

Admin
Solution

Reducing A to Hessenberg form, we have \(H=Q A Q^{-1}\), where H,Q, and \(Q^{-1}\) are

\(H=\left[\begin{array}{rrr}-1 & -4 & 1 \\-1 & 5 & 2 \\0 & -8 & -1\end{array}\right], \quad Q=\left[\begin{array}{rrr}1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0 \\0 & -4 & 1\end{array}\right], \quad \text { and } \quad Q^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{lll}1 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 0 \\0 & 4 & 1\end{array}\right]\).

The change of variables y(t) = Qx (t) converts x’ = Ax, \(x (0)= x _{0}\) to the problem \(y ^{\prime}=H y , y (0)=Q x _{0}\).
The characteristic polynomial for H is \(p(t)=(t-1)^{3}\), so λ = 1 is an eigenvalue of multiplicity 3 of H. Up to a scalar multiple, the only eigenvector of H is

\(v _{1}=\left[\begin{array}{r}4 \\-1 \\4\end{array}\right]\).

We obtain two generalized eigenvectors for H by solving \((H-I) x = v _{1} \text { to get } v _{2}\), and \((H-I) x = v _{2} \text { to get } v _{3}\). These generalized eigenvectors are

\(v _{2}=\left[\begin{array}{r}1 \\-1 \\2\end{array}\right] \quad \text { and } \quad v _{3}=\left[\begin{array}{r}3 \\-1 \\3\end{array}\right]\).

Thus the general solution of y’ = Hy is

\(y (t)=e^{t}\left[c_{1} v _{1}+c_{2}\left( v _{2}+t v _{1}\right)+c_{3}\left( v _{3}+t v _{2}+\frac{t^{2}}{2} v _{1}\right)\right]\),

and we can recover x(t) from \(x (t)=Q^{-1} y (t)\).
If H is an (n × n) reduced Hessenberg matrix, it can be shown that \(R^{n}\) has a basis consisting of eigenvectors and generalized eigenvectors of H. This general result is fairly difficult to establish, however, and we do not do so here.
