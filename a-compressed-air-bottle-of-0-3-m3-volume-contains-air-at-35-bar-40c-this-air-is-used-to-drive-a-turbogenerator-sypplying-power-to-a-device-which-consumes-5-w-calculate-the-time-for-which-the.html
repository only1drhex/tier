Guest Question.

A compressed air bottle of 0.3 \(\textbf{m}^{\textbf{3}}\) volume contains air at 35 bar, 40°C. This air is used to drive a turbogenerator sypplying power to a device which consumes 5 W. Calculate the time for which the device can be operated if the actual output of the turbogenerator is 60% of the maximum theoretical output. The ambient pressure to which the tank pressure has fallen is 1 bar. For air, \(\frac{C_{p}}{C_{v}}= 1.4.\) [U.P.S.C. 1993]

Admin
Solution

Here turbogenerator is fed with compressed air from a compressed air bottle. Pressure inside bottle gradually decreases from 35 bar to 1 bar. Expansion from 35 bar to 1 bar occurs isentropically. Thus, for the initial and final states of pressure, volume, temperature and mass inside bottle being given as \(P_{1}, V_{1}, T_{1} \& m_{1}\text{ and }P_{2}, V_{2}, T_{2} \& m_{2}\) respectively. It is transient flow process similar to emptying of the bottle.

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left\lgroup\frac{P_{2}}{T_{1}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{γ-1}{γ} } &= \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}, \text{Given: }P_{1} = 35 bar, T_{1} = 40°C or 313 K\\V_{1}&= 0.3 m^{3}; V_{2} = 0.3 m^{3}\\P_{2}&= 1 bar.\\T_{2}&= T_{1} \left\lgroup\frac{P_{2}}{T_{1}} \right\rgroup^{\frac{γ-1}{γ} }\\T_{2}&= 113.22 K\end{aligned}\)

By perfect gas law, initial mass in bottle,

 

\(\begin{aligned}&m_{1} = \frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{RT_{1}} = \frac{35 × 10^{2} × 0.3}{0.287 × 313}\\m_{1}&= 11.68 kg\end{aligned}\)

Final mass in bottle,

 

\(\begin{aligned}\qquad m_{2}&= \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{RT_{2}} = \frac{1 × 10^{2} × 0.3}{0.287 × 113.22}\\m_{2}&= 0.923 kg\end{aligned}\)

Energy available for running turbo generator or work;

 

\(\begin{aligned}\qquad \qquad\qquad \qquad \qquad W + (m_{1} – m_{2}) h_{2}&= m_{1} u_{1} – m_{2} u_{2}\\W&= (m_{1}u_{1} – m_{2}u_{2}) – (m_{1} – m_{2}) h_{2}\\&= (m_{1} c_{v} T_{1} – m_{2} c_{v} T_{2}) – (m_{1} – m_{2}) · c_{p} · T_{2}\end{aligned}\)

Taking

 

\(\begin{aligned}c_{v}&= 0.718 kJ/kg . K and c_{P} = 1.005 kJ/kg · K\\W&=\left\{(11.68 × 0.718 × 313) – (0.923 × 0.718 × 113.22)\right\} \\ &– \left\{(11.68 – 0.923) × 1.005 × 113.22\right\} \\W&= 1325.86 kJ\\\end{aligned}\)

This is the maximum work that can be had from the emptying of compressed air bottle between given pressure limits.

Turbogenerator’s actual output = 5 kJ/s

Input to turbogenerator =

 

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{5}{0.6} = 8.33 kJ/s.\end{aligned} \) Time duration for which turbogenerator can be run;

 

\(\begin{aligned}Δt &= \frac{1325.86}{8.33}\\Δt &= 159.17 sec.\\\end{aligned}\) Duration 160 seconds
