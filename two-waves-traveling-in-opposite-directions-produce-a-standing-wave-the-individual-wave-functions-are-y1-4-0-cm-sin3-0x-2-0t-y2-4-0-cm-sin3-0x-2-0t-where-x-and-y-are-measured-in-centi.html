Guest Question.

Two waves traveling in opposite directions produce a standing wave. The individual wave functions are

\( y_{1}=(4.0 cm ) \sin (3.0 x-2.0 t) \) ,

\( y_{2}=(4.0 cm ) \sin (3.0 x+2.0 t) \).

where x and y are measured in centimeters.

(A) Find the amplitude of the simple harmonic motion of the element of the medium located at x= 2.3 cm.

(B) Find the positions of the nodes and antinodes if one end of the string is at x = 0.

(C) What is the maximum value of the position in the simple harmonic motion of an element located at an antinode?

Admin
Solution

The standing wave is described by Equation 18.3; in this problem, we have A = 4.0 cm, k = 3.0 rad/cm, and ω = 2.0 rad/s. Thus,

\( y=(2 A \sin k x) \cos \omega t \) (18.3).

\( y=(2 A \sin k x) \cos \omega t=[(8.0 cm ) \sin 3.0 x] \cos 2.0 t \).

Thus, we obtain the amplitude of the simple harmonic motion of the element at the position x = 2.3 cm by evaluating the coefficient of the cosine function at this position:

\( y_{\max }=\left.(8.0 cm ) \sin 3.0 x\right|_{x=2.3} \).

\( =(8.0 cm ) \sin (6.9 rad )=4.6 cm \).

(B) Find the positions of the nodes and antinodes if one end of the string is at x = 0.

\( \text { With } k=2 \pi / \lambda=3.0 rad / cm , \text { we see that the } \)\( \text { wavelength is } \lambda=(2 \pi / 3.0) cm \text {. Therefore, from Equation } \) 18.4 we find that the nodes are located at

\( x=\frac{\lambda}{2}, \lambda, \frac{3 \lambda}{2}, \ldots=\frac{n \lambda}{2} \quad n=0,1,2,3, \ldots \) (18.4).

\( x=n \frac{\lambda}{2}=n\left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right) cm \quad n=0,1,2,3, \ldots \).

and from Equation 18.5 we find that the antinodes are located at

\( x=\frac{\lambda}{4}, \frac{3 \lambda}{4}, \frac{5 \lambda}{4}, \ldots=\frac{n \lambda}{4} \quad n=1,3,5, \ldots \) (18.5).

\( x=n \frac{\lambda}{4}=n\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\right) cm \quad n=1,3,5, \ldots \).

(C) What is the maximum value of the position in the simple harmonic motion of an element located at an antinode?

According to Equation 18.3, the maximum position of an element at an antinode is the amplitude of the standing wave, which is twice the amplitude of the individual traveling waves:

\( y=(2 A \sin k x) \cos \omega t \) (18.3).

\( y_{\max }=2 A(\sin k x)_{\max }=2(4.0 cm )(\pm 1)=\pm 8.0 cm \).

where we have used the fact that the maximum value of sin kx is ±1. Let us check this result by evaluating the coefficient of our standing-wave function at the positions we found for the antinodes:

\( y_{\max }=\left.(8.0 cm ) \sin 3.0 x\right|_{x=n(\pi / 6)} \).

\( =(8.0 cm ) \sin \left[3.0 n\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\right) rad \right] \).

\( =(8.0 cm ) \sin \left[n\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right) rad \right]=\pm 8.0 cm \).

In evaluating this expression, we have used the fact that n is an odd integer; thus, the sine function is equal to ±1, depending on the value of n.
