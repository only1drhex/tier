Guest Question.

Consider a time-dependent current across a \(2 \mu \mathrm{F}\) capacitor given as

\(i_{C}(t)=\left\{\begin{array}{rl}t, & 0<t<1 \mathrm{~s} \\1, & 1<t<3 \mathrm{~s} \\-t+4, & 3<t<4 \mathrm{~s} \\0, & \text { otherwise. }\end{array} \quad(\mu \mathrm{A}) .\right.\)

Assuming \(v_{C}(0)=1 \mathrm{~V}\), find and plot the voltage across the capacitor. In addition, find the power during \(1<t<3 \mathrm{~s}\), as well as the energy stored or released in this time period.

Admin
Solution

We have \(v_{C}(t)=1 \mathrm{~V}\) for \(t<0\). For \(0<t<1 \mathrm{~s}\), we derive

\(v_{C}(t)=v_{C}(0)+\frac{1}{2 \times 10^{-6}} \int_{0}^{t} 10^{-6} t^{\prime} d t^{\prime}=\left(\frac{t^{2}}{2}+1\right) \mathrm{V}\),

leading to \(v_{C}(1)=3 / 2 \mathrm{~V}\). Then, for \(1<t<3 \mathrm{~s}\), we have

\(v_{C}(t)=v_{C}(1)+\frac{1}{2 \times 10^{-6}} \int_{1}^{t} 10^{-6} d t^{\prime}=\frac{3}{2}+\frac{1}{2}(t-1)=\left(\frac{t}{2}+1\right) \mathrm{V}\)

and \(v_{C}(3)=5 / 2 \mathrm{~V}\). Moreover, when \(3<t<4 \mathrm{~s}\), we get

\(\begin{aligned}v_{C}(t) &=v_{C}(3)+\frac{1}{2 \times 10^{-6}} \int_{3}^{t} 10^{-6}\left(4-t^{\prime}\right) d t^{\prime}=\frac{5}{2}+\frac{1}{2}\left[4 t^{\prime}-\frac{\left(t^{\prime}\right)^{2}}{2}\right]_{3}^{t} \\&=\frac{5}{2}+\frac{1}{2}\left(4 t-\frac{t^{2}}{2}\right)-\frac{1}{2}(12-9 / 2)=\left(-\frac{t^{2}}{4}+2 t-\frac{5}{4}\right) \mathrm{V}\end{aligned}\)

and \(v_{C}(4)=11 / 4 \mathrm{~V} .\) Overall, the voltage across the capacitor can be written as

\(v_{C}(t)=\left\{\begin{aligned}1, \quad & t<0 \\t^{2} / 2+1,\quad & 0<t<1 \mathrm{~s} \\t / 2+1,\quad & 1<t<3 \mathrm{~s} \\-t^{2} / 4+2 t-5 / 4,\quad & 3<t<4 \mathrm{~s} \\11 / 4,\quad & t>4 \mathrm{~s}\end{aligned}\quad(\mathrm{V}) .\right.\)

Hence, the current and voltage across the capacitor can be plotted as follows.

Next, the power during \(1<t<3 \mathrm{~s}\) can be found as

\(p_{C}(t)=v_{C}(t) i_{C}(t)=\left(\frac{t}{2}+1\right) \mu \mathrm{W} .\)

Hence, the energy change in this time period can be obtained via integration as

\(w_{C}(3)-w_{C}(1)=\int_{1}^{3}\left(t^{\prime} / 2+1\right) d t^{\prime}=\left[\frac{t^{2}}{4}+t\right]_{1}^{3}=4 \mu \mathrm{J} .\)

Alternatively, we can use

\(w_{C}(3)-w_{C}(1)=\frac{1}{2} C\left[v_{C}(3)\right]^{2}-\frac{1}{2} C\left[v_{C}(1)\right]^{2}=\frac{25}{4}-\frac{9}{4}=4 \mu \mathrm{J} .\)
