Guest Question.

Pressurized water is often available at elevated temperatures and may be used for space heat-ing or industrial process applications. In such cases it is customary to use a tube bundle in which the water is passed through the tubes, while air is passed in cross flow over the tubes. Consider a staggered arrangement for which the tube outside diameter is 16.4 mm and the longitudinal and transverse pitches are \(S_{L}\) = 34.3 mm and \(S_{T}\) = 31.3 mm. There are seven rows of tubes in the airflow direction and eight tubes per row. Under typical operating condi-tions the cylinder surface temperature is at 70°C, while the air upstream temperature and velocity are 15°C and 6 m/s, respectively. Determine the air-side convection coefficient and the rate of heat transfer for the tube bundle. What is the air-side pressure drop?

Admin
Solution

Known: Geometry and operating conditions of a tube bank.

Find:
1. Air-side convection coefficient and heat rate.
2. Pressure drop.

Assumptions:
1. Steady-state, incompressible flow conditions.
2. Negligible radiation effects.
3. Negligible effect of change in air temperature across tube bank on air properties.

Properties: , air \((T_{∞} = 15°C): ρ = 1.217 kg/m^{3}, c_{p} = 1007 J/kg .K, \nu = 14.82 × 10^{-6} m^{2}/s, k = 0.0253 W/m. K, Pr = 0.710\). , air \((T_{s} = 70°C): Pr = 0.701.\) , air \((T_{f} = 43°C): \nu = 17.4 × 10^{-6} m^{2} /s, k = 0.0274 W/m. K, Pr = 0.705.\)

Analysis:
1. From Equations 7.58 and 7.59 , the air-side Nusselt number is

\(\left[ \overset{-}{Nu_{D}}= C_{1} Re^{m}_{D,max}Pr^{0.36}\left(\frac{Pr}{Pr_{s}} \right)^{1/4}\right] \\ \left[\begin{matrix} N_L \geq 20 \\ 0.7 \lesssim Pr\lesssim 500 \\ 10\lesssim Re_{D , max } \lesssim 2 \times 10^6 \end{matrix} \right] \) (7.58)

\(\overset{-}{Nu_{D}}|_{(N_{L} \lt 20)} = C_{2}\overset{-}{Nu_{D}}|_{(N_{L} \geq 20)}\) (7.59)

\( \overset{-}{Nu_{D}} = C_{2} C_{1} Re^{m}_{D,max} Pr^{0.36} \left(\frac{Pr}{Pr_{s}} \right)^{ 1/4 }\)

Since \(S_{D} = [S^{2}_{L} + (S_{T} /2)^{2}]^{1/2} = 37.7 mm \) is greater than \((S_{T} + D)/2,\) the maximum velocity occurs on the transverse plane, \(A_{1}\), of Figure 7.12. Hence from Equation 7.60

\(V_{max} = \frac{S_{T}}{S_{T} – D} V = \frac{31.3 mm}{(31.3 – 16.4) mm} 6 m/s = 12.6 m/s\)

With

\(Re_{D,max} = \frac{V_{max} D}{\nu } = \frac{12.6 m/s × 0.0164 m}{ 14.82 × 10^{-6} m^{2}/s } =13,943\)

and

\(\frac{S_{T}}{S_{L} } = \frac{31.3 mm}{34.3 mm} = 0.91 < 2 \)

it follows from Tables 7.5 and 7.6 that

\(C_{1} = 0.35 \left(\frac{S_{T}}{S_{L} } \right)^{1/5} = 0.34, m = 0.60,\) and \( C_{2}= 0.95\)

Hence

\(\overset{-}{Nu_{D}} = 0.95 × 0.34(13,943)^{0.60}(0.71)^{0.36} \left(\frac{0.710}{ 0.701} \right)^{0.25}=87.9\)

and

\( \bar{h} = \overset{-}{Nu_{D}} \frac{k}{D} = 87.9 × \frac{0.0253 W/m . K}{0.0164 m} = 135.6 W/m^{2} . K\)

From Equation 7.63

\( \frac{T_s – T_o}{T_s – T_i} = \text{exp} \left( – \frac{\pi DN\bar{h} }{\rho V N_T S_T c_p} \right) \) (7.63)

\(T_{s} – T_{o} = (T_{s} -T_{i}) \exp \left( – \frac{\pi DN\bar{h} }{\rho VN_{T} S_{T} c_{p}} \right) \)

 

\(T_{s} – T_{o} = (55°C) \exp \left( – \frac{\pi (0.0164 m) 56 (135.6 W/m^{2} . K)}{1.217 kg/m^{3} (6 m/s) 8 (0.0313 m) 1007 J/kg . K} \right) \)

 

\(T_{s} – T_{o} = 44.5°C\)

Hence from Equations 7.62 and 7.64

\(\Delta T_{lm} = \frac{(T_{s} – T_{i}) – (T_{s} – T_{o}) }{\ln \left(\frac{T_{s} – T_{i}}{T_{s} – T_{o}} \right) }= \frac{(55 – 44.5)°C}{\ln \left(\frac{55}{44.5} \right)} = 49.6°C\)

and

\(q^{\prime} = N( \bar{h} \pi D\Delta T_{lm}) = 56\pi × 135.6 W/m^{2} . K × 0.0164 m × 49.6°C\)

 

\(q^{\prime} = 19.4 kW/m\)

2. The pressure drop may be obtained from Equation 7.65.

\(\Delta p = N_{L} \chi \left( \frac{\rho V^{2}_{max}}{2} \right) f\)

With \(Re_{D,max} = 13,943, P_{T} = (S_{T} /D) = 1.91, P_{L} = (S_{L} /D) = 2.09\), and \((P_{T} /P_{L}) = 0.91\), it follows from Figure 7.15 that \(\chi ≈ 1.04\) and f ≈ 0.35. Hence with \(N_{L} = 7\)

\(\Delta p = 7 × 1.04 \left[\frac{1.217 kg/m^{3} (12.6 m/s)^{2}}{2} \right] 0.35\)

 

\(\Delta p = 246 N/m^{2} = 2.46 × 10^{-3} bars \)

Comments:
1. Had \(ΔT_{i} \equiv T_{s} – T_{i}\) been used in lieu of \(ΔT_{lm}\) in Equation 7.64 \(q^{\prime} = N( \bar{h} \pi D\Delta T_{lm}) \), the heat rate would have been overpredicted by 11%.
2. Since the air temperature is predicted to increase by only 10.5°C, evaluation of the air properties at \(T_{i} = 15°C\) is a reasonable approximation. However, if improved accuracy is desired, the calculations could be repeated with the properties reevaluated at \((T_{i} + T_{o})/2 = 20.25°C\). An exception pertains to the density ρ in the exponential term of Equation 7.63 \(T_{s} – T_{o} = (T_{s} -T_{i}) \exp \left( – \frac{\pi DN\bar{h} }{\rho VN_{T} S_{T} c_{p}} \right) \). As it appears in the denominator of this term, ρ is matched with the inlet velocity to provide a product \((ρ_{V})\) that is linked to the mass flow rate of air enter-ing the tube bank. Hence, in this term, ρ should be evaluated at \(T_{i}\).
3. The air outlet temperature and heat rate may be increased by increasing the number of tube rows, and for a fixed number of rows, they may be varied by adjusting the air velocity. For \(5 ≤ N_{L} ≤ 25\) and V = 6 m/s, parametric calculations based on Equations 7.58 \(\overset{-}{Nu_{D}}= C_{1} Re^{m}_{D,max}Pr^{0.36}\left(\frac{Pr}{Pr_{s}} \right)^{1/4}\), 7.59 \(\overset{-}{Nu_{D}}|_{(N_{L} \lt 20)} = C_{2}\overset{-}{Nu_{D}}|_{(N_{L} \geq 20)}\), and 7.62 \(\Delta T_{lm} = \frac{(T_{s} – T_{i}) – (T_{s} – T_{o}) }{\ln \left(\frac{T_{s} – T_{i}}{T_{s} – T_{o}} \right) }\) through 7.64 \(\acute{q} = N( \bar{h} \pi D\Delta T_{lm})\) yield the following results:

The air outlet temperature would asymptotically approach the surface temperature with increasing \(N_{L}\), at which point the heat rate approaches a constant value and there is no advantage to adding more tube rows. Note that Δp increases linearly with increasing \(N_{L}\). For \(N_{L}\) = 25 and 1 ≤ V ≤ 20 m/s, we obtain
