Guest Question.

Find the slope at A for the beam shown in Figure P17.120/121 by applying Castigliano’s second theorem. Assume that \(E I=11.4 \times 10^{6}\text { kip-in. }{ }^{2}\) for the beam.

Admin
Solution

Designate the concentrated moment at A as M’. The free-body diagram of the beam is shown.

 

 

Draw a free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section a–a and derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{aligned}\Sigma M_{a-a} &=M^{\prime}-\left(24 kips +\frac{M^{\prime}}{18 ft }\right) x_{1}+M=0 \\& \therefore M=\left(24 kips +\frac{M^{\prime}}{18 ft }\right) x_{1}-M^{\prime} & 0 \leq x_{1}<6 ft\end{aligned}\)

Differentiate this expression to obtain ∂M/∂M’.

\(\frac{\partial M}{\partial M^{\prime}}=\frac{x_{1}}{18}-1\)

Substitute M’ = 90 kip-ft into the bending-moment equation to obtain

\(M=(29 \text { kips }) x_{1}-90 \text { kip-ft }\)

 

Draw a second free-body diagram that cuts through the beam at section b–b and derive a moment equation M from this free-body diagram.

\(\begin{aligned}&\Sigma M_{b-b}=-\frac{6 kips / ft }{2} x_{2}^{2}+\left(48 kips -\frac{M^{\prime}}{18 ft }\right) x_{2}-M=0 \\&\therefore M=-\frac{6 kips / ft }{2} x_{2}^{2}+\left(48 \text { kips }-\frac{M^{\prime}}{18 ft }\right) x_{2} \quad 0 \leq x_{2}<12 ft\end{aligned}\)

Differentiate this expression to obtain ∂M/∂M’.

\(\frac{\partial M}{\partial M^{\prime}}=-\frac{x_{2}}{18}\)

Substitute M’ = 90 kip-ft into the bending-moment equation to obtain

\(M=-\frac{6 kips / ft }{2} x_{2}^{2}+(43 kips ) x_{2}\)

Castigliano’s second theorem applied to beam slopes is expressed by Equation (17.41).

\(\theta=\int_{0}^{L}\left(\frac{\partial M}{\partial M^{\prime}}\right) \frac{M}{E I} d x\)

The beam slope at A can now be determined.

\(\begin{gathered}\theta_{A}=\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{6}\left(\frac{x_{1}}{18}-1\right)\left(29 x_{1}-90\right) d x_{1}+\frac{1}{E I} \int_{0}^{12}\left(-\frac{x_{2}}{18}\right)\left(-\frac{6}{2} x_{2}^{2}+43 x_{2}\right) d x_{2} \\=\frac{29}{18 E I} \int_{0}^{6} x_{1}^{2} d x_{1}-\frac{90}{18 E I} \int_{0}^{6} x_{1} d x_{1}-\frac{29}{E I} \int_{0}^{6} x_{1} d x_{1}+\frac{90}{E I} \int_{0}^{6} d x_{1} \\\quad+\frac{3}{18 E I} \int_{0}^{12} x_{2}^{3} d x_{2}-\frac{43}{18 E I} \int_{0}^{12} x_{2}^{2} d x_{2}\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&=\frac{29}{54 E I}\left[x_{1}^{3}\right]_{0}^{6}-\frac{90}{36 E I}\left[x_{1}^{2}\right]_{0}^{6}-\frac{29}{2 E I}\left[x_{1}^{2}\right]_{0}^{6}+\frac{90}{E I}\left[x_{1}\right]_{0}^{6}+\frac{1}{24 E I}\left[x_{2}^{4}\right]_{0}^{12}-\frac{43}{54 E I}\left[x_{2}^{3}\right]_{0}^{12} \\&=\frac{116}{E I}-\frac{90}{E I}-\frac{522}{E I}+\frac{540}{E I}+\frac{864}{E I}-\frac{1,376}{E I} \\&=-\frac{468}{E I}=-\frac{\left(468 kip – ft ^{2}\right)(12 in . / ft )^{2}}{11.4 \times 10^{6} kip – in .^{2}}=-0.00591158 kip – ft \\&\therefore \theta_{A}=0.00591 rad CW\end{aligned}\)
