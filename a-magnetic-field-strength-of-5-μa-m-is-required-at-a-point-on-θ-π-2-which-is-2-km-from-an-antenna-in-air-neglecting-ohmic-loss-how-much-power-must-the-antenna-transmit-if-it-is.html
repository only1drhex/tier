Guest Question.

A magnetic field strength of 5 μA/m is required at a point on θ= π/ 2, which is 2 km from an antenna in air. Neglecting ohmic loss, how much power must the antenna transmit if it is
(a) A Hertzian dipole of length λ/25?
(b) A half-wave dipole?

(c) A quarter-wave monopole?
(d) A 10-turn loop antenna of radius \(ρ_{0} \)= λ/20?

Admin
Solution

(a) For a Hertzian dipole,

\(|H_{\phi s}|=\frac{I_{0}\beta dl \sin \theta }{4\pi r}\)
where \(dl=\lambda /25\) or \(\beta dl=\frac{2\pi }{\lambda }.\frac{\lambda }{25}=\frac{2\pi }{25}\). Hence,
\(5\times 10^{-6}=\frac{I_{0}.\frac{2\pi }{25}(1) }{4\pi (2\times 10^{3})}=\frac{I_{0}}{10^{5}}\)
or
\(I_{0}\)=0.5 A
\(P_{rad}=40\pi ^{2}[\frac{dl}{\lambda } ]^{2}I_{0}^{2}=\frac{40\pi^{2}(0.5)^{2}}{(25)^{2}}\)
=158 mW

(b) For a λ/2 dipole,
\(|H_{\phi s}|=\frac{I_{0}\cos (\frac{\pi}{2} \cos\theta )}{2\pi r \sin \theta }\)
\(5\times 10^{-6}=\frac{I_{0}.1}{2\pi (2\times 10^{3}).(1)}\)
or
\(I_{0}=20\pi\) mA
\(P_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}I_{0}^{2}R_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}(20\pi )^{2}\times 10^{-6}(73)\)
=144 mW

(c) For a λ/4 monopole,
\(I_{0}= 20 \pi \)mA

as in part (b ).
\(P_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}I_{0}^{2}R_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}(20\pi )^{2}\times 10^{-6}(36.56)\)
=72 mW

(d) For a loop antenna,
\(|H_{\theta s}|=\frac{\pi I_{0}}{r}\frac{S}{\lambda ^{2}}\sin \theta\)
For a single turn , \(S=\pi \rho ^{2}_{0}.\) For N-turn , \(S=N\pi \rho ^{2}_{0}\).Hence,
\(5\times 10^{-6}=\frac{\pi I_{0}10 \pi }{2\times 20^{3}}[\frac{\rho _{0}}{\lambda } ]^{2}\)
or
\(I_{0}=\frac{10}{10\pi^{2}}[\frac{\lambda }{\rho _{0}} ]^{2}\times 10^{-3}=\frac{20^{2}}{\pi^{2}}\times 10^{-3}\)
=40.53 mA
\(R_{rad}=\frac{320 \pi^{4}S^{2}}{\lambda ^{4}}=320 \pi^{6} N^{2}[\frac{\rho _{0}}{\lambda } ]^{4}\)
\(=320 \pi^{6}\times 100 [\frac{1}{20} ]^{4}=192.3 \Omega\)
\(P_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}I_{0}^{2}R_{rad}=\frac{1}{2}(40.53)^{2}\times 10^{-6}(192.3)\)
=158 mW
