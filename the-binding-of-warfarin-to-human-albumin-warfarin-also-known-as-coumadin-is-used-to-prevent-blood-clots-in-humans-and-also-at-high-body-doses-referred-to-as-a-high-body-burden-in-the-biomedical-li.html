The Binding of Warfarin to Human Albumin Warfarin, also known as coumadin, is used to prevent blood clots in humans and also, at high body doses (referred to as a high body burden in the biomedical literature), as a rat poison by promoting internal bleeding. Warfarin binds with human plasma albumin in the reaction

\(W + A \stackrel{K}{\rightleftharpoons} WA\)

The following measured or apparent (not standard-state) data at 25ºC are available for this binding reaction, \(\Delta_{ bnd } G=-30.8 kJ mol ^{-1}, \Delta_{ bnd } H=-13.1 kJ mol ^{-1}, \text { and } \Delta_{ bnd } C_{ P } \sim 0\), where these apparent changes are for the following equilibrium constant:

where M is the concentration in molality.

a. What is the entropy change for this reaction at 25ºC?

b. Starting with concentrations of warfarin and human plasma albumin of 0.0001 M, what is the fraction of unbound (unreacted) albumin over the temperature range of 0 to 50ºC?

Admin
Solution

a. Since ΔG = ΔH − TΔS, at 25ºC

 

\(\Delta S=\frac{\Delta H-\Delta G}{T}=\frac{-13100-(-30800) kJ mol ^{-1}}{298.15 K }=59.37 \frac{ J }{ mol K }\)

 

b. Since \(\Delta_{ bnd } C_{ P }=0, \Delta_{ bnd } H\)is independent of temperature, and the equilibrium constant as a function of temperature is computed from

 

\(\ln \frac{K(T)}{K(T=298.15 K )}=\int_{298.15 K }^{T} \frac{\Delta_{ bnd } H}{R T^{2}} d T=\frac{\Delta_{ bnd } H}{R} \cdot\left(\frac{1}{T}-\frac{1}{298.15}\right)\)

 

The results are given in the table that follows. Also, letting α be the fraction of the initial warfarin (or albumin) that has reacted, we have

 

\(M_{ WA }=\alpha \cdot M_{ A , o }=0.0001 \alpha \quad \text { and } \quad M_{ W }=M_{ A }=0.0001(1-\alpha)\)

 

so that

 

\(K=\frac{0.0001 \alpha}{(0.1)^{2} \cdot(1-\alpha)^{2}}=\frac{\alpha}{0.0001 \cdot(1-\alpha)^{2}}\)

 

the solutions of which are also given in the table.

 

\(T\left({ }^{\circ} C \right)\) K(T) α 1 − α
0 \(4.039 \times 10^{5}\) 0.855 0.145
10 \(3.295 \times 10^{5}\) 0.840 0.160
20 \(2.725 \times 10^{5}\) 0.826 0.174
30 \(2.282 \times 10^{5}\) 0.811 0.189
40 \(1.933 \times 10^{5}\) 0.797 0.203
50 \(1.654 \times 10^{5}\) 0.783 0.217

 

Consequently, we see that warfarin is strongly bound to human plasma albumin at all temperatures.
