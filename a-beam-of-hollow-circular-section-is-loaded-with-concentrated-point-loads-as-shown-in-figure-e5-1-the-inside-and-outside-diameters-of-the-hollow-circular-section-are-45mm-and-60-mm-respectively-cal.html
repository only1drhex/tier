Guest Question.

A beam of hollow circular section is loaded with concentrated point loads as shown in Figure E5.1. The inside and outside diameters of the hollow circular section are 45mm and 60 mm, respectively. Calculate the maximum normal stress of the beam.

Admin
Solution

For a beam with constant cross-section, the maximum normal stress and the maximum bending moment always occur on the same cross-section. A bending moment diagram is essential to determine the maximum bending moment and its location. This can be done by following the procedure described in Chapter 4.

Step 1: Compute support reactions (Figure E5.1(b)).

Taking anticlockwise moment about \(A(\curvearrowleft)\)

 

\(R_{D} \times 1.4 m-5 k N \times 0.4 m-3 k N \times 1.2 m-3 k N \times 1.7 m=0\)

 

\(R_{ D }=7.64 kN (\uparrow)\)

 

Resolving vertically:

 

\(R_{ A }+R_{ D }-5 kN -3 kN -3 kN =0\)

 

\(R_{ A }=11 kN -V_{ D }=3.36 kN (\uparrow)\)

 

Step 2: Compute the bending moments on the critical sections at A, B, C, D and E (Figure E5.1(c)).

(a) Section at A

Due to the pin support:

 

\(M_{A}=0\)

 

(b) Section at B

Taking anticlockwise moment about B:

 

\(M_{ B }-R_{ A } \times 0.4 m =0\)

 

\(M_{ B }=3.36 kN \times 0.4 m =1.34 kN m(\curvearrowleft)\)

 

(c) Section at C

Taking anticlockwise moment about C:

 

\(M_{ C }-R_{ A } \times 1.2 m +5 kN \times 0.8 m =0\)

 

\(M_{C}=3.36 kN \times 1.2 m -5 kN \times 0.8 m =0.032 kN m(\curvearrowleft)\)

 

(d) Section at D

Taking anticlockwise moment about D:

 

\(M_{ D }-R_{ A } \times 1.2 m +5 kN \times 1.0 m +3 kN \times 0.2 m =0\)

 

\(M_{ D }=3.36 kN \times 1.4 m -5 kN \times 1.0 m -3 kN \times 0.2 m\)

 

=âˆ’0.896kN m \((\curvearrowleft)\)

 

(e) Section at E

Due to the free end:

 

\(M_{E}=0\)

 

Step 3: Draw the bending moment diagram (Figure 5.1(d)).

Since there is no distributed load applied between the critical sections, the bending moment distributions between these sections are slopping lines.

It is clear from the bending moment diagram that the maximum magnitude of bending moment occurs on the section at B, that is:

 

\(M_{\max }=1.34 KN m\)

 

Thus, the maximum normal stress occurs on the same section.

Step 4: Compute second moment of inertia.

From Table 5.1 the moment of inertia of the hollow circular section:

 

\(\begin{aligned}l &=\frac{\pi}{64}\left(R_{\text {out }}^{4}-R_{\text {in }}^{4}\right)=\frac{\pi}{64}\left(D_{\text {out }}^{4}-D_{\text {in }}^{4}\right)=\frac{\pi}{64}\left(60^{4}-45^{4}\right) mm ^{4} \\&=4.35 \times 10^{-7} m ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

 

Step 5: Compute normal stresses.

From Equation (5.1),

 

\(\sigma=\frac{M y}{l}\) (5.1a)

 

\(l=\int_{A} y^{2} d A(\text { rea })\) (5.1b)

 

at B:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{\max } &=\frac{M_{\max } y_{\max }}{l}=\frac{M_{\max } D_{\text {out }} / 2}{l}=\frac{1.34 kN m \times 30 \times 10^{-3} m }{4.35 \times 10^{-7} m ^{4}} \\&=92.41 MN / m ^{2}\end{aligned}\)
