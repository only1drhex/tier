Guest Question.

AIWA Ltd. requires 7,500 cabinets for one of its niche products which it buys from a vendor for \(\text { ₹ } 350\) per unit. If the company starts making the cabinet in its manufacturing facility, it has to bear \(\text { ₹175 }\) per unit towards variable cost and \(₹ 10,00,000\) as fixed costs. Should the company buy or purchase the cabinets?

Admin
Solution

Selling price per unit (P) \(=₹ 350\)

Variable cost per unit (v) \(=₹ 175\)

Total fixed costs (TFC) \(=₹ 10,00,000\)

Using the above data and Equation (11.9), let us calculate break-even output QB

 

\(Q_{B}=\frac{T F C}{(P-v)}\) (11.9)

 

\(Q_{B}=\frac{10,00,000}{(350-175)}=5,714.29 \approx 5,715 \text{ units (approximately) }\)

 

Since the demand (7,500 units) is greater than break-even quantity, the firm should make the cabinets.
