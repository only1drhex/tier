Guest Question.

Using the Lagrangian method, derive the equations of motion for the 2-DOF robot arm shown in Figure 6.6. The center of mass for each link is at the center of the link. The moments of inertia are \( I_{1}\) and \( I_{2}\) .

Admin
Solution

The solution of this example robot arm is in fact similar to the solution of . However, in addition to a change in the coordinate frames, the two links have distributed masses, requiring the use of moments of inertia in the calculation of the kinetic energy. We follow the same steps as before. First we calculate the velocity of the center of mass of link 2 by differentiating its position:

\( x_{D} =l_{1} C_{1} +0.5l_{2} C_{12} \rightarrow \dot{x}_{D}=-l_{1}S_{1} \dot\theta _{1} -0.5l_{2}S_{12} (\dot{\theta }_{1} + \dot{\theta }_{2} )\)

 

\( y_{D} =l_{1} S_{1} +0.5l_{2} S_{12} \rightarrow \dot{y}_{D}=l_{1}C_{1} \dot\theta _{1} +0.5l_{2}C_{12} (\dot{\theta }_{1} + \dot{\theta }_{2} )\)

 

Therefore, the total velocity of the center of mass of link 2 is:

\(v^{2}_{D}=\dot x_{D}^{2}+\dot y_{D}^{2}= \dot{\theta }_{1}^{2}(l_{1}^{2}+0.25l_{2}^{2}+l_{1}l_{2}C_{2})+\dot{\theta }_{2}^{2}(0.25l_{2}^{2}) +\dot{\theta }_{1}\dot{\theta }_{2}(0.5 l_{2}^{2}+l_{1}l_{2}C_{2})\) (6.7)

 

The total kinetic energy of the system is the sum of the kinetic energies of links 1 and 2. Remembering that the kinetic energy for a link rotating about a fixed point (for link 1) and about the center of mass (for link 2) is given in Eq. (6.8), we get:

\(K=K_{1} +K_{2} =[\frac{1}{2}I_{A}\dot\theta ^{2}_{1}]+[\frac{1}{2}I_{D}(\dot{\theta }_{1} +\dot{\theta }_{2} )^{2}+\frac{1}{2}m_{2}v^{2}_{D} ]\)

 

\(=[\frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{3} m_{1}l^{ 2}_{1} )\dot\theta _{1}^{ 2} ]+[\frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{12}m_{2}l^{ 2}_{2} )(\dot\theta _{1} +\dot\theta _{2} ) +\frac{1}{2}m_{2}v^{2}_{D} ]\) (6.8)

 

Substituting Eq. (6.7) into Eq. (6.8) and rearranging, we get:

\(K=\dot{\theta }^{2}_{1}(\frac{1}{6}m_{1}l^{2}_{1}+ \frac{1}{6}m_{2}l^{2}_{2} +\frac{1}{2}m_{2}l^{2}_{1}+\frac{1}{2}m_{2}l_{1}l_{2} C_{2})\)

 

\(+\dot{\theta }^{2}_{2}(\frac{1}{6}m_{2}l^{2}_{2})+\dot{\theta }_{1}\dot{\theta }_{2}(\frac{1}{3}m_{2}l^{2}_{2}+\frac{1}{2}m_{2}l_{1}l_{2} C_{2})\) (6.9)

 

The potential energy of the system is the sum of the potential energies of the two links:

\(P=m_{1} g\frac{l_{1} }{2} S_{1} + m_{2} g (l_{1}S_{1}+\frac{l_{2} }{2}S_{12})\) (6.10)

 

The Lagrangian for the two-link robot arm is:

\(L = K âˆ’P=\dot{\theta }^{2}_{1}(\frac{1}{6}m_{1}l^{2}_{1}+ \frac{1}{6}m_{2}l^{2}_{2} +\frac{1}{2}m_{2}l^{2}_{1}+\frac{1}{2}m_{2}l_{1}l_{2} C_{2})+\dot{\theta }^{2}_{2}(\frac{1}{6}m_{2}l^{2}_{2})\)

 

\(+\dot{\theta }_{1}\dot{\theta }_{2}(\frac{1}{3}m_{2}l^{2}_{2}+\frac{1}{2}m_{2}l_{1}l_{2} C_{2})-m_{1} g\frac{l_{1} }{2} S_{1} + m_{2} g (l_{1}S_{1}+\frac{l_{2} }{2}S_{12})\)

 

Taking the derivatives of the Lagrangian and substituting the terms into Eq. (6.4) yields the following two equations of motion:

\(Ti=\frac{\partial}{\partial t}\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\theta }_{i} }\right) -\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{\theta }_{i} }\) (6.4)

 

\(T_{1} = \left(\frac{1}{3} m_{1}l^{2}_{1}+ m_{2}l^{2}_{1}+\frac{1}{3} m_{2}l^{2}_{2}+ m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}C_{2}\right) \ddot{\theta }_{1} +\left(\frac{1}{3} m_{2}l^{2}_{2}+\frac{1}{2} m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}C_{2}\right) \ddot{\theta }_{2}\)

 

\(-(m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}S_{2})\dot{\theta }_{1}\dot{\theta }_{2}-\left(\frac{1}{2} m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}S_{2}\right) \dot{\theta }_{2}^{2}+\left(\frac{1}{2}m_{1}+m_{2} \right)gl_{1} C_{1}+\frac{1}{2} m_{2}gl_{2} C_{12} \) (6.11)

 

\(T_{2} = \left(\frac{1}{3} m_{2}l^{2}_{2}+ \frac{1}{2} m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}C_{2}\right) \ddot{\theta }_{1} +\left(\frac{1}{3} m_{2}l^{2}_{2}\right) \ddot{\theta }_{2}+\left(\frac{1}{2} m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}S_{2}\right) \dot{\theta }_{1}^{2}+\frac{1}{2} m_{2}gl_{2} C_{12}\) (6.12)

 

Equations (6.11) and (6.12) can be written in matrix form as:

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} T_{1} \\ T_{2} \end{matrix} \right ] =\left [ \begin{matrix} \left(\frac{1}{3} m_{1}l^{2}_{1}+ m_{2}l^{2}_{1}+\frac{1}{3} m_{2}l^{2}_{2}+ m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}C_{2}\right) & \left(\frac{1}{3} m_{2}l^{2}_{2}+\frac{1}{2} m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}C_{2}\right) \\ \left(\frac{1}{3} m_{2}l^{2}_{2}+ \frac{1}{2} m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}C_{2}\right) & \left(\frac{1}{3} m_{2}l^{2}_{2}\right) \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} \ddot{\theta }_{1} \\ \ddot{\theta }_{2} \end{matrix} \right ]\)

 

\(+\left [ \begin{matrix} 0 &-(\frac{1}{2} m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}S_{2}) \\ (\frac{1}{2} m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}S_{2}) & 0 \end{matrix} \right ]\left [ \begin{matrix} \dot{\theta }_{1}^{2} \\ \dot{\theta }_{2}^{2} \end{matrix} \right ] +\left [ \begin{matrix}-(m_{2}l_{1}l_{2}S_{2}) & 0\\ 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} \dot{\theta }_{1} \dot{\theta }_{2} \\ \dot{\theta }_{2} \dot{\theta }_{1} \end{matrix} \right ]\) (6.13)

 

\( +\left [ \begin{matrix} \left(\frac{1}{2}m_{1}+m_{2} \right)gl_{1} C_{1}+\frac{1}{2} m_{2}gl_{2} C_{12}\\ \frac{1}{2} m_{2}gl_{2} C_{12} \end{matrix} \right ]\)
