Guest Question.

Determine the reactions and the force in each member of the truss shown in Fig. \( 17.11 (\mathrm{a})\) by the matrix stiffness method.

Admin
Solution

Degrees of Freedom. From the analytical model of the truss shown in Fig. 17.11(b), we observe that only joint 3 is free to translate. Thus the truss has two degrees of freedom, \(d_{1}\) and \(d_{2}\), which are the unknown translations of joint 3 in the \(X\) and \(Y\) directions, respectively.

Structure Stiffness Matrix.

Member 1 As shown in Fig. 17.11(b), joint 1 has been selected as the beginning joint and joint 3 as the end joint for member 1. By applying Eqs. (17.13), we determine

\(\cos \theta=\frac{X_{e}-X_{b}}{L}=\frac{X_{e}-X_{b}}{\sqrt{\left(X_{e}-X_{b}\right)^{2}+\left(Y_{e}-Y_{b}\right)^{2}}}\) (17.13a)

\(\sin \theta=\frac{Y_{e}-Y_{b}}{L}=\frac{Y_{e}-Y_{b}}{\sqrt{\left(X_{e}-X_{b}\right)^{2}+\left(Y_{e}-Y_{b}\right)^{2}}}\) (17.13b)

\( \begin{aligned} L &=\sqrt{\left(X_{3}-X_{1}\right)^{2}+\left(Y_{3}-Y_{1}\right)^{2}}=\sqrt{(15-0)^{2}+(20-0)^{2}}=25 \mathrm{ft} \\ \cos \theta &=\frac{X_{3}-X_{1}}{L}=\frac{15}{25}=0.6 \\ \sin \theta &=\frac{Y_{3}-Y_{1}}{L}=\frac{20}{25}=0.8 \end{aligned} \)

The member stiffness matrix in global coordinates can now be evaluated by using Eq. (17.29)

\(\mathbf{K}=\frac{E A}{L}\left[\begin{array}{cccc}\cos ^{2} \theta & \cos \theta \sin \theta & -\cos ^{2} \theta & -\cos \theta \sin \theta \\ \cos \theta \sin \theta & \sin ^{2} \theta & -\cos \theta \sin \theta & -\sin ^{2} \theta \\ -\cos ^{2} \theta & -\cos \theta \sin \theta & \cos ^{2} \theta & \cos \theta \sin \theta \\ -\cos \theta \sin \theta & -\sin ^{2} \theta & \cos \theta \sin \theta & \sin ^{2} \theta\end{array}\right]\) (17.29)

\( \mathbf{K}_{1}=\frac{(29,000)(9)}{(25)(12)}\left[\begin{array}{rrrr} 0.36 & 0.48 & -0.36 & -0.48 \\ 0.48 & 0.64 & -0.48 & -0.64 \\ -0.36 & -0.48 & 0.36 & 0.48 \\ -0.48 & -0.64 & 0.48 & 0.64 \end{array}\right] \)

or

(1)

From Fig. 17.11(b), we observe that the displacements of the beginning joint 1 for the member are zero, whereas the displacements of the end joint 3 are \(d_{1}\) and \(d_{2}\). Thus the structure degree of freedom numbers for this member are \(0,0,1,2\). These numbers are written on the right side and at the top of \(\mathbf{K}_{1}\) (see Eq. (1)) to indicate the rows and columns, respectively, of the structure stiffness matrix \(\mathbf{S}\), where the elements of \(\mathbf{K}_{1}\) must be stored. Note that the elements of \(\mathbf{K}_{1}\), which correspond to the zero structure degree of freedom number, are simply disregarded. Thus, the element in row 3 and column 3 of \(\mathbf{K}_{1}\) is stored in row 1 and column 1 of \(\mathbf{S}\), as shown in Fig. 17.11(c). Similarly, the element in row 3 and column 4 of \(\mathbf{K}_{1}\) is stored in row 1 and column 2 of \(\mathbf{S}\). The remaining elements of \(\mathbf{K}_{1}\) are stored in \(\mathbf{S}\) in a similar manner (Fig. 17.11(c)).

Member 2 From Fig. 17.11(b), we can see that joint 2 is the beginning joint and joint 3 is the end joint for member 2 . By applying Eqs. (17.13), we obtain

\(\cos \theta=\frac{X_{e}-X_{b}}{L}=\frac{X_{e}-X_{b}}{\sqrt{\left(X_{e}-X_{b}\right)^{2}+\left(Y_{e}-Y_{b}\right)^{2}}}\) (17.13a)

\(\sin \theta=\frac{Y_{e}-Y_{b}}{L}=\frac{Y_{e}-Y_{b}}{\sqrt{\left(X_{e}-X_{b}\right)^{2}+\left(Y_{e}-Y_{b}\right)^{2}}}\) (17.13b)

\( \begin{array}{l} \cos \theta=\frac{X_{3}-X_{2}}{L}=\frac{15-15}{20}=0 \\ \sin \theta=\frac{Y_{3}-Y_{2}}{L}=\frac{20-0}{20}=1 \end{array} \)

Thus, by using Eq. (17.29)

\(\mathbf{K}=\frac{E A}{L}\left[\begin{array}{cccc}\cos ^{2} \theta & \cos \theta \sin \theta & -\cos ^{2} \theta & -\cos \theta \sin \theta \\ \cos \theta \sin \theta & \sin ^{2} \theta & -\cos \theta \sin \theta & -\sin ^{2} \theta \\ -\cos ^{2} \theta & -\cos \theta \sin \theta & \cos ^{2} \theta & \cos \theta \sin \theta \\ -\cos \theta \sin \theta & -\sin ^{2} \theta & \cos \theta \sin \theta & \sin ^{2} \theta\end{array}\right]\) (17.29)

From Fig. 17.11(b), we can see that the structure degree of freedom numbers for this member are \(0,0,1,2\). These numbers are used to store the pertinent elements of \(\mathbf{K}_{2}\) in their proper positions in the structure stiffness matrix \(\mathbf{S}\), as shown in Fig. 17.11(c).

Member 3 \( cos \theta=1 \quad \sin \theta=0\)

By using Eq. (17.29),

\(\mathbf{K}=\frac{E A}{L}\left[\begin{array}{cccc}\cos ^{2} \theta & \cos \theta \sin \theta & -\cos ^{2} \theta & -\cos \theta \sin \theta \\ \cos \theta \sin \theta & \sin ^{2} \theta & -\cos \theta \sin \theta & -\sin ^{2} \theta \\ -\cos ^{2} \theta & -\cos \theta \sin \theta & \cos ^{2} \theta & \cos \theta \sin \theta \\ -\cos \theta \sin \theta & -\sin ^{2} \theta & \cos \theta \sin \theta & \sin ^{2} \theta\end{array}\right]\) (17.29)

The structure degree of freedom numbers for this member are \(0,0,1,2\). By using these numbers, the elements of \(\mathbf{K}_{3}\) are stored in \(\mathbf{S}\), as shown in Fig. \(17.11(\mathrm{c}) .\)

Note that the structure stiffness matrix S (Fig. 17.11(c)), obtained by assembling the stiffness coefficients of the three members, is symmetric.

Joint Load Vector. By comparing Fig. 17.11(a) and (b), we realize that

\( P_{1}=100 \cos 60^{\circ}=50 \mathrm{k} \quad P_{2}=-100 \sin 60^{\circ}=-86.6 \mathrm{k} \)

Thus the joint load vector is

\( \mathbf{P}=\left[\begin{array}{c} 50 \\ -86.6 \end{array}\right] \) (2)

Joint Displacements. The stiffness relations for the entire truss can be expressed as (Eq. (17.41) with \(\mathbf{P}_{f}=0\) )

\(\mathbf{P}-\mathbf{P}_{f}=\mathbf{S d}\) (17.41)

\( \mathbf{P}=\mathbf{S d} \) (3)

By substituting P from Eq. (2) and S from Fig. 17.11(c), we write Eq. (3) in expanded form as

\( \left[\begin{array}{c} 50 \\ -86.6 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{rr} 1,763.2 & 417.6 \\ 417.6 & 1,644.3 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} d_{1} \\ d_{2} \end{array}\right] \)

By solving these equations simultaneously, we determine the joint displacements to be

\( d_{1}=0.0434 \text { in. } \quad d_{2}=-0.0637 \text { in. } \)

or

\( \mathbf{d}=\left[\begin{array}{r} 0.0434 \\ -0.0637 \end{array}\right] \text { in } \)

Member End Displacements and End Forces.

Member 1 The member end displacements in global coordinates, \(\mathbf{v}\), can be obtained by simply comparing the member’s global degree of freedom numbers with the structure degree of freedom numbers for the member, as follows:

\( \mathbf{v}_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{l} v_{1} \\ v_{2} \\ v_{3} \\ v_{4} \end{array}\right] \begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{array}=\left[\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ d_{1} \\ d_{2} \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0.0434 \\ -0.0637 \end{array}\right] \text { in } \) (4)

Note that the structure degree of freedom numbers for the member \((0,0,1,2)\) are written on the right side of \(\mathbf{v}\), as shown in Eq. (4). Since the structure degree of freedom numbers corresponding to \(v_{1}\) and \(v_{2}\) are zero, this indicates that \(v_{1}=v_{2}=0\). Similarly, the numbers 1 and 2 corresponding to \(v_{3}\) and \(v_{4}\), respectively, indicate that \(v_{3}=d_{1}\) and \(v_{4}=d_{2}\). It should be realized that these compatibility equations could have been established alternatively simply by a visual inspection of the line diagram of the structure (Fig. 17.11(b)). However, the use of the structure degree of freedom numbers enables us conveniently to program this procedure on a computer.

The member end displacements in local coordinates can now be determined by using the relationship \(\mathbf{u}=\mathbf{T} \mathbf{v}\) (E q .(17.14)), with \(\mathbf{T}\) as defined in Eq. (17.22):

\(\mathbf{u}=\mathbf{T v}\) (17.14)

\(\mathbf{T}=\left[\begin{array}{cccc}\cos \theta & \sin \theta & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & \cos \theta & \sin \theta\end{array}\right]\) (17.22)

\( \mathbf{u}_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{l} u_{1} \\ u_{2} \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{llll} 0.6 & 0.8 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0.6 & 0.8 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0.0434 \\ -0.0637 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -0.0249 \end{array}\right] \mathrm{in} . \)

By using Eq. (17.7), we compute member end forces in local coordinates as

\( \begin{array}{l} \mathbf{Q}=\mathbf{k u} \\\end{array} \) (17.7)

\( \begin{array}{l} \mathbf{Q}_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{l} Q_{1} \\ Q_{2} \end{array}\right]=870\left[\begin{array}{rr} 1 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -0.0249 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{r} 21.66 \\ -21.66 \end{array}\right] \mathrm{k} \end{array} \)

Thus, as shown in Fig. 17.11(d), the axial force in member 1 is

\(21.66 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{C})\)

By applying Eq. (17.17), we can determine member end forces in global coordinates as

\(\mathbf{F}=\mathbf{T}^{T} \mathbf{Q}\) (17.17)

\( \begin{array}{l} \mathbf{F}_{1}=\left[\begin{array}{l} F_{1} \\ F_{2} \\ F_{3} \\ F_{4} \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ll} 0.6 & 0 \\ 0.8 & 0 \\ 0 & 0.6 \\ 0 & 0.8 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{r} 21.66 \\ -21.66 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l} 13 \\ 17.33 \\ -13 \\ -17.33 \end{array}\right] \mathrm{k} \end{array} \)

Member 2 The member end displacements in global coordinates are given by

\( \mathbf{v}_{2}=\left[\begin{array}{l} v_{1} \\ v_{2} \\ v_{3} \\ v_{4} \end{array}\right] \begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{array}=\left[\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ d_{1} \\ d_{2} \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0.0434 \\ -0.0637 \end{array}\right] \text { in. } \)

By using the relationship \(\mathbf{u}=\mathbf{T v}\), we determine the member end displacements in local coordinates to be

\( \mathbf{u}_{2}=\left[\begin{array}{l} u_{1} \\ u_{2} \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{llll} 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0.0434 \\ -0.637 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -0.0637 \end{array}\right] \text { in. } \)

Next, the member end forces in local coordinates are computed by using the relationship \(\mathbf{Q}=\mathbf{k u}:\)

\( \mathbf{Q}_{2}=\left[\begin{array}{l} Q_{1} \\ Q_{2} \end{array}\right]=1,087.5\left[\begin{array}{rr} 1 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ -0.0637 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{r} 69.27 \\ -69.27 \end{array}\right] \mathrm{k} \)

Thus, as shown in Fig. 17.11(d), the axial force in member 2 is

\( 69.27 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{C}) \)

By using the relationship \(\mathbf{F}=\mathbf{T}^{T} \mathbf{Q}\), we calculate the member end forces in global coordinates to be

\( \mathbf{F}_{2}=\left[\begin{array}{l} F_{1} \\ F_{2} \\ F_{3} \\ F_{4} \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ll} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{r} 69.27 \\ -69.27 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 69.27 \\ 0 \\ -69.27 \end{array}\right] \mathrm{k} \)

Member 3

\( \begin{array}{l} \mathbf{v}_{3}=\left[\begin{array}{l} v_{1} \\ v_{2} \\ v_{3} \\ v_{4} \end{array}\right] \begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{array}=\left[\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ d_{1} \\ d_{2} \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0.0434 \\ -0.0637 \end{array}\right] \mathrm{in} . \\ \mathbf{u}=\mathbf{T v} \end{array} \)

\(\mathbf{u}_{3}=\left[\begin{array}{l}u_{1} \\ u_{2}\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{llll}1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 0\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}0 \\ 0 \\ 0.0434 \\ -0.0637\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{l}0 \\ 0.0434\end{array}\right] \mathrm{in} .\)

\(\mathbf{Q}=\mathbf{k u}\)

\(\mathbf{Q}_{3}=\left[\begin{array}{l}Q_{1} \\ Q_{2}\end{array}\right]=1,450\left[\begin{array}{rr}1 & -1 \\ -1 & 1\end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{l}0 \\ 0.0434\end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{r}-62.93 \\ 62.93\end{array}\right] \mathrm{k}\)

Thus, the axial force in member 3 is (Fig. 17.11(d))

\(62.93 \mathrm{k}(\mathrm{T}) \quad\)

\( \begin{array}{l} \mathbf{F}=\mathbf{T}^{T} \mathbf{Q} \\ \mathbf{F}_{3}=\left[\begin{array}{l} F_{1} \\ F_{2} \\ F_{3} \\ F_{4} \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{ll} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]\left[\begin{array}{r} -62.93 \\ 62.93 \end{array}\right]=\left[\begin{array}{c} -62.93 \\ 0 \\ 62.93 \\ 0 \end{array}\right] \mathrm{k} \end{array} \)

Support Reactions. As shown in Fig. 17.11(e), the reactions at the support joints 1,2, and 4 are equal to the forces in global coordinates at the ends of the members connected to these joints.
