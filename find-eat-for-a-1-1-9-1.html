Guest Question.

Find \(e^{ A t} \text { for } A =\left[\begin{array}{ll}1 & 1 \\9 & 1\end{array}\right]\).

Admin
Solution

Here n = 2. From Eq. (16.3),

\(e^{ A t}=\alpha_{1} A t+\alpha_{0} I =\left[\begin{array}{cc}\alpha_{1} t+\alpha_{0} & \alpha_{1} t \\9 \alpha_{1} t & \alpha_{1} t+\alpha_{0}\end{array}\right]\) (1)

and from Eq. (16.5), \(r(\lambda)=\alpha_{1} \lambda+\alpha_{0}\). The eigenvalues of At are \(\lambda_{1}=4 t \text { and } \lambda_{2}=-2 t\), which are both of multiplicity one. Substituting these values successively into Eq. (16.6), we obtain the two equations

\(\begin{gathered}e^{4 t}=4 t \alpha_{1}+\alpha_{0} \\e^{-2 t}=-2 t \alpha_{1}+\alpha_{0}\end{gathered}\)

Solving these equations for \(\alpha_{1} \text { and } \alpha_{0}\), we find that

\(\alpha_{1}=\frac{1}{6 t}\left(e^{4 t}-e^{-2 t}\right) \quad \text { and } \quad \alpha_{0}=\frac{1}{3}\left(e^{4 t}+2 e^{-2 t}\right)\)

Substituting these values into (1) and simplifying, we have

\(e^{ A t}=\frac{1}{6}\left[\begin{array}{cc}3 e^{4 i}+3 e^{-2 i} & e^{4 i}-e^{-2 i} \\9 e^{4 t}-9 e^{-2 t} & 3 e^{4 i}+3 e^{-2 t}\end{array}\right]\)
