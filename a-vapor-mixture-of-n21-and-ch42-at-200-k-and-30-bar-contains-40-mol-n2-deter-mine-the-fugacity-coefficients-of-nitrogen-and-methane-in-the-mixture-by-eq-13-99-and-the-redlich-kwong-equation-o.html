Guest Question.

A vapor mixture of \(N_{2}(1)\) and \(CH_{4}(2)\) at 200 K and 30 bar contains \(40 mol-\% N_{2}.\) Deter-mine the fugacity coefficients of nitrogen and methane in the mixture by Eq. (13.99) and the Redlich/Kwong equation of state.

\( \ln \hat{\phi }_{i}=\frac{b_{i}}{b}(Z-1)-\ln (Z-\beta )-\bar{q}_{i}I\) (13.99)

 

Admin
Solution

For the Redlich/Kwong equation, \(\varepsilon = 0\) and \(\sigma = 1,\) and Eq. (13.87) becomes:

\( Z^{\nu }=1+\beta ^{\nu }-q^{\nu }\beta ^{\nu }\frac{Z^{\nu }-\beta ^{\nu }}{(Z^{\nu }+\varepsilon \beta ^{\nu })(Z^{\nu }+\sigma \beta ^{\nu })}\) (13.87)

 

\( Z=1+\beta -q\beta \frac{Z-\beta }{Z(Z+\beta )}\) (A)

where \(\beta\) and q are given by Eqs. (13.89) and (13.90). Superscripts are here omitted because all calculations are for a vapor phase. The mixing rules most commonly used with the Redlich/Kwong equation for parameters a(T ) and b are given by Eqs. (13.91) through (13.93). For a binary mixture they become:

\( \beta^{p}\equiv \frac{b^{p}P}{RT} (p=l,\nu)\) (13.89)

\( q^{p}\equiv \frac{a^{p}}{b^{p}RT} (p=l,\nu )\) (13.90)

\( b=\sum\limits_{i}{x_{i}b_{i}}\) (13.91)

\( a_{ij}=(a_{i}a_{j})^{1/2}\) (13.93)

 

\( a=y^{2}_{1}a_{1}+2y_{1}y_{2}\sqrt{a_{1}a_{2}}+y_{2}^{2}a_{2}\) (B)

\( b=y_{1}b_{1}+y_{2}b_{2}\) (C)

In Eq. (B), \(a_{1}\) and \(a_{2}\) are pure-species parameters given by Eq. (13.79) written for the Redlich/Kwong equation:

\( a_{i}(T)=\Psi \frac{\alpha (T_{r_{i}})R^{2}T^{2}_{c_{i}}}{P_{c_{i}}}\) (13.79)

 

\( a_{i}=0.42748\frac{T_{r_{i}}^{-1/2}(83.14)^{2}T_{c_{i}}^{2}}{P_{c_{i}}}bar\cdot cm^{6}\cdot mol^{-2}\) (D)

In Eq. (C), \(b_{1}\) and \(b_{2}\) are pure-species parameters, given by Eq. (13.80):

\( b_{i}=\Omega \frac{RT_{c_{i}}}{P_{c_{i}}}\) (13.80)

 

\( b_{i}=0.08664\frac{83.14T_{c_{i}}}{P_{c_{i}}}cm^{3}\cdot mol^{-1}\) (E)

Critical constants for nitrogen and methane from of App. B and calculated
values for \(b_{i}\) and \(a_{i}\) from Eqs. (D) and (E) are:

\(10^{-5}a_{i}\) \(b_{i}\) \(P_{c_{i}}/bar\) \(T_{r_{i}}\) \(T_{c_{i}}/K\)
10.995 26.737 34.00 1.5848 126.2 \(N_{2}(1)\)
22.786 29.853 45.99 1.0493 190.6 \(CH_{4}(2)\)

Mixture parameters by Eqs. (B), (C), and (13.90) are:

\(a = 17.560 \times 10^{5} bar\cdot cm^{6}\cdot mol^{−2} b = 28.607 cm^{3}\cdot mol^{−1} q = 3.6916\)

Equation (A) becomes:

\( Z=1+\beta -3.6916\frac{\beta (Z-\beta )}{Z(Z+\beta )}\) with \(\beta =0.051612\)

where \(\beta\) comes from Eq. (13.89). Solution yields Z = 0.85393. Moreover, Eq. (13.72) reduces to:

\( I=\frac{1}{\sigma -\varepsilon }\ln \left(\frac{Z+\sigma \beta }{Z+\varepsilon \beta } \right)\) (13.72)

 

\( I=\ln \frac{Z+\beta }{Z}=0.05868\)

Application of Eq. (13.94) to Eq. (B) yields:

\( \bar{a}_{i}\equiv \left[\frac{\partial (na)}{\partial n_{i}} \right]_{T,n_{j}}\) (13.94)

 

\( \bar{a}_{1}= \left[\frac{\partial (na)}{\partial n_{1}} \right]_{T,n_{2}}=2y_{1}a_{1}+2y_{2}\sqrt{a_{1}a_{2}}-a\)

 

\( \bar{a}_{2}= \left[\frac{\partial (na)}{\partial n_{2}} \right]_{T,n_{1}}=2y_{2}a_{2}+2y_{1}\sqrt{a_{1}a_{2}}-a\)

By Eq. (13.95) applied to Eq. (C),

\( \bar{b}_{i}\equiv \left[\frac{\partial (nb)}{\partial n_{i}} \right]_{T,n_{j}}\) (13.95)

 

\(\bar{b}_{i}= \left[\frac{\partial (nb)}{\partial n_{1}} \right]_{T,n_{2}}=b_{1} \bar{b}_{2}= \left[\frac{\partial (nb)}{\partial n_{2}} \right]_{T,n_{1}}=b_{2}\)

Whence, by Eq. (13.101):

\( nq=\frac{n(n a)}{R T(n b)} \)

 

Whence, \( \bar{q}_{i}\equiv \left[\frac{\partial (nq)}{\partial n_{j}} \right]_{T,n_{j}}=q\left(1+\frac{\bar{a}_{i}}{a}-\frac{\bar{b}_{i} }{b} \right)=q\left(1+\frac{\bar{a} _{i}}{a}-\frac{\bar{b}_{i} }{b} \right)\) (13.101)

 

\( \bar{q}_{1}=q\left(\frac{2y_{1}a_{1}+2y_{2}\sqrt{a_{1}a_{2}} }{a}-\frac{b_{1}}{b} \right)\) (F)

\( \bar{q}_{2}=q\left(\frac{2y_{2}a_{2}+2y_{1}\sqrt{a_{1}a_{2}} }{a}-\frac{b_{2}}{b} \right)\) (G)

Substitution of numerical values into these equations and into Eq. (13.99) leads to
the following results:

\( \hat{\phi }_{i}\) \(\ln \hat{\phi }_{i}\) \(\bar{q}_{i}\)
0.94493 − 0.05664 2.39194 \(N_{2}(1)\)
0.81901 − 0.19966 4.55795 \(CH_{4}(2)\)
