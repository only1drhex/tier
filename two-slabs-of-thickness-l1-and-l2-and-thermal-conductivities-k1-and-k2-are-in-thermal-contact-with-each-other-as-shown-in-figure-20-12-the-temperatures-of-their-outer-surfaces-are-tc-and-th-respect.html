Guest Question.

Two slabs of thickness \( L_{1} \text { and } L_{2} \) and thermal conductivities \( k_{1} \text { and } k_{2} \) are in thermal contact with each other, as shown in Figure 20.12. The temperatures of their outer surfaces are \( T_{c} \text { and } T_{h} \text {, respectively, and } T_{h}>T_{c} \text {. } \) Determine the temperature at the interface and the rate of energy transfer by conduction through the slabs in the steady-state condition.

Admin
Solution

To conceptualize this problem, notice the phrase “in the steady-state condition.” We interpret this to mean that energy transfers through the compound slab at the same rate at all points. Otherwise, energy would be building up or disappearing at some point. Furthermore, the temperature will vary with position in the two slabs, most likely at different rates in each part of the compound slab. Thus, there will be some fixed temperature T at the interface when the system is in steady state. We categorize this as a thermal conduction problem and impose the condition that the power is the same in both slabs of material. To analyze the problem, we use Equation 20.15 to express the rate at which energy is transferred through slab 1:

\( P =k A\left(\frac{T_{h}-T_{c}}{L}\right) \). (20.15).

(1) \( P _{1}=k_{1} A\left(\frac{T-T_{c}}{L_{1}}\right) \).

The rate at which energy is transferred through slab 2 is

(2) \( P _{2}=k_{2} A\left(\frac{T_{h}-T}{L_{2}}\right) \).

When a steady state is reached, these two rates must be equal; hence,

\( k_{1} A\left(\frac{T-T_{c}}{L_{1}}\right)=k_{2} A\left(\frac{T_{h}-T}{L_{2}}\right) \).

Solving for T gives

(3) \( T=\frac{k_{1} L_{2} T_{c}+k_{2} L_{1} T_{h}}{k_{1} L_{2}+k_{2} L_{1}} \).

Substituting Equation (3) into either Equation (1) or Equation (2), we obtain

(4) \( P =\frac{A\left(T_{h}-T_{c}\right)}{\left(L_{1} / k_{1}\right)+\left(L_{2} / k_{2}\right)} \).

To finalize this problem, note that extension of this procedure to several slabs of materials leads to Equation 20.16.

\( P =\frac{A\left(T_{h}-T_{c}\right)}{\sum_{i}\left(L_{i} / k_{i}\right)} \) (20.16).
