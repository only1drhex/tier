Entropy Change of a Substance in a Tank
A rigid tank contains 5 kg of refrigerant-134a initially at 20째C and 140 kPa. The refrigerant is now cooled while being stirred until its pressure drops to 100 kPa. Determine the entropy change of the refrigerant during this process.

Admin
Solution

The refrigerant in a rigid tank is cooled while being stirred. The entropy change of the refrigerant is to be determined.

Assumptions The volume of the tank is constant and thus \(v_{2}=v_{1}\).

Analysis We take the refrigerant in the tank as the system (Fig. 7-12). This is a closed system since no mass crosses the system boundary during the process. We note that the change in entropy of a substance during a process is simply the difference between the entropy values at the final and initial states. The initial state of the refrigerant is completely specified.

Recognizing that the specific volume remains constant during this process, the properties of the refrigerant at both states are

 

State 1: \(\left.\begin{array}{l}P_{1}=140 \mathrm{kPa} \\\quad\\T_{1}=20^{\circ} \mathrm{C}\end{array}\right\} \quad\quad\begin{array}{l}s_{1}=1.0624 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K} \\\quad\\ v_{1}=0.16544 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg}\end{array}\)

 

State 2:\(\left.\begin{array}{l}P_{2}=100 \mathrm{kPa} \\\quad\\ \left(v_{2}=v_{1}\right)\end{array}\right\} \qquad \begin{array}{l}v_{f}=0.0007259 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg} \\\quad\\ v_{g}=0.19254 \mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg}\end{array}\)

 

 

The refrigerant is a saturated liquid-vapor mixture at the final state since \(v_{f}<v_{2}<v_{g}\) at 100 kPa pressure. Therefore, we need to determine the quality first:

\(x_{2}=\frac{v_{2}-v_{f}}{v_{f g}}=\frac{0.16544-0.0007259}{0.19254-0.0007259}=0.859\)

 

Thus,

\(s_{2}=s_{f}+x_{2} s_{f g}=0.07188+(0.859)(0.87995)=0.8278 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}\)

 

Then, the entropy change of the refrigerant during this process is

\(\Delta S =m\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)=(5 \mathrm{~kg})(0.8278-1.0624) \mathrm{kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}\) =-1.173 kJ/K

 

Discussion The negative sign indicates that the entropy of the system is decreasing during this process. This is not a violation of the second law, however, since it is the entropy generation \(S_{\text {gen }}\) that cannot be negative.
