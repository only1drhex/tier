Guest Question.

The bank of a canal has the profile shown in Figure 10.11. The material is a homogenous clay with a unit weight of \(20 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\), undrained cohesion of \(30 \mathrm{kPa}\), and it is fully undrained; hence, assume \(\phi_{u}=0\). For the trial slip circle shown, the area \(A B C D\) is \(150 \mathrm{~m}^{2}\) and the centroid is at \(\mathrm{W}_{1} .\) A tension crack (CD) of \(2.5 \mathrm{~m}\) deep was also observed. Calculate the FOS for two different scenarios shown in Figure 10.11 and Table 10.1.

Table 10.1 Scenarios
Scenario Water level in the canal Depth of the water level inside the tension crack (m)
(a) Full – 7.5 m Full – 2.5 m
(b) Empty Full – 2.5 m
Admin
Solution

a. When the canal is full, the soil can be considered to be submerged, and the submerged unit weight is then used to determine the factor of safety. For this condition, the water pressure in the tension crack is balanced by the water pressure of the water in the canal. Considering \(1 \mathrm{~m}\) run of the bank and taking moments about Point \(\mathrm{O}\) :

Factor of safety \((\mathrm{FOS})=\left(\sum\right.\) mobilized resisting moments \() /(\Sigma\) disturbing moments \()\)

where

\(\Sigma\) Disturbing moments \(=W \bar{x}\)

\(\Sigma\) Disturbing moments \(=(20-9.81) \times 150 \times 2.5=3821.25 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}\)

\(\Sigma\) mobilized resisting moments \(=C_{u} \times A D \times R\)

\(\sum\) mobilised resisting moments \(=30 \times 14 \times \frac{\pi \times 80}{180} \times 14=8210.03 \mathrm{kN} . \mathrm{m}\)

Then

Factor of safety \((F)=\frac{8210.03}{3821.25}=2.15\)

b. When the canal is empty, the saturated unit weight is used instead. In addition, the water pressure in the tension crack adds to the disturbing moment component; thus:

\(\Sigma\) Disturbing moments \(=W \bar{x}+P_{w} \bar{y}\)

\(\Sigma\) Disturbing moments \(=20 \times 150 \times 2.5+9.81 \times \frac{2.5^{2}}{2} \times\left(5.5+\frac{2}{3} \times 2.5\right)\)

\(=7500+219.70=7719.7\) kN.m

The mobilized resisting moments remain the same as follows:

\(\Sigma\) mobilized resisting moments \(=C_{u} \times A D \times R\)

\(\Sigma\) mobilised resisting moments \(=30 \times 14 \times \frac{\pi \times 80}{180} \times 14=8210.03\) kN.m

Then

Factor of safety \((F)=\frac{8210.03}{7719.7}=1.06\)
