Guest Question.

At a certain state-supported university, annual tuition and fees have risen dramatically in recent years as shown in the table below. (4.6)

Year Tuition and Fees Consumer Price
Index
1982–1983 $827 96.5
1987–1988 $1,404 113.6
1993–1994 $2,018 144.5
2003–2004 $4,450 184.0
2005–2006 $5,290 198.1 (est.)
Source: www.bls.gov.
Admin
Solution

(a) \(\$ 5,290=\$ 827(F / P, i \%, 23) \quad i=8.4 \%\) per year

(b) \(\$ 5,290=\$ 2,018(F / P, i \%, 12) \quad i=8.36 \%\) per year

(c) 1982-2005: \(198.1=96.5(1+i)^{23} \quad i=3.18 \%\) per year

1993-2005: \(198.1=144.5(1+i)^{12} \quad i=2.66 \%\) per year

The cost of tuition and fees has risen \(2.6\) times faster than the CPI for the 1993-2005 time period. Over the 1982-2005 period, they have risen more than three times as fast.
