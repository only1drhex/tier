Centrifugal Pump Performance Based on Inlet/Outlet Velocities

GIVEN Water is pumped at the rate of 1400 rpm through a centrifugal pump operating at a speed of 1750 rpm. The impeller has a uniform blade height, b, of 2 in. with \(r_{1}=1.9\) in. and \(r_{2}=7.0\) in., and the exit blade angle \(\beta_{2}\) is 23° (see Fig. 12.8). Assume ideal flow conditions and that the tangential velocity component, \(V_{\theta 1}\), of the water entering the blade is zero \(\left(\alpha_{1}=90^{\circ}\right)\).

FIND Determine (a) the tangential velocity component, \(V_{\theta 2}\), at the exit, (b) the ideal head rise, \(h_{i}\), and (c) the power, \(\dot{W}_{\text {shaft }}\), transferred to the fluid. Discuss the difference between ideal and actual head rise. Is the power, \(\dot{W}_{\text {shaft }}\), ideal or actual? Explain.

Admin
Solution

(a) At the exit the velocity diagram is as shown in Fig. 12.8c, where \(V _{2}\) is the absolute velocity of the fluid, \(W _{2}\) is the relative velocity, and \(U _{2}\) is the tip velocity of the impeller with

 

\(\begin{aligned}U_{2} &=r_{2} \omega=(7 / 12 ft )(2 \pi rad / rev ) \frac{(1750 rpm )}{(60 s / min )} \\&=107 ft / s\end{aligned}\)

 

Since the flowrate is given, it follows that

 

\(Q=2 \pi r_{2} b_{2} V_{r 2}\)

 

or

 

\(\begin{aligned}V_{r 2} &=\frac{Q}{2 \pi r_{2} b_{2}} \\&=\frac{1400 gpm }{\left(7.48 gal / ft ^{3}\right)(60 s / min )(2 \pi)(7 / 12 ft )(2 / 12 ft )} \\&=5.11 ft / s\end{aligned}\)

 

From Fig. 12.8c we see that

 

\(\cot \beta_{2}=\frac{U_{2}-V_{\theta 2}}{V_{r 2}}\)

 

so that

 

\(\begin{aligned}V_{\theta 2} &=U_{2}-V_{r 2} \cot \beta_{2} \\&=\left(107-5.11 \cot 23^{\circ}\right) ft / s \\&=95.0 ft / s\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) From Eq. 12.15 the ideal head rise is given by

 

\(h_{i}=\frac{U_{2} V_{\theta 2}}{g}\) (12.15)

 

\(\begin{aligned}h_{i} &=\frac{U_{2} V_{\theta 2}}{g}=\frac{(107 ft / s )(95.0 ft / s )}{32.2 ft / s ^{2}} \\&=316 ft\end{aligned}\)

 

Alternatively, from Eq. 12.16, the ideal head rise is

 

\(h_{i}=\frac{U_{2}^{2}}{g}-\frac{U_{2} V_{r 2} \cot \beta_{2}}{g}\)

 

\(h_{i}=\frac{U_{2}^{2}}{g}-\frac{U_{2} V_{r 2} \cot \beta_{2}}{g}\) (12.16)

 

\(\begin{array}{l}=\frac{(107 ft / s )^{2}}{32.2 ft / s ^{2}}-\frac{(107 ft / s )(5.11 ft / s ) \cot 23^{\circ}}{32.2 ft / s ^{2}} \\=316 ft\end{array}\)

 

(c) From Eq. 12.11, with \(V_{\theta 1}=0\), the power transferred to the fluid is given by the equation

 

\(\dot{W}_{\text {shaft }}=\rho Q\left(U_{2} V_{\theta 2}-U_{1} V_{\theta 1}\right)\) (12.11)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{W}_{\text {shaft }} &=\rho Q U_{2} V_{\theta 2} \\&=\frac{\left(1.94 slugs / ft ^{3}\right)(1400 gpm )(107 ft / s )(95.0 ft / s )}{\left[1\left( slug \cdot ft / s ^{2}\right) / 1 b \right]\left(7.48 gal / ft ^{3}\right)(60 s / min )} \\&=(61,500 ft \cdot lb / s )(1 hp / 550 ft \cdot lb / s )=112 hp\end{aligned}\)

 

Note that the ideal head rise and the power transferred to the fluid are related through the relationship

 

\(\dot{W}_{\text {shaft }}=\rho g Q h_{i}\)

 

COMMENT It should be emphasized that results given in the previous equation involve the ideal head rise. The actual head-rise performance characteristics of a pump are usually determined by experimental measurements obtained in a testing laboratory. The actual head rise is always less than the ideal head rise for a specific flowrate because of the loss of available energy associated with actual flows. Also, it is important to note that even if actual values of \(U_{2} \text { and } V_{r 2}\) are used in Eq. 12.16, the ideal head rise is calculated. The only idealization used in this example problem is that the exit flow angle is identical to the blade angle at the exit. If the actual exit flow angle was made available in this example, it could have been used in Eq. 12.16 to calculate the ideal head rise.

The pump power, \(\dot{W}_{\text {shaft }}\), is the actual power required to achieve a blade speed of 107 ft/s, a flowrate of 1400 rpm, and the tangential velocity, \(V_{\theta 2}\), associated with this example. If pump losses could somehow be reduced to zero (every pump designer’s dream), the actual and ideal head rise would have been identical at 316 ft. As is, the ideal head rise is 316 ft and the actual head rise something less.
