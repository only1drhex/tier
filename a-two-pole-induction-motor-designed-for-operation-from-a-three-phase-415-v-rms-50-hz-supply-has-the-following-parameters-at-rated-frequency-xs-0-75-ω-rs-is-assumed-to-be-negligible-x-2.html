Guest Question.

A two pole induction motor designed for operation from a three-phase 415 V (rms) 50 Hz supply has the following parameters at rated frequency:

\(X_s = 0.75 \Omega ; R_s\) is assumed to be negligible; \(X^′_r = 0.7 \Omega ; R^′_r = 0.07 \Omega\); and \(L_m\) is assumed to be large.

The motor is to be operated at variable speed from a three-phase inverter which operates in open loop with constant voltage to frequency ratio (\(\widetilde{V}_s/f_s\) = constant).

Perform the following:

a) Calculate the motor torque and current when operating at a slip of 0.2 and with a supply frequency half of the rated value.

b) If the supply frequency is rapidly increased to the rated value, calculate the values of motor torque and current immediately after the step change in frequency.

c) Comment on the behavior of the motor in question (b), and how can be improved.

Admin
Solution

a) The parameters of the motor, when operating at half frequency will be:

\(X_s =\frac{0.75}{2} =0.375 \Omega\) \(X^′_r=0.35 \Omega\) \(R^′_r = 0.07 \Omega\)

The motor is operating through an inverter with constant ratio \(\frac{\widetilde{V}_s}{f_s} = \frac{415}{50} =4.79\).

Since the supply frequency is half of the rated value the inverter to keep the same \(\widetilde{V}_s/f_s\) the value of \(\widetilde{V}_s\) has to be reduced to half, \(\widetilde{V}_s = 415/2 =207.5V\).

Therefore, the following results are obtained:

\(n_{e(f/2)}\) = rotational synchronous speed =\(\frac{120f}{P} = \frac{120(25)}{2}=1500 rpm\)

 

\(\omega_{e(f/2)}\) = synchronous frequency =\(\frac{4\pi f}{P} = \frac{2}{P}\omega =\frac{4\pi (25)}{2} = 157.08 rad/s\)

where f = electrical frequency of the stator voltage

\(n_m\) = motor speed =\( (1 – s)n_e = (1 – 0.2) \times 1500 = 1200 rpm\)

 

\(T_e\) = developed torque =\(\frac{P_m}{\omega_m} =\frac{(1 – s)P_g}{(1 – s)\omega_e}\)

 

\(=\frac{3\widetilde{I}^2_s R^′_r}{s\omega_e} = \frac{3}{\omega_e} \frac{\frac{\widetilde{V}^2_{s(phase-rated)}}{2}}{(R_s + \frac{R^′_r}{s})^2 + (X_{ls} + X^′_lr)^2} \frac{R^′_r}{s}\)

 

\(=\frac{3}{157.08} \frac{(\frac{207.5}{\sqrt{3}})^2}{((0+\frac{0.07}{0.2})^2+(0.375 + 0.35)^2)} \frac{0.07}{0.2}\)

 

\(= \frac{3013.94}{20.36} = 148 Nm\)

The stator rms current is given by:

\(\widetilde{I}_s = \frac{\frac{\widetilde{V}_{s(phase-rated)}}{2}}{\sqrt{(R_s + \frac{R^′_r}{s})^2 + (X_{ls} + X^′_{lr})^2}}\)

 

\(= \frac{\frac{207.5}{\sqrt{3}}}{\sqrt{((0+\frac{0.07}{0.2})^2+(0.375 + 0.35)^2)}} = \frac{119.8}{0.81}= 147.9 A\)

b) If the supply frequency f is increased from 25 Hz to rated value of 50 Hz, then the following results are obtained:

\(n_e =\frac{120 f}{P} = \frac{120(50)}{2}=3000 rpm\)

 

\(\omega_e\) = synchronous frequency =\(\frac{4\pi f}{P} = \frac{2}{P}\omega = \frac{4\pi \times (50)}{2} =314.16 rad/s\)

Since the motor speed at this instant remains at the same value of 1200 rpm the slip will be

\(s\) = slip =\(\frac{n_e – n_m}{n_e} \frac{3000 – 1200}{3000} = 0.6\)

In this case the torque and the armature current are given by:

\(T_e = \frac{3}{314.16} \frac{(\frac{415}{\sqrt{3}})^2}{((0+\frac{0.07}{0.6})^2 + (0.75 + 0.7)^2)} \frac{0.07}{0.6} = \frac{12055.75}{398.41} = 30.26 Nm\)

 

\(\widetilde{I}_s = \frac{\frac{\widetilde{V}_{s(phase-rated)}}{2}}{\sqrt{(R_s + \frac{R^′_r}{s})^2 + (X_{ls} + X^′_lr)^2}}\)

 

\(= \frac{\frac{415}{\sqrt{3}}}{\sqrt{((0+\frac{0.07}{0.6})^2+(0.75 + 0.7)^2)}} = \frac{119.8}{0.81}\)

 

\(= \frac{239.6}{1.45} = 165.24 A\)

c) The previous results indicate that the torque is suddenly changed from 148 to 30.26 Nm and the stator current from 147.9 to 165.24 A. To avoid such abrupt change the variations in voltage and frequency of the inverter has to be gradually changed from 207.5 V, 20 Hz to 415 V, 50 Hz with a ratio \(\frac{V_s}{f} = 4.79\).
