Guest Question.

Consider the system of Figure 6.8, simplified to the undamped, unforced system in Figure 6.13. Let \(m_{1}\) = \(m\), \(m_{2}\) = 2\(m\), \(k_{1}\) = \(k\), \(k_{2}\) = 2\(k\), \(k_{3}\) = 3\(k\), and obtain the natural frequencies and modes of vibration.

Admin
Solution

Solution: The equations of motion are

 

\(\begin{array}{r}m \ddot{x}_{1}+3 k x_{1}-2 k x_{2}=0 \\2 m \ddot{x}_{2}+5 k x_{2}-2 k x_{1}=0\end{array}\)

 

In matrix-vector form, the symmetries of the mass and stiffness matrices are evident,

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left[\begin{array}{cc}m & 0 \\0 & 2 m\end{array}\right] &\left\{\begin{array}{c}\ddot{x}_{1} \\\ddot{x}_{2}\end{array}\right\} \\+&\left[\begin{array}{cc}3 k & -2 k \\-2 k & 5 k\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}x_{1} \\x_{2}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}0 \\0\end{array}\right\} .\end{aligned}\) (6.36)

 

Assuming harmonic responses of the form

 

\(\left\{\begin{array}{l}x_{1} \\x_{2}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}u_{1} \\u_{2}\end{array}\right\} \exp (i \omega t)\)

 

differentiating twice, and substituting into the governing Equation 6.36 gives

 

\(\begin{aligned}&-\omega^{2}\left[\begin{array}{cc}m & 0 \\0 & 2 m\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}u_{1} e^{i \omega t} \\u_{2} e^{i \omega t}\end{array}\right\} \\&+\left[\begin{array}{cc}3 k & -2 k \\-2 k & 5 k\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}u_{1} e^{i \omega t} \\u_{2} e^{i \omega t}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}0 \\0\end{array}\right\} .\end{aligned}\)

 

Combining terms, we have

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}-\omega^{2} m+3 k & -2 k \\-2 k & -2 \omega^{2} m+5 k\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}u_{1} \\u_{2}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}0 \\0\end{array}\right\}\) (6.37)

 

Setting the determinant of the coefficient matrix to zero leads to the characteristic equation

 

\(2 m^{2} \omega^{4}-11 m k \omega^{2}+11 k^{2}=0\)

 

with roots

 

\(\begin{aligned}\omega_{1,2}^{2} &=\frac{11 m k}{4 m^{2}} \mp \sqrt{\frac{(11 m k)^{2}}{16 m^{4}}-4 \frac{22(m k)^{2}}{16 m^{4}}} \\&=[2.75 \mp 1.44] \frac{k}{m}=1.31 \frac{k}{m} \text { and } 4.19 \frac{k}{m}\end{aligned}\)

 

The two natural frequencies are then

 

\(\omega_{1}=1.14 \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} rad / s , \quad \omega_{2}=2.05 \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} rad / s\)

 

With the natural frequencies known, Equation 6.30 (or Equation 6.37) can be used to obtain the respective modes of vibration,

 

\(\frac{u_{21}}{u_{11}}=-\frac{k_{11}-\omega_{1}^{2} m_{1}}{k_{12}}=-\frac{k_{12}}{k_{22}-\omega_{1}^{2} m_{2}}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\frac{u_{21}}{u_{11}} &=-\frac{k_{11}-\omega_{1}^{2} m_{1}}{k_{12}} \\&=-\frac{3 k-1.31(k / m) m}{-2 k}=0.845 \\\frac{u_{22}}{u_{12}} &=-\frac{k_{11}-\omega_{2}^{2} m_{1}}{k_{12}} \\&=-\frac{3 k-4.19(k / m) m}{-2 k}=-0.595 .\end{aligned}\)

 

The denominators cannot equal zero since the stiffness must be positive. Physically, zero stiffness would imply that one mass is motionless while the other is vibrating. This can occur only if the masses are not coupled. The modes are sketched in Figure 6.14. Modes generally have amplitudes of alternating signs and, therefore, the modal
amplitude lines13 cross the zero (\(n\) âˆ’ 1) times where \(n\) is the mode number. The crossing points are called nodes, indicative of no motion.

The complete response, following Equation 6.33, is

 

\(\begin{gathered}\left\{\begin{array}{c}x_{1}(t) \\x_{2}(t)\end{array}\right\}=\bar{C}_{1}\left\{\begin{array}{c}1 \\u_{21} / u_{11}\end{array}\right\} \cos \left(\omega_{1} t-\phi_{1}\right) \\+\bar{C}_{2}\left\{\begin{array}{c}1 \\u_{22} / u_{12}\end{array}\right\} \cos \left(\omega_{2} t-\phi_{2}\right)\end{gathered}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left\{\begin{array}{l}x_{1}(t) \\x_{2}(t)\end{array}\right\} &=\bar{C}_{1}\left\{\begin{array}{c}1 \\0.845\end{array}\right\} \cos \left(1.14 \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} t-\phi_{1}\right) \\&+\bar{C}_{2}\left\{\begin{array}{c}1 \\-0.595\end{array}\right\} \cos \left(2.05 \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} t-\phi_{2}\right) .\end{aligned}\)

 

The second mode in the figure has one node. The response of each mass is a combination of two harmonics that are weighted by the respective modal ratios.

Given the initial conditions \(x_{1}(0), x_{2}(0), \dot{x}_{1}(0)\) and \(\dot{x}_{2}(0)\), the constants of integration \(\bar{C}_{1}, \bar{C}_{2}, \phi_{1}\), \(\phi_{2}\) can be found by solving the four simultaneous algebraic
Equations 6.34 and 6.35.

 

\(\begin{aligned}\left\{\begin{array}{l}x_{1}(0) \\x_{2}(0)\end{array}\right\} &=\bar{C}_{1}\left\{\begin{array}{c}1 \\u_{21} / u_{11}\end{array}\right\} \cos \left(-\phi_{1}\right) \\&+\bar{C}_{2}\left\{\begin{array}{c}1 \\u_{22} / u_{12}\end{array}\right\} \cos \left(-\phi_{2}\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{r}\left\{\begin{array}{c}\dot{x}_{1}(0) \\\dot{x}_{2}(0)\end{array}\right\}=-\bar{C}_{1} \omega_{1}\left\{\begin{array}{c}1 \\u_{21} / u_{11}\end{array}\right\} \sin \left(-\phi_{1}\right) \\-\bar{C}_{2} \omega_{2}\left\{\begin{array}{c}1 \\u_{22} / u_{12}\end{array}\right\} \sin \left(-\phi_{2}\right) .\end{array}\)
