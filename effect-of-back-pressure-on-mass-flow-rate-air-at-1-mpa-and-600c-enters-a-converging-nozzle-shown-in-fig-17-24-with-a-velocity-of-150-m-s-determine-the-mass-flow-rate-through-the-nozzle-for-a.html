Guest Question.

Effect of Back Pressure on Mass Flow Rate
Air at 1 MPa and 600째C enters a converging nozzle, shown in Fig. 17-24, with a velocity of 150 m/s. Determine the mass flow rate through the nozzle for a nozzle throat area of 50 cm\(^2\) when the back pressure is (a) 0.7 MPa and (b) 0.4 MPa.

Admin
Solution

The constant-pressure specific heat and the specific heat ratio of air are \(c_p\) = 1.005 kJ/kg.K and k = 1.4, respectively ().

Analysis We use the subscripts i and t to represent the properties at the nozzle inlet and the throat, respectively. The stagnation temperature and pressure at the nozzle inlet are determined from Eqs. 17-4 and 17-5:

 

\(T_{0 i}=T_{i}+\frac{V_{i}^{2}}{2 c_{p}}=873 \mathrm{~K}+\frac{(150 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s})^{2}}{2(1.005 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K})}\left(\frac{1 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}}{1000 \mathrm{~m}^{2} / \mathrm{s}^{2}}\right)=884 \mathrm{~K} \)

 

\(P_{0 i}=P_{i}\left(\frac{T_{0 i}}{T_{i}}\right)^{k /(k-1)}=(1 \mathrm{MPa})\left(\frac{884 \mathrm{~K}}{873 \mathrm{~K}}\right)^{1.4 /(1.4-1)}=1.045 \mathrm{MPa}\)

 

These stagnation temperature and pressure values remain constant throughout the nozzle since the flow is assumed to be isentropic. That is,

\(T_{0}=T_{0 i}=884 \mathrm{~K} \quad \text { and } \quad P_{0}=P_{0 i}=1.045 \mathrm{MPa}\)

 

 

The critical-pressure ratio is determined from Table 17-2 (or Eq. 17-22) to be \(P^{*} / P_{0}=0.5283\).

\(\frac{P^{*}}{P_{0}}=\left(\frac{2}{k+1}\right)^{k/ (k-1)}\) (17-22)

(a) The back pressure ratio for this case is

 

\(\frac{P_{b}}{P_{0}}=\frac{0.7 \mathrm{MPa}}{1.045 \mathrm{MPa}}=0.670\)

 

 

which is greater than the critical-pressure ratio, 0.5283. Thus the exit plane pressure (or throat pressure \(P_{t}\) ) is equal to the back pressure in this case. That is, \(P_{t}=P_{b}=0.7 \mathrm{MPa}\), and \(P_{t} / P_{0}=0.670\). Therefore, the flow is not choked. From at \(P_{t} / P_{0}=0.670\), we read \(\mathrm{Ma}_{t}=0.778\) and \(T_{t} / T_{0}=0.892\).

The mass flow rate through the nozzle can be calculated from Eq. 17-24.
But it can also be determined in a step-by-step manner as follows:

 

\(T_{t} =0.892 T_{0}=0.892(884 \mathrm{~K})=788.5 \mathrm{~K} \)

 

\(\rho_{t} =\frac{P_{t}}{R T_{t}}=\frac{700 \mathrm{kPa}}{\left(0.287 \mathrm{kPa} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}\right)(788.5 \mathrm{~K})}=3.093 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3} \)

 

\(V_{t} =\mathrm{Ma}_{t} c_{t}=\mathrm{Ma}_t \sqrt{\mathrm{kRT}_{t}} \)

 

\(=(0.778) \sqrt{(1.4)(0.287 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K})(788.5 \mathrm{~K})\left(\frac{1000 \mathrm{~m}^{2} / \mathrm{s}^{2}}{1 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}}\right)} \)

 

\(=437.9 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\)

 

Thus,

\(\dot{m}=\rho_{t} A_{t} V_{t}=\left(3.093 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\right)\left(50 \times 10^{-4} \mathrm{~m}^{2}\right)(437.9 \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s})=6.77 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s}\)

 

 

(b) The back pressure ratio for this case is

\(\frac{P_{b}}{P_{0}}=\frac{0.4 \mathrm{MPa}}{1.045 \mathrm{MPa}}=0.383\)

 

which is less than the critical-pressure ratio, 0.5283. Therefore, sonic conditions exist at the exit plane (throat) of the nozzle, and \(M a=1\). The flow is choked in this case, and the mass flow rate through the nozzle can be calculated from Eq. 17-25:

 

\(\dot{m} =A^{*} P_{0} \sqrt{\frac{k}{R T_{0}}}\left(\frac{2}{k+1}\right)^{(k+1) /[2(k-1)]} \)

 

\(=\left(50 \times 10^{-4} \mathrm{~m}^{2}\right)(1045 \mathrm{kPa}) \times \sqrt{\frac{1.4}{(0.287 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K})(884 \mathrm{~K})}}\left(\frac{2}{1.4+1}\right)^{2.4 / 0.8} \)

 

\(=7.10 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s}\)

 

since \(\mathrm{kPa} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2} / \sqrt{\mathrm{kJ} / \mathrm{kg}}=\sqrt{1000} \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s}\).

 

 

Discussion This is the maximum mass flow rate through the nozzle for the specified inlet conditions and nozzle throat area.
