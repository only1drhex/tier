Guest Question.

In the design work for a model single cylinder reciprocating engine, the work done during the expansion stroke is to be represented as:

\( W = p\pi D^{2}C(1- Q^{n-1})/(4(n-1)) \)

where

Q = C/(C + 2 R)

and

C = M – R – L – H

The quantities are defined as: W, work done during the expansion stroke; P, cylinder pressure at start of stroke; D, piston diameter; C, length of the clearance volume; n, expansion stroke polytropic exponent; R, crank radius; M, distance from crank centre to underside of cylinder head; L, connecting rod length; H, distance from gudgeon pin to piston crown; Q, ratio of clearance to clearance-plus-swept volumes.
It is recognized that the independent quantities are subject to variability and it is desired to determine the consequent extreme and probable variability in the work done.
If

\( P = 20 \times 10^{5} \pm 0.5 \times 10^{5}N/m^{2}, \)
\( n = 1.3 \pm 0.05 , \)
\( D = 4 \times 10^{-2} \pm 25 \times 10^{-6} m, \)
\( R = 2 \times 10^{-2} \pm 50 \times 10^{-6} m, \)
\( M = 10.5 \times 10^{-2} \pm 50 \times 10^{-6} m \)
\( H = 2 \times 10^{-2} \pm 25 \times 10^{-6} m, \)
\( L = 6 \times 10^{-2} \pm 50 \times 10^{-6} m \)

determine the extreme and probable (normal model) limits of the work (Ellis,1990).
To help with your solution, certain partial derivatives have already been calculated.

\( \frac{W}{R} = - 2599.46 \)
\( \frac{W}{M} = 2888.39 \)
\( \frac{W}{H} = -2888.39 \)
\( \frac{W}{L} = -2888.39 \)

Note: It may be useful to recall that if \( y = a^{X} \) then \( \frac{dy}{dx} = x \log _{e} (a). \)

Admin
Solution

The sure-fit ‘extreme’ limit of the work done is given by

\( \Delta W_{sure-fit} = \left|\frac{W}{P} \right| \Delta P + \left|\frac{W}{D} \right| \Delta D + \left|\frac{W}{M} \right| \Delta M + \left|\frac{W}{R} \right| \Delta R + \left|\frac{W}{L} \right| \Delta L + \left|\frac{W}{H} \right| \Delta H + \left|\frac{W}{n} \right| \Delta n \)

The average values of C, Q, and W are: \( \overset{-}{C} = 0.005, \overset{-}{Q} = 0.1111, \overset{-}{W} = 20.22. \)
\( \frac{W}{P} = \frac{\pi D^{2}C(1-Q^{n-1})}{4(n-1)} \)
\( = \pi 0.04^{2} 0.005 \frac{1-0.1111^{0.3}}{4\times 0.3} \)
\( = 1.011035 \times 10^{-5}. \)
\( \Delta P = 2 \times 0.5 \times 10^{5} = 1 \times 10^{5} \)
\( \left|\frac{W}{p} \right| \Delta P = 1.011035. \)
\( \frac{W}{D} = 2P\pi DC \frac{1-Q^{n-1}}{4(n-1)} \)
\( = 2\pi 20 \times 10^{5} \times 0.04 \times 0.005 \frac{1 – 0.1111^{0.3}}{4 \times 0.3} \)
\( = 1011.35. \)
\( \Delta D = 50 \times 10^{-6}. \)
\( \left|\frac{W}{D} \right| \Delta D = 0.0505517. \)
\( \frac{W}{M} = 2888.39 \) \( \Delta M = 100 \times 10^{-6}. \)
\( \left|\frac{W}{M} \right| \Delta M = 0.288839. \)
\( \frac{W}{R} = – 2599.46 \) \( \Delta R = 100 \times 10^{-6}. \)
\( \left|\frac{W}{R} \right| \Delta R = 0.259946. \)
\( \frac{W}{L} = – 2888.39 \) \( \Delta L = 100 \times 10^{-6}. \)
\( \left|\frac{W}{L} \right| \Delta L = 0.288839. \)
\( \frac{W}{H} = -2888.39 \) \( \Delta H = 50 \times 10^{-6}. \)
\( \left|\frac{W}{H} \right| \Delta H = 0.1444195. \)

Let

\( W = K_{3} \frac{\downarrow 1 – Q^{n-1}}{\rightarrow n -1} \) \( K_{3} = \frac{P\pi D^{2}C}{4} \)
\( \left|\frac{W}{n} \right| = K_{3} \frac{\downarrow -(n-1) \underset{n}{-} Q^{n-1} – (1-Q^{n-1})1}{\rightarrow (n – 1)^{2}} \)
\( = K_{3} \frac{\downarrow -(n-1) Q^{n-1}ln Q – (1-Q^{n-1})1}{\rightarrow (n – 1)^{2}} \)

which on substituting for \( K_{3}\) gives

\( = \frac{W(n-1)}{1 – Q^{n-1}} \times \frac{\downarrow -(n-1) Q^{n-1}ln Q – (1-Q^{n-1})}{\rightarrow (n – 1)^{2}} \)
\( \frac{W}{n} = – \frac{W}{n – 1} \frac{\downarrow (n-1) Q^{n-1}ln Q}{\rightarrow 1 – Q^{n – 1}} + 1 \)
\( = \frac{20.22}{0.3} \times \frac{\downarrow 0.3 \times 0.1111^{0.3} \ln 0.1111}{1 – 0.1111^{0.3}} + 1 \)
\( = 19.7909. \)
\( \Delta n = 2 \times 0.05 = 0.1. \)
\( \left|\frac{W}{n} \right| \Delta n = 1.979. \)
\( \Delta W_{sure-fit} = 1.011035 + 0.0505517 + 0.288839 + 0.259946 + 0.288839 + 0.1444195 + 1.979 = 4.022. \)
\( W = \overset{-}{W} \pm (\Delta W/2) = 20.22 \pm 2.011 J. \)

Statistical tolerance limits:

\( \Delta W^{2}_{normal} = \frac{\downarrow W}{\rightarrow P} ^{2} \Delta P^{2} + \frac{\downarrow W}{\rightarrow D} ^{2} \Delta D^{2} + \frac{\downarrow W}{\rightarrow M} ^{2} \Delta M^{2} + \frac{\downarrow W}{\rightarrow R} ^{2} \Delta R^{2} + \frac{\downarrow W}{\rightarrow L} ^{2} \Delta L^{2} + \frac{\downarrow W}{\rightarrow H} ^{2} \Delta H^{2} + \frac{\downarrow W}{\rightarrow n} ^{2} \Delta n^{2} = 5.196. \)
\( \Delta W = 2.2796. \)

so

\( W = \overset{-}{W} \pm (\Delta W/2) = 20.22 \pm 1.1398 J. \)

If required
\( \sigma _{w} = 1.1398/3 = 0.38. \)
