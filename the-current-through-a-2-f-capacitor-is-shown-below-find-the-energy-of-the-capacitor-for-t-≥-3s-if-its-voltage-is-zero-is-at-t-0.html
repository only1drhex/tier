Guest Question.

The current through a \(2 \mathrm{~F}\) capacitor is shown below. Find the energy of the capacitor for \(t \geq 3 \mathrm{~s}\), if its voltage is zero is at \(t=0\).

Admin
Solution
  • \(0 \leq t \leq 1 \mathrm{~s}: v_{C}(t)=0+\frac{1}{2} \int_{0}^{t} 5 t^{\prime} d t^{\prime}=\left(5 t^{2} / 4\right) \mathrm{V}\) and \(v_{C}(1)=5 / 4 \mathrm{~V}\)
  • \(1 \leq t \leq 2 \mathrm{~s}: v_{C}(t)=5 / 4+\frac{1}{2} \int_{1}^{t} 5 d t^{\prime}=(5 t / 2-5 / 4) \mathrm{V}\) and
\(v_{C}(2)=15 / 4 \mathrm{~V}\)
  • \(2 \leq t \leq 3 \mathrm{~s}: v_{C}(t)=15 / 4+\frac{1}{2} \int_{2}^{t}\left(9-t^{\prime 2}\right) d t^{\prime}=\left(-t^{3} / 6+9 t / 2-47 / 12\right) \mathrm{V}\) and
\(v_{C}(3)=61 / 12 \mathrm{~V}\)
  • \(t \geq 3 \mathrm{~s}: v_{C}(t)=61 / 12 \mathrm{~V}\)
  • Then, for \(t \geq 3 \mathrm{~s}: w_{C}(t)=\frac{1}{2} C\left[v_{C}\right]^{2}=(61 / 12)^{2} \mathrm{~J}\)
