Guest Question.

A self-locking power screw is required for a screw jack. An initial proposal is to use a single start 1.25–5 Acme power screw. The axial load is 4000N and collar mean diameter is 1.75in. Determine the lifting and lowering torques, the efficiency of the power screw and whether the design proposal is self-locking.

 

Admin
Solution

Single start thread, so the lead, L, is equal to the pitch, P.

\( N = 5 teeth per inch \)
\( P = \frac{1}{N} = \frac{1}{5} = 0.2'' \)
\( L = 0.2'' = 5.08 mm\)
\( d_{P} = 1.15'' = 29.21 mm \)
\( d_{c} = 1.75 '' = 44.45 mm \)

Assume sliding friction, \( \mu = 0.15. \)

The torque to lift the load is given by Eq .12.23 \( T_{u} = \frac{Pd_{P}(\mu \pi d_{P} + L \cos \alpha )}{2(\pi d_{P}\cos \alpha – \mu L )} + \mu _{c}P \frac{d_{c}}{2} \).

\( T = \frac{4000 \times 0.02921}{2} \times \frac{0.15 \pi 0.02921 + 0.00508 \cos 14.5}{\pi 0.02921 \cos 14.5 – 0.15 \times 0.00508} + 0.15 \times 4000 \frac{0.04445}{2} \)
\( = 58.42 \frac{0.01376 + 4.9182 \times 10^{-3}}{0.08884 – 7.62 \times 10^{-4}} + 13.34 \)
\( = 12.39 + 13.34 = 25.73 Nm \)

The torque to lower the load is given by Eq.12.24 \( T_{d} = \frac{Pd_{P}(\mu \pi d_{P} – L \cos \alpha )}{2(\pi d_{P}\cos \alpha + \mu L )} + \mu _{c}P \frac{d_{c}}{2} \).

\( T = 58.42 \frac{0.01376 – 4.9182 \times 10^{-3}}{0.08884 + 7.62 \times 10^{-4}} + 13.34 \)
\( = 5.765 + 13.34 = 19.11 Nm \)
\( \eta = \frac{PL}{2 \pi T} \)
\( \eta _{screw} = \frac{4000 \times 0.00508}{2 \pi 12.39} = 0.261 \)
\( \eta _{both} = \frac{4000 \times 0.00508}{2 \pi 25.73} = 0.126 \)

The design will be self-locking if

\( \mu \geq \frac{L}{\pi d_{P}} \cos \alpha = \frac{0.00508}{\pi 0.02921} \cos 14.5 \)
\( = 0.05359 \)
\( \mu = 0.15, \) so the design is self-locking.
