Guest Question.

Figure 6.33(a) shows the skeleton representation of a two-stage planetary gear train. The num-bers of teeth are

\(N_{1} = 24; N_{2} = 60; N_{3} = 12\)
\(N_{4} = 48; N_{5} = 12; N_{6} = 24\)
Determine the speed ratios of all components with respect to gear 1 when
(a) gear 2 is held fixed
(b) gear 4 is held fixed

Admin
Solution

Speed ratios of components of the gear train relative to gear 1 may be expressed as

\( e_{j/1} = \frac{ω_{j}}{ω_{1}}, j = 2,…,7 \) (6.5-37)

Considering the case of j = 7 and substituting Equations (6.5-29) and (6.5-35) in Equation (6.5-37), we obtain

\( e_{7/1} = \frac{ω_{7}}{ω_{1}} =\frac{x_{2}}{x_{1} + y_{1}}\) (6.5-38)

In addition, the following equations are generated by considering the connections of the com-ponents between the stages:

• Connection of the sun gears

\(ω_{1} = (ω_{5}) or (ω_{1})_{1} = (ω_{1})_{2} or x_{1} + y_{1} = x_{2} + y_{2} \) (6.5-39)

• Connection of the crank of stage 1 to the ring gear of stage 2

\(ω_{2} = (ω_{2})_{1} = (ω_{4})_{2} or x_{1} = x_{2} – \frac{N_{5}}{N_{2}} y_{2}\) (6.5-40)

Equations (6.5-29)–(6.5-40) are valid for parts (a) and (b) of this problem. We now pro-ceed to calculate the speed ratios when either gear 2 or gear 4 is held fixed.

(a) If gear 2 is fixed, then from Equation (6.5-30) we get

\(ω_{2} = x_{1} = 0\) (6.5-41)

Solving Equations (6.5-39)–(6.5-41) for \(y_{1}\) and \(y_{2}\) in terms of \(x_{2}\) gives

\( y_{1} = \frac{(N_{2} + N_{5})}{N_{5}} x_{2} ; y_{2} = \frac{N_{2}}{N_{5}} x_{2}\) (6.5-42)

Substituting Equations (6.5-41) and (6.5-42) in Equation (6.5-38) gives

\( e_{7/1} = \frac{x_{2}}{x_{1} + y_{1}} \)

\(= \frac{x_{2}}{ 0 + \left\lgroup\frac{(N_{2} + N_{5})}{N_{5} } x_{2} \right\rgroup } = \frac{N_{2}}{N_{2} + N_{5}} = \frac{12}{60 + 12} = \frac{1}{6} \) (6.5-43)

Similar results of analyses for other components of the gear train are given in Table 6.11.

(b) If gear 4 is fixed, then from Equation (6.5-32) we obtain

\( ω_{4} = x_{1} – \frac{N_{1}}{N_{4}} y_{1} = 0\) (6.5-44)

Solving Equations (6.5-39), (6.5-40), and (6.5-44) for \(x_{2}, y_{1}\) and \(y_{2}\), in terms of \(x_{1}\) gives

\( x_{2} = \left[1 + \frac{N_{4} N_{5}}{N_{1} (N_{2} + N_{5})} \right] x_{1}; y_{1} = \frac{N_{4}}{N_{1}}x_{1} \) (6.5-45)

\( y_{2} = \frac{N_{2} N_{4}}{N_{1} (N_{2} + N_{5})} x_{1}\)

Substituting Equations (6.5-45) in Equation (6.5-38) gives

\( e_{7/1} = \frac{x_{2}}{x_{1} + y_{1}} = \frac{\left[1 + \frac{N_{4} N_{5}}{N_{1} (N_{2} + N_{5})} \right] x_{1}}{\left\lgroup x_{1} + \frac{N_{4}}{N_{1}}x_{1}\right\rgroup } \)

\( = \frac{N_{4} N_{5} + N_{1}( N_{2} + N_{5})}{( N_{1} + N_{4})( N_{2} + N_{5})} =\frac{48 × 12+ 24 (60 + 12)}{(24 + 48)(60 + 12)} = \frac{4}{9}\) (6.5-46)
