Guest Question.

A rectangular footing of size 10 x 20 ft is founded at a depth of 6 ft below the ground level in a cohesive soil \((\phi=0)\) which fails by general shear. Given: \(\gamma_{ sat }=114 lb / ft ^{3}, c=945 lb / ft ^{2}\). The water table is close to the ground surface. Determine \(q_{u}, q_{m u} \text { and } q_{n a}\) by (a) Terzaghi’s method, and (b) Skempton’s method. Use \(F_{s}=3\).

Admin
Solution

(a) Terzaghi’s method

Use Eq. (12.19)

 

\(c=0, \quad q_{u}=\gamma D_{f} N_{q}+\frac{1}{2} \gamma B N_{\gamma} 1-0.2 \times \frac{B}{L}\) (12.19)

 

For \(\phi=0^{\circ}, N_{c}=5.7, N_{q}=1\)

 

\(q_{u}=c N_{c} 1+0.3 \times \frac{B}{L}+\gamma_{b} D_{f}\)

 

Substituting the known values,

 

\(q_{u}=945 \times 5.71+0.3 \times \frac{10}{20}+(114-62.4) \times 6=6,504 lb / ft ^{2}\)

 

\(q_{n u}=\left(q_{u}-\gamma_{b} D_{f}\right)=6504-(114-62.4) \times 6=6195 lb / ft ^{2}\)

 

\(q_{n a}=\frac{q_{n u}}{F_{s}}=\frac{6195}{3}=2065 lb / ft ^{2}\)

 

(b) Skempton’s method

From Eqs. (12.22a) and (12.22d) we may write

 

\(q_{u}=c N_{c}+\gamma D_{f}\) (12.22a)

 

\(\left(N_{c}\right)_{R}=\left(0.84+0.16 \times \frac{B}{L}\right)\left(N_{c}\right)_{S}\) (12.22d)

 

\(q_{u}=c N_{c r}+\gamma D_{f}\)

 

where \(N_{c r}=\) bearing capacity factor for rectangular foundation.

 

\(N_{c r}=\left(0.84+0.16 \times \frac{B}{L}\right) \times N_{c s}\)

 

where \(N_{c s}=\) bearing capacity factor for a square foundation.

 

From Fig. 12.9, \(N_{c s}=7.2 \text { for } D_{f} / B=0.60\).

 

Therefore \(N_{c r}=0.84+0.16 \times \frac{10}{20} \times 7.2=6.62\)

 

Now \(q_{u}=945 \times 6.62+114 \times 6=6940 lb / ft ^{2}\)

 

\(q_{m u}=\left(q_{u}-\gamma D_{f}\right)=6940-114 \times 6=6,256 lb / ft ^{2}\)

 

\(q_{n a}=\frac{q_{n u}}{F_{s}}=\frac{6256}{3}=2,085 lb / ft ^{2}\)

 

Note: Terzaghi’s and Skempton’s values are in close agreement for cohesive soils.
