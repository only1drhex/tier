Guest Question.

For the branched system of Figure 10.8, the drive torque is illustrated in Figure 10.9. The load torque is constant. Gear A has an average speed of 300 rpm, and its diameter is two times that of gear B. The polar mass moments of inertia of the gears are

\(I_{A} = 8.0 kg-m^{2}; I_{B} = 1.0 kg-m^{2}\)

Determine the polar mass moment of inertia of a flywheel on the same shaft as gear B that is required to have a coefficient of speed fluctuation of 0.005.

Admin
Solution

From the drive torque curve, it may be determined that

\( T_{L} = -100 N-m; ∆E_{max} = 75π N-m\)

From Equation (10.2-13) \( I = \frac{∆E_{max}}{ω_{0}^{2} C_{S}} \) we have

\( I = I_{eff} = \frac{∆E_{max}}{ω_{0}^{2} C_{S}} = \frac{ 75π N-m}{\left\lgroup300 \times \frac{2\pi }{60} \right\rgroup^{2} \frac{rad^{2}}{sec^{2}} \times 0.005} = 47.6 kg-m^{2}\)

Rearranging Equation (10.3-3) \(I_{eff} = I_{A} + (I_{B} + I_{flywheel}) \left\lgroup\frac{r_{A}}{r_{B}} \right\rgroup^{2}\) gives

\( I_{flywheel} = ( I_{eff} – I_{A}) \left\lgroup\frac{r_{B}}{r_{A}} \right\rgroup^{2} – I_{B}\)

 

\( =\left[(47.6 – 8.0) \left\lgroup\frac{1}{2} \right\rgroup^{2} – 1.0 \right] kg-m^{2} = 8.90 kg-m^{2} \)
