Guest Question.

Consider the combustion of methanol, \(CH _{3} OH\), with 25% excess air. The combustion products are passed through a heat exchanger and exit at 200 kPa, 40°C. Calculate the absolute entropy of the products exiting the heat exchanger per kilomole of methanol burned, using the proper amounts of liquid and vapor for the water.

Admin
Solution
\(CH _{3} OH +1.25 \times 1.5\left( O _{2}+3.76 N _{2}\right) \rightarrow CO _{2}+2 H _{2} O +0.375 O _{2}+7.05 N _{2}\)

Products exit at 40 °C , 200 kPa, check for saturation:

\(y _{ V MAX }=\frac{ P _{ G }}{ P }=\frac{7.384}{200}=\frac{ n _{ V MAX }}{ n _{ V MAX }+1+0.375+7.05}\)

 

\(n _{ V }= n _{ V MAX }=0.323 \quad n _{ LIQ }=1.677\)

 

Gas mixture:

\(\begin{array}{cccccc}& n _{ i } & y _{ i } & \overline{ s }_{ i }^{\circ} & -\overline{ R } \ln \frac{ y _{ i } P }{ P _{0}} & \overline{ S }_{ i } \\CO _{2} & 1.0 & 0.1143 & 215.633 & +12.270 & 227.903 \\H _{2} O & 0.323 & 0.0369 & 190.485 & +21.671 & 212.156 \\O _{2} & 0.375 & 0.0429 & 206.592 & +20.418 & 227.01 \\N _{2} & 7.05 & 0.8059 & 193.039 & -3.969 & 189.07\end{array}\)

 

\(S _{ GAS MIX }=\sum n _{ i } \overline{ S }_{ i }=1714.50 kJ / K kmol \text { fuel }\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\overline{ s }_{ LIQ }=69.950+18.015(0.5725-0.3674)=73.645 kJ / kmol \\& S _{ LIQ }=1.677 \times 73.645=123.50 kJ / K kmol \text { fuel } \\& S _{ PROD }=1714.50+123.50= 1 8 3 8 kJ / K \text { kmol fuel }\end{aligned}\)
