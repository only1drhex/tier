Guest Question.

Five tons of 2-in round rod of 1030 hot-rolled steel has been received for workpiece stock. Nine standard-geometry tensile test specimens have been machined from random locations in various rods. In the test report, the ultimate tensile strength was given in kpsi. In ascending order (not necessary), these are displayed in Table 20–3. Find the mean \(\overline{x}\), the standard deviation \(s_{x}\) , and the coefficient of variation \(C_{x}\) from the sample, such that these are best estimates of the parent population (the stock your plant will convert to product).

Solution

From Eqs. (20–5) and (20–8),

\(\overline{x} =\frac{x_{1} + x_{2} + x_{3} + · · · + x_{N}}{N} =\frac{1}{N} \sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}{x_{i}}\) (20–5)

\(s_{x} =\sqrt{\frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}{x^{2}_{i} −\left(\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}{x_{i}}\right)^{2}/N}}{N − 1}} =\sqrt{\frac{\sum\limits_{i=1}^{N}{x^{2}_{i} − N \overline{x}^{2}}}{N − 1}}\) (20–8)

\(\overline{x} =\frac{1}{N} \sum^{9}_{i=1}{x_{i}}\)

and

\(s_{x} =\sqrt{ \frac{\sum{x^{2}_{i}} −\left(\sum{x_{i}}\right)^{2}/N}{N − 1}}\)

It is computationally efficient to generate \(\sum{x}\) and \(\sum{x^{2}}\) before evaluating \(\overline{x} and s_{x}\) .
This has been done in Table 20–3.

\(\overline{x} =\frac{1}{9} (611.8) = 67.98 kpsi\)

\(s_{x} =\sqrt{ \frac{41 689.24 − 611.8^{2}/9}{9 − 1}} = 3.543 \) kpsi

From Eq. (20–12),

\(C_{x} =\frac{s_{x}}{\overline{x}}\) (20-12)

\(C_{x} =\frac{s_{x}}{\overline{x}} =\frac{3.543}{67.98} = 0.0521\)

All three statistics are estimates of the parent population statistical parameters. Note that these results are independent of the distribution.
