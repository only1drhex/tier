Guest Question.

Let \( v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 4 \end{matrix} \right ] , \text{and} v_{3} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right ] \) . Find an implicit description \( [f:d] \) of the plane \( H_{1} \) that passes through \( v_{1} , v_{2} \), and \( v_{3} \).

Admin
Solution

\( H_{1} \) is parallel to a plane \( H_{0} \) through the origin that contains the translated points

\( v_{2} – v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ -2 \\ 3 \end{matrix} \right ] \text{and} v_{3} – v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Since these two points are linearly independent, \( H_{0} = \)Span {\( v_{2} – v_{1} , v_{3} – v_{1} \)}. Let \( n = \left [ \begin{matrix} a \\ b \\ c \end{matrix} \right ] \) be the normal to \( H_{0} \). Then \( v_{2} – v_{1} \) and \( v_{3} – v_{1} \) are each orthogonal to n. That is , \( (v_{2} – v_{1}) \cdot n = 0 \text{and} (v_{3} – v_{1}) \cdot n = 0 \). These two equations form a system whose augmented matrix can be row reduced:

\( \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & -2 & 3 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} a \\ b \\ c \end{matrix} \right ] = 0 , \left [ \begin{matrix} 2 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} a \\ b \\ c \end{matrix} \right ] = 0 , \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & -2 & 3 & 0 \\ 2 & 0 & 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Row operations yield \( a = (-\frac{2}{4})c , b = (\frac{5}{4})c \), with c free. Set \( c = 4 \), for instance. Then \( n = \left [ \begin{matrix} -2 \\ 5 \\ 4 \end{matrix} \right ] \text{and} H_{0} = [f:0] , \text{where} f(x) = -2x_{1} + 5x_{2} + 4x_{3} \) .

The parallel hyperplane \( H_{1} \) is \( [f:d] \). To find d, use the fact that \( v_{1} \) is in \( H_{1} \), and compute \( d = f(v_{1}) = f(1,1,1) = -2(1) + 5(1) + 4(1) = 7 \). As a check, compute \( f(v_{2}) = f(2,-1,4) = -2(2) + 5(-1) + 4(4) = 16 – 9 = 7 \). Observe \( f(v_{3}) = 7 \) also.
