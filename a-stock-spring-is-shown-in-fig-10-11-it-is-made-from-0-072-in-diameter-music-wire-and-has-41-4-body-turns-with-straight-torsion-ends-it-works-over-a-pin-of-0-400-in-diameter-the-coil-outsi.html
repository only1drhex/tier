Guest Question.

A stock spring is shown in Fig. 10–11. It is made from 0.072-in-diameter music wire and has 4\(\frac{1}{4}\) body turns with straight torsion ends. It works over a pin of 0.400 in diameter. The coil outside diameter is \(\frac {19}{32}\) in.
(a) Find the maximum operating torque and corresponding rotation for static loading.
(b) Estimate the inside coil diameter and pin diametral clearance when the spring is subjected to the torque in part (a)
(c) Estimate the fatigue factor of safety \(n_{f}\) if the applied moment varies between \(M_{min} = 1 to M_{max} = 5 lbf · in\).

Admin
Solution

(a) For music wire, from Table 10–4 we find that A = 201 kpsi · \(in^{m}\) and m = 0.145. Therefore,

\(S_{ut} =\frac {A}{d^{m}} =\frac {201}{(0.072)^{0.145}} = 294.4 kpsi\)

Using Eq. (10–57) gives

\(S_{y}= \begin{cases} 0.78 S_{ut} & Music wire and cold-drawn carbon steels\\ 0.87 S_{ut} & OQ\&T carbon and low-alloy steels \\ 0.61 S_{ut} & Austenitic stainless steel and nonferrous alloys \end{cases}\) (10-57)

\(S_{y} = 0.78S_{ut} = 0.78(294.4) = 229.6 kpsi\)

The mean coil diameter is D = 19/32 − 0.072 = 0.5218 in. The spring index C =D/d = 0.5218/0.072 = 7.247. The bending stress correction factor \(K_{i}\) from Eq. (10–43),is

\(K_{i} =\frac {4C^{2} − C − 1}{4C(C − 1)} K_{o} =\frac {4C^{2} + C − 1}{4C(C + 1)}\) (10–43)

\(K_{i} =\frac {4(7.247)^{2} − 7.247 − 1}{4(7.247)(7.247 − 1) }=1.115\)

Now rearrange Eq. (10–44), substitute \(S_{y}\) for σ , and solve for the maximum torque Fr to obtain

\(σ = K_{i} \frac {32Fr}{πd^{3}}\) (10–44)

\(M_{max} = (Fr )_{max} =\frac {πd^{3}S_{y}}{32K_{i}} =\frac{π(0.072)^{3}229 600}{32(1.115)} = 7.546 lbf · in\)

Note that no factor of safety has been used. Next, from Eq. (10–54), the number of turns of the coil body \(θ^{′}_{c}\) is

\(θ^{′}_{c} =\frac {10.8MDN_{b}}{d^{4}E}\) (10–54)

\(θ^{′}_{c} =\frac {10.8MDN_{b}}{d^{4}E} =\frac {10.8(7.546)0.5218(4.25)}{0.072^{4}(28.5)10^{6}} = 0.236 turn\)

\((θ^{′}_{c})_{deg} = 0.236(360^{◦}) = 85.0^{◦}\)

The active number of turns \(N_{a}\) , from Eq. (10–48), is

\(N_{a} = N_{b} + \frac {l_{1} + l_{2}}{3πD}\) (10–48)

\(N_{a} = N_{b} + \frac {l_{1} + l_{2}}{3πD} = 4.25 + \frac {1 + 1}{3π(0.5218)} = 4.657 turns\)

The spring rate of the complete spring, from Eq. (10–51), is

\(k^{′} = \frac {d^{4}E}{10.8DN_{a}}\) (10–51)

\(k^{′} = \frac {0.072^{4}(28.5)10^{6}}{10.8(0.5218)4.657} =29.18 lbf · in/turn\)

The number of turns of the complete spring \(θ^{′}\) is

\(θ^{′}=\frac {M}{k^{′}} =\frac {7.546}{29.18} = 0.259 turn\)

\((θ^{′}_{s})_{deg} = 0.259(360^{◦}) = 93.24^{◦}\)

(b) With no load, the mean coil diameter of the spring is 0.5218 in. From Eq. (10–53),

\(D^{′} =\frac {N_{b}D}{N_{b} + θ^{′}_{c}}\) (10–53)

\(D^{′} =\frac {N_{b}D}{N_{b} + θ^{′}_{c}} =\frac {4.25(0.5218)}{4.25 + 0.236} = 0.494 in\)

The diametral clearance between the inside of the spring coil and the pin at load is

\( Δ= D^{′} − d − D_{p} = 0.494 − 0.072 − 0.400 = 0.022 in\)

(c) Fatigue:

\(M_{a} = (M_{max} − M_{min})/2 = (5 − 1)/2 = 2 lbf · in\)
\(M_{m} = (M_{max} + M_{min})/2 = (5 + 1)/2 = 3 lbf·in\)

\(r =\frac {M_{a}}{M_{m}} =\frac {2}{3}\)

\(σ_{a} = K_{i} \frac {32M_{a}}{πd^{3}} = 1.115 \frac {32(2)}{π0.072^{3}} = 60 857 psi\)
\(σ_{m} =\frac {M_{m}}{M_{a}} σ_{a} = \frac {3}{2} (60 857) = 91 286 psi\)

From Table 10–10, \(S_{r} = 0.50S_{ut}\) = 0.50(294.4) = 147.2 kpsi. Then

\(S_{e} = \frac {147.2/2}{1 −\left( \frac {147.2/2}{294.4}\right)^{2}} = 78.51 kpsi\)

The amplitude component of the strength \(S_{a}\) , from Eq. (10–59), is

\(S_{a} =\frac {r^{2}S^{2}_{ut}}{2S_{e}}\left[-1+\sqrt {1+\left(\frac {2S_{e}}{r S_{ut}} \right)^{2} } \right] \) (10-59)

\(S_{a} =\frac {(2/3)^{2}294.4^{2}}{2(78.51)}\left[-1+\sqrt {1+\left(\frac {2}{2/3} \frac {78.51}{294.4} \right)^{2} } \right] = 68.85 kpsi\)

The fatigue factor of safety is

\(n_{f} =\frac {S_{a}}{σ_{a}} =\frac {68.85}{60.86} = 1.13\)
