Solution

We may treat the values \(R_{1}^{*}, \ldots, R_{100}^{*}\) as if they were a random sample of actual resistances. Therefore we estimate \(\mu_{R}\) with the sample mean \(\bar{R}^{*}\) and \(\sigma_{R}\) with the sample standard deviation \(s_{R^{*}}\). The sample mean and standard deviation of \(R_{1}^{*}, \ldots, R_{100}^{*}\) are \(\bar{R}^{*}=19.856\) and \(s_{R^{*}}=1.6926\), respectively. These are the estimates of \(\mu_{R}\) and \(\sigma_{R}\), respectively.
