Guest Question.

How long does it take (to the nearest whole year) for \(\$ 1,000\) to quadruple in value when the interest rate is \(15 \%\) per year? (4.6)

Admin
Solution
\(\$ 4,000=\$ 1,000(F / P, 15 \%, N)\)

 

\(4=(1.15)^{N}\)

\(N=\log (4) / \log (1.15)=9.9\) or \(N=10\) years

Alternative solution: \(4=(F / P, 15 \%, N)\) and from Table \(C-15\), the value of \(N\) is 10 .
