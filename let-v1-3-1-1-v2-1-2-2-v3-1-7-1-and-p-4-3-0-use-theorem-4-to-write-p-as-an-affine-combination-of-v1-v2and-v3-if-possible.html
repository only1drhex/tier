Guest Question.

Let \( v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 3 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{3} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 7 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ] , \text{and} p = \left [ \begin{matrix} 4 \\ 3 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \) . Use Theorem 4 to write p as an affine combination of \( v_{1} , v_{2} \), and \( v_{3} \), if possible.

THEOREM 4 :

A point y in \( \mathbb{R} ^{n} \) is an affine combination of \( v_{1} , . . . , v_{p} \) in \( \mathbb{R} ^{n} \) if and only if the homogeneous form of y is in Span {\( \tilde{v}_{1} , . . . , \tilde{v}_{p} \)}. In fact, \( y = c_{1}v_{1} + . . . + c_{p}v_{p} \), with \( c_{1} + . . . + c_{p} = 1 \), if and only if \( \tilde{y} = c_{1}\tilde{v}_{1} + . . . + c_{p}\tilde{v}_{p} \).

Admin
Solution

Row reduce the augmented matrix for the equation

\( x_{1}\tilde{v}_{1} + x_{2}\tilde{v}_{2} + x_{3}\tilde{v}_{3} = \tilde{p} \)

To simplify the arithmetic, move the fourth row of 1â€™s to the top (equivalent to three row interchanges). After this, the number of arithmetic operations here is basically the same as the number needed for the method using Theorem 1.

\( \left [ \begin{matrix} \tilde{v}_{1} & \tilde{v}_{2} & \tilde{v}_{3} & \tilde{p} \end{matrix} \right ] \sim \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 3 & 1 & 1 & 4 \\ 1 & 2 & 7 & 3 \\ 1 & 2 & 1 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \sim \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 0 & -2 & -2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 6 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -1 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

\( \sim . . . \sim \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & 1.5 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & .5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

By Theorem 4, \( 1.5v_{1} – v_{2} + .5v_{3} = p \). See Figure 4, which shows the plane that contains \( v_{1} , v_{2} , v_{3} \), and p (together with points on the coordinate axes).

THEOREM 1 :

A point y in \( \mathbb{R} ^{n} \) is an affine combination of \( v_{1} , . . . , v_{p} \) in \( \mathbb{R} ^{n} \) if and only if \( y – v_{1} \) is a linear combination of the translated points \( v_{2} – v_{1} , . . . , v_{p} – v_{1} \) .
