Guest Question.

The flat tension spring shown in Fig. P.15.7 consists of a length of wire of circular cross section, having a diameter, d, and Young’s modulus, E. The spring consists of n open loops each of which subtends an angle of 3π/2 radians at its centre; the length between the ends of the spring is L. Considering bending and axial strains only calculate the stiffness of the spring.

Ans. \((\sqrt{2}) \pi E d^{2} / L\left\{\left[48 L^{2}(\pi+1) / n^{2} d^{2}\right]+(3 \pi-2)\right\}\) .

Admin
Solution

Suppose a horizontal force, T, is applied to each end of the spring. Consider part of the left hand loop as shown in Fig. S.15.7

At any section B where the radius makes an angle θ with the horizontal the internal forces and moments are as shown. Then, resolving forces in a direction parallel to N

\(N=T \sin \theta\)

Also, taking moments about B

\(M=T\left(r \sin 45^{\circ}+r \sin \theta\right)=\operatorname{Tr}[(1 / \sqrt{2})+\sin \theta]\)

From geometry

\(2 r \cos 45^{\circ}=L / n\)

so that

\(r=L /(\sqrt{2}) n\)

Now apply a virtual unit load at A. Then

\(M_{ A }=\operatorname{Tr}[(1 / \sqrt{2})+\sin \theta], \quad M_{1}=1 r[(1 / \sqrt{2})+\sin \theta]\)

 

\(N_{ A }=T \sin \theta, \quad N_{1}=1 \sin \theta\)

From Eqs (15.21) and (15.12)

\(W_{ i , M}=\sum \int_{L} \frac{M_{ A } M_{ v }}{E I} d x\) (15.21)

\(w_{i, N}=\sum \int_{L} \frac{N_{ A } N_{ v }}{E A} d x\) (15.12)

the internal virtual work for the complete spring is

\(W_{ i }=n\left\{\int_{-\pi / 4}^{5 \pi / 4}\left(\frac{T^{2}}{E I}\right)[(1 / \sqrt{2})+\sin \theta]^{2} r d \theta+\int_{-\pi / 4}^{5 \pi / 4}\left(\frac{T}{A E}\right) \sin ^{2} \theta r d \theta\right\}\)

Integrating and substituting the limits gives

\(W_{ i }=n\left[\left(\frac{\operatorname{Tr}^{3}}{2 E I}\right)(3 \pi+3)+\left(\frac{\operatorname{Tr}}{8 A E}\right)(7 \pi-4)\right]\)

Now \(r=L /(\sqrt{2}) n, I=\pi d^{4} / 64\) and \(A=\pi d^{2} / 4\). Substituting in the above gives

\(W_{ i }=\left(\frac{T}{\pi E \sqrt{2} d^{2}}\right)\left[\left(\frac{48 L^{3}}{n^{2} d^{2}}\right)(\pi+1)+L(3 \pi-2)\right]\)

The external work done by the unit load is \(W_{ e }=1 \delta_{ A }\) where \(\delta_{ A }\) is the horizontal displacement of A. Equating the internal and external virtual work gives

\(\delta_{ A }=\left(\frac{T}{\pi E \sqrt{2} d^{2}}\right)\left[\left(\frac{48 L^{3}}{n^{2} d^{2}}\right)(\pi+1)+L(3 \pi-2)\right]\)

The spring stiffness, k, is equal to \(T / \delta_{ A }\) so that

\(k=\frac{(\sqrt{2}) \pi E d^{2}}{L\left[\left(48 L^{2} / n^{2} d^{2}\right)(\pi+1)+(3 \pi-2)\right]}\)
