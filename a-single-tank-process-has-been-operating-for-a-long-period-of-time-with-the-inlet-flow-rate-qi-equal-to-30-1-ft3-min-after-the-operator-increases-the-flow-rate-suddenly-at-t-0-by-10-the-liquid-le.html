Guest Question.

A single-tank process has been operating for a long period of time with the inlet flow rate \(q_{i}\) equal to \(30.1 ft ^{3} / min\). After the operator increases the flow rate suddenly at \(t=0\) by \(10 \%\), the liquid level in the tank changes as shown in Table E7.2.

Assuming that the process dynamics can be described by a first-order model, calculate the steady-state gain and the time constant using three methods:

(a) From the time required for the output to reach \(63.2 \%\) of the total change.

(b) From the initial slope of the response curve.

(c) From the slope of the fraction incomplete response curve.

(d) Compare the data and the three models by simulating their step responses.

Solution

Process gain,

\(K=\frac{h(5.0)-h(0)}{\Delta q_{i}}=\frac{6.52-5.50}{30.1 \times 0.1}=0.339 \frac{ min }{ ft ^{2}}\)

a) Output at \(63.2 \%\) of the total change

\(=5.50+0.632(6.52-5.50)=6.145 ft\)

Interpolating between \(h=6.07 ft \quad\) and \(h=6.18 ft\)

\(\tau=0.6+\frac{(0.8-0.6)}{(6.18-6.07)}(6.145-6.07) min =0.74 min\)

b)

\(\left.\frac{d h}{d t}\right|_{t=0} \approx \frac{h(0.2)-h(0)}{0.2-0}=\frac{5.75-5.50}{0.2} \frac{ ft }{ min }=1.25 \frac{ ft }{ min }\)

Using Eq. 7-15,

\(\tau=0.67\left(t_{2}-t_{1}\right)\) (7-15)

\(\tau=\frac{K M}{\left(\left.\frac{d h}{d t}\right|_{t=0}\right)}=\frac{0.339 \times(30.1 \times 0.1)}{1.25} \approx 0.82 min\)

c) The slope of the linear relation between \(t_{i}\) and \(z_{i} \equiv \ln \left[1-\frac{h\left(t_{i}\right)-h(0)}{h(\infty)-h(0)}\right]\) gives an approximation of \((-1 / \tau)\), according to Eq. 7-13.

\(\ln \left[\frac{y(\infty)-y_{i}}{y(\infty)}\right]=\frac{t_{i}-\theta}{\tau}\) (7-13)
