Guest Question.

For the plate bending problems (CPT and SDT) given in Fig. P12.4, give the specified primary and secondary degrees of freedom and their values for the meshes shown. The dashed lines in the figures indicate simply supported boundary conditions Use E, \(\nu \), h, a, and b in formulating the data. You are required to give values of the loads for at least a couple of representative loads.

Admin
Solution

The specified primary degrees of freedom in CPT(C) are

\(w_{1}=w_{6}=w_{11}=0, \quad \theta_{y 1}=\theta_{y 6}=\theta_{y 11}=\theta_{y 12}=\theta_{y 13}=\theta_{y 14}=\theta_{y 15}=0\)

 

\(\theta_{x 1}=\theta_{x 2}=\theta_{x 3}=\theta_{x 4}=\theta_{x 5}=\theta_{x 6}=\theta_{x 11}=0\)

 

For SDT replace \(\theta _{x}\) with \(\phi _{y}\) and \(\theta _{y}\) with \(\phi _{y}\).

 

As for the load vector, we have a load that varies linearly with the x-ccordinate. On a typical element, the load varies in the natural coordinate system as (see the figure below)

\(q(\xi, \eta)=q_{1} \psi_{1}(\xi)+q_{2} \psi_{2}(\xi), \quad \psi_{1}(\xi)=\frac{1}{2}(1-\xi), \quad \psi_{2}(\xi)=\frac{1}{2}(1+\xi)\)

Thus, the load vector components at nodes 1 and 2 of the SDT element are (\(F_{4}^{e}=F_{1}^{e}\) and \(F_{2}^{e}=F_{4}^{\epsilon}\))

\(F_{1}^{e}=\frac{h_{y}}{2}\left(\frac{q_{1}^{e} h_{x}}{2}+\frac{\left(q_{2}^{e}-q_{1}^{e}\right) h_{x}}{6}\right)=\frac{h_{y}}{2}\left(\frac{q_{1}^{e} h_{x}}{3}+\frac{q_{2}^{e} h_{x}}{6}\right)\)

 

\(F_{2}^{e}=\frac{h_{y}}{2}\left(\frac{q_{1}^{e} h_{x}}{2}+\frac{2\left(q_{2}^{e}-q_{1}^{e}\right) h_{x}}{6}\right)=\frac{h_{y}}{2}\left(\frac{q_{1}^{e} h_{x}}{6}+\frac{q_{2}^{e} h_{x}}{3}\right)\)

 

For example, we have

\(F_{1}=0.5 F_{6}=F_{11}=\frac{q_{0} h_{y}}{2}\left(\frac{h_{x} h_{x}}{6 a}\right)=\frac{q_{0} a b}{384}\)

 

\(F_{2}=0.5 F_{7}=F_{12}=\frac{q_{0} h_{y}}{2}\left(\frac{h_{x} h_{x}}{3 a}\right)+\frac{q_{0} h_{y}}{2}\left(\frac{h_{x} h_{x}}{3 a}+\frac{h_{x} h_{x}}{3 a}\right)=\frac{q_{0} a b}{64}\)

 

\(F_{3}=0.5 F_{8}=F_{13}=\frac{q_{0} h_{y}}{2}\left(\frac{h_{x} h_{x}}{6 a}+\frac{2 h_{x} h_{x}}{3 a}\right)+\frac{q_{0} h_{y}}{2}\left(\frac{2 h_{x} h_{x}}{3 a}+\frac{3 h_{x} h_{x}}{6 a}\right)\)

 

\(=\frac{q_{0} a b}{32}\)

For the CPT(N) element, the load vector can be computed using the load vector of a Eulerâ€”Bernoulli beam element foir linearly varying load . We have

\(\frac{q_{0} h_{x}}{a}\left\{\begin{array}{c}9 h_{x}+30 x_{a} \\h_{x}\left(2 h_{x}+5 x_{a}\right) \\21 h_{x}+30 x_{a} \\-h_{x}\left(3 h_{x}+5 x_{a}\right)\end{array}\right\} \frac{h_{y}}{12}\left\{\begin{array}{c}6 \\h_{y} \\6 \\-h_{y}\end{array}\right\}^{ T }\)

 

\(=\frac{q_{0} h_{x} h_{y}}{12 a}\left[\begin{array}{cccc}6\left(9 h_{x}+30 x_{a}\right) & h_{y}\left(9 h_{x}+30 x_{a}\right) & 6\left(9 h_{x}+30 x_{a}\right) & -h_{y}\left(9 h_{x}+30 x_{a}\right) \\6 h_{x}\left(2 h_{x}+5 x_{a}\right) & h_{y} h_{x}\left(2 h_{x}+5 x_{a}\right) & -6 h_{x}\left(2 h_{x}+5 x_{a}\right) & h_{y} h_{x}\left(2 h_{x}+5 x_{a}\right) \\6\left(21 h_{x}+30 x_{a}\right) & h_{y}\left(21 h_{x}+30 x_{a}\right) & 6\left(21 h_{x}+30 x_{a}\right) & -h_{y}\left(21 h_{x}+30 x_{a}\right) \\6 h_{x}\left(3 h_{x}+5 x_{a}\right) & h_{y} h_{x}\left(3 h_{x}+5 x_{a}\right) & 6 h_{x}\left(3 h_{x}+5 x_{a}\right) & -h_{y} h_{x}\left(3 h_{x}+5 x_{a}\right)\end{array}\right]\)

Hence, the loads at node 1, for example, are

\(Q_{1}=\frac{9 q_{0} a b}{64}, \quad M_{x 1}=\frac{q_{0} a^{2} b}{128}, \quad M_{y 1}=\frac{3 q_{0} a b^{2}}{64}, \quad M_{x y 1}=\frac{q_{0} a^{2} b^{2}}{1536}\)
