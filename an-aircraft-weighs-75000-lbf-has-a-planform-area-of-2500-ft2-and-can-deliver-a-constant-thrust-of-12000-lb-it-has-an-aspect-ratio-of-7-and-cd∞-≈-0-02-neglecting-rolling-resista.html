Guest Question.

An aircraft weighs 75,000 lbf, has a planform area of 2500 \(ft^2\), and can deliver a constant thrust of 12,000 lb. It has an aspect ratio of 7, and \(C_{D∞}\) ≈ 0.02. Neglecting rolling resistance, estimate the takeoff distance at sea level if takeoff speed equals 1.2 times stall speed. Take \(C_{L,max}\) = 2.0.

Admin
Solution

The stall speed from Eq. (7.72), with sea-level density ρ = 0.00237 slug/\(ft^3\), is

\(V_s = \left(\frac{2W}{C_{L,max} \rho A_p}\right)^{1/2}\) (7.72)

\(V_s = \left(\frac{2W}{C_{L,max} \rho A_p}\right)^{1/2} = \left[\frac{2(75,000)}{2.0(0.00237)(2500)}\right]^{1/2} = 112.5 ft/s\)

Hence takeoff speed \(V_0 = 1.2V_s\) = 135 ft/s. The drag is estimated from Eq. (7.71) for AR = 7 as

\(C_D \approx C_{D\infty} + \frac{C_L^2}{\pi AR}\) (7.71)

\(C_D \approx 0.02 + \frac{C_L^2}{7 \pi} = 0.02 + 0.0455C_L^2\)

A force balance in the direction of takeoff gives

\(F_s = m\frac{dV}{dt} = thrust – drag = T – kV^2 k = \frac{1}{2}C_D \rho A_p\) (1)

Since we are looking for distance, not time, we introduce dV/dt = V dV/ds into Eq. (1), separate variables, and integrate:

\(\int^{s_0}_0{dS} = \frac{m}{2} \int^{v_0}_0{\frac{d(V^2)}{T – kV^2}} k \approx const\)

or \(S_0 = \frac{m}{2k} \ln \frac{T}{T – kV_0^2} = \frac{m}{2k} \ln \frac{T}{T – D_0}\) (2)

where \(D_0 = kV_0^2\) is the takeoff drag. Equation (2) is the desired theoretical relation for takeoff distance. For the particular numerical values, take

m = \(\frac{75,000}{32.2}\) = 2329 slugs

\(C_{L_0} = \frac{W}{\frac{1}{2} \rho V_0^2 A_p} = \frac{75,000}{\frac{1}{2}(0.00237)(135)^2(2500)} = 1.39\)

 

\(C_{D_0} = 0.02 + 0.0455(C_{L_0})^2 = 0.108\)

\(k \approx \frac{1}{2}C_{D_0} \rho A_p = (\frac{1}{2})\)(0.108)(0.00237)(2500) = 0.319 slug/ft

\(D_0 = kV_0^2\) = 5820 lbf

Then Eq. (2) predicts that

\(S_0 = \frac{2329 slugs}{2(0.319 slug/ft)} \ln \frac{12,000}{12,000 – 5820} = 3650 \ln 1.94 = 2420 ft\)

A more exact analysis accounting for variable k [13] gives the same result to within 1 percent.
