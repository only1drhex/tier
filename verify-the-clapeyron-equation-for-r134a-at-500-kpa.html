\( \left(\frac{\partial P}{\partial T}\right)_{n}=\frac{600-400}{21.58-8.93}=15.81 kPa /{ }^{\circ} C \).

\( \text { We also observe that at } P=500 kPa , T=15.74^{\circ} C , h_{f g}=184.74 kJ / kg \text {, and } v_{f g}=0.0409-0.0008056=0.04009 m ^{3} / kg \text {. } \)

Checking the above Clapeyron equation, we have

\( 15.81 \stackrel{?}{=} \frac{184.74}{(15.74+273)(0.04009)}=15.96 kPa /{ }^{\circ} C \).

This is less than 1 percent difference, verifying the Clapeyron equation.
