Analyzing a Wye-Wye Circuit

A balanced three-phase Y-connected generator with positive sequence has an impedance of \(0.2 + j0.5 \ Ω/Φ\) and an internal voltage of \(120 \ V/Φ\) .The generator feeds a balanced three-phase Y-connected load having an impedance of \(39 + j28 \ Ω/Φ\) .The impedance of the line connecting the generator to the load is \(0.8 + j1.5 \ \ Ω/Φ.\) The a-phase internal voltage of the generator is specified as the reference phasor.

a) Construct the a-phase equivalent circuit of the system.
b) Calculate the three line currents \(I_{aA}, I_{bB}\) ,and \(I_{cC}\) .

c) Calculate the three phase voltages at the load, \(V_{AN}, V_{BN}\) ,and \(V_{CN}\) .

d) Calculate the line voltages \(V_{AB}, V_{BC}\) ,and \(V_{CA}\) at the terminals of the load.

e) Calculate the phase voltages at the terminals of the generator, \(V_{an}, V_{bn}\) ,and \(V_{cn}\) .

f) Calculate the line voltages \(V_{ab}, V_{bc}\) ,and \(V_{ca}\) at the terminals of the generator.

g) Repeat (a)–(f) for a negative phase sequence.

Admin
Solution

a) Figure 11.10 shows the single-phase equivalent circuit.

b) The a-phase line current is

\(I_{aA}=\frac{120\angle 0^\circ }{(0.2 + 0.8 + 39) + j(0.5 + 1.5 + 28)} \)
\(=\frac{120\angle 0^\circ }{40 + j30} \)
\(=2.4 \angle -36.87^\circ \) A

For a positive phase sequence,

\(I_{bB} =2.4 \angle -156.87^\circ \) A,
\(I_{cC} =2.4 \angle 83.13^\circ \) A.

c) The phase voltage at the A terminal of the load is

\(V_{AN} = (39 + j28)(2.4\angle -36.87^\circ) \)V,

\(= 115.22\angle -1.19^\circ\) V.

For a positive phase sequence,

\(V_{BN} = 115.22\angle -121.19^\circ\) V,

\(V_{CN} = 115.22\angle 118.81^\circ\) V.

d) For a positive phase sequence, the line voltages lead the phase voltages by 30° ; thus

\(V_{AB} = (\sqrt{3 } \angle 30^\circ )V_{AN} \)
\(= 199.58 \angle 28.81^\circ \)V,
\(V_{BC}= 199.58 \angle -91.19^\circ \)V,
\(V_{CA}= 199.58 \angle 148.81^\circ \)V.

e) The phase voltage at the a terminal of the source is

\(V_{an} = 120 – (0.2 + j0.5)(2.4 \angle -36.87^\circ)\)
\(=120 – 1.29\angle 31.33^\circ\)
\(=118.90 – j0.67\)
\(= 118.90\angle -0.32^\circ\)

For a positive phase sequence,

\(V_{bn}= 118.90 \angle -120.32^\circ\)V,
\(V_{cn}= 118.90 \angle 119.68 ^\circ\)V.

f) The line voltages at the source terminals are

\(V_{ab} = (\sqrt{3 } \angle 30^\circ )V_{an} \)
\(= 205.94\angle 29.68^\circ \)V,
\(V_{bc}= 205.94 \angle -90.32^\circ \)V,
\(V_{ca}= 205.94 \angle 149.68^\circ \)V.

g) Changing the phase sequence has no effect on the single-phase equivalent circuit. The three line currents are

\(I_{aA}= 2.4 \angle -36.87 ^\circ\)A,
\(I_{bB}= 2.4 \angle 83.13 ^\circ\)A,
\(I_{cC}= 2.4 \angle -156.87 ^\circ\) A.

The phase voltages at the load are

\(V_{AN}= 115.22 \angle -1.19^\circ \)V,
\(V_{BN}= 115.22 \angle 118.81^\circ \)V,
\(V_{CN}= 115.22 \angle -121.19^\circ\)V.

For a negative phase sequence, the line voltages lag the phase voltages by
30°:

\(V_{AB} = (\sqrt{3 } \angle 30^\circ )V_{AN} \)
\(= 199.58\angle -31.19^\circ \)V,
\(V_{BC}= 199.58 \angle 88.81^\circ \)V,
\(V_{CA}= 199.58 \angle -151.19^\circ \)V.

The phase voltages at the terminals of the generator are

\(V_{an}= 118.90 \angle -0.32 ^\circ\)V,
\(V_{bn}= 118.90\angle 119.68 ^\circ\)V,
\(V_{cn}= 118.90 \angle -120.32 ^\circ\) V.

The line voltages at the terminals of the generator are

\(V_{ab} = (\sqrt{3 } \angle 30^\circ )V_{an} \)
\(= 205.94\angle -30.32^\circ \)V,
\(V_{bc}= 205.94 \angle 89.68^\circ \)V,
\(V_{ca}= 205.94 \angle -150.32^\circ \)V.
