Guest Question.

Figure 12.30 illustrates an offset flywheel on a turntable, which is a problem typical of situations occurring in the design or analysis of machines in which gyroscopic forces must be considered. A round plate designated body 2 rotates about its central axis with a constant angular velocity \(\omega_{2}=5 \hat{ k } rad / s\). Mounted on this revolving plate are two bearings, A and B, which retain a shaft and a mass, 3, rotating at an angular velocity with respect to the rotating plate, \(\omega_{3 / 2}=350 \hat{ i } rad / s\). Rotating body 3 has a mass of 4.5 kg, a radius of gyration of 50 mm about its spin axis, and center of mass located at G. Calculate the bearing reactions at A and B.

ASSUMPTIONS

The weight of the shaft is negligible; body 3 rotates at a constant angular velocity with respect to the turntable; and bearing B can only support a radial load.

Admin
Solution

We begin by choosing the local body coordinate system shown by axes x and y in Fig. 12.30. The origin is at the center of mass, and the axes are principal axes of inertia. Note that this coordinate system is fixed to body 2, not to body 3, and it is not stationary. Since body 3 is radially symmetric about this x axis, its mass properties remain constant. From the given data and a kinematic analysis, we have

\(\begin{aligned}&I_{G_{3}}^{x x}=m_{3}\left(k_{3}^{x}\right)^{2}=(4.5 kg )(0.050 m )^{2}=0.01125 kg \cdot m ^{2} \\& \omega _{2}=5 \hat{ k }^{\prime} rad / s =5 \hat{ k } rad / s \\&\omega_{3}=\omega_{2}+\omega_{3 / 2}=350 \hat{ i }+5 \hat{ k } rad / s \\&\alpha_{2}=\alpha_{3}= 0\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}R _{ GO _{2}} &=-\left(150 \cos 60^{\circ}\right) \hat{ i }-\left(150 \sin 60^{\circ}\right) \hat{ j } mm \\&=-0.075 \hat{ i }-0.130 \hat{ j } m \\V _{G} &= \omega _{2} \times R _{G O_{2}}=0.650 \hat{ i }-0.375 \hat{ j } m / s \\R _{ AG } &=-0.050 \hat{ i } m \\R _{ BG } &=0.100 \hat{ i } m\end{aligned}\)

 

and the time rates of change of the unit vectors are

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{d \hat{ i }}{d t}=\omega_{2} \times \hat{ i }=5 \hat{ j } rad / s \\&\frac{d \hat{ j }}{d t}= \omega _{2} \times \hat{ j }=-5 \hat{ i } rad / s \\&\frac{d \hat{ k }}{d t}=\omega_{2} \times \hat{ k }= 0\end{aligned}\)

 

From Eq. (12.113), we find the momentum, which is,

\(L _{j}(t)=m_{j} V _{G_{j}}(t)\) (12.113)

 

\(\begin{aligned}L _{3} &=m_{3} V _{G}=(4.5 kg )(0.650 \hat{ i }-0.375 \hat{ j } m / s ) \\&=2.93 \hat{ i }-1.69 \hat{ j } kg \cdot m / s\end{aligned}\)

 

From Eq. (12.115), we find the dynamic force effects attributable to the motion of the center of mass. Remembering that \(\hat{ i } \text { and } \hat{ j }\) are moving and have derivatives, this equation gives

\(\sum F _{i j}=\frac{d L _{j}}{d t}\) (12.115)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sum F _{i 3} &=\frac{d L _{3}}{d t} \\F _{A_{23}}+ F _{B_{23}} &=2.93 \frac{d \hat{ i }}{d t}-1.69 \frac{d \hat{ j }}{d t}=8.45 \hat{ i }+14.65 \hat{ j } N \\F_{A_{23}}^{x}+F_{B_{23}}^{x} &=8.45 N \\F_{A_{23}}^{y}+F_{B_{23}}^{y} &=14.65 N \\F_{A_{23}}^{z}+F_{B_{23}}^{z} &=0\end{aligned}\) (1)

 

From Eqs. (12.118) and (12.117) we find the angular momentum, which is,

\(H _{G}=H_{G}^{x} \hat{ i }+H_{G}^{y} \hat{ j }+H_{G}^{z} \hat{ k }\) (12.117)

 

\(\begin{aligned}H_{G}^{x} &=+I_{G}^{x x} \omega^{x}-I_{G}^{x y} \omega^{y}-I_{G}^{x z} \omega^{z} \\H_{G}^{y} &=-I_{G}^{y x} \omega^{x}+I_{G}^{y y} \omega^{y}-I_{G}^{y z} \omega^{z} \\H_{G}^{z} &=-I_{G}^{z x} \omega^{x}-I_{G}^{z y} \omega^{y}+I_{G}^{z z} \omega^{z}\end{aligned}\) (12.118)

 

\(\begin{aligned}&H_{G_{3}}^{x}=\left(0.01125 kg \cdot m ^{2}\right)(350 rad / s )=3.94 kg \cdot m ^{2} / s \\&H_{G_{3}}^{y}=0 \\&H_{G_{3}}^{z}=I_{G}^{z z}(5 rad / s )=5 I_{G}^{z z} kg \cdot m ^{2} / s \\& H _{G_{3}}=3.94 \hat{ i }+5 I_{G}^{z z} \hat{ k } kg \cdot m ^{2} / s\end{aligned}\)

 

We can find the dynamic moment effects from Eq. (12.121); these are

\(\sum M _{i j}=\frac{d H }{d t}\) (12.121)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sum M _{G_{3}} &=\frac{d H _{G_{3}}}{d t} \\R _{A G} \times F _{A_{23}}+ R _{B G} \times F _{B_{23}} &=3.94 \frac{d \hat{ i }}{d t}+5 I_{G}^{zz} \frac{d \hat{ k }}{d t} N \cdot m \\(-0.050 \hat{ i } m ) \times F _{A_{23}}+(0.100 \hat{ i } m ) \times F _{B 23} &=3.94(5 \hat{ j }) N \cdot m .\end{aligned}\)

 

Equating the \(\hat{ j } \text { and } \hat{ k }\) components, respectively, gives

\(\begin{aligned}(0.050 m ) F_{A_{23}}^{z}-(0.100 m ) F_{B_{23}}^{z} &=19.7 N \cdot m \\-(0.050 m ) F_{A_{23}}^{y}+(0.100 m ) F_{B_{23}}^{y} &=0\end{aligned}\) (2)

 

Since bearing B can only support a radial load, \(F_{B_{23}}^{x}=0\). Therefore, solving Eqs. (1) and (2) simultaneously, we find

 

\(\begin{aligned}&F_{A_{23}}^{x}=8.45 N , \\&F_{A_{23}}^{y}=9.77 N , \\&F_{A_{23}}^{z}=131 N , \\&F_{B_{23}}^{y}=4.88 N , \\&F_{B_{23}}^{z}=-131 N .\end{aligned}\)

 

Therefore, the total bearing reactions at A and B are

\(\begin{aligned}& F _{A_{23}}=8.45 \hat{ i }+9.77 \hat{ j }+131 \hat{ k } N \\& F _{B_{23}}=4.88 \hat{ j }-131 \hat{ k } N\end{aligned}\)

 

We note that the effect of the gyroscopic couple is to lift the front bearing upward and to push the rear bearing against the plate. We can also note that there is no 5 x component in \(\sum M _{23}\); this justifies our assumption that Î±3 = 0 even though there is no motor controlling that shaft.
