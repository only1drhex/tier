Guest Question.

It is required to provide a strip footing to carry a wall load of 450 kN/m in a sandy soil with \(\phi^{\prime}=32^{\circ} \text { and } \gamma=18 kN / m ^{3}\). The unit weight of concrete is 23 kN/\(m ^{3}\). What is the necessary width so that the safety factor with respect to bearing capacity is 3?

Admin
Solution

Let’s assume \(D_{f}=0.5\) m, and that the entire 0.5 m is made of concrete.

 

\(\text { For strip footings, } L=\infty \text { and hence } s_{q}=s_{\gamma}=1\)

 

\(d_{q}=d_{\gamma}=1+0.1 \frac{0.5}{B} \tan \left(45+\frac{34}{2}\right)=1+0.0940 / B( m )\)

 

Plane-strain correction:

 

\(\phi_{\text {strip }}^{\prime}=32 \times 1.1-0.1 \times 0=35.2\)

 

\(\phi^{\prime}=35.2^{\circ} \rightarrow N_{q}=34.1 \text { and } N_{\gamma}=38.5\)

 

\(q_{\text {app,gross }}=\frac{450}{B}+0.5 \times 23=\frac{450}{B}+11.5 kPa\)

 

\(q_{\text {appnet }}=\frac{450}{B}+11.5-0.5 \times 18=\frac{450}{B}+2.5 kPa\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}q_{\text {ult, gross }} &=\left(1+\frac{0.0940}{B}\right) \times 18 \times 0.5 \times 34.1+\left(1+\frac{0.0940}{B}\right) \times 0.5 \times B \times 18 \times 38.5 \\&=339.5+346.5 B+\frac{28.85}{B} kPa\end{aligned}\)

 

\(q_{u l t, \text { net }}=339.5+346.5 B+\frac{28.85}{B}-0.5 \times 18=330.5+346.5 B+\frac{28.85}{B} kPa\)

 

\(q_{\text {all, net }}=\frac{q_{\text {ult, net }}}{3}=110.2+115.5 B+\frac{9.62}{B} kPa\)

 

Equating the applied and allowable pressures:

 

\(\frac{450}{B}+2.5=110.2+115.5 B+\frac{9.62}{B} \rightarrow B=1.55 m\)
