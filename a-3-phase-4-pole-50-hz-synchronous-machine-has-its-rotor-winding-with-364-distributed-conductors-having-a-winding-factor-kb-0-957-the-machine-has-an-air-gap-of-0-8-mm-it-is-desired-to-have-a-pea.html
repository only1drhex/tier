Guest Question.

A 3-phase, 4-pole, 50 Hz synchronous machine has its rotor winding with 364 distributed conductors having a winding factor \( K_{b} = 0.957\). The machine has an air-gap of 0.8 mm. It is desired to have a peak air-gap flux density of 1.65 T. Rotor length = 1.02 m, rotor diameter = 41 cm.

(a) Calculate the field current required and flux/pole.

(b) The armature has 3 full-pitch 11-turn coils/pole pair per phase. Calculate the open-circuit phase voltage and the line voltage of the phases are connected in star.

(c) It is desired to have an open-circuit rms 3/4th of that found in part (b). What should be the field current?

Admin
Solution

As the field has a distributed winding we can use the result for armature winding except that the field current is a fixed value i.e. \( \sqrt{2} \) will not be there.

(a) For the field (on fundamental basis)

\( F_{peak}=\frac{4}{\pi } \left[\frac{K_{b}N_{field}(series)I_{f}}{P} \right] \); Eq. (5.31) (i)

\( H_{peak}= F_{peak}/g\) (ii)

\( B_{peak} =\mu _{0}H_{peak}\) (iii)

or \( B_{peak}= \frac{a\mu _{0}}{\pi g} \left[ \frac {K_{b} N_{fields} (series)I_{f}}{4} \right]\) (iv)

\( 1.65= \frac{4\times 4\pi \times 10^{-7}}{\pi \times 0.8\times 10^{-2}} \left[\frac{0.957\times (364/2)\times I_{f}}{4} \right]\)

 

\( I_{f}= \frac{1.65\pi \times 8\times 10^{-3}\times 4\times 2}{4\times 4\pi \times 10^{-7}\times 0.957\times 364}\)

= 18.95 A

(b) Flux/pole, \( \Phi =\frac{4}{P} B_{peak} lr\) (Eq (5.7)) (v)

Substituting values

\( \Phi = 1.65\times 1.02\times 0.41/2=0.345 Wb\)

 

\( N_{ph}(series)=3\times 11\times \frac{P}{2} =66\)

 

Slot angle \( \gamma =\frac{60^{\circ}}{3} = 20^{\circ }, m=3\)

Breadth factor, \( K_{b} =\frac{\sin 3\times 10^{\circ}}{3 \sin 10^{\circ }} =0.96\)

\( E_{ph}=\sqrt{2} \pi K_{b} f N_{ph}(series) \Phi\)

Substituting values

\( E_{ph} =\sqrt{2} \pi \times 0.96 \times 50\times 66\times 0.345=4855 V\)

 

\( E_{line}= 4853 \sqrt{3} = 8409 V\)

\( I_{f}(new)=0.75\times 18.95=14.21 A\)
