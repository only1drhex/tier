Guest Question.

A 200-g block connected to a light spring for which the force constant is 5.00 N/m is free to oscillate on a horizontal, frictionless surface. The block is displaced 5.00 cm from equilibrium and released from rest, as in Figure 15.7.

(A) Find the period of its motion.

(B) Determine the maximum speed of the block.

(C) What is the maximum acceleration of the block?

(D) Express the position, speed, and acceleration as functions of time.

 

Admin
Solution

From Equations 15.9 and 15.10, we know that the angular frequency of a block–spring system is

\( \omega=\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \) (15.9)

\( T=\frac{2 \pi}{\omega} \) (15.10).

\( \omega=\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}=\sqrt{\frac{5.00 N / m }{200 \times 10^{-3} kg }}=5.00 rad / s \).

and the period is

\( T=\frac{2 \pi}{\omega}=\frac{2 \pi}{5.00 rad / s }=1.26 s \).

(B) Determine the maximum speed of the block.

We use Equation 15.17:

\( v_{\max }=\omega A=\sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} A \) (15.17).

\( v_{\max }=\omega A=(5.00 rad / s )\left(5.00 \times 10^{-2} m \right)=0.250 m / s \).

(C) What is the maximum acceleration of the block?

We use Equation 15.18:

\( a_{\max }=\omega^{2} A=\frac{k}{m} A \) (15.18).

\( a_{\max }=\omega^{2} A=(5.00 rad / s )^{2}\left(5.00 \times 10^{-2} m \right)=1.25 m / s ^{2} \).

(D) Express the position, speed, and acceleration as functions of time.

We find the phase constant from the initial condition that x= A at t = 0:

\( x(0)=A \cos \phi=A \).

\( \text { which tells us that } \phi=0 \text {. Thus, our solution is } x=A \cos \omega t \).

Using this expression and the results from (A), (B), and (C), we find that

\( x=A \cos \omega t=(0.0500 m ) \cos 5.00 t \).

\( v=\omega A \sin \omega t=-(0.250 m / s ) \sin 5.00 t \).

\( a=-\omega^{2} A \cos \omega t=-\left(1.25 m / s ^{2}\right) \cos 5.00 t \).
