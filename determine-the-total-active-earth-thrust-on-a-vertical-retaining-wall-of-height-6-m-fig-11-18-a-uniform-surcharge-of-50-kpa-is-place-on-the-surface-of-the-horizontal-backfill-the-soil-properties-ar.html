Guest Question.

Determine the total active earth thrust on a vertical retaining wall of height \(6 \mathrm{~m}\) (Fig.11.18). A uniform surcharge of \(50 \mathrm{kPa}\) is place on the surface of the horizontal backfill. The soil properties are: \(\phi^{\prime}=30^{\circ}, \gamma=18 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\) and \(c^{\prime}=0\).

Admin
Solution
\(K_{a}=\frac{1-\sin \phi^{\prime}}{1+\sin \phi^{\prime}}=\frac{1-\sin 30^{\circ}}{1+\sin 30^{\circ}}=1 / 3\)

At \(z=6 \mathrm{~m}\)

\(p_{a}=K_{a} \gamma z=\frac{1}{3} \times 18 \times 6=36 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\)

Active thrust due to surcharge load (Fig.11.18) is determined by:

\(p_{a}=K_{a} q=\frac{1}{3} \times 50=16.67 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\)

Total active thrust is now given by:

\(P_{a}=\frac{1}{2} \times 36 \times 6+16.67 \times 6=208 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}\)

 

\(\bar{z}=\frac{108 \times 2+100 \times 3}{208}=2.5 \mathrm{~m}\)
