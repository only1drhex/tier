Charging a Capacitor

A \(\text { 500. } \mu F\) capacitor is connected to a 9.00 V battery through a \(2.00 k \Omega\) resistor, thus completing a simple RC series circuit.

(a) How long does it take for the capacitor to reach 99% of \(q_{\max }\)? Express your answer in terms of the time constant, \(\tau\). Assume that 99% is an exact quantity.

(b) How much charge will have accumulated on the capacitor’s plates when \(t_{1}=\tau, t_{2}=2 \tau, \text { and } t_{3}=3 \tau ?\)

(c) Find the current in the circuit at the times in part (b).

Admin
Solution

(a) For this circuit,

 

\(\tau=R C=\left(2.00 \times 10^{3} \Omega\right)\left(5.00 \times 10^{-4} F \right)=1.00 s\)

 

\(0.99 q_{\max }=q_{\max }\left(1-e^{-\frac{t}{R C}}\right)\)

 

\(0.99=1-e^{-\frac{t}{R C}}\)

 

\(e^{-\frac{t}{R C}}=0.01\)

 

\(t=-R C \ln 0.01=-\tau \ln 0.01\)

 

\(t=-(1.00 s ) \ln 0.01\)

 

\(=4.61 s\)

 

(b) \(q_{\max }=\varepsilon C=(9.00 V )\left(5.00 \times 10^{-4} F \right)=4.50 \times 10^{-3} C\)

 

\(q(t)=q_{\max }\left(1-e^{-\frac{t}{R C}}\right)\)

 

\(q(\tau)=q_{\max }\left(1-e^{-\frac{\tau}{R C}}\right)=q_{\max }\left(1-e^{-\frac{R C}{R C}}\right)\)

 

\(=\left(4.50 \times 10^{-3} C \right)\left(1-\frac{1}{e}\right)=2.84 \times 10^{-3} C\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}q(2 \tau) &=q_{\max }\left(1-e^{-\frac{2 \tau}{R C}}\right)=q_{\max }\left(1-e^{-\frac{2 R C}{R C}}\right) \\&=\left(4.50 \times 10^{-3} C \right)\left(1-\frac{1}{e^{2}}\right)=3.89 \times 10^{-3} C\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}q(3 \tau) &=q_{\max }\left(1-e^{-\frac{3 \tau}{R C}}\right)=q_{\max }\left(1-e^{-\frac{3 R C}{R C}}\right) \\&=\left(4.50 \times 10^{-3} C \right)\left(1-\frac{1}{e^{3}}\right)=4.28 \times 10^{-3} C\end{aligned}\)

 

(c) \(I_{0}=\frac{\varepsilon}{R}=\frac{9.00 V }{2.00 k \Omega}=4.50 \times 10^{-3} A\)

 

\(I(t)=I_{0} e^{-\frac{t}{R C}}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}I(\tau) &=I_{0} e^{-\frac{\tau}{R C}}=I_{0} e^{-\frac{R C}{R C}}=\left(4.50 \times 10^{-3} A \right)\left(\frac{1}{e}\right) \\&=1.66 \times 10^{-3} A\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}I(2 \tau) &=I_{0} e^{-\frac{2 \tau}{R C}}=I_{0} e^{-\frac{2 R C}{R C}}=\left(4.50 \times 10^{-3} A \right)\left(\frac{1}{e^{2}}\right) \\&=0.609 \times 10^{-3} A\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}I(3 \tau) &=I_{0} e^{-\frac{3 \tau}{R C}}=I_{0} e^{-\frac{3 R C}{R C}}=\left(4.50 \times 10^{-3} A \right)\left(\frac{1}{e^{3}}\right) \\&=0.224 \times 10^{-3} A\end{aligned}\)

 

Making sense of the result

 

It is often helpful to calculate the time constant of the circuit first (\(\tau=1.00 s\) in this example). You can see that the capacitor acquired 99% of its maximum charge before \(t=5 \tau=5 s\).
