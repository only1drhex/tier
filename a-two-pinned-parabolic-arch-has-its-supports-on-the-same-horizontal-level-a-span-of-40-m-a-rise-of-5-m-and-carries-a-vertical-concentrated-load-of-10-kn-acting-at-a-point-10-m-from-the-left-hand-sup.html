Guest Question.

A two-pinned parabolic arch has its supports on the same horizontal level, a span of 40 m, a rise of 5 m and carries a vertical concentrated load of 10 kN acting at a point 10 m from the left-hand support. If the second moment of area of the arch cross section is governed by the secant rule calculate the horizontal thrust at the supports. With an origin of axes at the left-hand support the equation of the arch is \(y=4 h\left(L x-x^{2}\right) / L^{2}\) where h is the rise of the arch and L its span. Calculate also the maximum bending moment in the arch.
Ans. 11.2 kN, 33.0 kNm.

Admin
Solution

Referring to Fig. S.16.23 and taking moments about B

\(R_{ A , V } \times 40-10 \times 30=0\)

from which

\(R_{ A , V }=7.5 kN\)

Therefore

\(R_{ B , V }=2.5 kN\)

Since the secant assumption applies (see Eq. (16.15))

\(R_{1}=\frac{\int_{\text {Profile }} M_{0} y d x}{\int_{\text {Profile }} y^{2} d x}\) (16.15)

\(R_{ A , H }=R_{ B , H }=\frac{\int_{0}^{L} M_{0} y d x}{\int_{0}^{L} y^{2} d x}\)

where

\(M_{0}=7.5 x \quad 0 \leq x \leq 10 m\)

 

\(M_{0}=7.5 x-10(x-10)=100-2.5 x \quad 10 \leq x \leq 40 m\)

The equation of the arch is

\(y=\frac{4 h}{L^{2}}\left(L x-x^{2}\right)=\frac{4 \times 5}{40^{2}}\left(40 x-x^{2}\right)=0.0125\left(40 x-x^{2}\right)\)

From A to C

\(\int_{0}^{10} M_{0} y d x=\int_{0}^{10} 75 x \times 0.0125\left(40 x-x^{2}\right) d x=\left[1.25 x^{3}-0.023 x^{4}\right]_{0}^{10}=1020\)

From C to B

\(\int_{10}^{40} M_{0} y d x=\int_{10}^{40}(100-2.5 x) \times 0.0125\left(40 x-x^{2}\right) d x=\int_{10}^{40}\left(50 x-2.5 x^{2}+0.0313 x^{3}\right) d x=4954\)

Therefore

\(\int_{0}^{L} M_{0} y d x=1020+4954=5974\)

Also

\(\int_{0}^{40} y^{2} d x=\int_{0}^{40}\left[0.0125\left(40 x-x^{2}\right)\right]^{2} d x=533.3\)

Therefore

\(R_{ A , H }=R_{ B , H }=5974 / 533.3=11.2 kN\)

The maximum bending moment will occur at C (superimpose the simply supported beam bending moment diagram on the arch bending moment diagram) where

\(y_{ C }=0.0125\left(40 \times 10-10^{2}\right)=3.75 m\)

Then

\(M_{\max }=7.5 \times 10-11.2 \times 3.75=33.0 kNm\)
