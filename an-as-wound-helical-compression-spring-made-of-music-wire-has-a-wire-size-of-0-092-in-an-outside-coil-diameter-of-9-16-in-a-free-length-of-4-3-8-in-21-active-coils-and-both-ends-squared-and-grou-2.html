Guest Question.

An as-wound helical compression spring, made of music wire, has a wire size of 0.092 in, an outside coil diameter of \(\frac {9}{16}\) in, a free length of 4 \(\frac {3}{8}\) in, 21 active coils, and both ends squared and ground. The spring is unpeened. This spring is to be assembled with a preload of 5 lbf and will operate with a maximum load of 35 lbf during use.
(a) Estimate the factor of safety guarding against fatigue failure using a torsional Gerber fatigue failure criterion with Zimmerli data.
(b) Repeat part (a) using the Sines torsional fatigue criterion (steady stress component has no effect), with Zimmerli data.
(c) Repeat using a torsional Goodman failure criterion with Zimmerli data.
(d) Estimate the critical frequency of the spring.

Admin
Solution

The mean coil diameter is D = 0.5625 − 0.092 = 0.4705 in. The spring index is C =D/d = 0.4705/0.092 = 5.11. Then

\(K_{B} =\frac {4C + 2}{4C − 3} =\frac {4(5.11) + 2}{4(5.11) − 3 }= 1.287\)

From Eqs. (10–31),

\(F_{a} =\frac {F_{max} − F_{min}}{2}\) (10–31a)
\(F_{m} =\frac {F_{max} + F_{min}}{2}\) (10–31b)

\(F_{a} =\frac {35 − 5}{2} = 15 lbf F_{m} =\frac{35 + 5}{2}=20 lbf \)

The alternating shear-stress component is found from Eq. (10–32) to be

\(τ_{a} = K_{B} \frac {8F_{a}D}{πd^{3}}\) (10–32)

\(τ_{a} = K_{B} \frac {8F_{a}D}{πd^{3}}=(1.287) \frac{8(15)0.4705}{π(0.092)^{3}}(10^{−3}) = 29.7 kpsi\)

Equation (10–33) gives the midrange shear-stress component

\(τ_{m} = K_{B} \frac {8F_{m}D}{πd^{3}}\) (10–33)

\(τ_{m} = K_{B} \frac {8F_{m}D}{πd^{3}} = 1.287 \frac {8(20)0.4705}{π(0.092)^{3}} (10^{−3}) = 39.6 kpsi\)

From Table 10–4 we find A = 201 kpsi · \(in^{m}\) and m = 0.145. The ultimate tensile strength is estimated from Eq. (10–14) as

\(S_{ut} =\frac {A}{d_{m}}\) (10-14)

\(S_{ut} =\frac {A}{d_{m}} =\frac {201}{0.092^{0.145}} = 284.1 kpsi\)

Also the shearing ultimate strength is estimated from

\(S_{su} = 0.67S_{ut} = 0.67(284.1) = 190.3 kpsi\)

The load-line slope \( r = τ_{a}/τ_{m}\) = 29.7/39.6 = 0.75.

(a) The Gerber ordinate intercept for the Zimmerli data, Eq. (10–28), is

\(S_{sa} = 35 kpsi (241 MPa) S_{sm} = 55 kpsi (379 MPa) \) (10–28)

\(S_{se} =\frac {S_{sa}}{1 − (S_{sm}/S_{su})^{2}} =\frac {35}{1 − (55/190.3)^{2}} = 38.2 kpsi\)

The amplitude component of strength \(S_{sa}\) , from Table 6–7, p. 299, is

\(S_{sa} =\frac {r^{2}S^{2}_{su}}{2S_{se}} \left[ -1 +\sqrt { 1 + \left( \frac {2S_{se}}{r S_{su}}\right)^{2} } \right]\)

\(=\frac {0.75^{2} 190.3^{2}}{2(38.2)} \left\{-1+\sqrt { 1+ \left[\frac { 2(38.2)}{0.75(190.3)} \right]^{2} } \right\}=35.8 kpsi\)

and the fatigue factor of safety \(n_{f}\) is given by

\(n_{f} =\frac {S_{sa}}{τ_{a}} =\frac {35.8}{29.7} = 1.21\)

(b) The Sines failure criterion ignores \(S_{sm}\) so that, for the Zimmerli data with \(S_{sa}\) = 35 kpsi,

\(n_{f} =\frac {S_{sa}}{τ_{a}} =\frac {35}{29.7} = 1.18\)

(c) The ordinate intercept \(S_{se}\) for the Goodman failure criterion with the Zimmerli data is

\(S_{se} =\frac {S_{sa}}{1 − (S_{sm}/S_{su})} =\frac {35}{1 − (55/190.3)} = 49.2 kpsi\)

The amplitude component of the strength \(S_{sa}\) for the Goodman criterion, from Table 6–6, p. 299, is

\(S_{sa} =\frac {r S_{se} S_{su}}{r S_{su} + S_{se}} =\frac {0.75(49.2)190.3}{0.75(190.3) + 49.2} = 36.6 kpsi\)

The fatigue factor of safety is given by

\(n_{f} =\frac {S_{sa}}{τ_{a}} =\frac {36.6}{29.7} = 1.23\)

(d) Using Eq. (10–9) and Table 10–5, we estimate the spring rate as

\(k\dot{=} \frac {d^{4}G}{8D^{3}N}\) (10–9)

\(k =\frac {d^{4}G}{8D^{3}Na} =\frac {0.092^{4}[11.75(10^{6})]}{8(0.4705)^{3}21} = 48.1 lbf/in\)

From Eq. (10–27) we estimate the spring weight as

\(W = ALγ =\frac {πd^{2}}{4} (πDN_{a})(γ ) =\frac {π^{2}d^{2}DN_{a} γ}{4}\) (10–27)

\(W =\frac {π^{2}(0.092^{2})0.4705(21)0.284}{4} = 0.0586 lbf\)

and from Eq. (10–25) the frequency of the fundamental wave is

\(f =\frac {1}{2} \sqrt {\frac {kg}{W}}\) (10–25)

\(f_{n} =\frac {1}{2} \left [\frac {48.1(386)}{0.0586}\right]^{1/2}= 281 Hz\)

If the operating or exciting frequency is more than 281/20 = 14.1 Hz, the spring may have to be redesigned.
