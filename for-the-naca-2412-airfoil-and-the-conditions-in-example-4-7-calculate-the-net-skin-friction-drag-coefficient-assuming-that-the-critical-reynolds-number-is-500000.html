Guest Question.

For the NACA 2412 airfoil and the conditions in , calculate the net skin friction drag coefficient assuming that the critical Reynolds number is 500,000.

Admin
Solution

Consider Figure 4.44, which shows a flat plate with a laminar boundary layer extending from the leading edge over the distance \(x_{1}\) to the transition point (region 1), and a turbulent boundary extending over the distance \(x_{2}\) from the transition point to the trailing edge (region 2). The critical Reynolds number is

\(\operatorname{Re}_{x_{ cr }}=\frac{\rho_{\infty} V_{\infty} x_{ cr }}{\mu_{\infty}}=5 \times 10^{5}\)

where in Figure 4.44, \(x_{cr}= x_{1}\). Hence

\( \operatorname{Re}_{x_{ cr }}=\frac{\rho_{\infty} V_{\infty} x_{1}}{\mu_{\infty}}=5 \times 10^{5} \)

The Reynolds number based on chord length is given as

\(\operatorname{Re}_{c}=\frac{\rho_{\infty} V_{\infty} c}{\mu_{\infty}}=3.1 \times 10^{6}\)

Thus

\(\frac{\operatorname{Re}_{x_{ Cr }}}{\operatorname{Re}_{c}}=\frac{5 \times 10^{5}}{3.1 \times 10^{6}}=0.1613=\frac{\left(\rho_{\infty} V_{\infty} x_{1} / \mu_{\infty}\right)}{\left(\rho_{\infty} V_{\infty} c / \mu_{\infty}\right)}=\frac{x_{1}}{c}\)

This locates the transition point relative to the chord length, that is, in Figure 4.44, we have

\(\frac{x_{1}}{c}=0.1613\)

Because the Reynolds number in the equations for skin friction drag coefficient is always based on length measured from the leading edge, we cannot simply calculate the turbulent skin friction drag coefficient for region 2 by using Equation (4.88) with a Reynolds number based on \(x_{2}\). Rather, we must carry out the following procedure.

\(C_{f}=\frac{0.074}{\operatorname{Re}^{1 / 5}} (4.88)\)

Assuming all turbulent flow over the entire length of the plate, the drag (on one side of the plate) is \(\left(D_{f, c}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}\), where

\(\left(D_{f, c}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}=q_{\infty} S\left(C_{f, c}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}\)

As usual, we are dealing with the drag per unit span, hence S = c(1).

\(\left(D_{f, c}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}=q_{\infty} c\left(C_{f, c}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}\)

The turbulent drag on just region 1 is \(\left(D_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}\):

\(\left(D_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}=q_{\infty} S\left(C_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}\)

Here, \(S = (x_{1})(1):\)

\(\left(D_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}=q_{\infty} x_{1}\left(C_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}\)

Thus, the turbulent drag just on region 2,\(\left(D_{f, 2}\right)_{\text {turbulent }},\) is

\(\left(D_{f, 2}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}=\left(D_{f, c}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}-\left(D_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}\)

\(\left(D_{f, 2}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}=q_{\infty} c\left(C_{f, c}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}-q_{\infty} x_{1}\left(C_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}\)

The laminar drag on region 1 is \(\left(D_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {laminar }}\)

\(\left(D_{f .1}\right)_{\text {laminar }}=q_{\infty} S\left(C_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {laminar }}=q_{\infty} x_{1}\left(C_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {laminar }}\)

The total skin-friction drag on the plate, \(D_{f}\), is then

\(D_{f}=\left(D_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {laminar }}+\left(D_{f, 2}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}\)

or \(D_{f}=q_{\infty} x_{1}\left(C_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {laminar }}+q_{\infty} c\left(C_{f, c}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}-q_{\infty} x_{1}\left(C_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {turtulent }} (4.90)\)

The total skin-friction drag coefficient is

\(C_{f}=\frac{D_{f}}{q_{\infty} S}=\frac{D_{f}}{q_{\infty} c} (4.91)\)

Combining Equations (4.90) and (4.91):

\(C_{f}=\frac{x_{1}}{c}\left(C_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {laminar }}+\left(C_{f, c}\right)_{\text {turtulent }}-\frac{x_{1}}{c}\left(C_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {turtulent }} (4.92)\)

Since \(x_{1}/c = 0.1613,\) Equation (4.92) becomes

\(C_{f}=0.1613\left(C_{f .1}\right)_{\text {laminar }}+\left(C_{f, c}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}-0.1613\left(C_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {turbulent }} (4.93)\)

The various skin friction drag coefficients in Equation (4.93) are obtained as follows. The Reynolds number for region 1 is

\(\operatorname{Re}_{x_{1}}=\frac{\rho_{\infty} V_{\infty} x_{1}}{\mu_{\infty}}=\frac{x_{1}}{c}\left(\frac{\rho_{\infty} V_{\infty} c}{\mu_{\infty}}\right)=\frac{x_{1}}{c} \operatorname{Re}_{c}=0.1613\left(3.1 \times 10^{6}\right)=5 \times 10^{5}\)

(Of course, we could have written this down directly because \(x = x_{1}\) is the transition point, determined from the critical Reynolds number that is given as \(5 Ã— 10^{5}.\)) From Equation (4.86) for laminar flow, with the Reynolds number based on \(x_{1}\), we have

\(C_{f}=\frac{1.328}{\sqrt{\operatorname{Re}_{c}}} (4.86)\)

\(\left(C_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {laminar }}=\frac{1.328}{\sqrt{\operatorname{Re}_{x_{1}}}}=\frac{1.328}{\sqrt{5 \times 10^{5}}}=0.00188\)

The value of \(\left(C_{f, c}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}\) has already been calculated in , namely,

\(\left(C_{f, c}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}=0.00372 \text { (for one side) }\)

From Equation (4.88) with the Reynolds number based on \(x_{1}\),

\(C_{f}=\frac{0.074}{\operatorname{Re}_{c}^{1 / 5}} (4.88)\)

\(\left(C_{f, 1}\right)_{\text {turbulent }}=\frac{0.074}{\operatorname{Re}_{x_{1}}^{1 / 5}}=\frac{0.074}{\left(5 \times 10^{5}\right)^{0.2}}=0.00536\)

Inserting these values into Equation (4.93), we have

\(C_{f}=0.1613(0.00188)+0.00372-.1613(0.00536)=0.003158\)

Taking into account both sides of the flat plate,

\(\text { Net } C_{f}=2(0.003158)=0.0063\)
