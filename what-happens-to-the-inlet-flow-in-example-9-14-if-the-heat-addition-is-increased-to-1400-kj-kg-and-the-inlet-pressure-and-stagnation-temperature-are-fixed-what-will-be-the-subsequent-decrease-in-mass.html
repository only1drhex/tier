Guest Question.

What happens to the inlet flow in if the heat addition is increased to 1400 kJ/kg and the inlet pressure and stagnation temperature are fixed? What will be the subsequent decrease in mass flow?

Admin
Solution

For q = 1400 kJ/kg, the exit will be choked at the stagnation temperature:

\(T^*_0 = T_{01} + \frac{q}{c_p} = 303 + \frac{1.4 E6 J/kg}{1005 J/(kg \cdot K)} \approx 1696 K\)

This is higher than the value \(T^*_0 = 1521\) K in , so we know that condition 1 will have to choke down to a lower Mach number. The proper value is found from the ratio \(T_{01}/T_0^*\) = 303/1696 = 0.1787. From Table B.4 or Eq. (9.78a) for this condition, we read the new, lowered entrance Mach number: \(Ma_{1,new}\) â‰ˆ 0.203. With \(T_{01} and p_1\) known, the other inlet properties follow from this Mach number:

\(\frac{T_0}{T^*_0} = \frac{(k + 1) Ma^2 [2 + (k – 1) Ma^2]}{(1 + k Ma^2)^2}\) (9.78a)

\(T_1 = \frac{T_{01}}{1 + 0.2 Ma_1^2} = \frac{303}{1 + 0.2(0.203)^2} = 301\) K

\(a_1 = \sqrt{kRT_1} = [1.4(287)(301)]^{1/2} = 348\) m/s

\(V_1 = Ma_1 a_1 =\) (0.203)(348 m/s) = 71 m/s

Finally, the new lowered mass flow per unit area is

\(\frac{\dot{m}_{new}}{A} = \rho_1 V_1 = (1.74 kg/m^3)(71 m/s) = 123 kg/(s \cdot m^2)\)

This is 7 percent less than in , due to choking by excess heat addition.
