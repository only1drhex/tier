Guest Question.

In , if the total flow Q is 6.50 mgd, how much lost head occurs between A and E, and how does Q divide in the loop? Use two solutions, the percentage method and the equivalent pipe method.

Admin
Solution

In a parallel-pipe system, the common hydraulic quantity is the lost head across the loop (A to E).
The solution will proceed as if had not been solved.
Assuming a lost head, A to E, of 24 ft, the values of flow for the assumed lost head may be obtained from Diagram B-l.

\(\begin{array}{ll}S_{12}=24 / 12=2.0 ft / 1000 ft , & Q_{12}=0.95 mgd (41.3 \%) \\S_{8}=24 / 4=6.0 ft / 1000 ft , & Q_{8}=0.60 mgd (26.1 \%) \\S_{10}=24 / 8=3.0 ft / 1000 ft , & Q_{10}=0.75 mgd (32.6 \%) \\& \text { total } Q=2.30 mgd (100.0\%)\end{array}\)

(a) Percentage Method
The flow in each branch of the loop will be a constant percentage of the total flow through the loop for any reasonable range of lost heads across the loop. The percentages shown above agree favorably with the percentages tabulated in (within the accuracy of the diagram). Apply these percentages to the given flow of 6.50 mgd

\(\begin{aligned}Q_{12}=41.3 \% \times 6.50 &=2.68 mgd \\Q_{8}=26.1 \% \times 6.50 &=1.70 mgd \\Q_{10}=32.6 \% \times 6.50 &=2.12 mgd \\Q &=6.50 mgd\end{aligned}\) \(\begin{array}{ll}S_{12}=13.5 ft / 1000 ft , & LH _{A-E}=162 ft \\S_{8}=40.0 ft / 1000 ft , & LH _{A-E}=160 ft \\S_{10}=20.0 ft / 1000 ft , & LH _{A-E}=160 ft\end{array}\)

This method gives a check on the calculations, as indicated above for the three lost head values.
It is the preferred method.
(b) Equivalent Pipe Method (use a 12″ diameter) The calculation of flows for an assumed lost head must be made, as for the first method. Using the values above, for a lost head of 24 ft a flow of 2.30 mgd is produced in the given looping system.
An equivalent pipe must produce the same flow for the lost head of 24 ft, i.e.,

\(Q=2.30 mgd , \quad \text { lost head }=24 ft , \quad \text { and } \quad S_{12}=10.1 ft / 1000 ft \text { from Diagram B-1 }\).

\(\text { From } S=\frac{h}{L}, \quad 10.1=\frac{24 ft }{L_{E} \text { in } 1000 ft } \quad \text { and } \quad L_{E}=2380 ft \left(\text { of } 12^{\prime \prime} \text { pipe, } C_{1}=100\right)\)

For the given flow of 6.50 mgd, \(S_{12}\) = 68 ft/1000 ft and total lost head A to E = 68 Ã— 2.38 = 162 ft. With this lost head, the three values of flow can be obtained.
