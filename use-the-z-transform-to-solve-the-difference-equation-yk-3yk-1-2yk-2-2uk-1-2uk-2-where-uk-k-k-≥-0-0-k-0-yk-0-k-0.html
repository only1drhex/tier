Guest Question.

Use the z-transform to solve the difference equation

\(y(k)-3 y(k-1)+2 y(k-2)=2 u(k-1)-2 u(k-2)\),

where

\(u(k)= \begin{cases}k, & k \geq 0, \\ 0, & k<0,\end{cases}\)

\(y(k)=0, \quad k<0\).

Admin
Solution
\(\frac{Y(z)}{U(z)}=\frac{2\left(z^{-1}-z^{-2}\right)}{1-3 z^{-1}-2 z^{-2}}=\frac{2}{z-2}\)

 

\(u(k)=\left\{\begin{array}{ll} k & k \geq 0 \\ 0 & k<0\end{array}\right\}\)

 

\(\Longrightarrow U(z)=\frac{z}{(z-1)^{2}}\)

 

\(Y(z)=\frac{2}{z-2} \times \frac{z}{(z-1)^{2}}=\frac{2 z}{z-2} – \frac{2 z}{z-1}-\frac{2 z}{(z-1)^{2}}\)

Taking the inverse z-transform from Table 8.1,

\(y(k)=2\left(2^{k}-1-k\right) \quad(k \geq 0)\)
