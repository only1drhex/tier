Guest Question.

A half-wave ZVS quasi-resonant buck converter has the following specifications:

\(V_{in} = 20 V, \overline{V}_o = 10 V,\overline{I}_o = 4.215 A, f_s =\frac{f_R}{2}\) = switching frequency = \(260 kHz, C_F\) and \(L_F\) are chosen so that \(v_o\) and \(i_o\) are pure dc.

Assuming ideal components perform the following:

a) Calculate the resonant capacitance and inductance.

b) Calculate the peak resonant capacitor voltage.

c) Find the time duration of the modes and calculate the output voltage.

d) Verify the theoretical results with the respective simulation ones.

Admin
Solution

a) \(f_R = 2 \times f_s = 2 \times 260 kHz = 520 kHz\) or \(\omega_R = 2\pi f_s = 3267.25 \times 10^3 \frac{rad}{s}\)

The values of the resonant components are given by: \(\omega_R = \frac{1}{\sqrt{L_RC_R}}\).

Choosing \(L_R = 2 \mu H\) the value of the resonant capacitor is:

\(C_R = \frac{1}{L_R(\omega_R)^2} = \frac{1}{2 \times 10^{-6}(3267.25 \times 10^3)^2} = 0.047 \mu F\)

b) \(|Z_R|\) = characteristic impedance of the resonant circuit

\(= \sqrt{\frac{L_R}{C_R}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 10^{-6}}{0.047 \times 10^{-6}}}=6.52 \Omega\)

Therefore, the peak resonant capacitor voltage is:

\(\widehat{V}_{CR}=V_{in} + \overline{I}_o |Z_R| = 20 + 4.215 \times 6.52 = 47.48 V\)

The relation for ZVS \(\overline{I}_o \geq \frac{V_{in}}{|Z_R|} = \frac{20}{6.52} = 3.08 A\) is satisfied.

c) \(\tau_1 = \frac{V_{in} C_R}{\overline{I}_o} = \frac{20 \times 0.047 \times 10^{-6}}{4.215} = 0.23 \mu s\)

 

\(\tau_2 = \frac{1}{\omega_R}[\sin^{-1}(\frac{V_{in}}{\overline{I}_o |Z_R|})+ \pi] = \frac{1}{3267.25 \times 10^3}[\sin^{-1}(\frac{20}{4.215 \times 6.52})+ \pi] = 1.21 \mu s\)

(in order to convert degrees to rads , just multiply rads by \(\pi/180\))

\(\tau_3 = -(\frac{L_R}{V_{in}})\overline{I}_o \cos (\omega_R \tau_2)\)

 

\( = -(\frac{2 \times 10^{-6}}{20}) \times 4.215 \times \cos(3267.25 \times 10^3 \times 1.21 \times 10^{-6})\)

 

\(= 0.29 \mu s\)

 

\(\tau_4 = \overline{I}_o \frac{L_R}{V_{in}}= 4.215 \frac{2 \times 10^{-6}}{20} = 0.42 \mu s\)

 

\(T_s = \frac{1}{260 \times 10^3} = 3.846 \mu s\)

 

\(t_4 = \tau_1 + \tau_2 + \tau_3 + \tau_4 = (0.23 + 1.21 + 0.29 + 0.42) \times 10^{-6} = 2.15 \mu s\)

 

\(\tau_5 = T_s – (\tau_1 + \tau_2 + \tau_3 + \tau_4) = 3.846 \mu sec – (0.23 + 1.21 + 0.29 + 0.42) \mu s\)

 

\(= 1.696 \mu sec\)

 

\(\overline{V}_o = V_{in} f_s (\frac{\tau_1}{2} + \tau_5) = 20 \times 260 \times 10^3 \times (\frac{0.23}{2} + 1.696) \times 10^{-6} = 9.42 V\)

d) The SPICE simulated results of this example are presented in Fig. 7.58.

As can be seen from Fig. 7.58 the theoretical results are in close agreement with the simulation ones.
