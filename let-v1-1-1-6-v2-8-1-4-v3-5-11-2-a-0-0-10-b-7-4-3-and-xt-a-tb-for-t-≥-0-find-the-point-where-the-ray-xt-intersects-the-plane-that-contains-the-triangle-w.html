Guest Question.

Let \( v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ -6 \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{2} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 8 \\ 1 \\ -4 \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{3} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 5 \\ 11 \\ -2 \end{matrix} \right ] , a = \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{matrix} \right ] , b = \left [ \begin{matrix} .7 \\ .4 \\ -3 \end{matrix} \right ] \) , and x(t) = a + tb for t ≥ 0. Find the point where the ray x(t) intersects the plane that contains the triangle with vertices \( v_{1} , v_{2} \), and \( v_{3} \). Is this point inside the triangle?

Admin
Solution

The plane is aff {\( v_{1} , v_{2} , v_{3} \)}. A typical point in this plane may be written as \( (1 – c_{2} – c_{3})v_{1} + c_{2}v_{2} + c_{3}v_{3} \) for some \( c_{2} \) and \( c_{3} \). (The weights in this combination sum to 1.) The ray x(t) intersects the plane when \( c_{2} \), \( c_{3} \), and t satisfy

\( (1 – c_{2} – c_{3})v_{1} + c_{2}v_{2} + c_{3}v_{3} = a + tb \)

Rearrange this as \( c_{2}(v_{2} – v_{1}) + c_{3}(v_{3} – v_{1}) + t(-b) = a – v_{1} \). In matrix form,

\( \left [ \begin{matrix} v_{2} – v_{1} & v_{3} – v_{1} & -b \end{matrix} \right ] \left [ \begin{matrix} c_{2} \\ c_{3} \\ b \end{matrix} \right ] = a – v_{1} \)

For the specific points given here,

\( v_{2} – v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 7 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{matrix} \right ] , v_{3} – v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} 4 \\ 10 \\ 4 \end{matrix} \right ] , a – v_{1} = \left [ \begin{matrix} -1 \\ -1 \\ 16 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Row reduction of the augmented matrix above produces

\( \left [ \begin{matrix} 7 & 4 & -.7 & -1 \\ 0 & 10 & -.4 & -1 \\ 2 & 4 & 3 & 16 \end{matrix} \right ] \sim \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & .3 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & .1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 5 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Thus \( c_{2} = .3 , c_{3} = .1 \text{and} t = 5 \). Therefore, the intersection point is

\( x(5) = a + 5b = \left [ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 10 \end{matrix} \right ] + 5\left [ \begin{matrix} .7 \\ .4 \\ -3 \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 3.5 \\ 2.0 \\ -5.0 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Also,

\( x(5) = (1 – .3 – .1)v_{1} + .3v_{2} + .1v_{3} \)

\( = .6\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ -6 \end{matrix} \right ] + .3\left [ \begin{matrix} 8 \\ 1 \\ -4 \end{matrix} \right ] + .1\left [ \begin{matrix} 5 \\ 11 \\ -2 \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 3.5 \\ 2.0 \\ -5.0 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

The intersection point is inside the triangle because the barycentric weights for x(5) are all positive.
