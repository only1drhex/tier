Guest Question.

Solve the problem described by following differential equation and boundary conditions:
\(\frac{d^{2} u}{d x^{2}}-u+x^{2}=0\) for \(0<x<1\) (3.2.64)
\(u(0)=0 \quad u( 1 )=0\) (3.2.65)
using two elements of the least squares model
\(I=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left[\left(-\frac{d u}{d x}+\frac{P}{a}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{d P}{d x}+f\right)^{2}\right] d x\)
Compare the results with the exact solution and those of the weak form finite element model.

Admin
Solution

The governing equation is slightly more general than equation :

\(-\frac{d}{d x}\left(a \frac{d u}{d x}\right)=f\)

Hence, we consider the more general equation

\(-\frac{d}{d x}\left(a \frac{d u}{d x}\right)+c u=f\) (1)

and rewrite it as a pair of first-order equations

\(-\frac{d u}{d x}+\frac{P}{a}=0, \quad-\frac{d P}{d x}+c u-f=0\) (2)

The least-squares functional becomes

\(I=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left[\left(-\frac{d u}{d x}+\frac{P}{a}\right)^{2}+\left(-\frac{d P}{d x}+c u-f\right)^{2}\right] d x\)

Setting \(\delta_{u} I=0 \text { and } \delta_{P} I=0\),we obtain the integral statements

\(\delta_{u} I=2 \int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left[-\frac{d \delta u}{d x}\left(-\frac{d u}{d x}+\frac{P}{a}\right)+c \delta u\left(-\frac{d P}{d x}+c u-f\right)\right] d x=0\) (3)

\(\delta_{P} I=2 \int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left[\frac{\delta P}{a}\left(-\frac{d u}{d x}+\frac{P}{a}\right)-\frac{d \delta P}{d x}\left(-\frac{d P}{d x}+c u-f\right)\right] d x=0\) (4)

For this more general case, the finite element model (4) :

\(\left[\begin{array}{cc}{\left[K^{11}\right]} & {\left[K^{12}\right]} \\{\left[K^{12}\right]^{T}} & {\left[K^{22}\right]}\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{c}\{u\} \\\{P\}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}\left\{F^{1}\right\} \\\left\{F^{2}\right\}\end{array}\right\}\)

is still valid with

\(K_{i j}^{11}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left(\frac{d \psi_{i}}{d x} \frac{d \psi_{j}}{d x}+c^{2} \psi_{i} \psi_{j}\right) d x\)

 

\(K_{i j}^{12}=-\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left(\frac{1}{a} \frac{d \psi_{i}}{d x} \phi_{j}+c \psi_{i} \frac{d \phi_{j}}{d x}\right) d x=K_{j i}^{21}\)

 

\(K_{i j}^{22}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}}\left[\frac{1}{a^{2}} \phi_{i} \phi_{j}+\frac{d \phi_{i}}{d x} \frac{d \phi_{j}}{d x}\right] d x\)

 

\(F_{i}^{1}=\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}} c \psi_{i} f(x) d x, \quad F_{i}^{2}=-\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}} \frac{d \phi_{i}}{d x} f(x) d x\) (5)

For the choice of linear interpolation functions for \(\psi _{i}\) and \(\phi _{i}\) and elementwise constant values of a, c and f, the element coefficients in (5) are

\(K ^{11}=\frac{1}{h_{e}}\left[\begin{array}{rr}1 & -1 \\-1 & 1\end{array}\right]+\frac{c_{e}^{2} h_{e}}{6}\left[\begin{array}{ll}2 & 1 \\1 & 2\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(K ^{12}=\frac{1}{2 a_{e}}\left[\begin{array}{rr}1 & 1 \\-1 & -1\end{array}\right]+\frac{c_{e}}{2}\left[\begin{array}{ll}1 & -1 \\1 & -1\end{array}\right]\)

 

\(K ^{22}=\frac{h_{e}}{6 a_{e}^{2}}\left[\begin{array}{ll}2 & 1 \\1 & 2\end{array}\right]+\frac{1}{h_{e}}\left[\begin{array}{rr}1 & -1 \\-1 & 1\end{array}\right]\) (6)

we have a = 1, c = âˆ’1 and \(f(x)=-x^{2}\).Hence, the source vector is given by

\(F_{i}^{1}=-\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}} \psi_{i}\left(-x^{2}\right) d x \rightarrow F ^{1}=\frac{1}{h_{e}}\left\{\begin{array}{c}\frac{x_{b}}{3}\left(x_{b}^{3}-x_{a}^{3}\right)-\frac{1}{4}\left(x_{b}^{4}-x_{a}^{4}\right) \\-\frac{x_{a}}{3}\left(x_{b}^{3}-x_{a}^{3}\right)+\frac{1}{4}\left(x_{b}^{4}-x_{a}^{4}\right)\end{array}\right\}\)

 

\(F_{i}^{2}=-\int_{x_{a}}^{x_{b}} \frac{d \phi_{i}}{d x}\left(-x^{2}\right) d x \rightarrow F ^{2}=-\frac{x_{b}^{3}-x_{a}^{3}}{3 h_{e}}\left\{\begin{array}{r}1 \\-1\end{array}\right\}\)

 

The element equations become

 

\(\left[\begin{array}{cccc}\frac{1}{h_{e}}+\frac{h_{e}}{3} & 0 & -\frac{1}{h_{e}}+\frac{h_{e}}{6} & 1 \\0 & \frac{1}{h_{e}}+\frac{h_{e}}{3} & -1 & -\frac{1}{h_{e}} +\frac{h_{e}}{6} \\-\frac{1}{h_{e}}+\frac{h_{e}}{6} & -1 & \frac{1}{h_{e}}+\frac{h_{e}}{3} & 0 \\1 & -\frac{1}{h_{e}}+\frac{h{e}}{6} & 0 & \frac{1}{h_{e}}+\frac{h_{e}}{3}\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}u_{1} \\P_{1} \\u_{2} \\P_{2}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}F_{1}^{1} \\F_{1}^{2} \\F_{2}^{1} \\F_{2}^{2}\end{array}\right\}\)

 

Using \(h_{1}=h_{2}=0.5\), the element equations become

 

\(\frac{1}{12}\left[\begin{array}{rrrr}26 & 0 & -23 & 12 \\0 & 26 & -12 & -23 \\-23 & -12 & 26 & 0 \\12 & -23 & 0 & 26\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}u_{1} \\P_{1} \\u_{2} \\P_{2}\end{array}\right\}=\left\{\begin{array}{l}F_{1}^{1} \\F_{1}^{2} \\F_{2}^{1} \\F_{2}^{2}\end{array}\right\}\)

 

The assembled equations of the two-element mesh is

 

\(\frac{1}{12}\left[\begin{array}{rrrrrr}26 & 0 & -23 & 12 & 0 & 0 \\0 & 26 & -12 & -23 & 0 & 0 \\-23 & -12 & 52 & 0 & -23 & 12 \\12 & -23 & 0 & 52 & -12 & -23 \\0 & 0 & -23 & -12 & 26 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 12 & -23 & 0 & 26\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{r}U_{1} \\U_{2} \\U_{3} \\U_{4} \\U_{5} \\U_{6}\end{array}\right\}=\frac{1}{96}\left\{\begin{array}{r}1 \\-8 \\14 \\-48 \\17 \\56\end{array}\right\}\)

 

Using the boundary conditions \(U_{1}=U_{5}=0\), we obtain the condensed equations

 

\(\frac{1}{12}\left[\begin{array}{rrrr}26 & -12 & -23 & 0 \\-12 & 52 & 0 & 12 \\-23 & 0 & 52 & -23 \\0 & 12 & -23 & 26\end{array}\right]\left\{\begin{array}{l}U_{2} \\U_{3} \\U_{4} \\U_{6}\end{array}\right\}=\frac{1}{12}\left\{\begin{array}{r}-1.00 \\1.75 \\-6.00 \\7.00\end{array}\right\}\)

 

whose solution is

\(U_{2}=-0.11453, \quad U_{3}=-0.04746, \quad U_{4}=-0.06122, \quad U_{6}=0.23698\)

The two-element weak form solution for \(U_{3} \text { is } U_{3}=-0.03977\). The exact value is u(0.5) = 0.04076.
