Guest Question.

Solution

The given indeterminate structure is split into two determinate
structures as shown in Fig. 1.3b and 1.3c choosing\( R_{B} \) as the redundant force. The basic equation used is as given in Eq. 1.3 \(\Delta=\int{\frac{M m dX}{EI} } \).This is stated again below:The procedure will be followed as it is stated earlier in this section.

Step 1.

\(SI=n_{u}-n_{e}=4-3=1 \)

Step 2

Choose \(R_{B} \)as the redundant force (in the problem statement).

Step 3

The two determinate structures are shown below with Fig. 1.4a acted on by the applied loading, and Fig. 1.4c acted on by the redundant force RB (unit load). The deflection at B for the statically determinate structure \(\left(\Delta_{B} \right) _{L} \) due to applied loads can be obtained from Eq. 1.3\(\Delta=\int{\frac{M m dX}{EI} } \) using the values of M and in. Figures 1.4a — 1.4d are used to calculate M and in.The values for M and in will depend on the origin chosen and the corresponding change in limits.
