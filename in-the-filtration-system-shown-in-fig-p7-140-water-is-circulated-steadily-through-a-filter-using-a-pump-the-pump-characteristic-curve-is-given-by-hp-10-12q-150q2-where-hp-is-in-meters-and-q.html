Guest Question.

In the filtration system shown in Fig. P7. 140, water is circulated steadily through a filter using a pump.The pump characteristic curve is given by \(H_{p}=10+12 Q-150 Q^{2}, \text { where } H_{p}\) is in meters and Q is in \(m ^{3} / s\). A 10-cm-diameter pipe is used in the system. The total length of pipe used is 60 m, and the friction factor of the pipe is f = 0.04. Determine the flow rate and power input to the pump. All bends are 90 miter bend without vanes.

Admin
Solution

\(\frac{e}{D}=\frac{1.65}{1200}=0.0014 . \quad \therefore f=0.021 . \quad Q=\pi \times 0.6^{2} V =1.13 V\).

 

\(-H_{T}=-60+\left(0.8+1+0.021 \frac{1000}{1.2}\right) \frac{Q^{2}}{1.13^{2} \times 2 \times 9.81} . \quad \therefore H_{T}=60-0.77 Q^{2}\).

 

\(\dot{W}_{T}=\gamma Q H_{T} \eta_{T}=9810 \times\left(60 Q-0.77 Q^{3}\right) \times 0.9=530000 Q-6800 Q^{3}\).

 

But, the characteristic curve is \(\dot{W}_{T}=500000 Q\) (power in watts).

 

Hence, \(500000 Q=530000 Q-6800 Q^{3} . \quad \therefore Q=2.1 m ^{3} / s\).

 

\(\therefore \dot{W}_{T}=\underline{1.05 MW }\).
