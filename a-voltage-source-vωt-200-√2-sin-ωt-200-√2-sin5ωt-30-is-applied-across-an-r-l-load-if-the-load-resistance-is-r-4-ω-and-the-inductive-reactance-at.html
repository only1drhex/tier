Guest Question.

A voltage source \(v(\omega t) = 200 \sqrt{2} \sin \omega t + 200 \sqrt{2} \sin(5\omega t + 30°)\) is applied across an R-L load. If the load resistance is \(R = 4 \Omega\) and the inductive reactance at the fundamental frequency is \(X_{L,1} = 10 \Omega\), calculate the current drawn by the load and the values of P, Q, S, D, and \(\lambda\) .

Admin
Solution

\(\widetilde{V}\) = rms load voltage \(\sqrt{(200)^2 + (200)^2} = 283 V\)

 

\(|Z_{L,n}|\) = load impedance at the nth harmonic frequency = \(\sqrt{R^2 + (n\omega L)^2}\)

 

\(|Z_{L,1}|\) =load impedance at the fundamental frequency

\(= \sqrt{R^2 + (\omega L)^2} = \sqrt{4^2 + 10^2} = 10.8 \Omega\)

 

\(\omega\) = frequency of the voltage source fundamental component \(\frac{rads}{sec}\)

 

\(\varphi_1\) = phase displacement between voltage and current fundamental components

\(= \tan^{-1}(\frac{X_{L,1}}{R}) = \tan^{-1}(\frac{10}{4}) = 68.2°\)

 

\(X_{L,5}\) = load reactance at the 5th harmonic frequency = \(5 X_{L,1} = 50 \Omega\)

 

\(|Z_{L,5}|=\sqrt{R^2 + (5\omega L)^2} = \sqrt{4^2 + (50)^2} = 50 \Omega\) \(\varphi_1 = \tan^{-1}(\frac{50}{4}) = 85.4°\)

Therefore, the current drawn by the load is given by:

\(i(t) = \frac{\widehat{V}_1 }{|Z_1|}\sin (\omega t – \varphi_1) + \frac{\widehat{V}_5 }{|Z_5|}\sin (\omega t – \varphi_5)\)

 

\(= \frac{\sqrt{2}200}{10.8}\sin (5\omega t – 68.2°) + \frac{\sqrt{2}200}{50}\sin (\omega t – 85.4°)\)

 

\(= 26.19\sin (\omega t – 68.2°) + 6.22\sin (5\omega t – 85.4°)\)

The rms current and the apparent power are given by:

\(\widetilde{I} = \sqrt{(\frac{26.19}{\sqrt{2}})^2 + (\frac{6.22}{\sqrt{2}})^2} = 18.94 A\) and

\(S = \widetilde{V} \widetilde{I} = (283)(18.94) = 5362 V A\)

Also, the power components and the power factor are:

\(P = \sum\limits_{n=1}^{ \infty }{\widetilde{V}_n \widetilde{I}_n \cos \varphi_n} = \widetilde{V}_1 \widetilde{I}_1 \cos \varphi_1 + \widetilde{V}_5 \widetilde{I}_5 \cos \varphi_5\)

 

\( = 200 \frac{26.19}{\sqrt{2}}\cos 68.2° + 200 \frac{6.22}{\sqrt{2}}\cos (85.4° – 30°)\)

 

\(= 3703.83 \times 0.37 + 879.64 \times 0.056 = 1419.67 W\)

 

\(Q = \sum\limits_{n=1}^{ \infty }{\widetilde{V}_n \widetilde{I}_n \sin \varphi_n} = \widetilde{V}_1 \widetilde{I}_1 \sin \varphi_1 + \widetilde{V}_5 \widetilde{I}_5 \sin \varphi_5\)

 

\(= 3703.83 \times 0.93 + 879.64 \times 0.82 = 4165.87 VAR\)

 

\(D = \sqrt{S^2 – P^2 – Q^2} = \sqrt{(5362)^2 – (1419.67)^2 – (4165.87)^2}\)

 

\(= 3062.86 V A\) distortion

\(\lambda \) = power factor = \(\frac{P}{S} = \frac{1419.67}{5362} =0.265\)
