Guest Question.

Continue the case study by specifying appropriate gears, including pitch diameter, diametral pitch, face width, and material. Achieve safety factors of at least 1.2 for wear and bending.

Admin
Solution

Estimate the minimum diametral pitch for overall gearbox height = 22 in.
From Equation (18–3) and Figure 18–1,
\(P_{min} = \frac{(N_{3} +\frac{N_{2}}{2} + \frac{N_{5}}{2} + 2)}{(Y − clearances − wall thickness)}\)
Allow 1.5 in for clearances and wall thickness:

\( P_{min} = \frac{(72 +\frac{16}{2} +\frac{72}{2} + 2)}{(22 − 1.5)} = 5.76 teeth/in\)

Start with P = 6 teeth/in
\(d_{2} = d_{4} = N_{2}∕P \)= 16∕6 = 2.67 in
\(d_{3} = d_{5}\) = 72∕6 = 12.0 in
Shaft speeds were previously determined to be

\(ω_{2} = 1750 rev/min\) \(ω_{3} = ω_{4} = 388.9 rev/min\) \(ω_{5} = 86.4 rev/min\)
Get pitch-line velocities and transmitted loads for later use.

\(V_{23} = \frac{πd_{2}ω_{2}}{12} =\frac{π(2.67)(1750)}{12} = 1223 ft/min\) Equation (13–34) V = πdn∕12
\(V_{45} = \frac{πd_{5}ω_{5}}{12} = 271.5 ft/min\)

\(W^{t}_{23} = 33 000\frac{H}{V_{23}}= 33 000(\frac{20}{1223}) = 540.0 lbf\) Equation (13–35) \( W_{t} = 33 000\frac{ H}{V} \)

 

\( W^{t}_{45} = 33 000\frac{H}{V_{45}}= 33 000(\frac{20}{271.5}) = 2431 lbf\)

Start with gear 4, since it is the smallest gear, transmitting the largest load. It will likely be critical.
Start with wear by contact stress, since it is often the limiting factor.
Gear 4 Wear
\(I = \frac{cos 20° sin 20°}{2(1) }(\frac{4.5}{4.5 + 1}) = 0.1315\) Equation (14–23) \( I=\begin{cases} \frac{cos ϕ_{t} sin ϕ_{t}}{2m_{N}} \frac{m_{G}}{m_{G}+1} &external gears \\ \frac{cos ϕ_{t} sin ϕ_{t}}{2mN} \frac{m_{G}}{m_{G}-1}& internal gears \end{cases} \)

For \(K_{v}\), assume \(Q_{v}\) = 7. B = 0.731, A = 65.1 Equation (14–29) \( \begin{cases} [A + (Q_{v} − 3)]^{2} & ft/min \\ \frac{[A + (Q_{v} − 3)]^{2}}{200} & m/s \end{cases} \)

\( K_{v} = (\frac{65.1 +\sqrt{271.5}}{65.1}) ^{0.731}= 1.18 \) Equation (14–27) \( \begin{cases} (\frac{A+\sqrt{V} }{A})^{B} & V in ft/min \\ (\frac{A+\sqrt{200V} }{A})^{B} & V in m/s \end{cases} \)

Face width F is typically from 3 to 5 times circular pitch. Try
\(F = 4(\frac{π}{P}) = 4(\frac{π}{6}) = 2.09 in.\)

Since gear specifications are readily available on the Internet, we might as well check for commonly available face widths. On www.globalspec.com, entering P = 6 teeth/in and d = 2.67 in, stock spur gears from several sources have face widths of 1.5 in or 2.0 in. These are also available for the meshing gear 5 with d = 12 in.
Choose F = 2.0 in.

For \(K_{m} \) , \(C_{pf}\) = 0.0624 Equation (14–32) \( C_{pf}=\begin{cases} \frac{F}{10d_{P}}− 0.025 & F ≤ 1 in\\ \frac{F}{10d_{P}}− 0.0375 + 0.0125F & 1 < F ≤ 17 in \\ \frac{F}{10d_{P}} 0.1109 + 0.0207F − 0.000 228F^{2}&17 < F ≤ 40 in \end{cases} \)

\(C_{mc} = 1\) uncrowned teeth Equation (14–31) \(C_{mc} = \begin{cases} 1 & for uncrowned teeth \\ 0.8 & for crowned teeth\end{cases} \)

\(C_{pm} = 1\) straddle-mounted Equation (14–33)\(C_{pm} = \begin{cases} 1& for straddle -mounted pinion with S1∕S < 0.175\\ 1.1 & for straddle-mounted pinion with S1∕S ≥ 0.175\end{cases} \)

 

\(C_{ma} = 0.15\) commercial enclosed unit Equation (14–34) \( C_{ma} = A + BF + CF^{2} \)

 

\(C_{e} = 1\) Equation (14–35)\(C_{e}= \begin{cases} 0.8 &for gearing adjusted at assembly, or compatibility is improved by lapping, or both\\ 1&for all other conditions \end{cases}\)

\(K_{m} = 1.21\) Equation (14–30) \( K_{m} = C_{mf} = 1 + C_{mc}(C_{p f} C_{pm} + C_{ma}C_{e})\)

\(C_{p}\) = 2300 Table 14–8
\(K_{o} = K_{s} = C_{f} = 1\)

\( σ_{c} = 2300 \sqrt{\frac{2431(1.18)(1.21)}{2.67(2)(0.1315)}}= 161 700 psi \) Equation (14–16)

\( σ_{c} =\begin{cases} C_{P}\sqrt{W_{t}K_{o}K_{v}K_{s} \frac{K_{m}}{d_{p}f}\frac{C_{f}}{I} } & (U.S. customary units) \\ Z_{E}\sqrt{ W_{t}K_{o}K_{v}K_{s} \frac{K_{H}}{dw1b}\frac{Z_{R}}{Z_{I}} } & (SI units) \end{cases} \)

Get factors for \(σ_{c,all}\). For life factor \(Z_{N}\), get number of cycles for specified life of 12 000 h.

\( L_{4} = (12 000 h)(60\frac{min}{h} )(389 \frac{rev}{min}) = 2.8 × 10^{8} rev\)

\(Z_{N}= 0.9 \) Figure 14–15
\(K_{R} = K_{T} = C_{H} = 1\)

For a design factor of 1.2,
\(σ_{c,all} = S_{c}Z_{N}∕S_{H} = σ_{c}\) Equation (14–18)
\(S_{c} = \frac{S_{H}σ_{c}}{Z_{N}}=\frac{ 1.2(161 700)}{0.9} = 215 600 psi \)

From Table 14–6 this strength is achievable with Grade 2 carburized and hardened with
\(S_{c}\) = 225 000 psi. To find the achieved factor of safety,\( n_{c} = σ_{c,all}∕σ_{c} with S_{H} = 1\) . The factor of safety for wear of gear 4 is

\( n_{c} = \frac{σ_{c,all}}{σ_{c}}= \frac{S_{c}Z_{N}}{σ_{c}}=\frac{ 225 000(0.9)}{161 700} = 1.25 \)

Gear 4 Bending
J = 0.27 Figure 14–6
\(K_{B} \)= 1

Everything else is the same as before.
\(σ = W_{t}K_{v}\frac{P_{d}}{F}{K_{m}}{J} = (2431)(1.18)(\frac{6}{2})(\frac{1.21}{0.27}) \) Equation (14–15)
σ = 38 570 psi

\(Y_{N}\) = 0.9 Figure 14–14

Using Grade 2 carburized and hardened, same as chosen for wear, find \(S_{t} \)= 65000 psi (Table 14–3).
\(σ_{all} = S_{t}Y_{N} = 58 500 psi\)
The factor of safety for bending of gear 4 is

\( n = \frac{σ_{all}}{σ} = \frac{58 500}{38 570} = 1.52 \)

Gear 5 Bending and Wear
Everything is the same as for gear 4, except J, \(Y_{N}, and Z_{N}\).
J = 0.41 Figure 14–6
\(L_{5} = (12 000 h)(60 min/h)(86.4 rev/min) = 6.2 × 10^{7}\) rev
\(Y_{N} = 0.97\) Figure 14–14
\(Z_{N} = 1.0\) Figure 14–15

\( σ_{c} = 2300 \sqrt{\frac{2431(1.18)(1.21)}{2.67(2)(0.1315)}} = 161 700 psi \)

 

\( σ = (2431)(1.18)(\frac{6}{2})(\frac{1.21}{0.41}) = 25 400 psi \)

Choose Grade 2 carburized and hardened, the same as gear 4
\(n_{c} = \frac{σ_{c,all}}{σ_{c}}= \frac{225 000}{161 700} = 1.39\)

 

\(n = \frac{σ_{all}}{σ} = \frac{65000(0.97)}{25 400} = 2.48 \)

Gear 2 Wear
Gears 2 and 3 are evaluated similarly. Only selected results are shown.
\(K_{v}\) = 1.37
Try F = 1.5 in, since the loading is less on gears 2 and 3.
\(K_{m}\) = 1.19
All other factors are the same as those for gear 4.

\( σ_{c} = 2300\sqrt{\frac{(539.7)(1.37) 1.19)}{2.67(1.5)(0.1315)}}= 94 000 psi \)

\(L_{2} = (12 000 h)(60 min/h)(1750 rev/min) = 1.26 × 10^{9} rev Z_{N} = 0.8 \)
Try grade 1 flame-hardened, \(S_{c}\) = 170 000 psi
\(n_{c} = \frac{σ_{c,all}}{σ_{c}}=\frac{ 170 000(0.8)}{94 000} = 1.40 \)

Gear 2 Bending
J = 0.27 \(Y_{N}\) = 0.88
\( σ = 539.7(1.37) \frac{(6)(1.19)}{(1.5)(0.27)} = 13 040 psi\)

 

\( n = \frac{σ_{all}}{σ} = \frac{45 000(0.88)}{13 040} = 3.04 \)

Gear 3 Wear and Bending
\( J = 0.41 Y_{N} = 0.9 Z_{N} = 0.9\)

 

\( σ_{c} = 2300\sqrt{\frac{ (539.7)(1.37)(1.19)}{2.67(1.5)(0.1315)}}= 94 000 psi \)

σ =\( 539.7(1.37)\frac{(6)(1.19)}{1.5(0.41)} = 8584 psi\)

Try Grade 1 steel, through-hardened to 300 \(H_{B}\). From Figure 14–2, \(S_{t}\) = 36 000 psi and from
Figure 14–5, \(S_{c}\) = 126 000 psi.

\(n_{c} = \frac{126 000(0.9)}{94 000} = 1.21\) \(n = \frac{σ_{all}}{σ} = \frac{36 000(0.9)}{8584 }= 3.77\)

In summary, the resulting gear specifications are:
All gears, P = 6 teeth/in
Gear 2, Grade 1 flame-hardened, \(S_{c}\) = 170 000 psi and \(S_{t}\) = 45 000 psi
\(d_{2}\) = 2.67 in, face width = 1.5 in
Gear 3, Grade 1 through-hardened to 300 \(H_{B}, S_{c} = 126 000 psi and S_{t}\)
= 36 000 psi
\(d_{3}\) = 12.0 in, face width = 1.5 in
Gear 4, Grade 2 carburized and hardened, \(S_{c} = 225 000 psi and S_{t}\) = 65 000 psi
\(d_{4}\) = 2.67 in, face width = 2.0 in
Gear 5, Grade 2 carburized and hardened, \(S_{c} = 225 000 psi and S_{t}\) = 65 000 psi
\(d_{5}\)= 12.0 in, face width = 2.0 in
