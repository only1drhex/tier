Guest Question.

Do with R-134a as the working fluid in the Rankine cycle. Consider the ammonia Rankine-cycle power plant shown in Fig. P9.31, a plant that was designed to operate in a location where the ocean water temperature is 25°C near the surface and 5°C at some greater depth. The mass flow rate of the working fluid is 1000 kg/s.
a. Determine the turbine power output and the pump power input for the cycle.
b. Determine the mass flow rate of water through each heat exchanger.
c. What is the thermal efficiency of this power plant?

Admin
Solution

a) Turbine

\(\begin{aligned}& s _{2}= s _{1}=1.7183=1.0485+ x _{2} \times 0.6733 \quad \Rightarrow \quad x _{2}=0.9948 \\& h _{2}=213.58+0.9948 \times 190.65=403.24 kJ / kg \\& w _{ T }= h _{1}- h _{2}=409.84-403.24=6.6 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

 

\(\dot{ W }_{ T }=\dot{ m } w _{ T }=6600 kW\)

Pump: \(w _{ P } \approx v _{3}\left( P _{4}- P _{3}\right)=0.000794(572.8-415.8)=0.125 kJ / kg\)

\(w _{ P }= w _{ P } / \eta_{ S }=0.125 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \dot{ W }_{ P }=\dot{ m } w _{ P }= 1 2 5 k W\)

 

b) Consider the condenser heat transfer to the low T water

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ Q }_{\text {to low } T H 2 O }=1000(403.24-213.58)=189660 kW \\&\dot{ m }_{\text {low } T H 2 O }=\frac{189660}{29.38-20.98}= 2 2 5 7 9 kg / s \\ & h _{4}= h _{3}- w _{ P }=213.58+0.125=213.71 kJ / kg\end{aligned}\)

Now consider the boiler heat transfer from the high T water

\(\begin{aligned}&\dot{ Q }_{\text {from high } T H 2 O }=1000(409.84-213.71)=196130 kW \\&\dot{ m }_{ high T H 2 O }=\frac{196130}{104.87-96.50}= 2 3 4 3 2 kg / s\end{aligned}\)

 

c) \(\eta_{ TH }=\dot{ W }_{ NET } / \dot{ Q }_{ H }=\frac{6600-125}{196130}= 0 . 0 3 3\)
