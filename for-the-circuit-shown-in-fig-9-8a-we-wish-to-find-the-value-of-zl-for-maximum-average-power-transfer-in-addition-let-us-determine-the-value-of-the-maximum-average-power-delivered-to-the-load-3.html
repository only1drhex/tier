Guest Question.

For the circuit shown in Fig. 9.8a, we wish to find the value of \(Z_L\) for maximum average power transfer. In addition, let us determine the value of the maximum average power delivered to the load.

Admin
Solution

We will first reduce the circuit, with the exception of the load, to a Thévenin equivalent circuit. The open-circuit voltage can be computed from Fig. 9.8b. The equations for the circuit are

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{V}_{x}^{\prime}+4 &=(2+j 4) \mathbf{I}_{1} \\\\\mathbf{V}_{x}^{\prime} &=-2 \mathbf{I}_{1}\end{aligned}\)

 

Solving for \(\mathbf{I}_{1}\), we obtain

 

\(\mathbf{I}_{1}=\frac{1\underline{/-45^{\circ}}}{\sqrt{2}}\)

 

The open-circuit voltage is then

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{oc}} &=2 \mathbf{I}_{1}-4 \underline{/ 0^{\circ}} \\\\&=\sqrt{2} L-\underline{/-45^{\circ}}-4 \underline{/ 0^{\circ}} \\\\&=-3-j 1 \\\\&=+3.16 \underline{/-161.57^{\circ}} \mathrm{V}\end{aligned}\)

 

The short-circuit current can be derived from Fig. 9.8c. The equations for this circuit are

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{V}_{x}^{\prime \prime}+4 &=(2+j 4) \mathbf{I}-2 \mathbf{I}_{\mathrm{sc}} \\\\-4 &=-2 \mathbf{I}+(2-j 2) \mathbf{I}_{\mathrm{sc}} \\\\\mathbf{V}_{x}^{\prime \prime} &=-2\left(\mathbf{I}-\mathbf{I}_{\mathrm{sc}}\right)\end{aligned}\)

 

Solving these equations for \(I _{\mathrm{sc}}\) yields

 

\(\mathbf{I}_{\mathrm{sc}}=-(1+j 2) \mathrm{A}\)

 

The Thévenin equivalent impedance is then

 

\(\mathbf{Z}_{\mathrm{Th}}=\frac{\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{oc}}}{\mathbf{I}_{\mathrm{sc}}}=\frac{3+j 1}{1+j 2}=1-j 1 \Omega\)

 

 

Therefore, for maximum average power transfer the load impedance should be

 

\(\mathbf{Z}_{L}=1+j 1 \Omega\)

 

The current in this load \(\mathbf{Z}_{L}\) is then

 

\(\mathbf{I}_{L}=\frac{\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{oc}}}{\mathbf{Z}_{\mathrm{Th}}+\mathbf{Z}_{L}}=\frac{-3-j 1}{2}=1.58 \underline{/-161.57^{\circ}} \mathrm{A}\)

 

Hence, the maximum average power transferred to the load is

 

\(\begin{aligned}P_{L} &=\frac{1}{2}(1.58)^{2}(1) \\\\&=1.25 \mathrm{~W}\end{aligned}\)

 

[ hint ]

When there is a dependent source, both \(V_{\text {oc }}\) and \(I_{s c}\) must be found and \(Z_{T h}\) computed from the equation

 

\(Z_{T h}=\frac{V_{o c}}{I_{s c}}\)
