Guest Question.

The radiation intensity of a certain antenna is

\(U(\theta ,\phi )=\begin{cases} 2\sin \theta \sin^{3}\phi , & 0\leq \theta \leq \pi ,0\leq \phi \leq \pi \\ 0, & else where \end{cases}\)

Determine the directivity of the antenna.

Admin
Solution

The directivity is defined as

\(D=\frac{U_{max}}{U_{ave}}\)

From the given U,
\(U_{max}\)=2

From eqs. (13.40) and (13.41), we get the expression for the average radiated intensity.

\(P_{rad}=\oint\limits_{S}^{}{\mathscr{P_{ave}}dS}= \oint\limits_{S}^{}{\mathscr{P_{ave}}r^{2}\sin \theta d\theta d\phi }\)
\(=\int\limits_{S}^{}{U(\theta ,\phi )\sin\theta d\theta d\phi }\) (13.40)
\(=\int\limits_{\phi =0}^{2\pi }{}\int\limits_{\theta =0}^{\pi }{U(\theta ,\phi )}d\Omega\)
\(U_{ave}=\frac{P_{rad}}{4\pi }\) (13.41)

\(U_{ave}=\frac{1}{4\pi }\int{U(\theta ,\phi ) d\Omega }\)
\(=\frac{1}{4\pi }\int\limits_{\phi =0}^{\pi }{}\int\limits_{\theta =0}^{\pi }{2\sin \theta \sin^{3}\phi \sin\theta d\theta d\phi }\)
\(=\frac{1}{2\pi }\int\limits_{0}^{\pi }{\sin^{2}\theta d\theta }\int\limits_{0}^{\pi }{\sin^{3}\phi d\phi }\)
\(=\frac{1}{2\pi }\int\limits_{0}^{\pi }{\frac{1}{2}(1-\cos 2\theta )d\theta }\int\limits_{0}^{\pi }{(1-\cos^{2}\phi )d(-\cos \phi )}\)
\(=\frac{1}{2\pi }\frac{1}{2}(\theta -\frac{\sin 2\theta }{2} )|_{0}^{\pi }(\frac{\cos^{3}\phi }{3}-\cos\phi )|_{0}^{\pi }\)
\(=\frac{1}{2\pi }(\frac{\pi}{2} )(\frac{4}{3} )=\frac{1}{3}\)

Hence
\(D=\frac{2}{(1/3)}=6\)
