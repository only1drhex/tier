Guest Question.

A column foundation with dimensions \(1.2 m \times 1.8 m\) was placed at a depth of 1.1 m from the ground surface (Figure 8.36). The soil profile is mainly medium sand with a friction angle of \(35^{\circ}\) and a soil unit weight of \(17.1 kN/m^{2}.\) The inclination of the load is \(10^{\circ}\) from the vertical. In addition, the load is applied at an eccentricity of 0.2 m along the length and 0.1 m along the width. \((e_{L} = 0.2 m\) and \(e_{B} = 0.1 m).\) What is the allowable load in kN on the footing if the desired factor of safety is 3.0? Ignore the moment due to inclined load.

Admin
Solution

Step 1: Write down the Meyerhof bearing capacity equation for inclined loads.

\(q_{ult}=cN_{c}d_{c}i_{c}+qN_{q}d_{q}i_{q}+0.5BN_{\gamma }\gamma d_{\gamma }i_{\gamma }\)

 

\(c=0\) for medium sand

 

\(q=\gamma D=17.1\times 1.1=18.81 kN/m^{2}\)

 

\(N_{q}=e^{\pi \tan \varphi ^{\prime }}\tan ^{2}[45+\varphi ^{\prime }/2]\)

Figure 8.36 \(10^{\circ}\) inclination of the load.

\(N_{q}=e^{\pi \tan 35}\tan ^{2}[45+35/2]=9.023\times 3.69=33.3\)

 

\(N_{\gamma }=(N_{q}-1)\tan (1.4\varphi ^{\prime })\)

 

\(N_{\gamma }=(33.3-1)\tan (1.4\times 35)=37.16\)

 

\(d_{q}=d_{\gamma }=1+0.1\tan [45+\varphi ^{\prime }/2]\times D/B\) for \(\varphi ^{\prime }\gt 10^{\circ }.\)

 

\(d_{q}=d_{\gamma }=1+0.1\tan [45+35/2]\times 1.1/1.2\) for \(\varphi ^{\prime }10^{\circ }\).

 

\(d_{q}=d_{\gamma }=1.17\)

 

\(i_{c}=i_{q}=(1-\theta /90)^{2}\) for any \(\varphi ^{\prime }\)

 

\(i_{q}=(1-10/90)^{2}=0.79\)

 

\(i_{\gamma }=(1-\theta /\varphi )^{2}\) for \(\varphi ^{\prime }\gt 0^{\circ }.\)

 

\(i_{\gamma }=(1-10/35)^{2}=0.51\)

Step 2: Apply the equation.

\(q_{ult}=cN_{c}d_{c}i_{c}+qN_{q}d_{q}i_{q}+0.5BN_{\gamma }\gamma d_{\gamma }d_{\gamma }i_{\gamma }\)

 

\(q_{ult}=0+18.81\times 33.3\times 1.17\times 0.79+0.5\times 1.2\times 37.16\times 17.1\times 1.17\times 0.51\)

 

\(q_{ult}=0+578.9+227.5=806.4 k N/m^{2}\)

 

\(q_{allowable}=806.4/3.0=268.8 kNm^{2}\)

Step 3: Find the equivalent area \((A^{\prime }).\)

\(L^{\prime }=L-2e_{L}=1.8-2\times 0.2=1.4 m\)

 

\(B^{\prime }=B-2e_{B}=1.2-2\times 0.1=1 m\)

 

\(A^{\prime }=B^{\prime }\times L^{\prime }=1.4\times 1.0=1.40\)

 

\(Q_{allowable} = q_{allowable} \times\) area of the footing

 

\(Q_{allowable} = q_{allowable} \times (1.4) = 268.8 × 1.4 = 376.32 kN.\)
