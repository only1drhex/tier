Guest Question.

A specimen of a medium carbon steel was tested in tension. With an extensometer inp lace, the specimen was loaded then unloaded, to see if the extensometer reading returned to the no-load reading, then the next higher load was applied. The loads and extensometer elongations were reduced to stress σ and strain \(\epsilon\), producing the following data:

35 267 20 143 15 104 10 068 5033 σ, psi
0.001 15 0.000 65 0.000 50 0.000 30 0.000 20 \(\epsilon\)
Admin
Solution

From Table 20–7, \(\overline{x} = 0.002 80/5 = 0.000 56, \overline{y} = 85 615/5 = 17 123\). Note that a regression line always contains the data centroid. From Eq. (20–32)

\(\hat{m}=\frac{N\sum{x_{i} y_{i}} −\sum{x_{i}} \sum{y_{i}}}{N\sum{x^{2}_{i} −(\sum{x_{i})^{2}}}} =\frac{\sum{x_{i} y_{i}} − N \ \overline{x} \ \overline{y}}{\sum{x^{2}_{i}} − N \overline{x}^{2}}\) (20–32)

\(\hat{m} =\frac{5(65.229) − 0.0028(85 615)}{5(0.000 002 125) − 0.0028^{2}} = 31.03(10^{6}) psi =\overline{E}\)

From Eq. (20–33)

\( \hat{b} =\frac{∑y_{i} − \hat{m} ∑x_{i}}{N} =\overline{y}− \hat{m} \overline{x}\) (20–33)

\(\hat{b} =\frac{0.000 002 125(85 615) − 0.002 80(65.229)}{5(0.000 002 125) − 0.0028^{2}} = −254.69 psi\)

From Eq. (20–34), obtaining sx and sy from a statistics calculator routine,

\(r= \hat{m}\frac{s_{x}}{s_{y}}\) (20-34)

\(\hat{r}= \hat{m}\frac{s_{x}}{s_{y}} =\frac{31 031 597.85(3 162 163 10^{−4})}{11 601.11} = 0.998\)

From Eq. (20–37), the scatter about the regression line is measured by the standard deviation \(s_{y·x}\) and is equal to

\(s_{y·x}=\sqrt{\frac{\sum{y^{2}_{i}} − \hat{b}\sum{y_{i}} − \hat{m} \sum{x_{i}y_{i}}}{N − 2}}\) (20-37)

\(s_{y·x} =\sqrt{ \frac{\sum{y^{2}} − \hat{b}\sum{y} − \hat{m} \sum{xy}}{N − 2}}\)
\(=\sqrt{\frac{2 004 328 267 − (−254.69)85 615 − 31.03(10^{6})(65.229)}{5 − 2}}\)
= 811.1 psi
