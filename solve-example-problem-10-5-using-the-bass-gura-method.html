Guest Question.

Solve using the Bass-Gura method.

Admin
Solution

In the desired observer characteristic equation was shown to be

\(\lambda ^2+ 16.8\lambda + 143.8 = 0\)

therefore

\(\hat{a} =\left[\begin{matrix} \hat{a_1} \\ \hat{a_2} \end{matrix} \right] = \left[\begin{matrix} 16.8 \\ 143.8 \end{matrix} \right] \)

The characteristic equation of the plant matrix A can be shown to be

\(\lambda ^2 + 0.721\lambda + 2.65 = 0\)

or

\({a} =\left[\begin{matrix} {a_1} \\ {a_2} \end{matrix} \right] = \left[\begin{matrix} 0.721 \\ 2.65 \end{matrix} \right] \)

The observability matrix for a second-order system is given by

\(U= \left[\begin{matrix}C^T & A^TC^T \end{matrix} \right]\)

 

 

\(C^T=\left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 0\end{matrix} \right] \)

 

\(A^TC^T=\begin{bmatrix} – 0.334 & – 2.52 \\ 1.0 & – 0.387 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} 1 \\ 0\end{matrix} \right]=\left[\begin{matrix} – 0.334 \\ 1\end{matrix} \right]\)

 

\(U=\begin{bmatrix}1 & – 0.334 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\)

The transformation matrix W is defined in terms of the coefficient of the plant matrix:

\(W=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & a_{1} \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} 1 &0.721 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \)

The observer gains can now be estimated from Equation (10.20)

\(K_e=[(UW)^T]^{-1}(\hat{a} -a)\)

where

\(UW=\begin{bmatrix}1 & – 0.334 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} 1 &0.721 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\)

 

\(=\begin{bmatrix} 1 &0.387 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\)

 

\((UW)^T=\begin{bmatrix} 1 &0 \\ 0.387 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\)

The inverse of \((UW)^T\) is

\([(UW)^T]^{-1}=\begin{bmatrix} 1 &0 \\ -0.387 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\)

Therefore the observer gains are

\(k_e=\begin{bmatrix} 1 &0 \\ -0.387 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\left[\left[\begin{matrix} 16.8 \\ 143.8 \end{matrix} \right]-\left[\begin{matrix} 0.721 \\ 2.65 \end{matrix} \right]\right] \)

 

\(=\begin{bmatrix} 1 &0 \\ -0.387 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\left[\begin{matrix} 16.079 \\141.13 \end{matrix} \right]\) \(=\left[\begin{matrix}16.08 \\134.9 \end{matrix} \right]\)
