Guest Question.

A concentrated load of P = 6 kips is applied to the upper end of a 4-ft long pipe, as shown in Figure P9.25. The pipe is an 8 in. standard steel pipe, which has an outside diameter of D = 8.625 in. and an inside diameter of d = 7.981 in. Determine the magnitude of:
(a) the maximum vertical shear stress in the pipe.
(b) the maximum tension bending stress in the pipe.

Admin
Solution

Section properties:

\(\begin{aligned}&I=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[D^{4}-d^{4}\right]=\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(8.625 \text { in. })^{4}-(7.981 \text { in } .)^{4}\right]=72.489241 in. ^{4} \\&Q=\frac{1}{12}\left[D^{3}-d^{3}\right]=\frac{1}{12}\left[(8.625 \text { in. })^{3}-(7.981 \text { in. })^{3}\right]=11.104874 \text { in. }^{3}\end{aligned}\)

Maximum shear force magnitude:

\(V_{\max }=6 kips =6,000 lb\)

Maximum bending moment magnitude:

\(M_{\max }=(6,000 lb )(4 ft )(12 in . / ft )=288,000 lb – in.\)

(a) Maximum vertical shear stress:

\(\tau=\frac{V Q}{I t}=\frac{(6,000 lb )\left(11.104874 in. ^{3}\right)}{\left(72.489241 in .{ }^{4}\right)(8.625 in .-7.981 in .)}=1,427.268 psi =1,427 psi\)

(b) Maximum tension bending stress:

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{M c}{I}=\frac{(288,000 lb – in. )(8.625 in . / 2)}{72.489241 in .{ }^{4}}=17,133.578 psi =17,130 psi ( T )\)
