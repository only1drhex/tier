\( P_{a}=P-P_{v}=100-1.228=98.77 kPa \) .

\( \text { The humidity ratio is then } \omega=(0.622)\left(P_{v} / P_{a}\right)=(0.622)(1.228 / 98.77)=0.00773 kg _{v} / kg _{a} \text {. The difference in the humidity } \) ratio just calculated and the humidity ratio of \( \text { is } \Delta \omega=0.01205-0.00773=0.00432 kg _{v} / kg _{a} \text {. } \) The mass of water vapor removed (condensed) is found to be

\( \Delta m_{v}=\Delta \omega m_{a}=(0.00432)\left[\frac{(98.1)(150)}{(0.287)(298)}\right]=0.743 kg _{v} \).

where we have used the initial mass of dry air.
(b) As we reheat the air back to 25°C, the ω remains constant at 0.00773. Using (12.25), the relative humidity is then reduced to

\( \phi=1.608 \frac{\omega P_{a}}{P_{g}}=1.608 \frac{(0.00773)(98.77)}{3.169}=0.387 \quad \text { or } \quad 38.7 \% \).

\( \text { where } P_{g} \text { is used as the saturation pressure at } 25^{\circ} C \text { from Table C.1. } \)
