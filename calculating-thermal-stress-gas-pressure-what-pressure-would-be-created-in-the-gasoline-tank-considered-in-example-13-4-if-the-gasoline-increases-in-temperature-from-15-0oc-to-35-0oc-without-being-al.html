Calculating Thermal Stress: Gas Pressure
What pressure would be created in the gasoline tank considered in , if the gasoline increases in temperature from 15.0ºC to 35.0ºC without being allowed to expand? Assume that the bulk modulus B for gasoline is \(1.00 \times 10^{9} N / m ^{2}\). (For more on bulk modulus, see Elasticity: Stress and Strain.)

Strategy
To solve this problem, we must use the following equation, which relates a change in volume ΔV to pressure:

\(\Delta V=\frac{1 F}{B A} V_{0}\) (13.15)

 

where F / A is pressure, \(V_{0}\) is the original volume, and B is the bulk modulus of the material involved. We will use the amount spilled in as the change in volume, ΔV .

Admin
Solution

1. Rearrange the equation for calculating pressure:

 

\(P=\frac{F}{A}=\frac{\Delta V}{V_{0}} B\) (13.16)

 

2. Insert the known values. The bulk modulus for gasoline is \(B=1.00 \times 10^{9} N / m ^{2}\). In the previous example, the change in volume ΔV = 1.10 L is the amount that would spill. Here, \(V_{0}=60.0 L\) is the original volume of the gasoline. Substituting these values into the equation, we obtain

 

\(P=\frac{1.10 L }{60.0 L }\left(1.00 \times 10^{9} Pa \right)=1.83 \times 10^{7} Pa\) (13.17)

 

Discussion
This pressure is about 2500 lb/in² , much more than a gasoline tank can handle.
