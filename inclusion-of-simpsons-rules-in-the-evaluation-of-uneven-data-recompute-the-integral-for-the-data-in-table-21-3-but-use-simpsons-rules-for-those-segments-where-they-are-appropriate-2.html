Solution

The fi rst segment is evaluated with the trapezoidal rule

\(I=0.12 \frac{1.309729+0.2}{2}=0.09058376\)

Because the next two segments from x = 0.12 to 0.32 are of equal length, their integral can be computed with Simpson’s 1/3 rule:

\(I=0.2 \frac{1.743393+4(1.305241)+1.309729}{6}=0.2758029\)

The next three segments are also equal and, as such, may be evaluated with the 3/8 rule to give I = 0.2726863. Similarly, the 1/3 rule can be applied to the two segments from x = 0.44 to 0.64 to yield I = 0.6684701. Finally, the last two segments, which are of unequal length, can be evaluated with the trapezoidal rule to give values of 0.1663479 and 0.1297500, respectively. The area of these individual segments can be summed to yield a total integral of 1.603641.
