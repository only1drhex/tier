Guest Question.

Design a straight-bevel gear mesh for shaft centerlines that intersect perpendicularly, to deliver 6.85 hp at 900 rev/min with a gear ratio of 3:1, temperature of 300°F, normal pressure angle of 20°, using a design factor of 2. The load is uniform-uniform. Use a pinion of 20 teeth. The material is to be AGMA grade 1 and the teeth are to be crowned. The reliability goal is 0.995 with a pinion life of \(10^{9}\) revolutions.

Admin
Solution

First we list the a priori decisions and their immediate consequences.

Function: 6.85 hp at 900 rev/min, gear ratio \(m_{G}\) = 3, 300°F environment, neither gear straddle-mounted, \(K_{mb} =\) 1.25 [Eq. (15–11)], R = 0.995 at \(10^{9} \)revolutions of the pinion,

\(\begin{matrix} K_{m}=K_{mb}+0.0036F^{2} & (U.S. customary units) & Eq. (15–11) \\ K_{H\beta }=K_{mb}+5.6(10^{-6})b^{2} & (SI units) & \end{matrix} \)

Eq. (15–14): \( C_{L}=\begin{cases}2 &10^{3} ≤ NL < 10^{4} \\3.4822N^{−0.0602}_{L}& 10^{4} ≤ N_{L} ≤ 10^{10}\end{cases} \)

 

\( Z_{NT}=\begin{cases}2 &10^{3} ≤ nL < 10^{4} \\3.4822n^{−0.0602}_{L}& 10^{4} ≤ n_{L} ≤ 10^{10}\end{cases} \)

 

\((C_{L})_{G} = 3.4822(10^{9}∕3)^{−0.0602 }= 1.068\)

\((CL)P = 3.4822(10^{9})^{−0.0602} = \)1

Eq. (15–15):\( K_{L}=\begin{cases} 2.7 &10^{2} ≤ N_{L} < 10^{3} \\ 6.1514N^{−0.1192}_{L}& 10^{3} ≤ N_{L} ≤ 3(10^{6})\\1.683N^{−0.0323}_{L}&3(10^{6}) ≤ N_{L} ≤ 10^{10} &critical \\1.3558N^{−0.0178}_{L}&3(10^{6}) ≤ N_{L} ≤ 10^{10}&general \end{cases} \)

 

\(Y_{NT}=\begin{cases} 2.7 &10^{2} ≤ n_{L} < 10^{3} \\ 6.1514n^{−0.1192}_{L}& 10^{3} ≤ n_{L} ≤ 3(10^{6})\\1.683n^{−0.0323}_{L}&3(10^{6}) ≤ n_{L} ≤ 10^{10} &critical \\1.3558n^{−0.0178}_{L}&3(10^{6}) ≤ n_{L} ≤ 10^{10}&general \end{cases} \)

 

\((K_{L})_{G} = 1.683(10^{9}∕3)^{−0.0323 }= \)0.8929

\((K_{L})_{P} = 1.683(10^{9})^{−0.0323 }= \)0.8618

Eq. (15–19):\( Y_{Z} = K_{R} =\begin{cases} 0.50 − 0.25 log(1 − R) & 0.99 ≤ R ≤ 0.999 \\0.70 − 0.15 log(1 − R) & 0.90 ≤ R < 0.99 \end{cases} \)

 

\(K_{R} = 0.50 \)− 0.25 log(1 − 0.995) = 1.075

\(C_{R} = \sqrt{K_{R}} = \sqrt{1.075 }= 1.037\)

Eq. (15–18):\(K_{T}=\begin{cases} 1&32°F ≤ t ≤ 250°F\\(460 + t)∕710&t > 250°F\end{cases} \)

 

\(K_{θ} =\begin{cases}1&0°C ≤ θ ≤ 120°C\\(273 + θ)∕393&θ > 120°C\end{cases}\)

 

\(K_{T} = C_{T} = (460 + 300)∕710 = \)1.070

Design factor: \(n_{d} = 2, S_{F} = 2, S_{H} = \sqrt{2} = 1.414.\)

Tooth system: crowned, straight-bevel gears, normal pressure angle 20°,

Eq. (15–13): \(K_{x} = Y_{β} = 1\) \( K_{x} = 1\)

Eq. (15–12): \( C_{xc} = Z_{xc} = \begin{cases} 1.5&properly crowned teeth\\2.0&or larger uncrowned teeth \end{cases}\)

\(C_{xc} = 1.5.\)

With \(N_{P}\) = 20 teeth,\( N_{G} \)= (3)20 = 60 teeth and from Fig. 15–14,

\(γ = tan^{−1}(N_{P}∕N_{G}) = tan^{−1}(20∕60) = 18.43° Γ = tan^{−1}(60∕20) = 71.57 \)

From Figs. 15–6 and 15–7, I = 0.0825,\( J_{P}\) = 0.248, and \(J_{G} \)= 0.202. Note that \(J_{P} > J_{G}.\)

Decision 1: Trial diametral pitch,\( P_{d} = 8 \)teeth/in.

Eq. (15–10):\( K_{S} =\begin{cases} 0.4867 + 0.2132∕P_{d}&0.5 ≤ P_{d} ≤ 16 teeth/in\\0.5&P_{d} > 16 teeth/in\end{cases}\)

 

\( K_{s} = \)0.4867 + 0.2132∕8 = 0.5134

\(d_{P} = N_{P}∕P_{d} = 20∕8 = 2.5 \)in

\(d_{G} = 2.5(3)\) = 7.5 in

\(v_{t} = πd_{P}n_{P}∕12 = π(2.5)900∕12 = 589.0 \)ft/min

\(W^{t} = 33 000 hp/v_{t} = 33 000(6.85)∕589.0 = 383.8\) lbf

Eq. (15–25): \(A_{0} = d_{P}∕(2 sin γ) = 2.5∕(2 sin 18.43°) \)= 3.954 in

Eq. (15–24): F = min\((0.3A_{0}, 10∕P_{d}) = min[0.3(3.954), 10∕8] = min(1.186, 1.25) = 1.186 in\)

Decision 2: Let F = 1.25 in. Then,Eq. (15–9):\( C_{s}=\begin{cases} 0.5&F < 0.5 in\\0.125F + 0.4375&0.5 ≤ F ≤ 4.5 in\\1&F > 4.5 in \end{cases} \)

\( C_{s} = 0.125(1.25)\) + 0.4375 = 0.5937

Eq. (15–11):\( K_{m} = K_{mb} + 0.0036F^2 (U.S. customary units) \)

\(K_{m} = 1.25 + 0.0036(1.25)^{2} = 1.256 \)

Decision 3: Let the transmission accuracy number be 6. Then, from Eq. (15–6), A = 50 + 56(1 − B)
\( B = 0.25(12 − Q_{v})^{2∕3} \)

B = \(0.25(12 − 6)^{2∕3} = \)0.8255

A = 50 + 56(1 − 0.8255) = 59.77

Eq. (15–5):\(K_{v} =\left(\frac{A+\sqrt{v_{t}} }{A} \right)^{B} \) (U.S. customary units)

\( K_{v} =\left(\frac{A+\sqrt{200v_{et}} }{A} \right)^{B} \)(SI units)

\( K_{v} = (\frac{59.77 + \sqrt{589.0}}{59.77} )^{0.8255}= 1.325\)

Decision 4: Pinion and gear material and treatment. Carburize and case-harden grade ASTM 1320 to

Core 21 HRC \((H_{B}\) is 229 Brinell)

Case 55-64 HRC \((H_{B} \)is 515 Brinell)

From Table 15–4, \(s_{ac} =\) 200 000 psi and from Table 15–6,\( s_{at}\) = 30 000 psi.

Gear bending: From Eq. (15–3), the bending stress is

\((s_{t})_{G} =\frac{ W^{t}}{F}P_{d}K_{o}K_{v}\frac{K_{s}K_{m}}{K_{x} J_{G}}= \frac{383.8}{1.25} 8(1)1.325\frac{0.5134(1.256)}{(1)0.202}\)

= 10 390 psi

The bending strength, from Eq. (15–4), is given by

\((s_{wt})_{G} = (\frac{s_{at}K_{L}}{S_{F}K_{T}K_{R}})_{G}= \frac{30 000(0.8929)}{2(1.070)1.075} = 11 640 psi \)

The strength exceeds the stress by a factor of 11640∕10390 = 1.12, giving an actual factor of safety of \((S_{F})_{G} \)= 2(1.12) = 2.24.

Pinion bending: The bending stress can be found from

\( (s_{t})_{P} = (s_{t})_{G}\frac{J_{G}}{J_{P}}= 10 390\frac{0.202}{0.248} = 8463 psi \)

The bending strength, again from Eq. (15–4), is given by

\( (s_{wt})_{P} = (\frac{s_{at}K_{L}}{S_{F}K_{T}K_{R}})_{P}= \frac{30 000(0.8618)}{2(1.070)1.075} = 11 240 psi \)

The strength exceeds the stress by a factor of 11 240∕8463 = 1.33, giving an actual factor of safety of \((S_{F})_{P}\) = 2(1.33) = 2.66.

Gear wear: The load-induced contact stress for the pinion and gear, from Eq. (15–1), is

\(s_{c} = C_{p}(\frac{W^{t}}{Fd_{P}I}K_{o}K_{v}K_{m}C_{s}C_{xc})^{1∕2}\) \(= 2290 [\frac{383.8}{1.25(2.5)0.0825}{}(1)1.325(1.256)0.5937(1.5)]^{1∕2}\)

= 107 560 psi

From Eq. (15–2) the contact strength of the gear is

\( (s_{wc})_{G} = (\frac{s_{ac}C_{L}C_{H}}{S_{H}K_{T}C_{R}})_{G}= \frac{200 000(1.068)(1)}{\sqrt{2}(1.070)1.037} = 136 120 psi \)

The strength exceeds the stress by a factor of 136 120∕107 560 = 1.266, giving an actual factor of safety of \((S_{H})^{ 2} _{G} = 1.266^{2} (2) \)= 3.21.

Pinion wear: From Eq. (15–2) the contact strength of the pinion is

\( (s_{wc})_{P} = (\frac{s_{ac}C_{L}C_{H}}{S_{H} K_{T}C_{R}})_{P}= \frac{200 000(1)(1)}{\sqrt{2}(1.070)1.037}= 127 450 psi \)

The strength exceeds the stress by a factor of 127 450∕107 560 = 1.185, giving an actual factor of safety of \((S_{H})^{2}_{P} = 1.185^{2} (2) \)= 2.81.

The actual factors of safety are 2.24, 2.66, 3.21, and 2.81. Making a direct comparison of the factors, we note that the primary threat is from gear bending. We also note that the other three factors of safety are con-siderably higher than the target design factor. If optimization is desired, our goal would be to make changes in the design decisions that drive the factors closer to 2.
