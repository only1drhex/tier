Guest Question.

For the problem in, estimate the infinite-life fatigue factor of safety for each of the failure criteria defined in this section. Compare the results.

Admin
Solution

From , \(σ_{a} = 48 kpsi, σ_{m} = 24 kpsi, S_{ut} = 100 kpsi, and S_{e} = 40 kpsi. \)

Goodman:
Equation (6–41) \(n_{f} = (\frac{σ_{a}}{S_{e}}+ \frac{σ_{m}}{S_{ut}})^{−1}= (\frac{48}{40} +\frac{24}{100})^{−1}= 0.69 \)

Morrow:
Lacking specific material properties, use the estimate for steel in Equation (6–44),
\(σ^{′}_{f} = S_{ut} + 50 kpsi = 150 kpsi\)

Equation (6–46) \(n_{f} = (\frac{σ_{a}}{S_{e}}+ \frac{σ_{m}}{σ^{′}_{f}})^{−1}= (\frac{48}{40} +\frac{24}{150})^{−1}= 0.74 \)

Gerber:
Equation (6–48) \( n_{f}=\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{S_{ut}}{σ_{m}} \right)^{2}\left(\frac{σ_{a}}{S_{e}} \right) \left[-1+\sqrt{1+\left(\frac{2σ_{m}S_{e}}{S_{ut}σ_{a}} \right)^{2} } \right] \)

 

\( n_{f}=\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{100}{24} \right)^{2}\left(\frac{48}{40} \right) \left[-1+\sqrt{1+\left(\frac{2(24)(40)}{100(48) } \right)^{2} } \right]= 0.80 \)

Soderberg:
Equation (6–50) \( n_{f}= \left(\frac{\sigma _{a}}{S_{e}} +\frac{\sigma _{m}}{S_{y}} \right) ^{-1}= \left(\frac{48}{40} +\frac{24}{85} \right) ^{-1}=0.67 \)

ASME-Elliptic:
Equation (6–52) \( n_{f}= \left[\left(\frac{\sigma _{a}}{S_{e}}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{\sigma _{m}}{S_{y}}\right)^{2} \right] ^{-1/2}= \left[\left(\frac{48}{40}\right)^{2}+\left(\frac{24}{85}\right)^{2} \right]^{-1/2}=0.81 \)

SWT:
Equation (6–54) \( n_{f}=\frac{S_{e}}{\sqrt{(σ_{m} + σ_{a})σ_{a}} } =\frac{40}{\sqrt{(24 + 48)48} } =0.68 \)

Walker:
Lacking specific material test data, use the estimate in Equation (6–57),
γ = −0.0014\(S_{ut}\) + 0.8818 = −0.0014(100) + 0.8818 = 0.74

Equation (6–56) \(n_{f} = \frac{S_{e}}{(σ_{m} + σ_{a})^{1−γ}σ^{γ}_{a}}= \frac{40}{(24 + 48)^{1−0.74} 48^{0.74}} = 0.75\)

For comparison, sort all of the results in order of most conservative to least conservative.
Soderberg 0.67
SWT 0.68
Goodman 0.69
Morrow 0.74
Walker 0.75
Gerber 0.80
ASME-Elliptic 0.81
Probably, Walker and Morrow are the most accurate. Gerber and ASME-Elliptic are nonconservative, which they tend to be with low mean stress. Goodman is conservative as expected. SWT is about the same as Goodman, not being a particularly good match for the fitting parameter γ needed for this material (as estimated by Walker). Soderberg is, as always, conservative, though not as much for low mean stress where its line is not much different from Goodman. Which is best? It depends on the goal. Probably, in this case, Walker or Morrow were equally good for a result typical of the data, and Goodman is a good choice for a reasonably predictable amount of conservativeness.
