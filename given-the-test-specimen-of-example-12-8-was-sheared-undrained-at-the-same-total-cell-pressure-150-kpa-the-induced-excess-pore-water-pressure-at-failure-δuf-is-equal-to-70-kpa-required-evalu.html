Guest Question.

Given:

The test specimen of was sheared undrained at the same total cell pressure (150 kPa). The induced excess pore water pressure at failure \(\Delta u_{f}\) is equal to 70 kPa.

Required:

Evaluate the the following parameters:

a. \(\sigma_{1 f}^{\prime}\),

b. \(\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)_{f}\),

c. \(\phi\) in terms of total stress, and

d. the angle of the failure plane \(\alpha_{f}\).

Admin
Solution

a and b. Since the void ratio after consolidation would be the same for this test as for , assume \(\phi^{\prime}\) is the same. You can do this problem either (1) analytically or (2) graphically.

1. Analytically: We know from Eq. (12.3) that

 

\(\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)_{f}=\sigma_{3 f}^{\prime}\left[\left(\frac{\sigma_{1}^{\prime}}{\sigma_{3}^{\prime}}\right)_{\max }-1\right]\) (12.3)

 

\(\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)_{f}=\sigma_{3 f}^{\prime}\left[\left(\frac{\sigma_{1}^{\prime}}{\sigma_{3}^{\prime}}\right)_{\max }-1\right]\)

 

\(\sigma_{3 f}^{\prime}=\sigma_{3 f}-\Delta u_{f}=150-70=80 kPa\)

 

So

 

\(\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)_{f}=80(3.7-1)=216 kPa\)

 

\(\sigma_{1 f}^{\prime}=\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)_{f}+\sigma_{3 f}^{\prime}=216+80=296 kPa\)

 

These are the answers to parts a and b.

c. We can write Eqs. (11.13) and (12.1) in terms of total stresses. Using Eq. (11.13),

 

\(\sin \phi=\frac{\left(\sigma_{1 f}-\sigma_{3 f}\right)}{\left(\sigma_{1 f}+\sigma_{3 f}\right)}\) (11.13)

 

\(\left(\frac{\sigma_{1}^{\prime}}{\sigma_{3}^{\prime}}\right)_{\max }=\frac{1+\sin \phi^{\prime}}{1-\sin \phi^{\prime}}=\tan ^{2}\left(45+\frac{\phi^{\prime}}{2}\right)\) (12.1)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sin \phi_{\text {total }} &=\frac{\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}}{\sigma_{1}+\sigma_{3}}=\frac{216}{(296+70)+150}=0.42 \\\phi_{\text {total }} &=24.8^{\circ}\end{aligned}\)

 

Using Eq. (12.1),

 

\(\frac{\sigma_{1 f}}{\sigma_{3 f}}(\text { no primes })=\frac{(296+70)}{150}=2.44=\tan ^{2}\left(45^{\circ}+\frac{\phi}{2}\right)\)

 

2. Graphically: Plot the Mohr failure envelope with \(\phi^{\prime}=35^{\circ}\) on a Mohr diagram (Fig. Ex. 13.3). There is only one circle that is tangent to the envelope and with \(\sigma_{3 f}^{\prime}=80 kPa (150-70)\). Once the circle is drawn (trial and error), \(\sigma_{1 f}^{\prime}\) is automatically determined \(\left(\sigma_{1 f}^{\prime}=296 kPa \right)\) as is \(\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)_{f}\), the diameter of the failure circle (=216 kPa).

 

The Mohr circle at failure in terms of total stresses has the same diameter since \(\left(\sigma_{1}-\sigma_{3}\right)=\left(\sigma_{1}^{\prime}-\sigma_{3}^{\prime}\right)\). You can plot the total stress circle starting at \(\sigma_{3 f}=150\), the total cell pressure, and determine \(\phi_{\text {total }}\). Compare Figs. Ex. 13.2 and 13.3 with Fig. 13.59(a).

d. From Eq. (11.10), \(\alpha_{f}=45^{\circ}+\phi^{\prime} / 2=62.5^{\circ}\).

 

\(\alpha_{f}=45^{\circ}+\frac{\phi}{2}\) (11.10)
