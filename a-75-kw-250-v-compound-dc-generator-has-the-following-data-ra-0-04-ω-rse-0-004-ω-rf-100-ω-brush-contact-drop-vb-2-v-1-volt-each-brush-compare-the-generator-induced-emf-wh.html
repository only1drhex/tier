Guest Question.

A 75 kW, 250 V compound dc generator has the following data:

\( R_{a} = 0.04\Omega\), \( R_{se} = 0.004\Omega\) \( R_{f} = 100\Omega\)

Brush contact drop, \( V_{b} = 2 V\) (1 volt each brush)

Compare the generator induced emf when fully loaded in (i) long shunt compound, and (ii) short shunt compound

Admin
Solution

Long shunt and short shunt connection are drawn in Figs 7.65(a) and (b)

At full load

\( V_{t} = 250 V, I_{L} =\frac{75\times 10^{3}}{250}= 300 A\)

Long Shunt (LS)

\( I_{f} = 250/100 = 2.5 A, I_{a} = 300 + 2.5 = 302.5 A\)

 

\( E_{a} = 250 + (0.04 + 0.004) \times 302.5 + 2 = 265.31 A\)

 

Short Shunt (SS)

\( V_{b} = 250 + 0.004 \times 300 = 251.2 V\)

 

\( I_{f} = 251.2 /100 = 2.512 A\)

 

\( I_{a} = 300 + 2.512 = 302. 512 A\)

 

\( E_{a} = 250 + 0.04 \times 302.512 + 2 = 264.1 V\)

 

\( E_{a} (LS) – E_{a} (SS) = 265.31 – 264.1 = 1.21 V\)

 

Difference as percentage of \( V_{t} =\frac{1.21\times 100}{250} = 0.484% \)

Conclusion: There is no significant difference in LS and SS.
