Guest Question.

A 4 pole, 50 Hz, 24 kV, 600 MVA synchronous generator with a synchronous reactance of 1.8 pu is synchronized to a power system which can be represented by a Thevenin voltage of 24 kV in series with Thevenin reactance of 0.24 pu on generator base. The generator voltage regulator adjusts the field current to maintain its terminal voltage and 24 kV independent of load.

(a) The generator prime mover power is adjusted so that it feeds 400 MV.

(i) Draw the phasor diagram under this operating condition

(ii) Calculate the generator current and its power factor

(b) Repeat part (a) when the generator feeds 600 MW

\( (MVA)_{B} = 600, (kV)_{B} = 24, (MW)_{B} = 600\)

The system diagram is drawn in Fig. 8.49(a).

Admin
Solution

(a) (i) As the generator is feeding power to the systems

\( \bar{V}_{t}\) leads \( \bar{V}_{ TH }\) angle \( \delta\)

 

\( P_{e}=\frac{V_{t} V_{ TH }}{0.24} \sin \delta\)

 

\( P_{e}=\frac{400}{600}=2 / 3 pu , \quad V_{t}=1 pu , \quad V_{ TH }=1 pu\)

 

\( \frac{2}{3}=\frac{1}{0.24} \sin \delta_{1}\) or \( \delta_{1}=9.2^{\circ}\)

 

The phasor diagram is drawn in Fig. 4.49(b); \( \delta_{1}\) is not to scale

(ii) From the phasor-diagram geometry

\( 0.24 I_{a}=2 \times 1 \sin \delta_{1} / 2\)

Or \( I_{a}=\frac{2}{0.24} \sin 9.2^{\circ} / 2=\) 0.668 pu

\( \left(I_{a}\right)_{B}=\frac{(600 / 3) \times 10^{6}}{\frac{24}{\sqrt{3}} \times 10^{3}}=\) 14433 A

\( I_{a}=0.668 \times 14433=\) 9641 A

Phase angle, \( \phi=\frac{\delta}{2}=4.6^{\circ} lag\)

Power factor \( =\cos 4.6^{\circ}=0.9968\)

Check \( P_{e}=\sqrt{3} kV I_{a} \times 10^{-3} \cos \phi\)

 

\( =\sqrt{3} \times 24 \times 9.641 \times 0.9968=399.5 \approx 400 MW\)

Observation \( P_{e}=\frac{V_{t} V_{ TH }}{X_{ TH }} \sin \delta_{1}=V_{t} I_{a} \cos \phi \) (in pu)

\( \bar{E}_{f}=\bar{V}_{t}+j \bar{I}_{a} X_{s}\)

=\(1 \angle 0^{\circ}+j 0.668 \angle-4.6^{\circ} \times 1.8=1+1.2 \angle 85.4^{\circ}=1.622 \angle 47.4^{\circ}\)

Or \( E_{f}=1.622\) or \( 38.9 kV , \delta_{2}=47.4^{\circ}\)

(b) \( P_{e}=600 MW \text { or } P_{e}=\) 1 pu

\( 1=\frac{1}{0.24} \sin \delta_{1}, \delta_{1}=13.89^{\circ}\)

 

\( I_{a}=\frac{2}{0.24} \sin \delta_{1} / 2=\) 1 pu

Or \( I_{a}=14433 \times 1=\) 14433 A

Phase angle, \( \phi=13.89^{\circ} / 2=6.945^{\circ} lag\)

Power factor \( =0.993 lag\)

 

\( \bar{E}_{f}=1 \angle 0^{\circ}+j \times 1 \angle-6.95^{\circ} \times 1.8=1+1.8 \angle 83.05^{\circ}=2.157 \angle 55.6^{\circ}\)

 

\( E_{f}=2.157 pu\)

Or \( E_{f}=2.157 \times 24=31.77 kV , \delta_{2}=55.6^{\circ}\)

Let us find power factor from \( E_{f}\) to \( V_{TH}\)

\( P_{e}=\frac{E_{f} V_{ TH }}{X_{s}+X_{ TH }} \sin \left(\delta_{1}+\delta_{2}\right)\)

Or \( P_{e}=\frac{2.157 \times 1}{1.8+0.24} \sin \left(13.89^{\circ}+55.6^{\circ}\right)=0.99 pu\)

It should be 1 pu. The difference is due to rounding off errors.
