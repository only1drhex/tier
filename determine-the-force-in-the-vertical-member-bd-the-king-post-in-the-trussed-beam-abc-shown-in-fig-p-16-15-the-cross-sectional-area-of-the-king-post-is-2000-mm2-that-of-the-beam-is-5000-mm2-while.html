Guest Question.

Determine the force in the vertical member BD (the king post) in the trussed beam ABC shown in Fig. P.16.15. The cross-sectional area of the king post is \(2000 mm ^{2}\), that of the beam is \(5000 mm ^{2}\) while that of the members AD and DC of the truss is \(200 mm ^{2}\); the second moment of area of the beam is \(4.2 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\) and Young’s modulus, E, is the same for all members.

Ans. 91.6 kN.

Admin
Solution

This problem may be solved using strain energy and Castigliano’s theorem (Eq. (15.32)).

\(\frac{\partial U}{\partial \Delta_{j}}=P_{j}\) (15.32)

Suppose that the compressive load in the king post is R. Then there will be tensile loads in the members AD and DC equal to \((\sqrt{5}) R / 2\). Further there will be a resulting compressive load in the beam AC equal to R. Therefore, from Eq. (7.29)

\(U=\frac{P^{2} L_{0}}{2 A E}\) (7.29)

the strain energy due to the axial loads is given by

\(U_{ A }=\frac{R^{2}\left[\frac{4 \times 10^{3}}{5000}+\frac{1 \times 10^{3}}{2000}+\frac{(5 \sqrt{5}) \times 10^{3}}{2 \times 200}\right]}{2 E}=\frac{14.63 R^{2}}{E}\)

The bending moment at any section a distance x from A in AB is given by

\(M=\left(50 \times 10^{3}-0.5 R\right) x\)

Therefore, from Eq. (9.21)

\(U=\int_{L} \frac{M^{2}}{2 E I_{z}} d x\) (9.21)

\(U_{ B }=\frac{2}{2 E I} \int_{0}^{2000}\left[\frac{\left(50 \times 10^{3}-0.5 R\right)^{2}}{4}\right] x^{2} d x=\frac{\left(100 \times 10^{3}-R\right)^{2} \times 4^{3} \times 10^{9}}{96 E I}\)

The total strain energy is then

\(U_{ A }+U_{ B }=U=\frac{14.63 R^{2}}{E}+\frac{\left(100 \times 10^{3}-R\right)^{2} \times 158.7}{E}\)

From Castigliano’s theorem

\(\frac{\partial U}{\partial R}=0\) ,

therefore

\(0=29.26 R-2\left(100 \times 10^{3}-R\right) \times 158.7\)

from which
R = 91.6 kN.
