Guest Question.

A uniform cantilever of arbitrary cross section and length L has section properties \(I_{z}, I_{ y }\) and \(I_{z y}\) with respect to the centroidal axes shown (Fig. P.13.14). It is loaded in the vertical plane by a tip load W. The tip of the beam is hinged to a horizontal link which constrains it to move in the vertical direction only (provided that the actual deflections are small). Assuming that the link is rigid and that there are no twisting effects, calculate the force in the link and the deflection of the tip of the beam.

Ans. \(W I_{z y} / I_{z}\) (compression if \(I_{z y}\) is positive), \(W L^{3} / 3 E I_{z}\) (downwards).

Admin
Solution

Again the problem may be solved by working from first principles or the solution may be deduced from the symmetrical section beam case of Ex. 13.1, Eq. (v).

\(v_{\text {tip }}=-\frac{W L^{3}}{3 E I}\) Eq. (v) from Ex. 13.1

The horizontal component of deflection of the free end of the cantilever with W acting alone is then

\(u_{ fe }=\left(\frac{W L^{3}}{3 E}\right)\left(\frac{I_{z y}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}}\right)\)

and due to the tension T in the link acting alone

\(u_{ fe }=-\left(\frac{T L^{3}}{3 E}\right)\left(\frac{I_{z}}{I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}}\right)\)

The sum of these two expressions is zero since there is no horizontal deflection. Therefore \(T=W\left(I_{z y} / I_{z}\right)\).

With this value of T, \(M_{y}=-W\left(I_{z y} / I_{z}\right)(L-x)\) and \(M_{z}=-W(L-x)\). Substituting in Eq. (13.15)

\(\frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=\frac{M_{z} I_{y}-M_{y} I_{z y}}{E\left(I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}\right)}\) (13.15)

\(\frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=-\frac{\left[-W(L-x) I_{y}+W\left(\frac{I_{z y}}{I_{z}}\right) I_{z y}(L-x)\right]}{E\left(I_{z} I_{y}-I_{z y}^{2}\right)}=\frac{-W(L-x)}{E I_{z}}\)

This expression is identical to Eq. (ii) in Ex. 13.1

\(E I \frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=-W(L-x)\) Eq. (ii) from Ex. 13.1

so that we deduce that the vertical deflection of the free end of the cantilever is \(-W L^{3} / 3 E I_{z}\).
