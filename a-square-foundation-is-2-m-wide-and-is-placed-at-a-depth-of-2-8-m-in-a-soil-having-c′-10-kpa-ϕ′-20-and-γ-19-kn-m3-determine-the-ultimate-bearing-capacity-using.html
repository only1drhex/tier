Guest Question.

A square foundation is \(2 \mathrm{~m}\) wide and is placed at a depth of \(2.8 \mathrm{~m}\) in a soil having \(c^{\prime}=10 \mathrm{kPa} ; \phi^{\prime}=20^{\circ}\) and \(\gamma=19 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\). Determine the ultimate bearing capacity using the

i. Terzaghi,

ii. Meyerhof and

iii. Hansen approaches

Admin
Solution

i. Terzaghi approach:

\(q_{u l t}=c N_{c}+\gamma D N_{q}+0.5 B \gamma N_{\gamma}\)

From Table 9.1: \(s_{c}=1.3 ; s_{q}=1.0 ; s_{\gamma}=0.8\)

From Figure 9.1: \(N_{c}=17.7 ; N_{q}=7.4 ; N_{\gamma}=5.0\)

\(q_{u l t}=1.3 \times 10 \times 17.7+19 \times 2.8 \times 7.4+0.4 \times 2 \times 19 \times 5=700 \mathrm{kpa}\)

ii. Meyerhof (1963) approach:

\(q_{u l t}=c N_{c} d_{c} s_{c}+\gamma D N_{q} d_{q} s_{q}+0.5 B \gamma N_{\gamma} d_{\gamma} s_{\gamma}\)

From Figure 9.2: \(N_{c}=14.8 ; N_{q}=6.4 ; N_{\gamma}=2.9\)

Shape factors:

From Table 9.1: \(s_{c}=1.41 ; s_{q}=1.2 ; s_{\gamma}=1.2\)

Depth factors:

From Table 9.1: \(d_{c}=1.40 ; d_{q}=1.2 ; d_{\gamma}=1.2\)

\(\begin{aligned}&q_{u l t}=(10 \times 14.8 \times 1.40 \times 1.41)+(19 \times 2.8 \times 6.4 \times 1.2 \times 1.2)+(0.5 \times 2 \times 19 \times 2.9 \times 1.2 \times 1.2) \\ &q=861.79 \mathrm{kPa}\end{aligned}\)
