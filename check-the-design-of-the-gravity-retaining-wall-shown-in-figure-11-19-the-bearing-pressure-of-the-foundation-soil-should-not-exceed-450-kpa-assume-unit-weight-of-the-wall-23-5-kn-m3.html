Guest Question.

Check the design of the gravity retaining wall shown in Figure 11.19. The bearing pressure of the foundation soil should not exceed \(450 \mathrm{kPa}\). Assume unit weight of the wall \(=23.5 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\).

Admin
Solution

Coulomb’s theory is used to compute the active earth pressure considering the wall friction and the inclination of wall and soil surfaces (see Section 11.5).

\(K_{a}=\left[\frac{(\sin (\alpha-\phi) / \sin \alpha)}{\left.\sqrt{\sin (\alpha+\delta)}+\sqrt{\left[\frac{\sin (\phi+\delta) \cdot \sin (\phi-\beta)}{\sin (\alpha-\beta)}\right.}\right]}\right]^{2}=0.6\)

From Eq.11.17:

\(P_{p}=\int_{0}^{H} K_{a} \gamma z=\frac{1}{2} K_{a} \gamma H^{2}\) (11.17)

\(P_{a}=\frac{1}{2} K_{a} \gamma H^{2}=\frac{1}{2} \times 0.6 \times 18 \times 6.5^{2}=228.15 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\) and it acts at \(40^{\circ}\) above the horizontal.

Lever arm of base resultant: \(\sum \frac{M}{V}=\frac{345}{438}=0.8 \mathrm{~m}\)

Check for overturning

The restoring moment \(\left(M_{v}\right)\) is greater than the disturbing moment \(\left(M_{H}\right)\). Hence this satisfies the overturning limit state.

Check for bearing pressure

Eccentricity of base reaction, \(e=\frac{2.8}{2}-0.8=0.6 \mathrm{~m}\)

\(p_{\max }=\frac{V}{B}\left(1+\frac{6 e}{B}\right)=\frac{438}{2.8}\left(1+\frac{6 \times 0.6}{2.8}\right)=357.5 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{2}\)

The maximum bearing pressure is less than the allowable bearing pressure of the foundation soil, i.e. \(450 \mathrm{kPa}\). This satisfies the bearing pressure limit state.

Check for sliding

The restoring force:

\(V \tan \delta=438 \times \tan 25^{\circ}=204.2 \mathrm{kN}\)

The disturbing force:

\(\mathrm{H}=185 \mathrm{kN}\)

Hence this satisfies the sliding limit state.

Therefore, the design of the gravity wall is satisfactory.
