Guest Question.

In an experimental determination of principal stresses a cantilever of hollow circular cross section is subjected to a varying bending moment and torque; the internal and external diameters of the cantilever are 40 and 50 mm, respectively. For a given loading condition the bending moment and torque at a particular section of the cantilever are 100 and 50 Nm, respectively. Calculate the maximum and minimumprincipal stresses at a pointonthe outer surface of the cantilever at this sectionwhere the direct stress produced by the bendingmoment is tensile.Determine also themaximumshear stress at the point and the inclination of the principal stresses to the axis of the cantilever.
The experimental values of principal stress are estimated from readings obtained from a 45Â° strain gauge rosette aligned so that one of its three arms is parallel to and another perpendicular to the axis of the cantilever. For the loading condition of zero torque and varying bending moment, comment on the ratio of these strain gauge readings.

Ans. \(\sigma_{ I }=14.6 N / mm ^{2}, \sigma_{ II }=-0.8 N / mm ^{2}\)

\(\tau_{\max }=7.7 N / mm ^{2}, \theta=-13.3^{\circ}\) and \(-103.3^{\circ}\).

Admin
Solution

The direct stress due to bending is given by Eq. (9.9)

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{M y}{I}\) (9.9)

and is

\(\sigma_{x}=\frac{100 \times 10^{3} \times 25}{\left[\pi\left(\frac{50^{4}-40^{4}}{64}\right)\right]}=13.8 N / mm ^{2}\)

The shear stress due to the applied torque is obtained from Eq. (11.4)

\(\frac{T}{J}=\frac{\tau}{r}=G \frac{\theta}{L}\) (11.4)

and is

\(\tau_{x y}=\frac{50 \times 10^{3} \times 25}{\left[\pi\left(\frac{50^{4}-40^{4}}{32}\right)\right]}=3.45 N / mm ^{2}\)

Substituting these values in Eqs (14.8) and (14.9)

\(\sigma_{ I }=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2}+\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right)^{2}+4 \tau_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.8)

\(\sigma_{ II }=\frac{\sigma_{x}+\sigma_{y}}{2}-\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right)^{2}+4 \tau_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.9)

and noting that \(\sigma_{y}=0\) gives

\(\sigma_{ I }=14.6 N / mm ^{2}\) and \(\sigma_{ II }=-0.8 N / mm ^{2}\)

The maximum shear stress can be found from either Eq. (14.11)

\(\tau_{\max , \min }=\pm \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}\right)^{2}+4 \tau_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.11)

or Eq. (14.12).

\(\tau_{\max }=\frac{\sigma_{ I }-\sigma_{ II }}{2}\) (14.12)

Using the latter

\(\tau_{\max }=\frac{14.6+0.8}{2}=7.7 N / mm ^{2}\)

Finally, from Eq. (14.7)

\(\tan 2 \theta=-\frac{2 \tau_{x y}}{\sigma_{x}-\sigma_{y}}\) (14.7)

\(\tan 2 \theta=\frac{-2 \times 3.45}{13.8}=-0.5\)

i.e.

\(\theta=-13.3^{\circ}\) and \(-103.3^{\circ}\)
