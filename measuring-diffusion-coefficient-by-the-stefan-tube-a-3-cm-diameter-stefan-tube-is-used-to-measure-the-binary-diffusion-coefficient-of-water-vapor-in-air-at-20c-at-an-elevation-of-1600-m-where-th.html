Measuring Diffusion Coefficient by the Stefan Tube

A 3-cm-diameter Stefan tube is used to measure the binary diffusion coefficient of water vapor in air at \( 20^{\circ}C \) at an elevation of 1600 m where the atmospheric pressure is 83.5 kPa. The tube is partially filled with water, and the distance from the water surface to the open end of the tube is 40 cm (Fig. 14–37). Dry air is blown over the open end of the tube so that water vapor rising to the top is removed immediately and the concentration of vapor at the top of the tube is zero. In 15 days of continuous operation at constant pressure and temperature, the amount of water that has evaporated is measured to be 1.23 g. Determine the diffusion coefficient of water vapor in air at \( 20^{\circ}C \) and 83.5 kPa.

Admin
Solution

SOLUTION The amount of water that evaporates from a Stefan tube at a specified temperature and pressure over a specified time period is measured. The diffusion coefficient of water vapor in air is to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Water vapor and atmospheric air are ideal gases. 2 The amount of air dissolved in liquid water is negligible. 3 Heat is transferred to the water from the surroundings to make up for the latent heat of vaporization so that the temperature of water remains constant at 20°C.

Properties The saturation pressure of water at \( 20^{\circ}C \) is 2.34 kPa (Table A–9).

Analysis The vapor pressure at the air–water interface is the saturation pressure of water at \( 20^{\circ}C \), and the mole fraction of water vapor (species A) at the interface is determined from

\( y_{vapor, 0} = y_{A, 0} = \frac{P_{vapor, 0}}{P} = \frac{2.34 kPa}{83.5 kPa} = 0.0280 \)

 

Dry air is blown on top of the tube and, thus, \( y_{vapor, L} = y_{A, L} = 0 \). Also, the total molar density throughout the tube remains constant because of the constant temperature and pressure conditions and is determined to be

\( C = \frac{P}{R_{u} T} = \frac{83.5 kPa}{\left ( 8.314 kPa \cdot m^{3}/kmol \cdot K \right )\left ( 293 K \right )} = 0.0343 kmol/m^{3} \)

 

The cross-sectional area of the tube is

\( A = \pi D^{2}/4 = \pi \left ( 0.03 m \right )^{2}/4 = 7.069 \times 10^{-4} m^{2}\)

 

The evaporation rate is given to be 1.23 g per 15 days. Then the molar flow rate of vapor is determined to be

\( \dot{N}_{A} = \dot{N}_{vapor} = \frac{\dot{m}_{vapor}}{M_{vapor}} \)

 

\( = \frac{1.23 \times 10^{-3} kg}{\left ( 15 \times 24 \times 3600 s \right )\left ( 18 kg/kmol \right )} = 5.27 \times 10^{-11} kmol/s \)

 

Finally, substituting the information above into Eq. \( \overline{\dot{J}}_{A} = \dot{N}_{A}/A = \frac{CD_{AB}}{L} ln \frac{1 – y_{A,L}}{1 – y_{A,0}} \) we get

 

\( \frac{5.27 \times 10^{-11} kmol/s}{7.069 \times 10^{-4} m^{2}} = \frac{\left ( 0.0343 kmol/m^{3} \right ) D_{AB}}{0.4 m} ln \frac{1 – 0}{1 – 0.028} \)

 

which gives

\( D_{AB} = 3.06 \times 10^{-5} m^{2}/s \)

 

for the binary diffusion coefficient of water vapor in air at \( 20^{\circ}C \) and 83.5 kPa.
