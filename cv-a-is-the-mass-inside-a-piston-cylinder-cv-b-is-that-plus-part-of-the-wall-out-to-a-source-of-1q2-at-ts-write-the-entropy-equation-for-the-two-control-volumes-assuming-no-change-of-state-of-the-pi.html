Guest Question.

CV A is the mass inside a piston/cylinder, CV B is that plus part of the wall out to a source of \({ }_{1} Q _{2} \text { at } T _{ s }\). Write the entropy equation for the two control volumes assuming no change of state of the piston mass or walls.

Admin
Solution

The general entropy equation for a control mass is Eq.6.37

\(S _{2}- S _{1}=\int_{1}^{2} \frac{ dQ }{ T }+{ }_{1} S _{2 gen} \)

The left hand side is storage so that depends of what is inside the C.V. and the integral is summing the dQ/T that crosses the control volume surface while the process proceeds from 1 to 2.

 

C.V. A: \(m _{ A }\left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right)=\int_{1}^{2} \frac{ d Q }{ T _{ A }}+{ }_{1} S _{2 gen CV A} \)

 

C.V. B: \(m _{ A }\left( s _{2}- s _{1}\right)=\int_{1}^{2} \frac{ d Q }{ T _{ s }}+{ }_{1} S _{2 gen CV B } \)

 

In the first equation the temperature is that of mass \(m _{ A }\) which possibly changes from 1 to 2 whereas in the second equation it is the reservoir temperature \(T _{ s }\). The two entropy generation terms are also different the second one includes the first one plus any s generated in the walls that separate the mass \(m _{ A }\) from the reservoir and there is a Q over a finite temperature difference. When the storage effect in the walls are neglected the left hand sides of the two equations are equal.
