Guest Question.

A small current loop \(L_{1}\) with magnetic moment \(5a_{z}\) A·\(m^{2}\) is located at the origin while another small loop current \(L_{2}\) with magnetic moment \(3a_{y} A·m^{2}\) is located at (4,-3,10).

Determine the torque on \(L_{2}\).

Admin
Solution

The torque \(T_{2}\) on the loop \(L_{2}\) is due to the field \(B_{1}\) produced by loop \(L_{1}\).Hence,

\(T_{2}=m_{2}\times B_{1}\)

Since m1 for loop \(L_{1}\) is along \(a_{z}\), we find \(B_{1}\) using eq. (8.22):

\(B_{1}=\frac{\mu _{0}m_{1}}{4\pi r^{3}}(2 \cos \theta a_{r}+\sin \theta a_{\theta }) (8.22)\)

Using eq. (2.23), we transform \(m_{2}\) from Cartesian to spherical coordinates:

\(a_{x}=\sin \theta \cos \phi a_{r}+\cos \theta \cos \phi a_{\theta }-\sin \phi a_{\phi }\)
\(a_{y}=\sin \theta \sin \phi a_{r}+\cos \theta \sin \phi a_{\theta }+\cos \phi a_{\phi }\) (2.23)
\(a_{z}=\cos \theta a_{r}-\sin \theta a_{\theta }\)

\(m_{2}=3a_{y}=3(\sin \theta \sin \phi a_{r}+\cos \theta \sin \phi a_{\theta }+\cos \phi a_{\phi })\)

At (4, -3, 10),
\(r=\sqrt{4^{2}+(-3)^{2}+10^{2}}=5\sqrt{5}\)
\(\tan \theta =\frac{\rho }{z}=\frac{5}{10}=\frac{1}{2}\rightarrow \sin \theta =\frac{1}{\sqrt{5} }, \cos \theta = \frac{2}{\sqrt{5} }\)
\(\tan \phi =\frac{y}{x} =\frac{-3}{4}\rightarrow \sin \phi =\frac{-3}{5}, \cos \phi =\frac{4}{5}\)
Hence,
\(B_{1}=\frac{4\pi \times 10^{-7}\times 5}{4\pi 625\sqrt{5} }(\frac{4}{\sqrt{5} }a_{r}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{5} }a_{\theta })\)
\(=\frac{10^{-7}}{625}(4a_{r}+a_{\theta })\)
\(m_{2}=3[-\frac{3a_{r}}{5\sqrt{5}}-\frac{6a_{\theta }}{5\sqrt{5}}+\frac{4a_{\phi }}{5} ]\)
and
\(T=\frac{10^{-7}(3)}{625(5\sqrt{5})}(-3a_{r}-6a_{\theta }+4\sqrt{5}a_{\phi })\times(4a_{r}+a_{\phi })\)
\(=4.293\times10^{-11}(-8.944a_{r}+35.777a_{\theta }+21a_{\phi })\)
\(=-0.384a_{r}+1.536a_{\theta }+0.9015a_{\phi }\)nN.m
