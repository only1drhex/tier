Guest Question.

A charge \( q_{1}=7.0 \mu C\) is located at the origin, and a second charge \( q_{2}=-5.0 \mu \mathrm{C}\) is located on the x axis \( 0.30 \mathrm{~m}\) from the origin (Fig. 23.13). Find the electric field at the point P; Which has coordinates (0,0.40) \( \mathrm{m}\)

Admin
Solution

First, let us find the magnitude of the electric field at P due to each charge. The fields \(\mathbf{E}_{1}\) due to the \( 7.0-\mu \mathrm{C}\) charge and \( \mathbf{E}_{2}\) due to the \( -5.0-\mu \mathrm{C}\) charge are shown in Figure 29.13 . Their magnitudes are

\( E_{1} =k_{e} \frac{\left|q_{1}\right|}{r_{1}^{2}}=\left(8.99 \times 10^{9} \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2}}{\mathrm{C}^{2}}\right) \frac{\left(7.0 \times 10^{-6} \mathrm{C}\right)}{(0.40 \mathrm{~m})^{2}} \) \( =3.9 \times 10^{5} \mathrm{~N} / \mathrm{C}\) \( E_{2} =k_{\ell} \frac{\left|q_{2}\right|}{r_{2}^{2}}=\left(8.99 \times 10^{9} \frac{\mathrm{N} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{2}}{\mathrm{C}^{2}}\right) \frac{\left(5.0 \times 10^{-6} \mathrm{C}\right)}{(0.50 \mathrm{~m})^{2}} \) \( =1.8 \times 10^{5} \mathrm{~N} / \mathrm{C}\)

The vector \( \mathbf{E}_{1}\) has only a y component. The vector \( \mathbf{E}_{2}\) has an x component given by \( E_{2} \cos \theta=\frac{3}{5} E_{2}\) and a negative y component given by \( -E_{2} \sin \theta=-\frac{4}{5} E_{2} . \) Hence, we can express the vectors as

\( \mathbf{E}_{1}=3.9 \times 10^{5} \mathbf{j} \mathrm{N} / \mathrm{C} \) \( \mathbf{E}_{2}=\left(1.1 \times 10^{5} \mathbf{i}-1.4 \times 10^{5} \mathbf{j}\right) \mathrm{N} / \mathrm{C}\)

The resultant field \( \mathbf{E}\) at P is the superposition of \( \mathbf{E}_{1}\) and \( \mathbf{E}_{2}\)

\( \mathbf{E}=\mathbf{E}_{1}+\mathbf{E}_{2}=\left(1.1 \times 10^{5} \mathbf{i}+2.5 \times 10^{5} \mathbf{j}\right) \mathrm{N} / \mathrm{C}\)

From this result, we find that \( \mathbf{E}\) has a magnitude of \( 2.7 \times10^{5} \mathrm{~N} / \mathrm{C}\) and makes an angle \( \phi\) of \( 66^{\circ} \mathrm{w}\) the positive x axis.
