Guest Question.

A mixture is composed of 2 mol \( CO _{2} \text { and } 4 mol N _{2} \). It is compressed adiabatically in a cylinder from 100 kPa and 20째C to 2 MPa. Assuming constant specific heats, calculate (a) the final temperature, (b) the work required, and (c) the change in entropy.

Admin
Solution

(a) The temperature is found using the isentropic relationship \( T_{2}=T_{1}\left(P_{2} / P_{1}\right)^{(k-1) / k} \).

\( \text { Let's find } k \text { for the mixture. The mass is } m=N_{1} M_{1}+N_{2} M_{2}=(2)(44)+(4)(28)=200 kg \text {. The specific heats are } \).

\( C_{v}=x_{1} C_{v, 1}+x_{2} C_{v, 2}=\left(\frac{88}{200}\right)(0.653)+\left(\frac{112}{200}\right)(0.745)=0.705 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

\( C_{p}=x_{1} C_{p, 1}+x_{2} C_{p, 2}=\left(\frac{88}{200}\right)(0.842)+\left(\frac{112}{200}\right)(1.042)=0.954 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

\( \text { The ratio of specific heats is } k=C_{p} / C_{v}=0.954 / 0.705=1.353 \text {. Consequently, the final temperature is } \)

\( T_{2}=T_{1}\left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}=(293)\left(\frac{2000}{1000}\right)^{0.353 / 1.353}=640 K \quad \text { or } \quad 367^{\circ} C \).

(b) The work is found using the first law with Q = 0:

\( W=-\Delta U=-m \Delta u=-m c_{v} \Delta T=(-200)(0.705)(367-20)=-48.9 MJ \).

(c) The entropy change is

\( \Delta s=C_{p} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \).

\( =0.954 \ln \frac{640}{293}-\frac{8.314}{\left(\frac{2}{6}\right)(44)+\left(\frac{4}{6}\right)(28)} \ln \frac{2000}{100}=-0.00184 kJ / kg \cdot K \).

Obviously, the entropy change should be zero for this isentropic process. The above small value is a measure of the error in our calculations.
