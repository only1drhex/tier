Guest Question.

Linseed oil with a specific gravity of 0.93 enters the reducing bend shown in Fig. 16.9 with a velocity of 3 m/s and a pressure of 275 kPa. The bend is in a horizontal plane. Calculate the x and y forces required to hold the bend in place. Neglect energy losses in the bend.

Admin
Solution

The fluid in the bend is shown as a free body in Fig. 16.10. We must first develop the force equations for the x and y directions shown.
The force equation for the x direction is

\( F_x = \rho Q \left(v_{2_x}-v_{1_x}\right) \)

\(R_x – p_1A_1+p_2A_2\cos 30° = \rho Q \left[-v_2 \cos 30°-\left(-v_1\right) \right] \)

\(R_x = p_1A_1-p_2A_2\cos 30° – \rho Q v_2 \cos 30°+\rho Qv_1\)

Algebraic signs must be carefully included according to the sign convention shown in Fig. 16.10. Notice that all forces and velocity terms are the components in the x direction.

In the y direction, the force equation is

\( F_y = \rho Q \left(v_{2_y}-v_{1_y}\right)\)

\( R_y – p_2A_2\sin 30° = \rho Q \left(v_2 \sin 30° -0\right) \)

\(R_y =p_2A_2 \sin 30°+ \rho Q v_2 \sin 30°\)

The numerical values of several items must now be calculated. For the entering and leaving pipes,\( A_1 = 1.767 \times 10^{-2} m^2 \)and \( A_2 = 4.418 \times 10^{-3} m^2\). We have

\( \rho = \left(sg\right) \left(\rho_W\right) = \left(0.93\right) \left(1000kg/m^3\right) =930kg/m^3\)

\(\gamma = \left(sg\right) \left(\gamma_W\right) = \left(0.93\right) \left(9.81kN/m^3\right) =9.12kN/m^3\)

\( Q = A_1v_1 = \left(1.767 \times 10^{-2}m^2\right) \left(3m/s\right) =0.053m^3/s\)

Because of continuity,\( A_1 v_1 = A_2 v_2\). Then, we have

\( v_2 = v_1 \left(A_1/A_2\right) =\left(3m/s\right) \left(1.767 \times 10^{-2}/4.418 \times 10^{-3}\right) =12m/s\)

Bernoulli’s equation can be used to find \( p_2\):

\( \frac{p_1}{\gamma} +z_1 + \frac{v^2_1}{2g} =\frac{p_2}{\gamma} +z_2 + \frac{v^2_2}{2g} \)

But \( z_1 = z_2\). Then, we have

\(p_2 = p_1 +\gamma \left(v^2_1-v^2_2\right) /2g\)

\( = 275kPa + \left[\frac{\left(9.12\right)\left(3^2-12^2\right) }{\left(2\right) \left(9.81\right) } \times \frac{kN}{m^3}\times \frac{m^2}{s^2}\times \frac{s^2}{m} \right] \)

= 275 kPa – 62.8 kPa

\( p_2 = 212.2 kPa\)

The quantities needed for Eqs. (16–9) and (16–10) are

\( p_1A_1 = \left(275kN/m^2\right) \left(1.767 \times 10^{-2}m^2\right) =4859N\)

\( p_2A_2 = \left(212.2kN/m^2\right) \left(4.418 \times 10^{-3}m^2\right) =938N\)

\(\rho Qv_1 = \left(930 kg/m^3\right) \left(0.053m^3/s\right) \left(3m/s\right) =148N\)

\( \rho Qv_2 = \left(930 kg/m^3\right) \left(0.053m^3/s\right) \left(12m/s\right) =591N\)

From Eq. (16–9), we get

\( R_x = \left(4859-938\cos 30°-591\cos 30°+148\right) N=3683N\)

From Eq. (16–10), we get

\( R_y = \left(938 \sin 30°+591\sin 30°\right) N=765N\)
