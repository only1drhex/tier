Guest Question.

The frame shown in Fig. P.18.15 is pinned to the foundation at D and to a wall at A. The plastic moment of resistance of the column CD is 200 kNm while that of the rafters AB and BC is 240 kNm. For the loading shown calculate the value of M at which collapse will take place.
Ans. M = 106.3 kN.

Admin
Solution

In this case sway is prevented so that the possible collapse mechanisms are one of gable and two of rafter as shown in Figs S.18.15(a), (b) and (c).
For (a)

\(P\left(3 \theta+3 \theta+\frac{4 \theta}{5}\right)=240 \times 2 \theta+200 \times 2 \theta\)

which gives

P = 129.4 kN

For (b)

P × 3θ = 240 × 2θ + 240θ

i.e.

P = 240 kN

For (c)

P × 3θ = 240θ + 240 × 2θ + 200θ

from which

P = 306.7 kN

For (a) + 2 × (b) with a hinge cancellation at B

P(6.8θ + 2 × 3θ)= 200 × 2θ+ 2 × 240θ + 240 × 2θ

so that

P = 106.3 kN

For (a) + 2 × (c) with a hinge cancellation at B

\(P(6.8 \theta+2 \times 3 \theta)=200 \times 2 \theta+2 \times 240 \theta+240 \times 2 \theta+2 \times 200 \theta\)

from which

P = 137.5 kN

Therefore the minimum critical value of P is 106.3 kN.
