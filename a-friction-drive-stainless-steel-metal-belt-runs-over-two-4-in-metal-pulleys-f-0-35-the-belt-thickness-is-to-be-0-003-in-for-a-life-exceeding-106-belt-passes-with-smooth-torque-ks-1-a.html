Guest Question.

A friction-drive stainless steel metal belt runs over two 4-in metal pulleys ( f = 0.35). The belt thickness is to be 0.003 in. For a life exceeding 10^{6} belt passes with smooth torque (\(K_{s} = \)1), (a) select the belt if the torque is to be 30 lbf · in, and (b) find the initial tension \(F_{i}\).

Admin
Solution

(a) From step 1, \(ϕ_{d}\) = π, therefore exp(0.35π) = 3.00. From step 2,

\( (S_{f})_{10^{6}} = 15.16(10^{6})(10^{6})^{−0.412} = 51 100 psi \)

From steps 3, 4, 5, and 6,

\( (F_{1})_{a} = [51 100 −\frac{ 28(10^{6})0.003}{(1 − 0.285^{2})4}] 0.003b = 84.7b \) (1)

ΔF = 2T∕D = 2(30)∕4 = 15 lbf

\( F_{2} = (F_{1})_{a} − ΔF = 84.7b − 15 \) (2)

\( F_{i} = \frac{(F_{1})_{a} + F_{2}}{2} = 84.7b − \frac{15}{2} \) (3)

From step 7,

\( b_{min} =\frac{ ΔF}{a}\frac{exp( f ϕ)}{exp( f ϕ) − 1} = \frac{15}{84.7}\frac{3.00}{3.00 − 1 }= 0.266 in \)

From step 8, select an available 0.75-in-wide belt 0.003 in thick.

Eq. (1): \( (F_{1})_{a} = 84.7(0.75) = 63.5 lbf \)

Eq. (2): \( F_{2} = 84.7(0.75) − 15 = 48.5 lbf \)

Eq. (3): \( F_{i} = 63.5 − 15∕2 = 56.0 lbf \)

From step 9, \( f ^{′} =\frac{1}{ϕ} ln\frac{(F_{1})_{a}}{F_{2}}= \frac{1}{π} ln\frac{63.5}{48.5} = 0.0858 \)

Note f′ < f, that is, 0.0858 < 0.35.
