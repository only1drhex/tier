Guest Question.

A steam condenser is supplied with 1000 kg/min steam in 0.9 dry state. The pressure at suction of air extraction pump on condenser is 70 cm of Hg and barometer reads 77 cm of Hg. Temperature in suction pipe is 30°C and air leaks at the rate of 5 × 10\(^{–4}\) kg per kg of steam. Cooling water temperature gets increased by 15°C. Determine the mass handled by dry air extractor and cooling water circulation rate in kg/min.

Admin
Solution
\(\begin{aligned}\text{Absolute pressure in condenser}&= \text{(77 – 70) = 7 cm of Hg or }(7 × 0.0135951 × 10^{4} × 9.81)\\p_{t}&= 9.34 kPa\\\text{Partial pressure of steam, }\qquad\quad p_{s}&= \text{Saturation pressure corresponding to 30°C = 4.246 kPa}\\\text{Partial pressure of air, }\qquad\qquad p_{a}&= p_{t} – p_{s} = 5.094 kPa\\\text{Rate of air extraction per minute}&= 5 × 10^{-4} × 1000 = 0.5 kg/min\\\text{Volume of air extracted per minute}&= \frac{mRT}{p_{a}} = \frac{0.5 × 0.287 × (273 + 30)}{5.094}\\&= 8.54 m^{3}/min\end{aligned}\\\text{Specific volume corresponding to 30°C, }v_{g} = 32.89 m^{3}/kg\\\begin{aligned}\text{Volume of air extracted = Volume of mixture sucked per minute}&= 8.54 m^{3}/min\\\text{Mass of steam extracted in mixture handled per minute} = \frac{8.54}{32.89}& = 0.2596 kg/min\\\text{Therefore, mass handled by air extraction pump = 0.5 + 0.2596}&= 0.7596 kg/min\end{aligned}\\\qquad \quad \text{Mass handled by air pump = 0.7596 kg/min}\\\begin{aligned}\text{Enthalpy of steam entering condenser, h}&= h_{\text{f at 30°C}} + 0.9 (h_{\text{fg at 30°C}})\\&= 125.79 + (0.9 × 2430.5)\\&= 2313.24 kJ/kg\\\text{Mass flow rate of circulating water can b}&\text{e obtained by energy balance on condenser,}\\m_{w} × Cp_{w} × (ΔT_{w})&= m_{s} × (h – Cp_{w} × T_{c})\\m_{w} × 4.18 × 15 &= 1000 × (2313.24 – 4.18 × 303)\\m_{w} &= 16693.78 kg/min\\\text{Water circulation rate = 16693.}&\text{78 kg/min}\end{aligned}\)
