Guest Question.

The moment acting on the cross section of the unequal-leg angle has a magnitude of 14 kN-m and is oriented as shown in Figure P8.79.
Determine:
(a) the bending stress at point H.
(b) the bending stress at point K.
(c) the maximum tension and the maximum compression bending stresses in the cross section.
(d) the orientation of the neutral axis relative to the +z axis. Show its location on a sketch of the cross section.

Admin
Solution

Section properties


Centroid location in y direction:

 

\(\begin{array}{lccccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Width } b \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Height } h \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} \text { (from bottom) } \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}y_{i} A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { horizontal leg } & 150 & 19 & 2,850 & 190.50 & 542,925.0 \\\text { vertical leg } & 19 & 181 & 3,439 & 90.50 & 311,229.5 \\\hline & & & 6,289 & & 854,154.5\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{y}=\frac{\Sigma y_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{854,154.5 mm ^{3}}{6,289 mm ^{2}} &=135.82 mm \text{(from bottom of shape to centroid)}\\&=64.18 mm \text{ (from top of shape to centroid) }\end{aligned}\)

 

Centroid location in z direction:

 

\(\begin{array}{lccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Area } A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{2}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}z_{i} \text { (from right edge) } \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}z_{i} A_{i} \\\left( mm ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { horizontal leg } & 2,850 & 75.0 & 213,750.0 \\\text { vertical leg } & 3,439 & 9.5 & 32,670.5 \\\hline & 6,289 & & 246,420.5\end{array}\)

 

\(\begin{aligned}\bar{z}=\frac{\Sigma z_{i} A_{i}}{\Sigma A_{i}}=\frac{246,420.5 mm ^{3}}{6,289 mm ^{2}} &=39.18 mm \text{(from right edge of shape to centroid)} \\&=110.82 mm \text{(from left edge of shape to centroid)}\end{aligned}\)

 

Moment of inertia about the z axis:

 

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}\text { Shape } & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=y_{i}-\bar{y} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { horizontal leg } & 85,737.50 & 54.68 & 8,522,088.15 & 8,607,825.65 \\\text { vertical leg } & 9,388,756.58 & -45.32 & 7,062,503.99 & 16,451,260.58 \\\hline &{\text { Moment of inertia about the } z \text { axis }\left( mm ^{4}\right)=} & 25,059,086.23\end{array}\)

 

Moment of inertia about the y axis:

 

\(\begin{array}{lcccc}{\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C} \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d=z_{i}-\bar{z} \\( mm )\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}I_{C}+d^{2} A \\\left( mm ^{4}\right)\end{array} \\\hline \text { horizontal leg } & 5,343,750.00 & 35.82 & 3,656,188.87 & 8,999,938.87 \\\text { vertical leg } & 103,456.58 & -29.68 & 3,029,990.78 & 3,133,447.36 \\\hline {\text { Moment of inertia about the } y \text { axis }\left( mm ^{4}\right)=}&&&& 12,133,386.23\end{array}\)

 

Product of inertia about the centroidal axes:

\(\begin{array}{lcccccc} {\text { Shape }} & \begin{array}{c} I_{y^{\prime} z^{\prime}} \\ \left(\mathrm{mm}^{4}\right) \end{array} & \begin{array}{c} y_{c} \\ (\mathrm{~mm}) \end{array} & \begin{array}{c} z_{c} \\ (\mathrm{~mm}) \end{array} & \begin{array}{c} \text { Area } A_{i} \\ \left(\mathrm{~mm}^{2}\right) \end{array} & \begin{array}{c} y_{c} z_{c} A_{i} \\ \left(\mathrm{~mm}^{4}\right) \end{array} & \begin{array}{c} I_{y z} \\ \left(\mathrm{~mm}^{4}\right) \end{array} \\ \hline \text { horizontal leg } & 0 & 54.68 & 35.82 & 2,850 & 5,582,117.16 & 5,582,117.16 \\ \text { vertical leg } & 0 & -45.32 & -29.68 & 3,439 & 4,625,790.65 & 4,625,790.65 \\ \hline & & & & {\text { Product of inertia }\left(\mathrm{mm}^{4}\right)=10,207,907.81 \mid} \end{array}\) horizontal leg \(\begin{aligned}& 0 & 54.68 & 35.82 & 2,850 & 5,582,117.16 & 5,582,117.16\end{aligned}\) vertical leg \(\begin{aligned}& 0 & -45.32 & -29.68 & 3,439 & 4,625,790.65 & 4,625,790.65\end{aligned}\) Product of inertia \(\left( mm ^{4}\right)\) \(\begin{aligned}=10,207,907.81\end{aligned}\)

 

Since the angle shape has no axis of symmetry, Eq. (8.21) or Eq. (8.22) must be used to determine the bending stresses. Equation (8.21) will be used here.

 

\(\sigma_{x}=-\frac{\left(M_{z} I_{y}+M_{y} I_{y z}\right) y}{I_{y} I_{z}-I_{y z}^{2}}+\frac{\left(M_{y} I_{z}+M_{z} I_{y z}\right) z}{I_{y} I_{z}-I_{y z}^{2}}\)

 

\(\begin{array}{r}=-\left[\frac{\left(14 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)\left(12,133,386.23 mm ^{4}\right)}{\left(12,133,386.23 mm ^{4}\right)\left(25,059,086.23 mm ^{4}\right)-\left(10,207,907.81 mm ^{4}\right)^{2}}\right] y \\+\left[\frac{\left(14 \times 10^{6} N – mm \right)\left(10,207,907.81 mm ^{4}\right)}{\left(12,133,386.23 mm ^{4}\right)\left(25,059,086.23 mm ^{4}\right)-\left(10,207,907.81 mm ^{4}\right)^{2}}\right] z\end{array}\)

 

\(=\left(-0.84997 N / mm ^{3}\right) y+\left(0.71509 N / mm ^{3}\right) z\)

 

To compute the normal stress at H, use (y, z) coordinates of y = 45.18 mm and z = 110.82 mm:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\left(-0.84997 N / mm ^{3}\right)(45.18 mm )+\left(0.71509 N / mm ^{3}\right)(110.82 mm ) \\&=40.8444 MPa =40.8 MPa ( T )\end{aligned}\)

 

(b) Bending stress at K
To compute the normal stress at K, use (y, z) coordinates of y = 64.18 mm and z = −39.18 mm:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\left(-0.84997 N / mm ^{3}\right)(64.18 mm )+\left(0.71509 N / mm ^{3}\right)(-39.18 mm ) \\&=-82.5685 MPa =82.6 MPa ( C )\end{aligned}\)

 

(d) Orientation of neutral axis
Since the angle shape has no axis of symmetry, it is helpful to determine the orientation of the neutral axis from Eq. (8.23) to help identify points of maximum stress.

 

\(\begin{aligned}\tan \beta &=\frac{M_{y} I_{z}+M_{z} I_{y z}}{M_{z} I_{y}+M_{y} I_{y z}} \\&=\frac{(14 kN – m )\left(10,207,907.81 mm ^{4}\right)}{(14 kN – m )\left(12,133,386.23 mm ^{4}\right)} \\&=0.84131 \\&\left.\therefore \beta=40.07^{\circ} \quad \text { (i.e., } 40.07^{\circ} CW \text { from }+z \text { axis }\right)\end{aligned}\)

(c) Maximum tension and compression bending stresses
Sketch the orientation of the neutral axis. By inspection, the points on the angle cross section that are farthest from the neutral axis are on the top corner (at K) and on the inside corner of the vertical leg.

To compute bending stresses at the lower point, use (y, z) coordinates of y = −135.82 mm and z = −20.18 mm:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\left(-0.84997 N / mm ^{3}\right)(-135.82 mm )+\left(0.71509 N / mm ^{3}\right)(-20.18 mm ) \\&=101.0129 MPa =101.0 MPa ( T ) \quad \end{aligned}\) Maximum tension bending stress

The maximum compression bending stress is

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{x} &=\left(-0.84997 N / mm ^{3}\right)(64.18 mm )+\left(0.71509 N / mm ^{3}\right)(-39.18 mm ) \\&=-82.5685 MPa =82.6 MPa ( C ) \quad \end{aligned}\) Maximum compression bending stress
