Guest Question.

A trench for installing a stormwater pipe is proposed in a saturated clay site having \(c_{u}\) of \(25 \mathrm{kPa}\) and unit weight of \(17 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\). The unit weight of the bentonite slurry employed to assist in supporting the excavation cutting has \(11.5 \mathrm{kN} / \mathrm{m}^{3}\) (Fig. 12.8).

a. Derive the relationship for the maximum height of the trench based on force equilibrium considering undrained conditions (e.g. short-term analysis).

b. Calculate the maximum height of the trench if the trench is filled with slurry to depth of \(80 \%\) of the total height of the excavation (i.e. \(x=0.8\) ).

Admin
Solution

a. Considering force equilibrium for a soil wedge shown in Figure 12.8:

\(\begin{aligned}&P+S \cos \beta-N \sin \beta=0 \\ &W-S \sin \beta-N \cos \beta=0\end{aligned}\)

where

\(\begin{aligned}W &=\frac{\gamma H^{2}}{2 \tan \beta} \\S &=\frac{c_{u} H}{\sin \beta} \\N &=S \frac{\cos \beta}{\sin \beta} \text { (force equilibrium in horizontal direction, empty trench) }\end{aligned}\)

\(P=\int_{0}^{0.9 H} \gamma_{s} z d z=\frac{\gamma_{s}(x H)^{2}}{2}\) (hydrostatic pressure distribution in the trench derived from the slurry)

As \(P+S \cos \beta-N \sin \beta=0\), then \(N=\frac{P+S \cos \beta}{\sin \beta}\)

Substituting \(W, S\) and \(N\) into Eq. 12.1b,

\(W-S \sin \beta-N \cos \beta=0\) (12.1b)

\(\frac{\gamma H^{2}}{2 \tan \beta}-c_{u} \frac{H}{\sin \beta} \sin \beta-\frac{P+S \cos \beta}{\sin \beta} \cos \beta=0 \text { or }\)

 

\(\frac{\gamma H^{2}}{2 \tan \beta}-c_{u} H-\left(\frac{\gamma_{s}(x H)^{2}}{2}+\frac{c_{u} H \cos \beta}{\sin \beta}\right) \frac{\cos \beta}{\sin \beta}=0\)

 

\(\frac{\gamma H^{2}}{2 \tan \beta}-c_{u} \mathrm{H}-\frac{\gamma_{s}(x H)^{2}}{2 \tan \beta}-c_{u} \frac{H}{\tan ^{2} \beta}=0\)

Multiplying all terms by \(\frac{\tan \beta}{H}\), then

\(\begin{aligned}&\frac{\gamma H}{2}-c_{u} \tan \beta-\frac{\gamma_{s} x^{2} H}{2}-\frac{c_{u}}{\tan \beta}=0 \\ &H \frac{\left(\gamma-\gamma_{s} x^{2}\right)}{2}=\frac{c_{u}}{\tan \beta}+C_{u} \tan \beta \\ &H=\frac{2 c_{u}}{\sin \beta \cos \beta}\left[\frac{1}{\left(\gamma-\gamma_{s} x^{2}\right)}\right]\end{aligned}\)

Or

\(H=\frac{2 c_{u}}{\gamma \sin \beta \cos \beta}\left[\frac{1}{1-\left(\frac{\gamma_{s}}{\gamma}\right) x^{2}}\right]\)

Note that: \(\left(\frac{1}{\tan \beta}+\tan \beta\right)=\left(\sin ^{2} \beta+\cos ^{2} \beta\right) \frac{1}{\sin \beta \cos \beta}=\frac{1}{\sin \beta \cos \beta}\)

b. Substituting the values for the problem and assuming near-failure conditions for which \(\beta\) approaches \(45^{\circ}\), then

\(H=\frac{2 c_{u}}{\gamma \sin \beta \cos \beta}\left[\frac{1}{1-\left(\frac{\gamma_{s}}{\gamma}\right) x^{2}}\right] \text { or }\)

 

\(H=\frac{2 \times 25}{17 \sin 45^{\circ} \cos 45^{\circ}}\left[\frac{1}{1-\left(\frac{11.5}{17}\right)(0.8)^{2}}\right]=5.88 \times 1.76=10.35 \mathrm{~m}\)

The maximum depth of the trench is \(10.35 \mathrm{~m}\).

The consideration of slurry support enables the excavation depth to be increased by approximately \(76 \%\) without compromising stability.
