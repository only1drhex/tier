Guest Question.

Solid of Revolution Moment of Inertia About Axis of Revolution

Determine the mass moment of inertia about the x axis for the solid hemisphere of radius r and uniform density \(\rho\). Express the result in terms of the mass m of the object.

Admin
Solution

Road Map We will show two solutions, the first using a thin shell volume element and the second using a thin disk volume element. Note that the centroid of this object was determined in on p. 408, and the expressions for dV developed in this solution are identical to those used earlier.

Solution 1—thin shell volume element

Governing Equations & Computation We begin with Eq. (\(I_x = \int{\widetilde{r}^2_x dm } = \int{(\widetilde{y}^2 + \widetilde{z}^2) dm} ) with \)dm = \rho dV.

\(I_x = \int{\widetilde{r}^2_x \rho dV}\). (1)

The thin shell volume element shown in Fig. 2 is used to obtain expressions for dV and \(\widetilde{r}_x\). The merit of this volume element is that all the material in the element is at the same radial distance \(\widetilde{r}_x\) from the x axis. With y being the radius of the shell,

\(dV = 2\pi yx dy\) and \(\widetilde{r}_x = y\). (2)

In Eq. (2), \(2\pi y\) is the circumference of the shell, y is the radius, x is the length of the shell, and dy is the thickness. Substituting Eq. (2) into Eq. (1), with \(x = \sqrt{r^2 - y^2}\) , the moment of inertia about the x axis is

\(I_x = \int_{0}^{r}{y^2 \rho 2\pi y \sqrt{r^2 - y^2} dy} = \frac{2\pi \rho}{15}(y^2 - r^2)^{3/2}(2r^2 + 3y^2) |^r_0 = \frac{4\pi \rho r^5}{15}\). (3)

Using the expression for dV in Eq. (2), the mass m of a hemisphere is

\(m = \int{\rho dV} = \int_{0}^{r}{ \rho 2\pi y \sqrt{r^2 - y^2} dy} = \frac{2\pi rho}{3}(y^2 - r^2)^{3/2}|^r_0 = \frac{2\pi rho r^3}{3}\). (4)

Finally, we express Eq. (3) in terms of mass m as follows:

\(I_x = \frac{4\pi \rho r^5}{15} (\frac{m}{2\pi \rho r^3/3}) = \frac{2mr^2}{5}\). (5)

Solution 2—thin disk volume element

Governing Equations & Computation We begin with Eq. (\(I_y = \int{dI_y}\)) on p. 556

\(I_x = \int{dI_x}\). (6)

where \(dI_x\) is the moment of inertia of the thin circular disk mass element shown in Fig. 3 about the x axis. From the Table of Properties of Solids, or from Fig. 10.16(c),

\(dI_x =\frac{1}{2}(m_{disk})(r_{disk})^2 = \frac{1}{2}(\rho \pi y^2 dx)(y)^2\) (7)

where y is the radius of the disk. Substituting Eq. (7) into Eq. (6), with \(y = \sqrt{r^2 - x^2}\) , the moment of inertia about the x axis is

\(I_x = \int_{0}^{r}{\frac{1}{2} \rho \pi (r^2 - x^2)^2 dx} = \pi \rho (\frac{r^4 x}{2} - \frac{r^2 x^3}{3} + \frac{x^5}{10})|^r_0 = \frac{4\pi \rho r^5}{15}\), (8)

which is identical to Eq. (3). Hence, we obtain the same result, \(I_x = 2mr^2/5\).

Discussion & Verification Knowing the mass moments of inertia for a cylinder and cone, it is possible to show that the result obtained here is reasonable; see Prob. 10.59.
