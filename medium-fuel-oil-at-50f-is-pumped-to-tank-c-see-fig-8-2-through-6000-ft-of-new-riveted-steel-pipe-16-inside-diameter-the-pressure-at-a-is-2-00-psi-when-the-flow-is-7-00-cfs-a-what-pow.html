Guest Question.

Medium fuel oil at 50째F is pumped to tank C (see Fig. 8-2) through 6000 ft of new, riveted steel pipe, 16″ inside diameter. The pressure at A is +2.00 psi when the flow is 7.00 cfs. (a) What power must pump AB supply to the oil, and (b) what pressure must be maintained at Bl Draw the hydraulic grade line.

Admin
Solution

\(V_{16}=\frac{Q}{A}=\frac{7.00}{\pi(16 / 12)^{2} / 4}=5.01 ft / sec \quad \text { and } \quad \operatorname{Re}=\frac{5.01 \times(16 / 12)}{5.55} \times 10^{5}=120,000\).

\(\text { From Diagram A-1, } f=0.030 \text { for } \epsilon / d=0.0060 /(16 / 12)=0.0045 .\)

(a) The Bernoulli equation, A to C, datum A, gives

\(\left(\frac{2.00 \times 144}{0.861 \times 62.4}+\frac{(5.01)^{2}}{2 g}+0\right)+H_{p}-0.030\left(\frac{6000}{16} \times 12\right)\left(\frac{(5.01)^{2}}{2 g}\right)-\frac{(5.01)^{2}}{2 g}=0+0+80\).

\(\text { Solving, } H_{p}=127.3 ft \text { and } hp =\frac{\gamma Q H_{p}}{550}=\frac{0.861 \times 62.4 \times 7.00 \times 127.3}{550}=87.0 \).

The last term on the left-hand side of the energy equation is the lost head from pipe to tank (see in Appendix). In general, when the length-to-diameter ratio (L/d) is in excess of 2000 : 1, velocity heads and minor losses can be neglected in the Bernoulli equation (here they cancel).

(b) The pressure head at B can be calculated by using sections A and B or sections B and C. The former is less work; then

\(\left(5.4+\frac{V_{16}^{2}}{2 g}+0\right)+127.3=\frac{p_{B}}{\gamma}+\frac{V_{16}^{2}}{2 g}+0\).

\(\text { Thus } p_{B} / \gamma=132.7 ft \text { and } p_{B}=(0.861 \times 62.4)(132.7) / 144=49.5 psi \).

The hydraulic grade line elevations are shown in Fig. 8-2.
At A: elevation = (100.0 + 5.4) ft = 105.4 ft.
At B: elevation = (100.0 + 132.7) ft (or 105.4 + 127.3) = 232.7 ft.
At C: elevation = 180.0ft.
