Guest Question.

Let \(S=\left\{ v _{1}, v _{2}, v _{3}\right\}\) be the subset of \(R^{3}\), where

\(v _{1}=\left[\begin{array}{l}1 \\1 \\1\end{array}\right], \quad v _{2}=\left[\begin{array}{l}2 \\3 \\1\end{array}\right] \text { and } \quad{v}_{3}=\left[\begin{array}{c}3 \\5 \\1\end{array}\right].\)

Show that S is a linearly dependent set, and exhibit a subset T of S such that T contains only two vectors but Sp(T) = Sp(S).

Admin
Solution

The vector equation

\(x_{1} v _{1}+x_{2} v _{2}+x_{3} v _{3}= \theta\) (10)

is equivalent to the (3 × 3) homogeneous system of equations with augmented matrix

\(A=\left[\begin{array}{llll}1 & 2 & 3 & 0 \\1 & 3 & 5 & 0 \\1 & 1 & 1 & 0\end{array}\right]\).

Matrix A is row equivalent to

\(B=\left[\begin{array}{rrrr}1 & 0 & -1 & 0 \\0 & 1 & 2 & 0 \\0 & 0 & 0 & 0\end{array}\right]\)

in echelon form. Solving the system with augmented matrix B gives

\(x_{1}=x_{3}\)
\(x_{2}=-2 x_{3}\).

Because Eq.(10) has nontrivial solutions, the set S is linearly dependent. Taking \(x_{3}=1\), for example, gives \(x_{1}=1, x_{2}=-2\). Therefore,

\(v _{1}-2 v _{2}+ v _{3}= \theta\). (11)

Equation (11) allows us to express \(V _{3}\) as a linear combination of \(v _{1} \text { and } v _{2} \text { : }\)

\(v _{3}=- v _{1}+2 v _{2}\).

(Note that we could just as easily have solved Eq.(11) for either \(v _{1} \text { or } v _{2} \).) It now follows that

\(\operatorname{Sp}\left\{ v _{1}, v _{2}\right\}=\operatorname{Sp}\left\{ v _{1}, v _{2}, v _{3}\right\}\).

To illustrate, let v be in the subspace \(\operatorname{Sp}\left\{ v _{1}, v _{2}, v _{3}\right\}\):

\(v =a_{1} v _{1}+a_{2} v _{2}+a_{3} v _{3}\).

Making the substitution \(v _{3}=- v _{1}+2 v _{2}\), yields

\(v =a_{1} v _{1}+a_{2} v _{2}+a_{3}\left(- v _{1}+2 v _{2}\right)\).

This expression simplifies to

\(v =\left(a_{1}-a_{3}\right) v _{1}+\left(a_{2}+2 a_{3}\right) v _{2}\);

in particular, v is in \(\operatorname{Sp}\left\{ v _{1}, v _{2}\right\}\). Clearly any linear combination of \(v _{1} \text { and } v _{2}\) is in Sp(S) because

\(b_{1} v _{1}+b_{2} v _{2}=b_{1} v _{1}+b_{2} v _{2}+0 v _{3}\).

Thus if \(T=\left\{ v _{1}, v _{2}\right\}\), then Sp(T) = Sp(S).
