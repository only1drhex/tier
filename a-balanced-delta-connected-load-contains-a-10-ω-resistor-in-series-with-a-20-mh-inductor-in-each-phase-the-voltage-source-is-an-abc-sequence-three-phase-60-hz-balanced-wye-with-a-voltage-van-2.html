Guest Question.

A balanced delta-connected load contains a 10-Ω resistor in series with a 20-mH inductor in each phase. The voltage source is an abc-sequence three-phase 60-Hz, balanced wye with a voltage \(V_{an} = 120 \underline{/30^\circ}\)V rms. We wish to determine all Δ currents and line currents.

Admin
Solution

The impedance per phase in the delta load is \(\mathbf{Z}_{\Delta}=10+j 7.54 \Omega\). The line voltage \(\mathbf{V}_{a b}=120 \sqrt{3} \underline{/ 60^{\circ}} \mathrm{V} \mathrm{rms}\). Since there is no line impedance, \(\mathbf{V}_{A B}=\mathbf{V}_{a b}=120 \sqrt{3} \underline{/ 60^{\circ}} \mathrm{V} \mathrm{rms}\). Hence,

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{I}_{A B} &=\frac{120 \sqrt{3} \underline{/ 60^{\circ}}}{10+j 7.54} \\\\&=16.60 \underline{/ 22.98^{\circ}} \mathrm{A} \mathrm{rms}\end{aligned}\)

 

If \(\mathbf{Z}_{\Delta}=10+j 7.54 \Omega\), then

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{Z}_{\mathrm{Y}} &=\frac{1}{3} \mathbf{Z}_{\Delta} \\\\&=3.33+j 2.51 \Omega\end{aligned}\)

 

Then the line current

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{I}_{a A} &=\frac{\mathbf{V}_{a n}}{\mathbf{Z}_{\mathrm{Y}}}=\frac{120 \underline{/ 30^{\circ}}}{3.33+j 2.51} \\\\&=\frac{120 \underline{/ 30^{\circ}}}{4.17 \underline{/ 37.01^{\circ}}} \\\\&=28.78\underline{/-7.01^{\circ}} \mathrm{A} \mathrm{rms}\end{aligned}\)

 

Therefore, the remaining phase and line currents are

 

\(\begin{aligned}&\mathbf{I}_{B C}=16.60 \underline{/-97.02^{\circ}} \mathrm{Arms} \qquad \mathbf{I}_{b B}=28.78 \underline{/-127.01^{\circ}} \mathrm{Arms} \\\\&\mathbf{I}_{C A}=16.60 \underline{/+142.98^{\circ}} \mathrm{A} \mathrm{rms}\qquad \mathbf{I}_{c C}=28.78 \underline{/+112.99^{\circ}} \mathrm{A} \mathrm{rms}\end{aligned}\)
