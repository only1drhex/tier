Guest Question.

The per phase circuit equivalent of a synchronous generator feeding a synchronous motor through a transmission line is drawn in Fig. 8.67.

Each machine is rated 10 kVA, 400 V, 50 Hz. The motor is driving a load of 8 kW. The fi eld currents of the two machines are so adjusted that the motor terminal voltage is 400 V and its pf is 0.8 leading.

(a) Determine the magnitude of \( E_{g}\) and \( E_{m}\) and their relative angle.

(b) Minimum value of \( E_{m}\) for the machine to remain in synchronism.

Admin
Solution
\( V_{\text {Base }}=400 V ,( kVA )_{\text {Base }}=10,( MW )_{\text {Base }}=10\)

Motor load, \( P_{m}=\frac{8}{10}=0.8 pu\)

 

\( V_{t}=1 \angle 0^{\circ} pu\)

As there is no loss

\( P_{e} \text { (in) }=P_{m}=0.8 pu\)

 

\( P_{e}(\text { in })=V_{t} I_{a} \cos \theta ; pf =\cos \theta=0.8\) leading

\( 0.8=1 \times I_{a} \times 0.8\)

 

\( I_{a}=1 pu \quad \bar{I}_{a}=1 \angle 36.9^{\circ}\)

Motor

\( \bar{E}_{m}=1 \angle 0-j 1 \angle 36.9^{\circ} \times 0.8=1+0.8 \angle-53.1^{\circ}\)

 

\( =1.48-j 0.64=1.612 \angle-23.4^{\circ}\)

 

\( E_{m}=1.612 pu\) or \( 644.8 V\) (line)

\( \delta_{m}=-23.4^{\circ}\)

Generator

\( E_{g}=1 \angle 0^{\circ}+j 1 \angle 36.9^{\circ} \times(0.8+0.2)\)

 

\( =1+1 \angle 126.9^{\circ}=0.4+j 0.8\)

 

\( =0.894 \angle 63.4^{\circ}\)

 

\( E_{g}=0.894 pu\) or 357.6 V (line), \( \delta_{g}=63.4^{\circ}\)

Relative angle, \( \delta_{g m}=63.4^{\circ}+23.4^{\circ}=86.8^{\circ}\)

(a) Solution by phasor diagram

The phasor diagram is drawn in Fig. 8.68. From the geometry of the diagram

\( A E=V_{t} \cos \theta=0.8\)

 

\( B E=V_{t} \sin \theta=0.6\)

 

\( B C=1 \times 0.8=0.8\)

 

\( B D=1 \times(0.8+0.2)=1\)

 

CE = BC + BE = 0.8 + 0.6 = 1.4

 

\( E_{m}=\sqrt{(A E)^{2}+(C E)^{2}}\)

 

\( =\sqrt{(0.8)^{2}+(1.4)^{2}}=1.612 pu\)

 

\( \tan \left(\theta+\delta_{m}\right)=\frac{C E}{A E}=\frac{1.4}{0.8}=1.75\)

 

\( \theta+\delta_{m}=60^{\circ}, \delta_{m}=60^{\circ}-36.9^{\circ}=23.4^{\circ}\)

 

DE = BD – BE = 1 – 0.6 = 0.4

 

\( E_{g}=\sqrt{(A E)^{2}+(D E)^{2}}\)

 

\( =\sqrt{(0.8)^{2}+(0.4)^{2}}=0.894 pu\)

 

\( \tan \left(\delta_{g}-\theta\right)=\frac{D E}{A E}=\frac{0.4}{0.8}=0.5\)

 

\( \delta_{g}-\theta=26.6^{\circ} \quad \delta_{g}=63.5^{\circ}\)

 

(b) \( P_{e}=\frac{V_{t} E_{m}}{0.8} \sin \delta_{m}\)

For maximum \( E_{m}\) \( \delta_{m}=90^{\circ}\) , limit of stability

\( E_{m}(\min )=\frac{0.8 \times 0.8}{1}=0.64 pu\) or 256 V
