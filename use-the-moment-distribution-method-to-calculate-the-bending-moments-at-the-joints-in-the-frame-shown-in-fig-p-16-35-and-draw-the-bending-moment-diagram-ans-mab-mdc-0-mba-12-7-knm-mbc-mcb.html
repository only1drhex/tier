Guest Question.

Use the moment distribution method to calculate the bending moments at the joints in the frame shown in Fig. P.16.35 and draw the bending moment diagram.
Ans. \(M_{ AB }=M_{ DC }=0, M_{ BA }=12.7 kNm =-M_{ BC }, M_{ CB }=-13.9 kNm =-M_{ CD }\).

Admin
Solution

In this problem the FEMs are caused by sway only. The distribution factors are

\(DF _{ BA }=\frac{\left(\frac{3 \times 2 E I}{5}\right)}{\left[\left(\frac{3 \times 2 E I}{5}\right)+\left(\frac{4 \times 3 E I}{5}\right)\right]}=\frac{1}{3}\)

 

\(DF _{ BC }=\frac{2}{3}\)

 

\(DF _{ CB }=\frac{\left(\frac{4 \times 3 E I}{5}\right)}{\left[\left(\frac{4 \times 3 E I}{5}\right)+\left(\frac{3 \times 2 E I}{5}\right)\right]}=\frac{2}{3}\)

 

\(DF _{ CD }=\frac{1}{3}\)

Suppose that the frame is given an arbitrary sway as shown in Fig. S.16.35(a). The corresponding sway FEMs are

\(M_{ CD }^{ FS }=\frac{-3 \times 2 E I \times 5 \theta}{4 \times 5}=\frac{-3 \theta}{2}\)

 

\(M_{ BC }^{ FS }=M_{ CB }^{ FS }=\frac{6 \times 3 E I \times 3 \theta}{4 \times 5}=\frac{27 \theta}{10}\)

 

\(M_{ BA }^{ FS }=\frac{-3 \times 2 E I \times \theta}{5}=\frac{-6 \theta}{5}\)

Suppose that θ = 100 then the above moments become -150, 270 and -120, respectively. The moment distribution is carried out in Table S.16.35.

Using the principle of virtual work the sway equilibrium equation is

\(M_{ BA }(\theta)+M_{ BC }\left(\frac{-3 \theta}{4}\right)+M_{ CB }\left(\frac{-3 \theta}{4}\right)+M_{ CD }\left(\frac{5 \theta}{4}\right)+10 \times 5 \theta=0\)

Substituting from the above table

k[7 × (-161) + 8 × (-176)] = -200

so that k = 0.0789

The final moments are then

\(M_{ AB }=M_{ DC }=0, \quad M_{ BA }=-M_{ BC }=12.7 kNm , \quad M _{ CB }=-M_{ CD }=13.9 kNm\) .

The bending moment diagram is shown in Fig. S.16.35(b).
