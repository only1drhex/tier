Guest Question.

Consider a spinless particle of mass m, which is moving in a one-dimensional infinite potential well with walls at x = 0 and x = a.

(a) Find \(\hat{X}_{H}(t)\) and \(\hat{P}_{H}(t)\) in the Heisenberg picture.

(b) If at t = 0 the particle is in the state \(\psi(x, 0)=\left[\phi_{1}(x)+\phi_{2}(x)\right] / \sqrt{2}\), where \(\phi_{1}(x)\) and \(\phi_{2}(x)\) are the ground and first excited states, respectively, with \(\phi_{n}(x)=\sqrt{2 / a} \sin (n \pi x / a)\), find the state vector \(\psi(x, t)\) for t > 0 in the Schrödinger picture.

(c) Evaluate \(\langle\psi(x, t)|\hat{X}| \psi(x, t)\rangle\) and \(\langle\psi(x, t)|\hat{P}| \psi(x, t)\rangle\) as a function of time in the Schrödinger picture.

(d) Evaluate \(\left\langle\psi(x, t)\left|\hat{X}_{H}(t)\right| \psi(x, t)\right\rangle\) and \(\left\langle\psi(x, t)\left|\hat{P}_{H}(t)\right| \psi(x, t)\right\rangle\) as a function of time in the Schrödinger picture.

Admin
Solution

(a) Since the particle’s Hamiltonian is purely kinetic, \(\hat{H}=\hat{P}^{2} / 2 m\), we have \([\hat{H}, \hat{P}]=0\) and

\([\hat{H}, \hat{X}]=\frac{1}{2 m}\left[\hat{P}^{2}, \hat{X}\right]=-\frac{i \hbar}{m} \hat{P}\). (10.182)

Using these relations along with (10.155),

\(e^{\hat{A}} \hat{B} e^{-\hat{A}}=\hat{B}+[\hat{A}, \hat{B}]+\frac{1}{2 !}[\hat{A},[\hat{A}, \hat{B}]]+\frac{1}{3 !}[\hat{A},[\hat{A},[\hat{A}, \hat{B}]]]+\cdots\), (10.155)

we obtain

\(\hat{X}_{H}(t)=e^{i t \hat{H} / \hbar} \hat{X} e^{-i t \hat{H} / \hbar}=\hat{X}+\frac{i t}{\hbar}[\hat{H}, \hat{X}]+\frac{1}{2 !}\left(\frac{i t}{\hbar}\right)^{2}[\hat{H},[\hat{H}, \hat{X}]]+\cdots,\) (10.183)

and since \([\hat{H},[\hat{H}, \hat{X}]]=-(i \hbar / m)[\hat{H}, \hat{P}]=0\), we end up with

\(\hat{X}_{H}(t)=\hat{X}+\frac{t}{m} \hat{P}\). (10.184)

On the other hand, since \([\hat{H}, \hat{P}]=0\), we have

\(\hat{P}=\hat{P}_{H}(t)\). (10.185)

(b) Since the energy of the nth level is given by \(E_{n}=n^{2} \pi^{2} \hbar^{2} /\left(2 m a^{2}\right)\), we have

\(\psi(x, t)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left[\phi_{1}(x) e^{-i E_{1} t / \hbar}+\phi_{2}(x) e^{-i E_{2} t / \hbar}\right]\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{\sqrt{a}}\left[e^{-i E_{1} t / \hbar} \sin \left(\frac{\pi x}{a}\right)+e^{-i E_{2} t / \hbar} \sin \left(\frac{2 \pi x}{a}\right)\right]\). (10.186)

(c) Using (10.186) we can write

\(\langle\psi(x, t)|\hat{X}| \psi(x, t)\rangle=\frac{1}{2}\left[\left\langle\phi_{1}|\hat{X}|\phi_{1}\right\rangle+\left\langle\phi_{2}|\hat{X}|\phi_{2}\right\rangle+\left\langle\phi_{1}|\hat{X}| \phi_{2}\right\rangle e^{-i\left(E_{2}-E_{1}\right) t / \hbar}\right.\)

 

\(\left.+\left\langle\phi_{2}|\hat{X}| \phi_{1}\right\rangle e^{i\left(E_{2}-E_{1}\right) t / \hbar}\right]\). (10.187)

Since \(\left\langle\phi_{n}|\hat{X}| \phi_{n}\right\rangle=a / 2\) (Chapter 4) and

\(\left\langle\phi_{1}|\hat{X}|\phi_{2}\right\rangle=\left\langle\phi_{2}|\hat{X}| \phi_{1}\right\rangle=\frac{2}{a} \int_{0}^{a} x \sin \left(\frac{\pi x}{a}\right) \sin \left(\frac{2 \pi x}{a}\right) d x=-\frac{16 a}{9 \pi^{2}}\), (10.188)

we can rewrite (10.187) as

\(\langle\psi(x, t)|\hat{X}| \psi(x, t)\rangle=\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{a}{2}+\frac{a}{2}-\frac{16 a}{9 \pi^{2}}\left(e^{-i\left(E_{2}-E_{1}\right) t / \hbar}+e^{i\left(E_{2}-E_{1}\right) t / \hbar}\right)\right]\)

 

\(=\frac{a}{2}-\frac{16 a}{9 \pi^{2}} \cos \left(\frac{3 \pi^{2} \hbar t}{2 m a^{2}}\right)\), (10.189)

since \(E_{2}-E_{1}=3 \pi^{2} \hbar^{2} /\left(2 m a^{2}\right)\).

A similar calculation which uses \(\left\langle\phi_{n}|\hat{P}| \phi_{n}\right\rangle=0\) and

\(\left\langle\phi_{1}|\hat{P}| \phi_{2}\right\rangle=-i \hbar \frac{4 \pi}{a} \int_{0}^{a} \sin \left(\frac{\pi x}{a}\right) \cos \left(\frac{2 \pi x}{a}\right) d x=\frac{8 i \hbar}{3 a}=-\left\langle\phi_{2}|\hat{P}| \phi_{1}\right\rangle\) (10.190)

leads to

\(\langle\psi(x, t)|\hat{P}| \psi(x, t)\rangle=\frac{1}{2}\left[\left\langle\phi_{1}|\hat{P}|\phi_{1}\right\rangle+\left\langle\phi_{2}|\hat{P}|\phi_{2}\right\rangle+\left\langle\phi_{1}|\hat{P}| \phi_{2}\right\rangle e^{-i\left(E_{2}-E_{1}\right) t / \hbar}\right.\)

 

\(\left.+\left\langle\phi_{2}|\hat{P}| \phi_{1}\right\rangle e^{i\left(E_{2}-E_{1}\right) t / \hbar}\right]\), (10.191)

or to

\(\langle\psi(x, t)|\hat{P}| \psi(x, t)\rangle=\frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{8 i \hbar}{3 a} e^{-i\left(E_{2}-E_{1}\right) t / \hbar}-\frac{8 i \hbar}{3 a} e^{i\left(E_{2}-E_{1}\right) t / \hbar}\right]=\frac{8 \hbar}{3 a} \sin \left(\frac{3 \pi^{2} \hbar t}{2 m a^{2}}\right)\). (10.192)

(d) From (10.184) we have

\(\left\langle\psi(x, t)\left|\hat{X}_{H}(t)\right| \psi(x, t)\right\rangle=\langle\psi(x, t)|\hat{X}| \psi(x, t)\rangle+\frac{t}{m}\langle\psi(x, t)|\hat{P}| \psi(x, t)\rangle .\) (10.193)

Inserting the expressions for \(\langle\psi(x, t)|\hat{X}| \psi(x, t)\rangle\) and \(\langle\psi(x, t)|\hat{P}| \psi(x, t)\rangle\) calculated in (10.189) and (10.192), we obtain

\(\left\langle\psi(x, t)\left|\hat{X}_{H}(t)\right| \psi(x, t)\right\rangle=\frac{a}{2}-\frac{16 a}{9 \pi^{2}} \cos \left(\frac{3 \pi^{2} \hbar t}{2 m a^{2}}\right)+\frac{8 \hbar t}{3 m a} \sin \left(\frac{3 \pi^{2} \hbar t}{2 m a^{2}}\right)\), (10.194)

and \(\left\langle\psi(x, t)\left|\hat{P}_{H}(t)\right| \psi(x, t)\right\rangle\) is given by (10.192):

\(\left\langle\psi(x, t)\left|\hat{P}_{H}(t)\right| \psi(x, t)\right\rangle=\langle\psi(x, t)|\hat{P}| \psi(x, t)\rangle=\frac{8 \hbar}{3 a} \sin \left(\frac{3 \pi^{2} \hbar t}{2 m a^{2}}\right)\), (10.195)

since, as shown in (10.185), we have \(\hat{P}_{H}(t)=\hat{P}\).
