Guest Question.

The tube bank of a steam condenser consists of a square array of 400 tubes, each of diameter \(D = 2r_{1} = 6 mm.\)

1. If horizontal, unfinned tubes are exposed to saturated steam at a pressure of p = 0.15 bar and the tube surface is maintained at \(T_{s} = 25°C\), what is the rate at which steam is con-densed per unit length of the tube bank?

2. If annular fins of height \(h = r_{2} = r_{1} = 1 mm\), thickness t = 1 mm, and pitch S = 2 mm are added, determine the minimum condensation rate per unit length of tubing.

Admin
Solution

Known: Configuration and surface temperature of unfinned and finned condenser tubes exposed to saturated steam at 0.15 bar.

Find:
1. Condensation rate per unit length of unfinned tubing.
2. Minimum condensation rate per unit length of finned tubing.

Assumptions:
1. Spatially uniform cylinder and fin temperature.
2. Average heat transfer coefficient varies with tube row with n =-1/6 in Equation 10.49 \(\overset{-}{h}_{D,N}= \overset{-}{h}_{D} N^{n} \).
3. Negligible concentration of noncondensable gases in the steam.

Properties: , saturated vapor \((p = 0.15 bar): T_{sat} = 327 K = 54°C, ρ_{v} = 1/v_{g} = 0.098 kg/m^{3}, h_{fg} = 2373 kJ/kg, \sigma = 0.0671 N/m\). , saturated water \((T_{f} = 312.5 K): ρ_{l} = 1/v_{f} = 992 kg/m^{3}, μ_{l} = 663 × 10^{-6} N .s/m^{2}, k_{l} = 0.631 W/m. K,c_{p,l} = 4178 J/kg . K.\)

Analysis:
1. Equation 10.46 may be rearranged to yield an expression for the convection coefficient for the top, unfinned tube which is of the form

\( \overline{Nu}_D = \frac{\overline{h}_D D}{k_l} = C \left [ \frac{\rho _lg(\rho _l – \rho _v)h^{\prime}_{fg}D^3}{\mu _l k_l (T_{sat}-T_s)}\right]^{1/4} \) (10.46)

\(\overset{-}{h}_{D} = C \left[\frac{\rho _{l} g (\rho _{l} – \rho _{\upsilon })k^{3}_{l} h^{\prime}_{fg} }{\mu _{l} (T_{sat} – T_{s}) D} \right]^{1/4} \)

where C = 0.729 for a tube and

\( h^{\prime}_{fg} = h_{fg} (1 + 0.68 Ja) = h_{fg} + 0.68c_{p,l}(T_{sat} – T_{s})\)

 

\( = 2373 × 10^{3}J/kg + 0.68 × 4178 J/kg . K × (327 – 298) K\)

 

= 2455 kJ/kg

Therefore

\(\overset{-}{h}_{D} = 0.729 \left[\frac{992 kg/m^{3} × 9.81 m/s^{2} × (992 -0.098) kg/m^{3}× (0.631 W/m . K)^{3} × 2455 ×10^{3}J/kg }{633 ×10^{-6} kg/s . m × (327 – 298) K × 6 ×10^{-3}m} \right]^{1/4} \)

 

\(= 11,120 W/m^{2} . K\)

From Equation 10.49 the array-averaged convection coefficient is

\(\overset{-}{h}_{D,N}= \overset{-}{h}_{D} N^{n} = 11,120 W/m^{2} . K × 20^{-1/6} = 6747 W/m^{2} . K\)

From Equation 10.34 the condensation rate per unit length of tubing is

\(\dot{m}^{\prime }_{uft} = N \times N \frac{\bar{h}_{D,N}(\pi D)(T_{sat} – T_{s}) }{h^{\prime}_{fg} } \)

 

\(=20 × 20 × 6747 W/m^{2} . K × \pi × 6 × 10^{-3} m × (327 – 298) K/2455 × 10^{3}J/kg\)

 

\(= 0.601 kg/s . m\)

2. From Equation 10.48, the minimum enhancement attributable to the annular fins is

\(\varepsilon _{ft,min}= \frac{q_{ft,min}}{q_{uft}} = \frac{\dot{m}^{\prime }_{ft,min}}{\dot{m}^{\prime }_{uft}} = \frac{t r_{2}}{S r_{1}}\left[\frac{r_{1}}{r_{2} } + 1.02 \frac{\sigma r_{1}}{(\rho _{l} – \rho _{\upsilon }) g t^{3}} \right]^{1/4} \)

 

\(= \frac{1 × 4}{2× 3}\left[\frac{3}{4 } + 1.02 \frac{0.0671 N/m × 3 × 10^{-3} m}{(992 – 0.098) kg/m^{3} × 9.8 m/s^{2} × (1 × 10^{-3} m)^{3}} \right]^{1/4} \)

 

=1.44

Therefore, the minimum condensation rate for the finned tubes is

\(\dot{m}^{\prime }_{ft,min} = \varepsilon _{ft,min} \dot{m}^{\prime }_{uft} = 1.44 ×0.601 kg/s . m = 0.866 kg/s . m\)

Comment: A value of n = –1/6 was used in Equation 10.49. However, for finned tubes the value of n is expected to be between zero and -1/6. For n = 0, the condensation rate per unit length of tubing would

\(\dot{m}^{\prime }_{ft,min} = \varepsilon _{ft,min} ×N \times N \frac{\bar{h}_{D,N}(\pi D)(T_{sat} – T_{s}) }{h^{\prime}_{fg} } \)

 

\( = 1.44 × 20 × 20 × 11,120 W/m^{2} . K × π ×6 × 10^{-3}m ×(327 – 298) K/2455 × 10^{3} J/kg\)

 

=1.43 kg/s . m

The preceding rate is for a nonoptimized condition where condensate fills the entire inter-fin region. Actual enhancements of \(\varepsilon _{ft,max} ≈ 4\) might be expected [50]. For \(\varepsilon _{ft,max} \)= 4 and n = 0, the condensation rate would be

\(\dot{m}^{\prime }_{ft} = \varepsilon _{ft,max} ×N \times N \frac{\bar{h}_{D,N}(\pi D)(T_{sat} – T_{s}) }{h^{\prime}_{fg} } \)

 

\( = 4 × 20 × 20 × 11,120 W/m^{2} . K × π ×6 × 10^{-3}m ×(327 – 298) K/2455 × 10^{3} J/kg\)

=3.96 kg/s . m

Hence the condensation rate could potentially be increased by 100 × (3.96 – 0.601) kg/s.m/ 0.601 kg/s.m = 560% by using finned tubes.
