Guest Question.

Design a RC phase shift oscillator to generate 5 kHz sine wave with 20V peak to peak amplitude. Draw the designed circuit. Assume \(h_{fe}\) = 150.

Admin
Solution

Refer to Fig. 15.13.

Given \(f_{o}\) = 5 kHz, \(V_{CC}\) = 20 V, \(h_{fe}\) = 150, \(R_{E}\) = 1 kΩ and \(C_{E}\) = 100 μF.

Let \(V_{CE} = \frac{V_{CC}}{2}\) = 10 V and \(V_{E} = \frac{V_{CC}}{10}\) = 2 V

\(I_{C} ≈ I_{E} = \frac{V_{E}}{R_{E}} = \frac{2}{1 × 10^{3}} \) = 2 mA

\(R_{C} = \frac{V_{CC} – V_{CE}}{I_{C}} =\frac{20 – 10}{2 × 10^{– 3}} \)= 5 kΩ

\(I_{B} = \frac{I_{C}}{h_{fe}} = \frac{2 × 10^{– 3}}{150} \)= 13.33 μA

\(R_{B} = \frac{V_{CC} – 0.6}{I_{B}} = \frac{20 – 0.6}{13.33 × 10^{– 6}}\) = 1.45 MΩ

\(R_{in} = R_{B} || h_{ie}\)

\(h_{ie} =\frac{ηV_{T}}{I_{B}} = \frac{2 × 26 × 10^{– 3}}{6.67 × 10^{– 6}} \)= 7.79 kΩ

\(R_{i}\) = 1.45 MΩ || 7.79 kΩ ≈ 7.75 kΩ
R ≈ \(R_{i}\) = 7.75 kΩ

K = \(\frac{R_{C}}{R}= \frac{5 × 10^{3}}{7.75 × 10^{3}}\) = 0.65

\(f_{o} = \frac{1}{2 \pi R_{C} \sqrt{4K + 6}}\\\) \(5 × 10^{3} = \frac{1}{2 \pi × 7.75 × 10^{3} × C × \sqrt{4 × 0.65 + 6}}\)

Therefore, C = \(\frac{1}{2 \pi × 5 × 10^{3} × 7.75 × 10^{3} × 2.93} \)= 1.4 nF
