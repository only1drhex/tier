Guest Question.

Find the current through \(12\Omega\) resistor for the given circuit by nodal method as shown in Fig. 5.173.

Admin
Solution

Applying KCL at node 1

\(\frac{V_{1}-2}{2}+\frac{V_{1}}{12}+\frac{V_{1}-V_{2}}{1}\)

Applying KCL at node 2

\(\therefore \frac{V_{2}-4}{3}+\frac{V_{2}}{3}+\frac{V_{2}-V_{1}}{1}\)

\(\therefore \frac{V_{1}}{2}+\frac{V_{1}}{12}+\frac{V_{1}}{1}-\frac{V_{2}}{1}=1 (1)\)

\(\therefore \frac{V_{2}}{3}+\frac{V_{2}}{3}+\frac{V_{2}}{1}-\frac{V_{1}}{1}=\frac{4}{3} (2)\)

\(\therefore V_{1}\left [ 1+\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{12} \right ]-V_{2}=1\)

From (1)

\(V_{1}\left [ \frac{12+6+1}{12} \right ]-V_{2}=1\)

\(\frac{19}{12}V_{1}-V_{2}=1 (3)\)

From (2)

\(\therefore V_{2}\left [ \frac{1}{3}+\frac{1}{3}+1 \right ]-V_{1}=\frac{4}{3} (4)\)

\(\therefore V_{2}\left [ \frac{1+1+3}{3} \right ]-V_{1}=\frac{4}{3}\)

\(\therefore \frac{5}{3}V_{2}-V_{1}=\frac{4}{3} (4)\)

\(\therefore \frac{19}{12}V_{1}-V_{2}=1 (5)\)

\(-V_{1}+\frac{5}{3}V_{2}=\frac{4}{3} (6)\)

Simplifying (5) & (6), we get

\(\therefore V_{2}=1.89V\)

\(\therefore V_{1}=1.82V\)
