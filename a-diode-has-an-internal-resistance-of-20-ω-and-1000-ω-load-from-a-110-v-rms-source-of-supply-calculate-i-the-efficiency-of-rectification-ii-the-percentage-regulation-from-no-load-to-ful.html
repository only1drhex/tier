Guest Question.

A diode has an internal resistance of 20 Ω and 1000 Ω load from a 110 V rms source of supply. Calculate (i) the efficiency of rectification (ii) the percentage regulation from no load to full load.

Admin
Solution

Given \(r_{f}\) = 20 Ω, \(R_{L}\) = 1000 Ω and \(V_{rms}\) (secondary) = 110 V

The half-wave rectifier uses a single diode.
Therefore,

\(V_{m} = \sqrt{2} V_{rms}\) (secondary) = 155.56 V
\(I_{m} =\frac{V_{m}}{r_{f} + R_{L}}=\frac{155.56}{20 + 1000}\) = 0.1525 A
\(I_{d.c.} =\frac{I_{m}}{\pi} =\frac{0.1525}{\pi}\) = 0.04854 A
\(V_{d.c.} = I_{d.c.}R_{L}\) = 0.04854 × 1000 = 48.54 V
\(P_{d.c.} = V_{d.c.}I_{d.c.}\) = 48.54 × 0.04854 = 2.36 W
\(P_{a.c.} = I_{rms}^{2}(r_{f} + R_{L}) = (\frac{I_{m}}{2})^{2}(r_{f} + R_{L})\) ( since \(I_{rms} =\frac{I_{m}}{2}\) for half-wave )
= \((\frac{0.1525}{2})^{2}\)(1000 + 20) = 5.93 W

Efficiency, η = \(\frac{P_{d.c.}}{P_{a.c.}}× 100 = \frac{2.36}{5.93}\) × 100 = 39.7346%

Percentage of line regulation = \(\frac{V_{NL} – V_{FL}}{V_{FL}}× 100 =\frac{\frac{V_{m}}{\pi}–V_{d.c.}}{V_{d.c.}}× 100\)
= \(\frac{\frac{155.56}{\pi} – 48.54}{48.54}\) × 100 = 2%
