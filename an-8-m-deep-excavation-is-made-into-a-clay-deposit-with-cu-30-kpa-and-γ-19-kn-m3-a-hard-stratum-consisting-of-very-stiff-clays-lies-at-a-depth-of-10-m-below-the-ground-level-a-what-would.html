Guest Question.

An 8 m-deep excavation is made into a clay deposit with \(c_{u}=30 kPa \text { and } \gamma=19 kN / m ^{3}\).

A hard stratum consisting of very stiff clays lies at a depth of 10 m below the ground level.

a. What would be the steepest slope at which a cut could be made before any failure occurs?

b. Find the slope that would give a short-term safety factor of 1.2.

c. What would be the safety factor against any short-term failure if the excavation was made at 30° to horizontal?

Admin
Solution

a.

 

\(n_{d}=\frac{10}{8}=1.25\)

 

For the steepest slope without failure:

 

\(F=1 \rightarrow c_{u, mob }=30 kPa\)

 

\(\therefore N_{S}=\frac{\gamma H}{c_{u, \text { mob }}}=\frac{19 \times 8}{30}=5.07\)

 

\(\text { From Taylor's chart (Figure 15.7), } \beta=63^{\circ} \ldots \text { toe circle }\)

 

b.

 

\(F=1.2 \rightarrow c_{u, mob }=30 / 1.2=25.0 kPa \rightarrow N_{S}=\frac{19 \times 8}{25}=6.08\)

 

\(\text { From Taylor's chart, } \beta=39^{\circ} \ldots \text { midpoint circle }\)

 

\(\text { c. For } \beta=30^{\circ} \text { and } n_{d}=1.25 \text {, from Taylor's chart: }\)

 

\(N_{S}=6.4=\frac{19 \times 8}{c_{u, mob }}\)

 

\(\therefore c_{u, mob }=23.8 kPa \rightarrow F=30 / 17.8=1.26\)
