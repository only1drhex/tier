Guest Question.

Given are the following data for a commercial centrifugal water pump: \(r_1 = 4 in, r_2 = 7 in, β_1 = 30°, β_2 = 20°\), speed = 1440 r/min. Estimate (a) the design point discharge, (b) the water horsepower, and (c) the head if \(b_1 = b_2 = 1.75 in\).

Admin
Solution

Part (a)

The angular velocity is ω = 2π r/s = 2π(1440/60) = 150.8 rad/s. Thus the tip speeds are \(u_1 = ωr_1\) = 150.8(4/12) = 50.3 ft/s and \(u_2 = ωr_2\) = 150.8(7/12) = 88.0 ft/s. From the inlet velocity diagram, Fig. E11.1a, with \(α_1\) = 90° for design point, we compute

\(V_{n1} = u_1 \tan 30^{\circ} = 29.0 ft/s\)

whence the discharge is

\(Q = 2 \pi r_1b_1V_{n1} = (2 \pi) \left(\frac{4}{12}ft\right) \left(\frac{1.75}{12}ft\right) \left(29.0\frac{ft}{s}\right) = (8.87 ft^3/s)(60 s/min) \left(\frac{1728}{231}gal/ft^3 \right) = 3980 gal/min\)

(The actual pump produces about 3500 gal/min.)

Part (b)

The outlet radial velocity follows from Q:

\(V_{n2} = \frac{Q}{2 \pi r_2b_2} = \frac{8.87 ft^3/s}{2 \pi (\frac{7}{12} ft) (\frac{1.75}{12} ft)} = 16.6 ft/s\)

This enables us to construct the outlet velocity diagram as in Fig. E11.1b, given \(\beta_2\) = 20°. The tangential component is

\(V_{t2} = u_2 – V_{n2} \cot \beta_2 = 88.0 – 16.6 \cot 20^{\circ} = 42.4\) ft/s

\(\alpha_2 = \tan^{-1} \frac{16.6}{42.4} = 21.4°\)

The power is then computed from Eq. (11.11) with \(V_{t1}\) = 0 at the design point:

\(P_w = \omega T = \rho Q(u_2V_{t2} – u_1V_{t1})\)

\(H = \frac{P_w}{\rho gQ} = \frac{1}{g} (u_2V_{t2} – u_1V_{t1})\) (11.11)

\(P_w = \rho Qu_2V_{t2} = (1.94 slugs/ft^3)(8.87 ft^3/s)(88.0 ft/s)(42.4 ft/s) = \frac{64,100 ft \cdot lbf/s}{550 ft-lbf/(s-hp)} = 117 hp\)

(The actual pump delivers about 125 water horsepower, requiring 147 bhp at 85 percent efficiency.)

Part (c)

Finally, the head is estimated from Eq. (11.11):

\(H \approx \frac{P_w}{\rho gQ} = \frac{64,100 ft \cdot lbf/s}{(62.4 lbf/ft^3)(8.87 ft^3/s)} = 116 ft\)

(The actual pump develops about 140-ft head.) Improved methods for obtaining closer estimates are given in advanced references [for example, 7, 8, and 31].
