Guest Question.

The elevations of the energy line and hydraulic grade line at point G are 44.0 and 42.0 ft, respectively. For the system shown in Fig. 8-5, calculate (a) the power extracted between G and H if the energy line at H is at elevation 4.0′ and (b) the pressure heads at E and F, which are at elevation 20.0′ (c) Draw, to the nearest 0.1 ft, the energy and hydraulic grade lines, assuming K for valve CD is 0.40 and f = 0.010 for the 6″ pipes.

Admin
Solution

The flow must be from the reservoir because the energy line at G is below the reservoir level. GH is a turbine. Before the power extracted can be calculated, flow Q and the head extracted must be obtained.

\(\text { (a) At } G, V_{12}^{2} / 2 g=2.0\).(the difference between the elevations of the energy and hydraulic grade lines).

\(\text { Also } V_{6}^{2} / 2 g=16 \times 2.0=32.0 \text { and } V_{24}^{2} / 2 g=\frac{1}{16}(2.0)=0.13 \text { ft. To obtain } Q \),

\(V_{12}=11.35 ft / sec \quad \text { and } \quad Q=\frac{1}{4} \pi(1)^{2} \times 11.35=8.91 cfs\).

\(\text { Horsepower }=\gamma Q H_{T} / 550=(62.4)(8.91)(44.0-4.0) / 550=40.4 \text { hp extracted }\).

\((\text { energy at } F)-(0.030)(100 / 1)(2.0)=\text { energy at } G=44.0\).

energy at F = 50.0 ft
E to F, datum zero:
(energy at E) – (45.4 – 11.3)²/2g = energy at F =50.0
energy at E = 68.1 ft
Pressure head at E = 68.1 – (20 + 32) = 16.1 ft water.
Pressure head at F = 50.0 – (20 + 2) = 28.0 ft water.
(c) Working back from E.

\(\text { Drop in energy, line } D-E=(0.010)\left(25 / \frac{1}{2}\right)(32.0)=16.0 ft\).

line C-D = (0.40)(32.0) = 12.8 ft
line B-C = same as D-E = 16.0 ft
line A-B = (0.50)(32.0) = 16.0 ft
elevation at D – 16.0 = elevation at E of 68.0, El. D = 84.0
elevation at C – 12.8 = elevation at D of 84.0, El. C = 96.8
elevation at B – 16.0 = elevation at C of 96.8, El. B = 112.8
elevation at A – 16.0 = elevation at B of 112.8, El. A = 128.8.

The hydraulic grade line is V²/2g below the energy line: 32.0 ft in the 6″, 2.0 ft in the 12″, and 0.13 ft in the 24″. The values are shown in Fig. 8.5.
