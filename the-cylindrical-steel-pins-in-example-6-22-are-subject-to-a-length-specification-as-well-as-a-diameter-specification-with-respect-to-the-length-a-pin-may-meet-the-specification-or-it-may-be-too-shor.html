Guest Question.

The cylindrical steel pins in are subject to a length specification as well as a diameter specification. With respect to the length, a pin may meet the specification, or it may be too short or too long. A total of 1021 pins are sampled and categorized with respect to both length and diameter specification. The results are presented in the following table. Test the null hypothesis that the proportions of pins that are too thin, OK, or too thick with respect to the diameter specification do not depend on the classification with respect to the length specification.

Observed Values for 1021 Steel Pins
Diameter
Length Too
Thin		 OK Too
Thick		 Total
Too Short 13 117 4 134
OK 62 664 80 806
Too Long 5 68 8 81
Total 80 849 92 1021

Solution

We begin by using Equation (6.8) to compute the expected values. The expected values are given in the following table.

\(E_{i j}=\frac{O_{i .} O_{. j}}{O_{. .}}\) (6.8)

Expected Values for 1021 Steel Pins
Diameter
Length Too
Thin		 OK Too
Thick		 Total
Too Short 10.50 111.43 12.07 134.0
OK 63.15 670.22 72.63 806.0
Too Long 6.35 67.36 7.30 81.0
Total 80.0 849.0 92.0 1021.0

We note that all the expected values are greater than or equal to 5 . (One of the observed values is not; this doesn’t matter.) Therefore the chi-square test is appropriate. We use Equation (6.9) to compute the value of the chi-square statistic:

\(\chi^{2}=\sum_{i=1}^{I} \sum_{j=1}^{J} \frac{\left(O_{i j}-E_{i j}\right)^{2}}{E_{i j}}\) (6.9)

\(\begin{aligned}\chi^{2} &=\frac{(13-10.50)^{2}}{10.50}+\cdots+\frac{(8-7.30)^{2}}{7.30} \\ &=\frac{6.25}{10.50}+\cdots+\frac{0.49}{7.30}=7.46\end{aligned}\)

Since there are three rows and three columns, the number of degrees of freedom is \((3-1)(3-1)=4\). To obtain the \(P\)-value, we consult the chi-square table (Table A.7). Looking under four degrees of freedom, we find that the upper \(10 \%\) point is \(7.779\). We conclude that \(P>0.10\). There is no evidence that the length and thickness are related.
