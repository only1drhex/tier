Guest Question.

What is the maximum work that can be obtained in a steady-state flow process from 1 mol of nitrogen in its ideal-gas state at 800 K and 50 bar? Take the temperature and pressure of the surroundings as 300 K and 1.0133 bar.

Admin
Solution

The maximum possible work is obtained from any completely reversible process that reduces the nitrogen to the temperature and pressure of the surroundings, i.e., to 300 K and 1.0133 bar. Required here is the calculation of \(W_{ideal}\) by Eq. (5.22), in which \(\Delta S\) and \(\Delta H\) are the molar entropy and enthalpy changes of the nitrogen as its state changes from 800 K and 50 bar to 300 K and 1.0133 bar. For the ideal-gas state, enthalpy is independent of pressure, and its change is given by:

\( W_{ideal}=\Delta H-T_{\sigma }\Delta S\) (5.22)

 

\( \Delta H^{ig}=\int_{T_{1}}^{T_{2}}{C_{P}^{ig}}dT\)

The value of this integral is found from Eq. (4.8), and is represented by:

\(\int_{T_{0}}^{T}{\frac{C_{P}}{R} }dT=A(T-T_{0})+\frac{B}{2}(T^{2}-T^{2}_{0})+\frac{C}{3}(T^{3}-T^{3}_{0})+D\left(\frac{T-T_{0}}{TT_{0}} \right)\) (4.8)
