Guest Question.

Express the solution of

\(\frac{ d ^{2} y}{ d x^{2}}+a^{2} x^{2} y=0\) (A.50)

in terms of Bessel functions.

Admin
Solution

The given equation is in non-standard form, and hence is converted to standard form by a suitable transformation. Let \(y=x^{2} Y\) . Then, we have

\(\frac{ d y}{ d x}=x^{2} \frac{ d Y}{ d x}+2 x Y\) and \(\frac{ d ^{2} y}{ d x^{2}}=x^{2} \frac{ d ^{2} Y}{ d x^{2}}+4 x \frac{ d Y}{ d x}+2 Y\)

The given differential equation (A.50) then reduces to the required standard form

\(\frac{ d ^{2} y}{ d x^{2}}+a^{2} x^{2} y=0\) (A.50)

\(x^{2} \frac{ d ^{2} Y}{ d x^{2}}+4 x \frac{ d Y}{ d x}+\left[a^{2} x^{4}+2\right] Y=0\) (A.51)

Compare Eq. (A.51) with (A.46)to get the following:

\(x^{2} \frac{ d ^{2} y}{ d x^{2}}+(1-2 \alpha) x \frac{ d y}{ d x}+\left[\beta^{2} \gamma^{2} x^{2 \gamma}+\alpha^{2}-m^{2} \gamma^{2}\right] y=0\) (A.46)

\(1-\alpha=4\) or \(\alpha=-\frac{3}{2}\) ; \(2 \gamma=4\) or \(\gamma=2\)

\(\beta^{2} \gamma^{2}=a^{2}\) or \(\beta=\frac{a}{2}\) ; \(\alpha^{2}-m^{2} \gamma^{2}=2\) or \(m=\frac{1}{4}\)

Since the Bessel index is not an integer, we make use of Eq. A.47 to write

\(y=C_{1} x^{\alpha} J_{m}\left(\beta x^{\gamma}\right)+C_{2} x^{\alpha} J_{-m}\left(\beta x^{\gamma}\right)\) m not an integer (A.47)

\(Y(x)=C_{1} x^{-\frac{3}{2}} J_{\frac{1}{4}}\left(\frac{a}{2} x^{-\frac{3}{2}}\right)+C_{2} x^{-\frac{3}{2}} J_{-\frac{1}{4}}\left(\frac{a}{2} x^{-\frac{3}{2}}\right)\) (A.52)

or

\(y(x)=C_{1} \sqrt{x} J_{\frac{1}{4}}\left(\frac{a}{2} x^{-\frac{3}{2}}\right)+C_{2} \sqrt{x} J_{-\frac{1}{4}}\left(\frac{a}{2} x^{-\frac{3}{2}}\right)\) (A.53)
