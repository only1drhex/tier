Guest Question.

Determine the distribution of bending moment in the frame ABCD shown in Fig. 16.24(a); the flexural rigidity of all the members of the frame is EI.

Admin
Solution

Comparison with Fig. 16.23(a) shows that the frame has a degree of statical indeterminacy equal to 2 since the vertical leg CD is pinned to the foundation at D. We therefore require just 2 releases of reaction, as shown in Fig. 16.24(b), to obtain the statically determinate primary structure. For frames of this type it is usual to neglect the displacements produced by axial force and to assume that they are caused solely by bending.

The pointDin the primary structure will suffer vertical and horizontal displacements, \(\Delta_{ D , V }\) and \(\Delta_{ D , H }\).

Thus if we designate the redundant reactions as \(R_{1}\), and \(R_{2}\), the equations of compatibility are

\(\Delta_{ D , V }+a_{11} R_{1}+a_{12} R_{2}=0\) (i)

\(\Delta_{ D , H }+a_{21} R_{1}+a_{22} R_{2}=0\) (ii)

in which the flexibility coefficients have their usual meaning. Again, as in the preceding examples, we employ the unit load method to calculate the displacements and flexibility coefficients. Thus

\(\Delta_{ D , V }=\sum \int_{ L } \frac{M_{0} M_{1, V }}{E I} d x\)

in which \(M_{1, V }\) is the bending moment at any point in the frame due to a unit load applied vertically at D.

Similarly

\(\Delta_{ D , H }=\sum \int_{ L } \frac{M_{0} M_{1, H }}{E I} d x\)

and

\(a_{11}=\sum \int_{L} \frac{M_{1, V}^{2}}{E I} d x \quad a_{22}=\sum \int_{L} \frac{M_{1, H }^{2}}{E I} d x \quad a_{12}=a_{21}=\sum \int_{L} \frac{M_{1, V } M_{1, H }}{E I} d x\)

We shall now write down expressions for bending moment in the members of the frame; we shall designate a bending moment as positive when it causes tension on the outside of the frame. Thus in DC

\(M_{0}=0 \quad M_{1, V }=0 \quad M_{1, H }=-1 x_{1}\)

In CB

\(M_{0}=4 x_{2} \frac{x_{2}}{2}=2 x_{2}^{2} \quad M_{1, V }=-1 x_{2} \quad M_{1, H }=-3\)

In BA

\(M_{0}=4 \times 3.5 \times 1.75+10 x_{3}=24.5+10 x_{3} \quad M_{1, V }=-3.5 \quad M_{1, H }=-1\left(3-x_{3}\right)\)

Hence

\(\Delta_{ D , V }=\frac{1}{E I}\left[\int_{0}^{3.5}\left(-2 x_{2}^{3}\right) d x_{2}+\int_{0}^{3}-\left(24.5+10 x_{3}\right) 3.5 d x_{3}\right]=-\frac{489.8}{E I}\)

 

\(\Delta_{ D , H }=\frac{1}{E I}\left[\int_{0}^{3.5}\left(-6 x_{2}^{2}\right) d x_{2}+\int_{0}^{3}-\left(24.5+10 x_{3}\right)\left(3-x_{3}\right) d x_{3}\right]=-\frac{241.0}{E I}\)

 

\(a_{11}=\frac{1}{E I}\left[\int_{0}^{3.5} x_{2}^{2} d x_{2}+\int_{0}^{3} 3.5^{2} d x_{3}\right]=\frac{51.0}{E I}\)

 

\(a_{22}=\frac{1}{E I}\left[\int_{0}^{3} x_{1}^{2} d x_{1}+\int_{0}^{3.5} 3^{2} d x_{2}+\int_{0}^{3}\left(3-x_{3}\right)^{2} d x_{3}\right]=\frac{49.5}{E I}\)

 

\(a_{12}=a_{21}=\frac{1}{E I}\left[\int_{0}^{3.5} 3 x_{2} d x_{2}+\int_{0}^{3} 3.5\left(3-x_{3}\right) d x_{3}\right]=\frac{34.1}{E I}\)

Substituting for \(\Delta_{ D , V }, \Delta_{ D , H }, a_{11}\), etc., in Eqs (i) and (ii) we obtain

\(-\frac{489.8}{E I}+\frac{51.0}{E I} R_{1}+\frac{34.1}{E I} R_{2}=0\) (iii)

and

\(-\frac{241.0}{E I}+\frac{34.1}{E I} R_{1}+\frac{49.5}{E I} R_{2}=0\) (iv)

Solving Eqs (iii) and (iv) we have

\(R_{1}=11.8 kN \quad R_{2}=-3.3 kN\)

The bending moment diagram is then drawn as shown in Fig. 16.25.

It can be seen that the amount of computation for even the relatively simple frame of Ex. 16.12 is quite considerable. Generally, therefore, as stated previously, the moment distribution method or a computerbased analysis would be employed.
