Guest Question.

Consider the following circuit.

Find \(v_{L 1}(t)\) and \(v_{L 2}(t)\) in steady state.

Admin
Solution

First we convert the time-domain circuit into a phasor-domain version. Considering that \(\omega=5 \mathrm{rad} / \mathrm{s}\), we have the following conversions:

  • \(2 \cos \left(5 t+45^{\circ}\right) \mathrm{V} \longrightarrow \sqrt{2} \underline{/\pi/4} \mathrm{~V},\)
  • \(2 \mathrm{H} \longrightarrow j \times 5 \times 2=j 10 \Omega\),
  • \(4 \mathrm{H} \longrightarrow j \times 5 \times 4=j 20 \Omega\),
  • \(60 \mathrm{mF} \longrightarrow 1 /\left(j \times 5 \times 60 \times 10^{-3}\right)=-j 10 / 3 \Omega\).

With these conversions, we have the following circuit in the phasor domain.

In order to solve the problem, we apply the mesh analysis with three mesh currents. Considering that \(\underline{i}_{x}=\underline{i}_{a}\), we obtain

  • \(\operatorname{KVL}(a):-\sqrt{2} \underline{/\pi/4} +10 \underline{i}_{a}+j 10\left(\underline{i}_{a}-\underline{i}_{c}\right)=0\),

leading to

\((10+j 10) \underline{i}_{a}-j 10 \underline{i}_{c}=1+j .\)

Similarly, applying KVL in mesh \(b\), we derive

  • \(\operatorname{KVL}(b):(10 / 3) \underline{i}_{x}+j 20 \underline{i}_{b}=0 \longrightarrow \underline{i}_{b}=(j / 6) \underline{i}_{a}\).

Finally, considering mesh \(c\), we have

  • \(\operatorname{KVL}(c):-(10 / 3) \underline{i}_{x}-j(10 / 3) \underline{i}_{c}+j 10\left(\underline{i}_{c}-\underline{i}_{a}\right)=0\)

or

\(j(20 / 3) \underline{i}_{c}=(10 / 3+j 10) \underline{i}_{a}.\)

The final equality can be used to find \(\underline{i}_{c}\) in terms of \(\underline{i}_{a}\) :

\(\underline{i}_{c}=\frac{3}{j 20} \times\left(\frac{10}{3}+j 10\right) \underline{i}_{a}=(3-j) \underline{i}_{a} / 2 .\)

Therefore, we have

\(\begin{aligned}(10+j 10) \underline{i}_{a}-j 5(3-j) \underline{i}_{a} &=1+j \\ (5-j 5) \underline{i}_{a} &=1+j \longrightarrow \underline{i}_{a}=\frac{1+j}{5(1-j)}=j / 5 \mathrm{~A},\end{aligned}\)

and

\(\begin{aligned}&\underline{i}_{b}=\frac{j}{6} \times \frac{j}{5}=-1 / 30 \mathrm{~A}, \\&\underline{i}_{c}=\frac{3-j}{2} \times \frac{j}{5}=(1+j 3) / 10 \mathrm{~A}.\end{aligned}\)

Then the voltage across the \(2 \mathrm{H}\) inductor can be found to be

\(\underline{v}_{L 1}=j 10 \times\left(\underline{i}_{a}-\underline{i}_{c}\right)=j 10 \times\left(\frac{j}{5}-\frac{1+j 3}{10}\right)=-2-j+3=1-j \mathrm{~V} .\)

Similarly, we have

\(\underline{v}_{L 2}=j 20 \underline{i}_{b}=-j 2 / 3 \mathrm{~V} .\)

Finally, we obtain the time-domain voltage values

\(\begin{aligned}v_{L 1}(t) &=\sqrt{2} \operatorname{Re}\left\{\underline{v}_{L 1} \exp (j \omega t)\right\} \\&=\sqrt{2} \operatorname{Re}\{\sqrt{2} \underline{/(-\pi/4)} \exp (j 5 t)\}=2 \cos (5 t-\pi / 4) \mathrm{V}\end{aligned}\)

and

\(\begin{aligned}v_{L 2}(t) &=\sqrt{2} \operatorname{Re}\left\{\underline{v}_{L 2} \exp (j \omega t)\right\} \\&=\sqrt{2} \operatorname{Re}\{(2 / 3) \underline{/(-\pi/2)} \exp (j 5 t)\}=\frac{2 \sqrt{2}}{3} \cos (5 t-\pi / 2) \mathrm{V} .\end{aligned}\)
