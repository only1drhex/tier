Guest Question.

Calculating the Efficiency of a Brayton Cycle

Assuming the working fluid in a Brayton power cycle is an ideal gas of constant heat capacity, show that the efficiency of the cycle is

\(\eta=\frac{-\left(\dot{W}_{34}-\dot{W}_{12}\right)}{\dot{Q}_{23}}\)

and obtain an explicit expression for the efficiency in terms of the cycle temperatures.

Admin
Solution

For path 1 → 2, through the compressor, we have from the energy balance that

\(\frac{\dot{W}_{12}}{\dot{N}}=\underline{H}_{2}-\underline{H}_{1}=C_{ P }^{*}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right)\)

Also from \(\underline{S}_{2}=\underline{S}_{1}\), we have

\(0=C_{ P }^{*} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R \ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \quad \text { or } \quad \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right)^{R / C_{ P }^{*}}\)

Similarly, across the gas turbine, 3 → 4, we have

\(\frac{\dot{W}_{34}}{\dot{N}}=\underline{H}_{4}-\underline{H}_{3}=C_{ P }^{*}\left(T_{4}-T_{3}\right)\)

and

\(\frac{T_{4}}{T_{3}}=\left(\frac{P_{4}}{P_{3}}\right)^{R / C_{ P }^{*}}\)

But \(P_{3}=P_{2} \text { and } P_{4}=P_{1}\), so that

\(\frac{T_{4}}{T_{3}}=\left(\frac{P_{4}}{P_{3}}\right)^{R / C_{ P }^{*}}=\left(\frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}}\right)^{R / C_{ P }^{*}}=\frac{T_{1}}{T_{2}}\)

Across the high-temperature heat exchanger, 2 → 3, we have

\(0=\dot{N} \underline{H}_{2}-\dot{N} \underline{H}_{3}+\dot{Q}_{23}\)

or

\(\frac{\dot{Q}_{23}}{\dot{N}}=\underline{H}_{3}-\underline{H}_{2}=C_{ P }^{*}\left(T_{3}-T_{2}\right)\)

Similarly, across the low-temperature heat exchanger, 4 → 1,

\(\frac{\dot{Q}_{41}}{\dot{N}}=\underline{H}_{1}-\underline{H}_{4}=C_{ P }^{*}\left(T_{1}-T_{4}\right)\)

Therefore

\(\begin{aligned}\eta &=-\frac{\left(\dot{W}_{34}+\dot{W}_{12}\right)}{\dot{Q}_{23}}=\frac{-C_{ P }^{*}\left(T_{4}-T_{3}\right)-C_{ P }^{*}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right)}{C_{ P }^{*}\left(T_{3}-T_{2}\right)} \\&=\frac{\left(T_{3}-T_{4}\right)+\left(T_{1}-T_{2}\right)}{T_{3}-T_{2}}=\frac{\left(T_{3}-T_{2}\right)-\left(T_{4}-T_{1}\right)}{\left(T_{3}-T_{2}\right)}=1-\frac{\left(T_{4}-T_{1}\right)}{\left(T_{3}-T_{2}\right)} \\&=1-\frac{T_{1}}{T_{2}} \frac{\left(\frac{T_{4}}{T_{1}}-1\right)}{\left(\frac{T_{3}}{T_{2}}-1\right)}\end{aligned}\)

However, from \(T_{4} / T_{3}=T_{1} / T_{2}, \text { we have that } T_{4} / T_{1}=T_{3} / T_{2}\), so that

\(\eta=1-\frac{T_{1}}{T_{2}}=1-\left(\frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}}\right)^{R / C_{ P }^{*}}=1-K_{ T }^{-R / C_{ P }^{*}}\)

where \(K_{ T }=P_{22} / P_{1}\) is the compression ratio.
