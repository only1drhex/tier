Guest Question.

Two undisturbed clay samples were taken from the middle of the overconsolidated and normally consolidated clay layers in the soil profile shown. The water table is at the top of the overconsolidated clay layer. Consolidation tests were carried out on the two samples and the results are summarized.

O.C. Clay N.C. Clay
Natural water content (%) 20.0 29.0
Preconsolidation pressure (kPa) 50.0 65.0
Compression index 0.55 0.6.
Recompression index 0.06 0.07
Coefficient of consolidation \(\left( m ^{2}\right.\)/year) 13.0 2.5
Admin
Solution

a. For OC clay:

 

\(e=0.20 \times 2.70=0.540 \rightarrow \gamma_{\text {sat }}=20.6 kN / m ^{3}\)

 

For NC clay:

 

\(e=0.29 \times 2.70=0.783 \rightarrow \gamma_{ sat }=19.2 kN / m ^{3}\)

 

For OC clay:

 

\(\sigma_{\nu 0}^{\prime}=1 \times 16.0+1.0(20.6-9.81)\) \(=26.8 kPa \text { at the mid-layer }\)

 

For NC clay:

 

\(\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}=1 \times 16.0+2.0(20.6-9.81)+1.5(19.0-9.81)+1.5(19.2-9.81)\) \(=65.5 kPa \text { at the mid-layer }\)

 

The increase in vertical normal stress, \(\Delta \sigma^{\prime}=2 \times 20=40 kPa\), is the same at all
depths.

At mid-depth of OC clay:

 

\(\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}=26.8 kPa , \Delta \sigma^{\prime}=40 kPa \rightarrow \sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}+\Delta \sigma^{\prime}(=66.8 kPa )>\sigma_{p}^{\prime}(=50 kPa )\)

 

\(\therefore \Delta e=C_{r} \log \frac{\sigma_{p}^{\prime}}{\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}}+C_{c} \log \frac{\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}+\Delta \sigma^{\prime}}{\sigma_{p}^{\prime}}=0.06 \log \frac{50}{26.8}+0.55 \log \frac{66.8}{50}=0.0855\)

 

\(\frac{\Delta e}{1+e_{0}}=\frac{\Delta H}{H_{0}} \rightarrow \Delta H_{O C}=\frac{0.0855}{1+0.540} \times 2000=111.0 mm\)

 

At mid-depth of NC clay:

 

\(\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}=65.5 kPa , \Delta \sigma^{\prime}=40 kPa \rightarrow \sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}+\Delta \sigma^{\prime}=105.5 kPa\)

 

\(\therefore \Delta e=C_{c} \log \frac{\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}+\Delta \sigma^{\prime}}{\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}}=0.60 \log \frac{105.5}{65.5}=0.1242\)

 

\(\frac{\Delta e}{1+e_{0}}=\frac{\Delta H}{H_{0}} \rightarrow \Delta H_{N C}=\frac{0.1242}{1+0.783} \times 3000=209.0 mm\)

 

\(\therefore \text { The final consolidation settlement }=111.0+209.0=320 mm\)

 

b. After one month, letâ€™s find the time factors in both clays:

 

\(OC : T=\frac{c_{v} t}{H^{2}}=\frac{13 \times\left(\frac{1}{12}\right)}{1^{2}}=1.08 \rightarrow U_{ avg }=94 \% \rightarrow \text { settlement }=0.94 \times 111=104.3 mm\)

 

\(NC : T=\frac{c_{v} t}{H_{ dr }^{2}}=\frac{2.5 \times\left(\frac{1}{12}\right)}{3^{2}}=0.023 \rightarrow U_{ avg }=17 \% \rightarrow \text { settlement }=0.17 \times 209=35.5 mm\)

 

\(\therefore \text { Consolidation settlement after one month }=104.3+35.5=139.8 mm\)

 

\(\text { c. In both clays, } \Delta u_{0}=40 kPa \text {. }\)

 

At the middle of OC clay layer:

 

\(Z=z / H_{ dr }=1 / 1=1, T =1.08 \rightarrow U_{z}(t)=91 \%\)

 

\(\Delta u=40 \times 0.09=3.6 kPa \text { and } \Delta \sigma^{\prime}=40 \times 0.91=36.4 kPa\)

 

\(\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}=26.8 kPa , u_{0}=9.8 kPa\)

 

\(\therefore \sigma_{v}^{\prime}=\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}+\Delta \sigma^{\prime}=26.8+36.4=63.2 kPa , \text { and }\)

 

\(u=u_{0}+\Delta u=9.8+3.6=13.4 kPa\)

 

At the middle of NC clay layer:

 

\(Z=z / H_{d r}=1.5 / 3.0=0.5, T=0.023 \rightarrow U_{z}(t)=5 \%\)

 

\(\Delta u=40 \times 0.95=38.0 kPa \text { and } \Delta \sigma^{\prime}=40 \times 0.05=2.0 kPa\)

 

\(\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}=65.5 kPa , u_{0}=5 \times 9.81=49.1 kPa\)

 

\(\therefore \sigma_{v}^{\prime}=\sigma_{v 0}^{\prime}+\Delta \sigma^{\prime}=65.5+2.0=67.5 kPa , \text { and }\)

 

\(u=u_{0}+\Delta u=49.1+38.0=87.1 kPa\)
