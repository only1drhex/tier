Guest Question.

A smooth retaining wall with 2 m of embedment in the clayey sand retains a 6 m-high sandy backfill as shown in part (a) of the figure on page 242. Assuming that the entire soil mass on the right side of the wall is in the active state and the soil on the left is in the passive state, compute the active and passive thrusts on the wall.

Admin
Solution
\(K_{A, \text { sand }}=\tan ^{2}\left(45-\frac{33}{2}\right)=0.295\)

 

\(K_{A, \text { clayey sand }}=\tan ^{2}\left(45-\frac{25}{2}\right)=0.406\)

 

\(K_{P, \text { clayey sand }}=\tan ^{2}\left(45+\frac{25}{2}\right)=2.46\)

 

\(\text { Let's calculate } \sigma_{h}^{\prime}\) values on the right (active) side.

 

\(\text { Top of sand: } \sigma_{h}^{\prime}=0\)

 

\(\text { Just above the water table: } \sigma_{h}^{\prime}=0.295 \times 6 \times 17.0=30.1 kPa\)

 

Just below the water table:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{h}^{\prime} &=K_{A} \sigma_{v}^{\prime}-2 c^{\prime} V_{K_{A}} \\&=0.406 \times 6 \times 17-2 \times 15 \times V_{0} .406=22.3 kPa\end{aligned}\)

 

At 2 m into the clayey sand:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{h}^{\prime} &=K_{A} \sigma_{v}^{\prime}-2 c^{\prime} \sqrt{K_{A}} \\&=0.406 \times[6 \times 17+2 \times(20-9.81)]-2 \times 15 \times \sqrt{0.406} \\&=30.6 kPa\end{aligned}\)

 

Now, letâ€™s calculate \(\sigma_{h}^{\prime}\) values on the left (passive) side.

Top of clayey sand:

 

\(\sigma_{h}^{\prime}=K_{p} \sigma_{v}^{\prime}+2 c^{\prime} \sqrt{K_{P}}=2 \times 15 \times \sqrt{0.406}=19.1 kPa\)

 

At 2 m into clayey sand:

 

\(\begin{aligned}\sigma_{h}^{\prime} &=K_{P} \sigma_{v}^{\prime}+2 c^{\prime} V_{K} \\&=2.46 \times 2 \times(20-9.81)+2 \times 15 \times V_{0} .406=69.2 kPa\end{aligned}\)

 

These values of \(\sigma_{h}^{\prime}\) are plotted with depth as shown in part (b) in the figure on page 242.

 

Zone Horizontal load (kN/m) Height (m) Moment (kN-m/m)
1 \(0.5 \times 30.1 \times 6=90.3\) 4.0 361.2
2 \(22.3 \times 2=44.6\) 1.0 44.6
3 \(0.5 \times 8.3 \times 2=8.3\) 0.667 5.5
4 \(19.1 \times 2=38.2\) 1.0 38.2
5 \(0.5 \times 50.1 \times 2=50.1\) 0.667 33.4

 

\(P_{A}=90.3+44.6+8.3=143.2 kN\)

 

\(P_{P}=38.2+50.1=88.3 kN\)

 

\(P_{A} \text { acts at a height of } \frac{(361.2+44.6+5.5)}{143.2}=2.87 \text { mabove the bottom of the wall. }\)

 

\(P_{P} \text { acts at a height of } \frac{(38.2+33.4)}{88.3}=0.81 m \text { above the bottom of the wall. }\)

 

In addition to \(P_{A} \text { and } P_{P}\), there is also the water thrust on the wall due to the pore water pressure, which is the same on both sides.
