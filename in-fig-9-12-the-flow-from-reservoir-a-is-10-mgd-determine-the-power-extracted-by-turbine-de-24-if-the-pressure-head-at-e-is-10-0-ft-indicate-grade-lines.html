Guest Question.

In Fig. 9-12, the flow from reservoir A is 10 mgd. Determine the power extracted by turbine DE 24 if the pressure head at E is —10.0 ft. Indicate grade lines.

Admin
Solution

The analysis of a branching system of pipes should concentrate itself on the junction point C. First, the sum of the flows toward C must equal the sum of the flows away from C. Second, the elevation of the hydraulic grade line at C is often the key to the solution.
To calculate the elevation of the hydraulic grade line at C, assume the lost head, A to C, to be 24 ft.
Then

\(\begin{array}{llll}S_{20}=24 / 6=4.0 ft / 1000 ft , & Q_{20}=6.5 mgd & (41.9 \%) \\S_{24}=24 / 8=3.0 ft / 1000 ft , & Q_{24}=9.0 mgd & (58.1 \%)\end{array}\)

total Q = 15.5 mgd (100.0%).

Apply these percentages to the given 10 mgd flow from A to C.

\(\begin{array}{ll}Q_{20}=4.2 mgd , & S_{20}=1.77 ft / 1000 ft , \quad \text { lost head }=10.6 ft \\Q_{24}=5.8 mgd , & S_{24}=1.33 ft / 1000 ft , \quad \text { lost head }=10.6 ft \text { (check) }\end{array}\).

Thus the elevation of the hydraulic grade line at C = 220.6 — 10.6 = 210.0′. From this information, the grade line drops 8 ft from B to C, and the flow must be from B to C. Then

\(S_{30}=8 / 8=1.0 ft / 1000 ft , \quad Q=8.95 mgd\).

Also, flow from C = flow to C

\(Q_{C-D}=10.0+8.95=18.95 mgd\).

\(\text { Thus } S_{30}=4.0 ft / 1000 ft , LH _{C-D}=40.0 ft \text {, and elevation of grade line at } D=210-40=170.0^{\prime} \).

\(\text { horsepower extracted }=\frac{\gamma Q H_{T}}{550}=\frac{(62.4)(18.95 \times 1.547)(170-70)}{550}=333 hp\).
