Guest Question.

Consider a simply supported steel shaft as depicted in Figure 7–14, with 1 in diameter and a 31-in span between bearings, carrying two gears weighing 35 and 55 lbf.
(a) Find the influence coefficients.
(b) Find Σ wy and \(Σ wy^{2}\) and the first critical speed using Rayleigh’s equation, Equation (7–23).
(c) From the influence coefficients, find \(ω_{11}\) and \(ω_{22}\).
(d) Using Dunkerley’s equation, Equation (7–32), estimate the first critical speed.
(e) Use superposition to estimate the first critical speed.
( f ) Estimate the shaft’s intrinsic critical speed. Suggest a modification to Dunkerley’s equation to include the effect of the shaft’s mass on the first critical speed of the attachments.

Admin
Solution

(a) \( I=\frac{πd^{4}}{64}=\frac{π(1)^{4}}{64}=0.049 09 in^{4}\)

\( 6EIl = 6(30)10^{6}(0.049 09)31 = 0.2739(10^{9}) lbf · in^{3}\)

From Equation set (7–24), \( δ_{ij}=\begin{cases} \frac{b_{j}x_{i}}{6EIl} (l^{2} − b^{2}_{j} − x^{2}_{i} ) & \quad x_{i} ≤ a_{i} \\ \frac{a_{j}(l − x_{i})}{6EIl} (2lx_{i} − a^{2}_{j} − x^{2}_{i} ) & x_{i} > a\end{cases} \)

 

\( δ_{11} =\frac{24(7)(31^{2} − 24^{2} − 7^{2})}{0.2739(10^{9})}=2.061(10^{−4}) in/lbf\)

 

\( δ_{22} =\frac{11(20)(31^{2} − 11^{2} − 20^{2})}{0.2739(10^{9})}= 3.534(10^{−4}) in/lbf\)

 

\( δ_{12}=δ_{21} =\frac{11(7)(31^{2} − 11^{2} − 7^{2})}{0.2739(10^{9})}= 2.224(10^{−4}) in/lbf\)

\(y_{1} = w_{1}δ_{11} + w_{2}δ_{12} = 35(2.061)10^{−4} + 55(2.224)10^{−4} = 0.019 45 in\)

 

\(y_{2} = w_{1}δ_{21} + w_{2}δ_{22} = 35(2.224)10^{−4} + 55(3.534)10^{−4} = 0.027 22 in\)

 

\((b) ∑w_{i} y_{i} = 35(0.019 45) + 55(0.027 22) = 2.178 lbf ·in \)

 

\( ∑w_{i} y^{2}_{i} = 35(0.019 45)^{2} + 55(0.027 22)^{2} = 0.053 99 lbf · in^{2}\)

 

\( \omega =\sqrt{\frac{386.1(2.178)}{0.053 99} } =124.8 rad/s, or 1192 rev/min\)

(c)

\( \frac{1}{ω^{2}_{11}} =\frac{w_{1}}{g} δ_{11}\)

 

\( ω_{11}=\sqrt{\frac{g}{w_{1}δ_{11}} } =\sqrt{\frac{386.1}{35(2.061)10^{−4}} }=231.4 rad/s, or 2210 rev/min \)

 

\( ω_{22}=\sqrt{\frac{g}{w_{2}δ_{22}} } =\sqrt{\frac{386.1}{55(3.534)10^{−4}} }=140.9rad/s, or 1346 rev/min \)

(d)

\( \frac{1}{ω^{2}_{1}} \approx \sum{\frac{1}{ω^{2}_{ii}} } =\frac{1}{231.4^{4}} +\frac{1}{140.9^{9}} =6.905(10^{−5}) (1) \)

 

\( ω_{1}\approx \sqrt{\frac{1}{6.905(10^{−5})} } =120.3 rad/s, or 1149 rev/min \)

which is less than part b, as expected.
(e) From Equation (7–24),

\( δ_{cc} =\frac{b_{cc} x_{cc}(l^{2} − b^{2}_{cc} − x^{2}_{cc})}{6EIl}=\frac{15.5(15.5)(31^{2} − 15.5^{2} − 15.5^{2})}{0.2739(10^{9})}\)

 

\( = 4.215(10^{−4}) in/lbf \)

From Equation (7–33),

\( w_{1c} = w_{1}\frac{δ_{11}}{δ_{cc}}=35 \frac{2.061(10^{−4})}{4.215(10^{−4})}=17.11 lbf\)

 

\( w_{2c} = w_{2}\frac{δ_{22}}{δ_{cc}}=55 \frac{3.534(10^{−4})}{4.215(10^{−4})}=46.11 lbf\)

 

\( \omega =\sqrt{\frac{g}{δ_{cc}∑w_{ic}} } =\sqrt{\frac{386.1}{4.215(10^{−4})(17.11 + 46.11)} } =120.4 rad/s, or 1150 rev/min \)

which, except for rounding, agrees with part d, as expected.

(f) For the shaft,\( E = 30(10^{6}) psi, γ = 0.282 lbf/in^{3} , and A = π(1^{2})∕4 = 0.7854 in^{2}\) . Considering the shaft alone, the critical speed, from Equation (7–22), is

\( \omega _{s}=\left(\frac{\pi }{l} \right) ^{2}\sqrt{\frac{gEI}{Aγ} } =\left(\frac{\pi }{31} \right) ^{2}\sqrt{\frac{386.1(30)10^{6}(0.049 09)}{0.7854(0.282)} } \)

= 520.4 rad/s, or 4970 rev/min
We can simply add \(1∕ω^{2}_{s} \)to the right side of Dunkerley’s equation, Equation (1), to include the shaft’s contribution,

\( \frac{1}{\omega ^{2}_{1}} \approx \frac{1}{520.4^{2}} +6.905(10^{−5}) = 7.274(10^{−5}) \)

 

 

\( \omega _{1} \approx 117.3 rad/s, or 1120 rev/min \)

which is slightly less than part d, as expected.
The shaft’s first critical speed ωs is just one more single effect to add to Dunkerley’s equation. Since it doesnot fit into the summation, it is usually written up front.

\( \frac{1}{\omega ^{2}_{1}} \approx \frac{1}{\omega ^{2}_{s}}+\sum\limits_{i=1}^{n}{\frac{1}{\omega ^{2}}_{ii} } \)

Common shafts are complicated by the stepped-cylinder geometry, which makes the influence-coefficient determination part of a numerical solution.
