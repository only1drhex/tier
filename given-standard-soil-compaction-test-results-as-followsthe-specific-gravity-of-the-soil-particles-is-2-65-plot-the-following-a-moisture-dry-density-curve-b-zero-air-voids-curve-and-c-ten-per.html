Guest Question.

Given standard soil compaction test results as follows:

\(\begin{array}{|ccc|}\hline \text { Trial No. } & \text { Moisture content \% by dry weight } & \begin{array}{c}\text { Wet unit weight of compacted } \\\text { soil }\left( kN / m ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline 1 & 8.30 & 19.8 \\2 & 10.50 & 21.3 \\3 & 11.30 & 21.6 \\4 & 13.40 & 21.2 \\5 & 13.80 & 20.8 \\\hline\end{array}\)

The specific gravity of the soil particles is 2.65. Plot the following:

(a) Moisture-dry density curve,

(b) Zero air voids curve, and

(c) Ten per cent air content curve. (90% Saturation curve)

Determine the optimum moisture content and the corresponding maximum dry density of the soil.

Admin
Solution

Also determine the correct values of the maximum dry density and optimum moisture content if in the above test, the material retained on 20 mm Sieve, which was 9%, was eliminated. The specific gravity of these oversize particles was 2.79. The dry density values are

calculated from \(\gamma_{d}=\frac{\gamma}{\left(1+\frac{w}{100}\right)}\) , and are shown in Table 12.3.

Table 12.3 Dry density values

\(\begin{array}{|cccc|}\hline \text { Trial No. } & \text { Moisture content, w\% } & \begin{array}{c}\text { Wet unit weight } \\\left( kN / m ^{3}\right)\end{array} & \begin{array}{c}\text { Dry unit weight } \\\left( kN / m ^{3}\right)\end{array} \\\hline 1 & 8.30 & 19.8 & 18.28 \\2 & 10.50 & 21.3 & 19.28 \\3 & 11.30 & 21.6 & 19.41 \\4 & 13.40 & 21.2 & 18.70 \\5 & 13.80 & 20.8 & 18.28 \\\hline\end{array}\)

Since \(\gamma_{d}=\frac{G \cdot \gamma_{w}}{(1+e)}=\frac{G \cdot \gamma_{w}}{\left(1+\frac{w G}{s}\right)}=\frac{2.65 \times 9.81}{\left(1+\frac{w \times 2.65}{s}\right)}\)

for Zero air-voids condition, \(\gamma_{d}=\frac{2.65 \times 9.81}{\left(1+\frac{2.65 \times w}{100}\right)}, w \text { being in } \% \text {, }\)

and for Ten per cent air-voids condition,\(\gamma_{d}=\frac{2.65 \times 9.81}{\left(1+\frac{2.65 \times w}{90}\right)}, w \text { being in } \% \text {. }\)

From these equations, the following values are computed:

condition (90% saturation)

The moisture-dry density curve and the zero air-voids and ten per cent air-voids lines are shown in Fig. 12.12.

From the figure,
Optimum moisture content = 12%
Maximum dry density = 19.5 kN/\(m ^{3}\)

Correction for oversize fraction:

\(G=2.79 \text { for gravel } n_{1}=0.09 n_{2}=0.91\)

Corrected maximum dry density \(=\frac{G \cdot \gamma_{w} \gamma_{d_{\max }}}{n_{1} \gamma_{d_{\max }}+n_{2} G \gamma_{w}}\)

 

\(=\frac{2.79 \times 9.81 \times 19.5}{(0.09 \times 19.5+0.91 \times 2.79 \times 9.8)}=20 kN / m ^{3}\)

Corrected optimum moisture content

\(=n_{1} A_{0}+n_{2} w_{0}=0.91 \times 12 \% \text { (taking } A_{0} \text { as zero) }\)

= 10.9%
