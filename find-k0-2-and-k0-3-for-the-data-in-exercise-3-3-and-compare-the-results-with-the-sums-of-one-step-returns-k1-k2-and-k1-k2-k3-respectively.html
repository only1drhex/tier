Guest Question.

Find K(0, 2) and K(0, 3) for the data in and compare the results with the sums of one-step returns K(1)+K(2) and K(1)+K(2)+ K(3), respectively.

Admin
Solution

The return over two time steps as compared to the sum of one-step returns can take the following values:

Scenario K(0, 2) K(1) +K(2)
\(ω_1\) 15.50% 15.00%
\(ω_2\) 15.50% 15.00%
\(ω_3\) −5.50% −5.00%

Over three time steps we have

Scenario K(0, 3) K(1) + K(2) + K(3)
\(ω_{1}\) 3.95% 5.00%
\(ω_2\) 27.05% 25.00%
\(ω_3\) 3.95% 5.00%

The sum of one-step returns tends to be larger then the return over the total interval if the sign of one-step returns alternates.
