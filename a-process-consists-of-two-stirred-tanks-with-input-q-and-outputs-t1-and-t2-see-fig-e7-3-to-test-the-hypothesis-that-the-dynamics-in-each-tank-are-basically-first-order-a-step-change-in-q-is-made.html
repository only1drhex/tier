Guest Question.

A process consists of two stirred tanks with input \(q\) and outputs \(T_{1}\) and \(T_{2}\) (see Fig. E7.3). To test the hypothesis that the dynamics in each tank are basically first-order, a step change in \(q\) is made from 82 to \(85 L / min\), with output responses given in Table E7.3.

Solution

a)

\(\frac{T_{1}^{\prime}(s)}{Q^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{K_{1}}{\tau_{1} s+1} \quad \frac{T_{2}^{\prime}(s)}{T_{1}^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{K_{2}}{\tau_{2} s+1}\)

\(\frac{T_{2}^{\prime}(s)}{Q^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{K_{1} K_{2}}{\left(\tau_{1} s+1\right)\left(\tau_{2} s+1\right)} \approx \frac{K_{1} K_{2} e^{-\tau_{2} s}}{\tau_{1} s+1}\) (1)

where the approximation follows from Eq. 6-58 and the fact that \(\tau_{1}>\tau_{2}\), as revealed by an inspection of the data.

\(\frac{H_{2}^{\prime}(s)}{H_{1}^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{\frac{R_{2}}{R_{1}+R_{2}}}{\frac{R_{1} R_{2} A_{2}}{R_{1}+R_{2}} s+1}\) (6-58)

\(\begin{aligned}&K_{1}=\frac{T_{1}(50)-T_{1}(0)}{\Delta q}=\frac{18.0-10.0}{85-82}=2.667 \\&K_{2}=\frac{T_{2}(50)-T_{2}(0)}{T_{1}(50)-T_{1}(0)}=\frac{26.0-20.0}{18.0-10.0}=0.75\end{aligned}\)

Let \(z_{1}\) and \(z_{2}\) be the natural log of the fraction incomplete response for \(T_{1}\) and \(T_{2}\), respectively. Then,

\(\begin{aligned}&z_{1}(t)=\ln \left[\frac{T_{1}(50)-T_{1}(t)}{T_{1}(50)-T_{1}(0)}\right]=\ln \left[\frac{18-T_{1}(t)}{8}\right] \\&z_{2}(t)=\ln \left[\frac{T_{2}(50)-T_{2}(t)}{T_{2}(50)-T_{2}(0)}\right]=\ln \left[\frac{26-T_{2}(t)}{6}\right]\end{aligned}\)

A plot of \(z_{1}\) and \(z_{2}\) versus \(t\) is shown below. The slope of the \(z_{1}\) plot is \(-0.333\); hence \(\left(1 /-\tau_{1}\right)=-0.333\) and \(\tau_{1}=3.0\)

From the best-fit line for \(z_{2}\) versus \(t\), the projection intersects \(z_{2}=0\) at \(t \approx 1.15\). Hence \(\tau_{2}=1.15\).

\(\frac{T_{1}^{\prime}(s)}{Q^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{2.667}{3 s+1}\) (2)

\(\frac{T_{2}^{\prime}(s)}{T_{1}^{\prime}(s)}=\frac{0.75}{1.15 s+1}\) (3)
