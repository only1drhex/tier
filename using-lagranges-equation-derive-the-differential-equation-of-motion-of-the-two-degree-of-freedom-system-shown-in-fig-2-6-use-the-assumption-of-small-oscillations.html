Guest Question.

Using Lagrange’s equation, derive the differential equation of motion of the two degree of freedom system shown in Fig. 2.6. Use the assumption of small oscillations.

Admin
Solution

We select x and \(\theta\) as the system generalized coordinates, that is, \(q=\left[x \theta \right]^{T}\) . The kinetic energy of the system is

\( T=\frac{1}{2}m\dot{x}^{2} + \frac{1}{2}m_{r}\dot{x} ^{2}_{c} + \frac{1}{2}I\dot{\theta }^{2}\)

Where \( m_{r}\) is the total mass of the rod, \(\dot{x}_{c}\) is the absolute velocity of the center of mass of the rod, and I is the mass moment of inertia of the rod about its mass center, that is, \( I=m_{r}l^{2}/ 12\), where l is the length of the rod. The absolute velocity of the center of mass of the rod can be expressed in terms of the generalized velocities as

\(\dot{x} _{c} \approx \dot{x} + \dot{\theta } \frac{1}{2}\)

where the assumption of small oscillation is utilized.

The kinetic energy T can then be written in terms of the generalized velocities as

\(T=\frac{1}{2}m\dot{x} ^{2} + \frac{1}{2} m_{r}\left(\dot{x}+\dot{\theta } \frac{1}{2} \right)^{2} + \frac{1}{2}I\dot{\theta } ^{2}\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{2}\left(m+m_{r} \right)\dot{x} ^{2} + m_{r}\frac{1}{2} \dot{x} \dot{\theta } + \frac{1}{2}\left(I+m_{r}\frac{l^{2} }{4} \right)\dot{\theta } ^{2}\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{2} \left(m+m_{r}\right)\dot{x} ^{2} +m_{r}\frac{1}{2} \dot{x} \dot{\theta } +\frac{1}{2}I_{O}\dot{\theta } ^{2}\)

 

\(=\frac{1}{2}\left[\dot{x} \dot{\theta } \right] \begin{bmatrix} m+m_{r} & m_{r}\left(l/ 2\right) \\ m_{r}\left(l/ 2\right) & I_{O} \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} \dot{x} \\ \dot{\theta } \end{matrix} \right]\)

where \(I_{O}\) is the mass moment of inertia of the rod about point O and is given by \(I_{O}=I + m_{r} \left(l^{2}/ 4 \right)\). The virtual work of all the forces acting on the system is given by

\(\delta W=\left[F\left(t\right)-kx-c\dot{x} \right]\delta x-m_{r}g\delta y_{c}\)

where g is the gravitational constant and \(\delta y_{c}\) is the virtual displacement of the center of mass in the vertical direction which is given by \(\delta y_{c}=\left(l/ 2\right)\sin \theta \delta \theta\) . Therefore,

\(\delta W=\left[F\left(t\right)-kx-c\dot{x} \right] \delta x-m_{r}g\frac{1}{2}\sin \theta \delta \theta\)

 

\(=Q_{x}\delta x+Q_{\theta }\delta \theta\)

where \( Q_{x}\) and \(Q_{\theta }\) are the generalized forces associated, respectively, with the generalized coordinates x and\(\theta\) and defined as

\(Q_{x}=F\left(t\right)-kx-c\dot{x}\)

 

\(Q_{\theta }=-m_{r}g\frac{1}{2}\sin \theta \approx -m_{r}g\frac{1}{2}\theta\)

The application of Lagrange’s equation yields the following two equations:

\(\frac{d}{dt} \left(\frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{x} }\right) -\frac{\partial T}{\partial x } = Q_x, \frac{d}{dt} \left(\frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{\theta } }\right) -\frac{\partial T}{\partial \theta } = Q_\theta\)

Differentiation of the kinetic energy with respect to time and with respect to the generalized coordinates yields

\(\frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{x} }=\left(m+m_{r} \right)\dot{x} + m_{r}\frac{1}{2} \dot{\theta }\)

 

\(\frac{d}{dt} \left(\frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{x} }\right) =\left(m+m_{r} \right)\ddot{x} +m_{r}\frac{1}{2}\ddot{\theta } , \frac{\partial T}{\partial x } =0\)

 

\(\frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{\theta } } = m_{r}\frac{1}{2}\dot{x} +I_{O}\dot{\theta} , \frac{d}{dt} \left(\frac{\partial T}{\partial \dot{\theta } }\right) = m_{r}\frac{1}{2}\ddot{x} + I_{O}\ddot{\theta} , \frac{\partial T}{\partial \theta } =0\)

Substituting these equations in Lagrange’s equation and using the expressions for the generalized forces yields

\(\left(m+m_{r} \right)\ddot{x} +m_{r}\frac{1}{2}\ddot{\theta }=F\left(t\right)-kx-c\dot{x}\)

\(m_{r}\frac{1}{2}\ddot{x } + I_{O}\ddot{\theta }=-m_{r}g\frac{1}{2}\theta\)

Or

\(\left(m+m_{r} \right)\ddot{x} +m_{r}\frac{1}{2}\ddot{\theta }+c\dot{x}+kx=F\left(t\right)\)

 

\(m_{r} \frac{1}{2} \ddot{x} +I_{O}\ddot{\theta }+m_{r}g\frac{1}{2}\theta =0\)

which can be written in a matrix form as

\(\begin{bmatrix} m+m_{r} & m_{r}\left(l/ 2\right) \\ m_{r}\left(l/ 2\right) & I_{O} \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix}\ddot{x} \\ \ddot{\theta } \end{matrix} \right] + \begin{bmatrix} c & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} \dot{x} \\ \dot{\theta } \end{matrix} \right] +\begin{bmatrix} k & 0 \\ 0 & m_{r}g\left(l/ 2\right) \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{matrix} x \\ \theta \end{matrix} \right] = \left[\begin{matrix} F\left(t\right) \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right]\)
