Guest Question.

Natural gas as a mixture of 75% methane and 25% ethane by mass is flowing to a compressor at 17째C, 100 kPa. The reversible adiabatic compressor brings the flow to 350 kPa. Find the exit temperature and the needed work per kg flow.

Admin
Solution

C.V. Compressor. Steady, adiabatic q = 0, reversible \(s _{ gen }=0\)

Energy Eq.4.13: \(– w = h _{ ex }- h _{ in }\) ; Entropy Eq.7.8: \(s _{ i }+ s _{ gen }= s _{ e }\)

Process: reversible \(\Rightarrow s _{ gen }=0 \Rightarrow s _{ e }= s _{ i }\)

Assume ideal gas mixture and constant heat capacity, so we need k and \(C _{ P }\)
From Eq.11.15 and 11.23:

\(\begin{aligned}& R _{ mix }=\sum c _{ i } R _{ i }=0.75 \times 0.5183+0.25 \times 0.2765=0.45785 kJ / kg K \\& C _{ P \operatorname{mix}}=\sum c _{ i } C _{ Pi }=0.75 \times 2.254+0.25 \times 1.766=2.132 kJ / kg K \\& C _{ V }= C _{ P \text { mix }}- R _{\operatorname{mix}}=2.132-0.45785=1.6742 kJ / kg K\end{aligned}\)

Ratio of specific heats: \(k = C _{ p } / C _{ v }=1.2734\)

The isentropic process gives Eq.6.23, from Eq.11.24 with \(s _{ e }= s _{ i }\)

\(T_{ e }= T _{ i }\left( P _{ e } / P _{ i }\right)^{( k -1) / k }=290(350 / 100)^{0.2147}= 3 7 9 . 5 K\)

Work from the energy equation:

\(w _{ c \text { in }}= C _{ P }\left( T _{ e }- T _{ i }\right)=2.132 kJ / kg – K (379.5-290) K = 1 9 0 . 8 kJ / kg\)

……………………………………………

Eq.4.13 : \(q+h_{i}+\frac{ V _{i}^{2}}{2}+g Z_{i}=h_{e}+\frac{ V _{e}^{2}}{2}+g Z_{e}+w\)

Eq.7.8 : \(\dot{m}\left(s_{e}-s_{i}\right)=\sum_{ c.s. } \frac{\dot{Q}_{ c.v. }}{T}+\dot{S}_{ gen }\)

Eq.11.15:
\(\begin{aligned}R_{\operatorname{mix}} &=\frac{1}{m}\left(n_{A} \bar{R}+n_{B} \bar{R}\right) \\&=\frac{1}{m}\left(m_{A} R_{A}+m_{B} R_{B}\right) \\&=c_{A} R_{A}+c_{B} R_{B}\end{aligned}\)

Eq.11.23 : \(C_{p 0 mix }=c_{A} C_{p 0 A}+c_{B} C_{p 0 B}\)

Eq.11.24 :

\(\begin{aligned}s_{2}-s_{1} &=c_{A}\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)_{A}+c_{B}\left(s_{2}-s_{1}\right)_{B} \\&=c_{A} C_{p 0 A} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-c_{A} R_{A} \ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}+c_{B} C_{p 0} \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-c_{B} R_{B} \ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} \\&=C_{p 0 mix } \ln \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}-R_{\operatorname{mix}} \ln \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\end{aligned}\)

Eq.6.23 : \(\frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}=\left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right)^{(k-1) / k}\)
