Mortgages (Confidence Interval)

In a particular city 1,118 mortgages were financed last year. A random sample of 60 of these had a mean amount $87,300 and standard deviation $19,200. Estimate the mean amount of all mortgages financed in this city last year, and find a 95% confidence interval.

Admin
Solution

Denote the population mean by m. We know that

N = 1,118 n = 60 x \( \overline{x } = \$ 87,300 \) s = 19,200

To obtain interval estimates, use Equation 7.11,

\( \hat{\sigma } ^{2}_{\overline{x } } =\frac{s^{2} }{n} \left(\frac{N-n}{N-1} \right) = \frac{(19,200)^{2} }{60} \left(\frac{1,085}{1,117} \right)= 5,819,474 \)

and take the square root to obtain the estimated standard error,

\( \hat{\sigma } ^{2}_{\overline{x } }= 2,412 \)

With \( t_{59,0.025} \cong 2.00 \) (Appendix Table 8) the margin of error of a 95% confidence interval for the mean amount of all mortgages financed in this city last year is calculated from Equation 7.13 as follows:

\( ME = t_{n-1,{\alpha }/{2}}\hat{\sigma } ^{2}_{\overline{x } }= 2(2,412) = \$ 4,824 \)

The resulting 95% confidence interval estimate for the mean amount of all mortgages financed in this city last year is

\( \$ 87,300 \pm \$ 4,824 \)

That is, the interval runs from $82,476 to $92,124.
