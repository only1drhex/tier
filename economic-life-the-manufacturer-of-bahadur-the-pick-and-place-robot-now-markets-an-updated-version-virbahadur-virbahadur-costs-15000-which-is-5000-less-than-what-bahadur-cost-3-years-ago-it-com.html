Guest Question.

Economic Life

The manufacturer of Bahadur, the pick-and-place robot, now markets an updated version Virbahadur. Virbahadur costs $15,000 which is $5,000 less than what Bahadur cost 3 years ago. It comes with free maintenance for the first 2 years. For the salvage values and maintenance costs given as follows, determine Virbahadur’s economic life, assuming \(i\) to be 10%.

Year Year-End Salvage Value Maintenance Cost
1 $13,000 $0
2 $10,000 $0
3 $ 6,000 $200
4 $ 4,500 $300
5 $ 3,000 $800
6 $ 1,500 $200
7 $0 $300
Admin
Solution

The analysis of the challenger is similar to that of the defender; hence the procedure discussed in is applicable. Let us follow the equation-based approach. However, keep the appropriate cashflow diagrams in mind while determining the maintenance EUACs.

The economic life corresponds to the minimum EUAC. Each year’s EUAC is obtained by determining the EUAC of the capital recovery using Equation (12.1) and adding it to the corresponding maintenance EUAC.

\(EUAC_{n} =P({A}/{P},i,n)-S({A}/{F},i,n)\)

 

\(=P({A}/{P},i,n)-S\left\{({A}/{P},i,n)-i\right\}\)

 

\(=(P-S)({A}/{P},i,n)-iS\)

 

EUACs of capital recovery:

\(EUAC_{1 capital recovery} =(P-S_{1} ) ({A}/{P},10\%,1)+0.1S_{1}\)

 

\(=(15,000-13,000) \times 1.1+0.1\times 13,000\)

 

\(=2,200+1,300\)

 

\(=\$3,500\)

 

\(EUAC_{2 capital recovery} =(P-S_{2} ) ({A}/{P},10\%,2)+0.1S_{2}\)

 

\(=(15,000-10,000)\times 0.5762+0.1\times 10,000\)

 

\(=2,881+1,000\)

 

\(=\$3,881\)

 

\(EUAC_{3 capital recovery} =(P-S_{3} ) ({A}/{P},10\%,3)+0.1S_{3}\)

 

\(=(15,000-6,000) \times 0.4021+0.1\times 6,000\)

 

\(=3,619+600\)

 

\(=\$4,219\)

 

\(EUAC_{4 capital recovery} =(P-S_{4} ) ({A}/{P},10\%,4)+0.1S_{4}\)

 

\(=(15,000-4,500)\times 0.3155+0.1\times 4,500\)

 

\(=3,313+450\)

 

\(=\$3,763\)

 

\(EUAC_{5 capital recovery} =(P-S_{5} ) ({A}/{P},10\%,5)+0.1S_{5}\)

 

\(=(15,000-3,000)\times 0.2638+0.1\times 3,000\)

 

\(=3,166+300\)

 

\(=\$3,466\)

 

\(EUAC_{6 capital recovery} =(P-S_{6} ) ({A}/{P},10\%,6)+0.1S_{6}\)

 

\(=(15,000-1,500)\times 0.2296+0.1\times 1,500\)

 

\(=3,100+150\)

 

\(=\$3,250\)

 

\(EUAC_{7 capital recovery} =(P-S_{7} ) ({A}/{P},10\%,7)+0.1S_{7}\)

 

\(=(15,000-0)\times 0.2054+0.1\times 0\)

 

\(=3,081+0\)

 

\(=\$3,081\)

 

EUACs of maintenance\(^{19}\) :

 

\(EUAC_{1 maintenance}\) = 0 (given)

 

\(EUAC_{2 maintenance}\) = 0 (given)

 

\(EUAC_{3 maintenance} =200({A}/{F},10\%,3)\)

 

\(=200+0.3021\)

 

\(=\$60\)

 

\(EUAC_{4 maintenance} =\left[200({F}/{P},10\%,1)+300\right] ({A}/{F},10\%,4)\)

 

\(=(200\times 1.1+300)\times 0.2155\)

 

\(=520\times 0.2155\)

 

\(=\$112\)

 

\(EUAC_{5 maintenance} = \left[200({F}/{P},10\%,2)+300({F}/{P},10\%,1)+800\right] ({A}/{F},10\%,5)\)

 

\(=(200\times 1.21+300\times 1.1+800)\times 0.1638\)

 

\(=1,372\times 0.1638\)

 

\(=\$225\)

 

\(EUAC_{6 maintenance} =\left[200({F}/{P},10\%,3)+300({F}/{P},10\%,2)+800({F}/{P},10\%,1)+200\right] ({A}/{F},10\%,6)\)

 

\(=(200\times 1.331+300\times 1.21+800\times 1.1+200)\times 0.1296\)

 

\(=1,709\times 0.1296\)

 

\(=\$221\)

 

\(EUAC_{7 maintenance} =\left[200({F}/{P},10\%,4)+300({F}/{P},10\%,3)+800({F}/{P},10\%,2)+200({F}/{P},10\%,1)+300\right] ({A}/{F},10\%,7)\)

 

\(=(200\times 1.464+300\times 1.331+800\times 1.21+200\times 1.1+300) 0.1054\)

 

\(=2,180\times 0.1054\)

 

\(=\$230\)

Carefully post these EUACs in their columns and add them for each year to determine the total EUAC, as follows.

The economic life is obtained by browsing through the data in the last column and locating the minimum EUAC. This is evidently 7 years corresponding to the EUAC of $3,311.

\(^{19}\)Be careful while evaluating maintenance EUACs for fourth through seventh year. What is within the bracket [ ] yields the P-value at year 0, which is then converted into annual value.
