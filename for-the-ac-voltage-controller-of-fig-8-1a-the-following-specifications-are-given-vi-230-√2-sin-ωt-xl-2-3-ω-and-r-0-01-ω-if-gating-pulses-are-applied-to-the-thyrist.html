Guest Question.

For the ac voltage controller of Fig. 8.1(a), the following specifications are given:

\(v_i = 230 \sqrt{2} \sin \omega t, X_L = 2.3 \Omega\), and \(R = 0.01 \Omega\). If gating pulses are applied to the thyristors and the circuit has reached its steady-state operation, draw the thyristor voltage, the output voltage, and current for three different values of firing angles \(\alpha = 0° , \alpha =\frac{\pi}{2}\) and \(\alpha =\frac{3\pi}{4}\). Also, calculate:

a) The angle a at which the maximum positive or negative thyristor voltage occurs.

b) The maximum rms thyristor current.

c) The average thyristor current.

d) The regulation range for the particular load.

Admin
Solution

The load angle is \(\varphi = tan^{-1}(\frac{2.3}{0.01}) \cong 90°\) .

Knowing that the maximum value of the conduction angle g is 180° and the load angle \(\varphi\) = 90°, then from the curves of Fig. 3.12 of Chapter 3 the following results re obtained:

\(\alpha = 0°\)

\(\gamma = 180°\)

 \(\alpha = \frac{\pi}{2}\)

\(\gamma = 180°\)

 \(\alpha = \frac{3\pi}{4}\)

\(\gamma = 100°\)

Therefore, for \(\alpha = 0°\) and \(\alpha = \frac{\pi}{2}\) each thyristor conducts for 180° and so their voltages are equal to zero while the output current is a clear sinusoidal waveform. Fig. 8.7 shows the waveforms of the ac controller with the above specifications and when \(\alpha = \frac{3\pi}{4}\).

a) According to Fig. 8.7 the maximum thyristor voltage occurs when \(\frac{\pi}{2} \lt \alpha \lt \pi\) and is given by:

\(\widehat{V}_Q = \widehat{V}_{AK} = \sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i = 230\sqrt{2} = 325.3 V\)

b) According to Fig. 8.7 the maximum thyristor rms current occurs when \(\gamma = 180°\) and, consequently, the circuit current is a pure sinusoidal waveform and through each thyristor one current pulse is passing. From the results of the previous table, it can be concluded that the circuit current is sinusoidal when \(0\lt \alpha \lt \frac{\pi}{2}\) . Therefore, the maximum thyristor rms current is given by:

\(\widetilde{I}_{Q(max)} = \frac{\widetilde{I}}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{\frac{\widetilde{V}_i}{\sqrt{R^2 + (\omega L)^2}}}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{\frac{230}{\sqrt{0.01^2 + (2.3)^2}}}{\sqrt{2}} = 70.7 A\)

c) The maximum thyristor average current occurs when \(\gamma = 180°\) where the circuit current is a pure sinusoidal waveform and through each thyristor one current pulse is passing. Therefore, the maximum average thyristor current occurs when \(0\lt \alpha \lt \frac{\pi}{2}\) and is given by:

\(\overline{I}_{Q(max)} = \frac{Area of one current pulse}{period} = \frac{\int_{0}^{\pi}{\widehat{I}\sin (\omega t)d\omega t } }{2\pi} = \frac{\widehat{I} (-\cos \omega t)|^{\pi} _0}{2\pi} = \frac{2 \widehat{I}}{2 \pi}\)

 

\(= \frac{\widehat{I}}{\pi} = \frac{\frac{230 \sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{0.01^2 + 2.3^2}}}{\pi} = 45 A\)

d) From the above results, it can be concluded that the voltage regulation range is \(\frac{\pi}{2} \lt \alpha \lt \pi\).
