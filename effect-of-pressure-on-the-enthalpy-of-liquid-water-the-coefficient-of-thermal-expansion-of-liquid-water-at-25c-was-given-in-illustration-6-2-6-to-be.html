Guest Question.

Effect of Pressure on the Enthalpy of Liquid Water

The coefficient of thermal expansion of liquid water at 25°C was given in to be

 

\(\alpha=\frac{1}{\hat{V}}\left(\frac{\partial \hat{V}}{\partial T}\right)_{P}=2.56 \times 10^{-4} \mathrm{~K}\)

 

Use this information and the fact that \(\hat{V}\)25°C, saturated liquid at 3.169 kPa)=0.001003 \(\mathrm{~m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg}\) and \(\hat{H}\)(25°C, saturated liquid at 3.169 kPa) = 104.89 kJ/kg to determine the enthalpy of liquid water at (a) 25°C and 1 bar and (b) 25°C and 10 bar.

Admin
Solution

Using Eq. 6.4-33, on a mass basis we have

\(\underline{H}(T, P+\Delta P)-\underline{H}(T, P)=\underline{V}[1-T \alpha] \Delta P\) 6.4-33

 

 

\(\hat{H}\left(25^{\circ} \mathrm{C}, 1 \mathrm{bar}\right) =\hat{H}\left(25^{\circ} \mathrm{C}, 3.169 \mathrm{kPa}\right)+\hat{V}[1-T \alpha](100-3.169) \mathrm{kPa} \)

 

\(=104.89 \frac{\mathrm{kJ}}{\mathrm{kg}}+1.003 \times 10^{-3} \frac{\mathrm{m}^{3}}{\mathrm{~kg}}\left[1-298.15 \times 2.56 \times 10^{-4}\right] \times 96.83 \mathrm{kPa} \)

 

\(=104.89 \frac{\mathrm{kJ}}{\mathrm{kg}}+0.0897 \frac{\mathrm{Pa} \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{kg}} \times 1 \frac{\mathrm{kJ}}{\mathrm{kPa} \mathrm{m} 3}=104.98 \frac{\mathrm{kJ}}{\mathrm{kg}}\) (a)

 

and

\(\hat{H}\left(25^{\circ} \mathrm{C}, 10 \mathrm{bar}\right) =104.89+1.003 \times 10^{-3}\left[1-298.15 \times 2.56 \times 10^{-4}\right](1000-3.169) \)

 

\(=104.89+0.92=105.81 \frac{\mathrm{kJ}}{\mathrm{kg}}\) (b)

 

COMMENT

Here we see that for modest pressure changes, the liquid enthalpy changes little with pressure. Therefore, little error is incurred if, at fixed temperature, one uses the saturated liquid enthalpy in the steam tables at higher pressures. However, this will lead to errors near the critical point, where the density of the liquid changes rapidly with small changes in both temperature and pressure.
