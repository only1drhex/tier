Guest Question.

A four-stroke automotive gasoline engine has a stroke of 86.4 mm and is running at 6000 rev/min. The connecting rod has a mass of 0.55 kg, center-to-center length of 144 mm, and the center of mass is located 37 mm from the big-end center. The piston has a bore diameter of 74 mm, mass of 0.5 kg, and the peak cylinder pressure over the four-stroke cycle is 7 MPa. The big-end rod bearing has a diameter of 50.6 mm, length 25.3 mm, radial clearance 20 μm, and the dynamic viscosity of the oil is 4.332 mPa · s. Determinewhether the bearing specifications are acceptable for this application

Admin
Solution

The first step is to represent the connecting rod as two point masses. Assuming that the actual connecting rod and the model have the same mass and center of mass, the small end mass \(M_{s}\) and big-end mass\( M_{b}\) have values

\(M_{s} =\frac{ M\bar{x}}{L} =\frac{ (0.55)(0.037)}{(0.144)} = 0.14 kg\)

 

\(M_{b} = M − M_{s} \)= 0.55 − 0.14 = 0.41 kg

The reciprocating and rotating masses and their ratio are thus

\(M_{rec} = M_{s} + M_{piston}\) = 0.14 + 0.5 = 0.64 kg

 

\(M_{rot} = M_{b} = 0.41 kg\)

 

\(M_{rec}∕M_{rot}\) = 0.64∕0.41 = 1.57

Next, determine whether the chart, Figure 12–38, is applicable.

\(A_{cyl} =\frac{ πd^{2}_{bore}}{4} =\frac{ π(0.074^{2})}{4} = 0.0043 m^{2}\)

 

\(ω = \frac{2πN}{60} =\frac{ 2π(6000)}{60} = 628.32 rad/s\)

 

\(\frac{P_{cyl}A_{cyl}}{F_{rot}}=\frac{ P_{cyl}A_{cyl}}{M_{rot}Rω^{2}} =\frac{ 7(10^{6})(0.0043)}{(0.41)(0.0432)(628.32)^{2}} = 4.3 < 7 OK\)

 

\(0 ≤ M_{rec}∕M_{rot}\) = 1.57 ≤ 2 OK

0.2 ≤ R∕L = 0.0432∕0.144 = 0.3 ≤ 0.4 OK

Now,

\(\frac{8M_{rot}Rωc^{2}}{μl^{3}d} =\frac{ 8(0.41)(0.0432)(628.32)[(20)(10^{−6})]^{2}}{(0.004332)(0.0253^{3})(0.0506) }= 10.0\)

From Figure 12–38, \(h_{min}∕c ≈ 0.16, so h_{min} ≈ (0.16) [20(10^{−6})] = 3.2(10^{−6}) m\), or 3.2 μm. From Table 12–7,\( h_{min}\) > 1 μm, so the bearing specifications are acceptable for this application.
