Guest Question.

Carbon has the following three allotropes: graphite, diamond, and a metallic form called solid III. Graphite is the stable form of 298 K and 1 atm pressure, and increasing the pressure on graphite at temperatures less than 1440 K causes the transformation of graphite to diamond and then the transformation of diamond to solid III. Calculate the pressure which, when applied to one mole graphite at 298 K, causes the transformation of graphite to diamond, given

• \(H_{298 K, (graphite)}-H_{298 K,(diamond)}=-1900\) J.

• \(S_{298 K, (graphite)}=5.74\) J/K.

• \(S_{298 K, (diamond)}=2.37\) J/K.

• The density of graphite at 298 K is \(2.22 g/cm^3\).

• The density of diamond at 298 K is \(3.515 g/cm^3\).

Admin
Solution

For the transformation graphite → diamond at 298 K:

\(\Delta G=\Delta H-T\Delta S\)

 

\(=1900-298(2.37-5.74)=2904\) J

For the transformation of graphite to diamond at any temperature T :

\(\left\lgroup\frac{\partial \Delta G_{graphite\rightarrow diamond}}{\partial P} \right\rgroup _T=\Delta V_{graphite\rightarrow diamond}\)

 

\(V_{graphite}=\frac{12}{2.22}=5.405 cm^3 /mole \)

and

\(V_{diamond} =\frac{12}{3.515}=3.415 cm^3 /mole \)

Thus,

\(\Delta V=-1.99 cm^3 /mole \)

Equilibrium between graphite and diamond at 298 K requires that \(\Delta G_{graphite→ diamond}\) be zero. Since

\(\left\lgroup\frac{\partial \Delta G}{\partial P} \right\rgroup _T=\Delta V\)

then

\(\Delta G(P,T=298)=\Delta G(P=1,T=298)+\int_{1}^{p}{\Delta VdP} \)

If the difference between the isothermal compressibilities of the two phases is negligibly small (i.e., if the influence of pressure on Δ V can be ignored), then

\(1 cm^3 .atm =\frac{8.3144}{82.057} =0.1013\) J

 

\(\Delta G(P,T=298)=2904+(-1.99\times 0.1013)(P-1)\)

and thus,

\((P-1)=P=\frac{2904}{1.99\times 0.1013}=14,400 atm \)

Transformation of graphite to diamond at 298 K requires the application of a pressure greater than 14,400 atm.
