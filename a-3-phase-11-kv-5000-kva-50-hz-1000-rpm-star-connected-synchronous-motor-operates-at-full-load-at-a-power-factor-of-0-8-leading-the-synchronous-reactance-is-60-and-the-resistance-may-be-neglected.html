Guest Question.

A 3-phase, 11 kV, 5000 kVA, 50 Hz, 1000 rpm star-connected synchronous motor operates at full load at a power factor of 0.8 leading. The synchronous reactance is 60% and the resistance may be neglected. Calculate the synchronising power and torque per mechanical degree of angular displacement. What is the value of maximum torque and the ratio of maximum to full-load torque?

Admin
Solution

Here, \( Rating = 5000 kVA; f = 50 Hz; N_{s} = 1000 rpm; \cos \phi = 0.8 leading; \)

\(X_{s} = 60\% ; \delta_{mech} = 1^{\circ }; V_{L} = 11 kV \)

 

\(I = \frac{kVA\times 10^{3}}{\sqrt{3} V_{L} } = \frac{5000\times 10^{3}}{\sqrt{3} \times 11\times 10^{3}}= 262.4 A \)

 

Phase voltage, \(V = \frac{ V_{L}}{\sqrt{3} } = \frac{11\times 10^{3}}{\sqrt{3}}= 6351 V\)

Now, \( \% X_{s} = \frac{IX_{s} }{V}\times 100\) or \(X_{s} = \frac{\% X_{s}}{100}\times \frac{V}{I} = \frac{60}{100}\times \frac{6351}{262.4} =14.52 \Omega \)

\( \overline{E} = \overline{V} – \overline{I} \overline{Z}_{s} = V – (1 \angle \phi ) (X_{s}\angle 90^{\circ }) \\[0.5cm] \quad =\left(V – I X_{S} \sin \phi \right)- j I X_{s} \cos \phi \\[0.5cm] \quad =\left(6351 + 262.4 \times 14.52 \times 0.6\right) – j 262.4 \times 14.52 \times 0.8 \\[0.5cm] \quad =\left(6351 + 2286\right)– j 3048 = 8637 – j 3048 \\[0.5cm] \quad = 9159 \angle –19.44^{\circ } V \)

 

\(\therefore E = 9159 V, \delta = –19.44^{\circ } \)

 

Now, \( f = \frac{PN_{s}}{120} \)

or \( P = \frac{120\times f}{N_{s}}= \frac{120\times 50}{1000} = 6 ;\)

or pair of poles, \( P = \frac{P}{2}= \frac{6}{2} = 3 \)

Synchronising power, \(P_{syn} = \left\lgroup\frac{3 V E}{X_{s}} \cos \delta \right\rgroup P \frac{\pi }{180} \\[0.5cm] \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} \qquad \quad \quad \quad \enspace = \frac{3\times 6351 \times 9159 }{14.52} \left(\cos 19.44^{\circ } \right) \times \frac{3\pi }{180}=\mathbf{593404 W }\)

Synchronising torque, \(T_{syn} = \frac{P_{syn}}{2\pi N_{s} / 60} = \frac{593404 }{2\pi \times \frac{1000}{60} } \\[0.5cm] \hspace{30 pt} \hspace{30 pt} \qquad \quad \quad \quad \enspace = \mathbf{5666.6 Nm per mech degree }\)

Maximum power, \(P_{\max } = \frac{3 V E}{X_{s}} = \frac{3 \times 6352 \times 9159 }{14.52 } =12018349 W \)

\( Maximum torque = \frac{P_{\max }}{2\pi N_{s}/ 60} = \frac{12018349}{2\pi \times \frac{1000}{60} } = \mathbf{114767 Nm } \)

 

\( We know that full-load torque = maximum torque \times \sin \delta \)

 

\(\therefore \) Ration of max. and full-load torque, i.e.,

\(\frac{maximum torque}{full load torque} = \frac{1}{\sin \delta } = \frac{1}{\sin 19.44^{\circ }}=\mathbf{3} \)
