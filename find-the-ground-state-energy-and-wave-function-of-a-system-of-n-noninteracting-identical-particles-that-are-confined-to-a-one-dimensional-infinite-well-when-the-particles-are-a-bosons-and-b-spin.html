Guest Question.

Find the ground state energy and wave function of a system of N noninteracting identical particles that are confined to a one-dimensional, infinite well when the particles are (a) bosons and (b) spin \(\frac{1}{2} \) fermions.

Admin
Solution

In the case of a particle moving in an infinite well, its energy and wave function are \(\varepsilon _{n} =n^{2} \hbar ^{2} \pi ^{2} /(2m a^{2} )\) and \(\psi _{n} =(x_{i} )=\sqrt{2/a} \sin (n\pi x_{i} /2)\).

(a) In the case where the N particles are bosons, the ground state is obtained by putting all the particles in the state n = 1; the energy and wave function are then given by

\(E^{(0)} =\varepsilon _{1}+\varepsilon _{1}+\varepsilon _{1}+ …+\varepsilon _{1}=N\varepsilon _{1}=\frac{N\hbar ^{2} \pi ^{2}}{2ma^{2}} \), (8.104)

 

\(\psi ^{(0)} (x_{1},x_{2},…,x_{N})=\prod\limits_{i=1}^{N} \sqrt{\frac{2}{a} } \sin \left(\frac{\pi }{2} x_{i} \right) = \sqrt{\frac {2^{N} }{a^{N} } } \sin \left(\frac{\pi }{2} x_{1} \right) \sin \left(\frac {\pi }{2} x_{2} \right) … \sin \left(\frac{\pi }{2} x_{N} \right)\). (8.105)

(b) In the case where the N particles are spin \(\frac{1}{2} \) fermions, each level can be occupied by at most two particles having different spin states \(\left|\frac{1}{2} ,\pm \frac{1}{2} \right \rangle \). The ground state is thus obtained by distributing the N particles among the N/2 lowest levels at a rate of two particles per level:

\(E^{(0)} =2\varepsilon _{1}+2\varepsilon _{2}+2\varepsilon _{3}+…+2\varepsilon _{N/2}=2 \sum\limits_{n=1}^{N/2} \frac {n^{2} \hbar ^{2} \pi ^{2}}{2ma^{2}} =\frac{\hbar ^{2} \pi ^{2}}{ma^{2}}\sum\limits_{n=1}^{N/2} n^{2}\), (8.106)

If N is large we may calculate \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^{N/2} n^{2} \) by using the approximation

\(\sum\limits_{n=1}^{N/2} n^{2}\simeq \int_{1}^{N/2} n^{2} dn\simeq \frac{1}{3} \left(\frac{N}{2} \right) ^{3} \); (8.107)

hence the ground state energy will be given by

\(E^{(0)} \simeq N^{3} \frac{\hbar ^{2} \pi ^{2}}{24ma^{2}} \). (8.108)

The average energy per particle is \(E^{(0)} /N\simeq N^{2}\hbar ^{2} \pi ^{2} /(24ma^{2} )\). In the casewhere N is even, a possible configuration of the ground state wave function \(\psi ^{(0)} (x_{1},x_{2},…,x_{N})\) is given as follows:

\(\frac{1}{\sqrt{N!} } \left|\begin{matrix} \psi _{1} (x_{1} )\chi (S_{1} ) & \psi _{1} (x_{2} )\chi (S_{2} ) & \ldots & \psi _{1} (x_{N} )\chi (S_{N} ) \\ \psi _{1} (x_{1} )\chi (S_{1} ) & \psi _{1} (x_{2} )\chi (S_{2} ) & \ldots & \psi _{1} (x_{N} )\chi (S_{N} ) \\ \psi _{2} (x_{1} )\chi (S_{1} ) & \psi _{2} (x_{2} )\chi (S_{2} ) & \ldots & \psi _{2} (x_{N} )\chi (S_{N} ) \\ \psi _{2} (x_{1} )\chi (S_{1} ) & \psi _{2} (x_{2} )\chi (S_{2} ) & \ldots & \psi _{2} (x_{N} )\chi (S_{N} ) \\ \psi _{3} (x_{1} )\chi (S_{1} ) & \psi _{3} (x_{2} )\chi (S_{2} ) & \ldots & \psi _{3} (x_{N} )\chi (S_{N} ) \\ \psi _{3} (x_{1} )\chi (S_{1} ) & \psi _{3} (x_{2} )\chi (S_{2} ) & \ldots & \psi _{3} (x_{N} )\chi (S_{N} ) \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \psi _{N/2} (x_{1} )\chi (S_{1} ) & \psi _{N/2} (x_{2} )\chi (S_{2} ) & \ldots & \psi _{N/2} (x_{N} )\chi (S_{N} ) \\ \psi _{N/2} (x_{1} )\chi (S_{1} ) & \psi _{N/2} (x_{2} )\chi (S_{2} ) & \ldots & \psi _{N/2} (x_{N} )\chi (S_{N} ) \end{matrix} \right|\), (8.109)

where \(\chi (S_{i} )=\left|\frac{1}{2} ,\pm \frac{1}{2} \right \rangle \) is the spin state of the ith particle, with \(i=1, 2, 3, …,N\) . If N is odd then we need to remove the last row of the determinant.
