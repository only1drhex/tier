Guest Question.

One of the main girders of a bridge is the truss shown in Fig. P.20.12. Loads are transmitted to the truss through cross beams attached at the lower panel points. The self-weight of the truss is 30 kN/m and it carries a live load of intensity 15 kN/m and of length greater than the span. Draw influence lines for the force in each of the members CE and DE and determine their maximum values.
Ans. CE = +37.3 kN, -65.3 kN, DE = +961.2 kN.

Admin
Solution

When unit load is to the right of E, \(F_{ CE }\) may be found by taking moments about the intersection of GC and ED produced and is then equal to \(0.95 R_{ A }\). When unit load is to the left of D, \(F_{ CE }\) may be found by taking moments about the intersection of GH and DE produced. Then, \(F_{ CE }=-2.085 R_{ B }\). The IL is then as shown in Fig. S.20.12(a). Further, \(F_{ DE }=M_{ C } 3\) and the IL is as shown in Fig. S.20.12(b).

Then

\(F_{ CE }(\max \cdot \operatorname{pos})=-30\left(0.695 \times \frac{10.38}{2}\right)+45\left(0.475 \times \frac{13.62}{2}\right)=37.3 kN\)

 

\(F_{ CE }(\max . \text { neg })=-45\left(0.695 \times \frac{10.38}{2}\right)+30\left(0.475 \times \frac{13.62}{2}\right)=-65.3 kN\)

 

\(F_{ DE }=45 \times 24 \times \frac{1.78}{2}=961.2 kN\).
