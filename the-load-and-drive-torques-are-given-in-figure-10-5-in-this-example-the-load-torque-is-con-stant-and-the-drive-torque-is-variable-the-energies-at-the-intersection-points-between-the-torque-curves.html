Guest Question.

The load and drive torques are given in Figure 10.5. In this example, the load torque is con-stant, and the drive torque is variable.
The energies at the intersection points between the torque curves are listed in Table 10.2. Intersection point 1 is given an arbitrary energy level of E.

TABLE 10.2 ENERGY VALUES AT THE INTERSECTION POINTS IN FIGURE 10.5
Point Energy Level (N-m)
1 \(E_{1}\)
2 \(E_{2}= E-1600 \)
3 \(E_{3} = E + 3200 \)
4 \(E_{4} = E -800 \)
5 \(E_{5} = E + 4800 \)
6 \(E_{6} = E + 1500 \)
7 \(E_{7} = E + 3400 \)

Solution

Examination of Table 10.2 shows that the maximum energy level corresponds to point 5, and the minimum energy level is at point 2. Thus

TABLE 10.2 ENERGY VALUES AT THE INTERSECTION POINTS IN FIGURE 10.5
Point Energy Level (N-m)
1 \(E_{1}\)
2 \(E_{2}= E-1600 \)
3 \(E_{3} = E + 3200 \)
4 \(E_{4} = E -800 \)
5 \(E_{5} = E + 4800 \)
6 \(E_{6} = E + 1500 \)
7 \(E_{7} = E + 3400 \)
\(∆E_{max} = E_{5} – E_{2} = [( E + 4800 ) – (E – 1600 ) ] N-m = 6400 N-m \)

Substituting in Equation (10.2-13), we obtain

\( I =\frac{∆E_{max}}{ω_{0}^{2} C_{S}} = \frac{6400 N-m}{\left\lgroup800 \times \frac{2 \pi }{60} \right\rgroup^{2} \frac{rad^{2}}{sec^{2}} \times 0.02} = 45.5 N-m-sec^{2} =45.5 kg-m^{2}\)
