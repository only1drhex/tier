Guest Question.

Find a basis for the null space of the matrix

\( A = \left [ \begin{matrix} -3 & 6 & -1 & 1 & -7 \\ 1 & -2 & 2 & 3 & -1 \\ 2 & -4 & 5 & 8 & -4 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Admin
Solution

First, write the solution of Ax = 0 in parametric vector form:

\( [A 0] \sim \left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & -2 & 0 & -1 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 2 & -2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] , \begin{matrix} x_{1} – 2x_{2} – x_{4} + 3x_{5} & =0 \\ x_{3} + 2x_{4} – 2x_{5} & =0 \\ 0 & =0 \end{matrix} \)

The general solution is \( x_{1} = 2x_{2} + x_{4} – 3x_{5} , x_{3} = -2x_{4} + 2x_{5} \), with \( x_{2} \), \( x_{4} \), and \( x_{5} \) free.

\( \left [ \begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{3} \\ x_{4} \\ x_{5} \end{matrix} \right ] = \left [ \begin{matrix} 2x_{2} + x_{4} – 3x_{5} \\ x_{2} \\ -2x_{4} + 2x_{5} \\ x_{4} \\ x_{5} \end{matrix} \right ] = x_{2}\underset{\overset{\uparrow}{u}}{\left [ \begin{matrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ]} + x_{4}\underset{\overset{\uparrow}{v}}{\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 0 \\-2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right ]} + x_{5}\underset{\overset{\uparrow}{w}}{\left [ \begin{matrix} -3 \\ 0 \\ 2 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right ]} \)

\( = x_{2}u + x_{4}v + x_{5}w \) (1)

Equation (1) shows that Nul A coincides with the set of all linear combinations of u, v, and w. That is, {u, v, w} generates Nul A. In fact, this construction of u, v, and w automatically makes them linearly independent, because equation (1) shows that \( 0 = x_{2}u + x_{4}v + x_{5}w \) only if the weights \( x_{2} \), \( x_{4} \), and \( x_{5} \) are all zero. (Examine entries 2, 4, and 5 in the vector \( x_{2}u + x_{4}v + x_{5}w \).) So {u, v, w} is a basis for Nul A.
