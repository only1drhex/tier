Guest Question.

Energy and Cost Savings by Fixing Air Leaks

The compressors of a production facility maintain the compressed-air lines at a (gauge) pressure of 700 kPa at sea level where the atmospheric pressure is 101 kPa (Fig. 7–74). The average temperature of air is 20°C at the compressor inlet and 24°C in the compressed-air lines. The facility operates 4200 hours a year, and the average price of electricity is $0.078/kWh. Taking the compressor efficiency to be 0.8, the motor efficiency to be 0.92, and the discharge coefficient to be 0.65, determine the energy and money saved per year by sealing a leak equivalent to a 3-mm-diameter hole on the compressed-air line.

Admin
Solution

An air leak in the compressed air lines of a facility is considered. The energy and money saved per year by sealing the leak are to be determined.

Assumptions 1 Steady operating conditions exist. 2 Air is an ideal gas. 3 Pressure losses in the compressed air lines are negligible.

Analysis We note that the absolute pressure is the sum of the gauge and atmospheric pressures.

The work needed to compress a unit mass of air at 20°C from the atmospheric pressure of 101 kPa to 700 + 101 = 801 kPa is

\(\begin{aligned}w_{\text {comp,in }} &=\frac{n R T_{1}}{\eta_{\text {comp }}(n-1)}\left[\left(\frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}}\right)^{(n-1) / n}-1\right] \\&=\frac{(1.4)(0.287 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K})(293 \mathrm{~K})}{(0.8)(1.4-1)}\left[\left(\frac{801 \mathrm{kPa}}{101 \mathrm{kPa}}\right)^{0.4 / 1.4}-1\right]=296.9 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}\end{aligned}\)

The cross-sectional area of the 3-mm-diameter hole is

\(A=\pi D^{2} / 4=\pi\left(3 \times 10^{-3} \mathrm{~m}\right)^{2} / 4=7.069 \times 10^{-6} \mathrm{~m}^{2}\)

Noting that the line conditions are \(297 \mathrm{~K}\) and \(801 \mathrm{kPa}\), the mass flow rate of the air leaking through the hole is determined to be

\(\begin{aligned}\dot{m}_{\text {air }}=& C_{\text {discharge }}\left(\frac{2}{k+1}\right)^{1 /(k-1)} \frac{P_{\text {line }}}{R T_{\text {line }}} A \sqrt{k R\left(\frac{2}{k+1}\right) T_{\text {line }}} \\=&(0.65)\left(\frac{2}{1.4+1}\right)^{1 /(1.4-1)} \frac{801 \mathrm{kPa}}{\left(0.287 \mathrm{kPa} \cdot \mathrm{m}^{3} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K}\right)(297 \mathrm{~K})}\left(7.069 \times 10^{-6} \mathrm{~m}^{2}\right) \\& \times \sqrt{(1.4)(0.287 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg} \cdot \mathrm{K})\left(\frac{1000 \mathrm{~m}^{2} / \mathrm{s}^{2}}{1 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}}\right)\left(\frac{2}{1.4+1}\right)(297 \mathrm{~K})} \\=& 0.008632 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s}\end{aligned}\)

Then, the power wasted by the leaking compressed air becomes

\(\begin{aligned}\text { Power wasted } &=\dot{m}_{\text {air }} w_{\text {comp,in }} \\&=(0.008632 \mathrm{~kg} / \mathrm{s})(296.9 \mathrm{~kJ} / \mathrm{kg}) \\&=2.563 \mathrm{~kW}\end{aligned}\)

The compressor operates \(4200 \mathrm{~h} / \mathrm{yr}\), and the motor efficiency is \(0.92\). Then the annual energy and cost savings resulting from repairing this leak are determined to be

\(\begin{aligned}\text { Energy savings } &=(\text { Power saved })(\text { operatings hours }) / \eta_{\text {motor }} \\&=(2.563 \mathrm{~kW})(4200 \mathrm{~h} / \mathrm{yr}) / 0.92 \\&=11,700 \mathrm{kWh} / \mathrm{yr}\end{aligned}\)

\(\begin{aligned}\text { Cost savings}&=(\text{ Energy savings}) (\text{Unit cost of energy})\\&=(11,700 \mathrm{kWh} / \mathrm{yr})(\$ 0.078 / \mathrm{kWh}) \\&=\$ 913 / \mathrm{yr}\end{aligned}\)

Discussion Note that the facility will save \(11,700 \mathrm{kWh}\) of electricity worth \(\$ 913\) a year when this air leak is fixed. This is a substantial amount for a single leak whose equivalent diameter is \(3 \mathrm{~mm}\).
