Guest Question.

Design a cast iron piston for a single acting four-stroke diesel engine with the following data: Cylinder bore = 200 mm
Length of stroke = 250 mm
Speed = 600 rpm

Brake mean effective pressure = 0.60 MPa
Maximum gas pressure = 4 MPa
Fuel consumption = 0.25 kg per BP per h (l/d) ratio for bush in small end of connecting
rod = 1.5
Assume suitable data if required and state the assumptions you make.

Admin
Solution

Given D = 200 mm l = 250 mm = 0.25 m.

\( N=600 rpm \quad\left(p_{m}\right)_{b}=0.6 MPa =0.6 N / mm ^{2} \).

\( p_{\max .}=4 MPa =4 N / mm ^{2} \).

m = 0.25 kg per BP per h (l/d) for bush = 1.5

Step I Piston head or crown
Assumption No. 1 The permissible tensile stress for cast iron piston is 40 N/mm².
From Eq. 25.16, the thickness of the piston head by strength consideration is given by,

\( t_{h}=D \sqrt{\frac{3}{16} \frac{p_{\max .}}{\sigma_{b}}} \) (25,16)

\( t_{h}=D \sqrt{\frac{3}{16} \frac{p_{\max .}}{\sigma_{b}}}=200 \sqrt{\frac{3}{16} \frac{(4)}{(40)}}=27.39 mm \) (a).

For a four-stroke engine,

\( n=\frac{N}{2}=\frac{600}{2}=300 strokes / min \).

\( BP =\frac{\left(p_{m}\right)_{b} l A n}{60}=\frac{(0.6)(0.25)}{(60)}\left(\frac{\pi(200)^{2}}{4}\right)(300) \).

= 23 561.94 W or 23.56 kW
m = 0.25 kg per BP per h.

\( =\left(\frac{0.25}{60 \times 60}\right) kg \text { per BP per second } \).

\( =\left(69.44 \times 10^{-6}\right) kg \text { per BP per second } \).

Assumption No. 2 The ratio of heat absorbed by the piston to the total heat developed in the cylinder is 0.05 or 5%. (C = 0.05).
Assumption No. 3 The higher calorific value of fuel is 44 000 kJ/kg.
From Eq. 25.19,

\( H=[C \times HCV \times m \times BP ] \times 10^{3} \) (25.19).

\( H=[C \times HCV \times m \times BP ] \times 10^{3} \) .

\( =\left[0.05 \times 44000 \times\left(69.44 \times 10^{-6}\right) \times 23.56\right] \times 10^{3} \).

= 3599.21 W
Assumption No. 4 The thermal conductivity factor (k) for cast iron is 46.6 W/m/°C.

Assumption No. 5 The temperature difference \( \left(T_{c}-T_{e}\right) \) between the centre and the edge of the piston head is 220°C.
From Eq. 25.18, the thickness of the piston head by thermal consideration is given by,

\( t_{h}=\left[\frac{H}{12.56 k\left(T_{c}-T_{e}\right)}\right] \times 10^{3} \) (25.18).

\( t_{h}=\left[\frac{H}{12.56 k\left(T_{c}-T_{e}\right)}\right] \times 10^{3} \) .

\( =\left[\frac{3599.21}{12.56(46.6)(220)}\right] \times 10^{3} \).

\( t_{h}=27.95 mm \) (b).

From (a) and (b), thermal consideration is the criterion for piston thickness.

\( t_{h}=27.95 \text { or } 28 mm \).

Step II Radial ribs

\( \text { Since } t_{h}=28 mm \quad t_{h}>6 mm \).

Therefore, ribs are required.
Assumption No. 6 The number of radial ribs is 4.
From Eq. 25.22,

\( t_{R}=\left(\frac{t_{h}}{3}\right) \text { to }\left(\frac{t_{h}}{2}\right) \) (25.22).

\( t_{R}=\left(\frac{t_{h}}{3}\right) \text { to }\left(\frac{t_{h}}{2}\right)=\left(\frac{28}{3}\right) \text { to }\left(\frac{28}{2}\right) \).

= 9.33 to 14 mm.

\( t_{R}=12 mm \).

Step III Requirement of cup

\( \left(\frac{l}{D}\right)=\left(\frac{250}{200}\right)=1.25 \quad \therefore\left(\frac{l}{D}\right)<1.5 \).

Therefore, a cup is required.
Radius of cup = 0.7D = 0.7(200) = 140 mm.

Step IV Piston rings
Assumption No. 7 The allowable radial pressure \( \left(p_{w}\right) \) on the cylinder wall is 0.04 MPa.
Assumption No. 8 The piston rings are made of cast iron and permissible tensile stress is 100 N/mm².
Assumption No. 9 The number of compression rings is 3 and there are two oil rings.
From Eq. 25.25

\( b=D \sqrt{\frac{3 p_{w}}{\sigma_{t}}} \) (25.25).

\( b=D \sqrt{\frac{3 p_{w}}{\sigma_{t}}}=200 \sqrt{\frac{3(0.04)}{100}} \) .

= 6.93 or 7 mm
From Eq. 25.26,.

h = (0.7 b) to b (25.26).

h = (0.7 b) to b = (0.7 ×7) to 7 = 4.9 to 7 mm
h = 6 mm
z = 3 + 2 = 5
Also,

\( h_{\min }=\left(\frac{D}{10 z}\right)=\left(\frac{200}{10(5)}\right)=4 mm \quad h>h_{\min .} \)

The gap between the free ends of piston ring before assembly is given by,

\( G_{1}=3.5 b \text { to } 4 b \).

= 3.5(7) to 4(7) = 24.5 to 28 mm.

\( G_{1}=26 mm \).

The gap between the free ends of the piston ring after assembly is given by,

\( G_{2}=0.002 D \text { to } 0.004 D \).

= 0.002(200) to 0.004(200)
= 0.4 to 0.8 mm.

\( G_{2}=0.6 mm \).

From Eq. 25.28, the width of the top land is given by,

\( h_{1}=\left(t_{h}\right) \text { to }\left(1.2 t_{h}\right) \). (25.28).

\( h_{1}=\left(t_{h}\right) \text { to }\left(1.2 t_{h}\right)=(28) \text { to }(1.2 \times 28) \).

= 28 to 33.6 mm.

\( h_{1}=30 mm \).

From Eq. 25.29, the width of the ring grooves is given by,

\( h_{2}=0.75 h \text { to } h \) (25.29).

\( h_{2}=0.75 h \text { to } h=0.75(6) \text { to } 6=4.5 \text { to } 6 \) .

= 5 mm
Step V Piston barrel
From Eq. 25.30, the thickness of barrel at the top end is given by

\( t_{3}=(0.03 D+b+4.9) \) (25.30).

\( t_{3}=(0.03 D+b+4.9)=0.03(200)+7+4.9 \).

= 17.9 mm or 18 mm
From Eq. 25.31, the thickness of the barrel at the open end is given by,

\( t_{4}=\left(0.25 t_{3}\right) \text { to }\left(0.35 t_{3}\right) \).

\( =(0.25 \times 18) \text { to }(0.35 \times 18)=4.5 \text { to } 6.3 mm \).

\( t_{4}=6 mm \).

Step VI Piston skirt
Assumption No. 10 The allowable bearing pressure \( \left(p_{b}\right) \) for the skirt portion of the piston is 0.4 MPa.
Assumption No. 11 The ratio of the side thrust on the liner to the maximum gas load on the piston is 0.1. (μ = 0.1)
From Eq. 25.32, the length of the skirt is given by,

\( \mu\left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) p_{\max .}=p_{b} D l_{s} \) (25.32).

\( \mu\left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) p_{\max .}=p_{b} D l_{s} \text { or } \) ,

\( 0.1\left(\frac{\pi(200)^{2}}{4}\right)(4)=(0.4)(200) l_{s} \).

\( l_{s}=157.08 \text { or } 160 mm \).

Step VII Piston length
Refer to Fig. 25.11.

\( \text { Length of ring section }=5 h+4 h_{2} \).

= 5(6) + 4(5) = 50 mm [Refer Fig. 25.4].

\( \text { Length of piston }=h_{1}+\text { length of ring section }+l_{s} \).

= 30 + 50 + 160 = 240 mm
L = 240 mm
According to empirical relationship,
L = D to 1.5D = 200 to 300 mm

\( \therefore \quad D<L>1.5 D \).

Step VIII Piston pin
Assumption No. 12 The bearing pressure \( \left(p_{b}\right)_{1} \) at the
bush of the small end of the connecting rod is 30 MPa.

Assumption No. 13 The piston pin is made of heat treated alloy steel and the permissible tensile stress is 140 N/mm².

P = Force on piston

\( =\left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) p_{\max .}=\left(\frac{\pi(200)^{2}}{4}\right)(4) \).

= 125 663.71 N
From Eq. 25.37,

\( \left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) p_{\max }=\left(p_{b}\right)_{1} \times d_{o} \times l_{1} \) (25.37).

\( \left(\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}\right) p_{\max }=\left(p_{b}\right)_{1} \times d_{o} \times l_{1} \quad\left[\text { Given } l_{1} / d_{o}=1.5\right] \).

\( 125663.71=30 d_{o}\left(1.5 d_{o}\right) \).

\( d_{o}^{2}=2792.53 \).

\( d_{o}=52.84 mm \).

In order to make the pin hollow, we will increase the diameter to 70 mm.

\( d_{o}=70 mm \).

From Eq. 25.39,

\( M_{b}=\left(\frac{P D}{8}\right) \) (25.39).

\( M_{b}=\left(\frac{P D}{8}\right)=\frac{(125663.71)(200)}{8} \) .

\( =3141.59 \times 10^{3} N – mm \).

\( I=\frac{\pi\left(d_{o}^{4}-d_{i}^{4}\right)}{64}=\frac{\pi\left[(70)^{4}-d_{i}^{4}\right]}{64} mm ^{4} \).

\( y=\left(\frac{d_{o}}{2}\right)=\left(\frac{70}{2}\right)=35 mm \).

\( \sigma_{b}=\frac{M_{b} y}{I} \quad 140=\frac{\left(3141.59 \times 10^{3}\right)(35)}{\frac{\pi}{64}\left[(70)^{4}-d_{i}^{4}\right]} \).

\( (70)^{4}-d_{i}^{4}=16 \times 10^{6} \).

\( d_{i}=53.2 \text { or } 50 mm \).

The mean diameter of piston boss is given by, mean diameter of piston boss \( =1.4 d_{o} \).

= 1.4 (70) = 98 or 100 mm.
