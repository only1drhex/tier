Guest Question.

A proton of energy E is incident from the right on a nucleus of charge Ze. Estimate the transmission coefficient associated with the penetration of the proton inside the nucleus.

Admin
Solution

To penetrate inside the nucleus (i.e., to the left of the turning point r = a as shown in Figure 9.10), the proton has to overcome the repulsive Coulomb force of the nucleus. That is, it has to tunnel through the Coulomb barrier \(V(r)=Z e^{2} / r\). The transmission coefficient is given in the WKB approximation by (9.247), where \(x_{1}=a\) and \(x_{2}=0\) :

\(T=e^{-2 \gamma}, \quad \gamma=\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{a}^{0} \sqrt{2 m(V(r)-E)} d r\), (9.446)

where a is given by \(E=V(a): a=Z e^{2} / E\). Since \(V(r)=Z e^{2} / r\) we get

\(\gamma=\frac{1}{\hbar} \int_{a}^{0} \sqrt{2 m\left(\frac{Z e^{2}}{r}-E\right)} d r=\frac{\sqrt{2 m E}}{\hbar} \int_{Z e^{2} / E}^{0} \sqrt{\frac{Z e^{2}}{E r}-1} d r\). (9.447)

The change of variable \(x=E r /\left(Z e^{2}\right)\) gives

\(\gamma=\frac{Z e^{2}}{\hbar} \sqrt{\frac{2 m}{E}} \int_{0}^{1} \sqrt{\frac{1}{x}-1} d x=\frac{Z e^{2} \pi}{\hbar} \sqrt{\frac{m}{2 E}}\); (9.448)

in deriving this relation, we have used the integral \(\int_{0}^{1} \sqrt{1 / x-1} d x=\pi / 2\).

The transmission coefficient is thus given by

\(T=e^{-2 \gamma}=\exp \left\{-\frac{Z e^{2} \pi}{\hbar} \sqrt{\frac{2 m}{E}}\right\}\). (9.449)

The value of this coefficient describes how difficult it is for a positively charged particle, such as a proton, to approach a nucleus.
