The revenue and cost functions can be given as follows:

\(\begin{array}{rlr}R(Q, X) & =\left\{\begin{array}{lr}200 Q & x<20 \\2500(20)+200(Q-20) & 20 \leq x \leq Q\end{array}\right. \\C(Q, x) & =1,100 Q & 0 \leq x \leq Q \\P(Q, x) & =\left\{\begin{array}{lr}-900 Q & x<20 \\46,000-900 Q & 20 \leq x \leq Q\end{array}\right.\end{array}\)

Therefore, the expected profit can be given as follows:

\(E[P(Q)]=-\sum_{x=0}^{19} 900 Q p(x)+\sum_{x=20}^{Q}(46,000-900 Q) p(x)\)

The expected profit expression can be shown to reduce to

\(E[P(Q)]=-900 Q+46,000 \sum_{x=20}^{Q} p(x)\)

when 28 castings are produced, there is a zero probability of losing money; the penalty for not producing at least 20 good units is so severe that the minimum number of units having a zero probability of producing less than 20 good units is optimum.
