Guest Question.

Two balanced three-phase systems, X and Y, are interconnected with lines having impedance \(\mathbf{Z}_{\text {line }}=1+j 2 \Omega\). The line voltages are \(\mathbf{V}_{a b}=12 \underline{/ 0^{\circ}} \mathrm{kV} \mathrm{rms}\) and \(\mathbf{V}_{A B}=12 \underline{/ 5^{\circ}} \mathrm{kV} \mathrm{rms}\), as shown in Fig. 11.22a. We wish to determine which system is the source, which is the load, and the average power supplied by the source and absorbed by the load.

Admin
Solution

When we draw the per phase circuit for the system as shown in Fig. 11.22b, the analysis will be essentially the same as that of .
The network in Fig. 11.22b indicates that

 

\(\begin{aligned}\mathbf{I}_{a A} &=\frac{\mathbf{V}_{a n}-\mathbf{V}_{A N}}{\mathbf{Z}_{\text {line }}} \\\\&=\frac{\frac{12,000}{\sqrt{3}} \underline{/-30^{\circ}}-\frac{12,000}{\sqrt{3}} \underline{/-25^{\circ}}}{\sqrt{5} \underline{/ 63.43^{\circ}}} \\\\&=270.30\underline{/-180.93^{\circ}} \mathrm{Arms}\end{aligned}\)

 

The average power absorbed by system Y is

 

\(\begin{aligned}P_{Y} &=\sqrt{3} V_{A B} I_{a A} \cos \left(\theta_{\mathbf{V}_{a n}}-\theta_{\mathbf{1}_{a A}}\right) \\\\&=\sqrt{3}(12,000)(270.30) \cos \left(-25^{\circ}+180.93^{\circ}\right) \\\\&=-5.130 \mathrm{MW}\end{aligned}\)

 

Note that system Y is not the load, but rather the source and supplies 5.130 MW.

 

System X absorbs the following average power:

 

\(P_{X}=\sqrt{3} V_{a b} I_{A a} \cos \left(\theta_{\mathrm{V}_{a n}}-\theta_{\mathrm{I}_{a A}}\right)\)

 

where

 

\(\mathbf{I}_{A a}=-\mathbf{I}_{a A}=270.30 \underline{/-0.93^{\circ}} \mathrm{A} \mathrm{rms}\)

 

Therefore,

 

\(\begin{aligned}P_{X} &=\sqrt{3}(12,000)(270.30) \cos \left(-30^{\circ}+0.93^{\circ}\right) \\\\&=4.910 \mathrm{MW}\end{aligned}\)

 

and hence system X is the load.

 

The difference in the power supplied by system Y and that absorbed by system X is, of course, the power absorbed by the resistance of the three lines.
