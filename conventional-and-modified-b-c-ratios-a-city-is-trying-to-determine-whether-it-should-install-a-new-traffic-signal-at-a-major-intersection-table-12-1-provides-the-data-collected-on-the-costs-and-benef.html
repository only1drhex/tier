Guest Question.

Conventional and Modified B/C Ratios

A city is trying to determine whether it should install a new traffic signal at a major intersection. Table 12.1 provides the data collected on the costs and benefits of the existing traffic signal and the proposed traffic signal. Using an interest rate of 6% and a life of five years for both traffic signals, determine whether the proposed traffic signal should be installed by the city using both the conventional and modified incremental benefit/cost ratios.

TABLE 12.1
Data for Traffic Signal Alternatives

Costs and Benefits Existing Traffic Signal Proposed Traffic Signal
Initial cost — $68,670.00
Maintenance costs $3,000.00 per month $60,000 per year
Acceleration and deceleration costs (average cost to stop) $0.025 $0.025
Number of vehicles 80,000 per day 40,000 per day
Admin
Solution

Cost to stop for existing signal =\(\frac{\$0.025 }{Vehicle}\times \frac{80,000 vehicles}{Day} \times \frac{365 days}{Year}\)

= $730,000 per year

Cost to stop for existing signal =\(\frac{\$0.025 }{Vehicle}\times \frac{40,000 vehicles}{Day} \times \frac{365 days}{Year}\)

=$ 365,000 per year

\(EUAC_E\) of existing signal initial cost = 0

\(EUAC_P\) of proposed signal initial cost =$ 68,670.00(A/P,6,5)

= $ 68,670.00(0.23740)

= $ 16,302.26

Total benefits for existing signal = $730,000.00 + $95,280.00 = $825,280.00

Total benefits for proposed signal = $365,000.00 + $3,000.00 = $368,000.00

Total mainteance cost for existing signal = \(\frac{\$3,000.00 }{Month}\times \frac{12 month}{Year}= \$ 36,000.00\)

Total mainteance cost for proposed signal = $60,000.00

Conventional \(ΔB/ΔC=\frac{U_n}{C_n +M_n}= \frac{U_f – U_p}{(C_f – C_p)+ (M_f – M_p)}\)

 

\(= \frac{\$ 825,280.00 – \$368,000.00}{(\$16,302.26 -0)+ (\$60,000.00 -\$36,000.00)}\)

 

\(\frac{\$457,280.00}{\$16,302.26 + \$24,000.00}= \frac{\$457,280.00}{\$ 40,302.26}= 11.35\)

 

Modified \(ΔB/ΔC=\frac{U_n – M_n}{C_n}= \frac{(U_f – U_p)- (M_f – M_p)}{C_f – C_p}\)

 

\(= \frac{(\$825,280.00 – \$368,000.00) – (\$60,000.00 -\$36,000.00)}{(\$16,302.46.00 -0)}\)

 

\(= \frac{\$ 427,280.00 – \$24,000.00}{\$16,302.26}= \frac{\$403,280.00}{\$16,302.26}=24.74\)

Therefore, since the ΔB/ΔC ≥ 1.0 for both methods the new traffic signal should be installed by the city.
