Guest Question.

A column is fabricated from two 250 mm×75 mm channel sections attached to two flange plates each 300 mm×12 mm as shown in Fig. P.21.13; the column height is 10 m and the ends of the column are fixed. If the yield stress of mild steel in compression is \(250 N / mm ^{2}\) use the Perry-Robertson formula to calculate the crippling load for the column. Take \(E=205000 N / mm ^{2}\). The properties of a single 250 mm×75 mm channel are:
\(\text { Area }=36.6 cm ^{2}, I_{z}=3440 cm ^{4}, I_{y}=166 cm ^{4}\).
Ans. 2789 kN.

Admin
Solution

By inspection and by comparison with the column will buckle about its axis of symmetry along the web. The second moment of area of the section about this axis is then

\(I=2\left(166+36.6 \times 6.9^{2}+\frac{1.2 \times 30^{3}}{12}\right)=9217 cm ^{4}\)

The area of the cross section is

\(A=2(36.6+30 \times 1.2)=145.2 cm ^{2}\)

The radius of gyration of the section is

\(r=\sqrt{9217 / 145.2}=7.97 cm\)

The effective length of the column is \(=0.5 \times 10 \times 10^{2}=500 cm\) o that its slenderness ratio is equal to 500/7.97 = 62.8. Then, from Eq. (21.25)

\(\sigma_{ CR }=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(L_{ e } / r\right)^{2}}\) (21.25)

\(\sigma_{ CR }=\frac{\pi^{2} \times 200000}{62.8^{2}}=513 N / mm ^{2}\)

The yield stress of the material of the column is \(250 N / mm ^{2}\). Therefore, substituting these values in Eq. (21.46)

\(\sigma=\frac{1}{2}\left[\sigma_{ Y }+\left(1+0.003 \frac{L}{r}\right) \sigma_{ CR }\right]-\sqrt{\frac{1}{4}\left[\sigma_{ Y }+\left(1+0.003 \frac{L}{r}\right) \sigma_{ CR }\right]^{2}-\sigma_{ Y } \sigma_{ CR }}\) (21.46)

gives

\(\sigma=192.1 N / mm ^{2}\)

The crippling load for the column is then

\(P=\frac{192.1 \times 145.2 \times 10^{2}}{10^{3}}=2789 kN\)
