Guest Question.

(i) Convert \(\mathrm{A}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 1 \\ 3 & 2\end{array}\right)\) into an upper triangular matrix \(\mathrm{U}\) by using row operations.

(ii) Find a lower triangular matrix \(\mathrm{L}\) such that \(\mathrm{LU}=\mathrm{A}\).

Admin
Solution

(i) What is an upper triangular matrix?

All the entries below the leading diagonal are zero. This means that we need to convert the 3 in the bottom row of matrix A into zero.

How?

By applying row operations. Subtracting three times the top row from the bottom, \(\mathrm{R}_{2}-3 \mathrm{R}_{1}\) :

\( \begin{aligned} &\mathrm{R}_{1} \\ &\mathrm{R}_{2} \end{aligned}\left(\begin{array}{ll} 1 & 1 \\ 3 & 2 \end{array}\right) \quadâ‡’\begin{array}{c} \mathrm{R}_{1} \\ \mathrm{R}_{2}-3 \mathrm{R}_{1} \end{array}\left(\begin{array}{rr} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & -1 \end{array}\right)=\mathrm{U} \)

(ii) A lower triangular matrix has the form \(\mathrm{L}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}a & 0 \\ b & c\end{array}\right)\) where all the entries above the leading diagonal are zero. Substituting this \(\mathrm{L}\) and \(\mathrm{U}\) into \(\mathrm{LU}=\mathrm{A}\) gives:

\(\mathrm{LU}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}a & 0 \\b & c\end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{rr}1 & 1 \\0 & -1\end{array}\right)\\ \underbrace{=}_{\text { matrix multiplication }}\left(\begin{array}{cc}a & a \\b & b-c\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 1 \\3 & 2\end{array}\right)=\mathbf{A} \text { implies } a=1, \quad b=3 \text { and } c=1\)

Hence \(\mathrm{L}=\left(\begin{array}{ll}a & 0 \\ b & c\end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 0 \\ 3 & 1\end{array}\right)\). We can check this by multiplying out the matrices:

\( \mathrm{LU}=\left(\begin{array}{ll} 1 & 0 \\ 3 & 1 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{rr} 1 & 1 \\ 0 & -1 \end{array}\right)=\left(\begin{array}{ll} 1 & 1 \\ 3 & 2 \end{array}\right)=\mathbf{A} \)
