Guest Question.

Speed of Sound

GIVEN Consider the data in Table B.4.

FIND Verify the speed of sound for air at 0 °C.

Admin
Solution

In Table B.4, we find the speed of sound of air at 0 °C given as 331.4 m/s. Assuming that air behaves as an ideal gas, we can calculate the speed of sound from Eq. 11.36 as

 

\(c=\sqrt{R T k}\) (11.36)

 

\(c=\sqrt{R T k}\) (1)

 

The value of the gas constant is obtained from Table 1.8 as

 

\(R=286.9 J /( kg \cdot K )\)

 

and the specific heat ratio is listed in Table B.4 as

 

\(k=1.401\)

 

By substituting values of R, k, and T into Eq. 1 we obtain

 

\(\begin{aligned}c &=\sqrt{[(286.9) J /( kg \cdot K )](273.15 K )(1.401)} \\&=331.4( J / kg )^{1 / 2}\end{aligned}\)

 

Thus, since \(1 J / kg =1 N \cdot m / kg =1\left( kg \cdot m / s ^{2}\right) \cdot m / kg =1( m / s )^{2}\), we obtain

 

\(c=331.4 m / s\)

 

COMMENT The value of the speed of sound calculated with Eq. 11.36 agrees very well with the value of c listed in Table B.4. The ideal gas approximation does not compromise this result significantly.
