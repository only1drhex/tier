Guest Question.

Let

\(A = \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 & 4\end{matrix} \right\rgroup \)

Find the bases for \(N(A), R(A^{T} ), N(A^{T} )\), and R(A).

Admin
Solution

We can find bases for N(A) and \(R(A^{T} )\) by transforming A into reduced row echelon form:

\( \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 3 & 4\end{matrix} \right\rgroup \longrightarrow \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 & 2\end{matrix} \right\rgroup \longrightarrow \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0\end{matrix} \right\rgroup \)

Since (1, 0, 1) and (0, 1, 1) form a basis for the row space of A, it follows that \((1, 0, 1)^{T}\) and \((0, 1, 1)^{T}\) form a basis for \(R(A^{T}\) ). If x ∈ N(A), it follows from the reduced row echelon form of A tha

\( x_{1} + x_{3} = 0\)

 

\(x_{2} + x_{3}= 0\)

Thus,

\( x_{1} = x_{2} = −x_{3}\)

Setting \(x_{3} = α\), we see that N(A) consists of all vectors of the form \(α(−1, −1, 1)^{T}\) . Note that \((−1, −1, 1)^{T}\) is orthogonal to \(( 1, 0, 1)^{T}\) and \((0, 1, 1)^{T}\) .

To find bases for R(A) and \(N(A^{T}\) ), transform \(A^{T}\) to reduced row echelon form:

\( \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 3 \\ 2 & 1 & 4\end{matrix} \right\rgroup \longrightarrow \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\0 & 1 & 2 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup \longrightarrow \left\lgroup\begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\0 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right\rgroup \)

Thus, \((1, 0, 1)^{T}\) and \((0, 1, 2)^{T}\) form a basis for R(A). If \( ∈ N(A^{T}\) ), then \(x_{1} = −x_{3}, x_{2} = −2x_{3}\). Hence, \(N(A^{T}\) ) is the subspace of \(R^{3}\) spanned by \((−1, −2, 1)^{T}\) . Note that \((−1, −2, 1)^{T}\) is orthogonal to \((1, 0, 1)^{T}\) and \((0, 1, 2)^{T}\) .
