Guest Question.

Helium gas is stored at 20°C in a spherical container of fused silica \((SiO_{2})\), which has a diameter of 0.20 m and a wall thickness of 2 mm. If the container is charged to an initial pressure of 4 bars, what is the rate at which this pressure decreases with time?

Admin
Solution

Known: Initial pressure of helium in a spherical, fused silica container of prescribed diameter D and wall thickness L.

Find: The rate of change of the helium pressure, \(dp_{A}/dt\).

Assumptions:
1. Since D ≫ L, diffusion may be approximated as being one-dimensional through a plane wall.
2. Quasi-steady diffusion (pressure variation is sufficiently slow to permit assuming steady-state conditions for diffusion through the fused silica at any instant).
3. Stationary medium has uniform density ρ.
4. Pressure of helium in outside air is negligible.
5. Helium exhibits ideal gas behavior.

Properties: , helium-fused silica (293 K): \(D_{AB} = 0.4 × 10^{-13} m^{2}/s\). , helium-fused silica (293 K): \(S = 0.45 × 10^{-3} kmol/m^{3} . bar.\)

Analysis: The rate of change of the helium pressure may be obtained by applying the species conservation requirement, Equation 14.43 \( \dot{M}_{in} +\dot{M}_{g} – \dot{M}_{out} = \frac{dM_{A}}{dt} \equiv \dot{M}_{st}\), to a control volume about the helium. It follows that

\(– \dot{M}_{A,out} = \dot{M}_{A,st}\)

or, since the helium outflow is due to diffusion through the fused silica,

\(– \dot{M}_{A,out} = n^{\prime \prime }_{A,x} A \)

and the change in mass storage is

\(\dot{M}_{A,st} = \frac{dM_{A}}{dt} = \frac{d(ρ_{A} V)}{dt} \)

the species balance reduces to

\(– n^{\prime \prime }_{A,x} A = \frac{d(ρ_{A} V)}{dt} \)

Recognizing that \(ρ_{A} = M_{A} C_{A}\) and applying the ideal gas law

\( C_{A} = \frac{p_{A}}{R T} \)

the species balance becomes

\( \frac{d p_{A}}{dt} = – \frac{R T}{M_{A} V} A n^{\prime \prime }_{A,x} \)

For a stationary medium the absolute flux of species A through the fused silica is equal to the diffusion flux, \( n^{\prime \prime }_{A,x} = j_{A,x}\), in which case, from Fick’s law, Equation 14.12 \(j_{A} = -\rho D_{AB} \nabla m_{A}\),

\( n^{\prime \prime }_{A,x} = – ρ D_{AB} \frac{d m_{A}}{dx} = -D_{AB} \frac{d ρ_{A}}{dx} \)

or, for the assumed conditions,

\( n^{\prime \prime }_{A,x} = D_{AB} \frac{ρ_{A,1} – ρ_{A,2}}{L}\)

The species densities \(ρ_{A,1}\) and \(ρ_{A,2}\) pertain to conditions within the fused silica at its inner and outer surfaces, respectively, and may be evaluated from knowledge of the solubility through Equation 14.62 \(C_{A}(0) = Sp_{A}(0)\). Hence with \(ρ_{A} = M_{A} C_{A}\),

\(ρ_{A,1} = M_{A} Sp_{A,i} = M_{A} Sp_{A}\) and \(ρ_{A,2} = M_{A} Sp_{A,o} = 0 \)

where \(p_{A,i}\) and \(p_{A,o}\) are helium pressures at the inner and outer surfaces, respectively. Hence

\( n^{\prime \prime }_{A,x} = \frac{D_{AB} M_{A} Sp_{A} }{L}\)

and substituting into the species balance it follows that

\( \frac{d p_{A}}{dt} = – \frac{R T A D_{AB} S}{L V} p_{A} \)

or with A = πD² and V = πD³/6

\( \frac{d p_{A}}{dt} = – \frac{6 R T D_{AB} S}{L D} p_{A} \)

Substituting numerical values, the rate of change of the pressure is

\( \frac{d p_{A}}{dt} = [-6(0.08314 m^{3} . bar/kmol . K) 293 K (0.4 × 10^{-13} m^{2}/s) ×0.45 × 10^{-3} kmol/m^{3} . bar × 4 bar] ÷ [0.002 m (0.2 m)]\)

 

\( \frac{d p_{A}}{dt} = -2.63 × 10^{-8} bar/s\)

Comments: The foregoing result provides the initial (maximum) leakage rate for the system. The leakage rate decreases as the inside pressure decreases due to the dependence of \(ρ_{A,1}\) on \(p_{A}\).
