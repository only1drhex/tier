Guest Question.

A round bar subject to a bending load has a diameter d = LN(2.000, 0.002) in. This equivalency states that the mean diameter is \(\mu_{d}=2.000\) in and the standard deviation is \(\hat{\sigma}_{d}=0.002\) in. Find the mean and the standard deviation of the second moment of area.

Admin
Solution

The second moment of area is given by the equation

 

\(I =\frac{\pi d ^{4}}{64}\)

 

The coefficient of variation of the diameter is

 

\(C_{d}=\frac{\hat{\sigma}_{d}}{\mu_{d}}=\frac{0.002}{2}=0.001\)

 

Using Table 20–6, we find

 

Table 20–6 Means and Standard Deviations for Simple Algebraic Operations on Independent (Uncorrelated) Random Variables
Function Mean \((\mu)\) Standard Deviation \((\hat{\sigma})\)
a a 0
x \(\mu_{x}\) \(\hat{\sigma}_{x}\)
x + a \(\mu_{x}+a\) \(\hat{\sigma}_{x}\)
ax \(a \mu_{x}\) \(a \hat{\sigma}_{x}\)
x + y \(\mu_{x}+\mu_{y}\) \(\left(\hat{\sigma}_{x}^{2}+\hat{\sigma}_{y}^{2}\right)^{1 / 2}\)
x − y \(\mu_{x}-\mu_{y}\) \(\left(\hat{\sigma}_{x}^{2}\hat{\sigma}_{x}^{2}\right)^{1 / 2}\)
xy \(\mu_{x} \mu_{y}\) \(\mu_{x} \mu_{y}\left(C_{x}^{2}+C_{y}^{2}+C_{x}^{2} C_{y}^{2}\right)^{1 / 2}\)
x/y \(\mu_{x} / \mu_{y}\) \(\mu_{x}\mu_{y}\left[\left(C_{x}^{2}+C_{y}^{2}\right)\left(+C_{y}^{2}\right)\right]^{1 / 2}\)
\(x ^{n}\) \(\mu_{x}^{n}\left[1+\frac{n(n-1)}{2} C_{x}^{2}\right]\) \(|n| \mu_{x}^{n} C_{x}\left[1+\frac{(n-1)^{2}}{4} C_{x}^{2}\right]\)
1/x \(\frac{1}{\mu_{x}}\left(1+C_{x}^{2}\right)\) \(\frac{C_{x}}{u_{x}}\left(1+C_{x}^{2}\right)\)
\(1 / x ^{2}\) \(\frac{1}{\mu_{x}^{2}}\left(1+3 C_{x}^{2}\right)\) \(\frac{2 C_{x}}{\mu_{x}^{2}}\left(1+\frac{9}{4} C_{x}^{2}\right)\)
\(1 / x ^{3}\) \(\frac{1}{\mu_{x}^{3}}\left(1+6 C_{x}^{2}\right)\) \(\frac{3 C_{x}}{\mu_{x}^{3}}\left(1+4 C_{x}^{2}\right)\)
\(1 / x ^{4}\) \(\frac{1}{\mu_{x}^{4}}\left(1+10 C_{x}^{2}\right)\) \(\frac{4 C_{x}}{\mu_{x}^{4}}\left(1+\frac{25}{4} C_{x}^{2}\right)\)
\(\sqrt{ x }\) \(\sqrt{\mu_{x}}\left(1-\frac{1}{8} C_{x}^{2}\right)\) \(\frac{\sqrt{\mu_{x}}}{2} C_{x}\left(1+\frac{1}{16} C_{x}^{2}\right)\)
\(x ^{2}\) \(\mu_{x}^{2}\left(1+C_{x}^{2}\right)\) \(2 \mu_{x}^{2} C_{x}\left(1+\frac{1}{4} C_{x}^{2}\right)\)
\(x ^{3}\) \(\mu_{x}^{3}\left(1+3 C_{x}^{2}\right)\) \(3 \mu_{x}^{3} C_{x}\left(1+C_{x}^{2}\right)\)
\(x ^{4}\) \(\mu_{x}^{4}\left(1+6 C_{x}^{2}\right)\) \(4 \mu_{x}^{4} C_{x}\left(1+\frac{9}{4} C_{x}^{2}\right)\)

For correlated products and quotients see Charles R. Mischke, Mathematical Model Building, 2nd rev. ed., Iowa State University Press, Ames, 1980, App. C.

 

\(\mu_{I}=(\pi / 64) \mu_{d}^{4}\left(1+6 C_{d}^{2}\right)=(\pi / 64)(2.000)^{4}\left[1+6(0.001)^{2}\right]=0.785 in ^{4}\)

 

\(\hat{\sigma}_{I}=4 \mu_{d}^{4} C_{d}\left[1+(9 / 4) C_{d}^{2}\right]=4(2.000)^{4}(0.001)\left[1+(9 / 4)(0.001)^{2}\right]=0.064 in ^{4}\)

 

These results can be expressed in the form

 

\(I = L N (0.785,0.064)=0.785 L N (1,0.0815) in ^{4}\)
