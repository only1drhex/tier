Guest Question.

Given:

A cylindrical soil sample, 7.3 cm in diameter and 16.8 cm long, is tested in a constant-head permeability apparatus. A constant head of 75 cm is maintained during the test. After 1 min of testing, a total of 945.7 g of water was collected. The temperature was 20째C. The void ratio of the soil was 0.43.

Required:

Compute the coefficient of permeability in centimetres per second and in furlongs per fortnight.

Admin
Solution

First, calculate the cross-sectional area of the sample:

 

\(A=\frac{\pi D^{2}}{4}=\frac{\pi}{4}(7.3 cm )^{2}=41.9 cm ^{2}\)

 

From Eq. (7.9), solve for k:

 

\(k=\frac{Q L}{h A t}\) (7.9)

 

\(\begin{aligned}k &=\frac{Q L}{h A t} \\&=\frac{945.7 cm ^{3} \times 16.8 cm }{75 cm \times 41.9 cm ^{2} \times 1 min \times 60 s / min } \\&=0.08 cm / s\end{aligned}\)

 

To convert to furlongs per fortnight (see Appendix A):

 

\(\begin{aligned}k=&\left(0.08 \frac{ cm }{ s }\right)\left(60 \frac{ s }{ min }\right)\left(60 \frac{ min }{ h }\right)\left(24 \frac{ h }{ d }\right)\left(14 \frac{ d }{\text { fortnight }}\right) \\& \times\left(\frac{1 in. }{2.54 cm }\right)\left(\frac{1 ft }{12 in .}\right)\left(\frac{ mi }{5280 ft }\right)\left(\frac{8 \text { furlongs }}{ mi }\right) \\=& 4.8 \frac{\text { furlongs }}{\text { fortnight }}\end{aligned}\)
