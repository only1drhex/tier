Guest Question.

What is the Thevenin equivalent circuit seen from terminal A-B in the circuit of Fig. 1.49?

Admin
Solution

2.49. To find the Thevenin equivalent circuit, we need to calculate the Thevenin resistance \((R_{Th}) \)and the Thevenin voltage \((V_{Th})\). Since the circuit includes a dependent source (dependent current source), we need to connect a test source (with the voltage and current of\( V_{t} \)and \(I_{t}) \)to the A-B terminal to find the Thevenin resistance (\(R_{Th}=\frac{V_{t}}{I_{t}} \)), while all the independent sources are turned off. In addition,\( V_{Th}\) will be the same as the open circuit voltage \((V_{oc})\). As can be seen in

Fig. 2.49.2, nodal analysis is applied to find \(R_{Th}\).

Defining \(I_{x}\) based on the node voltages in the circuit of Fig. 2.49.2:

\(I_{x}=\frac{e_{1}}{8}.\)….(1)

KCL in node 1:

\(\frac{e_{1}}{8}-2I_{x}+\frac{e_{1}-e_{2}}{10}=0\)

Using (1)\(\Rightarrow -\frac{e_{1}}{8}+\frac{e_{1}-e_{2}}{10}=0\Rightarrow -\frac{e_{1}}{40}-\frac{e_{2}}{10}=0\Rightarrow e_{1}=-4e_{2}\)

KCL in node 2:

\(\frac{e_{2}-e_{1}}{10}+\frac{e_{2}}{3}+\frac{e_{2}-V_{t}}{4}+2I_{x}=0\)

Using (1)\(\Rightarrow \frac{e_{2}-e_{1}}{10}+\frac{e_{2}}{3}+\frac{e_{2}-V_{t}}{4}+\frac{e_{1}}{4}=0\Rightarrow \frac{41}{60}e_{2}+\frac{3}{20}e_{1}-\frac{V_{t}}{4}=0\Rightarrow \)

Using(2)\(\Rightarrow \frac{41}{60}e_{2}+\frac{3}{20}(-4e_{2})-\frac{V_{t}}{4}=0\Rightarrow \frac{5}{60}e_{2}-\frac{V_{t}}{4}=0\)

\(\Rightarrow e_{2}=3V_{t}.\)….(2)

Defining \(I_{t}\) based on the node voltages:

\(I_{t}=\frac{V_{t}-e_{2}}{4}\)

Using(3)\(\Rightarrow I_{t}=\frac{V_{t}-3V_{t}}{4}=-\frac{1}{2}V_{t}\Rightarrow \frac{V_{t}}{I_{t}}=-2\)

\(R_{Th}=\frac{V_{t}}{I_{t}}=-2Ω\)

Nodal analysis is applied to find \(V_{Th}\), as can be seen in Fig. 2.49.3.

Defining \(I_{x}\) based on the node voltages:

\(I_{x}=\frac{e_{1}}{8}.\)….(4)

KCL in node 1:

\(-I_{s}+\frac{e_{1}}{8}-2I_{x}+\frac{e_{1}-e_{2}}{10}=0\)

\(Using(1)\Rightarrow -I_{s}-\frac{e_{1}}{8}+\frac{e_{1}-e_{2}}{10}=0\Rightarrow -I_{s}-\frac{e_{1}}{40}-\frac{e_{2}}{10}=0\)…..(5)

KCL in node 2:

\(\frac{e_{2}-e_{1}}{10}+\frac{e_{2}}{3}+2I_{x}=0\)

\(Using(1)\Rightarrow \frac{e_{2}-e_{1}}{10}+\frac{e_{2}}{3}+\frac{e_{1}}{4}=0\Rightarrow \frac{13e_{2}}{30}+\frac{3e_{1}}{20}=0\Rightarrow e_{1}=-\frac{26}{9}e_{2}\)……(6)

Solving (5) and (6):

\(-I_{s}-\frac{-\frac{26}{9}e_{2}}{40}-\frac{e_{2}}{10}=0\Rightarrow -I_{s}-\frac{1}{36}e_{2}=0\Rightarrow e_{2}=-36I_{s}\)

\(\Rightarrow V_{oc}=e_{2}=-36I_{s}\), because there is no voltage drop on the 4 Ω resistor, since the terminal is open circuit.

\(V_{Th}=-36I_{s}\)
