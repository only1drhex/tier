Guest Question.

Verify in accordance with the EC3 provisions, the welded connection between a UPN 240 profile in bending, connected to a gusset plate by two fillet welds of same length (Figure E14.2.1).
Applied load T = 90 kN (20.2 kips)
Load eccentricity e = 600 mm (23.6 in.)
Steel S275 \(f_{u}\) = 430 \(N/mm^{2}\)(62.4 ksi)
\(\beta_{w}\) =0.85

Admin
Solution

Use two fillet welds of side length of 10mm (0.394 in.) and length of 200mm (7.87 in.) each.
Forces acting on each fillet:
(a) Force parallel to fillet axes (h is profile depth):
\(T_{1}=\frac{T.e}{h}=\frac{90\times 600}{240}\)=225 kN (50.6 kips)
(b) Force orthogonal to fillet axes:
\(T_{2}\)= T/2 =90/2 = 45 kN (10.1 kips)
(1) EC3 directional method
Compute fillet throat dimension, a:
\(a=d/\sqrt{2}=10\sqrt{2}\)=7mm(0.276 in.)
\(\sigma_{\perp}=\tau _{\perp}=\frac{T/2}{l.a}\cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}=\frac{(90/2)\cdot 10^{3}}{200\times 7}\cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}=22.7 N/mm^{2}(3.29 ksi)\)
\(\tau _{\parallel }=\frac{T_{1}}{a.l}=\frac{225\cdot 10^{3}}{7\times 200}=160.7 N/mm^{2}(23.3 ksi)\)
The verification formulas are:
\(\sqrt{\sigma _{\perp }^{2}+3(\tau _{\perp }^{2}+\tau ^{2}_{\parallel })}=\sqrt{22.7^{2}+3\times (22.7^{2}+160.7^{2})}=282 N/mm^{2}\)
\(\leq \frac{f_{u}}{\beta _{w}\cdot \gamma _{M2}}=\frac{430}{0.85\times 1.25}=404.7N/mm^{2}\)OK (40.9 ksi ≤ 58.7 ksi)
\(\sigma _{\perp }=22.7 N/mm^{2}\leq \frac{0.9f_{u}}{\gamma _{M2}}=\frac{0.9\times 430}{1.25}=309.6 N/mm^{2}\)OK (3.29 ksi ≤ 44.9 ksi)
Stress rate :282/404.7=0.70
(2) Simplified EC3 method
The verification formula is:
\(F_{w,Ed}\leq F_{w,Rd}=\frac{f_{u}\cdot a}{\sqrt{3}\cdot \beta _{w}\cdot \gamma _{M2}}\)
\(F_{w,Ed}=\sqrt{\left ( \frac{T_{1}}{l} \right )^{2}+\left ( \frac{T_{2}}{l} \right )^{2}}=\sqrt{\left ( \frac{225.10^{3}}{200} \right )^{2}+\left ( \frac{45.10^{3}}{200} \right )^{2}}\)= 1147N/mm (6.55 kips/in.)
\(F_{w,Rd}=\frac{f_{u}\cdot a}{\sqrt{3}\cdot \beta _{w}\cdot \gamma _{M2}}=\frac{430\times 7}{\sqrt{3}\times 0.85\times 1.25}\)=1636 N/mm > 1147N/mm
OK (9.34 kips/in > 6.55 kips/in.)
Stress rate :1147/1636 = 0.70
As can be noted, stress rate is the same with both methods (= 0.70).
