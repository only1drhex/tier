Guest Question.

A mild steel column is 6 m long, is fixed at both ends and has the cross section shown in Fig. P.21.11. Given that the yield stress in compression of mild steel is \(300 N / mm ^{2}\) calculate the maximum allowable load for the column using the Perry-Robertson formula. Take \(E=200000 N / mm ^{2}\) and assume a factor of safety of 2.
Ans. 406.8 kN.

Admin
Solution

The column will buckle about an axis along the line of symmetry through the web (see Section 21.4). The second moment of area of the column cross section about this axis is

\(I=2 \times \frac{10 \times 150^{3}}{12}+\frac{230 \times 8^{3}}{12}=5.6 \times 10^{6} mm ^{4}\)

The area of the cross section is

\(A=2 \times 150 \times 10+230 \times 8=4840 mm ^{2}\)

The radius of gyration of the cross section is then

\(r=\sqrt{5.6 \times 10^{6} / 4840}=34.0 mm\)

The equivalent length, \(L_{e}\), of the column is \(0.5 \times 6 \times 10^{3}=3000 mm\). Then, from Eq. (21.25)

\(\sigma_{ CR }=\frac{\pi^{2} E}{\left(L_{ e } / r\right)^{2}}\) (21.25)

\(\sigma_{ CR }=\frac{\pi^{2} \times 200000}{(3000 / 34.0)^{2}}=253.7 N / mm ^{2}\)

The yield stress of the material of the column is given as \(300 N / mm ^{2}\) . Then, substituting these values in Eq. (21.46)

\(\sigma=\frac{1}{2}\left[\sigma_{ Y }+\left(1+0.003 \frac{L}{r}\right) \sigma_{ CR }\right]-\sqrt{\frac{1}{4}\left[\sigma_{ Y }+\left(1+0.003 \frac{L}{r}\right) \sigma_{ CR }\right]^{2}-\sigma_{ Y } \sigma_{ CR }}\) (21.46)

gives

\(\sigma=168.1 N / mm ^{2}\)

Applying a factor of safety of 2 the safe working load is then

\(P_{\text {safe }}=\frac{168.1 \times 4840 \times 10^{-3}}{2}=406.8 kN\)
