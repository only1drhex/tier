Guest Question.

Determine the deflection curve of the beam AB shown in Fig. 13.8 when it carries a distributed load that varies linearly in intensity from zero at the left-hand support to \(w_{0}\) at the right-hand support.

Admin
Solution

To find the support reactions we first take moments about B. Thus

\(R_{ A } L=\frac{1}{2} w_{0} L \frac{L}{3}\)

which gives

\(R_{ A }=\frac{w_{0} L}{6}\)

Resolution of vertical forces then gives

\(R_{ B }=\frac{w_{0} L}{3}\)

The bending moment, M, at any section X, a distance x from A is

\(M=R_{ A } x-\frac{1}{2}\left(w_{0} \frac{x}{L}\right) x \frac{x}{3}\)

or

\(M=\frac{w_{0}}{6 L}\left(L^{2} x-x^{3}\right)\) (i)

Substituting for M in Eq. (13.3)

\(\frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=\frac{M}{E I}\) (13.3)

we obtain

\(E I \frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=\frac{w_{0}}{6 L}\left(L^{2} x-x^{3}\right)\) (ii)

which, when integrated, becomes

\(E I \frac{ d v}{ d x}=\frac{w_{0}}{6 L}\left(L^{2} \frac{x^{2}}{2}-\frac{x^{4}}{4}\right)+C_{1}\) (iii)

Integrating Eq. (iii) we have

\(E I v=\frac{w_{0}}{6 L}\left(L^{2} \frac{x^{3}}{6}-\frac{x^{5}}{20}\right)+C_{1} x+C_{2}\) (iv)

The deflection \(v=0\) at x = 0 and x = L. From the first of these conditions we obtain \(C_{2}=0\), while from the second

\(0=\frac{w_{0}}{6 L}\left(\frac{L^{5}}{6}-\frac{L^{5}}{20}\right)+C_{1} L\)

which gives

\(C_{1}=-\frac{7 w_{0} L^{4}}{360}\)

The deflection curve then has the equation

\(v=-\frac{w_{0}}{360 E I L}\left(3 x^{5}-10 L^{2} x^{3}+7 L^{4} x\right)\) (v)

An alternative method of solution is to use Eq. (13.5)

\(\frac{ d ^{4} v}{ d x^{4}}=-\frac{w}{E I}\) (13.5)

and express the applied load in mathematical form. Thus

\(E I \frac{ d ^{4} v}{ d x^{4}}=-w=-w_{0} \frac{x}{L}\) (vi)

Integrating we obtain

\(E I \frac{ d ^{3} v}{ d x^{3}}=-w_{0} \frac{x^{2}}{2 L}+C_{3}\)

When x = 0 we see from Eq. (13.4)

\(\frac{ d ^{3} v}{ d x^{3}}=-\frac{S}{E I}\) (13.4)

that

\(E I \frac{ d ^{3} v}{ d x^{3}}=R_{ A }=\frac{w_{0} L}{6}\)

Hence

\(C_{3}=\frac{w_{0} L}{6}\)

and

\(E I \frac{ d ^{3} v}{ d x^{3}}=-w_{0} \frac{x^{2}}{2 L}+\frac{w_{0} L}{6}\) (vii)

Integrating Eq. (vii) we have

\(E I \frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=-\frac{w_{0} x^{3}}{6 L}+\frac{w_{0} L}{6} x+C_{4}\)

Since the bending moment is zero at the supports we have

\(E I \frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=0\) when x = 0

Hence \(C_{4}=0\) and

\(E I \frac{ d ^{2} v}{ d x^{2}}=-\frac{w_{0}}{6 L}\left(x^{3}-L^{2} x\right)\)

as before.
