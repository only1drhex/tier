Guest Question.

In a hydrometer analysis, the corrected hydrometer reading in a 1000 ml uniform soil suspension at the start of sedimentation was 28. After a lapse of 30 minutes, the corrected hydrometer reading was 12 and the corresponding effective depth 10.5 cm. The specific gravity of the solids was 2.70. Assuming the viscosity and unit weight of water at the temperature of the test as 0.001 N-s/\(m ^{2}\) and 9.8 kN/\(m ^{3}\) respectively, determine the weight of solids mixed in the suspension, the effective diameter corresponding to the 30-min. reading and the percentage of particles finer than this size.

Admin
Solution

The corrected hydrometer reading initially,\(R_{h_{i}}=28\)

 

\(\therefore \quad \gamma_{i}=0.01028 N / cm ^{3}\)

But, by Eq. 3.29,

\(\gamma_{i}=\gamma_{w}+\frac{(G-1)}{G} \cdot \frac{W}{V}\) (Eq. 3.29)

\(\left.\gamma_{i}=\gamma_{w}+[G-1) / G\right] W / V\)

Substituting,

\(0.01028=0.01+\frac{(2.7-1)}{2.7} \times \frac{W}{1000}\)

whence \(W=\frac{0.028 \times 2.7}{17} N =0.445 N\)

∴ The weight of solid mixed in the suspension = 0.445 N. By Eqs. 3.21 and 3.22,

\(D=K \sqrt{H / t}\) ( Eq. 3.21 )

where \(K=\sqrt{\frac{3 \mu_{w}}{\gamma_{w}(G-1)}}\) ( Eq. 3.22 )

\(\therefore \quad K=\sqrt{\frac{3 \times 0.001}{9.8 \times(2.7-1)}}=0.01342 mm \frac{\sqrt{ min }}{\sqrt{ cm }} \text {. }\)

 

\(\therefore \quad D=0.01342 \sqrt{\frac{10.5}{30}} mm =0.00794 mm \approx 0.008 mm\)

∴ The effective diameter corresponding to the 30-min, reading = 0.008 mm By Eq. 3.34,

\(N=\frac{G \gamma_{w}}{(G-1)} \cdot \frac{V}{W} \cdot \frac{R_{h}}{10}\) (Eq. 3.34)

\(\therefore \quad N=\frac{2.7}{17} \times 0.01 \times \frac{1000}{0.445} \times \frac{12}{10}=42.83\)

∴ The percentage of particles finer than 0.008 mm is 43.
