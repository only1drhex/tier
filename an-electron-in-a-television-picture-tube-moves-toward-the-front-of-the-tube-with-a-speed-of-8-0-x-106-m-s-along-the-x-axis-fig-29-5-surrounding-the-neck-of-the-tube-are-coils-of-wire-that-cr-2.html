Guest Question.

An electron in a television picture tube moves toward the front of the tube with a speed of 8.0 × 10\(^6\) m/s along the x axis (Fig. 29.5). Surrounding the neck of the tube are coils of wire that create a magnetic ﬁeld of magnitude 0.025 T, di- rected at an angle of 60° to the x axis and lying in the xy plane. Calculate the magnetic force on and acceleration of the electron.

Admin
Solution

Using Equation 29.2, we can find the magnitude of the magnetic force:

\( F_{B} =|q| v B \sin \theta\) \( =\left(1.6 \times 10^{-19} \mathrm{C}\right)\left(8.0 \times 10^{6} \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}\right)(0.025 \mathrm{~T})\left(\sin 60^{\circ}\right) \) \( =2.8 \times 10^{-14} \mathrm{~N}\)

Because \( \mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B}\) is in the positive z direction (from the righthand rule) and the charge is negative, \( \mathbf{F}_{B}\) is in the negative z direction.

The mass of the electron is \( 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \mathrm{~kg},\) and so its acceleration is \(a=\frac{F_{B}}{m_{e}}=\frac{2.8 \times 10^{-14} \mathrm{~N}}{9.11 \times 10^{-31} \mathrm{~kg}}=3.1 \times 10^{16} \mathrm{~m} / \mathrm{s}^{2}\)

in the negative z direction.
