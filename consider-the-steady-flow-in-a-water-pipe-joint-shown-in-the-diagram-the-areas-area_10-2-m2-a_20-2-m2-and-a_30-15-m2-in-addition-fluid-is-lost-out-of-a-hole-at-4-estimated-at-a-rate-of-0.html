Guest Question.

Consider the steady flow in a water pipe joint shown in the diagram. The areas are: \(A_{1}= 0.2 m^{2}\), \(A_{2}= 0.2 m^{2}\),and \(A_{3}= 0.15 m^{2}\) In addition, fluid is lost out of a hole at 4 , estimated at a rate of \(0.1 m^{3}/s\) The average speeds at sections 1 and 3 are \(V_{1}=5 m/s \) and \(V_{3}=12 m/s\) respectively. Find the velocity at section 2 . Given: Steady flow of water through the device. \(A_{1}= 0.2 m^{2}\) \(A_{2}= 0.2 m^{2}\) \(A_{3}= 0.15 m^{2}\) \(V_{1}=5 m/s\) \( and V_{3}=12 m/s\) Volume flow rate at 4\( = 0.1 m^{3}/s\) Find: Velocity at section 2 .

Solution

Choose a fixed control volume as shown. Make an assumption that the flow at section 2 is outwards, and label the diagram accordingly (if this assumption is incorrect our final result will tell us). Governing equation: The general control volume equation is Eq. 4.12, but we can go immediately to Eq. 4.13b because of assumptions (2) and (3) below,

 

\(\sum{_{cs}} \overrightarrow{V}\cdot \overrightarrow{A} \)

 

Assumptions: (1) Steady flow (given). (2) Incompressible flow. (3) Uniform properties at each section.
Hence (using Eq. 4.14a for the leak)

 

\(\overrightarrow{V_{1}} \cdot \overrightarrow{A_{1}} +\overrightarrow{V_{2}}\cdot \overrightarrow{A_{2}}+\overrightarrow{V_{3}} \cdot \overrightarrow{A_{3}}+Q_{4} =0 \)

 

where

 

\(Q_{4}\)

 

is the flow rate out of the leak. Let us examine the first three terms in Eq. 1 in light of the discussion of Fig. 4.3 and the directions of the velocity vectors:

 

\(\overrightarrow{V_{1}} \cdot \overrightarrow{A_{1}}=-V_{1}A_1 \)Sign

 

of\(\overrightarrow{V_1} \cdot \overrightarrow{A_1} \)

 

is negative at surface,

 

\(\overrightarrow{V_{2}}\cdot \overrightarrow{A_{2}}=+V_2A_2\)

 

Sign of

 

\(\overrightarrow{V_2} \cdot \overrightarrow{A_2} \)

 

is
positive at surface,

 

\(\overrightarrow{V_{3}}\cdot \overrightarrow{A_{3}}=+V_3A_3\)

 

Sign of

 

\(\overrightarrow{V_3} \cdot \overrightarrow{A_3}\)positive at surface 3 Using these results in Eq. 1,\(-V_1A_1+V_2A_2+V_3A_3+Q_4=0\) \(V_{2}=\frac{V_1A_1-V_3A_3-Q_4}{A_2} \)

 

\(=\frac{5\frac{m}{s}\times 0.2m^2-12\frac{m}{s}\times 0.15m^2-\frac{0.1m^3}{s} }{0.2m^2} \)

 

\(=-0.4m/s\underleftarrow{V_2}\) Recall that \(V_2\)

 

represents the magnitude of the velocity, which we assumed was outwards from the control volume. The fact that \(V_2\) is negative means that in fact we have an inflow at location 2 â€”our initial assumption was invalid.This problem demonstrates use of the sign convention for evaluating
