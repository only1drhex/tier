Guest Question.

A copper sphere 12 mm in diameter is initially \(100^\circ C\). It is then immersed in cold surroundings with an ambient temperature of \(30^\circ C\), and a natural heat transfer coeffcient of \(h=7W/m^2K\). The thermal conductivity of copper is 369 W/mk.
1. For this transient conduction problem, determine the Biot number.
2. Can lumped capacitance method be used to solve this problem?
3. If the answer to (2) is yes, determine the temperature at the center of the sphere after 100 s.
4. What is the total enthalpy lost by the copper sphere in the frst 50 s? (The thermophysical properties of copper are thermal conductivity = 396 W/mK density = 8960 \(kg/m^3\), and specifc heat = 385 J/kg k).

Admin
Solution

Given data

The diameter of sphere (D) = 12 mm; initial temperature of the sphere \((T_i)=100^\circ C\) ;

ambient temperature \((T_\infty )=30^\circ C\) ;

heat transfer coeffcient \((h)=7W/m^2K\);

thermal conductivity \((k)=396W/mK\)

\(Bi=\frac{hL_c}{k} \)

 

\(L_c=\frac{V_s}{A_s}=\frac{4/3\pi r^3}{4\pi r^2} \)

 

\(L_c=\frac{D}{6} \)

This is a key result. The characteristic length in transient conduction is L for a plain slab of thickness 2L, R/2 or D/4 for a cylinder of radius R and R/3 or D/6 for a sphere of a radius of R, as already mentioned.

1- \(Bi=\frac{7\times 12\times 10^{-3}}{6\times 396} =35.35\times 10^{-6}\)

2- Since Bi < 0.1, the lumped capacitance method is applicable.

3- From the solution for lumped capacitance heat transfer, the temperature excess is given by

\(\frac{T-T_\infty }{T_i-T_\infty } =\exp \left\lgroup\frac{-hA_s}{\rho Vc_{_{p}}}\times t \right\rgroup \)

The temperature of the sphere at the center after 100 s (same everywhere in the sphere under the lumped capacitance assumption)

\(\frac{T_{100s}-30}{100-30}=\exp \left\lgroup\frac{-7\times 6\times 10^3}{8960\times 12\times 385}\times 100 \right\rgroup \)

 

\(T=30+70\times \exp \left\lgroup\frac{-42\times 10^3\times 10^2}{8960\times 12\times 385} \right\rgroup \)

 

\(T_{_{100s}}=93.25^\circ C\) .

4- Enthalpy lost \(=mc_p(T_i-T_{50s})=\rho Vc_p(T_i-T_{50s})\)

To be able to calculate this, frst we need to obtain the temperature of the sphere at 50 s

\(\frac{T_{50s}-30}{100-30}=\exp \left\lgroup\frac{-7\times 6\times 10^3}{8960\times 12\times 385}\times 50 \right\rgroup \)

 

\(T=30+70\times \exp \left\lgroup\frac{-2.1\times 10^6}{8960\times 12\times 385} \right\rgroup \)

 

\(T_{_{50s}}=96.54^\circ C\) .

Enthalpy lost \(=8960\times 9.04\times 10^{-7}\times 385\times (100-96.54)=10.79 J\)
