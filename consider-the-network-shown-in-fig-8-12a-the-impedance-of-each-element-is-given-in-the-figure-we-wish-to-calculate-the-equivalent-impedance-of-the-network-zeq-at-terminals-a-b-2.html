Guest Question.

Consider the network shown in Fig. 8.12a. The impedance of each element is given in the figure. We wish to calculate the equivalent impedance of the network \( Z_{eq} \) at terminals A–B.

Admin
Solution

The equivalent impedance \( Z _{ eq } \) could be calculated in a variety of ways; we could use only impedances, or only admittances, or a combination of the two. We will use the latter. We begin by noting that the circuit in Fig. 8.12a can be represented by the circuit in Fig. 8.12b.
Note that
\(Y _{4}= Y _{L}+ Y _{C}\)
\(=\frac{1}{j 4}+\frac{1}{-j 2}\)
\(=j \frac{1}{4} S\)
Therefore,
\(Z _{4}=-j 4 \Omega\)
Now
\(Z _{34}= Z _{3}+ Z _{4}\)
= (4 + j2) + (–j4)
= 4 – j2 Ω
and hence,
\(Y _{34}=\frac{1}{ Z _{34}}\)
\(=\frac{1}{4-j 2}\)
= 0.20 + j0.10 S
Since
\(Z _{2}=2+j 6-j 2\)
= 2 + j4 Ω
then
\(Y _{2}=\frac{1}{2+j 4}\)
= 0.10 − j0.20 S
\(Y _{234}= Y _{2}+ Y _{34}\)
= 0.30 − j0.10 S
The reader should carefully note our approach: we are adding impedances in series and adding admittances in parallel.
From \( Y _{234}\) we can compute \( Z _{234}\) as
\(Z _{234}=\frac{1}{ Y _{234}}\)
\(=\frac{1}{0.30-j 0.10}\)
= 3 + j1 Ω
Now
\(Y _{1}= Y _{R}+ Y _{C}\)
\(=\frac{1}{1}+\frac{1}{-j 2}\)
\(=1+j \frac{1}{2} S\)
and then
\(Z _{1}=\frac{1}{1+j \frac{1}{2}}\)
= 0.8 − j0.4 Ω
Therefore,
\( Z _{ eq }= Z _{1}+ Z _{234}\)
= 0.8 − j0.4 + 3 + j1
= 3.8 + j0.6 Ω
