Guest Question.

Transform the stator supply three-phase voltages of an induction machine to

a) two-phase stationary reference frame and

b) synchronously rotating reference frame.

Admin
Solution

Supposing that the stator three-phase voltages are sinusoidal and symmetrical, then the following equations hold:

\(v_{as} = \sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i \cos\omega t \) (1)

\(v_{bs} = \sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i \cos(\omega t -120°) \) (2)

\(v_{cs} = \sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i \cos(\omega t -240°) \) (3)

\(v_{as} + v_{bs} + v_{cs} = 0\) (4)

where

\(\omega\)= supply angular frequency.

a) Using Eq. (\(\begin{bmatrix} f_{ds^s} \\ f_{qs^s} \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} =T^{as-bs-cs }_{ds^s-qs^s} \begin{bmatrix} f_{as} \\ f_{bs} \\ f_{cs} \end{bmatrix}=\frac{2}{3}\begin{bmatrix} 1 &-\frac{1}{2} &-\frac{1}{2} \\ 0 & \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} &-\frac{\sqrt{3} }{2} \\ \frac{1}{\sqrt{2} } & \frac{1}{\sqrt{2} } & \frac{1}{\sqrt{2} } \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} f_{as} \\ f_{bs} \\ f_{cs} \end{bmatrix} \)), the following equations are obtained:

\(\begin{bmatrix} v_{ds^s} \\ v_{qs^s} \end{bmatrix} = \frac{2}{3}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -\frac{1}{2} & -\frac{1}{2} \\ 0 & \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} & -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} v_{as} \\ v_{bs} \\ v_{cs} \end{bmatrix}\) (5)

or

\(v_{ds^s} = \frac{2}{3}(v_{as} -\frac{1}{2}v_{bs} -\frac{1}{2}v_{cs})\) (6)

\(v_{qs^s} =\frac{2}{3}(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}v_{bs} – \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}v_{cs}\) (7)

Substituting \(v_{as}=-(v_{bs} + v_{cs})\) into Eqs. (6) and (7) yields:

\(v_{ds^s} =\frac{2}{3}(v_{as} + \frac{1}{2}v_{as}) = \frac{2}{3}(\frac{3}{2}v_{as}) = \sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i \cos\omega t = \sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i \angle 0°\) (8)

\(v_{qs^s} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(v_{bs} – v_{cs}) = \frac{\sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i}{\sqrt{3}} (\cos(\omega t -120°) – \cos(\omega t -240°) )\)

 

\( = \sqrt{\frac{2}{3}}\widetilde{V}_i (\sqrt{3}\sin\omega t) = \sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i \sin \omega_e t =\sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i \angle 90°\) (9)

b) Using Eq. (\(\left[\begin{matrix} f_{ds^e} \\ f_{qs^e} \end{matrix} \right] =T^{ds^s-qs^s}_{ds^e-qs^e} \left[\begin{matrix} f_{ds^s} \\ f_{qs^s} \end{matrix} \right] =\left[\begin{matrix} \cos \omega_e t & \sin \omega_e t \\ -\sin \omega_e t & \cos \omega_e t \end{matrix} \right]\left[ \begin{matrix} f_{ds^s} \\ f_{qs^s} \end{matrix} \right] \)) the transformation of the supply three-phase voltages to two-phase synchronously rotating reference frame is obtained and is given by the following equation:

\(\begin{bmatrix} v_{ds^e} \\ v_{qs^e} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \cos \omega_e t & \sin \omega_e t \\ -\sin \omega_e t & \cos \omega_e t \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_{ds^s} \\ v_{qs^s} \end{bmatrix}\) (10)

or

\(v_{ds^e} = v_{ds^s} \cos \omega_e t + v_{qs^s} \sin \omega_e t\) (11)

\(v_{qs^e }= -v_{ds^s} \sin \omega_e t + v_{qs^s} \cos \omega_e t\) (12)

Next, substituting Eqs. (8) and (9) into Eqs. (11) and (12) the synchronously rotating reference frame components are found and are given by:

\(v_{ds^e} = (\sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i \cos\omega_e t)(\cos \omega_et) + (\sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i \sin\omega_e t)(\sin \omega_et)\)

 

\( =\sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i (\frac{1+\cos 2\omega_e t}{2}) +\sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i (\frac{1-\cos 2\omega_e t}{2}) =\sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i\) (13)

\(v_{qs^e} = (-\sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i \cos\omega_e t)(\sin \omega_et) + (\sqrt{2}\widetilde{V}_i \sin\omega_e t)(\cos \omega_et) = 0\) (14)

Eqs. (13) and (14) indicate that the sinusoidal components of the supply voltages have been transformed to dc components in the synchronously rotating reference frame.
