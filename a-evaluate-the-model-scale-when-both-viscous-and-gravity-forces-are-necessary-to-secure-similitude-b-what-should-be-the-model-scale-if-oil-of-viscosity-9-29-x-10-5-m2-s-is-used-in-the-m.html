Guest Question.

(a) Evaluate the model scale when both viscous and gravity forces are necessary to secure similitude.
(b) What should be the model scale if oil of viscosity 9.29 x 10–5 m2/s is used in the model tests and the prototype liquid has a viscosity of 74.3 x 10–5 m2/s ?
(c) What would be the velocity and flow ratios for these liquids for the 1:4 model-prototype scale?

Admin
Solution

(a) For this situation, both the Reynolds number and the Froude number must be satisfied simultaneously. We shall equate the velocity ratios for each model law. Using information from and ,

Reynolds number \(V_{r} \) = Froude number \(V_{r} \)

\((\nu /L)_{r}=\sqrt{L_{r}g_{r}} \)

Since \(g_{r}=1 \) , we obtain \(L_{r}=\nu ^{2/3}_{r} \) .

(b) Using the above length ratio, \(L_{r}=\left(\frac{9.29\times 10^{-5}}{74.3\times 10^{-5}} \right)^{2/3}=\frac{1}{4} \) . Model scale is 1:4.

(c) Using the Froude model laws (see and ),

\(V_{r}=\sqrt{L_{r}g_{r}}=\sqrt{L_{r}}=\sqrt{\frac{1}{4} }=\frac{1}{2} \) and \(Q_{r}=L^{5/2}_{r}=\left(\frac{1}{4} \right)^{5/2}=\frac{1}{32} \)

Or, using the Reynolds model laws (see ),

\(V_{r}=\frac{\nu _{r}}{L_{r}}=\frac{9.29/74.3}{1/4} =\frac{1}{2} \) and \(Q_{r}=A_{r}V_{r}=L^2_{r}\times \frac{\nu _{r}}{L_{r}}=L_{r}\nu _{r}=\left(\frac{1}{4} \right)\left(\frac{9.29}{74.3} \right)=\frac{1}{32} \)
