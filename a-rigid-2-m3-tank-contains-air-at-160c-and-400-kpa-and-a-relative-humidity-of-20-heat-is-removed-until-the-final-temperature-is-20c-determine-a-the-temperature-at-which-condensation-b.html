Guest Question.

A rigid 2-m³ tank contains air at 160°C and 400 kPa and a relative humidity of 20%. Heat is removed until the final temperature is 20°C. Determine (a) the temperature at which condensation begins, (b) the mass of water condensed during the process, and (c) the heat transfer.

Admin
Solution

(a) The pressure in this problem is not atmospheric, so the psychrometric chart is not applicable. The initial partial pressure of the vapor is \( P_{v 1}=\phi P_{g 1}=(0.2)(617.8)=123.6 kPa \) . The specific volume of the water vapor is

\( v_{v 1}=\frac{R_{v} T_{1}}{P_{v 1}}=\frac{(0.462)(433)}{123.6}=1.62 m ^{3} / kg _{v} \).

At this specific volume (the volume remains constant), the temperature at which condensation begins is \( T_{\text {cond }}= \) 92.5°C.

\( \text { (b) The partial pressure of the dry air is } P_{a 1}=P-P_{v 1}=400-123.6=276.4 kPa \text {. The mass of dry air is } \)

\( m_{a}=\frac{P_{a 1} V_{1}}{R_{a} T_{1}}=\frac{(276.4)(2)}{(0.287)(433)}=4.45 kg _{a} \).

The initial humidity ratio is

\( \omega_{1}=0.622 \frac{P_{v 1}}{P_{a 1}}=(0.622)\left(\frac{123.6}{276.4}\right)=0.278 kg _{v} / kg _{a} \).

\( \text { The final relative humidity is } \phi_{2}=1.0 \text {, so that } P_{v 2}=2.338 kPa \text {. The final partial pressure of the dry air results } \) \( \text { from } P_{a 1} / T_{1}=P_{a 2} / T_{2} \text {, so that } P_{a 2}=\left(P_{a 1}\right)\left(T_{2} / T_{1}\right)=(276.4)(293 / 433)=187 kPa \text {. The final humidity ratio becomes } \)

\( \omega_{2}=0.622 \frac{P_{v 2}}{P_{a 2}}=(0.622)\left(\frac{2.338}{187}\right)=0.00778 kg _{v} / kg _{a} \).

\( \text { The moisture removed is } m_{\text {cond }}=m_{a}\left(\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}\right)=(4.45)(0.278-0.00778)=1.20 kg _{v} \text {. } \)

(c) The heat transfer results from the first law:

\( Q=m_{a}\left(u_{a 2}-u_{a 1}\right)+m_{v 2} u_{v 2}-m_{v 1} u_{v 1}+\Delta m_{w}\left(h_{f g}\right)_{ avg } \).

\( =m_{a}\left[C_{p}\left(T_{2}-T_{1}\right)+\omega_{2} u_{v 2}-\omega_{1} u_{v 1}+\left(\omega_{2}-\omega_{1}\right)\left(h_{f g}\right)_{ avg }\right] \).

\( \text { Treating the vapor as an ideal gas, that is, } u_{v}=u_{g} \text { at the given temperatures, we have } \)

Q = (4.45)[(0.717)(20 − 160) + (0.00778)(2402.9)
− (0.278)(2568.4) + (0.00778 − 0.278)(2365)] = − 6290 kJ.
