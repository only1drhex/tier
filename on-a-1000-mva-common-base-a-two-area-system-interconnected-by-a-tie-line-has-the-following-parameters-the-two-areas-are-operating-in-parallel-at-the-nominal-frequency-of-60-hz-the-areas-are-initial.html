Guest Question.

On a 1000-MVA common base, a two-area system interconnected by a tie line has the following parameters:

Area 1 2
Area Frequency Response Characteristic \(\beta_{1}=0.05 \text { per unit }\) \(\beta_{2}=0.0625 \text { per unit }\)
Frequency-Dependent Load Coefficient \(D _{1}=0.6 \text { per unit }\) \(D _{2}=0.9 \text { per unit }\)
Base Power 1000 MVA 1000 MVA
Governor Time Constant \(\tau_{g 1}=0.25 s\) \(\tau_{g 2}=0.3 s\)
Turbine Time Constant \(\tau_{ t 1}=0.5 s\) \(\tau_{ t 2}=0.6 s\)
Admin
Solution

(a) The per-unit load change in area 1 is

\(\Delta P_{L 1}=\frac{187.5}{1000}=0.1875\)

The per-unit steady-state frequency deviation is

\(\Delta W_{S S}=\frac{-\Delta P_{L 1}}{\left(\frac{1}{R_{1}}+D_{1}\right)+\left(\frac{1}{R_{2}}+D_{2}\right)}=\frac{-0.1875}{(20+0.6)+(16+0.9)}=-0.005\)

Thus, the steady-state frequency deviation in Hz is

\(\Delta f=(-0.005)(60)=-0.3 Hz\)

and the new frequency is \(f=f_{0}+\Delta f=60-0.3=59.7 Hz \text {. }\)

The change in mechanical power in each area is

\(\begin{aligned}&\Delta P_{m 1}=-\frac{\Delta W}{R_{1}}=-\frac{-0.005}{0.05}=0.1 pu =100 MW \\&\Delta P_{m 2}=-\frac{\Delta W}{R_{2}}=-\frac{-0.005}{0.0625}=0.08 pu =80 MW\end{aligned}\)

Thus area 1 increases the generation by 100 MW and area 2 by 80 MW at the new operating frequency of 59.7 Hz.

The total change in generation is 180 MW, which is 7.5 MW less than the 187.5 MW load change because of the change in the area loads due to frequency drop.

The change in area 1 load is \(\Delta W \cdot D_{1}=(-0.005)(0.6)=-0.003 pu \text { or }-3.0 MW\) , and the change in area 2 load is \(\Delta W \cdot D_{2}=(-0.005)(0.9)=-0.0045 pu \text { or }-4.5 MW\).

Thus, the change in the total area load is âˆ’7.5MW. The tie-line power flow is

\(\begin{gathered}\Delta P_{12}=\Delta W\left(\frac{1}{R_{2}}+D_{2}\right)=-0.005(16.9)=-0.0845 pu \\=-84.5 MW\end{gathered}\)

That is, 84.5 MW flows from area 2 to area 1. 80 MW comes from the increased generation in area 2, and 4.3MW comes from the reduction in area 2 load due to frequency drop.

 

(b) With the inclusion of the \(A C E_{5}\) , the frequency deviation returns to zero (with a settling time of about 20 seconds). Also, the tie-line power change reduces to zero, and the increase in area 1 load is met by the increase in generation \(\Delta P_{m 1}\) .
