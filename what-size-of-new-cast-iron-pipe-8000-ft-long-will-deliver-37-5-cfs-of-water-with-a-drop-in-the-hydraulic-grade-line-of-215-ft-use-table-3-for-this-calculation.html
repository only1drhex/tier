Guest Question.

What size of new cast iron pipe, 8000 ft long, will deliver 37.5 cfs of water with a drop in the hydraulic grade line of 215 ft? Use for this calculation.

Admin
Solution

\(\text { The Bernoulli theorem gives }\left(\frac{p_{A}}{\gamma}+\frac{V_{A}^{2}}{2 g}+z_{A}\right)-f\left(\frac{8000}{d}\right)\left(\frac{V^{2}}{2 g}\right)=\frac{p_{B}}{\gamma}+\frac{V_{B}^{2}}{2 g}+z_{B}\).

or

\(\left[\left(\frac{p_{A}}{\gamma}+z_{A}\right)-\left(\frac{p_{B}}{\gamma}+z_{B}\right)\right]=f\left(\frac{8000}{d}\right)\left(\frac{V^{2}}{2 g}\right)\).

The left-hand term in the brackets represents the drop in the hydraulic grade line. Expressing V as Q/A and assuming turbulent flow,

\(215=f\left(\frac{8000}{d}\right) \frac{\left(37.5 / \frac{1}{4} \pi d^{2}\right)^{2}}{2 g}, \text { which simplifies to } d^{5}=f \frac{(8000)(37.5)^{2}}{(39.7)(215)}=1318 f\).

Assume f = 0.020 (since both d and V are unknown, an assumption is necessary). Then

\(d^{5}=f(1318)=(0.020)(1318)=26.36, d=1.92 ft\).

From , for \(V=\frac{37.5}{\pi(1.92)^{2} / 4}=13.0 ft / sec , f=0.0165\).

For this magnitude of velocity in most pipes, turbulent flow of water exists. Recalculating,

\(d^{5}=(0.0165)(1318)=21.75, d=1.85 ft\).

Checking f, V = 14.0 ft/sec, and gives f = 0.0165 (check).
