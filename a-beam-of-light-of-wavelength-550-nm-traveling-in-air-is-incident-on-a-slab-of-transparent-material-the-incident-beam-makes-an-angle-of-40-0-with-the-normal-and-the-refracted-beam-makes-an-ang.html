Guest Question.

A beam of light of wavelength 550 nm traveling in air is incident on a slab of transparent material. The incident beam makes an angle of 40.0° with the normal, and the refracted beam makes an angle of 26.0° with the normal. Find the index of refraction of the material.

Admin
Solution

Using Snell’s law of refraction (Eq. 35.8) with these data, and taking \( n_{1}=1.00 \) for air, we have

\( n_{1} \sin \theta_{1}=n_{2} \sin \theta_{2} \) (35.8).

\( n_{1} \sin \theta_{1}=n_{2} \sin \theta_{2} \).

\( n_{2}=\frac{n_{1} \sin \theta_{1}}{\sin \theta_{2}}=(1.00) \frac{\sin 40.0^{\circ}}{\sin 26.0^{\circ}} \).

\( =\frac{0.643}{0.438}\).

From Table 35.1, we see that the material could be fused quartz.
