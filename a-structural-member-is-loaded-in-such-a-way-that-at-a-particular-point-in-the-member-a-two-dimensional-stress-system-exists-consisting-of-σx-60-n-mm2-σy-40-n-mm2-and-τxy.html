Guest Question.

A structural member is loaded in such a way that at a particular point in the member a two-dimensional stress system exists consisting of \(\sigma_{x}=+60 N / mm ^{2}, \sigma_{y}=-40 N / mm ^{2}\) and \(\tau_{x y}=50 N / mm ^{2}\).

a. Calculate the direct strain in the x and y directions and the shear strain, \(\gamma_{x y}\), at the point.

b. Calculate the principal strains at the point and determine the position of the principal planes.

c. Verify your answer using a graphical method. Take \(E=200000 N / mm ^{2}\) and Poisson’s ratio, v=0.3 .

Admin
Solution

a. From Section 7.8

\(\varepsilon_{x}=\frac{1}{200000}(60+0.3 \times 40)=360 \times 10^{-6}\)

 

\(\varepsilon_{y}=\frac{1}{200000}(-40-0.3 \times 60)=-290 \times 10^{-6}\)

The shear modulus, G, is obtained using Eq. (7.21);

\(G=\frac{E}{2(1+v)} \text { or } E=2 G(1+v)\) (7.21)

thus

\(G=\frac{E}{2(1+v)}=\frac{200000}{2(1+0.3)}=76923 N / mm ^{2}\)

Hence, from Eq. (7.9)

\(G=\frac{\tau}{\gamma}\) (7.9)

 

\(\gamma_{x y}=\frac{\tau_{x y}}{G}=\frac{50}{76923}=650 \times 10^{-6}\)

b. Now substituting in Eqs (14.21)

\(\varepsilon_{ I }=\frac{\varepsilon_{x}+\varepsilon_{y}}{2}+\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\varepsilon_{x}-\varepsilon_{y}\right)^{2}+\gamma_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.21)

and (14.22)

\(\varepsilon_{ II }=\frac{\varepsilon_{x}+\varepsilon_{y}}{2}-\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\left(\varepsilon_{x}-\varepsilon_{y}\right)^{2}+\gamma_{x y}^{2}}\) (14.22)

for \(\varepsilon_{x}, \varepsilon_{y}\) and \(\gamma_{x y}\) we have

\(\varepsilon_{ I }=10^{-6}\left[\frac{360-290}{2}+\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{(360+290)^{2}+650^{2}}\right]\)

which gives

\(\varepsilon_{ I }=495 \times 10^{-6}\)

Similarly

\(\varepsilon_{ II }=-425 \times 10^{-6}\)

From Eq. (14.23)

\(\tan 2 \theta=-\frac{\gamma_{x y}}{\varepsilon_{x}-\varepsilon_{y}}\) (14.23)

we have

\(\tan 2 \theta=-\frac{650 \times 10^{-6}}{360 \times 10^{-6}+290 \times 10^{-6}}=-1\)

Therefore

\(2 \theta=-45^{\circ} \text { or }-225^{\circ}\)

so that

\(\theta=-22.5^{\circ} \text { or }-112.5^{\circ}\)

c. Axes \(O \varepsilon\) and \(O _{\gamma}\) are set up and the points \(Q _{1}\left(360 \times 10^{-6},-\frac{1}{2} \times 650 \times 10^{-6}\right)\) and \(Q_{2}\left(-290 \times 10^{-6}, \frac{1}{2} \times 650 \times 10^{-6}\right)\) located. The centre C of the circle is the intersection of \(Q_{1} Q_{2}\) and the \(O \varepsilon\) axis (Fig. 14.16). The circle is then drawn with radius equal to \(CQ _{1}\) and the points \(B \left(\varepsilon_{ I }\right)\) and \(A \left(\varepsilon_{ II }\right)\) located. Finally, angle \(Q_{1} C B=-2 \theta\) and \(Q_{1} C A=-2 \theta-\pi \).
