Guest Question.

Determine whether X and Y are independent for the probability distribution of , , and with

\( p_{XY} (u, v) = (8e^{-4u-4v} + 3e^{-4u-3v} + 3e^{-3u-4v})U(u)U(v) \)
Admin
Solution

Using the marginal distribution from , we have

\( p_{X}(u)p_{Y}(v)=9(e^{-4u}+e^{-3u}/4)(e^{-4v}+e^{-3v}/4)U(u)U(v) \)

This time we do find that the domain of nonzero values is the same for \(p_{X}(u) p_{Y}(v) \) as for \(p_{XY}(u,v) \) (i.e., the first quadrant), but the functions of \(u\) and \(v\) are not the same. Thus, \(X\) and \(Y\) are not independent.

Looking at conditional probabilities, we found in and that the marginal distribution for \(X\) could be described by

\( p_{X}(u)=3(e^{-4u}+e^{-3u}/4)U(u) \)

and the conditional distribution had

\( p_{X}(u \mid Y = v)= \frac{(8e^{-4u-4v} + 3e^{-4u-3v} + 3e^{-3u-4v})}{3(e^{-4v}+e^{-3v}/4)} U(u) \)

Thus, the set of possible values of \(X\) is the semi-infinite interval \(X \gt 0\), and knowledge of the value of \(Y\) does not change this set. Knowledge of the value of \(Y\), though, does change the probability distribution on this set of possible values, confirming that \(X\) and \(Y\) are not independent.
