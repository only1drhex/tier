Guest Question.

Given:

The time-deformation data for the load increment 100 to 200 kPa of the test in Fig. 8.4. From Table 9.2 and Fig. 9.12, a \(c_{v} \text { value of } 0.81 m ^{2} / yr \left(2.56 \times 10^{-4} cm ^{2} / s \right)\) can be determined.

Required:

Compute the coefficient of permeability, assuming the temperature of the water is 20°C.

Solution

It is first necessary to compute the coefficient of compressibility from Eq. (8.5) and using Fig. 8.4(b):

 

\(a_{v}=\frac{-d e}{d \sigma_{v}^{\prime}}\) (8.5a)

 

\(a_{v}=\frac{-\Delta e}{\Delta \sigma_{v}^{\prime}}=\frac{e_{1}-e_{2}}{\sigma_{2}^{\prime}-\sigma_{1}^{\prime}}\) (8.5b)

 

\(\begin{aligned}a_{v} &=\frac{e_{1}-e_{2}}{\sigma_{2}^{\prime}-\sigma_{1}^{\prime}}=\frac{2.12-1.76}{(200-100) kPa } \\&=0.0036 / kPa =3.6 \times 10^{-6} \frac{ m ^{2}}{ N }\end{aligned}\)

 

From Eq. (9.24),

 

\(k=\frac{c_{v} \rho_{w} g a_{v}}{1+e_{o}}\) (9.24)

 

\(\begin{aligned}k &=\frac{c_{v} \rho_{w} g a_{v}}{1+e_{o}} \\&=\frac{2.56 \times 10^{-4} \frac{ cm ^{2}}{ s } \times 1000 \frac{ kg }{ m ^{3}} \times 9.81 \frac{ m }{ s ^{2}} \times 3.6 \times 10^{-6} \frac{ m ^{2}}{ N } \frac{1 m }{100 cm }}{1+2.12} \\&=2.9 \times 10^{-6} \frac{ cm }{ s }=2.9 \times 10^{-8} \frac{ m }{ s }\end{aligned}\)

 

Note that the e used in the equation is the void ratio at the start of the load increment rather than the original or in situ void ratio.
