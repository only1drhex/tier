Guest Question.

Given:

A 10 m thick clay layer with single drainage settles 9 em in 3.5 yr. The coefficient of consolidation for this clay was found to be \(0.544 \times 10^{-2} cm ^{2} / s\).

Required:

Compute the ultimate consolidation settlement, and find out how long it will take to settle to 90% of this amount.

Admin
Solution

From Eq. (9.5) solve for T:

 

\(T=c_{v} \frac{t}{H_{ dr }^{2}}\) (9.5)

 

\(\begin{aligned}T &=\frac{t c_{v}}{H^{2}} \\&=\frac{3.5 yr \left(0.544 \times 10^{-2}\right) cm ^{2}}{\left(100 m ^{2}\right) s }\left(\frac{1 m ^{2}}{10000 cm ^{2}}\right)\left(3.1536 \times 10^{7} \frac{ s }{ yr }\right) \\&=0.6\end{aligned}\)

 

From Table 9.1 we see that the average degree of consolidation is between 0.8 and 0.9. Therefore we can use either Eq. (9.11) or Fig. 9.5(a); or we can interpolate from Table 9.1. Using Eq. (9.11), we have

 

\(T=1.781-0.933 \log (100-U \%)\) (9.11)

 

\(\begin{aligned}0.6 &=1.781-0.933 \log (100-U \%) \\1.27 &=\log (100-U \%)\end{aligned}\)

 

or

 

\(U=81.56 \%, \text { or } 82 \%\)

 

Thus if 9 em of settlement represents 82% of the total settlement, then the total consolidation settlement is [Eq. (9.12)]:

 

\(U_{ avg }=\frac{s(t)}{s_{c}}\) (9.12)

 

\(s_{c}=\frac{s(t)}{U_{ avg }}=\frac{9 cm }{0.82}=11 cm\)

 

For the time for 90% settlement to occur, find T = 0.848 for \(U_{\text {avg }}=0.9\), from Table 9.1. Using Eq. (9.5) and solving for t, we find that:

 

\(\begin{aligned}t &=\frac{T H_{ dr }^{2}}{c_{v}}=\frac{0.848(10 m )^{2}}{0.544 \times 10^{-2} cm ^{2} / s } \frac{10000 cm ^{2}}{ m ^{2}} \\&=1.559 \times 10^{8} s \frac{ yr }{3.1536 \times 10^{7} s } \\&=4.94 yr\end{aligned}\)
