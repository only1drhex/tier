Guest Question.

if the soil consists of a deep stratum of medium-dense sand. Assume the following additional data:
For the sand, the saturated unit weight = \(19 kN/m^{3} \) and an average moist unit weight above the water table = \(16 kN/m^{3} \). Drained triaxial tests on sand samples show that the angle of shearing resistance of sand \(\varnothing \) varies with mean normal stress \(\sigma \) according to \(\varnothing = \varnothing_{1} - \left ( 5.5^{\circ} \right ) log \frac{\sigma }{\sigma_{1}},\) in which \(\varnothing_{1} = 38^{\circ} = \) the angle of shearing resistance of the sand at mean normal stress \(\sigma _{1} = 100 kPa. \) According to De Beer (1965), the average mean normal stress along the slip surface is \(\sigma = \frac{1}{4} \left ( q_{ult} + 3\overline{q} \right ) \left ( 1 - \sin \varnothing \right ).\) in which \(\overline{q}\) is the effective overburden pressure at the fundation level. In the low and elevated pressure range, the modulus of deformation E of sand increases with the mean normal stress according to the relationship \( E = E_{1} \sqrt{\frac{\sigma }{\sigma_{1}}}\) in which \(E_{1}\) is the modulus at the mean normal stress \(\sigma_{1}\) . Also, the following equations may be needed in solving the Problem: \(K_{0} = 1 - \sin \left ( 1.2 \varnothing \right ) =\) coefficient of earth pressure at rest
\( \mu = \frac{K_{0}}{1 + K_{0}} = \) Poisson’sratio
\( \sigma = \left (\frac{1 + 2K_{0}}{3} \right )\left ( \overline{q} \right ) = \) initial mean normal stress at depth z.

Solution

In sands, the drained condition prevails. Assume incompressibility soil condition.

A preliminary estimate of \( q_{ult} \) is required so that the mean normal stress along the slip surface can be found. For this preliminary analysis, assume that \( \varnothing = 34^{\circ}, \) and Table 4.4 gives \(N_{q} = 29.4; N_{y} = 41. \)

Table 4.6:

\( s_{q} = 1 + \frac{9}{27} \tan 34^{\circ} = 1.22 \)

 

\( s_{y} = 1 – 0.4 \times \frac{B}{L} = 1 – 0.4 \times \frac{9}{27} = 0.87 \)

 

\( d_{q} = 1 + 2 \tan \varnothing \left ( 1 – \sin \varnothing \right )^{2} k \)

 

\( = 1 + 2 \tan 34 \left ( 1 – \sin 34 \right )^{2} \left ( \frac{3}{9} \right ) = 1.09 \)

 

\( d_{y} = 1 \)

Equation : \( r_{y} = 1 – 0.25 log \left ( \frac{B}{K} \right ) for B \geq 2 m \)

 

\( = 1 – 0.25 log \left ( \frac{9}{2} \right ) = 0.84 \)

 

\( \overline{q} = 2.5 \times 16 + 0.5 \left ( 19 – 10 \right ) = 44.5 kPa\)

 

\( gross q_{ult} = 44.5 \times 29.4 \times 1.22 \times 1.09 \)

 

\( + 0.5 \left ( 19 – 10 \right )\left ( 9 \right )\left ( 41 \times 0.87 \times 1 \right ) \left ( 0.84 \right ) \)

 

\( = 1739.78 + 1213.49 = 2953 kPa \)

 

The mean normal stress along the slip surface is

\( \sigma = \frac{1}{4} \left ( q_{ult} + 3 \overline{q} \right ) \left ( 1 – \sin \varnothing \right ) = \frac{1}{4} \left ( 2953 + 3 \times 44.5 \right )\left ( 1 – \sin 34^{\circ} \right ) = 340 kPa\)

 

The representative angle of shearing resistance is \( \varnothing = \varnothing_{1} – \left ( 5.5^{\circ} \right ) log \frac{\sigma }{\sigma_{1}} = 38^{\circ} – \left ( 5.5^{\circ} \right ) log \frac{340}{100} \cong 35^{\circ}. \) The difference between this and the assumed value of \( \varnothing \) is small; however, to improve clarity the analysis is now repeated with \( \varnothing = 35^{\circ} \). Thus, from Table 4.2:

\( N_{q} = e^{\pi \tan \varnothing }\tan^{2} \left ( 45 + \frac{\varnothing }{2} \right ) = e^{\pi \tan 35 }\tan^{2} \left ( 45 + \frac{35}{2} \right ) = 33.4 \)

 

\( N_{y} = 2\left ( N_{q} + 1 \right ) \tan \varnothing = 2 \left ( 33.4 + 1 \right ) \tan 35 = 48.1 \)

 

\( s_{q} = 1 + \frac{9}{27} \tan 35 = 1.23; s_{y} = 1 – 0.4 \times \frac{B}{L} = 1 – 0.4 \times \frac{9}{27} = 0.87 \)

 

\( d_{q} = 1 + 2\tan \varnothing \left ( 1 – \sin \varnothing \right )^{2}k \)

 

\( = 1 + 2 \tan 35\left ( 1 – \sin 35 \right )^{2}\left ( \frac{3}{9} \right ) = 1.08 \)

 

\( d_{y} = 1; r_{y} = 0.84 \)

 

\( gross q_{ult} = 44.5 \times 33.4 \times 1.23 \times 1.08 + 0.5 \left ( 19 – 10 \right )\left ( 9 \right )\left ( 48.1 \times 0.87 \times 1 \right )\left ( 0.84 \right ) \)

 

\( = 1974.4 + 1423.6 = 3398 kPa \)

 

The mean normal stress along the slip surface is

\( \sigma = \frac{1}{4} \left ( q_{ult} + 3 \overline{q} \right ) \left ( 1 – \sin \varnothing \right ) \)

 

\( = \frac{1}{4} \left ( 3398 + 3 \times 44.5 \right ) \left ( 1 – \sin 35 \right ) = 376.5 kPa \)

 

The representative angle of shearing resistance is

\( \varnothing = \varnothing_{1} – \left ( 5.5^{\circ} \right ) log \frac{\sigma }{\sigma_{1}} = 38 – \left ( 5.5^{\circ} \right ) log \frac{376.5 }{100} = 34.8^{\circ}.\)

 

The analysis can be repeated again for more accuracy; however, in view of small change in mean normal stress from the previously found value, a value for gross \( q_{ult}\) using \( \varnothing = 34.8^{\circ}\) would be retained for incompressible soil. For this value of \( \varnothing\) , the equations of Tables 4.2 and 4.6 give

\( N_{q} = 32.8; N_{y} = 47; s_{q} = 1 + \frac{9}{27} \tan 34.7 = 1.23; d_{y} = 1;\)

 

\( s_{y} = 1 – 0.4 \times \frac{9}{27} = 0.87; d_{q} = 1 + 2\tan 34.8 \left ( 1 – \sin 34.8 \right )^{2} \left ( \frac{3}{9} \right ) = 1.09.\)

 

\( gross q_{ult} = 44.5 \times 32.8 \times 1.23 \times 1.09 \)

 

\( + 0.5 \left ( 19 – 10 \right )\left ( 9 \right )\left ( 47 \times 0.87 \times 1 \right )\left ( 0.84 \right ) \)

 

\( = 1956.9 + 1391.1 = 3348 kPa \)

 

To check whether the assumption of incompressibility is justified, the following computations may be necessary.

The mean normal stress in the expansion zone, needed in estimating the elastic parameters E and \( \mu \) of the sand soil, is taken as the initial mean normal stress at a depth \( = B / 2 \) below the foundation level. With \( \varnothing = 38^{\circ} \) for the sand in elastic zone the coefficient of earth pressure at rest is

\( K_{o} = 1 – \sin \left ( 1.2 \varnothing \right ) = 1 – \sin \left ( 1.2 \times 38 \right ) = 0.29 \)

 

The mean normal stress at a depth of 7.5 m, below ground surface, is

\( \sigma = \left ( \frac{1 + 2 K_{o}}{3} \right )\left ( \overline{q} \right ) = \left ( \frac{1 + 2 \times 0.29}{3} \right ) \left ( 2.5 \times 16 + 5 \times 9 \right ) = 44.77 kPa\)

 

the modulus of deformation is

\( E = E_{1} \sqrt{\frac{\sigma }{\sigma_{1}}} = 36400 \sqrt{\frac{44.77}{100}} = 24355 kPa \)

 

Poison’s ratio is \( \mu = \frac{K_{o}}{ 1 + K_{o}} = \frac{0.29}{ 1 + 0.29} = 0.225 \)

The representative angle of shearing resistance for the plastic zone is taken again to be \(34.8^{\circ}\).

The critical rigidity index is

Equation : \( I_{r\left ( cr \right )} = \frac{1}{2} \left \{ exp \left [ \left ( 3.30 – 0.45 \frac{B}{L} \right ) \cot \left ( 45 – \frac{\varnothing }{2} \right ) \right ] \right \} \)

 

\( = \frac{1}{2} \left \{ exp \left [ \left ( 3.30 – 0.45 \times \frac{9}{27} \right ) \cot \left ( 45 – \frac{34.8}{2} \right ) \right ] \right \} = 208 \)

 

\( I_{r} = \frac{G_{s}}{c + \overline{q} \tan \varnothing } = \frac{E_{d}}{2 \left ( 1 + \mu \right ) } \times \frac{1}{ \overline{q} \tan \varnothing_{d} } = \frac{24355}{2\left ( 1 + 0.225 \right )\left [ \left ( 2.5 \times 16 + 5 \times 9 \right ) \tan 34.8 \right ]} = 168 \)

 

\( I_{r} < I_{r\left ( cr \right )} \) . Thus, the assumption of soil incompressibility is not justified. The compressibility factors of Equation need to be used in the bearing capacity equation.

 

Equation : \( \zeta_{qc} = exp \left \{ \left ( – 4.4 + 0.6 \frac{B}{L} \right ) \tan \varnothing + \left [ \frac{\left ( 3.07 \sin \varnothing \right )\left ( log 2 I_{r} \right )}{1 + \sin \varnothing } \right ]\right \} \)

 

\( = exp \left \{ \left ( – 4.4 + 0.6 \frac{9}{27} \right ) \tan 34.8 + \left [ \frac{\left ( 3.07 \sin 34.8 \right)\left ( log 338 \right )}{1 + \sin 34.8} \right ]\right \} = 0.9 \)

 

\( \zeta_{yc} = \zeta_{qc} = 0.9 \)

 

\( gross q_{ult} = \left ( 44.5 \times 32.8 \times 1.23 \times 1.09 \right )\left ( 0.9 \right ) \)

 

\( + \left [ 0.5 \left ( 19 – 10 \right )\left ( 9 \right ) \left ( 47 \times 0.87 \times 1 \right ) \right ] \left ( 0.84 \right )\left ( 0.9 \right ) = 3013 kPa. \)

 

(This value is considered too high; however, the allowable soil pressure may be controlled by maximum tolerable settlement for the structure in question).
