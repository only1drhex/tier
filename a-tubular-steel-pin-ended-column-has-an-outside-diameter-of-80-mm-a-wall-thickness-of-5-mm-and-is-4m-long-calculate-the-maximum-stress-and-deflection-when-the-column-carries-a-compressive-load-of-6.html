Guest Question.

A tubular steel pin-ended column has an outside diameter of 80 mm, a wall thickness of 5 mm, and is 4m long. Calculate the maximum stress and deflection when the column carries a compressive load of 60 kN applied at an eccentricity of 5 mm from its longitudinal axis. Take \(E=200000 N / mm ^{2}\).

Admin
Solution

The area of cross section of the column is given by

\(A=\frac{\pi}{4}\left(80^{2}-70^{2}\right)=1178.1 mm ^{2}\)

The second moment of area of the cross section is

\(I=\frac{\pi}{64}\left(80^{4}-70^{4}\right)=83.2 \times 10^{4} mm ^{4}\)

In the expressions for maximum deflection and maximum bending moment, Eqs (i) and (ii) in Ex. 21.4

\(v_{\max }=e\left[\sec \left(\frac{\mu L}{2}\right)-1\right]\) Eq (i) in Ex. 21.4

\(M_{\max }=-P e \sec \left(\frac{\mu L}{2}\right)\) Eq (ii) in Ex. 21.4

\(\mu=\sqrt{(P / E I)}=\sqrt{\left[60 \times 10^{3} /\left(200000 \times 83.2 \times 10^{4}\right)\right]}=6.005 \times 10^{-4}\)

Then,

\(\frac{\mu L}{2}=6.005 \times 10^{-4} \times 4 \times 10^{3}=1.201\)

and \(\sec \frac{\mu L}{2}=2.767\)

Now substituting in Eq. (i) of Ex. 21.4

\(\nu_{\max }=5(2.767-1)=8.53 mm\)

From Eq. (ii) of Ex. 21.4

\(M_{\max }=60 \times 10^{3} \times 5 \times 2.767=83.01 \times 10^{4} Nmm\)

From Eq. (21.62)

\(\sigma=-\frac{P}{A}+\frac{M c}{I}\) (21.62)

\(\sigma_{\max }=-\frac{60 \times 10^{3}}{1178.1}-\frac{83.01 \times 10^{4} \times 40}{83.2 \times 10^{4}}=-90.84 N / mm ^{2}\)

Note that this stress will occur on the concave surface of the column where the direct stress due to bending is compressive.
