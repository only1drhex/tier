Guest Question.

Calculate the equilibrium constant for the vapor-phase hydration of ethylene at \(145^{\circ}C\) and at \(320^{\circ}C\) from data given in App. C.

Admin
Solution

First determine values for \(\Delta A, \Delta B, \Delta C,\) and \(\Delta D\) for the reaction:

\( C _{2} H _{4}(g)+ H _{2} O (g) \rightarrow C _{2} H _{5} OH (g) \)

The meaning of \(\Delta\) is indicated by: \(\Delta= (C_{2}H_{5}OH) − (C_{2}H_{4}) − (H_{2}O).\) Thus, from the heat-capacity data of :

\( \begin{aligned} & \Delta A=3.518-1.424-3.470=-1.376 \\ & \Delta B=(20.001-14.394-1.450) \times 10^{-3}=4.157 \times 10^{-3} \\ & \Delta C=(-6.002+4.392-0.000) \times 10^{-6}=-1.610 \times 10^{-6} \\ & \Delta D=(-0.000-0.000-0.121) \times 10^{5}=-0.121 \times 10^{5} \end{aligned} \)

Values of \(\Delta H^{ \circ}_{298}\) and \(\Delta G^{ \circ}_{298}\) at 298.15 K for the hydration reaction are found from the heat-of-formation and Gibbs-energy-of-formation data of :

\( \begin{gathered} \Delta H_{298}^{\circ}=-235,100-52,510-(-241,818)=-45,792 J \cdot mol ^{-1} \\ \Delta G_{298}^{\circ}=-168,490-68,460-(-228,572)=-8378 J \cdot mol ^{-1} \end{gathered} \)

For T = 145 + 273.15 = 418.15 K, values of the integrals in Eq. (14.18) are:

\( \frac{\Delta G^{\circ}}{R T}=\frac{\Delta G_{0}^{\circ}-\Delta H_{0}^{\circ}}{R T_{0}}+\frac{\Delta H_{0}^{\circ}}{R T}+\frac{1}{T} \int_{T_{0}}^{T} \frac{\Delta C_{P}^{\circ}}{R} d T-\int_{T_{0}}^{T} \frac{\Delta C_{P}^{\circ}}{R} \frac{d T}{T} \) (14.18)

IDCPH(298.15, 418.15; -1.376, 4.157E-3, -1.610E-6, -0.121E+5) = −23.121
IDCPS(298.15, 418.15; -1.376, 4.157E-3, -1.610E-6, -0.121E+5) = −0.0692
Substitution of values into Eq. (14.18) for a reference temperature of 298.15 gives:

\( \frac{\Delta G_{418}^{\circ}}{R T}=\frac{-8378+45,792}{(8.314) (298.15)}+\frac{-45,792}{(8.314)(418.15)}+\frac{-23.121}{418.15}+0.0692=1.9356 \)

For \(T = 320 + 273.15 = 593.15 K,\)

IDCPH ( 298.15, 593.15; -1.376, 4.157E-3, -1.610E-6, -0.121E+5 ) = 22.632
IDCPS(298.15, 593.15; -1.376, 4.157E-3, -1.610E-6, -0.121E+5) = 0.0173

Whence,

\( \frac{\Delta G_{593}^{\circ}}{R T}=\frac{-8378+45,792}{(8.314) (298.15)}+\frac{-45,792}{(8.314)(593.15)}+\frac{22.632}{593.15}-0.0173=5.8286 \)

Finally,

\( \begin{array}{llll} \text { @ } 418.15 K : & \ln K=-1.9356 & \text { and } & K=1.443 \times 10^{-1} \\ \text { @ } 593.15 K : & \ln K=-5.8286 & \text { and } & K=2.942 \times 10^{-3} \end{array} \)

Application of Eqs. (14.21), (14.22), and (14.24) provides an alternative solution to this example. By Eq. (14.21),

\( K_{0} \equiv \exp \left(\frac{-\Delta G_{0}^{\circ}}{R T_{0}}\right) \) (14.21)

 

\( K_{1} \equiv \exp \left[\frac{\Delta H_{0}^{\circ}}{R T_{0}}\left(1-\frac{T_{0}}{T}\right)\right] \) (14.22)

 

\( \begin{aligned} K_{2}=& \exp \left\{\Delta A\left[\ln \frac{T}{T_{0}}-\frac{T-T_{0}}{T}\right]+\frac{1}{2} \Delta B \frac{\left(T-T_{0}\right)^{2}}{T}\right.\\ &\left.+\frac{1}{6} \Delta C \frac{\left(T-T_{0}\right)^{2}\left(T+2 T_{0}\right)}{T}+\frac{1}{2} \Delta D \frac{\left(T-T_{0}\right)^{2}}{T^{2} T_{0}^{2}}\right\} \end{aligned} \) (14.24)

 

\( K_{0}=\exp \frac{8378}{(8.314)(298.15)}=29.366 \)

Moreover, \( \frac{\Delta H_{0}^{\circ}}{R T_{0}}=\frac{-45,792}{(8.314)(298.15)}=-18.473 \)

With these values, the following results are readily obtained:

K \(K_{2}\) \(K_{1}\) \(K_{0}\) T/K
29.366 1 1 29.366 298.15
\(1.443 \times 10^{−1}\) 0.9860 \(4.985 \times 10^{−3}\) 29.366 418.15
\(2.942 \times 10^{−3}\) 0.9794 \(1.023 \times 10^{−4}\) 29.366 593.15

Clearly, the influence of \(K_{1}\) is far greater than that of \(K_{2}.\) This is a typical result and is consistent with the observation that all of the lines on Fig. 14.2 are very nearly linear.
