Guest Question.

The motor shown in Figure P6.57/58 turns shaft (2) at 2 Hz. Shafts (1) and (2) are each solid 1- in.-diameter steel [G = 12,000 ksi] shafts with lengths of \(L_{1}\) = 32 in. and \(L_{2}\) = 45 in., respectively. The bearings shown permit free rotation of the shafts. If the rotation angle of gear D with respect to flange A, must not exceed 3.0°, what is the maximum power that is permissible for the motor?

Admin
Solution

The torque in shaft (2) is equal to the torque applied to gear D. Shaft (2) applies its torque to gear C;
therefore:

\(T_{C}=T_{2}=T_{D}\)

The magnitude of the torque applied to gear B is:

\(T_{B}=\frac{N_{B}}{N_{C}} T_{C}=\frac{36 \text { teeth }}{54 \text { teeth }} T_{C}=0.6667 T_{C}\)

and the torque in shaft (1) will equal the torque of gear B:

\(T_{1}=T_{B}=0.6667 T_{C}=0.6667 T_{D}\)

To determine the proper signs for \(T_{1} \text { and } T_{2}\), consider equilibrium. First, consider a FBD that cuts through shaft (2) and includes gear D.

\(\Sigma M_{x}=T_{D}-T_{2}=0 \quad \therefore T_{2}=T_{D}\)

Since \(T_{2}\) is positive, the torque in shaft (1) must be negative; therefore,

\(T_{1}=-0.6667 T_{D}\)

Rotation angles of gears B, C, and D: The rotation of gear B is found from the angle of twist in shaft (1):

\(\phi_{1}=\phi_{B}-\phi_{A} \quad \therefore \phi_{B}=\phi_{A}+\phi_{1}\)

As gear B rotates, gear C also rotates but it rotates in the opposite direction. The magnitude of the rotation is dictated by the gear ratio:

\(\phi_{C}=-\frac{N_{B}}{N_{C}} \phi_{B}=-\frac{36 \text { teeth }}{54 \text { teeth }} \phi_{B}=-0.6667 \phi_{B}\)

The rotation of gear D can be expressed as:

\(\phi_{2}=\phi_{D}-\phi_{C} \quad \therefore \phi_{D}=\phi_{C}+\phi_{2}\)

Altogether, the rotation of gear D with respect to flange A is given by:

\(\phi_{D}=\phi_{C}+\phi_{2}=-0.6667 \phi_{B}+\phi_{2}=-0.6667\left(\phi_{A}+\phi_{1}\right)+\phi_{2}=-0.6667 \phi_{1}+\phi_{2}\) (a)

Section properties: The polar moments of inertia for shafts (1) and (2) are equal:

\(J_{1}=\frac{\pi}{32} d_{1}^{4}=\frac{\pi}{32}(1.00 in .)^{4}=0.098175 in .^{4}=J_{2}\)

Additionally, the shear moduli for the two shafts are identical: \(G_{1}=G_{2}=12,000 ksi\).

Substitute torque-twist relationships: Substitute the torque-twist relationships for shafts (1) and (2)

\(\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}} \quad \phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}\)

into Equation (a):

\(\phi_{D}=-0.6667 \frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}\)

Then, substitute the expressions for \(T_{1} \text { and } T_{2}\) obtained previously from equilibrium to obtain and express for the rotation angle at D in terms of the torque applied at gear D.

\(\phi_{D}=-0.6667 \frac{\left(-0.6667 T_{D}\right) L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{D} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}=\frac{T_{D}}{J G}\left[0.444444 L_{1}+L_{2}\right]\)

Determine allowable torque based on rotation angle limit: Since the rotation angle at D must not exceed 3.0°, the torque applied to gear D must be:

\(\frac{T_{D}}{J G}\left[0.444444 L_{1}+L_{2}\right] \leq 3.0^{\circ}\)

 

\(\therefore T_{D} \leq \frac{\left(3.0^{\circ}\right)\left(\frac{\pi}{180^{\circ}}\right)\left(0.098175 in .^{4}\right)\left(12 \times 10^{6} psi \right)}{0.444444(32 \text { in. })+45 in .}=1,041.586 lb \text {-in. }=86.799 lb – ft\)

 

Power transmission: The power of the system at gear D is:

\(P \leq T_{D} \omega_{D}=86.799 lb – ft \left(\frac{2 rev }{ s }\right)\left(\frac{2 \pi rad }{1 rev }\right)\left(\frac{1 hp }{550 lb – ft / s }\right)=1.983 hp\)

The power at the motor is the same as the power at gear D; therefore, the motor power must be limited to:

\(P \leq 1.983 hp\)
