Use the data in to test \(H_{0}: \mu=16\) versus \(H_{1}: \mu \neq 16\).

Solution

Since the null hypothesis is of the form \(H_{0}: \mu=\mu_{0}\), this is a two-tailed test. The observed value of the test statistic is \(S_{+}=3\). Consulting Table A.5, we find that the area in the left-hand tail, corresponding to values less than or equal to 3 , is \(0.0781\). The \(P\)-value is twice this amount, since it is the sum of areas in two equal tails. Thus the \(P\)-value is \(2(0.0781)=0.1562\).
