Guest Question.

The torsional assembly shown in Figure P6.76/77 consists of solid 2.50-in.-diameter aluminum [G = 4,000 ksi] segments (1) and (3) and a central solid 3.00-in.-diameter bronze [G = 6,500 ksi] segment (2). Concentrated torques of \(T_{B}=T_{0} \text { and } T_{C}=2 T_{0}\) are applied to the assembly at B and C, respectively. If the rotation angle at joint C must not exceed 3°, determine:
(a) the maximum magnitude of \(T_{0}\) that may be applied to the assembly.
(b) the maximum shear stress magnitude in aluminum segments (1) and (3).
(c) the maximum shear stress magnitude in bronze segment (2).

Admin
Solution

Section Properties: The polar moments of inertia for the aluminum and bronze segments are:

\(\begin{aligned}&J_{1}=J_{3}=\frac{\pi}{32}(2.50 in .)^{4}=3.834952 in. ^{4} \\&J_{2}=\frac{\pi}{32}(3.00 in .)^{4}=7.952156 in. ^{4}\end{aligned}\)

Equilibrium: Consider a free-body diagram cut around joint B, where the external torque \(T_{B}\) is applied:

\(\Sigma M_{x}=-T_{1}+T_{2}+T_{0}=0\) (a)

and also consider a FBD cut around joint C, where the external torque \(T_{C}\) is applied:

\(\Sigma M_{x}=-T_{2}+T_{3}+2 T_{0}=0\) (b)

Torque-Twist Relationships:

\(\phi_{1}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}} \quad \phi_{2}=\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}} \quad \phi_{3}=\frac{T_{3} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}}\)

Torque in Segment (3): From the problem statement, the rotation angle at C must not exceed 3°; consequently, the angle of twist in segment (3) must be the opposite of this value:

\(\phi_{3}=-3^{\circ}=-0.052360 rad\)

From the torque-twist relationships, the internal torque in segment (3) can be computed:

\(\phi_{3}=\frac{T_{3} L_{3}}{J_{3} G_{3}} \quad \therefore T_{3}=\frac{\phi_{3} J_{3} G_{3}}{L_{3}}=\frac{(-0.052360 rad )\left(3.834952 in .^{4}\right)(4,000 ksi )}{12 in .}=-66.9325 kip \text {-in. }\)

Consider Rotation Angle of Flange C Relative to Support A: The rotation angle at C is given by the sum of the angles of twist in segments (1) and (2). This rotation angle must not exceed 3°.

\(\phi_{C}=\phi_{1}+\phi_{2}=\frac{T_{1} L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{T_{2} L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}} \leq 3^{\circ}=0.052360 rad\) (c)

from Eq. (b):

\(T_{2}=T_{3}+2 T_{0}\) (d)

and from Eq. (a):

\(T_{1}=T_{2}+T_{0}=\left(T_{3}+2 T_{0}\right)+T_{0}=T_{3}+3 T_{0}\) (e)

Substitute the expressions derived for \(T_{1} \text { and } T_{2}\) in Eqs. (d) and (e) into Eq. (c):

\(\frac{\left(T_{3}+3 T_{0}\right) L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{\left(T_{3}+2 T_{0}\right) L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}} \leq 0.052360 rad\)

Simplify

\(T_{3}\left[\frac{L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}\right]+T_{0}\left[\frac{3 L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{2 L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}\right] \leq 0.052360 rad\)

Solve for \(T_{0}\):

\(T_{0} \leq \frac{0.052360 rad -T_{3}\left[\frac{L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}\right]}{\frac{3 L_{1}}{J_{1} G_{1}}+\frac{2 L_{2}}{J_{2} G_{2}}}\)

and compute:

\(\begin{aligned}&T_{0} \leq \frac{0.052360 rad -(-66.9325 kip – in .)\left[\frac{12 in .}{\left(3.834952 in .^{4}\right)(4,000 ksi )}+\frac{24 in .}{\left(7.952156 in .^{4}\right)(6,500 ksi )}\right]}{\frac{3(12 in .)}{\left(3.834952 in. ^{4}\right)(4,000 ksi )}+\frac{2(24 in .)}{\left(7.952156 in .^{4}\right)(6,500 ksi )}} \\&\leq 41.4590 kip \text {-in. }\end{aligned}\)

Backsubstitute this value into Eq. (d) to compute \(T_{2}\):

\(T_{2}=T_{3}+2 T_{0}=-66.9325 \text { kip-in. }+2(41.4590 \text { kip-in. })=15.9855 \text { kip-in. }\)

and backsubstitute this value into Eq. (e) to compute \(T_{1}\):

\(T_{1}=T_{3}+3 T_{0}=-66.9325 \text { kip-in. }+3(41.4590 \text { kip-in. })=57.4445 \text { kip-in. }\)

(a) Maximum magnitude of \(T_{0}:\)

\(T_{0, \max }=41.5 kip \text {-in. }\)

Maximum Shear Stress Magnitudes: The maximum shear stress magnitudes are:

\(\begin{aligned}&\tau_{1}=\frac{T_{1} c_{1}}{J_{1}}=\frac{(57.4445 kip – in .)(2.500 in . / 2)}{3.834952 in .^{4}}=18.72 ksi \\&\tau_{2}=\frac{T_{2} c_{2}}{J_{2}}=\frac{(15.9855 kip – in .)(3.000 in . / 2)}{7.952156 in .{ }^{4}}=3.02 ksi \\&\tau_{3}=\frac{T_{3} c_{3}}{J_{3}}=\frac{(66.9325 kip – in .)(2.500 in . / 2)}{3.834952 in .{ }^{4}}=21.8 ksi\end{aligned}\)

(b) Maximum Shear Stress Magnitude in Aluminum Segments:

\(\tau_{3}=21.8 ksi\)

(c) Maximum Shear Stress Magnitude in Bronze Segment:

\(\tau_{2}=3.02 ksi\)
