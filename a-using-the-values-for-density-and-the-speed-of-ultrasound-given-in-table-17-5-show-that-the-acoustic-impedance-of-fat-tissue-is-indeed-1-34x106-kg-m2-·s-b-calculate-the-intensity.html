Guest Question.

(a) Using the values for density and the speed of ultrasound given in Table 17.5,

Medium Density \(\left( kg / m ^{3}\right)\) Speed of Ultrasound (m/s) Acoustic Impedance \(\left( kg /\left( m ^{2} \cdot s \right)\right)\)
Air 1.3 330 429
Water 1000 1500 \(1.5 \times 10^{6}\)
Blood 1060 1570 \(1.66 \times 10^{6}\)
Fat 925 1450 \(1.34 \times 10^{6}\)
Muscle (average) 1075 1590 \(1.70 \times 10^{6}\)
Bone (varies) 1400–1900 4080 \(5.7 \times 10^{6} \text { to } 7.8 \times 10^{6}\)
Barium titanate (transducer material) 5600 5500 \(30.8 \times 10^{6}\)

show that the acoustic impedance of fat tissue is indeed \(1.34 \times 10^{6} kg /\left( m ^{2} \cdot s \right)\) .
(b) Calculate the intensity reflection coefficient of ultrasound when going from fat to muscle tissue.
Strategy for (a)
The acoustic impedance can be calculated using Z = ρv and the values for ρ and v found in Table 17.5.

Strategy for (b)
The intensity reflection coefficient for any boundary between two media is given by \(a=\frac{\left(Z_{2}-Z_{1}\right)^{2}}{\left(Z_{1}+Z_{2}\right)^{2}}\), and the acoustic impedance of muscle is given in Table 17.5.

Admin
Solution

Solution for (a)
(1) Substitute known values from Table 17.5

Medium Density \(\left( kg / m ^{3}\right)\) Speed of Ultrasound (m/s) Acoustic Impedance \(\left( kg /\left( m ^{2} \cdot s \right)\right)\)
Air 1.3 330 429
Water 1000 1500 \(1.5 \times 10^{6}\)
Blood 1060 1570 \(1.66 \times 10^{6}\)
Fat 925 1450 \(1.34 \times 10^{6}\)
Muscle (average) 1075 1590 \(1.70 \times 10^{6}\)
Bone (varies) 1400–1900 4080 \(5.7 \times 10^{6} \text { to } 7.8 \times 10^{6}\)
Barium titanate (transducer material) 5600 5500 \(30.8 \times 10^{6}\)

into Z = ρv .

\(Z=\rho v=\left(925 kg / m ^{3}\right)(1450 m / s )\) (17.40)

(2) Calculate to find the acoustic impedance of fat tissue.

\(1.34 \times 10^{6} kg /\left( m ^{2} \cdot s \right)\) (17.41)

This value is the same as the value given for the acoustic impedance of fat tissue.

Solution for (b)
Substitute known values into \(a=\frac{\left(Z_{2}-Z_{1}\right)^{2}}{\left(Z_{1}+Z_{2}\right)^{2}}\) to find the intensity reflection coefficient:

\(a=\frac{\left(Z_{2}-Z_{1}\right)^{2}}{\left(Z_{1}+Z_{2}\right)^{2}}=\frac{\left(1.34 \times 10^{6} kg /\left( m ^{2} \cdot s \right)-1.70 \times 10^{6} kg /\left( m ^{2} \cdot s \right)\right)^{2}}{\left(1.70 \times 10^{6} kg /\left( m ^{2} \cdot s \right)+1.34 \times 10^{6} kg /\left( m ^{2} \cdot s \right)\right)^{2}}=0.014\) (17.42)

Discussion
This result means that only 1.4% of the incident intensity is reflected, with the remaining being transmitted.
