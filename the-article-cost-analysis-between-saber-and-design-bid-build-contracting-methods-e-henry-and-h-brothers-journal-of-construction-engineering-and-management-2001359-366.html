Guest Question.

The article “Cost Analysis Between SABER and Design Bid Build Contracting Methods” (E. Henry and H. Brothers, Journal of Construction Engineering and Management, 2001:359-366) presents data on construction costs for 10 jobs bid by the traditional method (denoted \(X\) ) and 19 jobs bid by an experimental system (denoted \(Y)\). The data, in units of dollars per square meter, and their ranks, are presented in Table 6.2. Test \(H_{0}: \mu_{X} \leq \mu_{Y}\) versus \(H_{1}: \mu_{X}>\mu_{Y}\).

Solution

The sum of the \(X\) ranks is \(W=1+12+13+16+18+22+25+26+27+28=188\). The sample sizes are \(m=10\) and \(n=19\). We use the normal approximation and compute

\( \begin{aligned}z &=\frac{188-10(10+19+1) / 2}{\sqrt{10(19)(10+19+1) / 12}} \\ &=1.74\end{aligned}\)

Large values of \(W\) provide evidence against the null hypothesis. Therefore the \(P\)-value is the area under the normal curve to the right of \(z=1.74\). From the \(z\) table we find that the \(P\)-value is \(0.0409\).
