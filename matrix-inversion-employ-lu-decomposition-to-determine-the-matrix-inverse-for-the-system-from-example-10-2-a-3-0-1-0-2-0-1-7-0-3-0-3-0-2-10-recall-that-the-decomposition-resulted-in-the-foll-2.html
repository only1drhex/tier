Matrix Inversion

Employ LU decomposition to determine the matrix inverse for the system from .

\([A] = \left [ \begin{matrix}3 & - 0.1 & - 0.2 \\ 0.1 & 7 & - 0.3 \\0.3 & - 0.2 & 10 \end{matrix} \right ]\)

Recall that the decomposition resulted in the following lower and upper triangular matrices:

\([U] = \left [ \begin{matrix}3 & - 0.1 & - 0.2 \\ 0 & 7.00333 & - 0.293333 \\0 & 0 & 10.0120 \end{matrix} \right ] [L] = \left [ \begin{matrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0.0333333 & 1 & 0 \\0.100000& - 0.0271300 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\)
Admin
Solution

The first column of the matrix inverse can be determined by performing the forward-substitution solution procedure with a unit vector (with 1 in the first row) as the right-hand-side vector. Thus, Eq. (10.8),

[L]{D} = {B} (10.8)

the lower-triangular system, can be set up as

\( \left [ \begin{matrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0.0333333 & 1 & 0 \\0.100000& – 0.0271300 & 1 \end{matrix} \right ]\left \{ \begin{matrix} d_1\\ d_2 \\ d_3 \end{matrix} \right \}= \left \{ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right \}\)

and solved with forward substitution for \(\left\{D\right\}^T =\left\lfloor1 – 0.03333 – 0.1009 \right\rfloor \). This vector can then be used as the right-hand side of Eq. (10.3),

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} u_{11} & u_{12} & u_{13} \\ 0 & u_{22} & u_{23} \\ 0 & 0 & u_{33} \end{matrix} \right ]\left \{ \begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{3} \end{matrix} \right \}= \left \{ \begin{matrix} d_{1} \\ d_{2} \\ d_{3} \end{matrix} \right \} \) (10.3)

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 3 & – 0.1 & – 0.2 \\ 0 & 7.00333 & – 0.293333 \\ 0 & 0 & 10.0120 \end{matrix} \right ]\left \{ \begin{matrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ x_{3} \end{matrix} \right \}= \left \{ \begin{matrix} 1 \\ – 0.03333 \\ – 0.1009\end{matrix} \right \} \)

which can be solved by back substitution for \(\left\{X\right\}^T = \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.33249 & – 0.00518 & – 0.01008\end{matrix} \right] \),which is the first column of the matrix,

\([A]^{-1}= \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.33249 & 0 & 0 \\ – 0.00518 & 0 & 0 \\ – 0.01008 & 0 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

To determine the second column, Eq. (10.8) is formulated as

\(\left [ \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\0.0333333 & 1 & 0 \\ 0.100000 & – 0.0271300 &1\end{matrix} \right ]\left \{ \begin{matrix} d_{1} \\ d_{2} \\ d_{3} \end{matrix} \right \}=\left \{ \begin{matrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right \} \)

This can be solved for \(\left\{{D}\right\} \), and the results are used with Eq. (10.3) to determine \(\left\{X\right\}^T= \left\lfloor\begin{matrix} 0.004944 & 0.142903 &0.00271 \end{matrix}\right\rfloor \), which is the second column of the matrix,

\([A]^{-1}= \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.33249 & 0.004944 & 0 \\ – 0.00518 & 0.142903 & 0 \\ – 0.01008 & 0.00271 & 0 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

Finally, the forward- and back-substitution procedures can be implemented with \(\left\{B\right\}^T= \left\lfloor\begin{matrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \end{matrix} \right\rfloor \) to solve for \(\left\{X\right\}^T= \left\lfloor\begin{matrix} 0.006798& 0.004183 & 0.09988 \end{matrix} \right\rfloor \) , which is the final column of the matrix,

\([A]^{-1}= \left [ \begin{matrix} 0.33249 & 0.004944 &0.006798 \\ – 0.00518 & 0.142903 &0.004183 \\- 0.01008 & 0.00271 & 0.09988 \end{matrix} \right ] \)

The validity of this result can be checked by verifying that \([A][A]^{-1} = [I] \).
