Guest Question.

A trapezoidal channel has a bottom width of 20 ft, side slopes of 1 to 1, and flows at a depth of 3.00 ft. For n = 0.015 and a discharge of 360 cfs, calculate (a) the normal slope, (b) the critical slope and critical depth for 360 cfs, and (c) the critical slope at the normal depth of 3.00 ft.

Admin
Solution

\(\text { (a) } \quad Q=A \frac{1.486}{n} R^{2 / 3} S_{N}^{1 / 2}, \quad 360=(60+9)\left(\frac{1.486}{0.015}\right)\left(\frac{69.0}{20+6 \sqrt{2}}\right)^{2 / 3} S_{N}^{1 / 2}, \quad S_{N}=0.000853\).

(b) \(V=\frac{Q}{A}=\frac{360}{20 y+y^{2}} \quad \text { and } \quad V_{c}=\sqrt{\frac{g A_{c}}{b^{\prime}}}=\sqrt{\frac{(32.2)\left(20 y_{c}+y_{c}^{2}\right)}{20+2 y_{c}}}\).

Equating the velocity terms, squaring, and simplifying, we obtain

\(\frac{\left[y_{c}\left(20+y_{c}\right)\right]^{3}}{10+y_{c}}=8050\).

\(\text { which when solved by successive trials gives critical depth } y_{c}=2.1 ft \).

\(\text { The critical slope } S_{c} \text { is calculated using the Manning equation: }\)

\(360=\left[(20)(2.1)+(2.1)^{2}\right]\left(\frac{1.486}{0.015}\right)\left(\frac{(20)(2.1)+(2.1)^{2}}{20+(2)(2.1 \sqrt{2})}\right)^{2 / 3} S_{c}^{1 / 2}, \quad S_{c}=0.00282\).

This slope will maintain uniform critical flow at a critical depth of 2.1 ft and with Q = 360 cfs.

\(\text { (c) From }(a) \text {, for } y_{N}=3.00 ft , R=2.42 ft \text { and } A=69.0 ft ^{2} \text {. Also, }\) using (6) of

\(V_{c}=\sqrt{g A / b^{\prime}}=\sqrt{(32.2)(69.0) /[20+(2)(3)]}=9.24 ft / sec\).

Substituting these values in the Manning equation for velocity gives

\(9.24=(1.486 / 0.015)(2.42)^{2 / 3} S_{c}^{1 / 2}, \quad S_{c}=0.00268\).

This slope will produce uniform critical flow in the trapezoidal channel at a depth of 3.00 ft.
Note that in this case the flow Q = AV = (69.0)(9.24) = 638 cfs.
