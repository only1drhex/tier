Solution

\(SL_{ annual} = 96\) percent D = 300 units a year
H = $5 S = $25 \(\sigma _{dLT}=7\)

a. \(E(N) = (1 – SL_{amnual} )D = (1 – .96)(300) =12\) units

b. \(E(N)= E(n)\frac{D}{Q}\). Solving for E(n), you have

\(E(n)= E(N)\div \left\lgroup\frac{D}{Q}\right\rgroup = 12 \div \left\lgroup\frac{300}{Q} \right\rgroup \)

 

\(Q = \sqrt{\frac{2DS}{H} }=\sqrt{\frac{2(300)(25)}{5} }=54.77 \) (round to 55)

Then \(E(n)= 12 \div \left\lgroup\frac{300}{55} \right\rgroup =2.2\).

c. In order to find the lead time service level, you need the value of E(z). Because the value of E(n) is 2.2 and \(E(n) =E(z)σ_{dLT}\), you have 2.2 = E(z)(7). Solving gives \(E(z) = 2.2\div 7=.314\) . Interpolating in Table 13.3 gives the approximate lead time level. Thus,

\(\frac{.307 – .314}{.307 – .324}= \frac{.5793 – x}{.5793 – .5636} \)

Solving,

x = .5728

[ To interpolate , find the two vaues between which the computed number falls in the E(z) column. Then find the difference between the computed value and one end of the range, and divide by the difference between the two ends of the range . Perform the corresponding calculation on the two service levels using x for the unknown value, and solve for x. Often , simply ” eyeballing ” the unknown value will surffice]
