Guest Question.

A bridge rectifier with capacitor filter is fed from 220 V to 40 V step down transformer. If average d.c. current in load is 1 A and capacitor filter of 800 μF, calculate the load regulation and ripple factor. Assume power line frequency of 50 Hz. Neglect diode forward resistance and d.c. resistance of secondary of transformer.

Admin
Solution

\(V_{rms}\) = 40 V, \(I_{d.c.}\) = 1A, C = 800 μF and f = 50 Hz

\(V_{m} = \sqrt{2} V_{rms} = \sqrt{2}\) × 40 = 56.5685 V

\(V_{d.c. (FL)} = V_{m} –\frac{I_{d.c.}}{4fC}= 56.5685 – \frac{1}{4 × 50 × 800 × 10^{– 6}}\) = 50.3185 V

On no load, \(I_{d.c.}\) = 0
Hence, \(V_{d.c. (NL)} = V_{m}\) = 56.5685 V
Therefore, percentage of regulation = \(\frac{V_{d.c. (NL)} – V_{d.c. (NL)}}{I_{d.c. (NL)}}\) × 100

= \(\frac{56.5685 – 50.3185}{50.1385}\) × 100 = 12.42%

\(R_{L} =\frac{V_{d.c.}}{I_{d.c.}}=\frac{50.1385}{1}\) = 50.1385 Ω

Γ = \(\frac{1}{4\sqrt{3} fCR_{L}}=\frac{1}{4 \sqrt{3} × 50 × 800 × 10^{– 6} × 50.3185} \)= 0.0717, i.e. 7.17%
