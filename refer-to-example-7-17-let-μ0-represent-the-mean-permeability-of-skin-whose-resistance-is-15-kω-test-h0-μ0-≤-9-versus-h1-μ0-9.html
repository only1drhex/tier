Guest Question.

Refer to . Let \(\mu_{0}\) represent the mean permeability of skin whose resistance is \(15 \mathrm{k} \Omega\). Test \(H_{0}: \mu_{0} \leq 9\) versus \(H_{1}: \mu_{0}>9\).

Admin
Solution

Since \(\mu_{0}\) is the mean permeability of skin whose resistance is \(15 \mathrm{k} \Omega, \mu_{0}=\beta_{0}+\beta_{1}(15)\).

Now let \(\hat{y}=\beta_{0}+\beta_{1}(15)\). The quantity

\(\frac{\widehat{y}-\left[\beta_{0}+\beta_{1}(15)\right]}{s_{\hat{y}}}=\frac{\widehat{y}-\mu_{0}}{s_{\hat{y}}}\)

has a Student’s \(t\) distribution with \(n-2=48\) degrees of freedom. Under \(H_{0}\), we take \(\mu_{0}=9\). The test statistic is therefore

\(\frac{\hat{y}-9}{s_{\hat{y}}}\)

We compute \(\widehat{y}\) and \(s_{\hat{y}}\) :

\(\begin{gathered}\widehat{y}=\widehat{\beta}_{0}+\widehat{\beta}_{1}(15)=14.3775-0.300094(15)=9.8761 \\s_{\hat{y}}=1.80337 \sqrt{\frac{1}{50}+\frac{(15-21.7548)^{2}}{1886.48}}=0.37908\end{gathered}\)

The value of the test statistic is

\(\frac{9.8761-9}{0.37908}=2.31\)

There are \(n-2=50-2=48\) degrees of freedom. This number of degrees of freedom is not found in Table A.3; however, the \(P\)-value can be determined with a calculator or computer software to be \(0.0126\). Alternatively, since the number of degrees of freedom is greater than 30 , one can use the \(z\) table (Table A.2) to approximate the \(P\)-value as \(0.0104\). It is reasonable to conclude that the mean permeability is greater than \(9 \mu \mathrm{m} / \mathrm{h}\).
