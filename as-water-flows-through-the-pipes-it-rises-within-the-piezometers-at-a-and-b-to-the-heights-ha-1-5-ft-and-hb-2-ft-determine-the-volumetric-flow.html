Guest Question.

As water flows through the pipes, it rises within the piezometers at A and B to the heights \(h_{A }= 1.5 ft\) and\( h_B = 2 ft \). Determine the volumetric flow

Admin
Solution

Continuity Equation. Consider the water within the transition from A to B to be the
control volume.

\(\frac{\partial }{\partial t} \int _{cs} \rho d\forall + \int _{cs} \rho V \cdot dA = 0 \)

 

\(0 – V_B A_B + V_A A_A = 0 \)

 

\(-V_B[\pi (\frac{1.25 ft}{2})^2] + V_A [\pi (\frac{0.75ft}{2})^2] = 0 \)

 

\(V_A = 2.778 V_B \) (1)

Bernoulli Equation. The static pressure at A and B is given by

\(P_A = \rho _{w}gh_{A} = \rho _{w}(32.2 ft/s^2)(1.5 ft) = 48.3 \rho _{w} \)

 

\(P_B = \rho _{w}gh_{B} = \rho _{w} (32.2 ft/s^2)(2 ft) = 64.4\rho _{w} \)

If we set the datum to coincide with the horizontal streamline connecting A and B, \(z_A = z_B = 0. \)
\(\frac{P_B}{\rho } + \frac{V^{2}_{B}}{2} + gz_B = \frac{P_A}{\rho } + \frac{V^{2}_{A}}{2} + gz_A \)

 

\(\frac{64.4\rho _w}{\rho _w} + \frac{V^{2}_{B}}{2} + +0 = \frac{48.3\rho _w}{\rho _w} +\frac{V^{2}_{A}}{2} +0 \)

 

\(V^{2}_{A} – V^{2}_{B} =32.2 \) (2)

Solving Eqs. (1) and (2) yields

\(V_A = 6.082 ft/s \)

 

\(V_B = 2.190 ft/s \)

Discharge.

\(Q = V_A A_A = (6.082 ft/s)[\pi (\frac{0.75ft}{2})^2] \)

 

\(= 2.69 ft^3/s \)
