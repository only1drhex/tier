\(T_{r}=\frac{170}{126.2}=1.347 \quad \text { and } \quad P_{r}=\frac{100}{33.94}=2.946\)

 

From Fig. 6.6-3, Z = 0.741, so

 

\(\underline{V}(t=0)=Z \frac{R T(t=0)}{P(t=0)}=Z \underline{V}^{ IG }(t=0)=1.047 \times 10^{-4} m ^{3} / mol\)

 

Therefore, following Illustration 6.5-1,

 

\(\begin{aligned}N(t=0) &=1432.7 mol \\N(t) &=1432.7-10 t mol\end{aligned}\)

 

and

 

\(\underline{V}(t=50 min )=\frac{0.15 m ^{3}}{(1432.7-500) mol }=1.6082 \times 10^{-4} m ^{3} / mol\)

 

To compute the temperature and pressure at the end of the 50 minutes, we use

 

\(\underline{S}(t=0)=\underline{S}(t=50 min )\)

 

and recognize that

 

\(\underline{S}(t=0)=\underline{S}^{ IG }(T, \underline{V})_{t=0}+\left(\underline{S}-\underline{S}^{ IG }\right)_{t=0}\)

 

 

 

and

 

\(\underline{S}(t=50 min )=\underline{S}^{ IG }(T, \underline{V})_{t=50 \min }+\left(\underline{S}-\underline{S}^{ IG }\right)_{t=50 \min }\)

 

so that

 

\(\begin{aligned}\underline{S}(t=50 min )-\underline{S}(t=0)=& \underline{S}^{ IG }(T, P)_{t=50 \min }-\underline{S}^{ IG }(T, P)_{t=0} \\&+\left(\underline{S}-\underline{S}^{ IG }\right)_{t=50 \min }-\left(\underline{S}-\underline{S}^{ IG }\right)_{t=0}\end{aligned}\)

 

where

 

\(\begin{aligned}\underline{S}^{ IG }(T, P)_{t=50 min }-\underline{S}^{ IG }(T, P)_{t=0} \\=& 27.2 \ln \frac{T(t=50 min )}{170} \\&+4.2 \times 10^{-3}[T(t=50 min )-17 P]-8.314 \ln \frac{P(t=50 min )}{100}\end{aligned}\)

 

Both of the \(\left(\underline{S}-\underline{S}^{ IG }\right)\) terms are obtained from the corresponding-states charts. \(\left(\underline{S}-\underline{S}^{ IG }\right)_{t=0} \text { is }\) easily evaluated, since the initial state is known; that is, \(T_{r}=1.347 \text { and } P_{r}=2.946\), so that, from Fig. 6.6-5 \(\left(\underline{S}-\underline{S}^{ IG }\right)_{t=0}=-2.08 cal /( mol K )=-8.70 J /( mol K ) \text {. To compute }\left(\underline{S}-S^{ IG }\right) \text { at }\) t = 50 minutes is more difficult because neither \(T_{r} \text { nor } P_{r}\) is known. The procedure to be followed is

 

1. \(\underline{V}\) (t = 50min) is known, so guess a value of T(t = 50min). (A reasonable first guess is the ideal gas solution obtained earlier.)

2. Use \(\underline{V}\) (t = 50 min) and T(t = 50 min) to compute, by trial and error, P(t = 50 min) from

 

\(P=\frac{R T}{\underline{V}} Z\left(\frac{T}{T_{ c }}, \frac{P}{P_{c}}\right)=\frac{R T}{\underline{V}} Z\left(T_{r}, P_{r}\right)\)

 

3. Use the values of P and T from steps 1 and 2 to compute \(\left(\underline{S}\underline{S}^{IG}\right)_{t=50}min\).

4. Determine whether \(\underline{S}\)(t = 50min) = \(\underline{S}\)(t = 0) is satisfied with the trial values of T and P. If not, guess another value of T(t = 50) and go back to step 2.

Our solution after a number of trials is

 

\(\begin{array}{l}T(t=50 min )=136 K \\P(t=50 min )=41 bar\end{array}\)

 

Comment

Because of the inaccuracy in reading numerical values from the corresponding-states graphs, this solution cannot be considered to be of high accuracy.
